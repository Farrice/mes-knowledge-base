# Andrew Lux: The Real Strategy

## Forget the Original Playbook

The original strategy was built for a "content creator DJ" — someone who wants to dance on TikTok, do goofy trends, and build audience through personality.

**That's not Andrew.**

Andrew is:
- An introvert who doesn't love the spotlight
- A producer first, DJ second (DJs because he has to)
- Someone with ghost production credits on hits
- Tired of soulless content that doesn't represent him
- Wanting to be taken seriously as an artist, known for his MUSIC

The Dillon Francis playful approach? He's tried it. It doesn't fit. It feels forced. And audiences can smell inauthenticity.

**This document is the real strategy for the real Andrew.**

## The Core Insight

Andrew has been trying to play a game that doesn't suit him.

The "dancing DJ doing trends" playbook works for extroverts who genuinely enjoy being performers. For introverts, it's unsustainable and eventually reads as fake.

But here's what the research revealed:

**Some of the most respected artists in electronic music built careers through mystique, not personality. Through craft, not content. Through the music speaking for itself.**

- **Burial** — Remained anonymous for years. Never performed live. Mercury Prize nominated.
- **ZHU** — Launched with zero identity, just a logo and music. Columbia Records deal in 9 months.
- **Gesaffelstein** — No interviews since 2014. Dark sunglasses and mask. Silence as brand.
- **Deadmau5** — Mau5head separated him from personality performance. Visual brand, not personal brand.
- **Four Tet** — 604K Instagram followers with minimal content. Just presence and musical output.

**The common thread: The music was undeniable. And they rejected the game rather than playing it poorly.**

## Andrew's Hidden Asset: Ghost Production

Andrew has produced hits for other people. He's been the secret sauce behind tracks that blew up — and got none of the credit.

This isn't a liability. **This is leverage.**

### The KSHMR Playbook

KSHMR (Niles Hollowell-Dhar) was half of The Cataracs ("Like a G6" — #1 Billboard). When his partner left, he pivoted to ghost production:
- Made "Tsunami" for DVBBS (one of the biggest EDM tracks of that era)
- Produced for Tiësto, Selena Gomez

Then in 2014, he created a new identity — KSHMR — and released anonymously. In 2015, he revealed himself during Tiësto's Ultra set while dropping "Secrets" with Tiësto.

His reflection: **"Going into dance music was like a second try at having a music career, and this time I wanted to make sure it reflects who I am."**

### What Andrew Can Do

**Option 1: The Strategic Reveal**

If Andrew has ghost production credits he can legally claim (check NDAs), this becomes content gold:
- "I produced this track you've heard 10 million times. Here's how."
- The "holy shit, HE made that?" moment transfers credibility instantly.
- One TikTok revealing a major ghost credit could be worth more than 100 dancing videos.

**Option 2: The Skills Demonstration**

Even if he can't name specific tracks, he can demonstrate the LEVEL:
- Production breakdowns showing world-class technique
- "How I'd approach producing [style of track]" content
- Let the craft speak without naming clients

**Option 3: The Industry Insider Positioning**

Frame himself as someone who's been in the rooms, worked on the hits, knows how the sausage is made — now stepping into his own spotlight.

Fred isn't doing trends. He's documenting his creative life. There's a difference.

**Andrew's version:**

**"His anonymity stems from his belief that music is faceless. He believes there is too much emphasis in our society on the creator as opposed to the creation."**

Andrew doesn't need to go full anonymous. But he can lean toward **"the music is the message"** rather than **"I am the message."**

## The Producer's Producer Path

There's another route entirely: **Industry respect over fan following.**

### The Max Martin Model

Max Martin has 28 #1 Billboard singles. More than Madonna, the Beatles, or Elvis.

His strategy: **Radical anonymity.**
- First interview in 16 years was in 2017
- Doesn't seek spotlight
- Extremely selective about who he works with

**Result:** If a song has Martin's name on it, artists and labels know it's quality. His selectivity IS the brand.

### What This Means for Andrew

If Andrew genuinely prefers producing to performing, he could:

1. **Build reputation as the go-to producer** in a specific lane
2. **Let credited collaborations drive discovery** — "Produced by Andrew Lux"
3. **Create content that signals craft** — Other producers watch, respect grows
4. **Accept that fan following may grow slower** — But industry respect compounds

The question: Does Andrew want fan fame or industry respect?

If he wants EDC main stage, he probably needs fan metrics. But if he'd be happier as a respected producer who occasionally performs his own material, the path is different.

## Revised Content Strategy

### The "Craft Over Clout" Weekly Rhythm

**4-6 pieces per week (not 10+)**

| Day | Content Type | Description |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| Mon | Studio session | Timelapse or clip with music overlay |
| Tue | Production breakdown | How a sound was made |
| Wed | Sample/sound design | Skill demonstration |
| Thu | Work in progress | Tease of upcoming release |
| Fri | Visualizer or release | New music when available |
| Sat | Optional: Behind-the-scenes | Only if authentic moment exists |

**What's different:**
- No trend-chasing
- No personality performance
- No dancing
- No forced humor
- Just craft, process, and music

### Content That Fits Andrew

**1. The "Making Of" Format**
- Screen recordings of production sessions
- Show the layers being built
- Music does the talking

**2. The Sample Flip**
- Take something unexpected, flip it into bass house
- Demonstrates skill without requiring personality

**3. The A/B Comparison**
- "Here's what this track sounded like at midnight vs. what it sounds like now"
- Progress and craft, no face required

**4. The Mood Board / Inspiration**
- What's influencing the sound
- Taste as brand

**5. The Ghost Credit Reveal (if possible)**
- Strategic reveal of past work
- Instant credibility

### Platform Approach

**YouTube** — Long-form production content (tutorials, breakdowns, sessions)
**Instagram** — Curated aesthetic, studio shots, visualizers
**TikTok** — Production clips, sample flips (if they perform, great; if not, no forcing)
**SoundCloud** — Bootlegs, remixes, works in progress

**What to deprioritize:**
- Trend-based TikTok content
- Personality-driven anything
- Content that requires being "on"
