# Antigravity: The System That Thinks With You

> A guide to what this system is, what it does, and why it matters — written for humans, not engineers.

## The One-Sentence Version

Antigravity is an AI orchestration system that gives one person access to 99 expert-level advisors across 15 business domains — copywriting, sales psychology, brand strategy, consumer research, video production, SEO, and more — with the ability to deploy them individually, in teams, or as entire councils, producing professional-grade deliverables in hours instead of weeks.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Most people using AI treat it like a search engine with better grammar. They type a prompt, get a generic answer, and wonder why the output feels flat.

That's because general-purpose AI has breadth but no depth. It knows a little about everything but hasn't studied anything the way a real expert has. Ask it to write sales copy and you get competent text — but not copy that understands NEPQ objection handling the way Jeremy Miner teaches it, or conviction architecture the way Alen Sultanic structures a VSL.

Antigravity fixes this.

Instead of one generalist AI, you get a system that routes your request to the right specialist — or combination of specialists — based on what you actually need. Each specialist carries the real methodology of a specific expert: their frameworks, their thinking patterns, their quality standards. Not a summary. The full operating system.

**The analogy**: Imagine hiring a consulting firm with 99 senior partners across 15 practice areas, each of whom studied under a specific master in their field. You walk in with a problem, and the firm assigns the right partner — or assembles a custom team — before you finish explaining what you need.

That's what this system does. Except the firm lives on your laptop, responds in seconds, and costs nothing per engagement.

## How It Actually Works

### The Intent Pipeline

Every request flows through a 4-stage pipeline:

**Stage 1 — SCORE**: Rate the clarity of your request on a 1-5 scale. Is there a specific deliverable? An audience? Constraints? A clear end state?

**Stage 2 — SHARPEN**: If the request is vague (score 3 or below), the system asks targeted questions to fill in what's missing — but only what's missing. One round, not an interrogation.

**Stage 3 — ROUTE**: Match the request to the right expert(s) using a 15-domain registry with explicit routing logic. Multi-domain requests get ensemble pairings — specific expert combinations known to produce force-multiplier results.

**Stage 4 — PRESENT**: For complex tasks, show the expert recommendation and get confirmation before executing. For simple tasks, skip straight to execution.

The whole pipeline takes seconds. You describe what you need; the system figures out who should handle it.

### The Context Engine

Not every task needs the same depth. The system loads expert knowledge in tiers:

| Tier | What Loads | When |
|------|-----------|------|
| **0 — Card** | Expert's identity, domain, key frameworks (~80 tokens) | Routing decisions, quick references |
| **1 — Standard** | Skill file + specific workflow (~1,350 tokens) | Single expert, clear task |
| **2 — Deep** | Skill + full genius methodology (~2,550 tokens) | Creative or complex work |
| **3 — Sub-Agent** | Spawns a fresh expert with dedicated context | Multi-expert work, large deliverables |

This means the system isn't wasting resources loading everything it knows about copywriting when you just need a quick hook. But when you need a full sales page with proof engineering and rhetorical polish, it can go deep across multiple experts without running out of context.

### Compound Combinations

Certain expert pairings produce results that exceed what either expert delivers alone. These are documented and the system knows when to deploy them:

- **Jeremy Miner + Michael Bernoff** — Identity-first sales. Bernoff rewires the prospect's self-image, Miner closes with neuro-emotional persuasion.
- **Shaan Puri + Kallaway** — Story-driven viral content. Puri's narrative structure + Kallaway's dopamine psychology.
- **Lara Acosta + Cardinal Mason** — LinkedIn revenue engine. Acosta builds authority, Mason monetizes the attention.
- **Bond Halbert + David Deutsch** — Legendary direct response. Halbert's market language excavation + Deutsch's musical architecture.

There are 15 documented compound combinations, each with a specific trigger condition.

### The Quality Gate

Every expert-driven output passes through a silent 3-point quality check before delivery:

1. **Intent Alignment** — Does this match what was actually asked for?
2. **Expert Standard** — Would the real expert be proud of this work?
3. **Adversarial Resilience** — Would a domain expert pick this apart?

If any check fails, the system self-corrects once. If it still fails, it flags the specific weakness. Maximum one retry — no infinite loops.

## The Architecture (For the Technically Curious)

Antigravity runs on a 3-layer architecture:

**Layer 1 — Directives**: 38 standard operating procedures written in Markdown. These define *what* to do — quality gates, routing logic, collaboration protocols, session management. They're human-readable and editable.

**Layer 2 — Orchestration**: The AI itself, operating as an intelligent router. It reads directives, applies the intent pipeline, selects experts, manages context loading, enforces quality gates, and handles errors. This is the decision-making layer.

**Layer 3 — Execution**: 31 deterministic Python scripts that handle API calls, data processing, and system operations. These are reliable, testable, and never hallucinate — they either work or they throw an error.

The design principle: push complexity into deterministic code. Let the AI focus on judgment, routing, and quality — the things it's actually good at.

### The Extraction Pipeline

Where do the 99 experts come from?

Each expert is built through a multi-stage extraction process:

1. **Source Material** — Video transcripts, books, courses, interviews from the real expert
2. **Extraction** — A structured process that pulls out frameworks, mental models, quality standards, and thinking patterns — not summaries, but *operating systems*
3. **Skill Construction** — The extracted methodology becomes a completion engine with 3-5 end-to-end workflows, each carrying full expert context
4. **Agent Assembly** — The skill gets wrapped in an agent persona with persistent memory, domain boundaries, and invocation protocols
5. **Registry Integration** — The agent gets registered in the domain registry with routing logic, swim lanes, and compound combination mappings

This means every expert in the system traces back to real source material. The system isn't generating expert-sounding text — it's deploying verified methodology from specific practitioners.

### Completion Engine Architecture

Each skill follows the same structure:

```
skills/[expert-name]/
  SKILL.md          — Expert identity, domain, capabilities, invocation
  genius.md         — Deep methodology: frameworks, mental models, quality tests
  workflows/
    01-primary.md   — Main workflow (e.g., "Write a Sales Page")
    02-secondary.md — Secondary workflow (e.g., "Audit Existing Copy")
    03-tertiary.md  — Third workflow (e.g., "Email Sequence")
```

Each workflow is self-contained: it carries the expert context, step-by-step process, quality criteria, and output format. You can hand someone a single workflow file and they could execute it independently.

## What This Is NOT

- **Not an AI wrapper**. This isn't a UI skin on ChatGPT. It's an orchestration layer with its own routing intelligence, quality gates, and expert methodology.
- **Not a prompt library**. Each expert carries full methodology, not a collection of prompt templates. The 1,750 original prompts were consolidated into 435 completion-engine workflows.
- **Not autonomous**. The system proposes, presents, and recommends. It doesn't make decisions without you. The intelligence layer observes patterns — you decide what to do with them.
- **Not a replacement for human expertise**. It's a multiplier. The experts in the system are modeled on real practitioners with real track records. The system makes their methodology accessible, not disposable.
