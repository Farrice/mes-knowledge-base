People defer to perceived experts.

**Deployment**: Establish expertise before making claims

**Ethical Use**: Authority must be genuine

Limited availability increases perceived value.

# The Content Batching Method Nobody Talks About

Every content creation system I tried made the same mistake.

I call it **Type Batching**, and it's changed everything about how I create.

---

## Why Platform Batching Fails

When you batch by platform, you're constantly switching between different **thinking modes:**

- Writing a tweet requires compression: maximum punch, minimum words
- Writing a newsletter requires expansion: deep exploration, full development
- Writing a LinkedIn post requires hybrid thinking: hook + value + engagement

Each of these modes requires different mental states. Every time you switch platforms, your brain has to completely reconfigure.

This is why "content creation day" is so exhausting. You're not just creating content‚Äîyou're context-switching between fundamentally different creative modes dozens of times.

---

## The Type Batching Method

Instead of batching by where content goes, batch by **what kind of thinking** it requires.

**Type 1: Idea Capture (Divergent Thinking)**
- Brainstorming topics
- Collecting insights from conversations/reading
- Noticing patterns worth exploring
- Writing rough observations

**Type 2: Core Development (Deep Thinking)**
- Turning ideas into full arguments
- Writing long-form drafts
- Developing frameworks and models
- Creating the "source" content

**Type 3: Compression (Reductive Thinking)**
- Extracting tweetable insights from long-form
- Writing hooks and headlines
- Creating summaries and takeaways
- Cutting and tightening

**Type 4: Distribution Packaging (Adaptive Thinking)**
- Formatting for specific platforms
- Adding platform-specific elements (hashtags, CTAs)
- Scheduling and publishing
- Creating visual elements

---

## How It Works in Practice

**Session Type 1: Idea Capture (30-45 min, 2-3x/week)**

Your only job: collect raw material. Don't evaluate, don't develop, just capture.

- Voice memo thoughts during the day
- Screenshot interesting ideas from reading
- Jot one-line observations after conversations
- Add to a single "idea inbox"

Mental mode: Open, curious, non-judgmental. No pressure to create finished content.

**Session Type 2: Core Development (90-120 min, 1-2x/week)**

Your only job: develop ONE idea into a complete piece.

- Pick the ripest idea from your inbox
- Write it fully‚Äîdon't worry about platform
- Aim for 800-1500 words of raw development
- This becomes your "source document"

Mental mode: Deep focus, exploratory, thorough. You're thinking, not polishing.

**Session Type 3: Compression (45-60 min, 1x/week)**

Your only job: extract smaller pieces from source content.

- Take your source document(s) from the week
- Pull out tweetable lines
- Identify standalone insights
- Write hooks for each platform
- Create "atomic" content pieces

Mental mode: Analytical, ruthless, focused on essence.

**Session Type 4: Distribution (30-45 min, 1x/week)**

Your only job: package and schedule.

- Take your atomic pieces and format them
- Add platform-specific elements
- Schedule everything for the week
- Create any needed images/graphics

Mental mode: Operational, systematic, finishing.

Old method (platform batching):
- 5 platform-switching sessions/week
- 3-5 context switches per session
- ~20 mode switches total
- High cognitive load, low output

New method (type batching):
- 4 dedicated sessions/week
- 1 mode per session
- 4 mode switches total
- Low cognitive load, high output

Same time investment. Completely different result.
