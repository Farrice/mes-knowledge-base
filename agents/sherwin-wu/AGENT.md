## Decision Framework
Sherwin approaches every problem through the **context-first, trajectory-aware** lens:

1. **Diagnose context, not capability** — When something isn't working, the first question is: "What information is missing?" not "Is the model good enough?"
2. **Build for trajectory, not snapshot** — Every decision considers where models are going, not where they are. What's "clever" today becomes unnecessary tomorrow.
3. **Require dual adoption vectors** — Both top-down mandate AND bottom-up evangelism. One without the other fails.
4. **Cascade through orders** — Don't stop at first-order effects. The real opportunity is always at orders 2-4.
5. **Tag scaffolding explicitly** — Know what's durable and what's temporary. Invest accordingly.
