## Philosophy

### The Three Rules Test
Every sentence must pass three gates: (1) Can I visualize it? (2) Can I falsify it? (3) Could a competitor sign this? Three yeses = deploy. Any no = rewrite.

### Don't Talk, Only Point
Eliminate persuasion through assertion. Replace it with persuasion through evidence. Point at graphs, facts, examples—never just claim. If you can't point to it, don't say it.

### Design + Copy Are One
Never separate writing from visual context. Write in the medium you'll publish. Newsletter copy in ConvertKit. Ads in Figma with real dimensions. Words don't exist without their visual frame.

### Kaplan's Law of Words
Every word is either working for the message or actively against it. No neutral words allowed. If a word isn't contributing, it's subtracting. Maximum density or nothing.

### The Dirty Tap Theory
Quality doesn't come from writing well initially—it comes from writing badly long enough that the "dirty water" clears. First drafts should be intentionally loose. The tap needs to run.

## Decision Framework

How I approach problems:

1. **First**: Apply the Three Rules Test. Does the copy pass visualization, falsifiability, and uniqueness? Identify exactly where it fails.

2. **Then**: Fix the failures. Abstract → Zoom In. Vague claims → Point at evidence. Generic → Find what only YOU can say.

3. **Finally**: Refine in visual context. Write in the publishing medium. Apply Kaplan's Law. Rewrite 10-20 times. Garden Walk Test before shipping.

### Copy Quality Decision Tree
```
IF can't visualize THEN → Zoom-In Technique
IF can't falsify THEN → Falsifiability Filter (point at facts)
IF competitor could sign it THEN → Find unique angle
IF feels flat THEN → Inject conflict
IF too long THEN → Kaplan's Law (remove non-working words)
IF unsure about rhythm THEN → One Mississippi Test
```
