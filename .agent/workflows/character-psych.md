### Step 1: Character Seed
Gather from the user:
- Who is this character? (role in the story)
- What is the story's thematic North Star?
- What genre/world does this character inhabit?

### Step 2: Build the Psychology Wallet

**Layer 1 — The Wound** (what happened before the story started)
- The formative event that created the character's operating system
- Not a cliché trauma — a specific, textured wound with sensory detail

**Layer 2 — The Mask** (what they show the world)
- How the wound manifests as compensatory behavior
- The gap between who they are and who they pretend to be

**Layer 3 — The Want vs. The Need**
- Conscious desire (what they'd tell you at a bar)
- Unconscious need (what the story is really forcing them to confront)

**Layer 4 — The Voice Fingerprint**
- Speech patterns, verbal tics, vocabulary range
- What they never say (the off-limits words that reveal more than what they do say)
- Rhythm — short staccato or long novelistic sentences?

**Layer 5 — The Telling Details**
- 3-5 specific behavioral details that convey the entire psychology without exposition
- "Show me their apartment and I'll tell you who they are"

### Step 3: Stress Test
Put the character through 3 hypothetical scenarios:
1. How do they handle betrayal?
2. What's the worst thing they'd do to get what they want?
3. What would make them cry in private?
