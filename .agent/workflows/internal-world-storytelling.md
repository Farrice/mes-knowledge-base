# /internal-world-storytelling — Consumer Internal World Stories

Deploy Dai Media's Crown Jewel #4 — the Internal World Storytelling System. Creates content that enters the consumer's mind and narrates thoughts they've never heard spoken. Uses the Four Movements (Entry → Recognition → Permission → Possibility) to create stories where the reader doesn't watch — they recognize themselves.

## Inputs Required

Ask the user for (or extract from context):

1. **[CONSUMER POSTURE]**: Detailed individual understanding (from `/consumer-posture-profile` or equivalent)
2. **[BRAND/PRODUCT]**: What the story connects to
3. **[STORY CONTEXT]**: Where it lives — ad, landing page, email, LinkedIn post, video script
4. **[PRIMARY EMOTIONAL OUTCOME]**: The feeling they're trying to access (not the problem they're solving)
5. **[STORY LENGTH]**: Short (100-300 words), Medium (300-700), or Long (700-1500+)

> **Best with**: A Consumer Posture Profile and VoC data. The more you know about the consumer's real language, the sharper the recognition will hit.

### 2. Build the Internal World

Following the prompt's execution protocol:

1. **MAP** — The internal landscape: repeating thoughts, dominant feelings, internal dialogues, the "mental furniture" of their daily experience.
2. **IDENTIFY** — The Unspeakable Register: thoughts they have but would never say aloud. This is where the recognition spike lives.
3. **CHOOSE** — Entry point: the moment that creates immediate recognition. A sensory detail, a behavioral snapshot, a thought they have every Tuesday at 11 PM.
4. **BUILD** the Four Movements:
   - **ENTRY**: Meet them inside their experience using sensory specificity (the weight of the laptop, the low hum of the notification, the cold coffee on the desk)
   - **RECOGNITION**: Name what they've never heard named. Collapse distance using second-person ("You"). This is the "how did they know?" moment.
   - **PERMISSION**: Remove shame. Validate the feeling they've been hiding. Make it okay to want what they want.
   - **POSSIBILITY**: Open a door to the brand's world without promising a destination. No hard pitch. Just a door.
5. **WEAVE** — Sensory specificity throughout. The weight of things. The sound of notifications. The feel of a specific fabric.
6. **CONNECT** — Acknowledge you understand this world. Don't pitch.

### 3. Deliver Output

Produce a structured deliverable containing:
- **Internal Landscape Map** — the terrain of their mind
- **The Unspeakable Register** — 10-15 thoughts they'd never say aloud
- **Entry Point Selection** — the chosen moment and why
- **Full Story Narrative** — the complete story following the Four Movements
- **Sensory Detail Layer** — the specific textures, sounds, and weights used
- **Connection Architecture** — how the brand enters without a pitch

If the user requests multiple stories, produce variations for different contexts (LinkedIn, email, ad, landing page).

## Quality Gate
- **Recognition Spike**: Does the narrative feel almost invasive — like you read their private journal?
- **Sensory Specificity**: Are there at least 3 sensory details that anchor the story in physical reality?
- **No Pitch**: Does the brand connection feel organic, not forced?
- **The Kristen Stewart Test**: Would the actual person feel genuinely "seen" — or just targeted?
- **Second Person**: Is "you" used to collapse distance and create intimacy?
