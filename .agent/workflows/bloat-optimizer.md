# /bloat-optimizer — Multi-Agent Complexity Reduction

Simplify over-engineered multi-agent systems by removing unnecessary layers and optimizing coordination.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Load Expert Context
Read `skills/nate-b-jones-orchestration-intelligence/SKILL.md` and `skills/nate-b-jones-orchestration-intelligence/genius.md`.

Read `skills/nate-b-jones-orchestration-intelligence/workflows/bloat-to-depth-optimizer.md` for the specific workflow.

### Step 2: Gather System State
Ask the user:
1. Describe your current multi-agent system architecture (number of agents, roles, coordination)
2. What symptoms are you seeing? (slow, inconsistent, expensive, fragile, hard to debug)
3. What's the actual objective this system serves?
4. What's the budget/latency constraint?

### Step 3: Execute the Bloat-to-Depth Optimization
Follow the 4-step reduction process:
1. **Complexity Census** — Count agents, tools, coordination layers, and handoff points
2. **Value-per-Layer Analysis** — Measure what each layer contributes vs. what it costs
3. **Removal Candidates** — Identify layers where removal improves or doesn't degrade quality
4. **Depth Investment** — Redirect freed resources into the layers that actually matter

### Step 4: Produce Deliverable
Output a complexity optimization report:
- Before/after architecture comparison
- Specific layers to remove or consolidate
- Expected impact on quality, speed, and cost
- Simplified coordination protocol
- Monitoring plan to verify improvements

### Step 5: Quality Gate
Score against: Intent Alignment, Expert Standard, Adversarial Resilience.
Composite < 7 → retry weakest section.
