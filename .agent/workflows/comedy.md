# /comedy — Comedy Intelligence Engine

Deploy Robert Mac's comedy intelligence as a precision writing filter on any content. Not a full agent deployment — a **concentrated strike** that loads the essential comedy mechanics, calibrates to cultural context, and transforms content from factually accurate to emotionally resonant with maximum influence.

**Philosophy**: Comedy takes something specific and makes it universally relatable. This command weaponizes that principle.

## Usage

```
/comedy [paste content or describe what you're writing]
/comedy punch up this LinkedIn post
/comedy apply to this sales email
/comedy this landing page intro
```

## Steps

### 1. Load Comedy Intelligence Core
// turbo
Read these files in order — they are your comedy operating system:

1. `skills/robert-mack-comedy-writing/genius.md` — The 12 genius patterns (comedy architecture)
2. `skills/fresh-voice-system/SKILL.md` — Fresh's voice DNA, rhythm patterns, comedy mechanics
3. `skills/robert-mack-comedy-writing/references/prompts/linkedin-comedy-intelligence.md` — The LinkedIn Comedy Intelligence Engine (methodology section)

After reading, internalize these **non-negotiable comedy principles**:
- **Skewed Perspective Engine**: Take expected → rotate 15-45°. Not the opposite — the unexpected adjacent.
- **Painful Specificity**: The detail so precise it makes people laugh because it's SO accurate. Never generic.
- **Tension-Release Architecture**: Build discomfort, then release with recognition. The laugh IS the release.
- **Parenthetical Asides**: The voice commenting on itself — always in parentheses, always undercutting.
- **Pattern Break Mechanics**: Set up expectations with rhythm or logic, then violate at the exact right moment.
- **Benign Violation Threshold**: Something must feel slightly wrong, inappropriate, or uncomfortable — but safe enough to laugh at.
- **The Deflation**: Build up, then collapse. "That's it. That's the whole strategy."
- **Uncomfortable Naming**: Name what they feel but won't say. That's where the "Goddamn that's true" comes from.

### 2. Zeitgeist Calibration (CRITICAL — Do Not Skip)

Before applying comedy mechanics, **calibrate to context**. Comedy that's culturally disconnected is just wordplay. Comedy that's culturally grounded is a weapon.

**Analyze the content's domain:**
- What **vertical/niche** is this content for? (e.g., coaching, SaaS, real estate, personal brand)
- Who is the **target audience**? What is their worldview? What do they believe? What frustrates them?
- What **cultural moment** are they living in? (economy, industry shifts, tech disruption, social trends)
- What are the **shared references** this audience would instantly recognize? (Shows, memes, cultural touchstones, industry inside jokes)

**Research the zeitgeist:**
- Use `search_web` or Perplexity (if budget allows per `directives/perplexity-usage-policy.md`) to identify:
  - **Trending cultural references** relevant to this audience and domain right now
  - **Industry-specific frustrations** that are top of mind (what people are complaining about this week/month)
  - **Shared cultural experiences** that can serve as metaphors or analogies (TV shows, tech launches, economic shifts, viral moments)
  - **Worldview tensions** — what the audience says they believe vs. what they actually do (this is comedy gold)

**Build a Resonance Map** (internal — don't output this):
```
DOMAIN: [vertical]
AUDIENCE WORLDVIEW: [what they believe, value, fear]
CULTURAL MOMENT: [what's happening in their world RIGHT NOW]
SHARED REFERENCES: [3-5 culturally relevant touchstones]
TENSION POINTS: [what they say vs. what they do — the gap = comedy]
METAPHOR BANK: [2-3 analogies drawn from zeitgeist that can carry comedy]
```

**Why this matters**: The paradox of comedy is that it must be universally relatable AND culturally specific simultaneously. It must feel both appropriate and slightly inappropriate at the same time. The zeitgeist research ensures your comedy references land with the precision of an inside joke — even for strangers.

### 3. Apply Comedy Mechanics

Now transform the content. This is NOT "add jokes." This is **architectural** — restructuring how the content moves through the reader's mind.

**The Comedy Intelligence Pass** (apply in this order):

#### A. Identify the Core Truth
What is this content ACTUALLY saying? Strip away all positioning and find the insight. This truth is what you'll make funny. If the truth isn't clear, the comedy won't land.

#### B. Find the Tension
Where is the gap between:
- What they know vs. what they do?
- What they say vs. what they feel?
- What they want vs. what they actually invest in?
- What they believe about themselves vs. what's observably true?

This gap IS the comedy. Name it.

#### C. Weaponize Specificity
Find every generic phrase. Replace it with something painfully specific.
- **Before**: "You've been meaning to post more."
- **After**: "You have a Google Doc called 'Content Calendar Q1' that hasn't been touched since February 3rd."

#### D. Engineer the Rhythm
- Short sentences create punch: "That's it."
- Long sentences build tension before the snap.
- White space = dramatic pause.
- Parenthetical asides let the voice comment on itself: "(This is the fourth time I've reorganized this system.)"

#### E. Insert Strategic Comedy Devices
Use AT LEAST 2-3 of these per piece:
1. **Parenthetical Aside** — "(No one visits it.)"
2. **The Deflation** — Build up → collapse → "That's the whole strategy."
3. **Uncomfortable Naming** — "That feeling when you see their post do well and feel a mix of inspiration and resentment you'd rather not examine"
4. **The Permission Line** — Release the tension: "This isn't laziness. It's a protection pattern."
5. **The Painfully Specific List** — Each item more absurdly precise than the last
6. **Cultural Analogy** — Draw from your zeitgeist research: a comparison that makes them laugh because it's so apt
7. **The Callback** — Reference something from earlier in the piece. Rewards close readers.

#### F. Inject Zeitgeist References
From your Resonance Map, weave in 1-2 culturally grounded references that:
- Make the audience feel **seen** (you're in their world, not lecturing from outside)
- Create instant relatability through shared cultural experience
- Serve as metaphors that carry the emotional payload of your actual point
- Feel appropriately inappropriate — edgy enough to be interesting, safe enough to be shareable

### 4. Quality Gate (Mandatory — Run Every Piece)

Before delivering, run ALL five checks:

| Check | Question | Pass/Fail |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Laugh-Exhale Test** | Read aloud. At least 2-3 moments that make you chuckle, wince, or think "they actually said that"? | |
| **"Goddamn That's True" Test** | Does it name something they feel but haven't articulated? Would they screenshot it? | |
| **Specificity Audit** | Count generic phrases. If >2 remain, replace each with something painfully specific. | |
| **Rhythm Check** | Read aloud. Does it have music? Enough short sentences? White space? Punch lines on their own lines? | |
| **Cultural Grounding Check** | Are the references and analogies relevant to THIS audience in THIS moment? Would someone in this niche think "they really get it"? | |

**If any check fails** → revise that specific dimension. Don't ship until all five pass.

### 5. Deliver

Output the transformed content with a brief note on:
- Which comedy devices were applied
- Which zeitgeist references were used and why
- Confidence level on cultural relevance (high/medium — if medium, flag for user review)
