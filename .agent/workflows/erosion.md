### Input
The user provides a draft — script, story, essay, content piece, or any written work that needs deepening.

### Step 1: The Full Read
Read the entire draft without making notes. Let it wash over you. Then write a one-paragraph "gut reaction" — what does this draft FEEL like? Where does it lose you? Where does it grab you?

### Step 2: The Erosion Pass (Not Revision)

Unlike revision (which fixes problems), erosion rediscovers the work by passing through it again and again. Each pass is a complete re-engagement:

**Pass 1 — The Muscle Memory Pass**
Re-type/rewrite the entire piece from memory and instinct. What stays is what matters. What you forget to include was never essential.

**Pass 2 — The Rhythm Pass**
Read every line aloud. Mark where the rhythm breaks, where sentences overstay their welcome, where the prose feels mechanical rather than musical.

**Pass 3 — The Subtext Pass**
Flag every line where a character (or the writer) says exactly what they mean. Apply displacement. Make the reader work for it.

**Pass 4 — The Courage Pass**
Find the moment the writer played it safe. Where did they pull the punch? Where did they write the expected thing instead of the true thing? Push those moments to the edge.

**Pass 5 — The Economy Pass**
Cut 15-20% of the word count. The draft should be leaner, denser, more potent after every pass. "If it doesn't serve the theme, it doesn't serve the story."

### Step 3: Deliver the Eroded Draft
Present the fully eroded version alongside a change log explaining what was removed and why, what was deepened and how, and what new material emerged organically through the re-engagement.

## PHASE 3: OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS

1. **Gut Reaction** — One paragraph on the draft's emotional impact
2. **Erosion Log** — What each of the 5 passes revealed and changed
3. **Eroded Draft** — The fully reworked piece
4. **Kill List** — What was cut and the specific reason for each cut
5. **Emergence Notes** — New material that appeared during the erosion process
