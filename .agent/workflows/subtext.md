### Input
The user provides either:
- A scene with "on-the-nose" dialogue that needs subtext transformation
- A scene concept with the emotional stakes described explicitly
- Content/copy that's too direct and needs layered meaning

### Step 1: Identify the Explicit Payload
What is the scene/dialogue actually trying to communicate? Strip it to the raw emotional transaction.

### Step 2: Apply the 4-Layer Displacement

**Layer 1 — Object Displacement**: Replace emotional statements with actions on objects. A character doesn't say "I'm scared" — they meticulously fold a napkin while discussing the weather.

**Layer 2 — Temporal Displacement**: Shift the conversation to a different time period. Characters talk about the past or future to avoid confronting the present.

**Layer 3 — Topic Displacement**: The conversation is "about" something else entirely. Two people argue about how to load a dishwasher when they're really arguing about control in the relationship.

**Layer 4 — Dream Logic Displacement**: The dialogue makes emotional sense but not literal sense. Roth's "I was running and I wasn't going anywhere" — it communicates a feeling, not a fact.

### Step 3: Rewrite the Scene
Transform the explicit scene into a subtext-driven version where:
- No character says what they actually mean
- The audience understands the real meaning through behavioral and contextual cues
- The emotional payload hits harder because it's earned, not stated

### Step 4: Annotate the Subtext
Provide a parallel annotation showing what each line "really" means beneath the surface dialogue.
