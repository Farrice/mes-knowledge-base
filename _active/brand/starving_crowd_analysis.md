### TOP 3 RECOMMENDED MARKETS

---

#### 🥇 #1: COACHES & CONSULTANTS ($5K-20K+ Offers) — Score: 72

**Why They're Starving**: Coaches consume more content than any other professional group. They've bought courses from every guru. They have notebooks full of frameworks, saved posts, bookmarked videos — *and none of it is organized into a usable system.* They KNOW they're sitting on gold but can't access it when it matters: during a sales call, while writing content, when coaching a client. The "course graveyard" is their defining pain point.

They've tried ChatGPT. It gave generic answers. They concluded "AI doesn't work for my business" — because nobody custom-built their prompts around *their* methodology, *their* voice, *their* niche. That gap is your entire value proposition.

**Why They Can Pay**: Coaches charging $5K-$20K per client are running real businesses doing $10K-$50K+/month. The AI coaching market is $6.69B in 2026 (22% growth). $2,500 is a single client fee for most — easy investment math. They're used to buying coaching, training, and done-for-you services. Budget friction is LOW.

**Who Holds the Budget**: The coach themselves. Solo decision-maker, no committees, no procurement. They say yes on a Zoom call and pay the same day. Speed of sale: **fast.**

**Where to Find Them**:
- **LinkedIn** — search "coach" + "AI" in posts (find the ones complaining about AI not working)
- **Twitter/X** — coaches building in public, sharing content creation struggles
- **Paid communities** — Skool, Circle, Heartbeat (join 2-3 where your avatar hangs out)
- **Warm network** — *you already know coaches.* Start there.

**Entry Strategy**: Post a "before/after" of someone's scattered knowledge vs. their organized AI Brain. Show the transformation visually. The "course graveyard → organized AI system" storyline is *extremely shareable* in coaching communities.

**Your Secret Weapon**: The Acusio build. You already have a complete case study of building a coach's entire system. That's not theoretical — it's proven.

---

#### 🥈 #2: COURSE CREATORS (Active Social Presence) — Score: 64

**Why They're Starving**: Course creators produce content at scale — YouTube, podcasts, social, email, course updates. They're drowning in their own IP. They've said the same thing 47 different ways across 200 videos and can't find the ONE time they explained the concept perfectly. They need a "second brain" that indexes their own body of work and makes it reusable.

They also face the "consistency problem": their writing voice drifts across platforms. LinkedIn sounds different from YouTube scripts which sounds different from emails. An AI Brain calibrated to their voice solves this permanently.

**Why They Can Pay**: Successful course creators do $20K-$500K+/month. They understand ROI math intimately — they track conversion on everything. If your system saves 5-8 hrs/week on content creation, that's $2K-$5K/month in equivalent productivity. $2,500 one-time is an obvious yes.

**Who Holds the Budget**: The creator themselves, or their COO/ops person (if they've scaled to $50K+/month). For most, it's the creator directly.

**Where to Find Them**:
- **Twitter/X** — course creators are *extremely* active here
- **YouTube** — find creators with 50K+ subs who post consistently
- **Teachable/Kajabi/Skool** communities — browse successful course pages, trace back to their social
- **Podcast circuit** — many are guests on business/marketing podcasts

**Entry Strategy**: Do an unsolicited "AI Brain audit" on a visible creator. Show what their scattered content would look like organized into a prompt system. Post it publicly (without being creepy). The creator sees it, their audience sees it — it's a public audition.

#### 🥉 #3: FUNDED SAAS FOUNDERS (Seed–Series A) — Score: 63

**Why They're Starving**: Post-funding founders are building everything simultaneously: product, marketing, sales, hiring. They're consuming massive amounts of information (competitor analysis, market research, user feedback) and need it organized into actionable intelligence. They've tried Notion, Obsidian, various "second brain" tools — and it's all still chaos.

They also need to brief team members, create SOPs, and maintain institutional knowledge. An AI Brain that captures and deploys their decision-making frameworks saves dozens of hours per week.

**Why They Can Pay**: They literally just got funded. $2,500 is less than one week of a junior hire. Budget exists specifically for "tools and systems that make the founding team more productive." The AI consulting market is projected at $14B in 2026 — they're actively seeking this help.

**Who Holds the Budget**: Founder directly. At Seed-A stage, there's no procurement process. Founder decides, founder pays. Speed of sale: **very fast** for the right founder.

**Where to Find Them**:
- **Crunchbase/AngelList** — filter by recent funding, stage, industry
- **Twitter/X** — founders announce raises publicly, creating visible trigger events
- **YC/Techstars alumni networks** — warm signal, high density of target
- **Product Hunt** — recently launched products = founders in build mode

**Entry Strategy**: Position as "the AI systems person founders bring in right after the raise." Content angle: "You just raised $2M. Here's why you need an AI Brain before you hire your 5th employee."

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Caveat**: SaaS founders are sharp buyers. They'll ask technical questions. Your MES 3.0 framework and 80+ skill library is a differentiator here — most "AI consultants" are just ChatGPT resellers. But expect a more analytical sales conversation than with coaches.

### 🚨 TRAP MARKETS (Avoid These)

---

#### ❌ New Solopreneurs (<6 months) — Starvation: 9, Payment: 2

**The Temptation**: They're EVERYWHERE. They're vocal about needing help. They engage with every AI post. They'll say "I need exactly this" with enthusiasm.

**The Trap**: They have no money. They're spending $29/month on Canva and $49/month on a course platform. $2,500 is their entire monthly revenue — or more. They'll ask about payment plans, then disappear after the first installment. The emotional labor of chasing payments will drain you faster than the work itself.

**Chris Do Would Say**: *"It's hard to squeeze blood from a stone. These folks are hungry, but they can't feed you. Love them from a distance — create free content for them. Sell to the people who can actually pay."*

---

#### ❌ Therapists & Wellness Practitioners — Starvation: 7, Payment: 4

**The Temptation**: They're knowledge-heavy, content-hungry, and would genuinely benefit from an AI Brain system. Your psychology background gives natural credibility. It *feels* like a perfect fit.

**The Trap**: Most solo therapists earn $60K-$100K/year. They're cautious buyers by nature (reflective, risk-averse). $2,500 feels like a major discretionary expense. They'll ask 15 questions, want references, think about it for 3 weeks, and then decide to "wait until next quarter." The sales cycle destroys your momentum.

**Chris Do Would Say**: *"Culture matters. Therapists live in a world where spending $2,500 on a business tool feels irresponsible. They'll love the idea and never buy it. Swim in a different pool."*

---

#### ❌ Content Creators (Influencers) — Starvation: 6, Payment: 5

**The Temptation**: Big audiences. Visible. Engaging. You think "if I get one influencer, their audience sees it."

**The Trap**: Influencers aren't business operators. They think in content, not systems. Most are supported by brand deals — they don't buy tools, brands buy FOR them. The ones who ARE business-savvy already have teams handling this. You end up pitching to people who say "that's cool" but never convert because they don't experience the *operational pain* your system solves.

**Chris Do Would Say**: *"Don't confuse visibility with viability. A million followers doesn't mean they'll buy. Sell to the person whose revenue depends on systematizing their knowledge — not the person who gets paid to be entertaining."*
