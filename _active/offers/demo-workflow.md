# Demo Workflow -- Step by Step

> Run 3-5 of these in your first week. Each demo creates:
> (1) a warm lead, (2) content for your LinkedIn, (3) proof the system works.
>
> **Skill reference**: `skills/ghostwriting-voice-engine/workflows/03-unsolicited-demo.md`
> **Before/After templates**: `_active/offers/before-after-template.md`

---

## Time: 45-60 minutes per demo (first 2-3 will take longer)

---

## Step 1: Find a Coach (5 min)

**Where to look:**
- LinkedIn search: `"coach" + [niche] + "helping"` -- filter by People, 2nd connections
- Your prospect tracking sheet (if you've been running the outreach system)
- "People Also Viewed" sidebar on good-fit profiles
- Coaching hashtags: #CoachingBusiness, #ExecutiveCoaching, #BusinessCoach

**What you're looking for:**
- Deep expertise, mediocre content (good ideas trapped in weak execution)
- Posts 1-3x per week with low engagement relative to follower count
- 500-10,000 followers (established enough to value content, not so big they have a team)
- Clear coaching niche and methodology visible in their About section

**Red flags -- skip these coaches:**
- Already working with a ghostwriter (polished, consistent, on-brand content)
- Very polished content that's clearly working (don't demo on someone who doesn't need you)
- Fewer than 5 posts total (not enough source material)
- Content is all promotional/sales posts (no thought leadership to elevate)
- No About section or bare-bones profile (not enough signal to build a voice profile)

---

## Step 2: Collect Their Content (10 min)

**Copy these into a single document:**

1. **About section** -- full text
2. **Last 5-10 posts** -- full text of each, note approximate engagement
3. **Featured section links** -- check for podcasts, YouTube, blog posts
4. **5-10 of their comments** on other people's posts (reveals natural voice)

**Save to**: `_active/demos/[coach-name]/source-posts.md`

**Pro tip**: Their comments are often more authentic than their posts. People don't edit comments. If their comments sound noticeably different from their posts, the comment voice is probably closer to the real person.

---

## Step 3: Build Abbreviated Voice Profile (15 min)

**Feed their content into the system and extract:**

- **Vocabulary patterns**: What words/phrases do they repeat? What's their technical vs. conversational balance?
- **Sentence rhythm**: Short and punchy? Long and flowing? Mixed? What's the dominant pattern?
- **Emotional range**: Where do they get intense? Where are they measured? (Limited from posts only -- note this.)
- **Structural patterns**: How do they open? How do they close? How do they transition?
- **Belief map**: What do they believe about their domain? What are they fighting against?

**Fill out the Voice Profile template** with confidence ratings. Mark anything you're guessing at as "needs call to confirm."

**Save to**: `_active/demos/[coach-name]/voice-profile-abbreviated.md`

**Remember**: You're working from polished output, not raw voice. Their real voice is probably less formal, more energetic, and more varied than their posts suggest. Lean slightly conversational.

---

## Step 4: Pick 2-3 Posts to Rewrite (5 min)

**Score each collected post on two axes:**
- **Idea quality** (1-5): How good is the underlying insight?
- **Execution quality** (1-5): How well is it written/structured?

**Pick the posts with the biggest gap** -- great ideas, weak execution.

**Rules:**
- Do NOT pick their best-performing post (don't fix what's working)
- Do NOT pick personal/vulnerable shares (feels invasive to rewrite)
- DO pick posts relevant to their core coaching offer
- DO pick posts where the lede is buried or the hook is weak

**For each post, write one sentence answering:**
- What's the core insight? (keep this)
- What's wrong with the execution? (fix this)
- What's the 10x version? (aim for this)

---

## Step 5: Rewrite (15-20 min)

**For each post:**

1. **Warm up** -- re-read their 3 best posts until you hear their voice in your head.
2. **Extract the core** -- reduce the original to one sentence: "The point is [X]."
3. **Rebuild** -- same message, dramatically better execution. Use their Voice Profile as the constraint.
4. **Refine** -- tighten sentence endings (power words last), compress (cut 20-30% without losing meaning), add ONE moment of rhetorical impact if it fits their voice.
5. **Verify** -- read it aloud. Does it sound like them on their best day?

**The rewrite MUST:**
- Keep the same core idea
- Sound like them, not like you
- Be obviously, dramatically better (not a subtle tweak)
- Follow LinkedIn formatting (hook in line 1, white space, scannable)
- Preserve their voice quirks (fragments, contractions, rhythm patterns)

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Making it sound too polished (their voice has texture -- keep it)
- Adding vocabulary they'd never use
- Changing their message (elevate the packaging, not the content)
- Over-formatting (match their formatting style, just cleaner)

---

## Step 6: Package (5 min)

**Format the demo as a before/after document:**

For each rewritten post:
- **BEFORE**: Their original post (full text)
- **WHAT I CHANGED**: 1-2 sentences on the structural changes (not voice criticism)
- **AFTER**: Your rewrite (full text)

**Add a closing note:**
"If you want to see what this looks like with your full voice captured -- not just from your public posts -- I do a $250 proof run. One 30-min call, 3 posts in 48 hours. DM me if you're curious."

**Save to**: `_active/demos/[coach-name]/demo-package.md`

**Also create your LinkedIn content** using the before/after template (`_active/offers/before-after-template.md`). Every demo should produce at least one post for your feed.

---

## Step 7: Deliver (2 min)

**The DM sequence:**

**Message 1** (permission request):
> "Hey [Name], I do something a little unusual -- I study how coaches communicate and occasionally I'll rewrite a couple of their posts to see if I can capture their voice. I did this with yours. Want me to send them over?"

**Wait for "yes."** Do NOT send the package in the first message.

**Message 2** (after they say yes):
> Send the demo package. Add: "Genuinely curious what you think. The goal was to keep your ideas and your voice but sharpen the execution."

**Message 3** (if they respond positively):
> Transition to proof run conversation. See outreach system scripts.

**If they don't respond to Message 1**: Follow up once in 3 days with a micro-value observation about their most recent post. Then let it go.

## Timing & Batching Tips

- **Best day to DM**: Tuesday-Thursday (highest LinkedIn activity)
- **Best time to DM**: 8-10am or 5-7pm in their timezone
- **Batch the collection**: Spend 30 min collecting source material for 3 coaches at once, then do the profiles and rewrites in sequence
- **Reuse patterns**: By demo 3, you'll have a rhythm. Your 5th demo will take half the time of your 1st.
- **Post your content the same day**: The before/after post goes on YOUR feed the day you finish the demo. Fresh energy = better writing.
