## 2.3 Scarcity Architecture

### The "Starting From Zero" Scarcity Playbook

You have no audience. No testimonials. No waitlist. Here's how to engineer genuine scarcity from nothing:

**Principle**: Scarcity doesn't require demand. Scarcity requires a *declared limit*. The limit is real (you can only serve 5 coaches/month at quality). Declare it from Day 1.

### Structure 1: The Proof Run Batch

- **5 Proof Run spots per month.** Not rolling. Batch.
- "March intake: 5 spots. Applications open March 3. Close when full."
- Each batch has an open date and a close date (or "closes when 5 spots fill, whichever comes first").
- When 1 fills, post: "4 spots left in the March batch."
- When 3 fill: "2 spots remaining."
- When all 5 fill: "March is full. April waitlist is open."
- Even if it takes 3 weeks to fill 5 spots, the structure creates the FEELING of movement and scarcity.

### Structure 2: The Intake Window

- **Applications only open during specific windows.** Not always available.
- "I open 5 Proof Run spots on the first Monday of each month. They typically fill within [X days]."
- "Typically" does heavy lifting. In Month 1, if they fill in 3 weeks, you can truthfully say "they fill within the month." By Month 3, if they fill in a week, you say that.
- This trains your audience to PAY ATTENTION to your posts. Miss the window, wait a month.

### Structure 3: The Waitlist From Day 1

- Create a simple Google Form or Notion page: "Join the waitlist for the next Proof Run batch."
- Link it in your LinkedIn bio.
- Every person who fills it out is demand you can reference: "12 coaches on the waitlist for April."
- The waitlist exists even if only 2 people are on it. You never lie about the number. But you reference the EXISTENCE of a waitlist, which implies demand.

### Structure 4: Sold-Out Announcement

The most powerful post you'll ever write:

```
March Proof Run: SOLD OUT.

5 coaches. 5 calls. 25 posts in production.

April waitlist is open — link in comments.

If you're a coach who's been sitting on "I should fix my LinkedIn" for 6 months, the waitlist costs nothing and saves you from missing another month.
```

This post works even if 3 of the 5 spots were friends or discounted. The SIGNAL is what matters. Demand is visible. Capacity is full. Others are waiting.

## 3.2 The 11/90 Visibility Architecture

**Priestley's rule**: Hit 11 touchpoints per prospect within 90 days to move from "who is this?" to "I need to work with this person."

### Touchpoint Map for LinkedIn

| Touchpoint | Type | How It Happens | Frequency |
|-----------|------|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Your post in their feed** | They see your content organically | 3x/week |
| 2 | **Your comment on their post** | You leave an insightful comment on THEIR content | 1-2x/week per target |
| 3 | **Your comment on a mutual connection's post** | They see your name + thinking in a shared space | 2-3x/week |
| 4 | **A DM from you** | Not a pitch. A genuine observation about their work | 1x per prospect |
| 5 | **A connection request with a note** | First point of contact | 1x per prospect |
| 6 | **A tag or mention** | You reference their methodology (with credit) in a post | 1x per prospect |
| 7 | **A result post featuring a client similar to them** | They see a coach in their niche get results | 2x/month |
| 8 | **A "sold out" or scarcity post** | They see demand exceeding supply | 1x/month |
| 9 | **A newsletter/email** | If they subscribed to your list | 1x/week |
| 10 | **A referral mention** | Someone they trust mentions you | Organic |
| 11 | **A DM follow-up** | Circle back after they've seen 6-8 touchpoints | 1x per prospect |

### The Math

- 3 posts/week = ~156 touchpoints across your feed presence in 90 days
- 5 comments/day x 5 days/week x 12 weeks = 300 comments (visible to target prospects repeatedly)
- 3 DMs/day x 5 days/week x 12 weeks = 180 direct conversations
- 12 weeks of posts + comments means any active LinkedIn user in your niche will see your name **minimum 11 times** within 90 days

The key: CONSISTENCY. Not virality. Not perfection. Showing up in the same spaces, with the same message, hitting the same frustrations, until they think "this person understands my problem better than I do."

# SECTION 5: ASSESSMENT ARCHITECTURE

## 5.1 The LinkedIn Voice Diagnostic

**Name**: "The LinkedIn Voice Gap Assessment"

**Purpose**: Surface problems coaches didn't know they had, amplify desire for the solution, and pre-qualify them as buyers — all in a 5-minute DM conversation or a self-serve form.

**Positioning**: This is NOT a quiz. It's a diagnostic. Like an X-ray before a prescription. Coaches respect diagnostics because that's what THEY do with clients.

---

## 5.2 The Question Set

### Method A: DM-Based Diagnostic (Farrice Walks Them Through It)

Use this in DM conversations. Ask 3-4 of these questions naturally, then deliver the diagnosis.

**Question 1: The Consistency Check**
> "How many LinkedIn posts did you publish in the last 30 days?"
>
> - 0-1: Ghost mode
> - 2-4: Dabbling
> - 5-8: Inconsistent
> - 9-12: Consistent
> - 13+: Machine mode

*Why it works*: Most coaches will say 0-4. Seeing themselves in "Ghost mode" or "Dabbling" creates immediate dissonance with their self-image as serious professionals.

**Question 2: The Voice Match Test**
> "If I read your last 3 LinkedIn posts out loud to you, would they sound like how you actually talk to clients?"
>
> - "Absolutely" — Rare. They're dialed in.
> - "Mostly" — Some disconnect.
> - "Not really" — Voice gap confirmed.
> - "I'd be embarrassed" — Deep voice gap. High-value prospect.

*Why it works*: Forces them to evaluate their content against their REAL voice, not against other coaches' content.

**Question 3: The Pipeline Audit**
> "Where do your clients currently come from? Rough percentages: referrals, LinkedIn, other social, speaking, paid ads, other?"
>
> *What you're looking for*: 80%+ referrals = pipeline risk they've normalized.

*Why it works*: When they say "90% referrals," you can reflect back: "So your entire business depends on other people's behavior. What happens if that changes?"

**Question 4: The Methodology Capture Test**
> "If I asked you to explain your core coaching methodology in 3 minutes right now — could you? Now: is that methodology written down anywhere someone else could read and understand?"
>
> *What you're looking for*: They can explain it. It's not written down. This is the gap.

*Why it works*: This IS the problem you solve. The gap between what they can SAY and what exists in WRITTEN form is exactly what you extract.

**Question 5: The Delegation History**
> "Have you ever tried hiring someone to create content for you? What happened?"
>
> - Never tried — hasn't solved it, hasn't even attempted
> - Tried, didn't work — burned, skeptical but still in pain
> - Currently have someone — satisfaction check (often low)

*Why it works*: If they've tried and failed, they know exactly how hard it is to capture their voice. Your process (actual voice extraction from a real call) is the differentiator.

**Question 6: The Impact Quantifier**
> "If LinkedIn was generating 3-5 inbound leads per month for you, what would that be worth to your business?"
>
> *What you're looking for*: Any number above $5,000/month means the $250 Proof Run is absurd ROI.

*Why it works*: They quantify the problem themselves. You don't have to sell the value — they just calculated it.

### Method B: Self-Serve Form (Google Form or Typeform)

**Title**: "The LinkedIn Voice Gap Assessment — For Coaches Making $100K+ Who Know They Should Be Posting"

**Subtitle**: "5 questions. 2 minutes. Find out where you're losing visibility — and revenue."

| # | Question | Format | Scoring |
|---|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | How many LinkedIn posts did you publish in the last 30 days? | Multiple choice: 0-1 / 2-4 / 5-8 / 9-12 / 13+ | 0-1 = 5pts, 2-4 = 4pts, 5-8 = 3pts, 9-12 = 1pt, 13+ = 0pts |
| 2 | If I read your last 3 posts out loud, would they sound like how you talk to clients? | Scale: Absolutely / Mostly / Not really / I'd be embarrassed | Absolutely = 0pts, Mostly = 2pts, Not really = 4pts, Embarrassed = 5pts |
| 3 | What percentage of your clients come from referrals? | Multiple choice: < 30% / 30-50% / 50-80% / 80%+ | <30% = 0pts, 30-50% = 1pt, 50-80% = 3pts, 80%+ = 5pts |
| 4 | Is your core methodology written down in a format someone else could use to create content for you? | Yes, fully documented / Partially / It's all in my head | Fully = 0pts, Partially = 3pts, All in head = 5pts |
| 5 | Have you ever hired someone to create LinkedIn content for you? | Never / Yes, it didn't work / Yes, it's working | Never = 3pts, Didn't work = 5pts, Working = 0pts |

**Scoring**:
- 0-5: Your LinkedIn game is solid. Keep going.
- 6-12: **Visibility Gap** — You're leaving opportunities on the table but you could fix this with effort.
- 13-19: **Voice Gap** — Your expertise isn't translating to LinkedIn. This is a systems problem, not a discipline problem.
- 20-25: **Critical Gap** — Your business is running on borrowed time. Your expertise is invisible, your pipeline is fragile, and every month without visibility costs you clients.
