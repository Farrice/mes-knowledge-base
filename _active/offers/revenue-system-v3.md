## 5.2 The Batch Model Mechanics

**Why batches instead of rolling availability**:

1. **Creates natural deadlines**. "March batch: 5 spots. Applications close when full." A deadline forces decision. Rolling availability creates perpetual "I'll think about it."

2. **Generates momentum signals**. "3 of 5 spots filled" is a sentence you can post that creates social proof through motion. "I'm available anytime" creates none.

3. **Enables the Sold-Out post**. The single most powerful post in the Priestley system: "[Month] Proof Run: SOLD OUT. 5 coaches. 25 posts in production. [Next month] waitlist is open." Even if Spot #5 took effort to fill, the signal is unambiguous.

4. **Matches real workflow**. Batch delivery lets you schedule calls in a cluster (e.g., Monday-Wednesday calls, Thursday-Friday writing). Rolling intake fragments your schedule.

**Batch cadence**:

| Phase | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Waitlist open** | Ongoing between batches | Coaches sign up for next batch. You reference waitlist size in posts. |
| **Intake opens** | First Monday of month | "5 spots open for [Month]. First access to waitlist." |
| **Intake fills** | 1-3 weeks (getting faster each month) | Countdown posts: "4 left" → "3 left" → "2 left" → "1 left" |
| **Sold Out** | When 5 fill or end of month | SOLD OUT announcement post. Next month waitlist opens. |
| **Delivery** | 2-3 weeks | Execute proof runs. Share build stories. Collect testimonials. |
| **Results Reveals** | End of batch | 20-min calls with each coach. Retainer conversions happen here. |
