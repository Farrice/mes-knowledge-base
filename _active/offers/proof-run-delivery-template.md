# Proof Run Delivery Template
## Standardized Process: Client Call → 5 Posts + Content Roadmap

> **Total time investment per client**: ~3-4 hours (60 min call + 2-3 hrs production/editing)
> **Delivery timeline**: 5 business days

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Call Prep (15 min)

Before the call, spend 15 minutes reviewing:

- [ ] Client's LinkedIn profile (current bio, any existing posts)
- [ ] Client's website/about page (if they have one)
- [ ] Any content they've shared (blog posts, podcast appearances, etc.)
- [ ] Their niche/industry (so you can ask informed questions)

**Write down 3 specific observations** to reference on the call:
1. Something about their methodology you noticed
2. Something their content is missing
3. A question about how they work with clients

---

## Phase 2: The Deep-Dive Call (60 min)

### Call Structure

**Minutes 1-5: Warm-up and framing**
- "This isn't an interview. Just talk to me the way you'd talk to a client. I'm going to ask questions and take notes. There's no wrong answer."
- "By the end of this, I'm going to know how you think and teach. That's what makes the content yours."

**Minutes 5-25: Methodology Extraction**

Ask these questions in order. Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take detailed notes.

1. "Walk me through what happens in your first session with a new client. What do you do?"
2. "What's the question you ask that always makes clients pause?"
3. "What does your industry get completely wrong? What makes you angry about how other coaches approach this?"
4. "Describe your process — the steps you take someone through. What are the stages?"
5. "What's the belief behind your approach? The thing you're convinced about that most people haven't figured out yet?"

**Minutes 25-40: Story Mining**

6. "Tell me about a client transformation that still gives you chills."
7. "What's the thing clients say about you that you'd never say about yourself?"
8. "Tell me a story where your methodology clicked for someone — the moment it landed."
9. "What's the problem your clients come to you with, in THEIR words? Not how you'd describe it — how THEY describe it."

**Minutes 40-55: Voice & Language Capture**

10. "If you were at a dinner party and someone asked what you do, what would you say? Not the LinkedIn version — the real version."
11. "What phrases do you use over and over with clients? The signature lines?"
12. "What would your best client say about you in one sentence to a friend?"
13. "Finish this sentence: 'The thing most people don't realize about [their problem] is...'"

**Minutes 55-60: Wrap-up**

- "I have everything I need. You're going to see 5 posts in your inbox within 5 days."
- "When you read them, the test is: do they sound like you? If anything feels off, we revise."
- Confirm email for delivery.

## Phase 3: Post Production (2-3 hours)

### Step 1: Review Notes (15 min)
- Identify the 5 strongest content angles from the call
- Match each to a post type:
  1. **Methodology post** — teaches a step from their framework
  2. **Story post** — client transformation narrative
  3. **Contrarian post** — the belief that makes them different
  4. **Recognition post** — makes their ideal client feel seen
  5. **Authority post** — demonstrates depth of expertise

### Step 2: Use the System to Draft (30-60 min)

For each post, use this production process:

**Input to the system:**
- Client's methodology notes
- Voice patterns and signature phrases
- Target post type (methodology, story, contrarian, recognition, authority)
- Specific angle from notes

**Quality check each draft against:**
- [ ] Does this sound like the client, not like generic coaching content?
- [ ] Does it reference specific methodology, not generic advice?
- [ ] Would the client recognize their own thinking in this?
- [ ] Is the hook strong enough to stop a scroll?
- [ ] Does it teach something or make someone feel understood?
- [ ] Would a reader know WHICH coach wrote this without seeing the name?

### Step 3: Humanize and Polish (60-90 min)

Read each post out loud. If any line sounds like "content" instead of a person talking, rewrite it.

Check for:
- [ ] No corporate language ("leverage," "optimize," "synergy")
- [ ] No AI tells ("delve," "tapestry," "testament," "In today's...")
- [ ] Short lines. Line breaks for rhythm. Mobile-first formatting.
- [ ] The opening line is 8 words or fewer
- [ ] The post has ONE clear idea, not three

## Phase 4: Content Roadmap Production (30-45 min)

### 30-Day Content Roadmap Template

Using the methodology notes from the call, map out 30 days of content:

```
WEEK 1
Mon: [Methodology post - Framework Step 1]
Wed: [Story post - Client transformation from Step 1]
Fri: [Contrarian post - Why most people get Step 1 wrong]

WEEK 2
Mon: [Methodology post - Framework Step 2]
Wed: [Recognition post - "You know that feeling when..."]
Fri: [Authority post - Data/insight about their niche]

WEEK 3
Mon: [Methodology post - Framework Step 3]
Wed: [Story post - Before/after from a real client]
Fri: [Contrarian post - Industry myth they debunk]

WEEK 4
Mon: [Deep methodology post - The full framework explained]
Wed: [Personal story - Why they do this work]
Fri: [CTA post - "If this resonated, here's how to work with me"]
```

**Include with each topic:**
- Suggested hook (first line)
- Key angle or story to reference
- Which framework/story from the call to draw from
