## PHASE 0: PRE-WARM PROTOCOL (Days 1-5)

**Why this exists**: Pre-warmed prospects accept connections at 40-45% vs 15-20% cold. Commenting on 10+ posts/day for 30 days leads to a 40% increase in profile views and 25% increase in your own engagement. You need both effects immediately.

### How to Find Target Coaches on LinkedIn

**Search Queries (exact syntax for LinkedIn search bar):**

1. `"executive coach" AND "leadership"` -- filter: People, Location: United States
2. `"business coach" AND ("helping" OR "serving")` -- filter: People, Connections: 2nd
3. `"coaching" AND "frameworks" AND "transformation"` -- filter: People
4. `"life coach" AND "LinkedIn"` -- catches coaches already thinking about the platform
5. `"coach" AND "speaker" AND ("author" OR "podcast")` -- multi-platform coaches most likely to value content

**Hashtag Monitoring (follow these + comment on posts):**
- #CoachingBusiness
- #ExecutiveCoaching
- #LifeCoach
- #BusinessCoach
- #CoachingTips

**LinkedIn Groups to Join (access to concentrated coach audiences):**
1. The Coaching Business Lounge (or similar active coaching groups)
2. LinkedIn for Coaches & Consultants
3. ICF Coaching Connection

**"People Also Viewed" Prospecting**: When you find one good-fit coach, LinkedIn's sidebar shows 8-10 similar profiles. Mine this on every prospect profile.

### Daily Commenting Plan (Days 1-5)

**Volume**: 15-20 substantive comments per day. This is your primary growth lever.

**Where to comment**:
- 5 comments on prospect posts (people you want as clients)
- 5 comments on influencer posts in the coaching space (exposure to their audience)
- 5 comments on posts from coaches already talking about LinkedIn/content/visibility

**Comment requirements (ENFORCED)**:
- Minimum 15 words (comments >15 words carry 2.5x more algorithmic weight)
- Must add a unique perspective, personal observation, or follow-up question
- Must demonstrate that you ACTUALLY READ the post (reference a specific point)
- Never: "Great post!", "Love this!", "So true!", emoji-only responses

### Comment Templates That Demonstrate Expertise Without Pitching

**Template 1 -- The Bridge Builder** (connects their insight to a broader pattern)
```
This is exactly the pattern I see with [related topic]. The piece most people
miss is [your insight about WHY this matters]. Curious -- have you noticed
[related question that extends the conversation]?
```

**Example on a coach's post about client retention:**
```
This retention pattern makes sense when you factor in the trust compound effect.
Most coaches front-load value in onboarding but taper off by month 3.
The ones who keep clients 12+ months structure their touchpoints differently --
less frequency, more depth. Have you experimented with quarterly deep dives
vs. weekly check-ins?
```

**Template 2 -- The Reframe** (shows you see what others miss)
```
Interesting angle. I'd push back slightly on [one point] -- in my experience,
[alternative perspective]. But the core insight about [agree with their main thesis]
is dead on. Especially the part about [specific detail from their post].
```

**Example on a post about "coaches need to niche down":**
```
Interesting take. I'd push back on the "niche = specialty" framing though --
the coaches I see winning aren't just picking a niche, they're picking a
*language*. Same expertise, different framing depending on who they're talking to.
The positioning piece you mentioned about first impressions? That's the real leverage point.
```

**Template 3 -- The Data Drop** (shows you bring substance)
```
[Their insight] tracks with what I've been seeing. [Relevant data point or observation].
The interesting question is [future-oriented question that shows strategic thinking].
```

**Example on a post about content being exhausting:**
```
This tracks. 75% of content creators who earn full-time income have experienced
burnout, and for coaches specifically, content creation eats up 25-40% of their
working time -- roughly equal to actual coaching hours. The interesting question is
whether the ROI justifies it or if there's a more efficient path to visibility.
```

**Template 4 -- The Case Study Nod** (shows practical knowledge)
```
Seen this play out in real time. [Brief anecdote or observation].
The key variable that separated [outcome A] from [outcome B] was
[specific factor]. Your point about [reference their post] is the part
most people underestimate.
```

**What NOT to do in comments:**
- Never mention your service
- Never say "DM me" or "I help coaches with this"
- Never link to anything
- Never make the comment about you -- make it about their insight
- Never copy-paste the same comment structure across multiple posts (LinkedIn detects patterns)

## PHASE 1: CONNECTION REQUESTS (Starting Day 4-5)

**Timing**: Start sending connection requests once you've commented on a prospect's content 2-3 times. They should recognize your name.

**Volume**: 15-20 requests/day (max 100/week for free accounts). LinkedIn will restrict your account if you exceed this.

**Length**: 40-80 words. No pitch. Pure recognition.

### Template A -- Content Reference (Highest acceptance rate)

```
Hey [Name], your post on [specific topic] stopped me mid-scroll --
especially [specific detail or phrase they used]. That's not generic
coaching advice, that's real methodology. Refreshing to see on LinkedIn.
Would love to connect.
```

**Personalization checklist**:
- [ ] Reference a specific post (by topic, not date)
- [ ] Quote or paraphrase a specific line
- [ ] Name what made it different from typical coaching content

### Template B -- Methodology Recognition

```
[Name], been following your take on [their framework or methodology].
The way you structure [specific element] -- most coaches handwave that part.
You clearly built it from real client work. Would love to connect and follow
more closely.
```

### Template C -- Mutual Audience (when you commented on the same posts)

```
[Name], keep seeing you in the comments on [influencer name]'s posts.
Your perspective on [topic] caught my attention -- especially [specific thing
they said in a comment]. Clearly thinking deeper than most on this.
Let's connect.
```

**What to reference in their profile/posts (in order of power)**:
1. A specific phrase or sentence from their most recent post
2. Their proprietary framework or methodology name
3. A contrarian take they made that others ignored
4. A comment they left on someone else's post
5. Their professional background or career arc (last resort -- less powerful)

## PHASE 2: VALUE-FIRST DM SEQUENCE

### Message 1 -- Warm Opener (2-4 hours after they accept)

**Never pitch in Message 1. The goal is to start a conversation.**

**Script 1A -- The Diagnostic Question** (highest response rate)

```
[Name], quick question --

Are you actively posting on LinkedIn these days,
or is it one of those "I know I should but haven't" situations?

No agenda -- genuinely curious. I've been studying how coaches
use LinkedIn and I'm noticing some patterns.
```

**Why it works**: Binary question lowers cognitive load. "No agenda" disarms. "Studying patterns" positions you as a peer researcher, not a salesperson. The question itself surfaces their pain.

**Script 1B -- The Genuine Compliment + Question**

```
[Name], wanted to say -- your [specific post topic] post was one of
the few on my feed that didn't read like it was written by ChatGPT.

Quick question: do you write all your own stuff?
Asking because I study coach content and yours has a distinctly different voice.
```

**Why it works**: The ChatGPT comparison is timely (54% of LinkedIn long-form posts are now AI-generated; audiences have developed an allergy to it). Calling out authenticity flatters without being sycophantic. The question opens a natural conversation about content creation.

**Script 1C -- The Micro-Value Drop**

```
Hey [Name], just read your post about [topic].

Quick thought -- your hook is burying the lead.
You started with context when the real punch is in paragraph 3:
"[quote their own powerful line]"

If you led with that, I think your engagement would jump.

Anyway, my ghostwriter brain never turns off. Keep posting.
```

**Why it works**: Demonstrates your skill immediately. No ask. Pure value. The "ghostwriter brain" phrase is a soft identity reveal without a pitch. If they respond positively, you've already proven you can improve their content.

# SECTION 2: LINKEDIN VOICE GAP ASSESSMENT (DM Version)

---

## Purpose

This assessment does three things simultaneously:
1. **Reveals problems the coach didn't know they had** (the Priestley "score and door" principle)
2. **Creates desire for the proof run as the logical next step** (assessment-to-call conversion: 40-60%)
3. **Gives genuine value even if they never buy** (builds trust and referral potential)

## How to Deliver

Send questions one at a time via DM. Don't dump all 6 at once -- it feels like a survey. Pace them conversationally.

**Opening frame:**

```
Alright, here we go. 6 questions, takes about 3 minutes.
I'll score your answers and share what it means on the other side.
Some of these might sting a little -- that's the point.
Ready?
```

## The 6 Questions

### Q1: Posting Frequency
```
How often are you currently posting on LinkedIn?

A) 4-5 times per week (consistent rhythm)
B) 1-3 times per week (when I get around to it)
C) A few times per month (sporadic)
D) Basically never (my last post has cobwebs)
```

**What it reveals**: Consistency gap. Coaches converting from LinkedIn post 3-5x/week. Anything below that = lost compound growth.

**Scoring**:
- A = 4 points
- B = 2 points
- C = 1 point
- D = 0 points

---

### Q2: Time Investment
```
When you DO write a post, how long does it take
from blank page to hitting publish?

A) Under 15 minutes
B) 15-30 minutes
C) 30-60 minutes
D) Over an hour (or I give up before finishing)
```

**What it reveals**: Efficiency gap. If they're spending 30-60 min per post, they're burning 2-5 hours/week on a task that should take 25 minutes of review time with a system in place.

**Scoring**:
- A = 4 points
- B = 3 points
- C = 1 point
- D = 0 points

### Q3: Voice Authenticity
```

A) Yes -- my content sounds like me talking
B) Mostly -- but it's a slightly polished version of me
C) Honestly? Probably not -- it sounds more "professional" than real
D) I don't even know what my content voice sounds like

**What it reveals**: Voice gap. This is THE core problem. 54% of LinkedIn content is AI-generated, and audiences have developed detection instincts. If their content doesn't sound like them, it's invisible.

**Scoring**:
- A = 4 points
- B = 2 points
- C = 1 point
- D = 0 points

### Who to AVOID (Red Flags)

- **Too early stage**: No website, no clear niche, under 100 connections, first year of coaching. They can't afford you yet and need to figure out their offer first.

- **Agency/team coaches**: If they have a marketing team or VA handling content, the decision-maker is harder to reach.
- **Not actually coaching**: LinkedIn "coaches" who are actually selling courses, MLM, or other non-coaching services.
- **Under 500 connections**: Too small a network for LinkedIn content to have meaningful reach.
- **Not on LinkedIn regularly**: If their last post was 6+ months ago AND they have no recent activity (comments, reactions), they may have abandoned the platform entirely.
- **Budget-constrained niches**: Fitness coaching, nutrition coaching, wellness coaching at the entry level (avg revenue: $48-68K). Target executive, business, leadership, and career coaches first (avg revenue: $78-165K).

# SECTION 5: THE "RESULTS REVEAL" CONVERSION SCRIPT

---

## Purpose

This is the structured moment at the END of a proof run that converts the $250 buyer into a $1,500/month retainer client. It happens during or immediately after you deliver the 5 posts.

**The key principle**: Don't ask "do you want more?" Ask "what would it look like if this never stopped?"

---

## Step 1: Present the 5 Posts (Delivery Message)

```
[Name], here are your 5 posts. Take a look.

I want your honest reaction before I share anything else.

Read them out loud if you can -- that's the real test
of whether the voice is right.

Take your time. I'll wait.
```

**Why "read them out loud"**: If they read the posts aloud and they feel natural, the voice capture is proven. This is a moment of genuine delight -- let it land before you say anything else.

---

## Step 2: Gather Their Reaction

Wait for their response. Don't rush. Let them process.

**If they love it:**
```
Really glad to hear that. That's exactly what I was going for.

Quick question before we go further:
which post surprised you the most?
As in -- "I wouldn't have thought to frame it that way,
but it's 100% how I think."
```

**Why this question**: It deepens the connection between the posts and their identity. "I wouldn't have thought to frame it that way, but it's 100% how I think" is the exact moment they realize they need this service ongoing.

**If they have concerns:**
```
Okay, tell me specifically what feels off.

Is it the tone? The framing? A specific phrase?

I'd rather nail this than move on with something
that doesn't feel like you.
```

Then fix it. The revision process IS the sales tool -- it shows you're responsive and care about accuracy.

---

## Step 3: The "Imagine This Every Week" Bridge

After they've confirmed the posts feel right:

```
Here's what I want you to imagine for a second.

These 5 posts? That's one week of content.

What if every week, you had posts like this
going out on your LinkedIn -- without you
spending an hour staring at a blank screen?

You'd have:
- 3-5 posts per week that sound like you
- Consistent visibility with your network
- Content that demonstrates your methodology
  (not generic "coaching tips")
- Time back to do what you actually do best -- coach

The coaches I've studied who post at this cadence
see meaningful traction within 90 days.
Not vanity metrics -- actual conversations with potential clients.

That's the gap between where you are now
and what we just proved is possible.
```

---

## Step 4: The Retainer Offer (Natural, Not Pushy)

```
Here's what working together monthly looks like:

**Growth Plan -- $1,500/month**
- 12 posts per month (3/week)
- One 30-minute voice extraction call per month
  (same process as our proof run)
- Content strategy aligned to your coaching niche
- Revision rounds until every post sounds right
- Month-to-month. Cancel anytime.

**Authority Plan -- $3,000/month**
- 16 posts per month (4/week)
- Two voice extraction calls per month
- Content repurposing (carousels, article drafts)
- Monthly analytics review + strategy adjustment
- Priority response time
- Month-to-month. Cancel anytime.

Which of these feels more aligned with where you want to be?
```

**Why "which feels more aligned" instead of "are you interested"**: Binary choice (A or B) converts better than yes/no. Both options are a yes. The question assumes the sale and lets them choose their level.

---

## Step 5: Handling "Let Me Think About It"

```
Totally fair. What specifically are you weighing?

Is it the investment, the timing, whether you're ready
to commit to consistent posting, or something else?
```

**Then listen. Don't fill the silence.** Their answer tells you exactly which objection to handle.

**If it's the money:**
```
I hear you. Here's how I think about the math:

You charge [$their rate] per hour for coaching.
Content that generates even 1 new client per month
pays for the service multiple times over.

At $1,500/month, you need ONE additional client
every 1-2 months to be net positive.

And that's before the compound effect of consistent posting --
coaches who post 3-5x/week see increasing returns over time
as the algorithm recognizes their authority.

But if the timing isn't right, I'd rather you start
when you're fully committed. The offer stands.
```

**If it's timing:**
```
When would timing be better?

The reason I ask: the compound effect of LinkedIn content
means every week you're not posting is a week
the algorithm isn't learning to amplify your voice.

Coaches who start now and stay consistent for 90 days
see a completely different pipeline by month 4.

But I don't want to pressure you into something
you're not ready for. What would need to be true
for the timing to feel right?
```

**If they go quiet after "let me think about it":**

Follow up once in 3 days:

```
[Name], circling back on the retainer conversation.

No rush -- but wanted to mention that I'm keeping
my client roster small right now (max 3-5 clients).

If you want to lock in the current rate and start
when you're ready, I can hold a spot for you
through [specific date, 1 week out].

If not, genuinely no hard feelings. The proof run posts
are yours either way.
```

Then let it go. If they don't respond, they're not ready. Move to the next prospect.

```
Hey [Name], one more thing --

Could you send me 2-3 sentences about the proof run?
Specifically:

## RULES OF ENGAGEMENT (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Personalize every message.** Reference their actual posts, frameworks, or content. Generic = delete.
2. **Never pitch in the connection request.** Connection = permission to talk. Not to sell.
3. **Lead with value or diagnosis, never the offer.** The offer comes after they've confirmed the problem.
4. **Follow up. Then follow up again.** 50-70% of responses come from follow-ups 2-5.
5. **Respect the breakup.** After Follow-Up 3, stop. Desperation kills deals.
6. **Track everything.** If you can't measure it, you can't improve it.
7. **Voice notes boost response rates 2x.** Use them on high-value prospects (mobile LinkedIn app, hold mic icon).
8. **Never send more than 20 connection requests/day.** LinkedIn will restrict your account.
9. **Post on your own LinkedIn daily.** When prospects check your profile (they will), they need to see activity.
10. **The proof run IS the sales tool.** Don't oversell. Let the work close the deal.
11. **Comments are 15x more valuable than likes** for driving the content-to-client funnel. Each comment on your post = 5% boost in reach within your network.
12. **Never send identical messages to multiple people.** LinkedIn's AI detects mass messaging patterns. Every DM must have at least one unique, personalized sentence.
