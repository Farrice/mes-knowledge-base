# Voice Capture Call -- 30-Minute Guide

> Pull this up during the call. Record the call. Let the conversation flow -- these are prompts, not a rigid script. Your job is to get them TALKING NATURALLY, not answering interview questions.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist

- [ ] Recording started (Zoom/Otter.ai)
- [ ] Intake form reviewed -- note anything interesting to reference
- [ ] Their best content pulled up (from intake link)
- [ ] Video/podcast link queued (if they provided one)
- [ ] Fresh doc open for live notes

---

## Opening (0-2 min)

**Say something like:**

"Hey [NAME], thanks for doing this. So here's how this works -- I'm going to ask you some questions that might feel unusual. I'm not trying to learn what you teach. I'm trying to capture how you actually think and talk when you're in your element. There are no wrong answers. The less polished, the better. Sound good?"

**What you're doing:** Setting the frame. They need to know this isn't a sales call or a normal intake. You want their REAL voice, not their "professional" voice.

**If they seem nervous:** "Honestly, the best thing you can do is forget I'm here and just talk the way you'd talk to a client you really like."

---

## Block 1: Story Capture (2-7 min)

**Primary question:**

> "Walk me through the last client transformation you're proudest of. What actually happened?"

**What you're capturing:** Narrative voice, storytelling rhythm, emotional range, how they build tension

**Listen for:**
- Specific details vs. generalizations
- Where emotion peaks (voice speeds up, gets louder, pauses)
- How they structure a story (chronological? lesson-first? scene-setting?)
- Vocabulary when they're excited vs. calm

**If they give you a short answer:**
- "Take me into the actual moment. What did they say to you?"
- "What was the turning point -- the thing that shifted?"
- "How did YOU feel when that happened?"

**Notes to self:** This is warm-up territory. They're still a little performative here. That's fine. You're listening for their natural storytelling instincts -- sentence length, pace changes, whether they use dialogue or summary.

---

## Block 2: Live Coaching (7-17 min) -- THE MOST IMPORTANT 10 MINUTES

**Primary question:**

> "I want you to coach me right now. Pretend I'm your ideal client sitting across from you. What's the first thing you'd say?"

**What you're capturing:** Teaching frameworks in natural language, their actual coaching voice, how they structure explanations, their real vocabulary

**Listen for:**
- Frameworks they use naturally (named or unnamed)
- Metaphors and analogies they reach for
- How they structure an explanation (principle first? example first?)
- The words they emphasize
- Where they get passionate or lean in

**Keep them going:**
- "Keep going -- what would you say next?"
- "What's the thing most people get wrong about that?"
- "If I pushed back and said 'but that won't work for me,' what would you say?"
- "What's the first thing you'd have me do?"

**Why this block matters more than anything else:** When people coach, they stop performing and start being themselves. The "professional voice" drops. This 10 minutes produces 70% of the usable voice material. Let it run long if it's flowing.

**Notes to self:** Pay attention to their sentence rhythm here. Short and punchy? Long and flowing? Do they use questions to teach? Do they repeat phrases for emphasis? This is where their real voice lives.

---

## Block 3: Contrarian Takes (17-22 min)

**Primary question:**

> "What does your industry get completely wrong?"

**What you're capturing:** Passion triggers, intensity markers, conviction language, unique positioning, belief system

**Listen for:**
- Voice speed and volume changes (this is where people get LOUD)
- Stronger language, absolute statements
- "Let me tell you..." or "Here's what kills me..." moments
- The specific thing they're pushing against

**If they stay surface-level:**
- "What advice do other coaches give that actually makes you angry?"
- "What's the popular opinion in your space that you think is dead wrong?"
- "If you could grab every coach in your niche and shake them, what would you say?"

**Notes to self:** This block reveals their belief map -- the assumptions that drive everything they do. It also surfaces their most intense, quotable language. Write down any phrases that feel like THEM.

---

## Block 4: Signature Insights (22-27 min)

**Primary question:**

> "What's the thing you tell clients that makes them go 'oh shit, I never thought of it that way'?"

**What you're capturing:** Their one-liners, hook-worthy insights, signature phrases, reframings

**Listen for:**
- Phrases that feel rehearsed (polished through repetition = signature material)
- Reframes -- how they flip a common assumption
- Analogies they've clearly used a hundred times
- The thing they say that gets quoted back to them

**If they struggle:**
- "What do clients quote back to you months later?"
- "What's the one sentence that usually creates the biggest shift?"
- "If you had to put your entire philosophy on a bumper sticker, what would it say?"

**Notes to self:** These are the headlines. The hooks. The phrases you'll use to open and close posts. If they say something that feels like a LinkedIn first line, WRITE IT DOWN VERBATIM.

---

## Block 5: Core Message (27-30 min)

**Primary question:**

> "If someone had 2 minutes with you in an elevator, and they were your perfect client -- what's the one thing you'd want them to walk away knowing?"

**What you're capturing:** Core positioning in their own words, the throughline that connects everything

**Listen for:**
- This often produces the headline or About section anchor
- The simplest version of what they do and why it matters
- Whether they lead with transformation or method

**If they give a pitch instead of a real answer:**
- "Forget the elevator pitch. What do you actually BELIEVE about your work?"
- "Why does this matter to you personally?"

**Closing:**

"This was incredible. I have everything I need to capture your voice. You're going to see posts that sound like you wrote them on your best day. I'll have them in your inbox within 48 hours."

---

## Post-Call Debrief (do this IMMEDIATELY -- within 5 minutes)

- [ ] Save/download recording
- [ ] Start transcription (Otter.ai or upload to processing)

**Write raw impressions (3-5 sentences):**
- What's their energy? (calm authority? intense passion? warm mentor?)
- What surprised you?
- What's the ONE WORD you'd use to describe their voice?
- Did they remind you of any writer or speaker?

**Capture verbatim:**
- [ ] Note 3-5 specific phrases that were uniquely them
- [ ] Note any frameworks or concepts they named
- [ ] Note the moment where they were most alive/passionate
- [ ] Flag the best 2-3 quotes for potential post hooks

**Red flags to note:**
- Topics they seemed uncomfortable with (avoid in content)
- Words or phrases that felt forced or borrowed
- Areas where they contradicted themselves (clarify later)
