# Client Intake Form -- Questions & Build Notes

> Use this to build the Google Form. Client-facing text is in **bold**. Notes for Farrice are in *italics* under each question.

---

- Q1 and Q5: Short answer (URL field)
- Q2: Short answer
- Q3: Long answer (paragraph)
- Q4: Long answer (paragraph)

**Title:** Voice Capture Intake

**Description:**

Thanks for signing up. Before our call, I need a few things from you. This takes about 10 minutes and helps me show up prepared to capture your voice accurately.

Be yourself. Don't overthink the answers -- rough and honest beats polished and corporate every time.

## Questions

### Q1: Best Content Link

**What the client sees:**

"Link to your best piece of content -- the one that sounds most like YOU. (LinkedIn post, blog, video, podcast, newsletter -- anything works.)"

*What it captures: Their self-perceived best voice sample. This is what they think represents them, which is useful data in itself -- sometimes their best content and their most "them" content are different things.*

*What to do with it: Read/watch before the call. Note vocabulary, sentence structure, emotional range. Compare to what emerges on the call.*

*Form setting: Short answer (URL)*

---

### Q2: Three Words

**What the client sees:**

"What 3 words do your clients most often use to describe working with you?"

*What it captures: External perception of their voice/energy. These words often reveal the emotional signature of their brand -- "direct," "warm," "no-BS," "inspiring" etc. This is how others experience them, which may differ from how they see themselves.*

*What to do with it: Use these as voice anchors. If clients say "direct and warm" but their writing is formal and distant, that's the gap to close. Reference these words during the call to build rapport ("Your clients describe you as X -- I can already see why").*

*Form setting: Short answer*

---

### Q3: Signature Frameworks

**What the client sees:**

"What are your top 3 frameworks, methods, or signature concepts? (Give each a name and one sentence explaining it. If you haven't named them yet, just describe them.)"

*What it captures: Their intellectual architecture. Named frameworks become content pillars. Unnamed-but-described methods reveal how they naturally organize their thinking -- which is often more authentic than the polished version.*

*What to do with it: These become the backbone of educational content. On the call, ask them to TEACH one of these live (Block 2). Compare their written description to how they explain it verbally -- the verbal version is usually better.*

*Form setting: Long answer (paragraph)*

---

### Q4: Ideal Client + Struggle

**What the client sees:**

"Who is your ideal client and what are they struggling with when they find you? (Be specific -- 'stressed-out founders scaling past $1M' is better than 'entrepreneurs.')"

*What it captures: Their ICP clarity and positioning language. How they describe their client's pain reveals their empathy vocabulary and how deeply they understand their market. Vague answers here = positioning work needed before content.*

*What to do with it: This shapes the "who" in every post. Their language about client struggles often becomes the most resonant hook material. Also reveals the transformation arc: struggle (before) to outcome (after).*

*Form setting: Long answer (paragraph)*

---

### Q5: Teaching Video/Podcast (Optional)

**What the client sees:**

"Link to any video or podcast where you're teaching or coaching naturally -- not a polished keynote, but you actually being you. (Optional but extremely helpful.)"

*What it captures: Their unfiltered speaking voice. Video/audio reveals things text never can -- pacing, emphasis patterns, humor timing, energy shifts. A 10-minute podcast clip is worth more than 20 written posts for voice extraction.*

*What to do with it: Watch/listen before the call. Note: sentence length when speaking naturally, words they emphasize, how they transition between ideas, their default energy level. This is the gold standard voice sample.*

*Form setting: Short answer (URL), marked as optional*

## Notes for Future Iteration

- If clients consistently give weak answers on Q3 (frameworks), consider adding an example: "e.g., 'The 3-Layer Method: I teach clients to separate strategy, execution, and mindset instead of treating them as one thing.'"
- If Q5 gets skipped by most clients, consider making Q1 accept video links more explicitly
- Track which intake answers prove most useful for voice extraction -- trim or expand accordingly
