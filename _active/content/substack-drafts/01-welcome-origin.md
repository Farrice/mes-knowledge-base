I'm going to tell you something that most people building a business would never admit publicly.

I have zero clients.

Zero testimonials. Zero case studies. Zero followers. Zero proof that any of this works.

And I'm writing this newsletter anyway -- not because I have answers yet, but because I think you deserve to see what it looks like when someone builds from actual zero. Not "zero" with a 10K email list and 8 years of content in the archive. Real zero.

Here's why I'm here, and why I think you should stick around.

---

**The short version of my story:**

I spent 18 years working in human psychology, coaching, and personal transformation. Most of that time at Equinox, studying one question: *why do people who know exactly what to do keep not doing it?*

Not people who lacked information. People who had the plan, the knowledge, the ability -- and still stopped.

The answer, I eventually learned, is never motivation. It's never discipline. It's translation.

The gap between knowing something and consistently doing something is a translation gap. The knowledge exists in one form inside your head. The action requires it in a different form. And most people can't make that conversion on their own.

I saw this with fitness clients for 17 years. And then I saw the exact same pattern somewhere else.

Coaches.

---

**The coaching content paradox:**

There are 232,000 coaches in the US alone. The industry generates $16 billion a year. And 71% of coaches say their number one struggle is getting clients.

Not because they're bad at coaching. Most of them are exceptional.

Because the best coaches in the world built their expertise in private -- in sessions, in messy transformational conversations, in real-time breakthroughs with real humans. They didn't build it in public.

So when a prospect goes to their LinkedIn page, they see a ghost. Maybe a bio that says "Helping leaders lead." Maybe three posts from eight months ago. Maybe a profile photo from 2019.

The prospect doesn't see incompetence. They don't see laziness.

They see silence. And their brain fills that silence with doubt.

*"If she's really that good, why isn't she talking about it?"*

Meanwhile, a coach with half the experience but a consistent LinkedIn presence is getting the calls. The referrals. The clients.

Not because she's better. Because she's visible.

---

**What I'm building:**

I'm building an AI-powered ghostwriting service specifically for coaches. Not a content agency. Not a template library. Not a ChatGPT wrapper.

A voice extraction practice.

Here's the difference: most ghostwriters interview you for an hour and try to "capture your voice." That's why most coaches fire their ghostwriter within two months. The content comes back technically correct and emotionally hollow. It could be about any coach in any niche.

Voice isn't the real problem. Thinking is.

What makes you different as a coach isn't *how you sound*. It's *how you think*. Your frameworks. Your methodology. The way you explain things that makes clients say "nobody's ever put it like that before."

I extract that. A 30-minute conversation, and I capture how you actually think and teach -- then translate it into LinkedIn content that sounds like you wrote it on your sharpest day.

That's the bet. And I'm going to prove it works by doing it in public.

Actual thinking about an actual problem.

I'm a stay-at-home dad to a toddler named JJ. My work window is 1-3 hours a day. I don't have the luxury of spending six months in stealth mode building the perfect launch.

I don't know yet. But I'm going to find out. And you'll see every step.
