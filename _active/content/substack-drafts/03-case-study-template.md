## The Call

We scheduled 30 minutes. I told her to talk the way she talks on a real coaching session. Not polished. Not prepared. Just the way she thinks when she's with a client and something clicks.

I didn't ask her to "describe her methodology." That's how you get Abstraction Drift -- the compressed, polished, generic version that lives on her website.

- "Tell me about a client who surprised you -- someone whose real issue was completely different from what they said in the intake."
- "What's the thing you find yourself saying to almost every client, regardless of their specific situation?"
- "What's a piece of advice your industry gives all the time that you privately think is wrong?"

## What Actually Happened Here

I want to break down the mechanics, because the magic isn't magic.

**The insight** [FILL IN: Coach's first name] dropped wasn't hidden. She says it on coaching calls regularly. She just couldn't see that it was remarkable, because she's been inside her own methodology for [FILL IN: number] years. The Curse of Knowledge had compressed it into something that felt obvious to her.

**The extraction question** that unlocked it was designed to bypass the polished pitch and access her teaching brain. When I asked about a client whose real problem was different from their stated problem, she stopped reciting her bio and started *thinking* -- the way she thinks in a session. That's where the content lives.

**The translation** from spoken insight to written post preserved three things:
1. **Her specific language** -- not cleaned up, not professionalized, not made to sound like "a coach." Made to sound like *her*.
2. **Her unique angle** -- the thing she sees that other coaches in her niche don't. The perspective that makes her clients say "nobody's ever put it like that."
3. **The emotional charge** -- the reason it matters. Not "5 Tips for Better Leadership." The feeling of recognizing yourself in someone else's story and thinking "I need to talk to this person."
