**Format**: Text post
**Goal**: Set the narrative. Create curiosity. Attract followers for the journey.

```

From a garage.

With zero followers.

then turns it into LinkedIn content.

I've ever produced...

Or a spectacular public failure.

Here's what I'm betting on:

Most ghostwriters interview you for an hour
and try to "capture your voice."

I capture your methodology — frameworks,
stories, language patterns, belief systems —
then turn it into posts.

The difference:

Ghostwriting = someone guessing what you'd say.
What I do = documenting what you actually teach.

I'm going to prove this works by doing it in public.

Every week I'll share:
→ What I tested
→ What worked (and what flopped)
→ Real numbers (engagement, DMs, revenue)
→ The raw mechanics behind every post

If you're a coach who's brilliant on calls
but invisible online — follow along.

Either you'll learn something useful.

Or you'll watch me fail spectacularly.

Either way, it's going to be interesting.
```

**Character count**: ~950
**Post time**: Tuesday 8-10am EST (highest engagement window)
**Engagement prompt**: The post itself is the hook — no explicit CTA needed. The transparency creates curiosity.

## POST 2 (Tuesday) — THE SKILL DEMONSTRATION

**Pillar**: Extraction Methodology
**Format**: Text post
**Goal**: Show don't tell. Demonstrate the extraction process in the post itself.

```
Most LinkedIn posts fail in the first 8 words.

Here's why.

LinkedIn's mobile app cuts off text at line 2.

If your hook doesn't force a "see more" click,
your post dies before anyone reads it.

I analyzed 200+ viral posts from coaches.

The pattern is surgical:

Line 1: 8 words or fewer. Broad hook.
Line 2: 8 words or fewer. Narrow qualifier.
Line 3: Hidden behind "see more." The niche payoff.

Example:

"I've been thinking about how coaches should
really focus on creating engaging content that
resonates with their ideal audience"

vs.

"Most coaches bury their best insights."

Same idea.

Different architecture.

The first dies at line 1.
The second forces a click.

This is the difference between capturing and guessing.

A ghostwriter writes what sounds good.
A content architect builds what gets read.

If your LinkedIn posts feel invisible,
it's probably not the ideas.

It's the first 8 words.
```

**Character count**: ~850
**Why this works**: You're teaching the 8-Word Rehook framework while using it. The post IS the proof of concept. Coaches reading this immediately see the skill gap — "I didn't even know about the 8-word rule."

## POST 3 (Wednesday) — THE METHODOLOGY DEMO

**Pillar**: Extraction Methodology
**Format**: Text post
**Goal**: Show the extraction process on a real coaching concept. Demonstrate how you transform generic into specific.

```
A leadership coach told me:

"Resilience is the ability to bounce back from adversity."

I said: That's not a LinkedIn post. That's a dictionary entry.

So I asked one question:

"Tell me about a client who THOUGHT they were resilient —
and then discovered they were just avoiding hard conversations."

Silence. Then:

"I had a CEO who 'bounced back' from a failed product launch
by pivoting to a new product. Everyone celebrated.
But he never addressed why his team stopped trusting him.
Two years later, same team, same trust problem, same failure."

Now THAT'S a LinkedIn post.

The rewrite:

"Resilience without accountability is just endurance.
You're not bouncing back. You're running in place."

That's the difference between a framework and a post.

Frameworks are abstract.
Posts are specific.

Most coaches have 10+ years of incredible frameworks.

They just can't extract the stories
that make those frameworks land on LinkedIn.

That's not a writing problem.
That's an extraction problem.
```

**Character count**: ~950
**Why this works**: This is a live demonstration of the extraction process. You're showing the before (generic) and after (specific) in real time. Coaches reading this will see their own content in the "before" and want the "after."

## POST 4 (Thursday) — THE ALGORITHM BREAKDOWN

**Pillar**: Algorithm Mechanics
**Format**: Text post (or document/carousel if you have Canva ready)
**Goal**: Position as someone who studies the mechanics, not just the creativity. Build trust through transparency.

```
I spent this week studying LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm.

3 things I didn't expect:

1. Document posts (PDF carousels) get 6.6% engagement.

That's 3x higher than text-only posts.

The reason: dwell time.

LinkedIn measures how long someone stays on your post.
Carousels force swipes. Swipes = time. Time = distribution.

2. External links get a 60% distribution penalty.

Read that again. 60%.

If you're linking to your website, newsletter, or calendar
in your posts — you're killing your own reach.

Put links in the comments. Or don't link at all.

3. AI-generated posts get 45% less engagement than original content.

LinkedIn's algorithm can now detect:
→ Perfect grammar with zero personality
→ No personal anecdotes
→ Generic structure patterns

If your posts could've been written for any coach in any niche,
the algorithm knows. And it buries them.

The lesson:

The game in 2026 isn't virality.

It's depth.

10 comments that turn into threads
beat 100 likes with zero discussion.

I'm testing all of this in real time.

Next week I'll share the actual numbers
from my first 5 posts.

Follow along if you want the raw data.
```

**Character count**: ~1,050
**Why this works**: You're positioning as a researcher, not just a writer. Coaches want someone who understands the platform mechanics, not just someone who writes pretty sentences. The specific data points (6.6%, 60%, 45%) create authority through precision.

### Rules for Every Post

1. **Post between 8-10am EST** (peak engagement)
2. **Zero external links** in the post body
3. **Respond to EVERY comment within 2 hours** (algorithm signal)
4. **Ask follow-up questions** in your replies to create threads (thread depth > likes)
5. **Wait 3-8 hours** before judging performance (algorithm needs assessment time)
6. **Single or double line breaks** only (F-Shape formatting for mobile readability)
7. **Bold key phrases** sparingly for skimmability

### Week 2+ Content Rotation

After Week 1, rotate between the 3 pillars:
- **Mon/Wed**: Extraction Methodology (skill demonstration)
- **Tue/Thu**: Algorithm Mechanics OR Proof in Public
- **Friday**: Weekly transparency report (always)

### Graduating to Document Posts

Once comfortable with text posts (Week 2-3), start creating PDF carousels:
- "The 8-Word Rehook Cheat Sheet" (7 slides)
- "Before/After: How I Rewrote a Coach's Top Post" (5 slides)
- "What I Learned Analyzing 50 Coaches' LinkedIn Content" (10 slides)

Document posts get 6.6% engagement vs ~2-3% for text. Start testing them by Week 3.
