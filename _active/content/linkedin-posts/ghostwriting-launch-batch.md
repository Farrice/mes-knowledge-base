## Mini-Brief 1: "The Invisible Expert Epidemic"
*Consolidated from: Brilliant On Calls, The Ghost of Your Expertise, Less Qualified*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches with 10+ years of transformational expertise watching less-qualified competitors book out from LinkedIn content. The rage is quiet but real — they know they're better and it doesn't matter.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "If my coaching is good enough, clients will find me through referrals and word of mouth."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Visibility beats expertise in every market, every time — not because the market is stupid, but because the market can't hire you if they don't know you exist. The fix isn't "learn marketing." It's learn TRANSLATION — what you do in a room → what you write on a screen.
- **Stakes**: Every month of invisibility is a month where less-qualified coaches capture the clients YOU should be serving. Your expertise has a shelf life if nobody sees it.
- **Narrative Hook**: Open with a specific scene — the moment a coach realizes the person she trained under her is now outearning her 3:1 from LinkedIn alone. Build the tension between deserving and having.
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Invisible Expert Diagnostic"** — A prompt kit that analyzes their LinkedIn profile + last 5 posts and scores them on methodology deployment, voice authenticity, and conversion architecture. Shows them the gap between their expertise and their online presence.
- **Expert Stack**: Kallaway (contrast violence between their coaching quality and online presence), Tommy Clark ("How I discovered this gap" narrative), Lara Acosta (SLAY — Story of the invisible expert, Lesson about translation, Actionable diagnostic, You as the bridge)

## Mini-Brief 2: "The 47 Google Docs Trap"
*From: Post 2 (standalone — unique concept)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches who invested in knowledge management (Notion, BASB, Google Docs) and still can't produce a single LinkedIn post. They did the hard work of organizing — and hit an unexpected wall.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "If I organize all my knowledge, content creation will be easy."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Organizing knowledge and deploying knowledge are completely different skills. You can have 500 notes in a perfect system and still freeze at "what should I say today?" The gap isn't organization — it's that your system can store information but it can't THINK like you.
- **Stakes**: Every framework sitting in your Notion is a month of content that's not working for you. You already did the hard part. The easy part — deployment — requires a different approach entirely.
- **Narrative Hook**: Start with the FEELING: "You open Notion. 47 databases. Color-coded tags. A system so beautiful you could frame it. And then you open LinkedIn and... nothing. Blank cursor. 45 minutes of staring. You close the tab and go back to what you're good at — coaching." Build from there.
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Deployment Gap Calculator"** — Prompt kit that inventories their existing frameworks/IP and shows them: "You're sitting on X weeks of content. Here's the first 5 posts hiding in your existing notes."
- **Expert Stack**: Kallaway (the dopamine of organization vs. the dopamine of deployment), Jasmin Alic (rhythm and recognition — "you know who you are")

## Mini-Brief 3: "The Referral Ceiling"
*From: Post 4 (standalone — unique concept)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches whose business is 90% referral-based. Feels stable until it doesn't. One quiet quarter and the panic sets in. They know it's a ceiling but don't know how to add a second acquisition channel.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "Referrals are the best business model. If my clients love me, the clients will keep coming."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Referrals are the best source of clients. They're also the most fragile. You can't scale word of mouth. You can't predict it. And every month you spend hoping the phone rings is a month where one bad quarter could change everything. LinkedIn isn't a replacement for referrals — it's what fills the gap when referrals slow down, and what makes every referral 10x easier.
- **Stakes**: You're one quiet quarter from a financial crisis. Not because your coaching is bad — because your pipeline has a single point of failure.
- **Narrative Hook**: "I got a DM last month from a coach who makes $250K/year. She said: 'I've never worried about clients before. But this quarter, three referral sources went quiet at the same time.' She hadn't posted on LinkedIn in 8 months."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Pipeline Fragility Test"** — 5-question diagnostic that calculates how dependent they are on a single acquisition channel and what their revenue risk looks like if referrals drop 30%.
- **Expert Stack**: Tommy Clark (the "How I" story of a coach who hit the ceiling), Caleb Ralston (trust-first framing — not "you need marketing" but "you deserve stability")

## Mini-Brief 4: "Your Clients Already Wrote Your Content"
*From: Post 6 (unique angle)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches sitting on gold — client testimonials, transformation stories, exact language prospects use — and not converting any of it into content. They think they need to CREATE content when they need to CAPTURE it.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "I need to come up with content ideas. I need to be creative and original."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Your best LinkedIn content already exists. It's in your client testimonials, your session notes, the exact words clients use when they describe what you did for them. "She asked one question and everything changed" is a better hook than anything a ghostwriter would invent. You don't need a content strategy. You need a translation layer between your client work and your LinkedIn.
- **Stakes**: Every coaching session you run without capturing the moments that matter is content lost forever. The reframes, the breakthroughs, the quotes — they're your highest-converting marketing material and you're letting them evaporate.
- **Narrative Hook**: Open with 3 real things clients say about coaches — visceral, specific praise — then reveal: "These aren't LinkedIn posts. They're client feedback forms. But they should be posts. Because this is what your next client needs to hear."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Client Language Miner"** — Prompt kit that takes their last 5 testimonials/client messages and extracts: hooks, content angles, positioning language, and 10 ready-to-post openings.
- **Expert Stack**: Lara Acosta (SLAY — the client quote IS the story), Jasmin Alic (the cadence of recognition), Josh Sanders (format as carousel: "3 things your clients say that are actually LinkedIn gold")

## Mini-Brief 5: "The 30-Minute Lie (Why Ghostwriting Fails)"
*Consolidated from: The 30-Minute Lie + What Nobody Tells You About Ghostwriting*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches who've tried ghostwriters — spent $500-$2,000/month — and got content that made them cringe. They spent more time editing drafts than writing from scratch. They've given up on outsourcing their content.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "A good ghostwriter captures your voice in a 30-minute interview."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Nobody can capture how you think in 30 minutes. Most ghostwriters interview you once, then spend months guessing. The result: posts that are technically fine and emotionally dead. The fix isn't a better ghostwriter — it's a fundamentally different process. One where someone learns how you THINK and TEACH, not just what you say.
- **Stakes**: Every month with a bad ghostwriter isn't just wasted money — it's diluting your brand. Your audience starts associating your name with generic content. That's worse than not posting at all.
- **Narrative Hook**: "She hired a ghostwriter for $1,800/month. Got the first batch of posts. Read the first one. Closed her laptop. Walked away for an hour. Then sat down and wrote one more check to fire him. That was the third ghostwriter in two years."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Voice Match Scorecard"** — A framework for evaluating any ghostwriter (or their own AI-generated content): 10-point checklist that tests whether content passes the "could you tell which coach wrote this without seeing the name?" test.
- **Expert Stack**: Kallaway (the pain-solution-trust triangle — pain of bad ghostwriting, solution of deep extraction, trust through the scorecard), Tommy Clark (the "How I" of discovering what makes the difference)

## Mini-Brief 6: "Imposter Syndrome Is a Deployment Problem"
*From: Post 7 (unique angle)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches who don't post because they think Brene Brown already said it. Comparison paralysis against published authors and TED speakers.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "Someone more qualified has already said everything I could say."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Your VERSION is the value. Brene Brown doesn't coach your clients. Simon Sinek doesn't know their names. The metaphor you invented on a Tuesday coaching call that your client still quotes 2 years later? That's content no bestselling author will ever produce. Imposter syndrome isn't about confidence — it's about confusing originality with uniqueness.
- **Stakes**: Every week you don't post because "someone already said it" is a week your unique version of that idea doesn't reach the one person who needed to hear it YOUR way.
- **Narrative Hook**: "She told me: 'I was about to post about resilience. Then I saw Adam Grant posted about resilience. So I deleted mine.' I asked her what she was going to say. She described a framework that made Adam Grant's version look like a Wikipedia entry."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Unique Version Finder"** — Prompt that takes a generic topic they want to write about and extracts: their specific angle, their client stories about it, their contrarian belief, and 3 hooks that could only come from THEM.
- **Expert Stack**: Caleb Ralston (relatable delivery — this is about validation, not teaching), Lara Acosta (SLAY — Story of the deleted post, Lesson about unique versions)

## Mini-Brief 7: "The Framework Content Machine"
*From: Post 11 (unique, actionable)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches who think they need "content ideas" every day. The mental load of invention vs. the simplicity of extraction.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "To post consistently, I need constant inspiration and new ideas."
- **Contrarian Truth**: One coaching framework = 8+ LinkedIn posts minimum. You don't need ideas. You need a system for decomposing what you already know. If you have 5 frameworks, you have 40 posts. If you have 10, you have 80. The coaches who post 4x a week aren't more creative — they have a decomposition system.
- **Stakes**: You're spending 45 minutes brainstorming ideas when your methodology is a content machine you haven't turned on yet.
- **Narrative Hook**: "I asked a coach: 'How many frameworks do you use with clients?' She said 7. I said: 'You have 56 LinkedIn posts sitting in your head right now. Let me show you.'"
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Framework Decomposition Engine"** — Prompt kit that takes ONE coaching framework and produces: 8 post concepts (problem, framework, client story, contrarian belief, step-by-step, FAQ, myth-buster, implementation guide).
- **Expert Stack**: Josh Sanders (format arbitrage — some of these are carousels, some are text), Kallaway (validation before creation — which of the 8 will perform best?)

## Mini-Brief 8: "Content That Pre-Sells (vs. Content That Gets Likes)"
*From: Post 12 (unique positioning concept)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches posting "value content" that gets polite engagement but zero DMs. Lots of likes, no leads.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "If I just provide enough free value, clients will eventually reach out."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Value content creates students. Pre-selling content creates clients. The difference: value content teaches what to do. Pre-selling content teaches how YOU think about the problem. After 5 pre-selling posts, your prospect already understands your methodology, trusts your thinking, and knows what working with you looks like. The sales call becomes: "I've been reading your stuff. How do we start?"
- **Stakes**: You're building a loyal audience of people who will never buy. They get enough from your free content that they don't need to hire you.
- **Narrative Hook**: "I studied 50 coaches' LinkedIn accounts. The ones making $30K+/month from content had something in common: their posts weren't 'educational.' They were demonstrations of how they think. The reader doesn't just learn something — they see the coaching brain at work."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Content-to-Client Audit"** — Prompt that analyzes their last 10 posts and categorizes each as "student content" (teaches what to do) or "client content" (demonstrates how you think). Shows the ratio and prescribes the fix.
- **Expert Stack**: Tommy Clark ("How I" — experience of discovering the difference), Kallaway (buyer vs. browser distinction)

## Mini-Brief 9: "Recognition > Teaching"
*Consolidated from: Stop Writing LinkedIn Posts + What Coaches Get Wrong About Content*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches whose content gets polite "great advice!" comments but no "I feel like you're inside my head" DMs.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "Good content teaches the audience something they didn't know."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Teaching creates students. RECOGNITION creates clients. The post that converts isn't "5 tips for better leadership." It's: "You know that moment in a meeting where you KNOW the answer but you don't say it because the last time you spoke up you got shot down?" That's not teaching. That's making someone feel SEEN. And being seen is what makes someone think: "This person gets me. I need to work with them."
- **Stakes**: Every "educational" post you write is training your audience to learn from you for free. Every recognition post you write is creating the feeling of "I need to talk to this person."
- **Narrative Hook**: "The best-performing post I've ever seen from a coach wasn't about coaching at all. It was 4 sentences about the specific feeling of eating lunch alone at your desk after a meeting where your idea got shot down. 2,400 likes. 87 DMs. 6 new clients in 2 weeks."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Recognition Post Generator"** — Prompt that takes their client avatar and produces 5 "recognition moments" — hyper-specific emotional scenarios their ideal client experiences — each one a ready-to-post opening.
- **Expert Stack**: Jasmin Alic (emotional triggers, rhythmic cadence), Erica Mallet (naming the invisible enemy), Kallaway (validation on the dopamine ladder)

## Mini-Brief 11: "The Consistency Trap"
*From: Post 13 (contrarian take)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches posting 3-4x per week with mediocre content because they were told "consistency is king." Getting 11 likes per post from engagement pods.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "Consistency is the most important thing on LinkedIn. Just keep posting."
- **Contrarian Truth**: Consistently posting mediocre content builds a graveyard, not a brand. 5 incredible posts that teach your methodology beat 20 generic posts that "add value." The actual formula is quality content posted consistently — but if you have to choose, choose fewer posts that represent the level of expertise you bring to your calls.
- **Stakes**: You're training the algorithm AND your audience to ignore you. Every mediocre post is a data point that tells LinkedIn "this account doesn't get engagement." You're digging your own algorithmic grave.
- **Narrative Hook**: "He posted every day for 6 months. 180 posts. Average engagement: 14 likes. He was exhausted. I asked him: 'Do any of these posts teach your actual framework?' He said: 'No, I save that for clients.' That's the problem."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Content Quality Audit"** — Prompt that scores their last 10 posts on: methodology deployment (1-10), voice authenticity (1-10), and conversion architecture (1-10). Shows them where the quality gap is.
- **Expert Stack**: Caleb Ralston (trust filter — consistency without quality erodes trust), Kallaway (systems over tactics — a quality system > a quantity habit)

## Mini-Brief 12: "The Voice Test"
*From: Post 20 (unique quality standard)*

- **Shadow Market Pain**: Coaches who can't tell if their content is actually good or just "fine." No clear standard for quality.
- **Flawed Common Belief**: "Good content gets lots of likes and engagement."
- **Contrarian Truth**: The real test for quality content isn't engagement — it's recognizability. Can someone read your post and know which coach wrote it WITHOUT seeing the name? If not, your content is interchangeable. Recognizable content builds a brand. Interchangeable content builds nothing.
- **Stakes**: If your content could have been written by any coach in your niche, it's doing literally nothing for your business. It's noise, not signal.
- **Narrative Hook**: "I have a test I run on every piece of content before I publish it. It's one question: Could someone read this and know it's from [Coach Name] without seeing the name? If the answer is no, I rewrite it. Because content that could be from anyone does nothing for anyone."
- **Recommended Asset**: **"The Recognizability Test"** — Prompt that takes 3 of their posts and evaluates: what % of this content is niche-generic vs. coach-specific. Identifies which lines could ONLY come from them and which are interchangeable.
- **Expert Stack**: Caleb Ralston (relatable > remarkable), Erica Mallet (naming what makes you different), Tommy Clark ("How I" built this quality standard)

Fill in: Why you do this work. The moment that made you angry about the expertise-visibility gap.
Tone: Emotional, honest, mission-driven. This is the post that creates TRUE fans.
