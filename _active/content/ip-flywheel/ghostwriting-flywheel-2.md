# IP FLYWHEEL — Package 2: "The 47 Google Docs Trap"

## THE CONTRARIAN TRUTH
**Common belief**: "If I organize all my knowledge, content creation will be easy."
**Our truth**: Organizing knowledge and deploying knowledge are two completely different skills. You built the most beautiful Notion system in the world. And you still freeze at "what should I say today?" The gap was never organization. It's that your system can store information — but it can't think like you.

---

## ASSET 1: LINKEDIN NARRATIVE ESSAY

### Copy-paste below:

---

You have 47 Google Docs.

Don't pretend you don't know the exact number.

You've got the Notion workspace.
Color-coded databases.
A "Content Ideas" folder you haven't opened since October.
The Tiago Forte book sitting on your shelf
with dog-eared pages and highlighted passages.

You did the work.
You organized your frameworks.
You categorized your IP.
You built a knowledge system
that would make a librarian weep.

And this morning you opened LinkedIn,
stared at the blank post editor,
and closed the tab after 12 minutes.

Again.

—

I know this because I've sat across
from 6 coaches in the last month
who described the exact same pattern.

One of them said something that stuck:

"I have everything I need to post every day
for the next year. I know this intellectually.
But I open the doc and my brain goes blank.
It's like the knowledge is there
but there's a wall between knowing it
and saying it publicly."

That wall has a name.

It's called the Deployment Gap.

—

Here's what nobody tells you
about knowledge management:

Organizing is a STORAGE skill.
It answers: "Where did I put this?"

Deploying is a TRANSLATION skill.
It answers: "How do I turn this
into something a stranger cares about
in 8 seconds on their phone?"

These are completely different cognitive operations.

You can be world-class at one
and terrible at the other.

And every knowledge management book,
every BASB course,
every Notion template —
they teach storage.

They don't teach translation.

So you end up with a warehouse full of IP
and no delivery trucks.

—

A coach I work with had 12 frameworks.

I asked her to describe framework #4
the way she'd explain it to a client
at minute 30 of a session
when all the small talk is over.

She talked for 6 minutes.

Inside those 6 minutes were:
→ 3 LinkedIn posts
→ 1 carousel concept
→ 2 email newsletters
→ A positioning statement she'd been
  trying to write for 6 months

She already HAD the content.
She just couldn't see it
through the lens of "post."

She could see it through the lens of "session."

So that's where we started.
Not "write a post about framework #4."
But "tell me how you'd explain this to someone
who just sat down across from you."

Different prompt.
Completely different output.

—

If you recognize yourself in this —
the organized expert who still can't post —
I built something for you.

It's a Deployment Gap Calculator.

You feed it your existing frameworks
(even just the names and a one-line description)
and it shows you:

→ How many weeks of content you're sitting on
→ The first 5 posts hiding inside your existing IP
→ Which framework is your highest-converting
  content source (and why)

It takes 10 minutes.
No email gate. No course to buy.
Just a diagnostic you can run right now.

Drop "DEPLOY" below and I'll send it over.

Your frameworks have been sitting there long enough.

## ASSET 2: THE PROMPT KIT — "The Deployment Gap Calculator"

> **Instructions**: Host on Notion/Google Doc. Link in first comment. DM to anyone who drops "DEPLOY."

### Copy everything below into Claude or ChatGPT:

```xml
<role>
You are a Content Deployment Strategist who specializes in turning organized expertise into published content. You understand the specific gap between knowledge management and content production. You are not a content coach — you are a translator between "what coaches know" and "what audiences read."
</role>

<mission>
Calculate this coaching professional's Deployment Gap — the distance between their organized IP and their published content output. Then show them exactly what content is hiding inside their existing frameworks.
</mission>

<phase-1-ip-inventory>
Ask the user:

1. "List your core coaching frameworks, methodologies, or processes by name. Just the names and a one-line description of each. (Example: 'The Resilience Reset — 4-step process for leaders recovering from failure')"

Wait for their answer.

2. "How many of these frameworks have you EVER turned into a LinkedIn post, article, or piece of public content?"

3. "When you explain these frameworks to clients, what format do you use? (Stories? Step-by-step? Diagnostic questions? Metaphors?)"
</phase-1-ip-inventory>

<phase-2-deployment-calculation>
Based on their answers, calculate:

TOTAL FRAMEWORKS: [X]
CONTENT POTENTIAL PER FRAMEWORK:
- 1 Problem Post (Why most people get this wrong)
- 1 Framework Post (The step-by-step process)
- 1 Client Story Post (Before/after transformation)
- 1 Contrarian Post (The belief that drives this framework)
- 1 Deep Dive Post (The specific moment where the breakthrough happens)
- 1 Myth-Buster Post (What your industry gets wrong about this topic)
- 1 FAQ Post (The question clients always ask about this)
- 1 Implementation Post (One thing they can do TODAY)

TOTAL CONTENT SITTING IN THEIR HEAD: [Frameworks x 8] posts
WEEKS OF DAILY CONTENT: [Total / 5]
MONTHS OF 3X/WEEK CONTENT: [Total / 12]

Present this as: "You're sitting on [X] LinkedIn posts. You have [Y] months of content already in your head. Here's the breakdown..."
</phase-2-deployment-calculation>

<phase-3-first-five-posts>
Select the framework that sounds most differentiated and produce 5 specific post concepts:

For each post concept, provide:
- A hook (first line, 8 words or fewer)
- The angle (what makes this post specific to THEM)
- The story or example to include
- The closing (recognition-based, not preachy)

Format as:

POST 1: [The Problem Post]
Hook: "[8 words]"
Angle: [Their specific take]
Story: [Pull from their framework description]
Close: [Recognition — "If you've ever felt X, this is why"]

Repeat for posts 2-5.

End with: "These 5 posts are the tip of the iceberg. You have [X minus 5] more sitting in your frameworks. The gap between your Notion and your LinkedIn isn't creativity — it's deployment. And deployment is a skill, not a talent."
</phase-3-first-five-posts>
```
