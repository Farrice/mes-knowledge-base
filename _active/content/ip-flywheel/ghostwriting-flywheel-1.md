# IP FLYWHEEL — Package 1: "The Invisible Expert Epidemic"

## THE CONTRARIAN TRUTH
**Common belief**: "If my coaching is good enough, clients will find me."
**Our truth**: Your clients can't hire you if they don't know you exist. Visibility beats expertise in every market — not because the market is dumb, but because the market can't evaluate what it can't see. The fix isn't "learn marketing." It's learn TRANSLATION.

---

## ASSET 1: LINKEDIN NARRATIVE ESSAY
*Expert Stack: Kallaway (contrast violence) + Tommy Clark ("How I" narrative) + Lara Acosta (SLAY + F-shape formatting)*

### Copy-paste below:

---

I watched a coach lose a client
she should have won.

The prospect told her:
"I went with someone else.
They just... I felt like I already knew her
before we even got on the phone."

The other coach had 6 years of experience.
This coach had 18.

The other coach had a signature framework
she'd learned at a weekend seminar.
This coach had a methodology she'd built
from 4,000+ hours of real client work.

The other coach charged $5,000.
This coach would have charged $8,000
and delivered 3x the transformation.

But the other coach posted on LinkedIn.
3 times a week.
Every post taught a piece of her methodology.
Every post made you feel something.

My coach? 3 posts in 8 months.
A bio that said "Helping leaders lead."
A profile photo from 2019.

The prospect never saw her brilliance.
She only saw silence.

—

I call this the Invisible Expert Epidemic.

And it's not rare. It's the default.

The coaches who built their expertise in sessions —
in messy, real, transformational conversations —
didn't build it in public.

Their proof of work lives in client results,
not in content.

So when a prospect goes to their LinkedIn,
they see a ghost.

Not incompetence. Not laziness.
Just... absence.

And the prospect's brain does what all brains do:
it fills the silence with doubt.

"If she's really that good,
why isn't she talking about it?"

—

Here's what I've learned sitting with coaches
and listening to how they actually think:

**The gap is NEVER talent.**

It's translation.

What you do in a room with a client
doesn't automatically translate
to what you write on a screen.

Those are two different skills.

And the second one — translation —
is learnable.

But most coaches skip it.
Or they try it once, hate it, and quit.
Or they hire a ghostwriter who interviews them
for 30 minutes and writes posts
that could be about any coach in any niche.

The result is the same:
**brilliant in private, invisible in public.**

—

I built something for this.

It's a diagnostic — takes about 8 minutes.

It looks at the gap between your expertise
(what you actually deliver to clients)
and your online presence
(what a prospect sees before they ever talk to you).

It scores you on 3 things:
→ Methodology deployment (are your frameworks visible?)
→ Voice authenticity (does your content sound like YOU?)
→ Conversion architecture (does your content lead somewhere?)

Most coaches who run it
score above 8/10 on expertise
and below 3/10 on presence.

That gap is where the clients you should be getting
are going to someone else.

Drop "INVISIBLE" below and I'll send it to you.

No pitch. Just the diagnostic.

What you do with the results is up to you.

## ASSET 2: THE PROMPT KIT — "The Invisible Expert Diagnostic"

> **Instructions**: Host this on a Notion public page or Google Doc. Link in the FIRST COMMENT of the LinkedIn post (never in the body — 60% distribution penalty). Send via DM to anyone who drops "INVISIBLE."

### Copy everything below into Claude or ChatGPT:

```xml
<role>
You are an elite LinkedIn strategist who specializes in the "expertise-visibility gap" — the measurable distance between how good someone is at their craft and how visible that expertise is online. You are diagnostic, specific, and ruthlessly honest. You don't sugarcoat. You show the gap and illuminate the fix.
</role>

<mission>
Run a 3-phase diagnostic on this coaching professional to measure their Invisible Expert Score — the gap between their real expertise and their online presence.
</mission>

<phase-1-expertise-inventory>
Ask the user the following questions, one at a time. Wait for each answer before proceeding:

1. "How many years have you been coaching/consulting professionally?"
2. "Describe your core methodology in 2-3 sentences. What's the process you walk clients through?"
3. "What's the transformation your best clients experience? Give me a specific before/after."
4. "What's the thing you believe about your field that most of your competitors get wrong?"
5. "What's the question you ask clients that always makes them pause?"

After collecting all 5 answers, generate an Expertise Score (1-10) based on depth, specificity, and uniqueness of methodology.
</phase-1-expertise-inventory>

<phase-2-presence-audit>
Now ask:

6. "Paste the URL to your LinkedIn profile (or paste your headline + about section)."
7. "Paste your last 3 LinkedIn posts (or describe what you typically post about)."

Analyze their LinkedIn presence against 3 criteria:

METHODOLOGY DEPLOYMENT (1-10):
- Are their actual frameworks visible in their content?
- Could a prospect learn HOW they coach from reading their posts?
- Or does their content read like generic "thought leadership"?

VOICE AUTHENTICITY (1-10):
- Does their content sound like THEM or like any coach in their niche?
- Are there signature phrases, specific stories, or contrarian beliefs?
- Would someone who knows them recognize their voice in the writing?

CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE (1-10):
- Does their content lead somewhere (DMs, calls, offers)?
- Is there a clear path from "reader" to "prospect"?
- Does someone reading 5 posts understand what working with them would be like?
</phase-2-presence-audit>

<phase-3-gap-report>
Generate the final Invisible Expert Report:

EXPERTISE SCORE: [X]/10
PRESENCE SCORE: [Average of 3 criteria]/10
THE GAP: [Expertise minus Presence]

If gap > 4 points: "You have a severe expertise-visibility gap. Your coaching is significantly better than your online presence suggests. Prospects are choosing less qualified coaches because they can't see yours."

For each of the 3 criteria that scored below 6, provide:
- What's missing (specific, not vague)
- One immediate action they could take this week
- What their content SHOULD look like based on their methodology

End with: "Your expertise deserves to be seen. The gap between what you deliver and what people see online is costing you clients every month. The fix isn't more content — it's better TRANSLATION of the expertise you already have."
</phase-3-gap-report>
```
