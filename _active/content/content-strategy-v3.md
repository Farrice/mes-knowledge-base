### POST FORMAT NOTES

**Every post follows these structural rules:**

1. **Hook in 8 words or fewer.** LinkedIn mobile cuts off at line 2. If the hook doesn't force "see more," the post dies.
2. **Single-line paragraphs.** One idea per line. White space creates scanability and increases dwell time (top-3 ranking factor).
3. **Tension before release.** Build the problem for 60-70% of the post. Solution in the last 30%.
4. **No emojis.** They signal consumer content, not professional authority.
5. **No engagement bait.** "Comment YES if you agree" is detected and penalized by the algorithm.
6. **No external links.** 60% distribution penalty. Put links in comments only if absolutely necessary.
7. **End with resonance, not a demand.** Questions work. Demands don't. "DM me POSTS" works because it's an invitation, not a command.
8. **Character count: 800-1,200.** Long enough for dwell time. Short enough to finish. The algorithm rewards completion rate.

### How to Find Coaches to Comment On

**Search Method 1: LinkedIn Search**
- Search "coach" + [niche]: "executive coach," "leadership coach," "business coach," "life coach," "career coach"
- Filter by: Posts (not People)
- Sort by: Recent
- Look for coaches who posted in the last 24 hours with fewer than 10 comments (they'll notice yours)

**Search Method 2: Hashtag Monitoring**
- Follow: #executivecoaching #leadershipcoaching #coachingbusiness #lifecoach #businesscoach
- Check the hashtag feeds daily for fresh posts
- Target posts from coaches with 500-5,000 followers (large enough to have reach, small enough to notice you)

**Search Method 3: Competitor Audiences**
- Find 5-10 established coaches in your target niche (10K+ followers)

**Search Method 4: LinkedIn Groups**

### Comment Templates That Demonstrate Expertise

**Do NOT leave generic comments.** Every comment should make the coach (and their audience) think: "This person knows what they're talking about."

**Template 1: The "Add Value" Comment** (most versatile)
```
This resonates, [Name]. The point about [specific thing they said]

**Template 2: The "Reframe" Comment** (positions you as a thinker)

**Template 3: The "Data Point" Comment** (builds authority)
```
This tracks with what the data shows.

**Comment Rules:**

3. Add a perspective, question, or insight. Don't just agree.

### How Commenting Feeds Into DM Outreach

This is the funnel:

```
Comment on their post (they see your name)
        |
Comment again 2-3 days later (they recognize you)
        |
They check your profile (headline hooks them)
        |
They read your About section (frustration mirroring lands)
        |
Send connection request (they accept because they recognize you)
        |
Wait for trigger (they like your post, view profile, or 5-7 days pass)
        |
Open DM with curiosity question (not a pitch)
        |
If they express frustration → offer proof run
```

**This sequence takes 5-10 days per prospect.** That's why you start commenting on Day 1 — by Day 7-8, your first batch of warm prospects are ready for DMs.

**The key insight**: You never cold-DM anyone. By the time you message them, they've seen your name 3-5 times, read your profile, and possibly engaged with your content. The DM feels like a natural conversation, not a sales pitch.
