Would it be useful if I shared a few insights I found? No pitch, just thought it might be relevant to your work.

I actually build these kinds of consumer intelligence reports as a service — I mine Reddit, reviews, and forums for real audience language and produce a 25-35 page intelligence report in 48-72 hours.

Currently taking 5 clients at launch pricing ($500). Would that be interesting for [their business]?

## Notes on Execution:

1. **Always do 10-15 min of pre-research before sending ANY cold outreach.** Find a specific Reddit thread, review, or forum post related to their niche. Lead with real value, not a pitch.

2. **Target 10-15 cold emails per day.** Quality over quantity. Each email should reference something specific about their business.

3. **Follow up on Day 3 and Day 7.** Short, no-pressure:
   - Day 3: "Just bumping this in case it got buried. Happy to share more about what the research would look like for [their brand]."
   - Day 7: "Last note on this — I found [1 new interesting data point] in my research. Let me know if you'd like the full picture."

4. **Track everything** in a simple spreadsheet: Name, Company, Date Sent, Response, Follow-up Status.
