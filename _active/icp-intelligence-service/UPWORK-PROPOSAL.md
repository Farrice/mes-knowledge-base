## Profile Overview (Upwork Bio):

I build consumer intelligence that makes your marketing actually work.

Most businesses market to a fictional avatar — a name, age, and income bracket they brainstormed. Then they wonder why conversion rates plateau at 2%.

I do something different. I mine real consumer conversations (Reddit, Amazon reviews, forums, Quora) and apply consumer psychology frameworks to build a 3-dimensional profile of your ideal customer. Not who they *could* be — who they *are*.

**What you get:**
→ 30-50 real consumer quotes organized by theme (plug directly into copy)
→ A full psychological portrait — decision logic, identity patterns, language preferences
→ Where to find these exact people online (with search methodology)
→ 3 ready-to-use content pieces calibrated to their internal world
→ A strategic action plan ranked by revenue impact

**Why hire me vs. a market research firm:**
- 48-72 hr turnaround (not 4-8 weeks)
- $500-$750 (not $5,000-$25,000)
- Grounded in real consumer data (not surveys and assumptions)
- AI-augmented research infrastructure (access to more data, faster)

I work with coaches, consultants, course creators, e-commerce brands, and service businesses.

## Response Framework for Common Job Types:

### Type 1: "I need help defining my target audience"
**Angle**: "Most target audience work stays at the surface. I go into the communities where your audience actually talks to each other and mine their real language. You'll know exactly what they say, think, and feel — not what you assume they do."

### Type 2: "I need buyer personas for my business"
**Angle**: "I don't build fictional personas. I build intelligence profiles grounded in real consumer data. The difference: my profiles include 30-50 actual quotes from your audience, organized by theme, so you can plug directly into your copy and content."

### Type 3: "I need market research for a product launch"
**Angle**: "For launches, you need to know 3 things: the exact language your audience uses, the emotional triggers driving purchase decisions, and the objections they have before they've even heard your pitch. That's exactly what my Consumer Intelligence Report delivers."

### Type 4: "I need help with my marketing/messaging"
**Angle**: "Messaging problems are usually targeting problems in disguise. Before touching your copy, I'd map your consumer's internal world — how they actually think about their problem, what identity they're protecting, and what language makes them feel understood. Then the messaging writes itself."
