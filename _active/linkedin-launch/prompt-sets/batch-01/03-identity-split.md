# Topic: The Identity Split — Private Expert vs. Public Brand

**Psychological core**: An attachment style issue playing out in professional identity. Coaches who equate visibility with superficiality aren't being "modest" — they're protecting a self-concept. Many entered coaching specifically because it was anti-marketing. Being told to "build a brand" feels like becoming the person they've spent their career critiquing.

**Why now**: LinkedIn 2026 demands personal profiles as the primary business development vehicle. Social selling is the dominant B2B strategy. For coaches who built careers on private, relational work — this is an existential identity crisis, not a marketing challenge.

---

## Talking Points — Talk Through These

*Don't write. Just talk. Skip any that don't land. Lean into the ones that light you up.*

### 1. Two versions of you
Walk me through the difference between who you are in a coaching session and who you are (or try to be) when writing LinkedIn content. What changes? Your language? Your confidence? Your sense of authority? Where does the gap live — and when did you first notice it?

### 2. The brand allergy
How do you feel about the word "brand"? Genuinely. When someone says "you need to build your personal brand" — what's your gut reaction? And where do you think that reaction comes from? Is there a part of you that associates branding with the kind of surface-level, self-promotional behavior you've spent your career working against?

### 3. A client's split
Think of a client who had a similar identity split — who they were in private vs. who they showed in public. Could be a leader who was brilliant in small groups but invisible in company-wide settings. Could be someone whose real personality disappeared the moment they wrote a bio. What did you help them see?

### 4. The attachment lens
From your psychology background — is there an attachment style dynamic happening here? Are coaches who avoid visibility exhibiting avoidant patterns in their professional identity? Or is it something else? What would you call this pattern if you were diagnosing it in a client?

### 5. Your own integration
How have you personally navigated (or are you still navigating) the tension between "private expert" and "public presence"? Not the resolved version — the honest, messy, in-progress version. What's still hard about it?

### 6. What "authenticity" actually means
Everyone says "be authentic on LinkedIn." But for coaches, that advice is almost cruel — because their most authentic self is the one that shows up in private sessions, not public feeds. What does authenticity actually look like for someone whose superpower is intimacy? Can you be intimate at scale?

### 7. The reframe
If this is an identity issue, not a marketing issue — what's the identity shift that has to happen? What does a coach need to BELIEVE about themselves differently before public content becomes possible? And have you seen that shift happen?
