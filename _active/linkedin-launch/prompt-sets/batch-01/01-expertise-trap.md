# Topic: The Expertise Trap — Why Knowing Too Much Creates Content Paralysis

**Psychological core**: The curse of knowledge — simplification feels like lying. The more you know, the more you see the nuance, the exceptions, the "it depends." Your standards for accuracy become a prison.

**Why now**: LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm literally rewards expertise signals. The platform is BEGGING for what expert coaches have. And they're frozen.

---

## Talking Points — Talk Through These

*Don't write. Just talk. Voice note, stream of consciousness, whatever feels natural. Skip any prompt that doesn't land. Lean into the ones that light you up.*

### 1. Your own freeze
Think back to a specific moment where you KNEW something deeply — had years of experience with it — and still couldn't bring yourself to post about it. What was the topic? What stopped you? What was the internal monologue? Not the "I was busy" excuse — the REAL reason. What did the hesitation feel like in your body?

### 2. The nuance prison
You've coached hundreds of people. When you sit down to write about something you know deeply, what happens? Walk me through the mental process. Do you start writing and then think "but it's more complicated than that"? Do you see all the exceptions before you've even finished the first sentence? Give me a specific example — a topic where you KNOW the answer but can't simplify it without feeling like you're lying.

### 3. The client parallel
Have you ever watched a client go through this same pattern — someone who was clearly expert-level at something but couldn't articulate it simply? Maybe in a different domain — fitness, business, relationships. What did that look like from the outside? What did you say to them? And did you hear the irony of giving that advice while struggling with the same thing in your own content?

### 4. The body's version
When you're about to post something and you pull back — what happens physically? Is it a tightness? A heaviness? Where do you feel it? As someone who reads bodies, what would you diagnose in yourself if you were your own client?

### 5. What you actually believe
Here's the contrarian question: Is simplification actually dishonest? Or is the refusal to simplify actually a defense mechanism disguised as integrity? What do you REALLY think about coaches who DO post simple, clear content? Are they dumbing it down — or are they serving their audience better than the expert who stays silent?

### 6. The mechanism
From your psychology lens — what's actually happening neurologically or psychologically when expertise creates paralysis? You've spent 18 years understanding human behavior. Name it. Not the pop-psych version — YOUR version, the one you'd explain to a colleague.

### 7. What breaks the pattern
If a client came to you with this exact problem — "I know too much to write simply" — what would you do? What's the intervention? Walk me through your actual methodology for breaking this pattern, not a tip list.
