# Topic: How Coaches Project Their Client's Resistance Onto Content Creation

**Psychological core**: Coaches develop elite pattern recognition for resistance through their client work. Then they unconsciously apply those same patterns to justify their own avoidance. The skill that makes them great coaches (reading resistance) makes them expert self-deceivers about their own. This is graduate-level insight.

**Why now**: This is a DIFFERENTIATOR topic — nobody else is saying this. It speaks directly to psychologically-sophisticated coaches who will immediately recognize the pattern. It positions Farrice as operating at a depth other content strategists can't reach.

---

## Talking Points — Talk Through These

*Don't write. Just talk. Skip any that don't land. Lean into the ones that light you up.*

### 1. Your client's resistance
Walk me through what client resistance looks like in a session. Not the textbook version — YOUR version. What do you notice? What do they say? What do they do with their body? How do you know when someone is resisting vs. when they genuinely need more time? Give me a specific moment.

### 2. Your own resistance
Now — think about the last time you sat down to create content and didn't follow through. What did you tell yourself? Walk me through the internal monologue. And then — looking at that monologue from the outside, as a coach — does it sound familiar? Does it sound like something a client would say?

### 3. The specific parallels
Can you name 2-3 specific resistance patterns you see in clients that you've ALSO caught yourself doing with content? For example: "I need to do more research first" (the client's version of avoiding action through preparation). "The timing isn't right" (the client's version of waiting for perfect conditions). "I tried it and it didn't work" (after one attempt). What are YOUR versions?

### 4. Clinical sophistication as camouflage
Here's the sharp question: Do you think coaches use their psychological training to build more SOPHISTICATED resistance? Like — a non-coach might just say "I don't want to." But a coach can say "I'm setting appropriate boundaries around my energy" or "I'm honoring my nervous system's capacity." It sounds clinical. It sounds healthy. And it's resistance wearing a lab coat. Have you seen this? Have you done this?

### 5. The diagnostic eye turned inward
As a coach who reads people for a living — what would you diagnose in YOURSELF if you were your own client? If someone described your relationship with content creation, what pattern would you name? What would you say to them?

### 6. Why this matters
Why is this pattern dangerous? Not just "you're not posting enough." What happens to a coach's practice, their business, their sense of purpose when they can identify everyone's resistance except their own? What's the cost — not financially, but psychologically?

### 7. The breakthrough
Have you had a moment where you caught yourself projecting client patterns onto your own content avoidance? What happened? What shifted? And for coaches reading this who just had the "oh sh*t" moment of recognition — what's the first step?
