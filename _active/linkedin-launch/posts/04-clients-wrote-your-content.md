---

"She saw something in me
that I'd been hiding from for 20 years."

"He asked one question
and my entire business strategy unraveled.
In a good way."

"I've worked with 4 coaches.
She's the only one who understood
that I wasn't afraid of failure.
I was afraid of succeeding
and having nothing left to prove."

These aren't LinkedIn posts.

They're feedback forms.
End-of-session texts.
Voice memos sent at 11pm
after a coaching call that changed something.

And every single one of them
is a better LinkedIn hook
than anything you'll ever brainstorm
staring at a blank screen.

---

I sat with a coach last month
who told me she had "nothing to post about."

I asked her to pull up
her last 10 client testimonials.

She read the first one out loud:

"Before working with Lisa,
I was running a $400K business
and felt like a fraud every single day.
After 3 months, same business, same revenue --
but I actually believe I deserve it now."

I said: "That's a post."

She looked confused.

So I showed her:

Hook: "A client told me she ran a $400K business
and felt like a fraud every day."

Body: The story of what shifted.
Not the technique (save that for the paid work).
The MOMENT. The conversation at minute 43
where everything clicked.

Close: "The gap was never her business.
It was her relationship with her own success.
If that sentence hit you in the chest --
that's probably not a coincidence."

She stared at it.

"I would never have thought
to turn a testimonial into a post."

That's the thing.

You don't need to THINK of content.

You need to recognize it
when it's already in front of you.

---

Your clients have been writing
your LinkedIn content for years.

Every time someone says:
"You changed how I see myself" --
that's a hook.

Every time someone says:
"Nobody ever asked me that question before" --
that's a story.

Every time someone sends a voice memo
months later saying:
"I still think about what you said" --
that's a positioning statement.

This isn't material for content.
This IS content.

You just need someone
to show you how to see it that way.
