# Post 11: "The Consistency Trap"

---

He posted every day for 6 months.

180 posts.

Average engagement: 14 likes.

He was exhausted.

Not creatively exhausted --
existentially exhausted.

The kind of tired that comes
from doing something every day
that produces nothing
but can't be stopped
because someone on a podcast said
"consistency is king."

So he kept posting.

Motivational quotes on Monday.
"3 tips for leaders" on Wednesday.
A carousel on Friday
that took 4 hours to design
and got 23 impressions.

I asked him one question:

"Do any of these posts
teach your actual framework?"

He looked at the floor.

"No. I save that for clients."

---

This is the consistency trap.

And it's everywhere.

Some LinkedIn guru says
"post 5 times a week."
So coaches start posting 5 times a week.

But nobody told them
WHAT to post.

So they post what's easy.
What's fast. What's safe.

Tips they found on another coach's page.
Quotes they agree with.
Generic advice that sounds professional
but teaches nothing proprietary.

And then they do it again.
And again.
And again.

180 times.

And 180 mediocre posts later,
their audience has been trained
on one thing:

**This account can be ignored.**

---

Here's what nobody says out loud
about the LinkedIn algorithm:

It's not measuring consistency.
It's measuring response.

When you post something
and nobody clicks, comments, shares, or saves --
LinkedIn learns.

"This account creates content
that people scroll past."

Do that 180 times
and you've built a reputation.
Not with your audience.
With the algorithm.

You've taught the machine
that your content doesn't matter.

And now even when you post
something genuinely brilliant --
LinkedIn shows it to 200 people
instead of 2,000.

Because you spent 6 months
proving that you weren't worth the reach.

Consistency didn't build your brand.
It built a graveyard.

---

I talked to a different coach last week.

She posts twice a week. Sometimes once.

But every single post
teaches a piece of her methodology.

Her last post was about the question
she asks every C-suite client
in the first 15 minutes.

The question itself.
Why she asks it.
What happens when they answer honestly.
What happens when they deflect.

It got 340 likes.
18 DMs.
2 discovery calls booked.

From one post.

Meanwhile, the coach posting daily
hasn't had a single DM from a prospect
in 4 months.

---

The formula isn't consistency.

The formula is:
**quality content, posted consistently.**

And if you have to choose --
and most coaches do have to choose
because they're running a business,
coaching clients,
and living a life --

Choose fewer posts
that represent your actual expertise level.

5 incredible posts that teach your methodology
will outperform
20 generic posts about "leadership mindset"
every single time.

Not because the algorithm rewards less.
Because the algorithm rewards REACTION.

And people react
when they encounter something they've never seen.

Not another tip list.
Not another "here's what great leaders do."

YOUR framework.
YOUR question.
YOUR specific, hard-won, can't-Google-this insight.

That's what stops a scroll.
