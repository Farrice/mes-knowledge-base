# Post #001: "The Audit" — 5 Lessons from Auditing Elite S&C Coaches on LinkedIn
**Type**: Text post | **Style**: Lessons | **Status**: APPROVED — Ready to publish
**Content Calendar**: Week 1, Monday (Educate)
**Character Count**: ~2,540

---

I spent 18 years as a personal trainer.

Then I audited the LinkedIn profiles of coaches who've trained Jokic, Olympic champions, and NFL teams.

What I found was alarming.

6 out of 8 scored 1 out of 9 on content infrastructure.

These coaches have 200+ podcast episodes. NSCA awards. Published books. Consulting gigs across every major league. Their LinkedIn looks like a resume from 2014.

The gap isn't talent. It's translation. Here are 5 lessons from the audit.

1/ Your best content already exists. You're just not capturing it.

One coach runs a weekly podcast with 200+ episodes interviewing elite S&C minds.

- None of it gets repurposed into LinkedIn posts
- No clips, no pull quotes, no threads from episode themes
- His podcast audience has nowhere to engage between episodes

The content is there. The distribution system isn't.

2/ Credentials don't build authority on LinkedIn. Stories do.

I found a coach who turned a pudgy second-round pick into a 3x MVP. Zero ACL re-injuries across a 21-year NBA tenure. The team named their weight room after him. His LinkedIn is event flyers. Credentials sit on a profile page nobody visits. Stories travel through feeds. One post about that transformation reaches more coaches than a decade of conference talks.

3/ A broken website is worse than no website.

Multiple coaches had:

- Primary websites returning 404 errors
- Email signups with no value proposition
- Two LinkedIn profiles splitting their audience

Someone Googles you after your keynote and your site is broken. They don't think "tech issues." They think "not as established as I heard." That's not neutral. That's damage.

4/ Every speaking gig without email capture is a one-night stand.

These coaches do TEDx talks. Corporate training. Healthcare conferences. Hundreds of people in the room leaning forward. Then the event ends and the audience goes home with no way to stay connected. No newsletter. No lead magnet. Nothing. A speaking gig without a capture mechanism is renting attention you could own. These coaches rebuild from scratch every time they walk off stage.

5/ The visibility gap compounds every year you wait.

- While elite coaches stay invisible, less experienced trainers with better content claim the authority
- Every year without a digital presence, someone with half the track record fills the space
- The gap doesn't hold. It widens.

Your in-room track record is an unfair advantage. But only if people outside the room can see it.

The best coaches in the world are invisible online.

Not because they lack expertise.

Because no one ever taught them how to translate it.
