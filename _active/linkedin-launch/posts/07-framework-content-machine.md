# Post 7: "The Framework Content Machine"
**CTA Keyword**: FRAMEWORK | **Format**: Text post | **Post time**: Tue-Thu, 8-10am EST
**Prompt Kit**: `prompt-kits/07-framework-decomposition-engine.md` | **First comment**: Link to hosted prompt kit

---

I asked a coach:
"How many frameworks do you use with clients?"

She thought for a second.

"Seven. Maybe eight if you count
the one I use for onboarding."

I said:
"You have 56 LinkedIn posts
sitting in your head right now."

She laughed.

Then she stopped laughing
because I wasn't joking.

---

Here's how most coaches approach content:

Monday morning.
Coffee. Blank screen.
Open LinkedIn.

"What should I post about today?"

Scroll. Scroll. Scroll.

See what other coaches posted.
Feel behind.
Start drafting something.
Hate it.
Save as draft.
Close laptop.

Tuesday: same thing.
Wednesday: skip it.
Thursday: guilt.
Friday: "I'll start fresh next week."

This cycle repeats for months.

And the whole time,
the coach has 5-8 proprietary frameworks
sitting inside their methodology
that they've never once turned into content.

Not because they don't know how to write.

Because they think content requires IDEAS.

It doesn't.

It requires decomposition.

---

Let me show you what I mean.

Take any single framework you use with clients.

I don't care what it is.
A 4-step process.
A diagnostic matrix.
A mindset model you sketched
on a napkin 3 years ago
that you've used with every client since.

That ONE framework contains
at minimum 8 LinkedIn posts:

1. The origin story.
How did you create it?
What problem were you solving?
What moment made you realize
the existing approach was broken?

2. The overview.
Here's my framework. Here's why it works.
Here's the transformation it produces.

3. Each step, individually.
If it's a 4-step process,
that's 4 posts -- one per step.
Each step gets a client story.
Each step gets a "here's what most people get wrong."

4. The contrarian belief behind it.
Why does your framework exist?
What does it reject about the conventional approach?

5. The client result.
Before and after.
What happened when someone went through this process?
Specific. Named emotions.
Real numbers when possible.

That's 8 posts from ONE framework.

You have 5-8 frameworks.

Do the math.

---

The coaches who post 3x a week
don't have more ideas than you.

They have a system.

They decomposed their methodology
into component parts
and turned each part into content.

Monday: a client story from Step 2.
Wednesday: the contrarian belief behind their approach.
Friday: the origin story of their framework.

No blank screens.
No brainstorming.
No scrolling for "inspiration."

Just extraction.

Because the content already exists.
It lives in their methodology.

They just built a machine
to pull it out.

---

This is why I push back
when coaches tell me
"I need help coming up with content ideas."

No you don't.

You need help SEEING what you already have.

Every framework you teach,
every process you walk clients through,
every diagnostic you run in session 1 --

That's not just coaching IP.
That's a content engine
you haven't turned on yet.

And the beautiful thing is:
this kind of content is the BEST kind.

Because it doesn't just "provide value."
It teaches people how you think.

A post about Step 3 of your process
isn't a tip.
It's a window into your methodology.

And a prospect reading that post
starts to feel like they already know
what it would be like to work with you.

That's not engagement.
That's pre-selling.
