# Post 8: "Content That Pre-Sells (vs. Content That Gets Likes)"
**CTA Keyword**: AUDIT | **Format**: Text post | **Post time**: Tue-Thu, 8-10am EST
**Prompt Kit**: `prompt-kits/08-content-to-client-audit.md` | **First comment**: Link to hosted prompt kit

---

Your best-performing content is the same content costing you clients.

The posts getting 70-100 likes? The saves, the shares, the "great advice!" comments? Those posts are training your audience to learn from you for free.

I watched a coach post 48 times in one quarter. Good engagement on every post. Likes, comments, saves. Discovery calls from all that content? Three.

Three calls. 48 posts. She did the math in real time and went quiet.

Her last 10 posts: "5 signs you're a people-pleaser." "The 3 biggest mistakes leaders make giving feedback." "Why emotional intelligence is the #1 skill for 2026." Clean. Structured. Valuable. And completely interchangeable with 10,000 other coaches' feeds.

---

Here's the split most coaches never see.

Value content says: "Here are 5 ways to manage conflict at work."

Pre-selling content says: "A client came to me convinced her team hated her. Session 2, I asked one question that changed the entire narrative. The team didn't hate her. They were afraid of her -- not because she was harsh, but because she was so competent they felt like they couldn't keep up."

The first teaches WHAT to do. The reader thinks: "Good tips." The second teaches HOW YOU THINK. The reader thinks: "This person sees things I didn't know could be seen."

One creates followers. The other creates prospects.

I learned this difference long before I thought about content. I used to teach group fitness classes and train private clients in the same week. In the group class, I'd teach the movement -- "here's how to deadlift, five cues for form." Everyone learned. Nobody's life changed. But in a private session, I'd watch a client move for 30 seconds and say, "Your left hip is compensating for a core stability issue you don't know you have -- that's why your lower back hurts every Thursday." Same knowledge. Completely different experience. One educated a room. The other made one person feel read.

Pre-selling content is the private session. You're not listing tips. You're showing the reader you already see their specific pattern.

---

The coaches making $30K+/month from content aren't better writers. Their posts follow a different pattern.

They open with a specific moment -- a client's face, a conversation, something that happened. Not a tip list. They reveal their diagnostic thinking -- not "here's what you should do" but "here's the question I asked, here's what I noticed, here's what most coaches miss." They create proximity. After 3 posts, you feel like you've already had a session with them.

That's not engagement. That's pre-selling. The reader is experiencing your methodology in miniature. When they're ready to buy, they already know who to call.

If your content gets consistent likes but no DMs, you haven't failed at content. You've succeeded at the wrong kind.
