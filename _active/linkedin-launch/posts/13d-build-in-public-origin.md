# Post 13D: "Build In Public — The Origin Story"
**CTA Keyword**: N/A (journey post) | **Format**: Text post | **Post time**: Week 3-4, after trust is built
**Series**: Build In Public (4 of 4) | **Prompt Kit**: `prompt-kits/13-build-in-public-toolkit.md`

---

People keep asking me
why I do this.

Not the ghostwriting.
The obsession with coaches specifically.

Here's the honest answer.

---

I used to watch my wife leave for work
at 7:15am.

I'd be standing in the kitchen
with our kids,
a half-finished bottle,
a to-do list that started with "survive today,"
and the quiet hum
of a house that was about to get loud.

I was a stay-at-home dad.

And for a while, that was the whole identity.

Not because I chose it lightly --
because it consumed everything.
Every hour. Every decision.
Every ounce of creative energy
I used to pour into building things.

I didn't resent it.
But I missed MAKING things.

---

So during naps and after bedtime,
I started building.

Not a business at first.
Systems.

I'd been fascinated with AI --
not the hype, the mechanics.
What it could actually do
when pointed at a real problem.

And the problem I kept thinking about
was this:

Why do the best experts
have the worst online presence?

I'd seen it everywhere.
The coach who transforms lives
but has 200 followers
and a bio that says
"Passionate about leadership."

The consultant who's solved the problem
a hundred times
but can't explain it in a post
that would make a stranger stop scrolling.

The therapist, the strategist, the advisor
who's brilliant in the room
and invisible on the screen.

---

It made me angry.

Not annoyed. Angry.

Because the gap isn't about talent.
It's not about laziness.
It's not about "not wanting it enough."

It's a translation problem.

The skills that make someone extraordinary
in a coaching session
are completely different
from the skills that make someone visible online.

And the industry's solution?

"Post more. Be consistent.
Hire a ghostwriter
who'll interview you for 30 minutes
and write content that could be about
any coach in your niche."

That's not a solution.
That's a tax on experts
who deserve better.

---

So I built something different.

Instead of starting with "what do you post about" --
I started with "how do you actually think?"

What's the question you ask
that nobody else asks?

What happens in session 3
when the real work begins?

What's the belief you hold about your field
that your competitors would argue with?

That material -- the methodology,
the stories, the conviction --
doesn't live in a 30-minute interview.

It lives in the way a coach's brain works.

And I built tools to extract it.

Not just transcribe it.
Not just summarize it.
TRANSLATE it.

From session language to content language.
From "how I coach"
to "what a stranger needs to read
to know this is the person they've been looking for."

---

I'm not building this
because I think the world needs
another content agency.

I'm building this because I believe
alignment matters more than hustle.

That the coaches who built their expertise
the hard way --
in rooms, with real people,
through thousands of hours of practice --
deserve to be found by the people
who need them most.

And right now,
they're being outmarketed
by people half as qualified
who just figured out LinkedIn first.

That's the gap I'm here to close.

---

I don't know exactly
where this goes.

I'm a stay-at-home dad
building an AI-powered ghostwriting practice
during nap times and after bedtime.

I don't have a team.
I don't have investors.
I don't have an audience (yet).

What I have is a methodology that works,
tools that I trust,
and the belief that expertise
shouldn't be invisible.

If that resonates with you --
you're in the right place.

I'll keep building.
I'll keep showing you everything.

And if you're a coach
whose brilliance lives in sessions
but not in your content --

I see you.

That's exactly who I'm here for.
