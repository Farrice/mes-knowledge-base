# Transition Post #1: "The Translation Problem"
**Type**: Text post | **Format**: Narrative origin story | **Purpose**: Introduce new positioning
**Post time**: ASAP — transition post, not arc chapter
**Series**: Transition (0 of 0 — standalone bridge into serial narrative world)

---

My wife left for work at 7:15am.

I was standing in the kitchen
with a bottle, a toddler,
and a to-do list that started with "survive today."

Stay-at-home dad.
Best decision I ever made.
But a part of me was going quiet —
the part I'd built everything on.

---

That quiet part started paying attention.

For 18 years as a coach and trainer,
I'd watched the same pattern:

The most brilliant people in any room
were the ones nobody could find online.

A consultant who'd solved the problem
a hundred times —
losing deals to firms with better websites
and worse answers.

A strategist charging $15K a quarter
with a LinkedIn bio that said
"Helping businesses grow."

Brilliant in a room.
Ghost online.

---

I used to think they just couldn't write.

Eighteen years taught me otherwise.

They could explain a $200K strategy
in a conversation and make it feel obvious.
But ask them to write 200 words
for strangers on LinkedIn?

Silence.

It's not a writing problem.
It's a translation problem.

The skill that makes you extraordinary in a room
has nothing to do with the skill
that makes a stranger stop scrolling.

Different language entirely.
One most experts never needed —
until the room moved online.

---

So I built something for it.

Not templates. Not "post more."
Not "just be authentic" —
as if the problem was honesty
and not language.

A practice built around one question:
How do you actually think?

Not what you know.
How you connect the dots
when you're sitting with a client
and the real work begins.

That's the part that disappears online.
That's the part worth translating.

Expertise shouldn't be invisible.
And I got tired of watching it disappear.

If you've ever lost a client to someone less qualified
who just showed up better online —
I'd love to hear that story.

More on this soon.
