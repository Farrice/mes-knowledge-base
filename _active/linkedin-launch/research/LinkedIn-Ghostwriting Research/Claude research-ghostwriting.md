### The Ghostwriter Income Staircase: Five levels of pricing power

Cole's most actionable framework is the **Ghostwriter Income Staircase**, which maps the progression from commodity pricing to premium positioning:

**Level 1 — Per Word:** The lowest tier, where writers earn pennies. Cole advises escaping this level immediately because it ties value to volume, not impact. **Level 2 — Per Hour:** Better but still fundamentally limited. "You are tying your earning potential to time." No incentive for efficiency; faster writers earn less. **Level 3 — Per Month:** The retainer model most ghostwriters settle into. Stable but still trades time for money without capturing the full value of expertise. **Level 4 — Per Asset:** Cole's recommended sweet spot. Flat-rate pricing for specific deliverables like 5-Day Educational Email Courses ($3,000–$5,000+). This decouples income from hours and rewards pattern recognition. **Level 5 — Per Outcome:** The premium tier where pricing ties directly to business results generated. Few ghostwriters reach this level, but those who do command the highest rates.

The core reframe: *"What the client is paying you for are all the years it took you to acquire knowledge in order to deliver the outcome."*

### The PGA framework: Three pillars to premium positioning

PGA's curriculum centers on three pillars. **Premium Positioning** covers niche selection, establishing yourself as a trusted advisor rather than "just another freelancer," and specializing in a specific industry. **Premium Offer** teaches packaging services as "Packaged Services" rather than hourly or per-word billing, with the primary asset being **5-Day Educational Email Courses (EECs)** — revenue-critical client assets that justify premium pricing. **Premium Outreach** provides the method for sparking conversations, educating potential clients, and landing high-ticket opportunities without being salesy.

### Ship 30 for 30: The content engine frameworks

Ship 30's core insight is that aspiring writers should produce **30 Atomic Essays in 30 days** — short-form pieces under 250 words expressing a single idea. The program's **Endless Idea Generator** uses four steps: apply the 2-Year Test (find topics you've thought about for 2+ years), add specificity, run ideas through the **4A Framework** (Actionable, Analytical, Aspirational, Anthropological), then select the three strongest ideas.

The **4A Framework** is Cole's most widely adopted content categorization system. **Actionable** content delivers tips, how-tos, and step-by-step guides. **Analytical** content provides data, research, and breakdowns. **Aspirational** content tells personal transformation stories. **Anthropological** content offers observations about human behavior and "why" things happen. Every piece of content should fit one of these four categories.

Cole's writing philosophy emphasizes **Rate of Revelation** — how quickly new information reaches the reader. On the internet, writing optimized for speed wins. Combined with **Lean Writing** (every sentence pushes the story forward), **Pinpoint Writing** (writing for one specific person, not everyone), and the **Curiosity Gap** (crafting headlines that create an irresistible urge to click), these frameworks form a complete content creation system.

### The client upsell ladder and acquisition engine

Cole's client acquisition begins with the **"Offer to Write for Free, First"** method and his **Comments as Consulting** strategy, where ghostwriters leave substantive, consulting-level comments on potential clients' posts for 2–4 weeks before any pitch. Once engaged, the upsell ladder progresses from a **5-Day Educational Email Course** (flat-rate project, $3,000–$5,000+) to email sequences to social media ghostwriting (monthly retainer) to weekly newsletter ghostwriting (second retainer stream). This systematic progression builds revenue per client over time.

Cole's **10-80-10 Rule for AI** captures his hybrid approach: write the first 10% (hook, direction), use AI for the middle 80% (drafting), then write the final 10% (editing, voice refinement). This workflow enables the speed of AI with the authenticity of human craft.

## 4. Content domination playbook: Algorithms, hooks, formats, and frameworks

### How the 2025–2026 LinkedIn algorithm actually works

The LinkedIn algorithm distributes content through a three-step funnel. **Step 1** is a quality filter that classifies content as spam, low-quality, or high-quality using AI. Engagement bait and pod behavior are now actively detected and penalized. **Step 2** is trial distribution during the "golden hour" — the first 60 minutes after posting — where content is shown to a small test audience of 1st-degree connections. **Step 3** is broader distribution based on relevance signals, including connection strength (Social Graph), content topic matching (Interest Graph), and topic authority scoring.

Richard van der Blom's 2025 Algorithm InSights report revealed sobering data: views dropped **50%**, engagement dropped **25%**, and follower growth dropped **59%** compared to prior years. The "Brew 360" algorithm update fundamentally rebalanced format performance. However, LinkedIn now shows posts **2–3 weeks old** if they remain relevant to user interests — a significant change that rewards evergreen content.

The algorithmic ranking signals in priority order are: **comments (especially 15+ words)** at the highest weight, followed by shares/reposts, saves (an increasingly important authority signal), dwell time, "See More" click rate, profile visits from the post, and reactions at the lowest individual weight. The critical insight for ghostwriters: **engineering meaningful comments is more valuable than chasing likes**.

### The content format hierarchy, ranked by performance data

**Carousels/document posts** dominate every performance metric. Buffer's analysis of millions of posts found carousels generate **278% more engagement than videos, 303% more than images, and 596% more than text-only posts**. Only **10% of creators** have ever posted a carousel, meaning competition is remarkably low. Optimal carousel length has shifted from 12–13 slides to **6–9 slides** as carousel fatigue sets in. Document posts generate **2.5x more shares** than other formats.

**Text + image posts** represent **65% of all LinkedIn content** but deliver solid engagement at approximately **4.2%**. **Vertical images get a 32% algorithmic boost** from occupying more feed real estate. Personal photos dramatically outperform stock imagery. This format offers the best ROI for time invested — roughly 5 minutes to create.

**Video** faces a challenging landscape. Viewer retention **drops sharply after 60 seconds**, with only **18% continuing past the one-minute mark**. Short-form video under 60 seconds achieves **53% more engagement** than long-form. Captions are essential since most viewers watch on mute. A minimum **30% completion rate** is needed to avoid algorithmic penalties.

**LinkedIn newsletters** represent the most underutilized format. Only **184,000 newsletters** exist on the platform, yet subscribers constitute an **owned audience**. Publishing bi-weekly delivers the optimal balance between frequency and reach. Newsletter subscriptions hit **450 million** in 2025 — a 3x year-over-year increase — confirming explosive audience demand for this format.

### Thirty hook formulas that stop the scroll

Ninety percent of a post's performance hinges on the opening line. LinkedIn shows only 2–3 lines before the "See more" fold, giving writers under 2 seconds to capture attention. The five core hook categories, each with specific formulas:

**Contrarian hooks** challenge conventional wisdom: "Stop [common practice]. It's killing your [outcome]." or "Everyone told me not to post this. I did it anyway." **Pain/problem hooks** call out shared frustrations: "Your [asset] isn't broken. Your [strategy] is." **Confession/vulnerability hooks** share something raw: "I almost quit [endeavor] last month. Here's why I didn't." or "It took me [long time] to realize [realization]." **List/framework hooks** set expectations: "X steps to [impressive result] — even if you [face obstacle]." **Big outcome hooks** lead with results: "From [humble beginning] to [milestone] in [timeframe]. Here's what happened."

Additional categories include data/statistic hooks ("Did you know [X]% of [audience] never [action]?"), question hooks ("What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"), pattern-interrupt hooks (a single provocative word, a short dialogue snippet, a confession), and tension hooks ("I opened my laptop. Typed three words. Deleted them."). Each hook should be **1–2 short lines, approximately 50–60 characters**, followed by a strategic line break.

### Eighteen storytelling frameworks for LinkedIn

The most versatile frameworks for LinkedIn ghostwriters include **PAS (Problem–Agitate–Solution)** for short-form posts and B2B content; **SLA (Story–Lesson–Application)** for personal brand content; **MRS (Mistake–Realization–Shift)** for vulnerability and authenticity posts; **VSQ (Value–Story–Question)** for engagement-driven thought leadership; and the **3-1-3 Framework** (3 opening lines, 1 core message, 3 key takeaways) for quick authority posts. 

The **GAP Content Framework** provides the strategic rotation model: **Growth content** (reach-focused carousels and viral hooks), **Authority content** (expertise-focused frameworks and data), and **Personal content** (connection-focused stories and behind-the-scenes). The optimal weekly mix is 3 Authority + 2 Growth + 1 Personal post.

### LinkedIn SEO and discoverability in a post-hashtag world

LinkedIn **removed hashtag following** in late 2024 and retired Creator Mode, shifting toward **AI-driven content discovery** rather than hashtag-based systems. Hashtags still boost engagement by **12.6%** and modestly increase reach (9%), with **3–5 hashtags per post** being optimal. However, the real SEO game is now **topical authority** — consistent posting on specific topics that builds algorithmic recognition as a subject-matter expert. The first 2–3 sentences of posts are indexed and visible before "See more," making keyword-rich openings essential for discovery.

## 5. Client acquisition machine: Zero to first client, then to ten

### Eight strategies for landing your first client with nothing

**Strategy 1: Your Network is Your Niche.** Cole emphasizes that "there are 10 people in your life right now who either need your help or know someone who needs your help." Start with former colleagues, LinkedIn connections, and friends running businesses. The faulty belief that "I don't want to pitch people I know" keeps ghostwriters broke.

**Strategy 2: The Free Work Pitch.** Do 1–2 projects free to build proof. Cole's philosophy: "Free work is the ultimate stress test for your skills. If you can't make someone over-the-moon happy FOR FREE, you're never going to make them happy in exchange for money." Offer 5 LinkedIn posts in the client's voice in exchange for a testimonial and referral.

**Strategy 3: Spear Fishing Cold Outreach.** Build a list of 50–100 potential clients already posting inconsistently on LinkedIn. Send a specific compliment plus request permission to share insights. If granted, list 6–8 topics they should cover more, write 3–5 sample posts in their voice, and offer to discuss ongoing support. This approach from Dakota Robertson converts at high rates because it demonstrates skill before asking for payment.

**Strategy 4: Comments as Consulting.** Nicolas Cole's signature strategy. Identify 20–30 ideal clients who post regularly. Follow them and turn on notifications. Leave substantive comments that add new angles, include frameworks, or reframe their ideas compellingly. Do this consistently for 2–4 weeks. When they check your profile — and they will — your headline says exactly what you do. This is value-first client acquisition that proves expertise in real time.

**Strategy 5: Write Under Your Own Name First.** You cannot sell LinkedIn ghostwriting if your own LinkedIn is empty. Post 4–5 times weekly on your own profile for 60–90 days. This serves as a living portfolio and proves you understand the platform.

**Strategy 6: The Spec Work Portfolio.** Write 5 sample posts in different executive voices covering your target niche: a storytelling post, a data-driven post, a contrarian take, a how-to framework, and a case study. These demonstrate voice adaptability without needing actual client work.

**Strategy 7: Freelance Platforms as Launchpad.** Use Upwork or Fiverr to get initial paid work and reviews. Use platform reviews to show credibility when pitching outside the platform.

**Strategy 8: The Exchange Model.** For prospects who "can't afford" full rates, Cole offers four options: 50% off in exchange for testimonial plus referral; free work in exchange for network introductions; a payment plan; or a revenue-share arrangement for trusted clients.

### Cold outreach templates that convert

The highest-performing DM template is the **Compliment + Permission** approach: "Hey [Name], I've been following your content and loved [specific post]. I had quick thoughts on getting more traction with your LinkedIn content. Would you be open to hearing them?" This generates responses because it demonstrates genuine familiarity while requesting permission before pitching.

The **Spec Work Follow-Up** delivers immediate value: "Hey [Name], I noticed on your podcast you discussed [topic] but you don't cover it on LinkedIn. Here are some posts where you could do that: [attach 3–5 custom posts]. If you need help with this consistently, let's hop on a call." The **Content Audit DM** shows analytical ability: "I spent 20 minutes analyzing your last 30 LinkedIn posts and noticed patterns — your storytelling posts get 3x more engagement than promotional ones. I put together 6 content angles I think would resonate. Want me to send it?"

The pipeline math is straightforward. Goal: 1 new client per month. Requires 4–6 qualified discovery calls, which requires 20–30 warm conversations, which requires **15–30 personalized outreach messages per day** plus 2–3 pieces of content weekly. LinkedIn DMs typically achieve **20–35% response rates** compared to 15–25% for cold emails, and follow-ups account for **50–70% of total responses**.

### The discovery call that closes at premium rates

The four-phase framework begins with **Rapport & Agenda** (5 minutes): "I want to learn about your business and what's working on LinkedIn. Then I'll share ideas on how I can help. If it makes sense, we'll talk next steps." **Discovery Questions** (15 minutes) uncover the client's situation, frustrations, and the lifetime value of a single client acquired through LinkedIn. **Position the Solution** (10 minutes) maps your package to their stated problems with specific metrics from past results. **Pricing & Close** (5 minutes) states the investment and then goes silent — *whoever speaks first loses*.

The value-based pricing anchoring technique asks the prospect for their client's lifetime value before stating any price. If a $50K-deal client says ghostwriting costs $3,000/month, the frame shifts to: "If our content generates even 1–2 clients per quarter, we're looking at 5–10x ROI." This transforms a cost conversation into an investment conversation.

### The 30-60-90 day portfolio acceleration

**Days 1–30** focus on foundation: choose niche, optimize LinkedIn profile, write 5 spec samples, begin posting 4x/week, complete 2 free projects in exchange for testimonials. **Days 31–60** accumulate social proof: collect 2–3 testimonials, create anonymized case studies ("Helped a SaaS founder increase engagement by 340% in 60 days"), build a simple portfolio on Notion or Authory. **Days 61–90** shift to scaling: raise rates 50–100% above initial pricing, launch referral requests with existing clients, begin outbound at scale with 15–30 DMs daily, add upsells like newsletter ghostwriting and profile optimization.

## 7. The AI ghostwriting stack: Tools, workflows, and prompt library

### The optimal tool stack at four budget levels

**Budget Stack ($50–$80/month):** ChatGPT Plus ($20) + Grammarly Free + Hemingway Free + Buffer Free + Canva Free. This handles the essentials for a ghostwriter with 1–3 clients.

**Growth Stack ($100–$180/month):** ChatGPT Plus ($20) + Claude Pro ($20) + AuthoredUp ($20) + Buffer Essentials ($6/channel) + Canva Pro ($13) + Shield ($19). Adds superior writing quality from Claude, LinkedIn-specific formatting from AuthoredUp, and analytics depth from Shield.

**Professional Stack ($200–$350/month):** ChatGPT Plus ($20) + Claude Pro ($20) + Taplio Standard ($49) + Grammarly Pro ($25) + Typeshare Pro ($29) + Perplexity Pro ($20) + Canva Pro ($13). This is the stack for a solo ghostwriter managing 5–8 clients with full research, scheduling, and design capabilities.

**Agency Stack ($400+/month):** Add Jasper Pro ($59), Taplio Pro ($149), and Otter.ai ($20) for voice transcription. This supports 10+ clients with team collaboration features and enterprise-grade AI brand voice consistency.

### The end-to-end AI ghostwriting workflow

**Phase 1: Voice Capture.** Record the onboarding interview using Otter.ai or Fireflies.ai. Feed transcripts plus 5–10 writing samples into Claude or ChatGPT with the voice analysis prompt: "Analyze the writing voice, tone, diction, style, and structure. Output detailed bullet points covering voice characteristics, tone patterns, sentence structure preferences, vocabulary tendencies, storytelling approach, and emotional register." Convert the analysis into a reusable instruction set under 300 words. Build a Custom GPT or Claude Project with this style guide as permanent context. Each client gets their own dedicated AI instance to prevent "voice bleed."

**Phase 2: Content Production.** Use Perplexity for industry research and trend identification. Feed the outline plus client voice instructions into the Custom GPT to generate first drafts. Generate 3–5 hook options using hook-specific prompts. Apply human editing for 15–30 minutes per post — reshaping for authenticity, adding personal anecdotes, removing "AI feel." Run through Grammarly for consistency and Hemingway for readability. Preview formatting in AuthoredUp for desktop and mobile.

Pull monthly analytics through Shield. Identify top and bottom performers, refine voice guide and AI prompts based on what resonates.
