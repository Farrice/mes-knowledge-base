### 1. CLIENT ONBOARDING PROCESS FOR GHOSTWRITING SERVICES

#### The 5-Step Client Onboarding Checklist (Nicolas Cole Framework)

**Step 1: Express Excitement & Recap**
- Send DM/email/call expressing enthusiasm
- Recap everything you're going to do
- Tell them you're sending an invoice
- Ask if they'd like assistant/finance team included
- Subtly mention: "as soon as the invoice is paid, I'll get rocking & rolling!"

**Step 2: Send Invoice via Stripe/PayPal/QuickBooks**
- Payment terms: Full payment upfront for projects under $5,000
- For projects above $5,000: 50% upfront, 50% on completion
- Monthly retainers: Paid in full at beginning of each month
- Work does NOT begin until payment is received

**Step 3: Send Project Plan/Dashboard with Loom Video**
- Bundle something of value with payment request
- Project dashboard shows client what to expect over coming weeks/months
- Demonstrates ownership of the project
- Loom walkthrough personalizes the experience

**Step 4: Sign Necessary Agreements**
- Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Statement of Work (SOW)
- Independent Contractor Agreement
- Work-for-hire clause

**Step 5: Project Kick-Off Call (When Needed)**
- Ask if they have questions about project plan
- Ask about content ideas they have top of mind
- Discuss your first 3-5 content ideas (come prepared!)
- Only schedule if client seems unsure or has many questions

#### Premium Ghostwriting Academy 3-Step Onboarding

1. **Onboarding Email + Payment** - Position yourself as premium by bundling invoice with project dashboard
2. **Payment Collection** - Use Stripe, PayPal, or QuickBooks; follow up until money hits bank
3. **Project Kick-Off Call** - Demonstrate you're on top of the project

### 2. CLIENT VOICE EXTRACTION INTERVIEW FRAMEWORK

#### The Three-Call Voice Extraction Framework

**Call One: Business Foundation and Offer Clarity (45-60 minutes)**
- Core offer: What they sell, how delivered, differentiation
- Pricing structure: Actual numbers, payment terms, what's included
- Target audience specifics: Not "B2B SaaS founders" but "Series A SaaS founders with 10-50 employees who just hired their first VP of Sales"
- Product/service details: Mechanics of delivery, common objections, client misunderstandings
- Results and proof: Specific client outcomes, case study details, metrics tracked

**Call Two: Personal Narrative Extraction**
- Origin story and pivotal moments
- Personal journey to current position
- Values and beliefs that drive decisions
- Personal anecdotes and stories they tell frequently

**Call Three: Industry Positioning and Authority**
- Contrarian opinions about their industry
- What they believe that peers would disagree with
- What people oversimplify that annoys them
- What they would never say publicly, even if it performed well

#### Voice of Executive (VoE) Document Components

- **Phrases they use and avoid** - Tone slider preferences (direct vs diplomatic, witty vs formal)
- **10 non-negotiables** - Topics, claims, and boundaries they won't cross
- **Story library** - Pivotal moments, contrarian stances, repeated lessons
- **Speech patterns** - Sentence structure, transition preferences, question patterns
- **Favorite words and phrases** - Vocabulary that feels authentically them

#### Alternative Voice Capture Techniques

**10-Minute Story Calls (High-Impact Technique)**
- Replace questionnaires with short recorded conversations
- Ask story-based questions:
  - "What client conversation this week stuck with you?"
  - "What's something people in this industry believe that you think is wrong?"
  - "When was the last time we could've taken on more clients but didn't? Walk me through that decision."
- Total executive time: Under 20 minutes
- Draft using their actual language and stories
- Share in Slack with: "Does this sound like you?"

**Strong Opinions, Weakly Held Interview**
1. What's a belief you hold about your industry that most peers would disagree with?
2. What do people oversimplify that annoys you?
3. What would you never say publicly, even if it performed well?

**Monthly Voice Interview (15-minute format)**
- What they shipped this month
- What surprised them
- One opinion they're willing to stand behind
- One mistake they made
- One metric that matters

### 3. FRICTIONLESS CONTENT APPROVAL WORKFLOWS

#### The Slack-First Approval Workflow

1. **15-minute recorded conversation** (Zoom or phone)
2. **Draft using actual language and stories**
3. **Share in Slack** with one question: "Does this sound like you?"
4. **Quick tweaks or clarifications**
5. **Publish within 24 hours**

**Why this works:**
- Executives aren't staring at blank documents
- They're not writing—they're talking about work they already do
- Informal Slack review removes friction of formal approval cycle
- Reduced from 3 rounds of review to 1

#### The Greenlight Checklist Approach

- One-page document asking what must stay verbatim
- What can be edited
- Approvals get faster because leader recognizes themselves on the page

#### Batch Drafting for Consistency

- Draft 5-10 pieces at once to maintain tonal consistency
- Reduces context switching
- Single approval pass focused on accuracy and emphasis, not rewriting

#### Decision & Rationale Intake Technique

Instead of: "What are your thoughts on X?"
Ask: "Tell me about a difficult decision you made about X, what the alternatives were, and why you picked the path you did?"

This captures actual stories and mental models, substantially reducing revision cycles.

### 5. WEEKLY/MONTHLY CLIENT COMMUNICATION PROCESS

#### The Monday Morning Email System (Agency SOP)

Every Monday before 9am, send each active client:
- What's happening with their project this week
- When to expect contact or delivery
- Any changes or blockers
- What you need from them

**Result:** 80% reduction in "just checking in" queries

#### Weekly Update Email Template

**Subject:** Weekly Project Update: [Project Name]

Hi [Client's Name],

I hope this email finds you well. Here's a quick update on [Project Name]:

**1. Completed Tasks:** This week, we successfully completed [Task 1], [Task 2], and [Task 3].

**2. Current Status:** We are currently working on [Current Task] and are on track to complete it by [Date].

**3. Next Steps:** Our next focus will be [Next Task], which we plan to start on [Date].

Please feel free to reach out if you have any questions.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

#### Monthly Strategy Sync

- Refine content pillars
- Address business goals
- Review analytics and performance
- Adjust strategy based on results

#### Regular Communication Cadence (Book Projects)

- "Regular" generally means a few times a week via email, phone, or Zoom
- Send emails asking for background material
- Request names of outside sources
- Watch for marketing and publicity opportunities
- Suggest ways to maximize book (audiobook, bonus guide, workbook)

#### Standing Weekly Async Update

- One Loom or summary email
- Prevents meetings from eating calendar
- Includes: progress, blockers, needs from client

### 6. HANDLING CLIENT EDITS, REVISIONS, AND FEEDBACK LOOPS

#### Setting Clear Revision Expectations Upfront

**Contract must specify:**
- Number of revision rounds included: Typically 2-3 rounds for book projects
- What constitutes a "round": One complete pass through manuscript with consolidated feedback
- Timeline for each round: Client provides feedback within X days, you deliver revisions within Y days
- Cost of additional revisions: Hourly rate ($150-300/hour) beyond included rounds
- Scope limitations: Revisions mean improving existing manuscript, not rewriting from scratch

#### The 9 Revision Strategies

**Strategy 1: Set Clear Expectations Upfront**
Document everything in contract before work begins.

**Strategy 2: Request Consolidated Feedback**
"Please review the complete manuscript and provide all feedback in one consolidated document within 14 days. Feedback in batches makes it difficult to maintain consistency."

**Strategy 3: Differentiate Between Revision Types**
- **Type 1: Factual Corrections** - Straightforward fixes, make immediately
- **Type 2: Clarity Improvements** - Valid feedback requiring rewriting
- **Type 3: Preference Changes** - Subjective style, accommodate reasonable requests
- **Type 4: Scope Changes** - Require additional payment or trade-offs

**Strategy 4: Explain Your Reasoning, Then Execute**
When clients request changes that would harm the manuscript:
1. Acknowledge their concern
2. Explain the craft reason
3. Offer a compromise
4. Document if they insist against your advice

**Strategy 5: Use the "Feedback Call"**
- Schedule 30-60 minute call after receiving written feedback
- Clarify vague comments
- Walk through sections together
- Prevents misinterpretation

**Strategy 6: Revise in Passes, Not Randomly**
- Pass 1: Structural/Major Content Changes
- Pass 2: Voice and Style
- Pass 3: Clarity and Detail
- Pass 4: Line Edits and Polish

**Strategy 7: Show Progress to Maintain Confidence**
Brief update every 7-10 days during revisions.

**Strategy 8: Mark What You've Changed**
Include revision summary document:
- Major Changes
- Voice/Style Changes
- Detail/Clarity Improvements

**Strategy 9: Know When to Fire a Client**
Red flags:
- Unlimited scope creep
- Contradictory feedback
- Disrespectful communication
- Non-payment
- Paralysis (can't make decisions)

Professional exit: "I don't think I'm the right fit to deliver what you're looking for. I recommend we pause here."

#### Managing Difficult Feedback

**When feedback is vague:**
- Don't guess
- Hop on a call for line-by-line walkthrough
- Client often just needs to talk it through

**When feedback feels like a personal attack:**
- Step away from keyboard
- Do something physical (rage run)
- Vent to friend/fellow writer
- Give yourself 24 hours to cool down
- Then respond professionally

**When feedback is excessive:**
- Go back to approved brief
- Does copy match what was agreed upon?
- Has something changed in their business?
- If scope change: "Let's talk about how adjusting scope impacts project"

#### The Golden Rule of Feedback Management

"It's your responsibility to tell the client what you recommend, but ultimately it's their decision."

Avoid:
1. Being too reactive (doing everything client wants)
2. Not meeting client halfway (stubbornly sticking to your guns)

### 7. CONTRACT STRUCTURES AND PRICING MODELS

#### Three Primary Pricing Structures

**1. Flat-Rate, Per Project**
- Books: $25,000-$75,000
- Blog posts: $500-$2,000 per post
- White papers: $3,000-$10,000

**2. Monthly Rate, Per Recurring Service**
- LinkedIn ghostwriting: $3,000-$7,000/month
- Newsletter ghostwriting: $2,000-$5,000/month
- Executive content retainer: $5,000-$15,000/month
- Bill in full at beginning of month

**3. Milestone-Based Payment (Most Common for Books)**
- 25% deposit upon signing
- 25% at outline approval
- 25% at first draft delivery
- 25% at final approval

#### Retainer Models for Executives

- Monthly rate for ongoing content (blogs, articles, LinkedIn posts)
- Can include one book per year
- Provides predictable income for writer
- Provides consistent content for executive

#### Payment Best Practices

- Deposits: 25-50% upfront
- Late fees: 1.5% per month
- Work does not begin until payment received
- For large companies: Accept Net-30 terms professionally

## (b) TOP 5 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Voice Extraction is a Systematic Process, Not Osmosis** - The most successful ghostwriters don't wait months to "absorb" client voice. They use structured 3-call frameworks (Business Foundation → Personal Narrative → Industry Positioning) combined with Voice of Executive (VoE) documents to capture 80% of voice in the first week, not months of trial and error.

2. **Approval Friction is the #1 Bottleneck** - Top agencies have replaced formal document reviews with Slack-based approvals and 10-15 minute story calls. The workflow: record conversation → draft using actual language → Slack share with "Does this sound like you?" → publish within 24 hours. Total executive time: under 20 minutes.

3. **Clear Revision Boundaries Prevent Scope Creep** - Professional ghostwriters specify 2-3 revision rounds in contracts, define what constitutes a "round" (one consolidated pass), and charge $150-300/hour for additional work. They differentiate between factual corrections (immediate), clarity improvements (legitimate), preference changes (negotiable), and scope changes (additional payment).

4. **The Monday Morning Email Eliminates 80% of Status Queries** - Agencies that send a brief weekly update (what's happening, when to expect delivery, any blockers, what we need from you) before 9am every Monday see dramatic reductions in "just checking in" emails. This simple SOP transforms client relationships.

5. **Payment Structure Signals Professionalism** - Premium ghostwriters require payment before work begins, use milestone-based structures (25% at deposit, outline, first draft, final), and accept Net-30 terms gracefully for enterprise clients. They never start work without payment and follow up professionally until money hits the bank.

## (c) ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Implement the Three-Call Voice Extraction Framework** - Schedule Business Foundation (60 min), Personal Narrative (45 min), and Industry Positioning (45 min) calls in the first week. Create a Voice of Executive document after each call documenting phrases, non-negotiables, and story library.

2. **Replace Questionnaires with 10-Minute Story Calls** - Stop sending intake forms. Instead, ask: "What client conversation this week stuck with you?" and "What's something people in your industry believe that you think is wrong?" Record, transcribe, and draft from actual language.

3. **Create a Standardized Onboarding Package** - Include: (a) Welcome email with invoice and Loom walkthrough, (b) Project dashboard in Notion/ClickUp, (c) NDA and SOW for signature, (d) Kick-off call agenda, (e) First 3-5 content ideas ready to discuss.

4. **Establish Revision Boundaries in Every Contract** - Specify: 2-3 rounds included, what defines a round, 14-day feedback window, $150-300/hour for additional work, and scope limitations. Never begin work without signed agreement.

5. **Implement the Monday Morning Email SOP** - Before 9am every Monday, send each active client: (a) What's happening this week, (b) When to expect contact/delivery, (c) Any changes or blockers, (d) What you need from them. Track the reduction in status queries.

6. **Build Your Tech Stack Around These Core Tools** - Notion (CRM + content storage), Google Docs (collaborative drafting), Loom (async communication), Slack (quick approvals), Fathom (meeting notes), and either Buffer/Hootsuite (scheduling) or GhostSync (all-in-one ghostwriting platform).

7. **Create a Revision Summary Template** - For every revision round delivered, include: (a) Major Changes (structural), (b) Voice/Style Changes, (c) Detail/Clarity Improvements. This shows systematic work and focuses client attention on changed sections.

8. **Use Milestone-Based Payments for Books** - Structure as 25% deposit, 25% at outline approval, 25% at first draft, 25% at final approval. For recurring services, bill monthly retainers ($3,000-$7,000) in full at the beginning of each month.

9. **Document SOPs Using the "Asked Twice" Rule** - When someone asks how to do something, note it. When asked again, record a 5-minute Loom video. Create written checklists for reference during tasks. Start with client onboarding, project delivery, and invoicing.

10. **Know Your Exit Strategy** - When red flags appear (scope creep, contradictory feedback, disrespect, non-payment, paralysis), have a professional exit script ready: "I don't think I'm the right fit to deliver what you're looking for. I recommend we pause here."
