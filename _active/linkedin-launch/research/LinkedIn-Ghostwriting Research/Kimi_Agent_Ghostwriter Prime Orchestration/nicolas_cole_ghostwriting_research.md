### 3. "LEAKS & FAUCETS" CONTENT STRATEGY

**Definition:**
- **Leaks:** People you know you can help (direct connections)
- **Faucets:** People who know someone else you can help (referral sources)

**5-Step Client Acquisition Framework:**
1. **Say exactly what you do in your bio** - Clear service + target audience
2. **Be your own case study** - Build proof by doing it for yourself first
3. **Make a list of "Leaks" & "Faucets"** - Tap into existing network
4. **Pitch In Public** - Demonstrate expertise publicly
5. **Follow up (till they call the cops!)** - Persistent follow-up

**Application:**
This strategy helps ghostwriters identify immediate opportunities sitting "right under your nose" rather than chasing cold leads.

### 5. CLIENT ONBOARDING PROCESS - 5-Step Checklist

**Step 1: Express Excitement & Recap**
- Send DM/email/call expressing enthusiasm
- Recap everything you'll do
- Tell them you're sending an invoice
- Ask about including assistant/finance team
- Subtly mention: "as soon as the invoice is paid, I'll get rocking & rolling!"

**Step 2: Send Invoice via Stripe**
- Payment terms:
  - Under $5,000: Charge in full upfront
  - Above $5,000: Can split 50% upfront, 50% on completion
  - Monthly retainers: Paid in full at beginning of cycle
- **Golden Rule:** "Work does not begin until payment is received"
- Exception: Large companies with accounting teams (Net-30, Net-60, Net-90)

**Step 3: Sign Contracts (if needed)**
- Under $5,000: Often no contract needed
- Google Doc with responsibilities/deliverables sufficient
- May need: Statement of work, NDA, W-9 form

**Step 4: Project Kick-Off Call**
- Ask if they have questions about project plan
- Ask for any content ideas they have top of mind
- **Come prepared with first 3-5 content ideas**
- Position as Premium Ghostwriter, not commodity freelancer

**Step 5: Begin Content Extraction Process**
- Schedule regular interview calls
- Use structured questioning framework

### 6. VOICE EXTRACTION QUESTIONNAIRE SYSTEMS

**The 5 Archetypes of Client Voices:**

**1. The Storyteller**
- Loves dates, times, locations, real-life examples
- Phrases: "In 1999...", "The first time I...", "A few years ago..."
- Writing technique: Use two 1/3/1 blocks (1 sentence open, 3 sentences description, 1 sentence close)

**2. The Opinionator**
- Emphasizes strong opinions
- Wants to prove their point
- Phrases: "There's a reason why...", "It's unbelievable how...", "It's abundantly clear that..."
- Writing technique: Use adverbs (really, rarely, honestly) for emphasis

**3. The Fact Presenter**
- Backs up opinions with stats/research
- Tends to be academic (formally or aspirationally)
- Avoids "fluff" - wants data-backed claims
- **Golden Rule:** One source per section

**4. The Frameworker**
- Gives actionable "how-tos"
- Creates organized, step-by-step content
- Subtitles: "A Proven Process...", "A Timeless Framework...", "A Simple Checklist..."
- Writing technique: Name and claim frameworks ("The Endless Idea Generator")

**5. The F-Bomber**
- Abrasive, pushes boundaries
- Uses cursing, ranting, sarcasm
- Writing technique: Add 1 expletive per section/paragraph

**Voice Matching Pro Tip:**
In the first draft, give them ALL FIVE elements (story, opinion, study, framework, abrasive rant), then see which they like/dislike and adjust.

### 7. CONTENT EXTRACTION METHOD - Client Interview Framework

**The Ghostwriter's Three Roles:**
1. The Interviewer
2. The Writer
3. The Editor

**Content Puzzle Pieces to Extract:**
- A personal story
- A strong opinion
- A relevant study, trend, or data point
- A Named & Claimed framework

**5 Questions Clients Love Answering:**
1. "What was it like the first time you...?"
2. (Additional questions implied but not fully documented in search results)

**Interview Best Practices:**
- **Don't ask:** "What ideas do you have?" (they won't know)
- **Do:** Show up with ideas that would perform well
- Guide them - you're the expert
- Winning ideas = overlap between what client knows and what audience finds interesting
- **Dial in one idea at a time** - Don't cram 10 ideas into one piece
- Focus on ONE thing for 30 minutes
- Put unrelated items in a "later list" folder

**Pro Tip on Client Calls:**
Many clients start looking forward to calls because they view it as "showing up to the gym" - the one time they practice articulating their thinking.

## (b) TOP 5 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Voice Archetype System is the Secret Weapon** - Cole's 5 Archetypes (Storyteller, Opinionator, Fact Presenter, Frameworker, F-Bomber) allow ghostwriters to categorize ANY client and write in their style immediately. The pro move is including ALL FIVE in the first draft, then letting the client reveal their preference through edits.

2. **"Leaks & Faucets" is a Network-First Strategy** - Instead of cold outreach to strangers, Cole teaches mining your existing network first: "Leaks" are people you can directly help; "Faucets" are people who can refer you. Most ghostwriters ignore immediate opportunities sitting "right under their nose."

3. **The Free Work Pitch Reverses Traditional Sales** - Rather than pitching services, Cole's method involves DOING the work first (rewriting a post, creating a newsletter) then showing the prospect. This demonstrates value before asking for money and eliminates the "salesy" feeling.

4. **Payment-Before-Work is Non-Negotiable** - Cole's golden rule: "Work does not begin until payment is received." This positioning separates Premium Ghostwriters from commodity freelancers and protects against non-payment.

5. **Content Extraction is an Interview Skill** - The ghostwriter's job isn't to ask "What ideas do you have?" but to GUIDE the client with prepared questions. The goal is extracting: personal stories, strong opinions, data points, and named frameworks - then organizing their thinking for them.

## (c) ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Implement the 5 Archetypes Voice System** - Create a template document with writing examples for each archetype. For every new client, write one piece incorporating ALL FIVE styles, then refine based on their feedback.

2. **Build Your "Leaks & Faucets" List** - Spend 30 minutes listing: 20 people you could directly help (Leaks) and 20 people who might know someone you could help (Faucets). This is your immediate prospect pool.

3. **Create a Free Work Pitch Template** - Develop 3 "before/after" examples in your niche. When reaching out, include specific feedback + a rewritten sample. Use Loom videos for higher impact.

4. **Establish Payment-First Onboarding** - Set up Stripe for invoicing. Create a standard onboarding email template that sends invoice + project dashboard together. Never start work before payment clears.

5. **Develop the Content Extraction Interview Framework** - Create a standard set of 10 questions for client interviews. Record all calls. Use AI prompts to transcribe and extract key insights, stories, and frameworks.

6. **Build a Voice Matching Prompt Library** - Use Cole's approach: feed AI examples of client's writing and ask it to analyze word choice, sentence structure, pacing, and rhythm. Create a reusable prompt that outputs a "voice profile."

7. **Create a 30-Day Implementation Roadmap** - Map out exactly what you'll do each day for 30 days: Week 1 (setup, portfolio), Week 2 (outreach), Week 3 (free work pitches), Week 4 (follow-up and closing).

8. **Implement the Upsell Prompt System** - For every current client, use AI to identify 3+ additional projects you could offer. Create a standard upsell email template.

9. **Use the 1/3/1 Story Structure for Storyteller Clients** - Practice this format: 1 sentence opening, 3 sentences of description, 1 sentence closing. Master this for immediate improvement in storytelling content.

10. **Join or Build a Ghostwriting Accountability Group** - Cole emphasizes community and mentorship. Find 3-5 other ghostwriters to share wins, troubleshoot challenges, and hold each other accountable.
