5. THE 4A FRAMEWORK
---

FRAMEWORK: "The 4A Framework"
Purpose: Write the same idea in 4 different ways

Most writers don't realize you can write every idea in 4 different ways:

1. ACTIONABLE (Here's how)
   - Tips, Hacks, Resources, Ultimate guides
   - Help reader put idea into practice

2. ANALYTICAL (Here's a breakdown)
   - Trends, Reasons, Numbers, Examples, Swipe files, Teardowns
   - Help reader unlock new way of thinking

3. ASPIRATIONAL (Yes, you can)
   - Traits, Stories, Quotes, Lessons, Mistakes, Reflections
   - Help reader understand benefits they unlock

4. ANTHROPOLOGICAL (Here's why)
   - Fears, Failures, Struggles, Limiting beliefs, Misconceptions
   - Create urgency to embrace the idea

EXAMPLE - Personal Finance:
• "I Will Teach You To Be Rich" (Ramit Sethi) = Actionable
• "The Changing World Order" (Ray Dalio) = Analytical
• "Rich Dad Poor Dad" (Robert Kiyosaki) = Aspirational
• "The Psychology of Money" (Morgan Housel) = Anthropological

6. THE "FOR WHO / SO THAT" FRAMEWORK
---

FRAMEWORK: "For Who / So That"
Purpose: Beat writer's block and reach target reader

STEP 1: FOR WHO
- Choose a topic
- Ask: WHO am I writing for?
- Different audiences require different context, action steps, language

Example - "Investing Tips for..."
• Ambitious Middle Schoolers
• Startup Founders
• Retired Moms

STEP 2: SO THAT
- Ask: WHY are you writing for them?
- SO THAT they can do... what?

Example - Investing Tips for College Students:
• To Graduate Without Any Debt
• To Save Enough Money For Your First Car
• To Set You Up For Retirement By Age 65

7. THE 3-PART IRRESISTIBLE OFFER FRAMEWORK
---

FRAMEWORK: "3-Part Irresistible Offer Framework"
Cole's Result: Sold 800-word articles for $1,000 vs Upwork writers at $100

PART 1: Niche down until you have an Information Advantage
- Solve A Different Problem (specific problem you help solve)
- For A Different Person (specific industry/role)
- In A Different Way (your unique method)

PART 2: The Problems and The Solutions
Problem Narrative:
Specific Problem → Reason Why They Have It → Consequence of Not Solving → Ultimate Negative Outcome

Solution Narrative:
Specific Solution → Reason Why It Works → Benefit of Solution → Ultimate Positive Outcome

PART 3: Make the trade explicit
3 "Trades" customers want:
1. Insane value for minimal relative cost
2. Urgent & painful problem solved for minimal effort
3. Highly desirable outcome, generated instantly

8. THE 4-STEP FORMATTING FRAMEWORK
---

FRAMEWORK: "4-Step Formatting Framework"
Purpose: 10x your writing without spending hours editing

STEP 1: Start every section with a single-sentence opener
6 Most Effective Openers:
• Open with a moment in time
• Open with a vulnerable statement
• Open with a controversial opinion
• Open with a weird, unique insight
• Open with a thought-provoking question
• Open with 1 strong, declarative sentence

STEP 2: Turn long paragraphs into bulleted lists
- If listing anything → bullet list
- If rambling examples → bullet list
- If making distinct points → bullet list

STEP 3: Use subheads
- Bolded sentences as "milestones"
- Help reader follow your thinking
- Split page evenly, give clarity

STEP 4: Use the 1/3/1 writing rhythm
- 1 sentence (candy) → 3 sentences (value) → 1 sentence (candy)
- Variations: 1/4/1, 1/5/1, 1/2/5/2/1
- Key: Alternating sentence/section length for skimmability
