### 5. FREE WORK PITCH FRAMEWORK

**Core Philosophy:**
- Free work is the ultimate stress test for your skills
- If you can't make someone over-the-moon happy FOR FREE, you'll never make them happy for money
- Stop trying to "monetize" and focus on getting better - money follows

**The Framework:**
1. **Identify a specific problem** the prospect has
2. **Educate them** on the problem (don't be salesy)
3. **Demonstrate the solution** through free work
4. **Show the contrast** between what they're doing and what they COULD be doing

**Why It Works:**
- Nearly infinite upside
- No risk for the client
- Proves you can deliver results
- Either you learn what to improve OR you prove yourself

**Cole's Rule:** "Free work never goes away. To this day, I do work for free."

**Reasons for Free Work (Even When Established):**
- Access to their network
- Mentorship from them
- Cash in a favor in the future
- Some things are more valuable than upfront fees

### 6. PRICING CONVERSATION SCRIPTS & PREMIUM RATE JUSTIFICATION

**The Pricing Reframe:**
- Freelance writers charge per word or per hour
- Premium Ghostwriters charge per project or outcome
- "Want to guess which one makes you more $$$?"

**Charging by Project vs. Time:**
- Example: Instead of $30/hour for emails, charge $5,000 for an Educational Email Course
- Customer gets: Automated sequence that runs forever, sales-generating asset, your honed skills for a one-off fee
- As you get more proficient, do it in half the time and still charge the same
- Decouple yourself from charging for time

**You're Charging For:**
- Pattern recognition
- Experience
- NOT the hours you work

**Handling Price Objections:**

**The Objection:** "This is too expensive, what you're proposing isn't worth it."

**What they're really doing:** Asking you to defend your pricing

**The 2-Step Response:**
1. Don't immediately lower your price or get defensive
2. Do napkin math to help them understand ROI:
   - Find the price of their product/service
   - Work out how many they'd need to sell to get their investment back
   - Explain how the asset helps them sell more

**The "Whoever Speaks First, Loses" Rule:**
- After stating your price, let the silence hang
- Don't offer a discount before they respond
- Most people wait 48-72 hours then decide to work with you

**4 Options When They "Can't Afford" You:**
1. 50% off in exchange for testimonial or referral
2. Free in exchange for network access or value
3. Payment plan (useful for startups raising funding)
4. Revenue share deal (only with trusted people where service can drive sales)

**Justifying Premium Rates:**
- Frame the problem → reason → consequence → ultimate negative outcome
- Frame the solution → reason it works → benefit → ultimate positive outcome
- Show what happens if they DON'T solve the problem
- Show what happens when they DO

## (b) TOP 5 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Free Work is the Ultimate Stress Test** - Cole's most powerful insight: "If you can't make someone over-the-moon happy FOR FREE, you're never going to be able to make them happy in exchange for money." Free work removes risk for clients while proving your capabilities.

2. **Charge for Outcomes, Not Time** - The fundamental reframe: Premium ghostwriters charge per project/outcome, not per word or hour. This decouples your income from time worked and allows you to charge $5,000+ for assets that take weeks, not months.

3. **The "Whoever Speaks First, Loses" Rule** - When handling price objections, state your price and stay silent. Don't discount prematurely. Most prospects who say "let me think about it" convert within 48-72 hours if you don't undermine yourself.

4. **Comments As Consulting Beats Cold Pitching** - Instead of sending 100 DMs pitching services, engage meaningfully on prospects' content. Demonstrate expertise publicly, build trust, and let clients come to you.

5. **The Rule of Ones for Niching** - Solve 1 specific problem for 1 specific person in 1 specific way. Ultra-specificity makes you the obvious choice and allows you to charge premium rates because you're the specialist, not a generalist.

## (c) ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Create Your Own Case Study First** - Before pitching clients, build the asset you plan to sell (Educational Email Course, newsletter, etc.) for yourself. Prove it works, then use it as your portfolio.

2. **Send 3 Free Consulting Loom Videos Per Week** - Identify prospects with clear problems, record 3-minute videos showing how you'd solve them, and send with no pitch attached. This is how Cole's student landed a $5,000 client.

3. **Rewrite Your Bio Using the 2-Part Formula** - State exactly: (a) The service you provide, and (b) The person you provide it to. Example: "I ghostwrite Educational Email Courses for HR Tech startups."

4. **Implement the "Leaks & Faucets" Exercise** - Make two lists: People you know you can help (leaks) and people who know people you can help (faucets). Start with warm outreach to leaks first.

5. **Use the Irresistible Offer Framework on Sales Calls** - For every prospect, map: Problem → Reason → Consequence → Ultimate Negative Outcome, then Solution → Reason → Benefit → Ultimate Positive Outcome.

6. **Practice the 80/20 Time Split** - Dedicate 80% of work time to existing clients, 20% to premium outreach. This ensures consistent income while building pipeline.

7. **Apply the Rule of Ones** - Narrow your niche until you have: 1 problem you solve, 1 type of person you help, 1 unique way you do it. Say no to everything else.

8. **Handle Price Objections with Napkin Math** - When prospects say "too expensive," calculate their ROI: How many sales to break even? How does the asset help them sell more? Make the math undeniable.

9. **Do 1-2 Free Projects Before Charging** - If you're new, complete 1-2 projects for free to build confidence, get testimonials, and prove your process works.

10. **Use the 3-Prompt DM System** - Structure outreach using: Connection Request → Rapport Building → Free Consulting. Never lead with your services or rates.
