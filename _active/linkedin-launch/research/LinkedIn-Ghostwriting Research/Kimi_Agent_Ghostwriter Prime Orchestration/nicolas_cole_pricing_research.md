### 2. STARTER, MID-TIER, AND PREMIUM PACKAGE STRUCTURES

**Tier 1: Below $3,000/month**

- Newsletter Ghostwriting

**Tier 3: Above $7,000/month (Premium)**

- **Educational Email Course (EEC):** $5,000 flat rate (recommended starting point)

- **Website Copy:** Flat-rate per project
- **Automated Email Sequences:** Flat-rate per project
- **eBooks/Books:** Flat-rate per project

**Pricing Structure Options:**
1. **Flat-Rate, Per Project:** Best for assets with clear endpoints
2. **Monthly Rate, Per Recurring Service:** Best for ongoing work
3. **Up-Front Cash + Performance Upside:** For high-level deals (e.g., $350k up-front + 1% royalty)

### 5. JUSTIFYING PREMIUM RATES WITHOUT A PORTFOLIO

**Cole's 3-Step Process:**

**Step 1: Do Free Work for Potential Clients**
- Nearly infinite upside
- No risk for client
- Showcases your skills
- If work flops, you know what to improve
- If work succeeds, you've proven your value

**Step 2: Write About the Industry You Ghostwrite In**
- Talk about trends, frameworks, insider knowledge
- Part of warm content strategy
- Potential clients immediately see your expertise
- Most obvious but overlooked method

**Step 3: Create the Asset You Sell (Be Your Own Case Study)**
- Actively create and demonstrate power of assets
- Point to YOUR asset and prove it works
- Instantly removes need for portfolio
- Shows real-world results

**Handling Testimonial Requests:**
- Response: "Because of the nature of ghostwriting, I can't share specific names with you—however I can tell you that I've ghostwritten for [relevant experience like: some of the largest enterprise technology companies in the world.]"
- Badge of authority satisfies client concerns
- Most clients are accommodating

**Key Quote:** "You are your own case study. So, you should be actively creating and demonstrating the power of the assets you ghostwrite for clients."

### 6. PHILOSOPHY: WHY RAISING PRICES ATTRACTS BETTER CLIENTS

**The Ferrari Pricing Principle:**
- If you offer a Ferrari for $500, people don't think "What a steal!"
- They think: "What's wrong with it?"
- Making something less expensive signals lower quality
- Higher prices signal higher value

**Faulty Beliefs Holding Writers Back:**
1. "I'm afraid the client is going to say 'No.'"
2. "I'm afraid the client is going to get upset."
3. "I'm afraid I'm not worth what I'm asking for."
- All self-inflicted, all wrong

**The "Avoid the Middle" Rule:**
- Making it less expensive won't make people buy
- The number you pick says tremendous amount about your business
- Clients assume you're not the best if you're cheap
- Why would they settle for less than the best?

**Value-Based Reframe:**
- "You're charging for your pattern recognition and experience, not the hours you work."
- Clients care about outcomes, not hours worked
- Higher-level decision-makers play a different game
- They're concerned about outcomes, not time/effort

**The ROI Conversation:**
When facing price objections:
1. Don't immediately lower price or get defensive
2. Do napkin math to help client understand ROI
3. Find price of their product/service
4. Work out how many they'd need to sell to get investment back
5. Explain how the asset helps them sell more

**Confidence Formula:**
- Confidence comes from understanding the value of your value
- Not about proving yourself
- About knowing what you have and why it's valuable
- Being happy to share if compensated appropriately

**The "What Would I Need to Do" Question:**
- Instead of "I can't charge this much," ask "What would I need to deliver to charge this amount?"
- Make list of everything client would want
- Price point rationalizes itself

## (b) TOP 5 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Decouple From Time-Based Pricing:** Cole's core philosophy is to stop charging per hour or per word immediately. The 5-level Ghostwriter Income Staircase shows the path from commodity pricing ($0.01/word) to premium outcome-based pricing ($5,000+ per asset). The key is charging for pattern recognition and experience, not hours worked.

2. **The "Avoid the Middle" Pricing Psychology:** Lower prices signal lower quality. Cole's Ferrari analogy demonstrates that premium pricing actually attracts better clients who value quality, while cheap pricing repels serious buyers who assume something is wrong. Raising prices filters for clients who understand value.

3. **The Hybrid Model is the Future:** Cole's $15M empire is built on combining free content → low-ticket digital products → high-ticket services → premium programs. The Educational Email Course (EEC) is the bridge asset that converts social traffic to owned channels and generates $1M+/year on autopilot.

4. **You Are Your Own Portfolio:** Cole's method for justifying premium rates without testimonials is to become your own case study. Create the assets you sell, demonstrate they work in the real world, and do free work strategically to prove value. Testimonials are less important than most writers believe.

5. **Packaging Beats Raw Skills:** Cole scaled from $10k to $180k/month not by becoming a better writer, but by mastering the "3 Ps": Packaging (bundling outcomes, not tasks), Pricing (value-based, not time-based), and Positioning (agency vs. freelancer). The same skills, packaged differently, command 10-50x higher rates.

## (c) ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Immediately Stop Charging Per Hour/Word:** Audit your current pricing model. If you're charging by time or word count, restructure to project-based or outcome-based pricing. Use Cole's formula: "What would I need to deliver to charge [target amount]?" and build your package around that deliverable list.

2. **Create Your First Educational Email Course:** Build a 5-day EEC as your primary lead magnet and service offering. Price it at $5,000 (the recommended starting point). This single asset can serve as both your marketing engine AND your primary service offering.

3. **Implement the "Avoid the Middle" Rule:** Raise your prices by 2x-3x immediately. If you're charging $1,000/month, move to $3,000/month. If you're at $3,000/month, test $5,000-7,000/month. Use the Ferrari principle: higher prices signal higher value and attract better clients.

4. **Build Your Hybrid Business Model:** Map out your funnel: Free content (social) → Free EEC (lead magnet) → Low-ticket offer ($350-800) → High-ticket service ($3k-7k/month) → Premium program ($5k+). Start with the asset you can create fastest.

5. **Become Your Own Case Study:** Stop waiting for portfolio pieces. Create the exact asset you want to sell for clients, prove it works with your own audience, then use those results to sell to clients. Your own success is the only portfolio you need.

6. **Master the ROI Conversation:** When facing price objections, never discount immediately. Instead, do napkin math: Calculate how many units of their product they need to sell to recoup their investment in you. Frame your fee as an investment with measurable return.

7. **Package Everything as Outcomes:** Rename your services using Cole's formula: [Problem/Person/Way] + [Desirable Outcome] + "Package." Example: "LinkedIn Thought Leadership Package" or "Newsletter Autopilot Package." Sell outcomes, not tasks.

8. **Position as Ghostwriter, Not Freelancer:** The words you use matter. "Freelance writer" signals commodity, stress, and low pay. "Ghostwriter" signals specialization, premium positioning, and working with industry leaders. Change your title today.
