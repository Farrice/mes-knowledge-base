### Week 8: Deliver First Month & Gather Proof

**The Voice Extraction Process**

**Three-Call Framework (First Week):**

| Call | Focus | Duration | Output |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| Call 1 | Business Foundation | 60 min | Core offer, audience, differentiation |
| Call 2 | Personal Narrative | 45 min | Origin story, values, anecdotes |
| Call 3 | Industry Positioning | 45 min | Contrarian views, authority angles |

**Voice of Executive (VoE) Document:**
- Phrases they use and avoid
- 10 non-negotiables (topics/boundaries)
- Story library (pivotal moments)
- Speech patterns and favorite words

**Content Creation Workflow:**
1. Interview client (record all calls)
2. Transcribe using Fathom or Otter.ai
3. Extract key insights and stories
4. Draft posts using AI + voice guide
5. Human edit for flow and authenticity
6. Client approval via Slack (not email)
7. Schedule and publish

**Action Steps:**

3. [ ] Draft first 5 posts
4. [ ] Get client approval
5. [ ] Schedule posts for Week 9-10

## PHASE 3: WEEKS 9-12 — SYSTEMIZE & SCALE

### Week 9: Build AI-Assisted Workflows

**The AI Ghostwriting Workflow**

**Step 1: Interview Your Client**
- 1-2 hour interviews per month
- Ask about: Stories, insights, lessons learned
- Record everything

**Step 2: Transcribe & Analyze**
- Upload to transcription tool (Read.ai, Otter.ai)
- AI analyzes: Wording, tonality, style
- Create ghostwriting style guide

**Step 3: Generate Content**
- Upload transcript to Claude
- Use voice-matching prompts
- Create 70% of content via AI
- Human touch-up for final 30%

**Voice Matching Prompt Template:**
```
You are a ghostwriter capturing [CLIENT NAME]'s voice.

VOICE RULES:
- [Rule 1 from VoE document]
- [Rule 2 from VoE document]
- [Rule 3 from VoE document]

SAMPLE PHRASES:
- "[Phrase 1]"
- "[Phrase 2]"
- "[Phrase 3]"

TRANSCRIPT INSIGHTS:
[Paste key sections from interview]

TASK: Write a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC] in this voice.
```

**Action Steps:**

## (b) TOP 5 KEY INSIGHTS

### Insight #1: "Free Work" Is Actually "Free Consulting" — And It's The Fastest Path

The most successful ghostwriters all started with free work, but the key is positioning. Frame it as "free consulting" or "education" rather than desperation. As Nicolas Cole notes: "Free work is the ultimate stress test for your skills. If you can't make someone over-the-moon happy, FOR FREE, you're never going to be able to make them happy in exchange for money."

**Action:** Offer to rewrite ONE post for prospects before asking for payment.

### Insight #2: You Don't Need a Portfolio — You Need ONE Working Asset

Every case study shows the same pattern: create the asset you want to sell FOR YOURSELF first. Marcos built his own Twitter following. Flor built her own Educational Email Course. Dakota Robertson became his own first client. When you can point to a working asset, you don't need testimonials or a portfolio.

**Action:** Build your own LinkedIn presence as your case study.

### Insight #3: The "Rule of Ones" — Hyper-Specificity Beats General Skills

All successful zero-to-one ghostwriters picked ONE type of client, ONE type of writing, ONE problem to solve. "I ghostwrite newsletters for fractional CMOs at tech startups" beats "I'm a ghostwriter." Specificity creates perceived expertise instantly.

**Action:** Define your "Rule of Ones" before doing any outreach.

### Insight #4: Voice Extraction Is a Systematic Process, Not Osmosis

The most successful ghostwriting operations treat voice as data collected upfront through structured analysis of client samples, not something "edited in" later. This requires 10-20 writing samples, AI-assisted voice autopsy, and documented voice rules that all writers must follow.

**Action:** Use the 3-call voice extraction framework for every new client.

### Insight #5: Higher Prices Signal Quality and Actually Increase Conversion in B2B Services

The Veblen effect is real—enterprise buyers view lower prices as red flags. Zendesk's 10x price increase for enterprise plans resulted in INCREASED demand because buyers associated higher prices with reliability and performance.

**Action:** Don't compete on price. Compete on value and outcomes.
