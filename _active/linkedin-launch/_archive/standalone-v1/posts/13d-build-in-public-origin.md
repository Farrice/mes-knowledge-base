# Post 13D: "Build In Public — The Origin Story"
**CTA Keyword**: N/A (journey post) | **Format**: Text post | **Post time**: Week 3-4, after trust is built
**Series**: Build In Public (4 of 4) | **Prompt Kit**: `prompt-kits/13-build-in-public-toolkit.md`

---

7:15am. My wife walks out the door.

I'm standing in the kitchen
with a half-finished bottle,
two kids,
and a to-do list that starts with "survive today."

That was the whole identity for a while.
Stay-at-home dad.
Not because I chose it lightly --
because it consumed everything.

Every hour. Every decision.
Every ounce of creative energy
I used to pour into building things.

I didn't resent it.
But I missed MAKING things.

---

So during naps and after bedtime,
I started building.

Not a business. Systems.

I'd been fascinated with AI --
not the hype, the mechanics.
What it could actually do
pointed at a real problem.

And the problem I kept circling:

Why do the best experts
have the worst online presence?

The coach who transforms lives
but has 200 followers
and a bio that says "Passionate about leadership."

The consultant who's solved the problem
a hundred times
but can't write a post
that makes a stranger stop scrolling.

Brilliant in the room.
Invisible on the screen.

---

It's not a talent problem.
It's not laziness.
It's a translation problem.

The skills that make someone extraordinary
in a session
are completely different from the skills
that make someone visible online.

And the industry's answer?
"Post more. Be consistent.
Hire a ghostwriter who'll interview you for 30 minutes
and write content that could be about
any coach in your niche."

That's not a solution.
That's a tax on expertise.

---

So I built something different.

Instead of "what do you post about" --
I start with "how do you actually think?"

What's the question you ask
that nobody else asks?

What happens in session 3
when the real work begins?

What belief do you hold about your field
that your competitors would argue with?

That material doesn't live
in a 30-minute interview.
It lives in the way a coach's brain works.

I built tools to extract it.
Not transcribe it. Not summarize it.
TRANSLATE it.

From session language to content language.
From "how I coach"
to "what a stranger needs to read
to know this is the person
they've been looking for."

That's what I'm building.
That's who I'm building it for.

The coaches who earned their expertise
in rooms, with real people,
through thousands of hours of practice --
who deserve to be found
by the people who need them most.

Expertise shouldn't be invisible.
