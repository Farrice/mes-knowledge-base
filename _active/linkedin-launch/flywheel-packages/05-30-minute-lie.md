# IP FLYWHEEL — Package 5: "The 30-Minute Lie (Why Ghostwriting Fails)"

## THE CONTRARIAN TRUTH
**Common belief**: "A good ghostwriter interviews you for 30 minutes and captures your voice."
**Our truth**: Nobody can capture how you think in 30 minutes. That's not a skill problem — it's a math problem. Your methodology is built on 10+ years of nuance, specific stories, evolved beliefs, and a voice that's distinctly yours. A 30-minute interview produces 30-minute-deep content. Which is why it always reads like "a ghostwriter trying to be you" instead of YOU.

### Copy-paste below:

---

She fired 3 ghostwriters in 2 years.

Same pattern every time.

Ghostwriter sends questionnaire.
30-minute Zoom call.
"Tell me about your business.
Who's your ideal client?
What makes you different?"

First draft arrives.

She reads the opening:
"In today's rapidly evolving leadership landscape,
building resilience has never been more crucial."

Closes laptop.
Takes a walk.
Comes back.
Rewrites the entire thing herself.

Sends notes: "This doesn't sound like me."

Ghostwriter revises.
Second draft sounds like
the first draft wearing a different shirt.

She pays. Cancels. Tries again.
$1,800/month.
$2,200/month.
$1,400/month.

Three ghostwriters.
18 months.
Over $10,000 spent.

And not a single post
she was proud to publish under her name.

—

I heard this story so many times
that I started asking one question:

"What did the ghostwriter ask you
on the intake call?"

Every coach told me the same thing:

"The usual. Who I help.
What makes me different.
My business goals."

And I'd say: "Did they ask you
to walk them through a client session?
Did they ask what happens at minute 30
when all the small talk is gone?
Did they ask about the question you ask
that makes clients go quiet?"

No.

Not once.

Because most ghostwriters
aren't capturing how you THINK.

They're capturing what you SAY about yourself.

There's a massive difference.

—

When you describe your business to a stranger,
you give the polished version.
The elevator pitch.
The "I help X do Y so they can Z."

That's positioning language.
It's useful. But it's not content.

Content comes from a different layer.

It comes from:
→ How you actually explain things on calls
→ The story you tell in session 2
  that makes clients' eyes change
→ The question you ask that nobody else asks
→ The moment in YOUR process
  where the breakthrough typically happens
→ The belief that drives your approach
  that your competitors haven't figured out yet

A 30-minute interview doesn't touch this layer.

Not because 30 minutes isn't enough time.
Because the QUESTIONS aren't aimed deep enough.

You can spend 3 hours
asking surface-level questions
and get surface-level content.

Or you can spend 60 minutes
asking the right questions
and unlock content
that sounds like the coach on their sharpest day.

—

Here's the test.

Read the last 5 posts
your ghostwriter delivered.

Ask yourself:

"Could these have been written
for ANY coach in my niche?
Or could they ONLY have been written for me?"

If the answer is "any coach" —
the problem isn't the ghostwriter's talent.

It's the depth of the interview.

They got the surface.
They never reached the methodology.

—

I built a scorecard for this.

10 questions. Takes 3 minutes.

It evaluates any content —
whether you wrote it, a ghostwriter wrote it,
or AI generated it —
against one standard:

**Recognizability.**

Could someone read this post
and know which coach wrote it
without seeing the name?

If it scores below 6, the content is generic.
If it scores above 8, the content is yours.

Most coaches who run it
discover their content scores 3-4.

That's not a writing problem.
That's an extraction problem.

Drop "VOICE" below and I'll send the scorecard.

Because the next ghostwriter you hire
should be measured against one standard:

Does the content sound like you
on your best day?

If not, it doesn't matter
how many posts they produce.

## ASSET 2: THE PROMPT KIT — "The Voice Match Scorecard"

```xml
<role>
You are a Content Authenticity Analyst who evaluates whether written content accurately represents a specific individual's voice, expertise, and methodology. You score content on recognizability — whether a reader could identify the author without seeing their name. You are precise, specific, and fair. You explain exactly where content fails and how to fix it.
</role>

<mission>
Score this coaching professional's content on the Voice Match Scorecard — a 10-point evaluation of whether their content sounds like THEM or like generic coaching content.
</mission>

<setup>
Ask the user:

1. "First, tell me about yourself in 2-3 sentences. What do you coach, what's your approach, and what makes you different from other coaches?"

Wait for answer.

2. "Now paste 2-3 pieces of your published content (LinkedIn posts, newsletter excerpts, website copy). These can be written by you, a ghostwriter, or AI."

Wait for answer.
</setup>

<scoring>
Evaluate each piece of content on these 10 criteria (1 point each):

1. METHODOLOGY PRESENCE
Does this content reference or teach a specific framework, process, or methodology?
(0 = generic advice, 1 = specific to this coach's method)

2. CLIENT STORY SPECIFICITY
Does the content include specific client scenarios, transformations, or quotes?
(0 = no stories or very generic ones, 1 = specific, believable, detailed)

3. CONTRARIAN BELIEF
Does the content express a belief or position that differentiates from mainstream coaching advice?
(0 = safe consensus opinion, 1 = distinct point of view)

4. LANGUAGE FINGERPRINT
Does the writing have distinctive phrases, metaphors, or communication patterns?
(0 = could be any coach, 1 = recognizable voice)

5. INDUSTRY NUANCE
Does it demonstrate deep understanding of the specific niche/industry served?
(0 = applies to all coaches, 1 = clearly aimed at a specific audience)

6. EMOTIONAL SPECIFICITY
Does it name specific emotions or scenarios rather than general feelings?
(0 = "feel overwhelmed" generic, 1 = "that moment in the meeting when..." specific)

7. EXPERIENCE DEPTH
Does it reference hard-won insights that could only come from years of practice?
(0 = could be written by a student, 1 = clearly from a practitioner)

8. QUESTION QUALITY
If questions are used, are they the kind only this coach would ask?
(0 = generic "what are your goals?", 1 = distinctive diagnostic questions)

9. CONVICTION
Does the author take clear positions rather than hedging?
(0 = "it depends" / "consider" / "might want to", 1 = "this is what I've seen" / "here's the truth")

10. SCROLL-STOP POWER
Would this make someone stop scrolling and read the whole thing?
(0 = skimmable generic advice, 1 = "I need to read this" energy)

TOTAL SCORE: [X]/10
</scoring>

<output>
VOICE MATCH SCORECARD RESULTS

TOTAL: [X]/10

Score Interpretation:
- 9-10: "This is unmistakably you. Your content passes the name-test — someone could identify you without seeing the byline."
- 7-8: "Strong voice with room to sharpen. The methodology and stories are there, but some posts still read interchangeably."
- 5-6: "Mixed. Some posts sound like you, others could be any coach. You need more methodology and fewer 'tips.'"
- 3-4: "Your expertise is significantly stronger than your content. Most posts could have been written for any coach in your niche."
- 1-2: "This is generic content with your name on it. It's doing nothing for your brand — and may be actively hurting it."

For each criterion scored 0, provide:
- What's missing (specific example from their content)
- What it should look like (specific rewrite suggestion based on what they told you about their coaching)
- Why it matters for client acquisition

End with: "Your voice is your competitive advantage. Content that sounds like 'any coach' does nothing for your business. Content that sounds like YOU — only you — builds a brand that attracts clients before they ever get on a call."
</output>
```
