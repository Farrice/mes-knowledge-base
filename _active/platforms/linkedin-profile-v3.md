### Template 1: Warm (After Pre-Warm Commenting)

Use this AFTER you've left 2-3 substantive comments on their posts over 3-5 days.

```
[Name], your post on [specific topic] hit different —
especially the part about [specific detail from their post].

Most coaches talk about [generic version of what they said].
You actually get into the mechanics of [their specific insight].

I write LinkedIn content for coaches — would love to connect
and keep learning from how you think about this.
```

*Why it works: You've already established presence through comments. They may recognize your name. The request references their specific content — proves you're not mass-sending.*

### Template 2: Cold but Coach-Targeted

Use when you find a coach through search who fits the ICP but you haven't engaged with their content yet.

```
[Name], I found your profile through [how you found them —
a post, a group, a mutual connection].

I noticed you've been coaching for [X] years in [their niche].
I help coaches who are great in conversation turn that into
LinkedIn content that actually sounds like them.

Not pitching — just building connections in the coaching space.
Would love to connect.
```

*Why it works: Acknowledges their experience (triggers pride, not defense). Describes the service without selling. "Not pitching" disarms the sales reflex that coaches hate (Frustration: "DM outreach feels sleazy").*

### Template 3: Peer / Networker (Other Content Creators, Ghostwriters, Marketers)

```
[Name], been following your work on [topic/platform].

I'm building a LinkedIn content service specifically for coaches
— different angle from traditional ghostwriting.
The focus is on capturing coaching methodology, not just voice.

Always good to connect with people doing interesting work
in the content space. Would love to stay in touch.
```

*Why it works: Positions you as a peer, not a competitor. "Different angle" creates curiosity. Peers become referral sources — ghostwriters who don't serve coaches will send coaches your way.*

### CONNECTION REQUEST RULES

1. **Send 15-20 per day** (safe daily limit for free accounts). Do not exceed.
2. **Personalize every single one.** LinkedIn's AI detects spray-and-pray patterns.
3. **Target acceptance rate: 40%+.** If below 30%, your requests are too generic.
4. **Never pitch in the connection request.** The request opens the door. The DM conversation happens after they accept — and only if they engage with your content first.
5. **Track in a simple spreadsheet**: Name, date sent, accepted Y/N, DM sent Y/N, outcome.
6. **Maximum pending requests: 500.** LinkedIn hard caps at 700 pending. Withdraw old unaccepted requests monthly.

---

### DM SEQUENCE (After Connection Accepted)

**Do NOT DM immediately after connection acceptance.** Wait until one of these triggers:

- They like or comment on one of your posts
- They view your profile (LinkedIn shows this)
- 5-7 days after connection, if neither of the above happens

**Opening DM** (keep it short):
```
Hey [Name], glad we connected.

Quick question — how are you currently handling
your LinkedIn content? DIY, outsourced, or just
not posting consistently?

No pitch. Genuinely curious how coaches
in [their niche] approach it.
```

**If they respond with frustration** (and they will — 71% struggle):
```
That tracks with what I hear from a lot of coaches.

The gap between how brilliant you are on calls
and how that shows up online is one of the hardest
problems in the coaching industry.

I'm actually running a small experiment right now —
taking 3 coaches through a proof run:

One 30-min call. I capture how you actually think and teach.
You get 3 posts back in 48 hours that sound like you.

If they hit, we talk about ongoing content.
If they don't, you keep the posts and we part ways.

$250 for the proof run. Zero risk.

Would you want to see an example of what this looks like?
```

*This DM sequence is grounded in the research finding that coaches hate being sold to ("scary and sleazy") but respond to curiosity-driven conversations. The proof run removes risk — validated by ghostwriting market research showing that "paid test month" models have high conversion but almost nobody offers them.*
