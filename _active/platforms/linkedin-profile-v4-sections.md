### Connection Request Rules

1. **15-20 per day** max (free account safe limit)
2. **Personalize every one.** LinkedIn's AI detects mass-send patterns and restricts your account.
3. **Target 40%+ acceptance rate.** Below 30% = requests are too generic.
4. **Never pitch in the request.** The request opens the door. The DM happens after acceptance — and only after a trigger.
5. **Track in a spreadsheet**: Name | Date Sent | Accepted Y/N | DM Sent Y/N | Outcome
6. **Withdraw unaccepted requests monthly.** Max pending: 500 (hard cap at 700).

## DM SEQUENCE (After Connection Accepted)

### Trigger Conditions — When to DM

Do NOT DM immediately after they accept. Wait for one of these:

| Trigger | Priority | Wait Time |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| They comment on one of your posts | Highest | DM same day |
| They like one of your posts | High | DM within 24 hours |
| They view your profile | High | DM within 24 hours |
| None of the above happen | Normal | Wait 5-7 days after acceptance |

**Why wait**: Coaches hate being sold to ("scary and sleazy" — validated research). Waiting for a trigger means they've already shown interest. Your DM arrives when they're warm, not cold.

---

### Opening DM

```
Hey [Name], glad we connected.

Quick question — how are you handling your LinkedIn
content right now? Writing it yourself, outsourcing,
or just not posting consistently?

Genuinely curious. I hear wildly different answers
from coaches in [their niche].
```

**Why this works**: Asks a question (creates a reply). Does not pitch. "Genuinely curious" is disarming. "I hear wildly different answers" implies you talk to a lot of coaches (social proof) without bragging. Mentioning their niche proves it's not a mass DM.

---

### Follow-Up If They Express Frustration

Only send this if their reply contains frustration signals: "I know I should post more," "I hate writing," "I tried a ghostwriter and it didn't work," "I just don't have time," or anything similar.

```
That tracks with what I hear from a lot of coaches.

The gap between how sharp you are on calls and how
that shows up online is one of the hardest problems
in the coaching industry. Not a talent issue —
a translation issue.

I'm running a proof run right now for a small group:

→ One 30-min call. You talk the way you normally coach.
→ I capture your frameworks, stories, and language.
→ You get 3 posts back in 48 hours that sound like you.

If they hit, we talk about ongoing content.
If they don't, you keep the posts and we part ways.

$250 for the proof run. Zero risk.

Want to see an example of what a finished post looks like?
```

**Why this works**: Validates their frustration first ("that tracks"). Reframes the problem as translation, not talent (removes shame). The proof run structure removes risk — they're not buying a retainer, they're testing a sample. "$250" is low enough to be a no-brainer for a coach charging $200+/hour. "Want to see an example?" is a soft close that moves them forward without pressure.

---

### If They Say "Yes, Show Me"

Send one of your best demo posts — either a real client transformation or a before/after rewrite. Then:

```
That's what it looks like when content comes from
how you actually think — not from a ghostwriter guessing.

If you want to test this with your own frameworks,
I've got [1-2] proof run slots open this week.

30 minutes on a call + 3 posts in 48 hours.
Link to book: [your scheduling link]
```

---

### If They Go Silent After the Opening DM

Do nothing for 14 days. Then engage with their content (like + comment) for a week. If they respond to your comment, you can re-open the conversation organically. If not, move on. Never follow up on an unanswered DM with another DM.
