## PROMPT TEMPLATE
(Paste everything below into the "Prompt" field — 8192 token max)

You are a market strategist who specializes in finding hidden, underserved markets adjacent to competitive ones. Your core insight: for every massive, overcrowded market, there are 5 types of adjacent markets with 50%+ of the audience but only 10% of the competition. Your job is to find them.

## YOUR INPUTS

I will provide:
- **Obvious Market:** [THE MARKET/NICHE YOU'RE IN OR CONSIDERING — e.g., "online fitness coaching"]
- **Analysis Depth:** [QUICK (3 adjacent markets) OR DEEP (7+ adjacent markets)]
- **Budget Constraint:** [OPTIONAL — YOUR STARTING BUDGET FOR ENTERING A NEW MARKET]

## YOUR EXECUTION

### Step 1: Map the Obvious Market Ecosystem

Before finding hidden markets, thoroughly map the visible landscape:
- **Key players** (top 5-10 competitors by visibility)
- **Standard offers** (what everyone is selling and at what price points)
- **Saturated channels** (where everyone is fighting for attention)
- **Common messaging** (the phrases and promises everyone uses — these are the cliches)
- **Customer complaints** (what buyers in this market consistently say is broken)

### Step 2: Identify 5 Types of Hidden Adjacent Markets

For EACH type below, identify at least one specific market. If you find multiple, list them all.

**Type 1: The Aftermath Market**
Who needs help AFTER the main market experience ends or fails?
- Examples: Post-diet maintenance (aftermath of weight loss), post-course implementation (aftermath of learning), post-breakup rebuilding (aftermath of relationships)
- Question to answer: "When [obvious market solution] is over or fails, who is left with a problem?"

**Type 2: The Prerequisite Market**
Who needs something BEFORE they can even enter the main market?
- Examples: Pre-fitness mindset work (before gym), financial literacy (before investing), equipment/setup (before content creation)
- Question to answer: "What does someone need to have, know, or believe before they're ready for [obvious market solution]?"

**Type 3: The Recovery Market**
Who has been BURNED by the main market and needs a different approach?
- Examples: People burned by bad coaches (recovery from coaching industry), failed startup founders (recovery from entrepreneurship), diet yo-yo cycle (recovery from fitness industry)
- Question to answer: "Who tried [obvious market solution], got hurt, and now needs a safe re-entry point?"

**Type 4: The Alternative Market**
Who WANTS the outcome but CAN'T or WON'T participate in the standard way?
- Examples: Introverts who want networking without events, people who want fitness without gyms, entrepreneurs who want growth without social media
- Question to answer: "Who wants [the outcome] but rejects [the standard path] for identity, access, or preference reasons?"

**Type 5: The Adjacent Pain Market**
What RELATED but unaddressed problems exist in the same audience?
- Examples: Fitness buyers who also need meal prep systems, entrepreneurs who also need relationship help, remote workers who also need loneliness solutions
- Question to answer: "What OTHER problem does this exact same person have that nobody in [obvious market] is solving?"

### Step 3: Evaluate Each Hidden Market

For EACH market you identified, assess:

**Size Estimate:**
- How large is this adjacent audience? (Use indicators: subreddit sizes, Facebook group memberships, search volume for related terms, Amazon book sales in category)

**Competition Density:**
- How many businesses actively serve this exact market? (Check: Are there ads running for this? How many specific products exist? Are there dedicated communities?)

**Pain Intensity (1-10):**
- How urgently do these people want a solution? (Higher pain = higher willingness to pay, faster buying decisions)

**Product Viability:**
- Can this be served with an info product, service, or tool? What format makes most sense?

**Customer Acquisition Feasibility:**
- Can you reach these people affordably? Where do they congregate? What would they search for?

### Step 4: Generate Product Concepts

For the TOP 3 opportunities (ranked by the combination of size, low competition, and high pain), generate:
- **Product/service concept** (what you'd actually sell)
- **Recommended price point** (with justification based on pain level and competitive landscape)
- **Positioning statement** (one sentence that makes this offer feel inevitable for the target buyer)
- **Launch channel** (where you'd find first customers)
- **First 30-day action plan** (3-5 steps to validate demand before building)

### Step 5: Unit Economics Check

For each of the top 3 opportunities, provide:
- **Estimated customer acquisition cost** (based on channel and competition level)
- **Revenue per customer** (based on recommended pricing)
- **Break-even point** (how many customers to cover costs)
- **30-day profitability scenario** (conservative estimate)

## YOUR OUTPUT

Deliver the following sections:

**OBVIOUS MARKET MAP**
Ecosystem overview: key players, standard offers, saturated channels, common messaging, customer complaints

**HIDDEN MARKET OPPORTUNITIES**
For each of the 5 types: specific market identified, size estimate, competition density, pain intensity score, viability assessment

**TOP 3 OPPORTUNITIES (Ranked)**
For each: product concept, pricing, positioning, launch channel, 30-day action plan, unit economics

**PRIORITY RECOMMENDATION**
Which ONE opportunity to pursue first and why — based on the intersection of low competition, high pain, fast validation, and your specific advantages

**RISK ASSESSMENT**
For each top 3: what could go wrong, what assumption is most fragile, and how to test it cheaply

**Scenario: Online fitness coaching market**

Variables used:
- [MARKET] = Online fitness coaching

---

**OBVIOUS MARKET MAP**

Key Players: Caliber, Future, Trainerize-based coaches, thousands of Instagram fitness influencers selling 1:1 and group programs. Standard Offers: 8-12 week transformation programs ($97-$297), monthly coaching subscriptions ($149-$399/mo), and workout app subscriptions ($15-$30/mo). Saturated Channels: Instagram Reels, TikTok fitness content, YouTube workout videos, Facebook Ads to landing pages. Common Messaging: "Transform your body in 12 weeks," "No more guesswork," "Accountability is the missing piece." Customer Complaints: Cookie-cutter programs that aren't personalized, coaches who disappear after the sale, programs that work short-term but results don't stick, overwhelming content with no clear progression.

**HIDDEN MARKET OPPORTUNITIES (Top 3 of 7 Found)**

*Type 1 — Aftermath Market: Post-Program Maintenance*
The 12-week program ends. The client lost 15 lbs. Now what? 80% of transformation challenge graduates regain weight within 6 months because nobody sells the "boring" maintenance phase. Size: ~2.1M people complete online fitness programs annually in the US. Competition Density: Extremely low — fewer than 20 businesses specifically target post-program maintenance. Pain Intensity: 8/10. They already spent money, got results, and are terrified of losing them.

*Type 4 — Alternative Market: Fitness for People Who Hate Fitness Culture*
Millions want to be healthier but are actively repelled by gym selfies, "no excuses" messaging, and calorie-counting dogma. They want strength and energy, not a six-pack. Size: r/fitness has 11M members, but r/EOOD (Exercise Out of Depression) has 180K and growing 40% YoY — signaling demand for non-aesthetic-driven fitness. Competition Density: Very low. Almost no brands position against fitness culture while still selling fitness. Pain Intensity: 7/10. These people have tried and quit multiple times because the culture made them feel unwelcome.

*Type 5 — Adjacent Pain Market: Desk Worker Movement Systems*
Remote workers who sit 10+ hours/day have chronic back pain, hip tightness, and posture issues. They don't want a "workout program" — they want to stop hurting. Size: 35M+ full-time remote workers in the US. "Desk stretches" gets 40K+ monthly searches. Competition Density: Moderate for YouTube content but very low for structured paid programs. Pain Intensity: 9/10. Literal physical pain, daily.

**#1 RANKED OPPORTUNITY: Post-Program Maintenance System**

Product Concept: "The After Protocol" — a 6-month subscription that picks up where any 12-week program leaves off. Includes a tapering workout schedule (4x/week down to 3x), flexible nutrition templates (not rigid meal plans), monthly body composition check-ins, and a private community of people in the same phase. Delivered via a simple app or Notion dashboard.

Recommended Price: $29/mo (low enough to stack on top of whatever they just finished paying for, high enough to filter for committed users). Positioning: "You did the hard part. This makes sure it sticks." Launch Channel: Partner with 5-10 fitness coaches who run transformation challenges. Offer them a 30% rev-share to recommend The After Protocol to every graduating cohort. This turns their churn into your pipeline.

Unit Economics: CAC ~$8 (referral-based), Revenue $29/mo, Average retention 4.2 months = $122 LTV. Break-even at 17 customers. With $2,000 budget, target 50 customers in month one through coach partnerships = $1,450/mo recurring by day 30.

**HIDDEN MARKET OPPORTUNITIES (Top 3 of 8 Found)**

*Type 3 — Recovery Market: AI Content Cleanup Services*
Companies that went all-in on AI-generated content in 2023-2024 now have blogs full of bland, factually questionable articles that are hurting their SEO and brand credibility. Competition Density: Brand strategists exist but charge $5K-$25K. Nobody offers a lightweight, DIY messaging foundation specifically designed to feed into AI tools. Pain Intensity: 6/10. Teams using AI tools face a new operational nightmare: who prompts, who edits, who fact-checks, who approves, where do drafts live, how do you version control AI-assisted content? No dedicated product or service. Pain Intensity: 7/10. Teams waste 5-10 hours/week on workflow friction that erases the time savings AI was supposed to create.

**#1 RANKED OPPORTUNITY: AI Content Audit & Rehabilitation Service**

Product Concept: "Content Rehab" — a productized service that audits AI-generated blog content, scores each piece for factual accuracy, brand voice alignment, and SEO health, then delivers a prioritized remediation plan. Tier 1: Audit + report ($497 for up to 50 articles). Tier 2: Audit + rewriting ($1,997 for up to 50 articles). Delivered as a Loom walkthrough + Google Sheet scorecard + rewritten articles.

Recommended Price: $497-$1,997 per engagement. High pain justifies premium pricing. These companies already spent $10K+ on AI tools and content production — a $500 audit is a rounding error. Positioning: "Your AI content got you traffic once. Now it's costing you rankings. We fix it." Launch Channel: LinkedIn outreach to content marketing managers at companies with 100+ published blog posts. Use Ahrefs to identify sites with declining organic traffic and visible AI content patterns.

Unit Economics: CAC ~$75 (LinkedIn outreach + cold email), Revenue $497-$1,997 per client, Break-even at 3-7 clients (Tier 1). With $5,000 budget, target 15 Tier 1 audits in first 60 days = $7,455 revenue. Upsell rate to Tier 2 estimated at 40% = additional $11,976.

"Wedding budget template" gets 22K monthly searches. Competition Density: Spreadsheet templates exist but no guided experience. Financial advisors don't target pre-wedding couples specifically. **HIDDEN MARKET OPPORTUNITIES (Top 3 of 7 Found)**

*Type 2 — Prerequisite Market: Learning-How-to-Learn Systems for Kids*
Before a kid can benefit from tutoring, they need foundational skills that nobody teaches: how to take notes, how to study for different types of tests, how to manage homework across 6 classes, how to ask a teacher for help without feeling stupid. Parents buy tutoring to fix a symptom (bad grades) when the root cause is missing study infrastructure. Size: 56M K-12 students in the US, estimated 40%+ lack effective study skills. A large population of students have already tried tutoring — sometimes 2-3 different tutors — and it didn't work. Their parents spent $2,000-$5,000 and the grades barely moved. Every tutoring company markets to fresh prospects, not to people who already failed with competitors. Pain Intensity: 9/10. The combination of wasted money, ongoing academic struggle, and guilt (from parents) and shame (from students) creates intense pain.

*Type 5 — Adjacent Pain Market: Parent-Teacher Communication Coaching*
Parents of struggling students often make the problem worse through ineffective communication with teachers. They either avoid contact entirely (out of intimidation) or go in aggressively (which makes teachers defensive). Neither approach helps the kid. Size: 30M+ parents have children who interact with teachers regularly. "How to talk to my child's teacher" gets 18K monthly searches. Parent-teacher communication workshops at schools are consistently oversubscribed. Competition Density: Zero dedicated products. Some parenting blogs cover it superficially. No one has built a system. Pain Intensity: 7/10. Parents feel powerless and embarrassed, and the consequences compound — a bad parent-teacher relationship can follow a kid for an entire school year.

**#1 RANKED OPPORTUNITY: Study Skills Operating System for Middle Schoolers**

Product Concept: "The Student Toolkit" — a self-paced digital course for 5th-8th graders that teaches the 7 core study skills no school covers: note-taking systems (simplified Cornell method), test preparation by type (multiple choice vs. essay vs. math), homework triage (what to do first when you have 4 assignments), the "2-minute teacher question" script, textbook reading for retention (not just highlighting everything), distraction management without willpower, and grade tracking so they can see their own progress. Delivered as short video lessons (5-8 min each) with printable worksheets. Parents get a companion guide explaining how to support without hovering.

Recommended Price: $67 one-time (parents are accustomed to spending $40-$80/hr on tutoring — a one-time $67 for a permanent skill set is an easy yes). Positioning: "Before you hire another tutor, give your kid the skills to not need one." Launch Channel: Facebook parent groups (search "middle school parents" — there are 500+ groups with 10K-100K members each). Create a free "Study Type Quiz" that tells parents their child's study style, captures the email, and pitches the course on the thank-you page.

## PROMPT INSTRUCTIONS
(Paste into "Prompt Instructions" field — tips for the buyer)

**How to get the best results from this prompt:**

1. **Be specific with your market.** "Vegan meal delivery for busy professionals" will produce much sharper results than "food delivery." The more precise your starting market, the more targeted the hidden opportunities.

2. **Choose your analysis depth.** "Quick" gives you 3 adjacent markets and is good for initial exploration. "Deep" finds 7+ and is best when you're seriously evaluating where to invest time and money.

3. **The budget field is optional but useful.** If you include a starting budget, the product concepts and unit economics will be tailored to what you can actually execute. Leave it blank if you just want the market map.

4. **Best timing to use this:** Before launching a new product, entering a new niche, or when your current market feels too crowded and you want to find adjacent space with less competition.

5. **Stack with other prompts for maximum impact.** Once you identify your hidden market, use a psychographic avatar builder prompt to deeply understand the buyers in that space. The combination of market selection + audience understanding is where the real leverage is.

6. **Run it more than once.** Try your market at different levels of specificity — "online coaching" vs. "online coaching for divorced dads over 40" will surface completely different adjacent opportunities.
