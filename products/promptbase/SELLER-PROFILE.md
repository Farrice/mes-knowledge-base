## Review Strategy (Critical for Scaling)

**First 10 reviews matter most.** Here's how to accelerate:

1. **Ask 3-5 people you know** to buy a prompt and leave a review (they get a useful tool, you get social proof)
2. **Include a note in your prompt**: At the end of the actual prompt text, add: "If this prompt delivered value, I'd genuinely appreciate a review on PromptBase — it helps other creators find tools that actually work."
3. **Respond to every review** — shows you're active and engaged
4. **If a prompt gets a bad review**, update it immediately and note "Updated based on feedback" in the description

## Listing Consistency Rules

To look like a professional seller (not someone who listed 5 random prompts):

1. **Consistent description format** — Every listing follows the same structure: Problem → What you get → Closing line. (Your 5 current prompts already do this.)

3. **Consistent tags** — Always include "solopreneur," "business," and "strategy" alongside category-specific tags.
