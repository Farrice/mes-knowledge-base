## Critical Reframe: Kill the "Ghostwriting" Label

**All 7 agents agree: the word "ghostwriting" is a liability.**

The term triggers the authenticity paradox — coaches who sell their unique perspective fear that outsourced content undermines credibility. The service must be repositioned:

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Ghostwriting | Voice articulation & authority amplification |
| Content creation | Thought leadership engineering |
| We write for you | We translate your expertise into influence |
| LinkedIn management | C-suite client acquisition system |
| Posts per month | Strategic impact pieces |

**Recommended positioning title:** "Executive Voice Architect" or "C-Suite Authority Strategist"

**Core promise:** "I don't write *for* you; I articulate *you*. Leveraging 18 years of psychological insight and proprietary AI, I capture the essence of your unique executive voice and amplify it across LinkedIn — ensuring every word resonates with your authentic authority and attracts your ideal C-suite clients."

## Positioning Canvas (April Dunford's Framework)

### Competitive Alternatives (What coaches do today)
1. **DIY** — Write their own posts (time-consuming, inconsistent, often generic)
2. **AI tools** — Taplio ($65/mo), ContentIn, Oiti (cheap but sounds like AI, no strategic depth)
3. **Generic agencies** — $1-3K/mo (team-based, no psychological understanding, voice mismatch)
4. **Virtual assistants** — Repurpose existing content (surface-level, no original thought leadership)
5. **Do nothing** — Post sporadically and rely on referrals (leaving money on the table)

### Unique Differentiation
- **18 years of psychology + coaching experience** — understands the coach's mind, their client's mind, and the psychological barriers to content creation
- **Voice-first AI methodology** — content starts from the coach's actual words (voice notes), not from a blank page
- **95 expert AI agents** — research, structure, and craft at a level no solo ghostwriter can match
- **Authenticity guarantee** — every post passes the "would this person actually say this?" test because it literally started from their speech

### Value Chain ("So What?")
Features → Benefits → Ultimate Value:
- Voice-first capture → Content sounds exactly like them → Their credibility is enhanced, not undermined
- Psychology expertise → Understands their resistance and their clients' triggers → Content converts, not just publishes
- AI augmentation → Research + structure at scale → More strategic depth per post than any human ghostwriter alone
- **Ultimate value:** Predictable pipeline of qualified C-suite discovery calls from LinkedIn, without spending any time on content

### Category
**Not** ghostwriting. **Not** content marketing. This is: **"Executive Authority Engineering"** — a new category that combines voice capture, psychological strategy, and AI-augmented production to turn a coach's private expertise into public influence that drives revenue.

## Pricing Strategy (Resolved Conflict)

The swarm had a genuine conflict between "discount for proof" (Samuel Thompson, April Dunford, Lara Acosta) and "premium from day one" (Rory Sutherland, Jeremy Miner, Daniel Priestley).

### Resolution: Premium Price + Risk-Reversal Guarantee

| Phase | Price | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|-------|----------|---------|
| **Founding Partners** (3-5 clients) | $2,500/month (3-month minimum) | Months 1-3 | Build case studies with outcome guarantees |
| **Growth Phase** | $3,500/month | Months 4-9 | Scale with proven results |
| **Premium Phase** | $4,500-$5,000/month | Month 10+ | Full value pricing with portfolio of proof |

**Setup fee:** $1,000 for "Voice & Authority Blueprint Session" (costly signal + deep voice capture)

**The guarantee that resolves the conflict:**

> "We will help you book 5 qualified discovery calls in 90 days, or we work for free until you do."

This maintains premium positioning (no discounting) while removing buyer risk (they're not gambling). It attracts outcome-focused clients, not price-shoppers.

## Launch Sequence (Days 1-30)

### Days 1-3: Foundation

1. **Rebrand LinkedIn profile:**
   - Headline: "Executive Voice Architect | C-Suite Client Acquisition via AI-Voiced Thought Leadership | 18 Years Decoding Human Behavior"
   - Summary: Lead with the problem (brilliant coaches invisible online), the insight (it's not a writing problem, it's a translation problem), the solution (voice-first AI + psychology)

2. **Create 3-5 launch posts** addressing executive coach pain points:
   - "Why brilliant Executive Coaches feel invisible on LinkedIn"
   - "The Authenticity Trap: Why doing it yourself isn't more authentic"
   - "Your best content is dying in private rooms — here's how to rescue it"
   - "I decoded 14 patterns in how executives buy coaching. Here's what surprised me."
   - "The myth of 'doing it all yourself' for thought leadership"

3. **Build the lead magnet:** "Executive Voice & Impact Audit" — a free 15-minute diagnostic where you analyze their current LinkedIn presence using your psychological expertise and provide immediate, actionable insights. This demonstrates "proof of insight" before they buy.

### Days 4-10: Targeted Outreach

4. **Build target list** using LinkedIn Sales Navigator:
   - Title: "Executive Coach" OR "Leadership Coach" OR "CEO Coach"
   - Followers: 10K+
   - Posting frequency: Inconsistent (less than weekly)
   - Industry: Business consulting, professional services
   - Goal: 50 qualified prospects

5. **Personalized DM outreach** (NOT mass messaging):

   > "Hi [Name], I noticed your work in [specific area]. Many executive coaches I speak with are brilliant, but struggle to consistently translate that brilliance into a powerful, authentic LinkedIn presence that attracts their ideal C-suite clients — often due to time constraints or the fear of sounding 'inauthentic.' Are you the type of coach who's actively looking for a strategic way to amplify your genuine voice and impact without sacrificing your time, or is that something you've already mastered?"

6. **Target response rate:** 5-10% on DMs, 20-30% conversion from audit call to Founding Partner

### Days 10-20: Proof of Insight

7. **Run 5-10 Executive Voice & Impact Audits:**
   - 15-minute calls focused on diagnosing their specific content challenges
   - Ask them to send a 5-minute voice note on their core methodology beforehand
   - During the call, play back a sample post you created from their voice note in 24 hours
   - The "how did they know?" moment: they hear their own voice, perfectly articulated

8. **Close 3-5 Founding Partners:**
   - Pitch: "I'm taking 5 Founding Partners for my Executive Authority Engineering program. $2,500/month, 3-month minimum. I guarantee 5 qualified discovery calls in 90 days, or I work for free until you get them."
   - Emphasize exclusivity and the psychology + AI combination

### Days 20-30: Delivery & Documentation

9. **Begin delivering for Founding Partners:**
   - Week 1: Deep voice capture session (voice notes, not Zoom — async, natural)
   - Week 2: First 2 posts published, calibrated against their feedback
   - Week 3-4: Full cadence (4-6 posts/month, strategic engagement coaching)

10. **Document everything:**
    - Track discovery calls booked per client per month
    - Capture testimonials and before/after LinkedIn analytics
    - Build case study framework for scaling to Growth Phase pricing

## Behavioral Economics (Rory Sutherland's Framework)

### Why coaches resist (and how to counter)

| Behavioral Barrier | Reframe |
|-------------------|---------|
| **Loss aversion** — "What if it doesn't work and I've wasted $7,500?" | Risk-reversal guarantee removes the loss scenario entirely |
| **Status quo bias** — "My referral network is working well enough" | "How many clients are you *not* getting because they found a more visible coach?" |
| **Authenticity reactance** — "Outsourcing content feels like cheating" | "Great leaders delegate to amplify. Your COO runs operations so you can focus on strategy. This is the same principle applied to thought leadership." |
| **Endowment effect** — Overvaluing their current approach | The free audit shows them the gap between their current output and their potential |
| **Social proof vacuum** — "Nobody I know uses a service like this" | "The most visible executive coaches on LinkedIn almost all have support. They just don't talk about it." |

### Pricing psychology
- $1,000 setup fee = costly signal that this is serious, not a commodity
- $2,500/month = less than one client engagement ($15K+), framed as "this investment pays for itself with a single new client"
- 3-month minimum = commitment device that gives the system time to work
- Outcome guarantee = transforms the purchase from "gamble" to "guaranteed return"

## Success Metrics

### Month 1-3 (Founding Partner Phase)
- 3-5 Founding Partners enrolled
- Content cadence established for each client
- First discovery calls tracked

### Month 3-6 (Proof Phase)
- 5+ qualified discovery calls per client per month
- 2-3 strong case studies with measurable ROI
- First testimonials captured
- Price increase to $3,500/month for new clients

### Month 6-12 (Growth Phase)
- 8-12 active clients
- Inbound inquiries from LinkedIn content about the service
- Referral pipeline from satisfied clients
- Price established at $4,500-$5,000/month
- Consider hiring first AI-assisted engagement manager

### Kill Criteria
If after 60 days, 2 out of 3 pilot clients show no measurable lead generation improvement, re-evaluate:
- Is the content quality meeting the standard?
- Are clients participating in the voice capture process?
- Is the executive coaching niche actually right, or should you narrow further (e.g., "coaches for tech CEOs")?

## The Bowling Pin Strategy (April Dunford)

Consider narrowing further within executive coaching for maximum initial dominance:

| Sub-Niche | Advantage | Consideration |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Coaches for Tech CEOs | High-ticket, LinkedIn-native, fast adopters | Competitive, may already have content support |
| Coaches for Women in Senior Leadership | Underserved, growing, strong community | May be more price-sensitive |
| Coaches for Leadership Transitions | Specific pain point, time-urgent | Smaller addressable market |
| Coaches for First-Time CEOs | Acute need for visibility, eager for help | May not have established revenue yet |

**Recommendation:** Start with "Executive Coaches serving Tech Founders/CEOs" — they're the most LinkedIn-native, the most tech-comfortable (won't resist AI), and the most likely to see fast results.
