## The One Individual: Dr. Evelyn Thorne

*Not a demographic segment. A specific person you can picture, speak to, and serve.*

**Background:** Ex-McKinsey partner, now coaching Fortune 500 CEOs on leadership transformation. Charges $20K per engagement. 15+ years executive experience before coaching. Based in a major metro, travels frequently for speaking and client work.

**Income:** $200K-$500K+ annually from coaching engagements
**LinkedIn:** 10K-25K followers, posts inconsistently (2-3x/month instead of 3-5x/week)
**Content quality when she does post:** Insightful but rushed, often sharing others' content rather than creating her own

---

## 3D Consumer Posture

### 1. OCCUPATION (The role she plays in the world)

She occupies the role of the **"Strategic Oracle"** — the trusted, insightful advisor whose words carry immense weight in private rooms. Board members defer to her judgment. CEOs call her before making major decisions. Her authority is absolute in 1:1 settings.

**The gap:** Her private authority doesn't translate to her public presence. On LinkedIn, she looks like every other coach — occasional shares, generic commentary, sporadic original posts that don't reflect the depth of her thinking.

**What she wants to occupy:** The definitive voice in executive leadership development on LinkedIn. A beacon of foresight whose posts get saved, shared, and referenced in board meetings.

### 2. ACTIVITY (The rituals and behaviors that define her days)

**Current reality:**
- 60-hour weeks packed with client calls, board meetings, speaking engagements
- Morning starts with Slack, email triage, and prep for the day's sessions
- Content creation happens in stolen moments — late at night, between meetings, on planes
- When she does write, she agonizes over every word (perfectionism + impostor fear)
- Engages deeply in 1:1 client relationships and peer referral networks
- Attends 2-3 industry conferences per quarter

**Desired reality:**
- 3-5 thought-provoking LinkedIn posts per week that sound exactly like her
- Consistent engagement from C-suite leaders in her comments
- A steady stream of inbound inquiries from ideal clients who say "I've been following your content"
- Zero time spent on content creation — it just happens, as if by magic

**The activity gap:** She *does* create brilliant content — in coaching sessions, in keynotes, in private conversations. But none of it gets captured, structured, or published. Her best insights live in her head and die in private rooms.

### 3. THOUGHT PROCESS (The internal logic about what feels aligned vs. inauthentic)

**Her internal monologue:**

> "My insights are invaluable, but my time is finite. I know I *should* be more visible on LinkedIn to capture the next wave of C-suite clients, but the thought of crafting compelling content consistently, day after day, feels like another full-time job I don't have."

> "I fear my current sporadic presence makes me look less active or less 'thought leader' than my peers. Sarah just posted another viral take on leadership. David has a newsletter with 50K subscribers. I have better insights than both of them but nobody knows I exist online."

> "I need my content to sound *exactly like me* — to carry my gravitas, my specific vocabulary, my unique psychological insights. I can't afford to be generic or, worse, inauthentic. If someone found out I didn't write my own posts, it would undermine everything."

> "I've looked at AI tools. They produce drivel. I've talked to ghostwriting agencies. They don't understand my world. I need someone who can think at my level and translate what's in my head into what should be on the screen."

**The thought process paradox:** She believes thought leadership is essential for her business growth, but she also believes that outsourcing it would be inauthentic. These two beliefs are in direct conflict, and the conflict keeps her stuck — doing nothing.

---

## The Authenticity Paradox (Core Psychological Barrier)

This is the central tension that every agent identified unanimously:

**The desire:** To be recognized as a definitive thought leader, attracting C-suite clients through a commanding LinkedIn presence.

**The fear:** That outsourcing content creation will expose her as inauthentic, undermining the very credibility she's built over decades.

**The result:** Paralysis. She does nothing, posting sporadically, losing ground to less-qualified coaches who are more active online.

**What she cannot yet say:**

> "I need a way to clone my unique wisdom and voice into the digital realm, so I can scale my impact without burning out, and without being exposed as inauthentic."

**What would unlock her:**

The realization that a ghostwriter who uses *her* voice notes, *her* frameworks, *her* stories, and *her* specific language isn't "faking it" — they're *translating* her private brilliance into public impact. The best ghostwritten content is MORE authentic than what she'd produce alone, because it removes the friction between what she thinks and what she publishes.

## Buying Triggers (When She's Ready to Act)

1. **Time desperation:** "I haven't posted in 3 weeks and just lost a prospect to a coach with a bigger LinkedIn presence"
2. **Competitor envy:** A peer coach just went viral, got a book deal, or landed a keynote — all from LinkedIn content
3. **Failed DIY attempt:** She tried Taplio/ContentIn, produced generic content, felt embarrassed, and stopped
4. **Team pressure:** Her COO or marketing person says "You really need to be posting more consistently"
5. **Revenue plateau:** Client acquisition has slowed and she can trace it to lack of inbound leads
6. **Referral fatigue:** She's tired of relying entirely on word-of-mouth and wants a predictable pipeline
