### Your Positioning vs. Competitors

**Coachvox/Personify = SaaS.** They sell ongoing subscriptions. The coach does the work of training the AI themselves. It's a *tool*.

**Freelancers = Commodity.** They build a custom GPT and hand it off. No extraction methodology. No voice calibration. No psychology background.

**You = Done-for-you service with live extraction.** You SHOW UP, pull the expertise out of their head, and build the system. That's a fundamentally different category. You're not selling software. You're not selling prompts. You're selling the *extraction* and *organization* of their expertise.

> **Key insight**: Your actual unique value isn't the "AI Brain." It's the **EXTRACTION** — the 90-minute deep-dive where you pull out frameworks, voice, and methodology that the coach can't articulate on their own. The AI Brain is just the container.

## MARKET SENTIMENT (What Coaches Actually Feel)

**The research confirms 3 dominant emotional states:**

1. **Frustration** — "I tried ChatGPT and it sounds nothing like me." Coaches report *extensive rewriting* of AI outputs, sometimes spending MORE time editing AI content than writing from scratch. They feel gaslit by AI hype ("it'll save you hours!") when the reality is copy-pasting between 4-5 tools.

2. **Fear** — Top fears: AI replacing the human connection in coaching. Data privacy. Being seen as "inauthentic" by clients who detect AI content. Losing the trust they've built.

3. **Overwhelm** — Too many tools, too steep a learning curve. They want *results*, not education. The phrase "tool sprawl" keeps appearing. They don't want to learn prompt engineering.

### What This Means for Your Offer:
Your messaging must address **all three** — frustration ("yours won't sound generic"), fear ("this amplifies you, not replaces you"), and overwhelm ("you don't learn anything — you just show up and talk").

## 🔴 RED TEAM: 7 VULNERABILITIES IN THE CURRENT OFFER

### 1. "AI Brain" Sounds Like a Tool — Not a Transformation
**The problem:** "Build" and "Brain" both describe *what you make*, not *what they become*. Coaches don't buy tools. They buy *outcomes*. The current name tells them what they'll *have*. It doesn't tell them who they'll *be*.

**The fix:** Keep "AI Brain" as the deliverable name. Add the transformation to the tagline and all copy: *"Go from invisible expert to visible authority."* The deliverable is the AI Brain. The *promise* is visibility, leverage, and time back.

### 2. The $250 Preview Tier Has a Revenue Ceiling Problem
**The problem:** You're giving them a *blueprint* and a Loom walkthrough. The blueprint alone — seeing their expertise organized — might satisfy their itch enough that they don't upgrade. You've given them the "aha" moment without the urgency to buy more.

**The fix:** The Preview should deliver a *taste* that creates hunger, not satisfaction. Instead of a written Blueprint, give them:
- The Loom walkthrough showing what the Brain *would produce* for them (desire)
- 1 real output generated from a quick extraction (proof)
- A clear "here's what you're leaving on the table" gap analysis (urgency)
- Frame it explicitly as "This is the diagnostic. The treatment is the Build."

### 3. No Proof of Concept — You Have Zero Case Studies
**The problem:** The #1 conversion driver for coaches buying $500-$2,500 services is *proof*. Testimonials. Before/after examples. Screenshots of output. You currently have none.

**The fix:**
- Build your OWN AI Brain first and demo it constantly. Your content should be *produced by* your Brain as proof of concept.
- Offer 1-2 free Builds in exchange for testimonials + case studies. Non-negotiable. This is your first move, not a revenue play.
- Every piece of content should contain *output examples*. Show, don't tell.

### 4. "Done For You" Framing Without Addressing the Fear
**The problem:** Research shows coaches' top fear is *loss of control*. "Done for you" triggers the anxiety of "will this actually sound like me?" and "what if I don't like what they build?" DFY is the premium model, but it needs guardrails that address the control fear.

**The fix:** Frame it as **"Done With You in 90 Minutes, Built For You in 5 Days."** This acknowledges:
- They're involved in the extraction (control preserved)
- You do the technical work (convenience delivered)
- There's a defined window (no open-ended anxiety)

### 5. The Content Engine Tier ($1,500) Is Fuzzy
**The problem:** "Content system + framework library + content calendar" sounds like a lot of words for an outcome that isn't clear. What does the coach *actually get to do differently* after this tier? The gap between the $500 Build and the $1,500 Content Engine isn't viscerally clear.

**The fix:** Define the $1,500 tier by a *concrete weekly output*:
- "After the Build + Content Engine, you open your Brain on Monday morning, type '5 LinkedIn posts this week' and get 5 posts — in your voice, using your frameworks, ready to post. That's the difference."
- Give it a measurable promise: "From 3 hours of content creation to 30 minutes."

### 6. No Recurring Revenue Path Is Visible to the Buyer
**The problem:** The ascension map shows a $500/mo retainer at the bottom, but the buyer never sees it. From the buyer's perspective, every tier feels like a one-time purchase. Once they have the Brain... what keeps them paying?

**The fix:** Frame the retainer as *"Brain Maintenance"* — because an AI Brain, like any system, needs updating as the coach's methodology evolves. New stories. New frameworks. New client language. New content trends. Position it as: *"Your Brain grows as you grow."* Make this visible from Tier 2 up.

### 7. Price Anchoring Is Weak — No "Compared to What?"
**The problem:** $500 and $2,500 floating in space feel arbitrary. Without an anchor, the buyer's brain defaults to "is this worth it?" instead of "this is obviously the better deal."

**The fix:** Anchor against real alternatives:
- *"Coachvox charges $99/mo + $3,000 setup. That's $4,188/year for a chatbot you train yourself. The AI Brain Build is $500 and I extract, build, and deliver it to you in 5 days."*
- *"Most coaches spend 8-12 hours/week on content. At $150/hr (a coach's typical rate), that's $4,800-$7,200/month in opportunity cost. The Content Engine is a one-time $1,500."*
- *"The average course graveyard costs $10K+. This is $500 and you'll actually use it."*
