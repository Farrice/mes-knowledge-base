## DAY 1 — TUESDAY FEB 25

### Post: Education — "The Scattered Expert Problem"
**Sub-Character**: Rachel (Invisible Expert)
**Hook**: "You have 10 years of expertise trapped in notebooks you'll never open again."

**Post Direction**: Name the problem Rachel lives every day. 10+ years of expertise, scattered across Google Docs, Notion, Apple Notes, old course notebooks. She can teach her framework flawlessly on a call but can't find it when she needs to write. End with the reframe: the problem isn't a lack of knowledge, it's a lack of a retrieval system.

**Visual Concept**: 📊 **Simple infographic** — "Where Your Expertise Actually Lives"
- Pie chart or bar graphic showing: 30% in your head, 25% in Google Docs, 15% in notebooks, 10% in saved posts, 10% in course notes, 10% in old Notion pages
- Clean design, dark background, white/gold text
- Your logo or name in the corner
- **Tool**: Canva template or AI-generate with Midjourney/DALL-E
- **Why it works**: It's screenshot-worthy. Coaches will tag each other saying "this is literally me."

**Outreach**:
- 5 warm DMs to coaches you've engaged with (use Script 2)
- Comment on 10 coaching posts

## DAY 2 — WEDNESDAY FEB 26

### Post: Contrarian — "'Just Use ChatGPT' Is Bad Advice"
**Sub-Character**: Marcus (Existential Threat Processor)
**Hook**: "'Just use ChatGPT' is the worst advice in coaching right now."

**Post Direction**: Take a position. Generic AI gives generic output because it has no context about YOUR methodology, YOUR voice, YOUR frameworks. The advice to "just use ChatGPT" is like telling a surgeon to "just use a knife." The tool isn't the problem. The lack of YOUR data inside the tool is the problem. End with the distinction: ChatGPT is a general brain. An AI Brain is YOUR brain.

**Visual Concept**: 📸 **Candid photo of you at your desk** (laptop visible, maybe a notebook next to it)
- Real photo, not polished. Phone camera is fine.
- Slight edit for contrast/warmth (VSCO or Lightroom mobile)
- **Why it works**: Contrarian opinions hit harder with a human face attached. The photo says "this is a real person with a real take," not a content mill.

**Outreach**:
- 3 cold DMs to coaches you've never spoken to (use Script 1)
- 2 warm DMs
- Comment on 10 posts

## DAY 3 — THURSDAY FEB 27

### Post: Story — "First 60 Minutes of an AI Brain Build"
**Sub-Character**: Keisha (I Should Know This Already)
**Hook**: "Here's what happens in the first 60 minutes of an AI Brain Build Sprint."

**Post Direction**: Pull back the curtain on the process. Keisha needs to see what happens before she commits. Walk through: we start by extracting everything you know (frameworks, stories, language, philosophy). Most coaches have never seen their own methodology written down in one place. That moment when they see 15 years of expertise organized on a screen for the first time? That's when the imposter whisper goes quiet. Be specific about the process without giving away the full system.

**Visual Concept**: 📊 **Carousel (3-5 slides)** — "Inside an AI Brain Build: The First 60 Minutes"
- Slide 1: Hook/title slide (dark bg, bold text)
- Slide 2: "Step 1: The Knowledge Extraction" (brief description)
- Slide 3: "Step 2: Voice & Methodology Mapping" (brief description)
- Slide 4: "Step 3: The First Output" (brief description)
- Slide 5: CTA — "DM me BRAIN for a free preview"
- **Tool**: Canva carousel template
- **Why it works**: Carousels get 2-3x the engagement of text posts on LinkedIn. The process reveal builds trust and de-risks the purchase for Keisha.

**Outreach**:
- 5 DMs (mix of cold and warm)
- Comment on 10 posts
- Reply to ALL comments on your previous posts

## DAY 4 — FRIDAY FEB 28

### Post: Education — "The $50K Course Graveyard"
**Sub-Character**: Devon (Course Graveyard Mourner)
**Hook**: "Coaches spend $50K on courses and certifications... then can't access any of it when they actually need it."

**Post Direction**: Hit Devon's nerve directly. Name the dollar amount. Name the shame. Then reframe: the $50K isn't wasted, it's undeployed. Every framework you've ever learned is still in your head. You don't have a knowledge problem. You have a retrieval problem. The AI Brain is the retrieval system that turns $50K of scattered learning into a searchable, deployable asset.

**Visual Concept**: 📸 **Photo of actual notebooks/courses/materials stacked up**
- Stack some real books, notebooks, printed course materials on a desk
- Take a photo from above or at an angle
- Caption overlay: "This is $50K worth of expertise. It's not wasted. It's undeployed."
- **Tool**: Phone photo + Canva text overlay
- **Why it works**: Visceral and real. Every coach has this stack. The photo triggers instant recognition.

**Outreach**:
- 3 referral-ask DMs (use Script 3)
- 2 warm DMs
- Comment on 10 posts
- Follow up on any unanswered DMs from earlier in the week

## DAY 5 — MONDAY MAR 2

### Post: Story — "The Voice Breakthrough Moment"
**Sub-Character**: Marcus (Existential Threat Processor)
**Hook**: "The moment a coach opens their AI Brain and it responds in their voice? That's when imposter syndrome goes quiet."

**Post Direction**: This is the emotional climax post. Tell the story of what happens when the AI Brain is finished and the coach uses it for the first time. They type a question. The system responds using THEIR methodology, THEIR examples, THEIR language. It doesn't sound like ChatGPT. It sounds like them on their best day. For Marcus, this is the proof moment. The AI Brain can only work because his 17 years of depth are irreplaceable. The system NEEDS his unique methodology. That's the proof no chatbot could replicate him.

**Visual Concept**: 📊 **Before/After side-by-side graphic**
- Left side: "Generic ChatGPT Output" (a bland, motivational-poster-style paragraph)
- Right side: "AI Brain Output" (specific, voice-driven, framework-based content)
- Clear visual contrast (left = gray/muted, right = vibrant/branded)
- **Tool**: Canva or Figma
- **Why it works**: Shows, don't tell. The difference between generic and custom AI is abstract until you SEE it side by side. This makes it concrete and undeniable.

**Outreach**:
- 5 cold DMs to new coaches (use Script 1)
- Comment on 10 posts
- Reply to all weekend comments

## DAY 6 — TUESDAY MAR 3

### Post: Story — "What Coaches Get in 5 Days"
**Sub-Character**: Devon (Course Graveyard Mourner)
**Hook**: "What coaches get in 5 days that they couldn't build in 8 months of YouTubing:"

**Post Direction**: List the tangible deliverables. Devon has been trying to organize his methodology for years. Three failed Notion builds. Hours of YouTube tutorials. This post shows him exactly what he GETS: a complete AI Brain trained on his methodology, a content system that produces in his voice, frameworks deployed on command instead of rebuilt from memory. Keep it concrete. No fluff. End with CTA.

**Visual Concept**: 📊 **Checklist-style infographic** — "What's Inside Your AI Brain"
- Clean checklist with 5-6 deliverables
- Checkmark icons next to each item
- "Built in 5 days. Yours forever." at the bottom
- **Tool**: Canva
- **Why it works**: Tangible. Devon has been burned by vague promises. A concrete checklist of what he gets removes the "is this another course I won't finish?" objection.

**Outreach**:
- 5 DMs (warm priority — people who've engaged with your posts this week)
- Comment on 10 posts

### Post: Promotion — "3 Spots Open"
**Sub-Character**: Rachel (Invisible Expert)
**Hook**: "I have 3 spots open for AI Brain Build Sprints this month. Here's exactly what you get."

**Post Direction**: Direct offer. No hiding. Rachel has been warming up all week. She's seen the scattered expert post. She's seen the before/after. She's ready, she just needs permission to DM. Lay out: what it is, who it's for, what they get, what it costs ($2,500), and the CTA. Keep it short and confident. No begging. "If this is you, DM me BRAIN."
