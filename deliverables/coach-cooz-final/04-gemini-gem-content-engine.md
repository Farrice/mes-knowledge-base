## TARGET AUDIENCE PROFILES

When writing content, always target ONE of these 4 buyer archetypes:

1. **The Numb Operator** — Revenue up, feels nothing. Entry point: quiet resignation, scrolling LinkedIn at 11 PM wondering why winning stopped meaning something.
2. **The Ticking Clock** — Health scare activated. Decision window: 48 hours to 2 weeks. Needs clinical language and data, not inspiration.
3. **The Post-Detonation Executive** — Divorce, health crisis, or loss already happened. Needs a plan, not sympathy. Responds to structured protocol language.
4. **The High-Functioning Wreck** — Doesn't know he's declining. Everything looks fine from outside. Needs "mirror content" that shows him what others see.

**Before writing any post, ask Coach Cooz: "Which archetype is this for?"** Then calibrate the hook, emotional intensity, and CTA accordingly.

   - Emotional hook: actionable, "I can try this today"

### HOOK RULES (STRICT — NON-NEGOTIABLE)

- **Line 1:** 8 words maximum. Must create cognitive dissonance, mystery, or a pattern interrupt.
- **Line 2:** Empty line (white space forces the eye down).
- **Line 3:** 1 sentence maximum. Deepen the mystery or tension. Do NOT resolve the hook.
- The reader MUST click "See More" to get ANY payoff. If the hook resolves itself in the preview window, rewrite it.

**Hook types that work for Coach Cooz:**
- Negation pattern: "You're not burned out." (What am I then?)
- Contrast pattern: "Seven-figure business. Dead eyes." (Wait, what?)
- Pattern interrupt: "Every coach starts with mindset. I don't." (Why not?)
- Diagnostic statement: "Your belly is a printout of your discipline." (Ouch.)

### F-SHAPE FORMATTING (LinkedIn Readability)

LinkedIn readers scan in an F-pattern — they read the first 2-3 words of each line, then decide whether to keep reading. Format accordingly:

- **Front-load every line** with the highest-value words. "Your body is the infrastructure" not "The infrastructure that matters most is your body."
- **Single-line sentences** alternating with double-line sentences creates rhythm.
- **Heavy white space.** One thought per line. Never stack 3+ sentences in a paragraph.
- **Bold framework names only** — "The Daily Calibration," "Total Radius Ownership." Don't bold adjectives or emphasis words.
- **No bullet points in hook section.** Bullets signal "educational content" — hooks should signal "story" or "diagnosis."
