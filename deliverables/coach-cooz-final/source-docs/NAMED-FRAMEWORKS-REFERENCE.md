## 1. THE DAILY CALIBRATION
**What it is:** A 25-minute morning protocol that reactivates clarity before the day starts.

**Structure:**
- **10 min Silence** — Zero input. No phone, no music, no podcasts. Let the signal surface.
- **10 min Input** — Intentional learning. Read, listen, or review something that sharpens you.
- **5 min Output** — Write down the ONE thing that matters today. Not 5 things. One.

**Why it works:** Most executives start their day reactive — email, Slack, fires. The Daily Calibration puts you in proactive mode before the noise starts. You're calibrating the compass before you travel.

**Who needs it most:** All archetypes, but especially the Exhausted Operator (who starts every day already behind).

**When introduced:** Phase 1 (Week 1), maintained throughout.

## 2. TOTAL RADIUS OWNERSHIP
**What it is:** A personal accountability framework. You own 100% of what happens inside your radius.

**The concept:** Draw a circle around everything you can influence. That's your radius. Everything inside it is your responsibility — not your fault, but your responsibility.

**Key distinction:** Ownership ≠ blame. You're not saying everything is your fault. You're saying everything inside your radius is yours to fix.

**The unlock:** Every time you blame someone for your situation, you lock your own power behind their door. Ownership is the key that unlocks it.

**Application:**
- When something goes wrong → "What did I do — or not do — that contributed to this?"
- When you're stuck → "Am I outsourcing responsibility for this to someone else?"
- When you're resentful → "Did I communicate my needs, or did I assume they should know?"

**Who needs it most:** Isolated Builder (who blames the team) and Hollow Emperor (who blames the void).

**When introduced:** Phase 2 (Tenet 6), maintained throughout.

## 3. THE AFTER-ACTION REPORT (AAR)
**What it is:** A weekly diagnostic review framework used in coaching calls and self-assessment.

**Structure:**
1. **What was supposed to happen?** — Set clear targets (training sessions, nutrition compliance, sleep, business goals)
2. **What actually happened?** — Honest numbers. No rounding up.
3. **Why the gap?** — Diagnose the root cause (not the surface excuse)
4. **What do we adjust?** — One specific action for next week

**Why it works:** Accountability isn't about motivation — it's about visibility. You can't fix what you can't see. The AAR forces honest data into the conversation.

**Who uses it:** Coach Cooz uses it with every client. The Business Coach Gem runs a business-specific AAR. The Weekly AAR is the Sunday non-negotiable.

**When introduced:** Phase 2 (Tenet 5), maintained every Sunday.

## 4. THE BURIED CONTRACT
**What it is:** The unspoken agreement high-performers make with the people in their lives — without ever saying it out loud.

**The pattern:**
- "I'll build the empire. You'll appreciate me for it."
- "I'll sacrifice my body, my sleep, my presence. And in return, you'll recognize how hard I'm working."
- "I'll be the provider. You'll be the grateful partner."

**Why it's destructive:** Nobody signed this contract. Nobody agreed to the terms. But when the appreciation doesn't come — when the sacrifice isn't recognized — resentment builds. Silently. For years.

**The diagnosis:** The resentment isn't about the other person. It's about an agreement that only existed in your head.

**The fix:** Name it. Say it out loud. Replace the buried contract with a spoken one. "Here's what I need. Here's what I'm offering. Are we agreed?"

**Who needs it most:** Isolated Builder (who resents the team/partner for not recognizing sacrifice).

**Content pillar:** This is one of Coach Cooz's most provocative topics. High engagement on LinkedIn because executives immediately recognize themselves.

## 5. THE 4 ARCHETYPES
**What it is:** A diagnostic framework that identifies which version of dormancy a prospect is experiencing.

### Archetype 1: THE HOLLOW EMPEROR
- **Profile:** Has everything. Feels nothing.
- **What went dormant:** Purpose and meaning
- **How they present:** Successful on paper, empty behind it. "I have everything I said I wanted. I don't know why I feel like this."
- **Body state:** Probably decent shape physically, but disconnected from their body as a source of power
- **Key question:** "When did winning stop meaning something?"

### Archetype 2: THE EXHAUSTED OPERATOR
- **Profile:** Running on fumes. Hardware failing.
- **What went dormant:** Physical capacity and energy
- **How they present:** Gained weight, sleep destroyed, surviving on caffeine and willpower. Still performing but degrading.
- **Body state:** 20-40 lbs overweight, poor sleep, high cortisol, brain fog
- **Key question:** "What would change if your energy came back to where it was 10 years ago?"

### Archetype 3: THE FADING TITAN
- **Profile:** Was formidable. Now hesitates.
- **What went dormant:** Competitive edge and decisiveness
- **How they present:** Used to make bold calls. Now defaults to safe plays. Second-guesses. Deliberates when they used to decide.
- **Body state:** Physical decline mirrors the mental slowdown. Lost the physicality that powered the confidence.
- **Key question:** "When was the last time you made a call that scared you — and it worked?"

### Archetype 4: THE ISOLATED BUILDER
- **Profile:** Built everything alone. Now alone.
- **What went dormant:** Connection and vulnerability
- **How they present:** Successful but isolated. No real peers. Partner feels distant. Children don't know the real him.
- **Body state:** Often neglects body as "not a priority" — but the physical neglect mirrors the emotional isolation
- **Key question:** "Who actually knows how you feel right now?"

**How to use in Strategy Calls:**
1. Listen to the prospect's assessment answers
2. Assign archetype based on dominant dormancy pattern
3. Lead with their specific dormancy angle
4. Match to the relevant Tenet emphasis in the protocol
