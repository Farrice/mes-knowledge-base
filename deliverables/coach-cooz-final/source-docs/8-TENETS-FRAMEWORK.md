# THE 8 TENETS OF OPERATING SYSTEM INTEGRITY
## Coach Cooz's Resurrection Framework — Installed in Weeks 5-8

---

These are the 8 operating principles installed during Phase 2 of The Resurrection. They're not tactics. Not hacks. They're the operating system that keeps the hardware running after the initial reactivation.

Most men skip straight to #8 (Purpose). They want clarity, vision, meaning. But purpose without infrastructure collapses. You can't calibrate a dormant compass.

**The sequence matters:**
- Tenets 1-4: Reactivate the hardware
- Tenets 5-7: Install the frameworks
- Tenet 8: Recalibrate purpose

---

## TENET 1: SILENCE
**10 min/day. Calibrate the signal.**

Most men never stop. The radio is always on — notifications, meetings, podcasts, news, the constant hum of input. Silence brings the dormant operating system back online, one signal at a time.

This isn't meditation (though it can be). It's signal calibration. Sit with zero input. Let the noise clear. What surfaces in silence is the signal your body has been trying to send you for years.

**Daily practice:** 10 minutes. No phone. No music. No input. Just you and the signal.

---

## TENET 2: MOVEMENT
**3-5x/week. Reactivate the hardware.**

Not cardio for calories. Strength training for resilience. The body is the infrastructure — and infrastructure that doesn't get loaded eventually collapses under its own weight.

Resistance training is the single most effective reactivation tool because it sends a signal to every system in the body: *we're not in survival mode anymore. We're building.*

**Daily practice:** Structured training program (provided via TrueCoach). 3-5 sessions/week. Progressive overload. Calibrated to current capacity, not ego.

---

## TENET 3: FUEL
**200g protein/day. Feed the system.**

Not meal plans. Flexible nutrition. Compliance over perfection.

The goal is simple: give the body what it needs to rebuild. 200g protein is the foundation. Everything else — timing, macros, supplementation — is secondary to hitting this number consistently.

**Daily practice:** Track protein. Hit 200g. Everything else follows.

---

## TENET 4: RECOVERY
**7+ hours sleep. Let the system reset.**

Sleep is non-negotiable. Without recovery, the operating system stays dormant. Every function — decision-making, emotional regulation, physical performance, cognitive clarity — degrades without adequate sleep.

Most executives treat sleep as the variable they sacrifice first. It should be the last thing they touch.

**Daily practice:** 7+ hours. Sleep hygiene protocol. No screens 60 min before bed. Room cold and dark.

---

## TENET 5: ACCOUNTABILITY
**Weekly AAR. Review, adjust, repeat.**

The After-Action Report is the weekly diagnostic:
- What was supposed to happen?
- What actually happened?
- Why the gap?
- What do we adjust?

No man has ever coached himself to peak performance. Accountability isn't about motivation — it's about visibility. You can't fix what you can't see.

**Weekly practice:** AAR every Sunday. Honest numbers. No rounding up.

---

## TENET 6: RADICAL SELF-OWNERSHIP
**No blame. Total radius ownership.**

You own 100% of what happens inside your radius. No excuses. No victim mentality. Not because everything is your fault — but because everything inside your radius is your responsibility.

Every time you blame someone for your situation, you lock your own power behind their door. Ownership is the key that unlocks it.

**Daily practice:** When something goes wrong, the first question is: "What did I do — or not do — that contributed to this?" Start there. Every time.

---

## TENET 7: THE BENEVOLENT NO
**Boundaries without apology.**

Saying no to the wrong things is saying yes to the right things. Boundaries equal freedom.

Most high-performers say yes to everything because they built their success on being the person who always shows up. The result: they're everywhere but present nowhere. The Benevolent No is the skill of protecting your radius without guilt.

**Daily practice:** Before every commitment, ask: "Does this serve the man I'm reactivating — or the man I'm leaving behind?"

---

## TENET 8: PURPOSE CALIBRATION
**Goal =/= Purpose. Direction > target.**

A goal is a target you hit. Purpose is a direction you travel. Goals end. Purpose sustains.

Most men confuse the two. They hit every goal and still feel empty — because goals without purpose are just checkboxes. Purpose Calibration reconnects the daily work to the reason you built any of this in the first place.

This tenet comes last because it requires the hardware to be online. You cannot calibrate a compass that's dormant. Fix the body first. Then the direction becomes clear.

**Weekly practice:** During the AAR, ask: "Is what I did this week aligned with why I do any of this?"

---

## INSTALLATION SEQUENCE

| Phase | Weeks | Tenets | Focus |
|-------|-------|--------|-------|
| Phase 1: Stabilization | 1-4 | 1-4 | Reactivate the hardware (Silence, Movement, Fuel, Recovery) |
| Phase 2: Installation | 5-8 | 5-7 | Install the frameworks (Accountability, Ownership, Boundaries) |
| Phase 3: Integration | 9-13 | 8 | Recalibrate purpose on top of restored hardware |

All 8 Tenets run concurrently after installation. The sequence is about when each one is *introduced and emphasized* — not when it starts and stops.

---

*The 8 Tenets are the operating system. The body is the hardware. You can't run new software on dormant hardware — and you can't sustain reactivated hardware without an operating system to run it.*
