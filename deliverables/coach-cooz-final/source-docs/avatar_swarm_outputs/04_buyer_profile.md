## PSYCHOGRAPHIC BUYING LAYERS

### Layer 1: Identity Beliefs That Enable or Block the Purchase

**Beliefs That ENABLE a $10K Purchase**:

1. **"I invest in specialists"** — This man pays for elite lawyers, executive coaches, financial advisors, concierge medicine. He's already in the habit of paying 10-50x market rate for personalized, high-caliber guidance. The Resurrection Protocol slots into this existing mental model.

2. **"My body is enterprise infrastructure"** — He runs the company. If the CEO goes down, everything goes down. He's started to see health investment as a business expense, not a personal luxury.

3. **"Generic solutions are beneath me"** — He doesn't use public gyms. He doesn't buy mass-market products. Personalization isn't a nice-to-have — it's a requirement commensurate with his status.

4. **"Other operators I respect do this"** — Peer validation is the ultimate buying signal. If he learns that another executive at his level invested in this kind of program, resistance dissolves.

**Beliefs That BLOCK the Purchase**:

1. **"I should be able to figure this out myself"** — The competence identity. Paying for health guidance feels like admitting incompetence in a domain he believes should be simple.

2. **"Health stuff is for people who are really sick"** — He doesn't identify as "unhealthy." He's "busy." Reframing health investment from treatment to optimization is essential.

3. **"This is a personal trainer with better marketing"** — If the offering looks, sounds, or smells like fitness coaching, the $10K price triggers fraud radar.

4. **"Vulnerability undermines authority"** — Admitting he needs health help — especially to another man — threatens his identity as someone who has everything handled.

### Layer 3: The $10K Price Psychology

**Why $10K Works** (Not Despite the Price — Because of It):

1. **Self-selection filter**: The price eliminates tire-kickers. Anyone who says yes at $10K is pre-qualified — they have resources, they're serious, and they'll actually do the work (because they can't afford not to).

2. **Commitment device**: A $10K investment rewires the buyer's identity. He's no longer "considering" a health change. He *invested* in one. The sunk cost becomes fuel, not regret.

3. **Status signal**: Paying $10K for health optimization is itself a status marker. "I work with a $10K executive health specialist" carries social cachet that "I have a personal trainer" does not.

4. **Perceived expertise calibration**: In the client's mental model, $200/month = commodity, $2K/month = specialist, $10K engagement = elite. The price *is* the credibility.

**Price Objection Reframing Script**:
> "You spent $50K last year on legal counsel to protect your company. You spent $100K+ on technology infrastructure to keep it running. The Resurrection Protocol is a $10K investment in the single piece of infrastructure that runs *everything else* — including you. When that infrastructure degrades, every dollar you spent on the company becomes less efficient. This isn't an expense. It's the highest-leverage investment you'll make this year."
