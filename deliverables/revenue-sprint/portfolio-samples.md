## The One Individual: Dr. Evelyn Thorne

*Not a demographic segment. A specific person you can picture, speak to, and serve.*

**Background:** Ex-McKinsey partner, now coaching Fortune 500 CEOs on leadership transformation. Charges $20K per engagement. 15+ years executive experience before coaching. Based in a major metro, travels frequently for speaking and client work.

**Income:** $200K-$500K+ annually from coaching engagements
**LinkedIn:** 10K-25K followers, posts inconsistently (2-3x/month instead of 3-5x/week)
**Content quality when she does post:** Insightful but rushed, often sharing others' content rather than creating her own

---

## 3D Consumer Posture

### 1. OCCUPATION (The role she plays in the world)

She occupies the role of the **"Strategic Oracle"** -- the trusted, insightful advisor whose words carry immense weight in private rooms. Board members defer to her judgment. CEOs call her before making major decisions. Her authority is absolute in 1:1 settings.

**The gap:** Her private authority doesn't translate to her public presence. On LinkedIn, she looks like every other coach -- occasional shares, generic commentary, sporadic original posts that don't reflect the depth of her thinking.

**What she wants to occupy:** The definitive voice in executive leadership development on LinkedIn. A beacon of foresight whose posts get saved, shared, and referenced in board meetings.

### 2. ACTIVITY (The rituals and behaviors that define her days)

**Current reality:**
- 60-hour weeks packed with client calls, board meetings, speaking engagements
- Morning starts with Slack, email triage, and prep for the day's sessions
- Content creation happens in stolen moments -- late at night, between meetings, on planes
- When she does write, she agonizes over every word (perfectionism + impostor fear)
- Engages deeply in 1:1 client relationships and peer referral networks
- Attends 2-3 industry conferences per quarter

**Desired reality:**
- 3-5 thought-provoking LinkedIn posts per week that sound exactly like her
- Consistent engagement from C-suite leaders in her comments
- A steady stream of inbound inquiries from ideal clients who say "I've been following your content"
- Zero time spent on content creation -- it just happens, as if by magic

**The activity gap:** She *does* create brilliant content -- in coaching sessions, in keynotes, in private conversations. But none of it gets captured, structured, or published. Her best insights live in her head and die in private rooms.

### 3. THOUGHT PROCESS (The internal logic about what feels aligned vs. inauthentic)

**Her internal monologue:**

> "My insights are invaluable, but my time is finite. I know I *should* be more visible on LinkedIn to capture the next wave of C-suite clients, but the thought of crafting compelling content consistently, day after day, feels like another full-time job I don't have."

> "I fear my current sporadic presence makes me look less active or less 'thought leader' than my peers. Sarah just posted another viral take on leadership. David has a newsletter with 50K subscribers. I have better insights than both of them but nobody knows I exist online."

> "I need my content to sound *exactly like me* -- to carry my gravitas, my specific vocabulary, my unique psychological insights. I can't afford to be generic or, worse, inauthentic."

> "I've looked at AI tools. They produce drivel. I've talked to agencies. They don't understand my world. I need someone who can think at my level and translate what's in my head into what should be on the screen."

**The thought process paradox:** She believes thought leadership is essential for her business growth, but she also believes that outsourcing it would be inauthentic. These two beliefs are in direct conflict, and the conflict keeps her stuck -- doing nothing.

---

## The Authenticity Paradox (Core Psychological Barrier)

This is the central tension that every agent identified unanimously:

**The desire:** To be recognized as a definitive thought leader, attracting C-suite clients through a commanding LinkedIn presence.

**The fear:** That outsourcing content creation will expose her as inauthentic, undermining the very credibility she's built over decades.

**The result:** Paralysis. She does nothing, posting sporadically, losing ground to less-qualified competitors who are more active online.

**What she cannot yet say:**

> "I need a way to clone my unique wisdom and voice into the digital realm, so I can scale my impact without burning out, and without being exposed as inauthentic."

**What would unlock her:**

The realization that a partner who uses *her* voice notes, *her* frameworks, *her* stories, and *her* specific language isn't "faking it" -- they're *translating* her private brilliance into public impact. The best externally-produced content is MORE authentic than what she'd produce alone, because it removes the friction between what she thinks and what she publishes.

---

## Buying Triggers (When She's Ready to Act)

1. **Time desperation:** "I haven't posted in 3 weeks and just lost a prospect to a competitor with a bigger LinkedIn presence"
2. **Competitor envy:** A peer just went viral, got a book deal, or landed a keynote -- all from LinkedIn content
3. **Failed DIY attempt:** She tried AI tools, produced generic content, felt embarrassed, and stopped
4. **Team pressure:** Her COO or marketing person says "You really need to be posting more consistently"
5. **Revenue plateau:** Client acquisition has slowed and she can trace it to lack of inbound leads
6. **Referral fatigue:** She's tired of relying entirely on word-of-mouth and wants a predictable pipeline

## Behavioral Economics Analysis (Rory Sutherland's Framework)

### Why prospects resist (and how to counter)

| Behavioral Barrier | Strategic Reframe |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Loss aversion** -- "What if it doesn't work and I've wasted $7,500?" | Risk-reversal guarantee removes the loss scenario entirely |
| **Status quo bias** -- "My referral network is working well enough" | "How many clients are you *not* getting because they found a more visible competitor?" |
| **Authenticity reactance** -- "Outsourcing this feels like cheating" | "Great leaders delegate to amplify. Your COO runs operations so you can focus on strategy. This is the same principle applied to visibility." |
| **Endowment effect** -- Overvaluing their current approach | A free diagnostic shows them the gap between their current output and their potential |
| **Social proof vacuum** -- "Nobody I know uses a service like this" | "The most visible practitioners in your space almost all have support. They just don't talk about it." |

### Pricing Psychology

- **Setup fee ($1,000)** = Costly signal that this is serious, not a commodity
- **Monthly retainer ($2,500)** = Less than one client engagement ($15K+), framed as "this investment pays for itself with a single new client"
- **3-month minimum** = Commitment device that gives the system time to work
- **Outcome guarantee** = Transforms the purchase from "gamble" to "guaranteed return"

## AVATAR 1: THE NUMB OPERATOR

### The One-Line Truth
**He's winning the game but can't feel the wins.** Revenue is up. Respect is earned. But when someone asks "what does it feel like?" -- silence, then: "empty."

### Demographics
- **Age**: 38-48
- **Income**: $200K-$500K
- **Role**: VP, SVP, Managing Director, or founder of a company doing $2M-$15M
- **Family**: Married 8-15 years. 1-3 kids (often under 12)
- **Location**: Suburbs of major metros (Dallas, Atlanta, Denver, Scottsdale, Chicago)
- **Education**: MBA or equivalent terminal degree
- **Health Status**: 25-40 lbs overweight. Hasn't trained consistently in 3+ years. Sleep 4-5.5 hrs/night. Lives on caffeine and willpower.

### Consumer Posture (How He Enters)

**Posture**: Resigned High-Performer
- He doesn't enter from crisis -- he enters from quiet resignation. He's accepted that "this is just what it costs to win."
- He's **not googling solutions**. He's scrolling LinkedIn at 11 PM, half-reading posts about "being present," and feeling the dull recognition that those posts are about him.
- **Entry emotion**: Not panic. Not desperation. *Numbness*. The absence of something he can't name.

**Trigger Events:**
- His wife says: "You're here but you're not here." (Not angry -- *sad*.)
- His kid stops asking him to play. (Not because the kid is busy -- because the kid gave up.)
- He catches his reflection in a hotel bathroom and doesn't recognize the body looking back.
- A peer his age has a health scare (heart event, cancer diagnosis, hospitalization).
- He cancels something meaningful (anniversary, kid's game, vacation) for work -- and feels *nothing*.

**What He Actually Searches (Reddit/Forum Verbatim):**

> *"How do other executives deal with feeling nothing? Like, objectively everything is great but I feel like I'm watching my own life from the outside."*

> *"Anyone else feel like they're on autopilot? Used to be excited about closing deals. Now I close them and feel... nothing."*

> *"My wife told me she misses who I was 10 years ago. That hit different."*

### Deep Psychology

**Core Identity Conflict**: He built his identity around achievement. But achievement stopped producing the internal reward. He's addicted to a drug that stopped working -- and the withdrawal symptom is emptiness.

**The Story He Tells Himself:**
- "This is just the cost of winning. Everyone at this level feels this way."
- "I'll fix my body when things slow down." (They never slow down.)
- "I don't need a coach -- I've built a company. I can figure this out myself."
- "Maybe I just need a vacation." (He takes the vacation. Nothing changes.)

**The Story He Won't Tell Anyone:**
- "I'm terrified that my best years are behind me -- and I didn't even enjoy them."
- "My wife is one conversation away from leaving. I can feel it."
- "I used to be the guy in the room with the most energy. Now I'm the guy who leaves early."
- "I don't know when I stopped caring about how I look. I just stopped."

**Hidden Fears (Excavated):**
1. **Obsolescence fear**: "If I lose my edge, I lose everything -- the money, the respect, the relevance."
2. **Identity collapse**: "If I'm not the guy who grinds, who am I?"
3. **Irreversibility panic**: "What if I've done permanent damage? What if 45 is too late?"
4. **Intimacy terror**: "If I actually slow down and feel things, the pain might break me."
