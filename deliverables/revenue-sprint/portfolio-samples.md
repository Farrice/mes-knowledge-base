## PORTFOLIO SAMPLE -- LinkedIn Content Series | Written by Farrice Cain

# LinkedIn Content Series: 10-Post Narrative Arc

**Client:** A leadership transformation coach targeting high-income executives ($200K-$500K+)
**Format:** 10-post LinkedIn series, published weekly over 10 weeks
**Strategy:** TV-style 4-act narrative arc -- each post builds on the last, creating compound engagement and a cliffhanger loop that brings readers back every week

---

## Series Structure (10 Episodes)

### ACT 1: THE DORMANCY (Posts 1-3)
Identify what went dormant. Make them see themselves. Build quiet recognition.

### ACT 2: THE DIAGNOSIS (Posts 4-6)
Why it went dormant. The cost of ignoring it. Reframe the problem.

### ACT 3: THE REACTIVATION (Posts 7-9)
The body-first protocol. Show the path. Prove it works.

### ACT 4: THE INVITATION (Post 10)
The filter. The offer. Leave them standing at a binary door.

---

### Why This Structure Works

1. **Narrative compounds** -- each post builds the next, deepening the diagnosis before offering the solution
2. **Cliffhangers every week** -- TV-series structure keeps them coming back
3. **Beneficial tension arc** -- Recognition (1-3) > Diagnosis (4-6) > Hope + Proof (7-9) > Decision (10)
4. **Language stays in the client's voice** -- direct, diagnostic, confessional. Short sentences. Second-person. Metaphors that anchor. Not preachy.
5. **Ends with a polarizing CTA** -- filters for serious buyers, repels motivation-seekers

---

### Theme: You're Not Failing. Something Just Went Dormant.

You're not in crisis.

Because it still shows up. In flashes.

Then it goes back to sleep.

You've been calling this burnout.
You've been calling this stress.
You've been calling this "just how it is at this level."

It's none of those things.

Something in you -- the thing that made you dangerous, magnetic, and fully alive -- got archived.

And no one is talking about how to bring it back.

That's what this series is about.

Over the next 10 weeks, I'm going to show you what went dormant, why it happened, and the exact protocol I use to reactivate it.

Not motivation.
Not mindset hacks.
Not another morning routine.

A system to wake up the version of you that built everything -- and then got buried under it.

Next week: the silent agreement that's draining your power without you knowing it.

## FULL POST -- Post 5: "The Circle of Ownership"
### Theme: Total Radius Ownership as Reactivation

Your body is a printout of your discipline.

Your bank account is a printout of your decisions.

Your relationships are a printout of your ability to lead them.

Now look down. Everything inside a 3-foot radius is yours.

It's tempting to look outside the circle.

"My dad was absent."
"The economy imploded."
"My partner doesn't understand."

I don't dismiss those. They happened.

But you can't reactivate from outside the circle.

If you were dealt a bad hand -- that wasn't your choice.
But playing the next hand? That's yours.

Stop arguing with the weather. Fix the roof.

I call this Total Radius Ownership.

Not because everything is your fault. But because everything inside your radius is your responsibility.

And here's what I discovered when I started expanding that radius.

When I stopped outsourcing the blame for losing her -- I could rebuild how I show up in relationships.

When I stopped outsourcing the blame for my finances -- I could rebuild the systems that generate income.

When I stopped outsourcing the blame for my body -- I could rebuild the daily protocol that transforms it.

Every inch of radius you reclaim, a piece of your dormant power comes back online.

But ownership without tools is just guilt.

That's why I built a protocol. Because knowing you're responsible doesn't help if you don't know what to do about it.

Next week: the signal most men have turned off -- and why it's the difference between operating with direction and operating in circles.

# Email Nurture Sequence: 7-Email + 3-Email Close

**Client:** A leadership transformation coach targeting founders/executives ($150K+)
**Format:** 7-email cold-lead nurture (12 days) + 3-email post-call close sequence (5 days)
**Strategy:** Psychological arc mapping -- each email advances the prospect through a deliberate emotional progression from empathy to decision

---

## Sequence Overview

### Email Journey Map
1. **Email 1 (Day 0)**: Welcome + Diagnosis -- build trust, name the pattern
2. **Email 2 (Day 2)**: Why the Body First -- educate, differentiate from mindset coaches
3. **Email 3 (Day 3)**: Origin Story -- vulnerability, credibility, personal proof
4. **Email 4 (Day 5)**: Case Study -- proof with metrics
5. **Email 5 (Day 8)**: The Framework -- authority, show the system
6. **Email 6 (Day 10)**: Price Justification -- damaging admission, pre-handle objection
7. **Email 7 (Day 12)**: The Invitation -- soft close, application CTA

### Post-Call Close Sequence
8. **Email 8 (Day 1 after call)**: Summary -- recap, present offer
9. **Email 9 (Day 3 after call)**: The Decision -- reframe inaction
10. **Email 10 (Day 5 after call)**: Final Invitation -- last touch

### Psychological Arc
- **Emails 1-2**: "You're not broken -- something powerful went dormant" (empathy, diagnosis)
- **Email 3**: "I lived this. Here's my story." (origin, trust)
- **Email 4**: "This is what reactivation looks like" (proof)
- **Email 5**: "Here's the system" (authority)
- **Email 6**: "Here's why it costs what it costs" (price justification)
- **Email 7**: "Let's talk" (invitation)
- **Emails 8-10**: "You've seen the roadmap -- now decide" (close)

## AVATAR 1: THE NUMB OPERATOR (Complete)

### The One-Line Truth
**He's winning the game but can't feel the wins.** Revenue is up. Respect is earned. But when someone asks "what does it feel like?" -- silence, then: "empty."

### Demographics
- **Age**: 38-48
- **Income**: $200K-$500K
- **Role**: VP, SVP, Managing Director, or founder of a company doing $2M-$15M
- **Family**: Married 8-15 years. 1-3 kids (often under 12)
- **Location**: Suburbs of major metros (Dallas, Atlanta, Denver, Scottsdale, Chicago)
- **Education**: MBA or equivalent terminal degree
- **Health Status**: 25-40 lbs overweight. Hasn't trained consistently in 3+ years. Sleep 4-5.5 hrs/night. Lives on caffeine and willpower.

### Consumer Posture (How He Enters the Buying Journey)

**Posture**: Resigned High-Performer
- He doesn't enter from crisis -- he enters from quiet resignation. He's accepted that "this is just what it costs to win."
- He's **not googling solutions**. He's scrolling LinkedIn at 11 PM, half-reading posts about "being present," and feeling the dull recognition that those posts are about him.
- **Entry emotion**: Not panic. Not desperation. *Numbness*. The absence of something he can't name.

**Trigger Events** (what finally moves him from passive to active):
- His wife says: "You're here but you're not here." (Not angry -- *sad*.)
- His kid stops asking him to play. (Not because the kid is busy -- because the kid gave up.)
- He catches his reflection in a hotel bathroom and doesn't recognize the body looking back.
- A peer his age has a health scare (heart event, cancer diagnosis, hospitalization).
- He cancels something meaningful (anniversary, kid's game, vacation) for work -- and feels *nothing*.

**What He Actually Searches** (Reddit/Forum Verbatim):

> *"How do other executives deal with feeling nothing? Like, objectively everything is great but I feel like I'm watching my own life from the outside."*

> *"Anyone else feel like they're on autopilot? Used to be excited about closing deals. Now I close them and feel... nothing."*

> *"My wife told me she misses who I was 10 years ago. That hit different."*

### Deep Psychology

**Core Identity Conflict**: He built his identity around achievement. But achievement stopped producing the internal reward. He's addicted to a drug that stopped working -- and the withdrawal symptom is emptiness.

**The Story He Tells Himself:**
- "This is just the cost of winning. Everyone at this level feels this way."
- "I'll fix my body when things slow down." (They never slow down.)
- "I don't need a coach -- I've built a company. I can figure this out myself."
- "Maybe I just need a vacation." (He takes the vacation. Nothing changes.)

**The Story He Won't Tell Anyone:**
- "I'm terrified that my best years are behind me -- and I didn't even enjoy them."
- "My wife is one conversation away from leaving. I can feel it."
- "I used to be the guy in the room with the most energy. Now I'm the guy who leaves early."
- "I don't know when I stopped caring about how I look. I just stopped."

**Hidden Fears (Excavated):**
1. **Obsolescence fear**: "If I lose my edge, I lose everything -- the money, the respect, the relevance."
2. **Identity collapse**: "If I'm not the guy who grinds, who am I?"
3. **Irreversibility panic**: "What if I've done permanent damage? What if 45 is too late?"
4. **Intimacy terror**: "If I actually slow down and feel things, the pain might break me."

**Hidden Desires (Excavated):**
1. **To feel alive again** -- not happy, not motivated, just *alive*. To feel the wins.
2. **To be physically formidable** -- to walk into a room and have his body match his title
3. **To reconnect with his wife** -- not through "date nights" but through *presence*
4. **To have his kids look at him with admiration** -- not for his money, but for who he is

**Step 1: Research first, write second.** I built 4 complete avatar dossiers before writing a single word of copy. Every piece of content is rooted in real buyer psychology -- not assumptions.

**Step 2: Voice capture, not voice invention.** I documented the client's natural speaking patterns, metaphors, and vocabulary from interviews. The copy sounds like them because it IS them -- structured and refined, but authentically theirs.

**Step 3: Architecture, not atoms.** Every deliverable connects to every other deliverable. The LinkedIn series feeds the email sequence. The email sequence feeds the strategy call. The strategy call feeds the offer pages. Nothing exists in isolation.
