## 1. BRAND FOUNDATION

**The Resurrection Coach** is the only executive health coaching practice that treats the body as biological infrastructure — not a vanity project. For leaders earning $150K+ who optimized everything except themselves, we deliver a 90-day, body-first operating system reset that restores the physical edge buried under years of empire-building.

### The One-Line Truth

> "Something powerful in you went dormant. We bring it back."

### Brand Promise

In 90 days, you will inhabit a body that matches your ambition — and an experience of being alive that you forgot was possible.

### Brand Pillars

| Pillar | Expression |
|--------|-----------|
| **Body First** | We start with the hardware. You cannot think your way out of a biological failure. |

## 2. CATEGORY CODE MAP

### What Executive Coaching Currently Looks Like

*Using Oren's Category Code methodology: mapping the visual, tonal, behavioral, and cultural codes of the "executive coaching" category to identify where we conform and where we break.*

| Code Layer | Industry Default | What Most Coaches Do |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Visual** | Navy blue/charcoal suits, corporate headshots, boardroom imagery, gradient banners | "I'm serious and professional" → visually forgettable |
| **Tonal** | Motivational, high-energy, "unlock your potential" | Tony Robbins derivative → executive skeptics tune out |
| **Behavioral** | Group programs, mastermind models, webinar funnels, free challenges | "Scale to 100 clients" → doesn't feel exclusive |
| **Cultural** | Self-help culture repackaged for executives: journaling, visualization, "mindset shifts" | Starts from mind → misses the body entirely |
| **Pricing** | $3K-$8K for most online coaches, $15K-$50K for in-person consultancies | Either too cheap (no perceived value) or too clinical (medical tourism) |

### Where "The Resurrection Coach" Breaks the Code

| Code Layer | Category Default | Resurrection Inversion |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Visual** | Corporate/motivational | **"Private club"** — composed, architectural, understated power |
| **Tonal** | "You can do it! Let's crush it!" | **Clinical warmth** — peer-level, data-first, mythic undertone |
| **Behavioral** | Group programs + webinar funnels | **1-on-1 only. Application-based.** The scarcity is real. |

4. **The technology metaphor.** "Hardware/software/operating system" speaks executive language without the self-help associations that repel this buyer.

## 4. BRAND VOICE SYSTEM

### Voice Architecture

| Dimension | Setting | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Authority level** | Specialist, not guru | *"The data shows..."* not *"I believe..."* |
| **Emotional register** | Composed intensity | Calm surface, deep weight underneath. Never shouting. |
| **Formality** | Business-elevated | Not casual, not stiff. The tone of an executive dinner, not a board meeting. |
| **Warmth** | Present but contained | Shows up in understanding, not enthusiasm. *"I know"* > *"I feel you bro!"* |
| **Humor** | Dry, rare, earned | One-liners only. Never sarcastic about pain. |
| **Rhythm** | Short. Declarative. Then expansive when it matters. | Alternating sentence length. Staccato bursts with occasional long insights. |

### Vocabulary Bank

#### ✅ ALWAYS USE (The Resurrection Lexicon)

| Category | Words & Phrases |
|----------|----------------|
| **State language** | Dormant, buried, silent, offline, hibernating, untapped, latent |
| **Process language** | Restore, reclaim, reactivate, resurrect, install, rebuild, re-architect |
| **Technical metaphor** | Operating system, hardware, software, infrastructure, protocol, diagnostic, installation, calibration |
| **Executive framing** | Board-level, strategic asset, ROI, performance ceiling, decision-making bandwidth, competitive advantage |
| **Outcome language** | Edge, presence, clarity, sharpness, formidable, composed, alive |
| **Relationship** | "My wife said she has her husband back." "My kids look at me differently." "I'm present." |

#### ❌ NEVER USE (The Blacklist)

| Category | Banned Words & Phrases | Why |
|----------|----------------------|-----|
| **Crisis/shame** | Broken, failing, crashed, corrupted, dead, destroyed, weak | Implies the executive is damaged — instant exit |
| **Bro culture** | Crush it, beast mode, no excuses, grind, hustle, warrior, king | Wrong cultural register — repels executives |
| **Generic motivation** | Unlock your potential, level up, 10x, breakthrough, mindset shift | Every coach says this — invisible language |
| **Filler** | Honestly, literally, absolutely, amazing, incredible, game-changing | Undermines specialist authority |
| **Self-help** | Journey, sacred, divine feminine/masculine, soul, spirit, alignment | Wrong cultural container for the buyer |
| **Hard sell** | Limited spots, act now, don't wait, this is your sign, DM me "READY" | Destroys premium positioning |

### Voice by Platform

| Platform | Tone Calibration |
|---------|-----------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Most authoritative. Clinical precision. Data-backed. Short-form (150-250 words). One insight per post. |
| **Instagram** | Slightly warmer. Visual-first. One-liner captions with occasional micro-stories. |
| **Website** | Most comprehensive. Peer-level conviction. Long-form authority copy. |
| **Sales call** | Most human. Questions-first (NEPQ). Listening > talking. Composed validation. |
| **Email** | Personal, direct, no-fluff. One idea per email. Always a clear CTA. |
| **Voxer** | Most casual of all channels. Still clinical, but warmer. |

### Writing Mechanics

1. **Open with recognition, not diagnosis.** Show the buyer their own experience — don't tell them what's wrong with them.
2. **One idea per piece of content.** Never stack multiple insights.
3. **Use the second person sparingly.** Too much "you" feels accusatory. Alternate with third-person observation: *"Most executives..."*
4. **End with implication, not instruction.** Don't say "Do this." Say "That's the difference between 90 days from now... and 90 more days of this."
5. **Line breaks are punctuation.** Use whitespace to control reading pace. Short paragraphs. Single-sentence lines for emphasis.

#### Color Do's and Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use navy as the primary power color | Use bright red, neon, or electric blue |
| Use gold sparingly — 5% maximum | Gold backgrounds or gold text on white |
| Alternate navy sections with cream/white | All-dark design (reads "underground fitness") |
| Keep backgrounds either navy or cream | Use gradient backgrounds |
| Use slate for body text | Use pure black body text on white |

### Typography System

| Usage | Font | Weight | Size Range | Rationale |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Primary Heading (H1)** | **Playfair Display** | Bold (700) | 36-56px | Refined serif = authority + taste |
| **Secondary Heading (H2)** | **Playfair Display** | SemiBold (600) | 24-36px | Consistency within hierarchy |
| **Subheading (H3)** | **Inter** or **DM Sans** | SemiBold (600) | 18-24px | Clean bridge between serif/sans |
| **Body Text** | **Inter** or **DM Sans** | Regular (400) | 16-18px | Maximum readability, modern clean |
| **Caption/Label** | **Inter** | Medium (500) | 12-14px | Letter-spaced for metadata |
| **Pull Quote / Highlight** | **Playfair Display** | Italic (400i) | 22-28px | Elegance for testimonials |
| **CTA Buttons** | **Inter** | SemiBold (600) | 14-16px | All-caps, letter-spaced (0.05-0.1em) |

#### Typography Rules

1. **Never use more than 2 fonts.** Playfair Display (headings) + Inter (everything else).
2. **Headings are never all-caps.** Title case only. All-caps is reserved for tiny labels/CTAs.
3. **Line height: 1.5-1.7 for body, 1.1-1.2 for headings.**
4. **Maximum content width: 720px.** Text should never span edge-to-edge.
5. **Pull quotes get Playfair Italic** — always at the midpoint of long content.

### Logo Usage

**Current logo**: Sword/lightning emblem with "COACH COOZ" text.

**Recommended evolution**:
- **Retire the sword/lightning.** This reads "warrior coaching" — wrong register.
- **Commission a typographic wordmark**: "THE RESURRECTION COACH" in Playfair Display, tight letter-spacing, horizontal format.
- **Optional brandmark**: A subtle "R" monogram in the same typographic family. Clean, architectural. Think consulting firm, not fitness brand.
- **Color variations**: Deep navy on cream, cream/white on navy, gold monochrome (business cards only).

## 6. PHOTOGRAPHY & MEDIA

### Photo Direction

*Think: "Executive who happens to be in extraordinary physical condition" — not "Fitness guy targeting executives."*

| Dimension | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| **Setting** | Clean, architectural environments — hotel lobbies, penthouse views, studio spaces, training facilities with natural light | Gyms with equipment clutter, garage gyms, bedrooms, cars |
| **Wardrobe** | Well-fitted dark clothing (black/navy henley, fitted tee, dark jeans). Tailored casual. Blazer + clean sneakers. | Tank tops, cutoffs, gym wear with visible logos, bright colors |
| **Expression** | Composed. Thoughtful. Direct eye contact or 3/4 profile looking off-frame. Quiet confidence. | Flexing, grimacing, arms crossed, pointing at camera, "intense" faces |
| **Lighting** | Directional. Window light preferred. Moody but not dark. Professional studio quality. | Flash photography, ring lights (YouTube look), fluorescent gym lighting |
| **Body language** | Standing with natural confidence. Seated with purpose. Hands visible and relaxed. | Arms crossed, fists, aggressive lean-in, sitting on equipment |
| **Composition** | Rule of thirds. Generous negative space. Subject is 40-60% of frame. | Tight crops, selfie angles, full-body mirror shots |

### Photo Hierarchy (Priority Order)

1. **Professional headshot** (LinkedIn/website) — navy henley or dark blazer, clean background, warm directional light
2. **Coaching contextualized** — working with a client (both dressed appropriately, not mid-exercise)
3. **Lifestyle authority** — in a premium environment that signals the life his clients want
4. **Results-oriented** — before/after of *executive clients* (with permission, professional photos)
5. **Behind the protocol** — clean office setup, nutrition planning, the systems behind the process

### Photo Anti-Patterns (Hard No)

- ❌ Shirtless posts
- ❌ Mirror selfies (any setting)
- ❌ iPhone bedroom videos
- ❌ Gym selfies with equipment
- ❌ "Before" photos of himself (invites pity, undermines current authority)
- ❌ Stock photos of any kind
- ❌ Female client transformations (conflicting target + positioning)
- ❌ Photo collages or amateur grid layouts

### Video Standards

| Element | Standard |
|---------|---------|
| **Background** | Clean, intentional. Neutral wall, bookshelf, or architectural backdrop. Zero clutter. |
| **Framing** | Chest-up for talking heads. Wide for movement/coaching demonstrations. |
| **Audio** | External mic required. No echo, no background noise. |
| **Lighting** | Key light at 45°, fill light opposite. Natural window light acceptable if consistent. |
| **Length** | LinkedIn: 60-90 sec. Instagram Reels: 15-45 sec. Website: 90-120 sec. |
| **Editing** | Clean cuts. No flashing transitions, no "bro-edit" effects. Subtle text overlays in Inter font. |

### Instagram Carousel Template (Slide-by-Slide)

| Slide | Content | Design |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 — Hook** | Bold statement or question. 1-2 lines max. | Navy background, Playfair heading, large text centered |
| **2 — Recognition** | "You might notice..." or "This is what it looks like when..." | Cream background, Inter body text, left-aligned |
| **3-5 — Insight** | One idea per slide. Short paragraphs. Data if applicable. | Alternating navy/cream. Consistent font sizing. |
| **6 — Implication** | "The truth is..." or "Here's what nobody tells you..." | Navy background, Playfair italic, pull-quote style |
| **7 — CTA or Close** | Save for later / Follow for more / Link in bio (rotate) | Navy background, gold accent on CTA button/text |

**Carousel Design Rules**:
- Always 7 slides minimum, 10 maximum
- Consistent margins: 60px from all edges
- Brand colors only — no additional colors
- No stock photos inside carousels — clean typography only
- Logo watermark bottom-right on slide 1 only

### Instagram Reel Template

```
[0:00-0:03] — HOOK (text on screen + voice)
Pattern interrupt or surprising statement.

[0:03-0:20] — INSIGHT
One concept. Delivered with composed authority.
B-roll of hands, environment, or demonstration.

[0:20-0:30] — CLOSE
Implication or reframe. Never a hard CTA.
End on a strong line. Cut to black.
```

**Reel Production Rules**:
- No "talking head for 60 seconds" — use B-roll cuts every 3-5 seconds
- Captions always on (60% of viewers watch silent)
- Font: Inter Bold, white with dark shadow, bottom-center
- Background music: Low, cinematic, ambient (not pop/trending audio)
- No trending dances, pointing, or mimicry

### Email Template (Nurture Sequence)

```
Subject: [Observation-based — never clickbait]

[Name],

[Opening — 1-2 sentences. Recognition or observation.]

[Body — 3-4 short paragraphs. Story, data, or insight. 
One concept only.]

[Close — 1 sentence. Direct. Clear CTA OR 
implication to sit with.]

— Acusio

P.S. [Optional — human aside or case study teaser]
```

**Email Rules**:
- Plain text only. No images, no HTML templates.
- Subject lines under 50 characters.
- Under 350 words per email.
- CTA in maximum 2 of every 5 emails. The other 3 are pure value.
- Always sign "Acusio" — never "Coach Cooz" in emails.

## 10. BRAND CONSISTENCY CHECKLIST

### The One-Line Test

Before publishing anything — a post, an email, a page, a DM — run this filter:

> **"Would a $500K/year VP of Operations read this and think: 'This person understands my world?'"**

If no → rewrite.
If yes → publish.

### Quick-Reference Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] **Language check**: Zero banned words (broken, failing, crush it, grind, etc.)
- [ ] **Tone check**: Clinical warmth. Not motivational. Not aggressive. Not preachy.
- [ ] **Visual check**: On-brand colors only (navy, cream, slate, gold accent). No off-palette colors.
- [ ] **Font check**: Playfair Display for headings, Inter for body. No other fonts.
- [ ] **Photo check**: Composed, directional, professional. No gym selfies, no mirror shots.
- [ ] **Metaphor check**: Technology metaphor used (hardware, software, OS). No sports/military metaphors.
- [ ] **Identity check**: "Dormant" framing, not "broken" framing. Recognition, not diagnosis.
- [ ] **Audience check**: Would a $500K VP feel respected by this? Or lectured?
- [ ] **CTA check**: No hard sell. CTA max 1 in 5 posts/emails. Application-based, not "Buy now."
- [ ] **Proof check**: Any claims backed by specific data, case studies, or named sources?

### Brand Red Flags — Instant Stop

If ANY of these appear, the content must be reworked before publishing:

| Red Flag | Why It's Fatal |
|----------|---------------|
| "System Failure" as a diagnosis | Shames the buyer — they leave |
| Shirtless photos | Reads "fitness influencer" — wrong category |
| "Limited spots available!" | Hard-sell destroys premium positioning |
| Testimonials without names/titles | Zero credibility for $10K buyers |
| Multiple CTAs in one piece | Confusion kills conversion |
| "You are broken/failing/crashed" | Direct identity attack — permanent exit |
| Female client transformations | Audience mismatch on a male executive brand |
| Google Forms | Signals budget operation, not premium service |
| Trending audio/dances on Reels | Wrong cultural container completely |
