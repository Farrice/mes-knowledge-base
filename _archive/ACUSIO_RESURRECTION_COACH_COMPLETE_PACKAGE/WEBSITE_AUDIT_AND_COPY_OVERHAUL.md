## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The current coachcooz.com has strong bones — the brand name is magnetic, the site structure is logical, and there's a clear CTA. But the **copy and visual tone** are actively sabotaging the $10K price point:

> [!CAUTION]
> **The website is using the OLD crisis language at full volume.** "System Failure." "Corrupted." "Your word is dead." "Crash." This is the exact language our strategic analysis identified as repelling $10K executives. The site currently screams "$50/month personal trainer" — not "$10K executive biological infrastructure partner."

### Overall Scores (Oren's CEV Framework)

| Dimension | Current Score | Target Score | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Craft (Execution Quality)** | 4/10 | 8/10 | Copy is intense but undifferentiated. Visual design is template-tier. |
| **Emotionality (Feeling Transfer)** | 5/10 | 9/10 | The emotion is there but it's the WRONG emotion (shame → desperation instead of recognition → aspiration) |
| **Virality (Shareability)** | 3/10 | 7/10 | Nothing on the site is screenshot-worthy or share-worthy |

### The 3 Fatal Errors

1. **Crisis framing repels the $10K buyer.** "System Failure," "corrupted," "your word is dead" — this language implies the visitor is broken. Executives earning $150K-$750K don't identify as broken. They identify as *busy* or *dormant*.
2. **No proof architecture.** Two short testimonials from "Robin" and "Sam" (no last names, no titles, no context). A $10K buyer needs forensic proof: before/after metrics, named executives, specific timelines.
3. **Visual taste mismatch.** The aesthetic reads mid-market fitness, not premium executive health. Oren scored this 3/10. The word "Resurrection" demands a sophisticated visual container.

1. **The Problem** (3-4 sentences) — Why executive health deteriorates (not because of laziness — because of system prioritization)

5. **The Investment** — Pricing (either displayed or "Apply to discuss")
6. **FAQ** — Top 6-8 questions
