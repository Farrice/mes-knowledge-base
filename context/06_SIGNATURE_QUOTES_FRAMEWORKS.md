### The "Forever-Learning Industrial Complex" Critique

**What It Is**: The ecosystem of courses, coaches, and content that profits from keeping people in perpetual preparation.

**How It Works**:
1. Creates dependency (never "graduates" people)
2. Manufactures inadequacy (you always need more)
3. Rewards consumption (celebrates learning, not doing)
4. Delays action ("you're not ready yet")
5. Profits from stuck (recurring revenue from perpetual students)

**The Antidote**: Aligned Execution—learning in service of immediate action.

### The "Golden Goose" Recognition

**The Problem**: Farrice (and many like him) sits on exceptional value but doesn't leverage it.

**The Insight**:
> "While others put out mediocre work, I'm sitting on gold—literally have a golden goose—but don't know how to leverage it effectively."

**The Framework**:
1. **Recognize**: Identify what you have that others would pay for
2. **Value**: Price it at what it's worth, not what feels comfortable
3. **Package**: Create clear offerings around the value
4. **Ship**: Get it to market (imperfect is fine)
5. **Iterate**: Improve based on feedback
