# David Deutsch Hidden Knowledge

8 tacit insights from 40+ years of elite copywriting.

---

## 1. Non-Threatening Questions
**Insight**: Never tell clients they're wrong. Ask questions that lead to realization.
**Deploy**: "Have you considered...?" beats "You should..." every time.

## 2. The Ogilvy Editor Identity
**Insight**: "I'm not a great writer, but I'm a hell of an editor."
**Deploy**: Write fast, edit obsessively. Editing IS the skill.

## 3. Prove Critics Wrong Motivation
**Insight**: People often care more about proving doubters wrong than money.
**Deploy**: "How good it'll feel to drive up showing your 6th grade teacher who said you'd never amount to anything."

## 4. Lottery Psychology
**Insight**: Lottery buyers aren't stupid—they're buying the dream experience, not the probability.
**Deploy**: Sell the emotional experience, not the statistical outcome.

## 5. AI as Infinite Intern
**Insight**: AI is "a really inexperienced intern that doesn't have real understanding, but is really fast at sounding knowledgeable."
**Deploy**: Verify everything, push back constantly.

## 6. Don't Write First Drafts with AI
**Insight**: "You do the thinking first... then at least you will have that interaction with the material."
**Deploy**: Think first, then use AI for enhancement.

## 7. Copy Coach Voice Installation
**Insight**: The goal is to install the coach's critical voice permanently.
**Deploy**: "I still have his voice when I write sometimes."

## 8. The Cleaning Person Test
**Insight**: Deutsch's mentor showed copy to anyone—cleaning staff were often closer to the market.
**Deploy**: Show copy to non-experts for real feedback.
