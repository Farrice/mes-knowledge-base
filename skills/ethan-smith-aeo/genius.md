## Core Patterns

### 1. The 2007 Pattern Recognition Filter
Every new trend maps to a historical analogue. AI content spam = 2007 shopping comparison scraping. "SEO is dead" = every platform launch ever. The historical pattern predicts the outcome.

**Test**: "What did this look like in 2007/2012/2018?" If the pattern repeated, the outcome is predictable. Invest only in what persists.

### 2. Citation Frequency Over Page Rank
In LLMs, being mentioned most often across the retrieval corpus wins. Position #1 on Google → single winner. LLM summary → the brand with the most citations wins the recommendation.

**Test**: "Am I optimizing for one page or for frequency across the entire citation landscape?"

### 3. The Growing Pie Thesis
Each new platform adds ON TOP of Google. TikTok added search. LLMs added search. None replaced Google. Google's slice stays the same; total search volume increases.

**Test**: "Is this new channel growing the pie, or truly cannibalizing?" (Answer is almost always: growing the pie.)

### 4. The Anti-Spam Immune Response
If someone at the platform would build an algorithm to stop what you're doing, it will work temporarily and then destroy you. Every manipulation tactic has a half-life.

**Test**: "Would the platform applaud or punish this if they saw it?"

### 5. The Longtail Resurrection
LLM users ask 25-word questions vs. 6 in Google. The longtail — killed by Google's preference for broad authority pages — is alive again. Many tail questions have ZERO existing answers.

**Test**: "Does this question have a definitive existing answer anywhere? If not, we own it by default."

### 6. The 5% Landing Page Rule
1 in 20 landing pages drives ~85% of all traffic. The investment model is inverted: spend 20x on the 1 page that will win, not 1x on 20 mediocre pages.

**Test**: "If we could only publish ONE page this month, which topic would generate the most value?"

### 7. Reddit Authenticity Protocol
Reddit's community immune system is stronger than any automation. Winning strategy: real person, real name, real company, genuinely useful answer. Even 5 high-quality comments can drive significant AEO impact.

**Test**: "Would this comment survive if I posted it from my real, named account?"

### 8. The Experimental Design Imperative
Control groups (untouched questions) vs. test groups (intervention). Track over time. Reproduce at least once. Published "best practices" are unvalidated hypotheses until you've personally reproduced them.

**Test**: "Did we use a control group? Can we reproduce this result?"

### 9. Surface Divergence Awareness
ChatGPT citations overlap only 35% with Google. Perplexity overlaps 70%. Different LLM surfaces produce different citation results from similar RAG architectures.

**Test**: "Are we tracking share of voice across 3+ LLM surfaces, not just one?"

### 10. Information Gain Heuristic
The fundamental quality signal: did you say something nobody else said? Combined with typicality detection — if content is too similar to existing results, it's flagged as derivative.

**Test**: "What does this content say that NO existing result says? If nothing — it's wasted."

### 11. Help Center as AEO Weapon
Help centers are ideal AEO assets because LLM users ask detailed product questions that map perfectly to help center content. Most companies have zero coverage of the tail.

**Test**: "If someone asked an LLM a specific question about our product, would our help center be cited?"

### 12. Hidden Attribution Problem
LLM traffic is massively underreported. Users don't click citations — they copy brand names, type into Google, or navigate directly. This shows up as branded search or direct traffic.

**Test**: "Are we measuring AEO impact through post-conversion surveys AND branded search correlation, not just referral tracking?"

## Quality Tests

### The 2007 Test
"Has this pattern played out before?" Map any new tactic/channel to its historical analogue. If the historical version got nuked, this one will too.

### The Control Group Test
"Did this experiment have a control group?" If not, the result is anecdotal, not validated. Demand reproducibility.

### The Information Gain Test
"What does this say that nothing else says?" Content without information gain is invisible to both humans and algorithms.

### The Citation Landscape Test
"How many sources currently mention us for this query?" If the answer is 1 or 0, the optimization opportunity is massive. If it's 10+, you're fighting for incremental share.

### The Platform Applause Test
"Would the platform applaud or punish this tactic?" If punish, it has a half-life. Invest accordingly.

### The WebFlow 6x Test
"Are we treating LLM-sourced leads the same as Google leads?" LLM traffic converts 6x higher. Weight AEO ROI accordingly.
