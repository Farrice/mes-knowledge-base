# RAPID EXPERIMENT DESIGN: FRACTIONAL HR CONSULTING VALIDATION

## CORE HYPOTHESIS

"Early-stage startups (10-50 employees) will pay $150-250/hour for fractional HR consulting from an experienced HR professional, even without prior startup-specific experience."

**Sub-hypotheses to test**:
1. Startups have HR pain points they're willing to pay to solve
2. My corporate HR experience transfers to startup contexts
3. I can close consulting deals through LinkedIn outreach

---

## THE CHEAPEST VIABLE TEST

**Experiment Name**: "The 5-Conversation Sprint"

**Cost**: $0 cash + 10 hours of time
**Timeline**: 14 days
**Goal**: 5 discovery conversations with startup founders/operators + 1 paid engagement offer

**Why this is the right test**: You don't need market research reports or competitive analysis. You need 5 startup operators to tell you their HR problems in their own words, and at least 1 person to say "yes, I'd pay you for help." That single data point is worth more than 8 months of research.

---

## SUCCESS METRICS

**Validation Threshold (Hypothesis Confirmed)**:
- ✅ 5/5 conversations reveal significant HR pain points
- ✅ At least 2 people express interest in paid help
- ✅ At least 1 person accepts a paid engagement (even if discounted for first client)

**Partial Validation (Iterate Required)**:
- ⚠️ 3-4/5 conversations reveal pain points
- ⚠️ 1 person expresses interest but no one commits
- ⚠️ Pain points exist but differ from what you expected

**Invalidation Threshold (Pivot Required)**:
- ❌ Fewer than 3/5 conversations reveal meaningful pain points
- ❌ Zero interest in paid help
- ❌ Consistent objection: "We'd only work with someone with startup experience"

---

## DAY-BY-DAY EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Days 1-2: Target and Message Prep
**Actions**:
- Identify 15 startup founders/operators in your LinkedIn network (or 2nd-degree connections)
- Filter for companies sized 10-50 employees (HR pain starts here)
- Draft outreach message:

> "Hey [Name], I'm exploring starting a fractional HR consulting practice focused on helping startups. I've spent 15 years in HR leadership and I'm curious what HR challenges you're facing at [Company]. Would you have 20 minutes for a coffee chat (virtual or in-person)? Not selling anything—just learning. Happy to share what I've seen work in other orgs."

**Deliverable**: 15 names, message drafted and ready

### Days 3-5: Outreach Wave 1
**Actions**:
- Send personalized messages to first 10 targets
- Accept any conversation slots offered
- Track responses in simple spreadsheet

**Deliverable**: 10 messages sent, tracking system live

### Days 6-9: Conversations + Wave 2
**Actions**:
- Conduct first 2-3 conversations
- Use this question framework:
  1. "What's your biggest HR headache right now?"
  2. "How are you currently handling [that thing]?"
  3. "What would solving that be worth to you?"
  4. "Have you considered outside help? What's held you back?"
- Send 5 more outreach messages if needed to hit 5 conversations
- Take detailed notes—their exact words are gold

**Deliverable**: 2-3 conversations completed, pain points documented

### Days 10-12: Remaining Conversations + Pattern Recognition
**Actions**:
- Complete remaining 2-3 conversations
- Identify patterns: What pain points repeat? What objections appear?
- Draft a simple one-page offer based on what you heard

**Deliverable**: All 5 conversations complete, patterns documented, offer drafted

### Days 13-14: Offer Extension + Results Analysis
**Actions**:
- Send offer to 1-2 people who expressed pain + interest
- Offer: "Based on our conversation, I think I could help with [specific problem]. I'm offering my first three clients a pilot engagement: [X hours for $Y]. Would that be worth exploring?"
- Document all responses and feedback

**Deliverable**: Offer sent to qualified prospects, experiment results documented

---

## LEARNING EXTRACTION FRAMEWORK

**Regardless of outcome, document**:

1. **Pain Point Inventory**: What HR problems did founders mention? (Ranked by frequency and intensity)

2. **Language Bank**: What exact words did they use to describe their problems? (This becomes your marketing copy)

3. **Objection Catalog**: What concerns or hesitations came up? (This becomes your FAQ and positioning)

4. **Price Sensitivity Data**: What reactions did you get to pricing discussions? (This calibrates your rates)

5. **Trust Factors**: What would make them trust an outside HR consultant? (This shapes your credibility building)

**This data is valuable regardless of whether anyone says yes.** If no one buys, you now know exactly why and can iterate.

---

## NEXT EXPERIMENT PATHWAYS

### If Validated (1+ Paid Engagement):
**Next Experiment**: "The Delivery Test"
- Execute the paid engagement flawlessly
- Document time spent vs. value delivered
- Request testimonial and referral
- Test: Can you deliver profitably at this rate?

### If Partially Validated (Interest But No Commitment):
**Next Experiment**: "The Offer Iteration"
- Adjust pricing, scope, or positioning based on objections
- Test with 5 new conversations
- Consider: lower entry price, specific niche (e.g., "first HR hire coaching"), or different format (workshop vs. consulting)

### If Invalidated (No Pain Points or Interest):
**Next Experiment**: "The Pivot Probe"
- Interview 5 different-stage companies (later stage? different industry?)
- Explore adjacent services (executive coaching, recruiting support)
- Test: Is the problem in the market or in the positioning?

---

## COST COMPARISON: EXPERIMENT VS. DELIBERATION

**Cost of this experiment**:
- Time: 10 hours over 14 days
- Money: $0
- Risk: Zero—worst case you have 5 good conversations and learn something

**Cost of continued deliberation**:
- You've already spent 8 months (roughly 340 hours of thinking time if we assume 10 hrs/week of mental energy)
- Revenue delayed: Even one client at $200/hr × 10 hrs/month = $2,000/month × 8 months = $16,000 minimum opportunity cost
- Clarity delayed: You still don't know if this works

**The math is clear**: 10 hours of experimentation will tell you more than 340 hours of research did.

- Clear success metrics
- A network to tap
- Zero financial risk

The only question remaining: **Will you spend the next 14 days researching, or running this experiment?**

# RAPID EXPERIMENT DESIGN: LINKEDIN CONTENT VALIDATION

## CORE HYPOTHESIS

"I can build a growing LinkedIn audience by posting product management insights daily, and this will create professional opportunities (speaking, consulting, new role offers) within 6 months."

**Sub-hypotheses to test**:
1. I have enough unique insights to post daily for 30 days
2. My content will resonate (measured by engagement)
3. I can maintain the habit with 30 minutes/day
4. Some posts will significantly outperform others (learning what works)

---

## THE CHEAPEST VIABLE TEST

**Experiment Name**: "The 30-Day Content Sprint"

**Cost**: $0 cash + 15 hours of time (30 min × 30 days)
**Timeline**: 30 days
**Goal**: 30 posts published + engagement data + habit validation

**Why this is the right test**: You're uncertain about three things: uniqueness, commitment, and resonance. The only way to answer all three is to actually post. 30 days gives you enough data to see patterns without committing to a year of content. And it's free.

---

## SUCCESS METRICS

**Validation Threshold (Scale This)**:
- ✅ 25+ posts completed (83% adherence)
- ✅ Average engagement improves from week 1 to week 4
- ✅ 3+ posts significantly outperform others (10x normal engagement)
- ✅ At least 1 inbound opportunity (DM, comment, invitation)
- ✅ You enjoyed the process more than you dreaded it

**Partial Validation (Iterate)**:
- ⚠️ 20-25 posts completed (some habit friction)
- ⚠️ Engagement flat but not declining
- ⚠️ 1-2 posts outperformed but pattern unclear
- ⚠️ Process felt like a chore but not terrible

**Invalidation (Reconsider)**:
- ❌ Fewer than 20 posts completed
- ❌ Engagement declining over time
- ❌ No posts outperformed significantly
- ❌ Process was miserable and unsustainable

---

## DAY-BY-DAY EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Week 1 Setup (Days 1-7): Foundation + First Posts
**Day 1 Actions**:
- Brainstorm 30 post topics (doesn't have to be perfect—just 30 ideas)
- Topic categories: lessons learned, mistakes made, frameworks used, observations about PM, book/article reactions
- Schedule posting time (same time daily—habit stacking)

**Days 2-7 Actions**:
- Post daily at scheduled time
- Format: Keep it simple—no need for fancy graphics. Text posts work.
- Length: 100-200 words is fine. Short > long for building habit.
- Track: Likes, comments, profile views

**Deliverable**: 7 posts live, baseline engagement data

### Week 2 (Days 8-14): Pattern Recognition
**Daily Actions**:
- Post at scheduled time
- After posting, spend 5-10 minutes engaging with others' posts (builds visibility)
- End of week: Review what got most engagement. Why?

**Deliverable**: 14 total posts, first pattern hypothesis

### Week 3 (Days 15-21): Intentional Iteration
**Daily Actions**:
- Post at scheduled time
- Apply Week 2 learnings: Do more of what worked
- Try one "different" post mid-week (video? carousel? different topic?) to test edges

**Deliverable**: 21 total posts, refined content approach

### Week 4 (Days 22-30): Validation Sprint
**Daily Actions**:
- Post at scheduled time
- Double down on your best-performing format/topic
- Day 30: Comprehensive review

**Deliverable**: 30 posts, complete data set, clear validation signal

---

## LEARNING EXTRACTION FRAMEWORK

**Document weekly**:

1. **Engagement Patterns**: What topics/formats got most engagement? What bombed?

2. **Idea Generation**: Was finding topics easy or hard? What sources fed ideas?

3. **Time Tracking**: Did 30 minutes feel sustainable? Where did you struggle?

4. **Emotional Experience**: Did you dread posting or look forward to it?

5. **Network Effects**: Who engaged? Any unexpected connections?

**Week 4 Synthesis**:
- Your top 5 performing posts—why did they work?
- Your content "voice"—what makes your perspective unique?
- Sustainable pace—can you do this for 6 more months?
- Opportunity signals—any inbound interest?

---

## NEXT EXPERIMENT PATHWAYS

### If Validated (Strong Engagement + Sustainable + Enjoying It):
**Next Experiment**: "The Depth Test"
- Increase to 45 min/day
- Add one long-form post per week (300-500 words)
- Test: Does deeper content accelerate growth?

### If Partially Validated (Some Traction But Hard to Sustain):
**Next Experiment**: "The Cadence Test"
- Reduce to 3 posts/week instead of daily
- Focus on highest-performing format only
- Test: Can you maintain quality with less volume?

### If Invalidated (Low Engagement or Unsustainable):
**Next Experiment**: "The Niche Test"
- Narrow topic dramatically (e.g., "PM for fintech" instead of "PM thoughts")
- Or try different platform (Twitter/X for shorter content?)
- Test: Is the problem format, platform, or positioning?

---

## COST COMPARISON: EXPERIMENT VS. DELIBERATION

**Cost of this experiment**:
- Time: 15 hours over 30 days (30 min/day)
- Money: $0
- Risk: A few awkward posts that get 3 likes. That's it.

**Cost of continued uncertainty**:
- Every month you don't start is a month of compounding you don't get
- LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistency—later starts mean slower growth
- You'll never know if your content would resonate until you post it

**The revelation**: Your uncertainty about "uniqueness" and "commitment" can only be resolved through posting. There is no research that will answer these questions. Only the experiment.
