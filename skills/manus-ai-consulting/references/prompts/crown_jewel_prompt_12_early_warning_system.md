## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **BASELINE SNAPSHOT**: Capture the current state of each competitor across all monitored dimensions. This becomes the measurement benchmark against which all future changes are detected. Dimensions include:
   - Traffic volume and source mix (organic, paid, referral, direct, social)
   - Top landing pages and their traffic share
   - Estimated advertising spend level
   - Content publication velocity and topic focus
   - Pricing page structure and positioning
   - Job posting volume and role distribution
   - Social media posting frequency and topic themes
   - Messaging and positioning language on homepage/key pages

2. **SIGNAL TAXONOMY**: Define the specific change signals that matter and their severity thresholds:
   - **RED signals** (immediate strategic response needed): Pricing changes >15%, new market entry, direct feature attack on your positioning, major hiring surge in your core domain
   - **AMBER signals** (monitor closely, prepare response): Traffic source mix shift >10%, content topic pivot, new partnership announcements, leadership changes
   - **GREEN signals** (note and track): Gradual traffic trends, minor messaging updates, routine content publication, standard hiring patterns

3. **MONITORING PROTOCOL**: For each tool in the available stack, define exact queries to run at each monitoring cycle:
   - What to query
   - What to compare it against (the baseline or last period)
   - What threshold triggers an alert
   - How to interpret the change

4. **INTELLIGENCE BRIEFING PRODUCTION**: Produce the actual briefing document for the current period:
   - Executive summary (30-second read: what changed, what matters, what to do)
   - Signal-by-signal analysis for each detected change
   - Strategic interpretation (what does this change MEAN for competitive dynamics)
   - Recommended responses with timelines
   - Updated baseline for next period

5. **OPERATIONAL CALENDAR**: Produce the complete monitoring schedule with specific tasks assigned to specific dates, creating a repeatable intelligence operation that runs with minimal effort.

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

The most valuable competitive signals often come from non-obvious sources. A competitor's Glassdoor reviews might reveal internal strategic debates. Their investor relations page (if public) might telegraph future moves. Their support forum might reveal product stability issues. Their API documentation changes might signal platform strategy shifts. Patent filings might reveal technology directions years before product launches.

Design the monitoring system to include at least 2-3 non-obvious signal sources that a standard competitive analysis would miss. These "edge signals" are where the intelligence advantage livesâ€”everyone monitors traffic and pricing; few monitor job posting language, patent filings, or support forum sentiment.

Also consider what ABSENCE of signal means. If a competitor that was publishing 20 blog posts per month suddenly drops to 5, that silence is a signal. If they stop advertising on a channel they used to dominate, that withdrawal is a signal. Build detection of absence into the system.
