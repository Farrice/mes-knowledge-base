## 1. Curiosity × Relevance Architecture
**What They Do**: Every hook is evaluated on two axes simultaneously — how much *curiosity* it creates and how much *personal relevance* it carries. Too much curiosity without relevance = clickbait that doesn't convert. Too much relevance without curiosity = boring ad that doesn't get clicked.
**Executable Behavior**: Score every hook on a 1-10 scale for both curiosity and relevance. Only deploy hooks scoring 7+ on BOTH axes. If curiosity is high but relevance is low, add a personal stakes element ("...and it's costing you $X every month"). If relevance is high but curiosity is low, add a contradiction or paradox ("...but the solution is the opposite of what you'd expect").
**Deploy When**: Ad hooks, email subject lines, YouTube thumbnails, social media openers — any first-contact element.
**Success Metric**: CTR above 2% (baseline) with conversion rate above 1% (not just clicks, but qualified clicks).

## 2. Worldview Porn
**What They Do**: Instead of presenting new information, they articulate beliefs the audience *already holds* but hasn't seen expressed. This creates an instant "god, YES" identity resonance that bypasses all skepticism because you're not persuading — you're mirroring.
**Executable Behavior**: Research your audience's tribal beliefs — what do they already believe but rarely see validated? Frame these beliefs as declarations. "Everyone knows that [tribal belief] but nobody's willing to say it." Use language pulled from forums, Reddit, comment sections where the audience speaks candidly.
**Deploy When**: Top-of-funnel ads for unaware audiences, content hooks, brand manifestos, community building copy.
**Success Metric**: High share rates and comment engagement ("I've been saying this for years!" / "Finally someone gets it").

## 3. The Open Loop Engine
**What They Do**: Never resolve curiosity until after the mechanism reveal. Each section opens a new question while partially answering the previous one. The reader is always one step behind, chasing resolution.
**Executable Behavior**: After the hook, open a loop ("And the reason this works is something most people have never heard of..."). Deliver the payoff of the HOOK, but open a NEW loop about the mechanism. Deliver the mechanism, but open a loop about the solution. The product is the resolution of ALL open loops simultaneously.
**Deploy When**: Long-form ads, VSLs, webinar scripts, email sequences — any asset longer than 300 words.
**Success Metric**: Read-through rate above 60% for long-form ads (vs. typical 20-30%).

## 4. Organic Hook Theft
**What They Do**: Systematically steal hooks validated by organic platforms — YouTube titles with millions of views, viral tweets, Reddit posts with thousands of upvotes. If it went viral organically, the curiosity mechanism is pre-validated.
**Executable Behavior**: Build a hook bank: (1) Search YouTube for "[topic] + surprising/secret/hidden" — note titles with 500K+ views. (2) Scan Reddit top posts in relevant subreddits. (3) Collect viral tweet threads. (4) Adapt the hook structure to your product — keep the curiosity engine, swap the subject.
**Deploy When**: Ad creation, content ideation sprints, email subject line generation.
**Success Metric**: Hook bank of 50+ validated hooks per niche. 3x faster ad creation than starting from scratch.

## 5. The Paradox Hook
**What They Do**: "The thing everyone does to [desired outcome] is actually the reason they never achieve it." Contradicts common belief to create cognitive dissonance the reader MUST resolve.
**Executable Behavior**: Identify the most common advice in your market. Invert it. "Why [popular approach] is secretly killing your [desired result]." Support with a mechanism that explains why the paradox is true.
**Deploy When**: Unaware and problem-aware audience ads. Contrarian content. Category creation campaigns.
**Success Metric**: CTR 2-4x higher than direct benefit hooks.

## 6. The Conspiracy Frame
**What They Do**: "They don't want you to know this." Creates an enemy (the establishment), a secret (the hidden mechanism), and a hero (the reader, now armed with truth).
**Executable Behavior**: Identify your market's "establishment" or conventional wisdom gatekeepers. Frame their advice as either ignorant or deliberately misleading. Present your mechanism as the suppressed alternative. Critical tone calibration: rebellious insider, NOT tinfoil conspiracy.
**Deploy When**: Health, finance, education, self-improvement — any market with an "old guard" or mainstream consensus.
**Success Metric**: High emotional engagement. Strong "us vs. them" community formation.

## 7. The Lost Wisdom Hook
**What They Do**: "A 2,000-year-old technique that [modern experts] forgot." Taps both nostalgia and anti-establishment sentiment simultaneously.
**Executable Behavior**: Research historical practices in your market. Find one with genuine efficacy that's been abandoned or overlooked. Frame as rediscovered wisdom. Stack with modern scientific validation for maximum credibility ("Ancient monks knew this — and now Stanford has confirmed it").
**Deploy When**: Health, productivity, relationships, spirituality — markets with genuine historical depth.
**Success Metric**: Curiosity + authority in one hook. Higher trust baseline than pure novelty hooks.

## 8. Vectors of Winning Angles
**What They Do**: Generate unlimited hook variations by running any topic through three systematic axes: **Time** (past/present/future patterns), **Behavior** (what they do vs. don't do), and **Object** (unexpected tools, ingredients, or mechanisms).
**Executable Behavior**: For any product, fill this matrix:
- **Time**: "In 2024, [surprising trend]..." / "10 years ago, nobody knew..." / "By 2030, everyone will..."
- **Behavior**: "People who [unexpected behavior] get [result]..." / "Stop [common action] if you want [outcome]..."
- **Object**: "This [unexpected object] is the secret to [outcome]..." / "Why [common tool] is worse than [obscure alternative]..."
Generate 3-5 hooks per axis. Select the best 5 for testing.
**Deploy When**: Ad creative generation sprints, content ideation sessions, hook bank expansion.
**Success Metric**: 20+ unique hooks generated per session. Zero blank pages.
