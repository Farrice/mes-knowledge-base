# Workflow 03: Cohort Launch Engine

> **Load genius context first**: Read `genius.md` before executing this workflow.

## Purpose
Produce a complete monthly cohort operations playbook — from intake rhythm to retention architecture — that generates predictable revenue without launch exhaustion.

## Input Required
- **Program name**: What's it called?
- **Price point**: Current or target
- **Cohort size**: How many per month? (Start small — 10-30)
- **Program duration**: How long is the initial commitment?
- **Existing audience/list**: Size and engagement level
- **Content channels**: Where do you publish? (YouTube, LinkedIn, newsletter, etc.)

## Execution

You are Taki Moore designing a monthly enrollment machine that fills itself through trust-based marketing and natural urgency.

### 1. Monthly Rhythm Calendar (GP-06)
Design the operational calendar:

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **Week 1** | New cohort starts. Onboarding. Content about the journey. |
| **Week 2-3** | Deliver program. Share behind-the-scenes content. Collect testimonials from current cohort. |
| **Week 4** | Doors open for next cohort. 5-7 day enrollment window. |
| **Between cycles** | Wait-list capture. Evergreen doc available for warm audience. |

**Key principle**: The calendar creates its own marketing material. Week 1 content = "We just kicked off with [X] new members." Week 2-3 = student wins. Week 4 = "Doors open, [X] spots."

### 2. Evergreen Leak Layer
Not everyone buys during the official open window:
- **Explainer Video**: Permanent YouTube/content piece explaining how the program works
- **Evergreen Offer Doc**: Always-accessible doc with application link
- **Passive Pipeline**: People discover → watch → read doc → apply → join next cohort
- **Pre-fill tracking**: "Before we even opened doors, 18 of 30 spots were gone" — this IS your marketing

### 3. Application-Deposit-Approval Flow (GP-09)
Design the enrollment funnel:

```
Content → Offer Doc → "Apply" Button
                          ↓
                   Application Form
                   (8-12 questions)
                          ↓
                   Deposit Payment
                   ($500-$2,000 — intent signal)
                          ↓
                   Team Reviews Application
                   ↓                    ↓
              Approved              Not Approved
              (deposit = 1st         (full refund +
               payment)               honest feedback)
```

**Application Questions** (template):
1. What do you do currently? (Job/business)
2. What expertise or passion would you build a business around?
3. Have you tried to monetize this before? What happened?
4. What does your ideal week look like?
5. How much time can you commit weekly?
6. What's your revenue goal for the next 12 months?
7. What's the biggest thing holding you back?
8. Why now? What made you apply today?
9. How did you find us?
10. Anything else we should know?

**Deposit placement**: Last step (after application questions), not first. "People are like, wait, I don't even know what questions you're asking me and you're asking for money."

### 4. Onboarding Sequence
Design the first 72 hours after acceptance:
- **Hour 0**: Welcome email — "You're in! Here's what happens next."
- **Hour 2**: Access to program platform (Notion, Teachable, etc.)
- **Day 1**: Orientation video from founder (5 min — what to expect, how to win)
- **Day 2**: Introduce to accountability group/squad
- **Day 3**: First milestone countdown starts ticking
- **Day 7**: First group call or check-in

### 5. Retention Architecture (GP-12)
Design the post-program continuation:
- **Initial period**: 6 months (or whatever the core program is)
- **After initial**: Auto-transitions to month-to-month at lower rate
- **No re-enrollment friction**: "Stay as long as it's useful. Cancel anytime."
- **Monthly diagnostic cycle continues**: Same structure (diagnose → experiment → test → iterate)
- **Pricing shift**: Month-to-month at 40-60% of monthly program rate (heavy lifting is done)

**Language**: "Most of the heavy lifting comes up front. After that, we just keep running the cycle until you hit as big as you want."

### 6. Capacity Planning
Scale projections:

| Cohort Size | Annual Capacity | At $6,800 | At $10,000 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15/month | 180 | $1.22M | $1.8M |
| 30/month | 360 | $2.45M | $3.6M |
| 50/month | 600 | $4.08M | $6.0M |

**Plus retention revenue**: If 60% continue month-to-month, add 60-80% recurring revenue after month 6.

## Output

**THE COHORT LAUNCH PLAYBOOK** — Complete operations document containing:

1. **Monthly Rhythm Calendar**: Week-by-week operational cadence
2. **Evergreen Pipeline**: Always-on discovery-to-application flow
3. **Application-Deposit-Approval System**: Full funnel with question template
4. **Onboarding Sequence**: First 7 days scripted
5. **Retention Architecture**: Post-program continuation model
6. **Capacity Projections**: Revenue modeling at different cohort sizes

**Format**: Operations manual ready for team implementation. Includes templates, email copy, and process flows.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Does the enrollment process feel exclusive but not gate-keepery?
- [ ] Are there zero sales calls in the entire flow?
- [ ] Does the retention model create "profitable addiction" (they stay because it's valuable, not contractual)?
- [ ] Could a 2-person team (founder + one support person) execute this?
