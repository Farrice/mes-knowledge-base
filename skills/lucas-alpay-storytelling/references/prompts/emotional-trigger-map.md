5. **Create trigger inventory** — Specific language for copy

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

**Context**: Marketing a course for new freelancers

### EMOTIONAL TRIGGER MAP

**AUDIENCE**: People who just left (or want to leave) their 9-to-5 for freelancing

**SURFACE DESIRES**:
- Get clients
- Make money
- Work from anywhere
- Be their own boss

**EMOTIONAL CORE** (dig deeper):
- **Fear of failure**: "What if I can't make this work?"
- **Fear of judgment**: "Everyone will think I'm crazy"
- **Fear of poverty**: "What if I run out of money?"
- **Desire for validation**: "I need to prove I can do this"
- **Craving for freedom**: "I want to feel like myself again"

**UNIVERSAL TRUTHS**:
- The terror of betting on yourself
- The secret suspicion that you're not as good as you think
- The shame of potentially having to go back
- The desperate need for someone to tell you it's possible

**TRIGGER PHRASES**:

1. "The fear that you're not ready" — Speaks to imposter syndrome
2. "Everyone who questioned your decision" — External judgment = motivation
3. "The backup plan you're afraid to need" — Real fear is going back
4. "Proving it was the right call" — Validation drive
5. "The freedom you pictured vs. the anxiety you got" — Dream ≠ reality gap

**COPY APPLICATIONS**:

*For addressing imposter syndrome:*
> "You're not ready. That's what you keep thinking. Everyone else seems to have it figured out—the clients, the systems, the confidence. You're just... trying. What if you never figure it out?"

*For speaking to external judgment:*
> "Your brother-in-law is waiting for you to fail. He didn't say it, but you know. When you went freelance, something in his eyes said 'let's see how long this lasts.' Imagine the dinner table when you hit your first $10K month."

*For connecting to freedom desire:*
> "You didn't quit your job for a different job. You quit for a different LIFE. So why does freelancing feel like a worse version of what you escaped?"
