## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

**Context**: Executive coaching program

### SENSORY CONVERSION COPY

**BEFORE STATE (Sensory)**
You know the feeling. 3am, eyes open, brain already running the meeting you haven't had yet. Your jaw is clenched—you didn't notice until just now. The phone on your nightstand glows with emails you won't read but can't ignore.

That tightness in your chest during the morning stand-up. The way your shoulders climb toward your ears by noon. The coffee that stopped working three cups ago.

**AFTER STATE (Sensory)**
Six months from now, you'll wake up at 6:30. Not because you set an alarm—because your body decided it was time.

You'll make coffee and actually taste it. Sit in a chair. Read something that has nothing to do with quarterly numbers.

When you walk into the office, you'll notice that your shoulders are down. Your voice is lower. The team responds to you differently because you're taking up a different space.

**TRANSITION MOMENT**
The shift happens in session four, usually. The moment when you realize that the knot in your chest has been there for years and you'd stopped noticing it.

And then the morning you notice it's gone.

**FULL SENSORY COPY BLOCK**

Here's what nobody tells you about burning out slowly: You stop noticing.

The clenched jaw becomes normal. The 3am inventory of tomorrow's problems feels standard. The coffee that stopped working is just... coffee.

Our executive clients come in thinking they need tactics. Time management. Better systems.

What they actually need is to remember what it feels like to be in their own body without it being a warning system.

Six months from now, you won't remember how it felt to be wound this tight. You'll make coffee and taste it. You'll sit in silence and not fill it with planning. Your voice will be lower—not because you're trying, but because you're not chronically braced for impact.

This is what changes when you change how you lead. Not just your calendar. Your nervous system.
