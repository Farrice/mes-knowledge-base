# Oren - The Thrift Test Designer
*Budget-Independent Taste Validation*

---

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

**Context**: Fashion domain, $50 budget, intermediate ability

**THE THRIFT TEST: Fashion**

**CHALLENGE**: Create one complete outfit from a thrift store that looks intentional, not assembled from castoffs. Total budget: $50.

**CONSTRAINTS**:
- Only thrift/secondhand stores (no retail)
- Cannot buy based on brand recognition
- Must leave with complete outfit (top, bottom, outerwear or accessory)
- 2-hour time limit
- Document your reasoning for each choice

**SUCCESS CRITERIA**:
1. **Cohesion Test**: Does the outfit look like it was planned, not found?
2. **Context Test**: Would you wear this to a specific occasion you'd normally dress carefully for?
3. **Blind Test**: If someone saw you wearing this, would they assume it was new?
4. **Materials Test**: Can you identify why each fabric works?
5. **Fit Test**: Does every piece fit properly or look intentional if oversized?

**DOCUMENTATION TEMPLATE**:

For each piece, record:
- What caught your eye initially
- What made you pick it up
- What made you put it back OR keep it
- What it cost
- How it fits the overall vision

**SELF-EVALUATION RUBRIC**:

**Pass (7-10 points)**:
- Outfit reads as intentional (2 pts)
- You'd genuinely wear it (2 pts)
- Can articulate why each piece works (2 pts)
- Materials/construction are acceptable (2 pts)
- Came in under budget (2 pts)

**Partial (4-6 points)**: Some elements work, others don't
**Fail (0-3 points)**: Outfit looks like random thrift finds

**POST-TEST ANALYSIS**:
1. What was hardest about this exercise?
2. Where did you compromise taste for convenience?
3. What did you learn about your actual preferences?
4. What would you do differently next time?
