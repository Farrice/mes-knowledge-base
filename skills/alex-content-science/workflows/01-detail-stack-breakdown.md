# Detail Stack Breakdown

> Load `skills/alex-content-science/genius.md` before executing.

## Inputs Required
- **Content link** (URL, screenshot, or description of the specific content to analyze)
- **Platform** (YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, X, etc.)
- **Optional**: Creator name and follower count (for ratio analysis)

## The Process

### Step 1 — Stop Signal Analysis
Identify what stopped the scroll. One of:
- Visual hook (color, composition, text treatment, face, thumbnail design)
- Text hook (opening line, title, caption — what promise or curiosity?)
- Audio hook (music choice, silence, voice tone, pattern interrupt)
- Identity hook (who is this person, what do they represent?)

Document: "I stopped because ___."

### Step 2 — 4-Layer Deconstruction

Break down across ALL four layers:

**Layer 1 — Topic**
- What is the content actually about?
- Is the topic niche-standard or unexpected?
- Is there a contrarian angle or fresh frame?

**Layer 2 — Packaging**
- Thumbnail/cover: colors, composition, text, face expression
- Title/caption: promise, curiosity, specificity
- First frame: what does the opening 1 second communicate?
- Visual treatment: style, filters, aspect ratio

**Layer 3 — Content Style**
- Visuals: editing pace, transitions, camera angles, B-roll
- Audio: music vs silence, voice tone, pacing, sound effects
- Delivery: eye contact, confidence level, energy, gestures
- Structure: how information unfolds (list, story, demonstration?)
- Text overlays: font, placement, timing, animation

**Layer 4 — Emotional Triggers**
- What feeling does this create? (aspiration, relief, curiosity, FOMO, belonging?)
- WHY does it create that feeling? (what specific elements trigger it?)
- What identity does the viewer see themselves as while watching?

### Step 3 — The Invisible Decision Inventory

Go DEEPER than anyone else. Map every invisible decision:

| Decision Layer | What They Chose | What It Signals |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Environment** | [describe setting] | [what credibility/status does it signal?] |
| **Wardrobe** | [describe outfit] | [what trust/identity does it build?] |
| **Color Palette** | [describe colors] | [what mood before words?] |
| **Audio Strategy** | [music/silence/effects] | [pattern interrupt? authority? energy?] |
| **Text Placement** | [where/how text appears] | [eye guidance, visual interest] |
| **Camera Angle** | [eye level/above/below] | [authority, intimacy, power dynamic] |
| **Pacing of Cuts** | [fast/slow/rhythm] | [retention engineering, energy] |
| **Set Dressing** | [objects, people, details] | [status signals, trust multipliers] |
| **Opening Framing** | [how they introduce themselves/topic] | [status positioning, authority claim] |
| **What They Refused** | [what they deliberately DIDN'T do] | [brand definition through exclusion] |

### Step 4 — Purple Cow Assessment
- If you covered the account name, could you tell who posted this?
- If 10 competitors made this same content, would it be interchangeable?
- What makes this a purple cow (or a brown cow)?

### Step 5 — Retention Architecture
- Where are the likely retention peaks/drops?
- What retention tools are disguised as content? (physical objects, visual disruptions, reveals)
- How does the content maintain visual variety without losing coherence?
- What makes you want to watch to the end?
