# Analogy Engineering

> Load `skills/alex-content-science/genius.md` before executing.

## Inputs Required
- **Boring topic** — the valuable skill/concept that people avoid because it feels like work
- **Your audience** — who needs this BUT wouldn't normally consume this content?
- **Audience interests** — what topics/domains does this audience already obsess over? (list 5-10)

## The Process

### Step 1 — Boredom Diagnosis

Why is this topic boring? Identify the specific barrier:

| Barrier Type | Signal | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| **Too technical** | Jargon-heavy, requires prerequisites | "Progressive overload in resistance training" |
| **Too abstract** | Can't visualize it, no tangible outcome | "Compound interest over 30 years" |
| **Too effortful** | Feels like homework, requires discipline | "Building your active vocabulary" |
| **Too familiar** | Everyone's heard this advice, sounds generic | "Eat less, move more" |
| **Too unsexy** | Important but not shareable or identity-forming | "Email subject line best practices" |

The barrier type determines which analogy strategy works best.

### Step 2 — Audience Interest Mapping

Map 5-10 domains your audience is already obsessed with:

| Domain | Why They Love It | Shared Structure with Boring Topic |
|--------|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| Fashion | Identity, status, self-expression | ? |
| Gaming | Progression, leveling up, mastery | ? |
| Dating | Psychology, first impressions, strategy | ? |
| Sports | Competition, training, measurable improvement | ? |
| Music | Creative expression, taste, curation | ? |

Fill the "Shared Structure" column — this is where analogies live.

### Step 3 — Analogy Generation

For each high-potential domain match, generate a full analogy:

**Formula**: [Boring Topic] IS LIKE [Interest Domain] BECAUSE [shared structural element]

Generate 5+ analogy candidates:

| Boring Topic | Mapped Onto | The Analogy | Strength (1-5) |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Vocabulary building | Fashion/wardrobe | "Your vocabulary IS your wardrobe. Streetwear = everyday words. Runway = words nobody uses. Go shopping one level up." | 5 |
| Email subject lines | Dating app openers | "Your subject line is your first message on Hinge. Same psychology, same stakes, same 3-second judgment." | 4 |
| Progressive overload | Video game leveling | "You don't fight the final boss on Day 1. You level up gradually. Same with muscle." | 3 |

### Step 4 — Analogy Stress Test

For each top analogy, test:

1. **Does it simplify without dumbing down?** The analogy should make the concept ACCESSIBLE, not reductive.
2. **Does it carry for the full explanation?** A good analogy extends through the entire lesson, not just the intro.
3. **Does it create an emotion shift?** From "ugh, homework" to "oh, this is actually like [thing I love]"
4. **Is it original?** Has this specific mapping been done before in your niche?
5. **Does it respect the audience?** Not condescending. Makes them feel smart for getting it.

Score each 1-5 on all five dimensions. Top scorer(s) move forward.

### Step 5 — Full Content Architecture

Build the content around the winning analogy:

**Title/Hook**: Frame the content through the analogy, NOT the boring topic
- ❌ "5 Tips to Build Your Vocabulary"
- ✅ "Your Vocabulary Is Your Wardrobe (Here's How to Upgrade)"

**Structure**: Let the analogy do ALL the heavy lifting:
1. **Establish the analogy** — make the connection explicit and vivid
2. **Teach through the analogy** — every concept explained in analogy terms
3. **Extend the analogy** — add layers (streetwear → business casual → formal → runway)
4. **Land the practical** — transition smoothly from analogy to actionable advice
5. **Close with the analogy** — bookend so the entire lesson is memorable

**The Test**: If someone described your content to a friend, would they use the analogy? ("He explained vocabulary like it's a wardrobe — so good.") If YES → the analogy is working as the vehicle.

## Quality Gate
- [ ] Analogy simplifies without dumbing down the topic
- [ ] Analogy carries through the FULL explanation (not just the intro)
- [ ] Analogy creates emotion shift from "homework" to "interesting"
- [ ] Content is framed through the analogy in title/hook (not the boring topic)
- [ ] Someone retelling this content would naturally use the analogy
