---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### The 4-Version Framework

Create each case study in 4 formats:

1. **Long-Form Narrative** (500-800 words): For website, sales materials
2. **Sales Soundbite** (30 seconds): For live conversations
3. **Social Snippet** (280 characters): For posts/ads
4. **Client Voice** (quote): For testimonial use

### Objection-Preempting Structure

Every case study should address at least ONE common objection:
- "Will this work for MY industry?" → Same-industry case study
- "This sounds expensive" → ROI-focused case study
- "I've been burned before" → Trust-building case study
- "I'm not sure about the timeline" → Speed-focused case study

### Strategic Placement

- **LinkedIn bio**: Soundbite version
- **Sales calls**: Reference during discovery when relevant
- **Proposals**: Embedded as proof of capability
- **Objection handling**: Deploy specific case for specific objection

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT: AI Consulting Case Study

**LONG-FORM: Flight Freerun**

*The Challenge*

Flight Freerun is a 15-person e-commerce brand selling parkour and movement apparel. Their operations manager was drowning—spending 3+ hours daily on order management, inventory updates, and customer service responses.

They'd tried hiring, but couldn't find good candidates. They'd looked at software, but nothing integrated properly with their Shopify + Gorgias setup.

When they reached out, they were skeptical. They'd been burned before by automation consultants who delivered complicated systems nobody could maintain.

*What We Did*

We started with a $1,500 Opportunity Audit. Over 5 days, we mapped every touchpoint in their order-to-delivery workflow and identified 23 automation candidates.

The top opportunity: order status inquiries. These represented 40% of all customer service tickets—and every single one could be answered automatically with the right integration.

Based on the audit, they approved a $12,000 Custom Implementation focused on their top 5 opportunities.

*The Results*

Within 6 weeks:
- Customer service volume dropped 52%
- Operations manager reclaimed 15 hours per week
- Order processing errors went to near-zero
- Team morale improved (no more repetitive tasks)

ROI: The implementation paid for itself in under 60 days.

*What They Said*

"I was skeptical because we'd been burned before. But the audit approach—proving it works before committing to a bigger project—made the decision easy. Now I can't imagine going back to manual." — Sarah, Operations Manager
