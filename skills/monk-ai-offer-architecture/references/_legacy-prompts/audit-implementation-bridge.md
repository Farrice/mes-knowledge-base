---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### The Bridge-Building Audit Structure

Every audit section should do THREE jobs:
1. **Diagnose** a specific problem with evidence
2. **Quantify** the impact of that problem
3. **Preview** what solving it would look like

### Section Template

**[AREA 1]: [Problem Found]**
*Current State*: [What we observed with specific evidence]
*Impact*: [What this costs them—time, money, opportunity]
*Opportunity*: [What becomes possible if fixed]
*Quick Win*: [Something they could do immediately]
*Full Solution*: [What comprehensive implementation would include]

### Demand-Creating Elements

**ROI Projections**: For each opportunity, include conservative and aggressive projections of value recovered or created.

**Quick Win Validation**: Include 2-3 things they can do BEFORE implementation. When these work, they trust your analysis AND want the rest.

**Stakeholder Bait**: Include findings that would interest executives, operators, and finance. Give your champion ammunition.

**Implementation Roadmap Preview**: Show them what 90 days of implementation could accomplish—without pitching.

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT: AI Operations Audit Structure

**AUDIT DELIVERABLE: AI Automation Opportunity Assessment**

**Section 1: Current Workflow Analysis**

*What We Found*: Your team spends approximately 42 hours per week on manual data entry across three systems: [System A], [System B], and [System C].

*Evidence*: Based on our process mapping sessions, we documented 147 manual handoffs between systems, with an average of 3.2 minutes per handoff.

*Impact*: At an average fully-loaded cost of $45/hour for operations staff, this represents approximately $98,000 annually in labor cost for work that can be automated.

*Quick Win*: The [System A to System B] handoff can be automated with a simple Zapier connection. We estimate this takes 2 hours to set up and saves 8 hours weekly. Your team could implement this immediately.

*Full Solution*: A custom integration layer would eliminate all 147 handoffs, reducing the 42 weekly hours to approximately 5 (primarily for exception handling and oversight).

---

**Section 2: Error Rate Analysis**

*What We Found*: Manual processes introduce errors at a rate of approximately 4.2%, based on our sample analysis.

*Evidence*: Of 500 records reviewed, 21 contained data discrepancies requiring correction downstream.

*Impact*: Each error requires an average of 12 minutes to identify and correct. With an estimated 3,400 monthly transactions, this equals approximately 285 hours of error correction annually ($12,825 in labor plus downstream customer impact).

*Quick Win*: Implement a simple validation rule on [Field X] that catches 60% of common errors at input.

*Full Solution*: Automated validation + exception flagging would reduce error rate to under 0.5%, with remaining errors caught before downstream impact.

---

**Section 3: Implementation Roadmap Preview**

*Phase 1 (Days 1-14)*: Quick win implementation + integration architecture design
*Phase 2 (Days 15-45)*: Core integrations + validation automation
*Phase 3 (Days 46-90)*: Full workflow automation + team training + optimization

*Expected Outcome*: Based on similar implementations, we project 35+ hours weekly time savings within 90 days, with annual ROI of 8-12x the implementation investment.
