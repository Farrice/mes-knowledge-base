# Hidden Knowledge — Jonathan Courtney

## 1. The Ask-User-Question Interrogation Method

**Insight**: Courtney's most powerful webinar prep technique isn't prompting — it's being grilled. He trains a Claude Project on his ideal structure, then uses "ask user question" mode to have the AI interrogate him for 30 minutes. The AI's questions force him to articulate what he hasn't thought through yet.

**Why Others Miss It**: Most people use AI to get answers. Courtney uses it to provoke better questions from himself. The value isn't in Claude's output — it's in what Courtney produces under interrogation.

## 2. The 3-Day-to-Half-Day Compression Is the Real ROI

**Insight**: Courtney's key metric isn't cost savings — it's time compression. A lead magnet campaign that took 2-3 days now takes half a day. This 4-6x compression ratio is the actual ROI for AI-assisted marketing, not the "100x" hyperbole.

**Why Others Miss It**: The AI discourse focuses on "replacing" work. Courtney's actual experience shows AI doesn't replace — it compresses. And 4-6x on the right activities (selling events, campaigns) is worth more than 100x on the wrong ones (admin).

## 3. The Off-The-Shelf Trap Warning

**Insight**: Laura's cautionary tale — she spent 3 days building a custom proposal builder, then discovered an off-the-shelf solution existed. AI tools tempt you into building custom solutions for solved problems. The meta-skill is knowing when to build vs. buy.

**Why Others Miss It**: The vibe coding community celebrates building everything custom. But a CEO's time is allocated to promotion, not engineering. Every hour building something that already exists is an hour not spent promoting.

## 4. The 17,766-Line Swipe File Strategy

**Insight**: Courtney compiled a 17,766-line document of other people's marketing emails, had Claude extract patterns and learnings, and feeds those into his marketing project's context. This is systematic competitive intelligence, not casual research.

**Why Others Miss It**: People set up Claude with their own content. Courtney sets up Claude with the best content from his entire industry, creating outputs grounded in battle-tested copy rather than generic marketing.

## 5. The "I Don't Prepare" Preparation Method

**Insight**: Courtney claims he doesn't prepare for podcasts, then reveals he voice-dumps for 15 minutes into Claude, has it generate structured reference docs, and glances at them during recording. He has redefined "preparation" from sit-down-and-write to talk-and-glance.

**Why Others Miss It**: Traditional preparation is visual and linear. Courtney's is verbal and non-linear. The output (scannable docs he barely reads) is less important than the input process (verbal brain dump that organizes his thinking).
