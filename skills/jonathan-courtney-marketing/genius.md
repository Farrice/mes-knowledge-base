## Genius Patterns

## 1. The Builder Trap Diagnosis

**Pattern**: Before discussing any AI tool, reframe the CEO's job description. Most founders self-identify as "builders" when their primary job is promotion. Diagnose the identity misalignment before offering solutions.

**Execute**: When evaluating any business, first ask: "Who is doing the promoting?" If the answer is "nobody directly," the business has a structural deficiency that no product improvement will fix.

**Deploy When**: Consulting engagements, business audits, "why isn't my product getting traction?" conversations.

**Success**: The CEO can articulate their promotion activities as clearly as their building activities.

---

## 2. The Promoter Blueprint Loop

**Pattern**: Operate from a closed-loop marketing system: Traffic → Holding Pattern → Selling Event → (conversion or loop back). Every activity maps to this loop. If it doesn't map, it's procrastination.

**Execute**: Map every marketing activity to one of three buckets: (1) Traffic generation (organic/paid), (2) Holding pattern nurture (newsletter, podcast, social), (3) Selling events (webinars, email campaigns, retargeting, direct outreach).

**Deploy When**: Building a marketing system, planning campaigns, diagnosing why revenue isn't flowing despite audience engagement.

**Success**: You can predict revenue from traffic inputs — "5,000 new subs → 200 webinar attendees → 12 sales."

---

## 3. The Proof-by-Celebrity Legitimacy Stack

**Pattern**: Validate counterintuitive theses by demonstrating that Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Peter Levels, and Jason Fried already do the thing you're advocating. Turn their implicit behavior into explicit evidence.

**Execute**: When making any counterintuitive business argument, stack 3-5 examples of undeniably successful people who already do it. Let the audience connect the dots.

**Deploy When**: Sales pitches, webinars, content marketing, persuasion situations where you need to dislodge a pre-existing belief.

**Success**: The audience stops defending the status quo and starts asking "how?"

## 4. The Claude-to-Claude Code Handoff

**Pattern**: Use standard Claude Projects for research, ideation, and copywriting (creative phase), then transition to Claude Code for building (execution phase). The handoff preserves context via a CLAUDE.md file and HTML draft.

**Execute**: (1) Start in Claude Projects with instructions + reference files. (2) Do ideation, research, copywriting. (3) Ask Claude: "Create a CLAUDE.md file with full project context and export the current HTML." (4) Download both, open in Claude Code project folder. (5) Build from there.

**Deploy When**: Any project that starts conceptual and becomes technical — campaign pages, lead magnets, event pages.

**Success**: Context loss between tools is near zero; Claude Code picks up exactly where Claude Projects left off.

---

## 5. The Voice-First Brain Dump Protocol

**Pattern**: Record 15-minute verbal brain dumps on phone, feed audio directly into Claude, produce ADHD-friendly scannable documents. Bypasses the written-to-written bottleneck.

**Execute**: (1) Record voice memo of everything in your head. (2) Upload to Claude. (3) Produce scannable, non-linear reference docs. (4) Use voice-to-prompt tools for all subsequent interactions.

**Deploy When**: Podcast prep, webinar planning, creative ideation, ADHD-friendly workflows.

**Success**: Total prep time under 1 hour for content that would previously take a full day.

---

## 6. The Self-Teaching Context Loop

**Pattern**: At the end of every Claude chat session, ask Claude to produce updated instructions that compound the project's knowledge base. The AI teaches itself what it learned.

**Execute**: End every meaningful chat with: "Give me a pasteable set of instructions for this project that captures everything we've learned so far, ready to be added to your system instructions."

**Deploy When**: Any long-running Claude Project where context accumulates over multiple sessions.

**Success**: Session 10 of a project is meaningfully smarter than session 1 without manual briefing.

---

## 7. The Abundance Mindset Over Efficiency

**Pattern**: Use AI to multiply output, not reduce team size. Instead of "do 1 campaign/month cheaper," it's "do 5 campaigns/month with the same team."

**Execute**: When evaluating any AI-enabled process change, ask: "Am I using this to do less or to do more?" The correct answer is always more. Keep your team and triple their throughput.

**Deploy When**: Team planning, AI tool evaluation, business scaling decisions.

**Success**: Output volume increases 3-5x while team size stays constant.
