## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **Platform Pattern Analysis**: Apply the specific rules, norms, and expectations of the target platform. What works on TikTok fails on LinkedIn.

2. **Hook Architecture**: Design the first 1-3 seconds to stop the scroll. This is the most important part of any social video.

3. **Retention Engineering**: Build in "pattern interrupts" every 3-5 seconds that reward continued watching and prevent scroll-away.

4. **Native Format Application**: Structure the content to feel platform-native‚Äînot like repurposed advertising.

5. **Engagement Optimization**: Design for the specific engagement behaviors the platform rewards (comments, shares, saves, duets).

6. **AI-Optimized Execution**: Create specifications that play to AI generation strengths while meeting platform requirements.

## HOVER LEVITATING PLANT POT - TIKTOK LAUNCH

### Platform Analysis: TikTok

**Key Platform Rules**:
- Hook MUST land in first 0.5-1 second or you're dead
- "Pattern interrupt" every 3 seconds maintains watch time
- Sound is crucial‚Äî50%+ watch with sound ON (unlike other platforms)
- Native aesthetic > polished commercial aesthetic
- Comments drive algorithm‚Äîdesign for comment triggers
- "Wait for it" / "satisfying" content has proven viral mechanics

**What Dies on TikTok**:
- Slow builds
- Brand logos in first 3 seconds
- "Ad voice" narration
- 16:9 letterboxed content
- Anything that feels like a commercial

**What Thrives on TikTok**:
- Immediate visual intrigue
- Unexpected moments
- Satisfying visuals/sounds
- "I need to share this" moments
- Comment-worthy questions or debate triggers

---

### Hook Options

**Hook A: "The Glitch"**
Open on a plant pot on a table. Hand pushes it‚Äîbut instead of sliding, it levitates off the surface. "Wait... what?" energy.
- Scroll-stop factor: HIGH (physics violation)
- Production difficulty: MEDIUM (requires clean levitation shot)

**Hook B: "Wrong Placement"**
Hand carefully places plant pot‚Äîbut places it in MID-AIR about 4 inches above the surface. Releases. It stays. Begins rotating.
- Scroll-stop factor: HIGH (impossible action)
- Production difficulty: MEDIUM (clean release-to-float transition)

**Hook C: "The Double Take"**
Someone walks past a levitating plant, stops, backs up, stares. Classic "am I seeing this right?" reaction.
- Scroll-stop factor: MEDIUM-HIGH (relies on implied reaction)
- Production difficulty: LOW (no complex effects needed)

**Recommended**: Hook B - "Wrong Placement" has the cleanest "impossible moment" that demands continued watching.


## SARAH CHEN AI LEADERSHIP - LINKEDIN VIDEO

### Platform Analysis: LinkedIn

**Key Platform Rules**:
- Professional context‚Äîcredibility matters more than entertainment
- Hook must promise VALUE, not just attention
- Talking head format is native and accepted
- Longer average watch times than TikTok (people are in "learning mode")
- Text overlays for silent viewing (most watch without sound)
- Personal insight outperforms corporate messaging

**What Dies on LinkedIn**:
- Obviously AI-generated humans (professional audience is AI-savvy)
- Overly casual tone (mismatched to platform)
- Pure entertainment without takeaway
- Aggressive sales pitches
- Clickbait hooks without substance payoff

**What Thrives on LinkedIn**:
- "One thing I've learned" format
- Counterintuitive insights
- Personal experience + framework
- Calm authority (not hype)
- Actionable takeaway

---

### Hook Options

**Hook A: The Counterintuitive Claim**
"The biggest AI mistake I see companies make isn't starting too slow. It's starting too fast."
- Scroll-stop factor: HIGH (violates expectations)
- Requires: Strong follow-through explaining WHY

**Hook B: The Experience Signal**
"After implementing AI at 50+ companies, here's what actually matters."
- Scroll-stop factor: MEDIUM-HIGH (credibility claim)
- Requires: Genuine insight that justifies the credibility claim

**Hook C: The Pattern Interrupt**
"Everyone asks me about AI tools. Almost no one asks about this‚Äîand it's the only thing that matters."
- Scroll-stop factor: HIGH (curiosity gap)
- Requires: Payoff that feels worth the wait

**Recommended**: Hook A - Counterintuitive claims work exceptionally well on LinkedIn because professionals pride themselves on "being ahead" and want to learn what they're missing.

**Text Overlay Strategy**:

- Short phrases, not sentences
- Readable at phone size
- Consistent positioning (usually lower third)
