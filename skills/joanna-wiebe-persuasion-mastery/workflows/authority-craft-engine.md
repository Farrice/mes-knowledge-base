# Workflow 04: Authority Craft Engine

> **Produces**: Any piece of writing, reforged at the sentence level to command
> respect, earn trust, and sound like the top 1%. Applies all 5 authority craft
> mechanics — catchy phrasing, revelation ordering, preemptive disqualification,
> anti-hype, and bucket brigades.
> **Use When**: You have a draft (LinkedIn post, email, sales page, case study,
> proposal, DM) that needs to SOUND authoritative, not just be persuasively
> structured. This is the sentence-level polish that separates good writing from
> writing people remember.

## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Draft Intake & Format Detection

**Input Required**: The piece of writing to be refined + the audience it's written for + the desired outcome (trust, authority, memorability, sales, engagement).

**Execute**:

Identify the format and calibrate the craft intensity:

| Format | Primary Mechanics | Intensity |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn hook** | Catchy Phrasing + Anti-Hype | Maximum compression — every word earns its place |
| **LinkedIn post body** | Revelation Order + Bucket Brigades + Anti-Hype | Strategic pacing through dense insights |
| **Email subject line** | Catchy Phrasing | Meme test mandatory |
| **Email body** | Revelation Order + Bucket Brigades | Sequence facts to control narrative |
| **Sales page** | All 5 mechanics | Full deployment (each section uses different primary mechanic) |
| **Case study / results** | Revelation Order + Anti-Hype + Stealing Thunder | Sequence for audience type; understate to build credibility |
| **Proposal / pitch** | Stealing Thunder + Revelation Order + Anti-Hype | Disqualify first, then sequence the argument |
| **DM / outreach** | Stealing Thunder + Catchy Phrasing | Expert positioning from first line |
| **Long-form content** | Bucket Brigades + Revelation Order + Anti-Hype | Maintain flow through dense material |

### Step 2 — The Catchy Phrasing Pass

**Execute on every key line** (hooks, insight lines, CTAs, section headers):

1. **Syntax Check**: Is the syntax familiar? (Subject-verb-object, common sentence patterns)
2. **Distinctive Word Check**: Is there ONE word that's unexpected in this syntax?
3. **Compression Check**: Can any words be removed? Kill every conjunction (and, or, but) that isn't load-bearing.
4. **Portability Check**: Would this line work lifted out of context?

**Run the Meme Test**: "Could this work as a meme? Could it go on a t-shirt?"

If the line fails, do this:
- Strip to the essential meaning
- Rebuild with familiar syntax
- Insert one distinctive word
- Remove all padding
- Add white space after the line

**Before/After Examples**:
- ❌ "In order to effectively leverage your expertise and build meaningful connections with potential clients..." → ✅ "Your expertise is invisible. That's the problem."
- ❌ "Our comprehensive voice-matching methodology ensures authentic representation" → ✅ "We write like you, on your sharpest day."

### Step 3 — The Revelation Order Audit

**Execute on every section or paragraph transition**:

For each important fact, insight, or claim in the piece:

1. **Ask the Schema Programming Question**: "What do I want the audience to **assume is true** before they learn this next thing?"
2. **Identify the current order**: What fact appears first? What interpretation does that create?
3. **Test alternative orderings**: Would resequencing change how the audience receives this?
4. **Match to audience type**:
   - Risk-averse audience → Lead with safety/results, reveal innovation second
   - Innovation-seeking audience → Lead with the rule-breaking, reveal results second
   - Status-conscious audience → Lead with exclusivity, reveal the work required second

**Before/After Example**:
- ❌ "We use an unconventional process. It gets 3x more engagement." (Conservative coach reads "unconventional" and stops)
- ✅ "Our clients see 3x more engagement. The process is unconventional — that's why." (Conservative coach reads "3x" and leans in)

### Step 4 — The Stealing Thunder Injection

**Execute where trust is the bottleneck**:

Identify the moment in the piece where the audience is most skeptical. Then, VOLUNTEER the objection before they voice it.

**Scan for**:
- Areas where you're making a bold claim (inject a small concession first)
- Sections where the audience might think "but what about..." (preempt that objection)
- Qualification points where being selective INCREASES perceived value

**Deploy the template structures**:
- "If you want [fast/cheap thing], this isn't for you."
- "This works best for [specific qualifier]. If that's not you yet, solve that first."
- "I'm not the right fit if [specific anti-pattern]."
- "Most [commodity version of what you do] do X. I don't. Here's why."

**The Test**: After adding the preemptive disqualification, the remaining audience should lean in HARDER, not softer. If they pull back, you disqualified too broadly.

### Step 5 — The Anti-Hype Sweep

**Execute on every line that sounds even slightly inflated**:

Scan the entire piece for hype markers:
- Superlatives ("best," "most," "incredible," "game-changing")
- Hyperbolic adverbs ("completely," "absolutely," "totally")
- Dramatic framing ("everything changed," "nothing was ever the same," "shocking results")
- Exclamation marks (almost always remove)

For each hype marker:

1. **Delete it** and read the sentence without it
2. **If the meaning weakens**: Replace with an understated version that carries WEIGHT
3. **If the meaning holds**: Leave it deleted — the hype was padding

**Anti-Hype Conversion Table**:

| Hype Version | Anti-Hype Version |
|---|---|
| "This completely changed everything" | "The numbers moved. Not dramatically, but consistently." |
| "An incredible breakthrough" | "A small shift that happened to matter." |
| "What happened next will shock you" | "Nothing surprising happened. That was the problem." |
| "The most powerful strategy I've ever used" | "The strategy I keep coming back to." |
| "This destroyed our competition" | "Our competitors stopped showing up in the same conversations." |
| "Mind-blowing results" | "Results we didn't have to explain." |

**The Physics**: Hype creates an unconscious expectation of exaggeration. Anti-hype creates an unconscious expectation of truthfulness. When you then present genuinely impressive information in understated language, the contrast makes it MORE impressive, not less.

### Step 6 — The Bucket Brigade Placement

**Execute on long-form content (>300 words)**:

Scan for three types of drop-off points:

1. **After dense passages** (3+ sentences of complex ideas without a break)
2. **Before belief shifts** (where you're about to challenge or reframe something)
3. **At energy dips** (where the writing feels flat or the momentum stalls)

At each drop-off point, insert a bucket brigade:

**Tier 1 — Curiosity Bridges** (use most often):
- "Here's the thing."
- "Here's why."
- "Here's what matters."

**Tier 2 — Authority Bridges** (use for expert positioning):
- "Fact is."
- "The truth?"
- "What nobody mentions."

**Tier 3 — Transition Bridges** (use for belief shifts):
- "Let me explain."
- "Think about it this way."
- "Now here's where it gets interesting."

**Placement Rules**:
- Never stack two bucket brigades within 100 words of each other
- Always follow a bucket brigade with the payoff within 1-2 sentences
- If you can delete the bucket brigade and the writing improves, it wasn't earning its place
- Bucket brigades work best as standalone lines (their own paragraph or preceded by a line break)

### Step 7 — The Full-Stack Integration Test

**Execute after all 5 passes**:

Read the complete piece from start to finish as the target audience. Check:

```
AUTHORITY CRAFT SCORECARD:

CATCHY PHRASING:
- [ ] Key lines pass the meme/t-shirt test
- [ ] At least one line per section has familiar syntax + distinctive word
- [ ] No conjunctions in impact lines (unless load-bearing)

REVELATION ORDER:
- [ ] The schema programming question was asked for every major claim
- [ ] Facts are sequenced for the specific audience, not chronologically
- [ ] First impressions are engineered, not accidental

STEALING THUNDER:
- [ ] At least one preemptive disqualification where trust is the bottleneck
- [ ] The disqualification makes the remaining audience lean in harder
- [ ] Expert mode signal is active (consulting, not selling)

ANTI-HYPE:
- [ ] Zero superlatives that aren't earning their place
- [ ] Understated language carries MORE weight than inflated language would
- [ ] The confidence reads as natural, not performative

BUCKET BRIGADES:
- [ ] Placed at actual drop-off points, not randomly
- [ ] Each one creates micro-curiosity paid off within 1-2 sentences
- [ ] None are filler — all earn their keep

OVERALL:
- [ ] Does this sound like someone who doesn't NEED the reader's approval?
- [ ] Is the authority felt, or is it stated?
- [ ] Would an expert in this field respect this writing?
```
