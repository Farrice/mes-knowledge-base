## 1. The "Search Gap" Method
**Origin**: *Words At Scale* / EMD Striker
**Concept**: Google *wants* to rank local businesses, but often lacks quality data. When it can't find a good local site, it ranks "Weak" pages as placeholders.
- **The Telltale Signs of a Gap**:
  1.  **Forums**: Reddit, Quora, or TripAdvisor threads in the Top 3.
  2.  **Directories**: Yelp, YellowPages, Angi in the Top 3.
  3.  **Social Profiles**: A Facebook Page ranking (Google hates ranking FB pages).
  4.  **Zombie Sites**: Copyright 2014, non-mobile responsive, HTTP (not HTTPS).

> **The Striker Score**: If you see 2+ of these signs, the Striker Score is > 80. PROCEED.

## 2. The "Pure HTML" Advantage
**Origin**: *Locus Pilot* Logic
**Concept**: Modern JS frameworks (React, Next.js) are heavy and harder for Google's "Crawl Budget" to digest efficiently.
- **The Pattern**: Static HTML sites load instantly (< 200ms).
- **The Result**: Google preferentially ranks these "Lightweight Assets" because they offer a superior User Experience (Core Web Vitals) compared to bloated WordPress competitors.

## 3. Visual Packaging Theory ("The Trust Trifecta")
**Origin**: *Nano Banana* / Words At Scale
**Concept**: "Packaging is more important than product."
- **The Psychology**: Users judge *credibility* in 50 milliseconds based on visual design.
- **The 'Trust Trifecta'**:
  1.  **Aesthetic Continuity**: Colors, fonts, and images must match the *price point* of the service. (High-end plumbing = Clean blues, whites, crisp uniforms).
  2.  **Glassmorphism**: Using modern UI trends (glass panels, blurs) signals "State of the Art" technology, which subconsciously signals "State of the Art" service.
  3.  **Consistent Characters**: Using AI to generate the *same* mascot or worker across multiple photos builds a "Brand Universe".

## 4. The "Rent" Economics
**Concept**: Don't sell the site. Sell the *leads*.
- **Mechanism**:
  - Rank the site.
  - Put a specific phone number (CallRail) on it.
  - Forward calls to a local business.
  - Charge $50-$100 per lead OR a flat monthly "Rent" ($500-$2000/mo).

## 5. The "Harry Dry" Copy Protocol
**Origin**: Harry Dry (*Marketing Examples*)
**Concept**: "Don't say it's good. Prove it."
- **Rule 1**: Falsifiability. Use specific numbers.
  - *Bad*: "We are fast."
  - *Good*: "On-site in 45 minutes."
- **Rule 2**: Concrete Visuals.
  - *Bad*: "High quality service."
  - *Good*: "Licensed, Bonded, and 5-Star Rated on Google."

## 6. The "Oren" Taste Protocol
**Origin**: Oren (Taste Mastery)
**Concept**: "The aesthetic gap is the trust gap."
- **The Standard**: If the website doesn't look like a $10,000 agency built it, it's failed.
- **Micro-Interactions**: Use hover states (`hover:shadow-xl`) and glassmorphism (`backdrop-blur`) to signal "Premium".
