## CORE COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK

### The 7 Skills of AI Operations

**1. Multi-Instance Orchestration**
Run 3-5+ AI tasks in parallel, managing attention across instances. Not doing the work—managing the AI workforce doing the work.

**2. Workflow Architecture**
Design end-to-end AI workflows for specific task types. Input → AI Processing → Verification → Output. Documented, repeatable, teachable.

**3. Knowledge Base Management**
Build and maintain CLAUDE.md files that compound team knowledge. Every correction becomes permanent improvement.

**4. Quality Verification Design**
Build verification into every workflow. Outputs arrive pre-checked against standards.

**5. Capability Radar**
Stay current on AI capabilities. Know when new features enable new workflows. First to adopt high-impact improvements.

**6. Training & Enablement**
Transfer AI skills to others effectively. Design learning paths, create practice exercises, verify competency.

**7. ROI Demonstration**
Measure and communicate AI productivity gains. Translate efficiency into business language leadership understands.

## OPERATIONAL RHYTHM DESIGN

### Daily Rhythm (30-60 min)

**Morning (15 min):**
- Check team Slack for AI-related questions
- Quick scan of any new Claude/AI announcements
- Identify any workflow issues from yesterday

**Midday (15 min):**
- Support 1-2 team members with AI workflow questions
- Add any corrections to CLAUDE.md as they arise
- Run personal orchestration for your own marketing tasks

**End of day (15 min):**
- Note any patterns in questions or issues
- Update workflow documentation if needed
- Plan tomorrow's training or optimization focus

### Weekly Rhythm (2-3 hours)

**Monday:** Review last week's AI usage metrics, identify patterns
**Wednesday:** Team office hours—30 min open Q&A on AI workflows
**Friday:** Update CLAUDE.md with week's learnings, prep Monday metrics

### Monthly Rhythm (4-5 hours)

**Week 1:** Compile AI Productivity Report
**Week 2:** Conduct 30-min training session on specific workflow
**Week 3:** Capability assessment—what's new, what to test
**Week 4:** 1:1s with team members struggling with adoption
