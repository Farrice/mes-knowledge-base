### Phase 2: 60-30-10 Expectation Setting

Apply the distribution model to the sprint:

For a sprint producing **[N] pieces**:
- **~60% ([X] pieces)** will feel awful. You'll cringe. You'll want to delete them. **Ship anyway.** These are the tax for the 10%.
- **~30% ([Y] pieces)** will be "meh." Functional but uninspiring. Ship them without fuss.
- **~10% ([Z] pieces)** will surprise you. These are the ones that land. You don't get to choose which ones they are in advance.

**The Picasso Reframe**: Of 14,990 paintings, Picasso is known for ~5. Your sprint of [N] pieces might produce 1 that matters. That's the point.

### Phase 3: De-Shielding Protocol

Identify shields to remove during the sprint. Each shield is a comfort behavior that feels productive but blocks skill acquisition:

| Shield | Why It Feels Productive | Skill It Blocks | Sprint Rule |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Scripting | "Ensures quality" | Off-the-cuff speaking, improvisation | No scripts. Bullet points max. |
| Over-editing | "Makes it polished" | On-camera charisma, natural presence | Max 1 editing pass. Ship imperfect. |
| Waiting for inspiration | "Ensures authenticity" | Discipline, showing up without motivation | Create on schedule, not on mood. |
| Excessive review | "Catches mistakes" | Confidence, bias toward action | Publish within 2 hours of creation. |
| Platform optimization | "Maximizes reach" | Content quality (the actual skill) | Same format, same platform. No optimization during sprint. |

### Phase 5: Momentum Mechanics

Volume alone doesn't work — you need **compounding velocity.** Design the sprint's psychological architecture to create momentum that builds on itself:

**The "Eyes Forward" Rule**: No reviewing previous content during the sprint. Yesterday's post is dead. This isn't just productivity advice — it's neurological. Looking backward activates self-assessment circuitry that kills creative output. The sprint moves in one direction only: forward.

**Distraction Elimination Protocol**:
- Remove analytics apps during sprint (no checking engagement, comments, or follower counts)
- Set phone to focus mode during creation blocks
- No A/B testing, no optimization experiments — ship and move
- If someone gives negative feedback mid-sprint → log it for post-sprint review, don't process it now

**Velocity Compounding Architecture**:
| Sprint Day | Expected State | Intervention |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Day 1-3 | Resistance. Every piece feels wrong. | Expected — the Grind Zone IS the point. Ship anyway. |
| Day 4-7 | Slight loosening. Still uncomfortable but faster. | Notice: prep time dropping? That's the signal. |
| Day 8-14 | Patterns emerging. Your "voice" starts showing up uninvited. | Document recurring themes — HK-5 activating. |
| Day 15-21 | Flow moments. Some pieces feel effortless. | These are Impact Zone previews. Note what was different. |
| Day 22-30 | New baseline established. Can't go back to old volume. | Your skill floor has permanently risen. |

**Accountability Architecture** (optional but powerful):
- Post publicly with a sprint hashtag or daily log
- Share daily piece count with 1 person (not the content itself — just the count)
- The sprint is the constraint. The constraint is the creative freedom.

### Phase 7: Post-Sprint Retrospective Framework

Design the retrospective the creator will run after the sprint — this is where the REAL insight lives:

**Voice Emergence Tracking**:
- What topics kept appearing even when you didn't plan them? → These are your natural territories
- What phrases or structural patterns repeated across pieces? → This is your emerging voice signature
- Which pieces felt effortless vs. forced? → Map the effort-to-impact for YOUR specific content
- What did your audience respond to that surprised you? → Audience-revealed genius (they see what you can't)

**The Audience Mirror Protocol** (HK-2 applied retrospectively):
- Review the 10% that performed best: What energy were you in when you created them?
- Review the 60% that performed worst: What energy were you in?
- Pattern will emerge: your best work correlates with a specific emotional/motivational state. That's your creative sweet spot. Engineer conditions for it.
