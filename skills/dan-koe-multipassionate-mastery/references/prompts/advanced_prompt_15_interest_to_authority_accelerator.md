## CORE METHODOLOGY: THE AUTHORITY ACCELERATION FRAMEWORK

### The Authority Equation

**Authority = (Visible Learning × Unique Perspective × Consistent Output) ÷ Positioning Dilution**

- **Visible Learning**: Show the process, not just the results
- **Unique Perspective**: Your intersection makes your take valuable
- **Consistent Output**: Regular content compounds authority
- **Positioning Dilution**: The more you claim, the weaker each claim

### The 4 Types of Authority

**Type 1: Credential Authority**
"Trust me, I have degrees/certifications"
- Slowest to build (years)
- Most expensive (education costs)
- Easiest to verify
- LEAST relevant in creator economy

**Type 2: Results Authority**
"Trust me, I've achieved X"
- Medium to build (requires doing the thing)
- Requires proof/documentation
- Highly persuasive
- VERY relevant in creator economy

**Type 3: Teaching Authority**
"Trust me, I can explain this clearly"
- Fast to build (90 days)
- Requires communication skill
- Built through content
- MOST relevant in creator economy

**Type 4: Perspective Authority**
"Trust me, I see this differently"
- Fastest to build (immediate)
- Requires unique viewpoint
- Built through positioning
- HIGHEST leverage in creator economy

## DEPLOYMENT PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

**Week 1: Authority Positioning**

*Step 1: Interest-Authority Mapping*
- List all your genuine interests
- Identify which ones have market demand
- Find the unique intersection that only you occupy

*Step 2: Authority Claim Definition*
Create your specific authority position:
"I help [specific people] understand/achieve [specific thing] through [your unique intersection/method]."

Keep it narrow. Authority dilutes when you claim too much.

*Step 3: The "Two Steps Ahead" Validation*
You don't need to be an expert. You need to be:
- Actively learning (demonstrably)
- Able to explain clearly (teaching authority)
- Offering unique perspective (intersection)
- Two steps ahead of your audience

*Questions to validate:*
- Have you learned more about this than 80% of your target audience?
- Can you explain concepts clearly to beginners?
- Do you have a unique perspective from your intersection?
- Are you actively improving your knowledge?

If yes to all: You're qualified to teach.

**Week 2: Visible Learning Infrastructure**

*Step 1: Learning Documentation System*
Set up system to capture learning:
- Notes from every book/course/podcast
- Screenshots of key insights
- Personal experiments and results
- Questions that arise during learning

*Step 2: Content Translation Pipeline*
Every piece of learning becomes content:
- Key insight → Twitter thread
- Experiment result → Case study post
- Book notes → Newsletter
- Question exploration → Long-form content

*Step 3: The "Learn in Public" Commitment*
Commit to sharing your learning process:
- "Here's what I'm reading this week"
- "Here's what I learned from X"
- "Here's an experiment I'm running"
- "Here's a question I'm exploring"

**Week 3: First Content Sprint**

*Create 15 Pieces of Content:*

- 5 "What I Learned" posts (from recent learning)

- 2 "Question Exploration" posts (thinking out loud)

*The Authority Signal:*
People who share what they're learning consistently are perceived as:
- Curious (positive)
- Growing (positive)
- Generous (positive)
- Knowledgeable (because they keep demonstrating knowledge)

**Week 4: Community Positioning**

*Step 1: Identify Authority Hubs*
Where do people interested in your topic gather?

- Subreddits
- Newsletters

*Step 2: Value-First Engagement*
