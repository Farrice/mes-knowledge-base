## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Part A: Launch Sequence Design

#### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (14-30 days before)
1. **BUILD** anticipation through staged reveals:
   - **Week 4**: Seed the problem (content that agitates the pain or gap)
   - **Week 3**: Introduce the solution category (not the product—the TYPE of solution)
   - **Week 2**: Social proof and teaser reveals (results, behind-the-scenes, countdowns)
   - **Week 1**: Full reveal announcement + waitlist / early access
2. **DESIGN** the pre-launch content calendar:
   - Daily email sequence for warm audience (7-10 emails minimum)
   - Social content cadence across platforms
   - Strategic content drops (free value that positions the paid offer)
   - Partner/affiliate activation timeline
3. **BUILD** launch assets:
   - Landing/sales page (deploy Copy Assembly Engine, Prompt #2)
   - Email sequences for each audience segment
   - Ad creative (deploy Ad Script Assembly Line, Prompt #3)
   - Retargeting sequences for funnel stages

#### Phase 2: Launch Window (3-7 days)
4. **ORCHESTRATE** the compressed buying event:
   - **Day 1**: Cart open announcement (surge of launch energy)
   - **Days 2-4**: Proof, proof, proof (testimonials, case studies, demonstrations)
   - **Day 5**: Objection handling (address the top 3 reasons people haven't bought)
   - **Days 6-7**: Urgency close (deadline, price increase, bonus removal)
5. **MANAGE** real-time optimization:
   - Monitor conversion rates hourly during launch
   - Deploy contingency emails for soft open rates
   - Adjust ad spend based on early performance signals
   - A/B test critical elements (subject lines, CTA buttons)

#### Phase 3: Post-Launch (7-14 days after)
6. **CAPTURE** residual demand:
   - Downsell sequence for non-buyers
   - Extended payment plan offer
   - Waitlist for next cohort/launch
7. **ANALYZE** launch performance:
   - Revenue by source (email, paid, organic, affiliate)
   - Conversion rates by funnel stage
   - Email engagement metrics by sequence
   - Ad cost per acquisition by creative
8. **EXTRACT** assets for evergreen deployment:
   - Best-performing emails → automated sequences
   - Winning ad creative → always-on campaigns
   - Proven objection-handling content → sales page updates

### Part B: Cold Traffic Funnel Architecture

#### Phase 4: Funnel Design
9. **ARCHITECT** the cold traffic funnel:

   **AWARENESS STAGE** (Top of Funnel)
   - Lead magnet design (must be genuinely valuable, not a glorified brochure)
   - Landing page for cold traffic (skepticism-aware copy)
   - Entry offer (low-ticket product that covers ad costs and qualifies buyers)
   
   **CONSIDERATION STAGE** (Middle of Funnel)
   - Nurture sequence that builds trust, demonstrates expertise, and addresses objections
   - Content that educates on the PROBLEM and positions your approach as the logical solution
   - Trigger events that identify high-intent prospects
   
   **CONVERSION STAGE** (Bottom of Funnel)
   - Main offer presentation (sales page, VSL, or webinar)
   - Application/consultation for high-ticket offers
   - Fast-action incentives for conversion
   
10. **DESIGN** the traffic → conversion flow:
    - Cold traffic → Lead magnet → Welcome sequence → Offer
    - Cold traffic → Entry offer → Ascension → Core offer
    - Cold traffic → Content → Retargeting → Offer

### Part C: Retargeting Strategy

#### Phase 5: Retargeting Architecture
11. **SEGMENT** retargeting audiences by behavior:
    - **Site visitors** (saw the page, didn't convert) → Different message than cold traffic
    - **Content consumers** (watched video, read blog) → Social proof + soft offer
    - **Cart abandoners** (started but didn't finish) → Urgency + objection handling
    - **Email subscribers who didn't buy** → Deeper value + testimonial-driven approach
    - **Past buyers** (for cross-sell/upsell) → New offer or upgrade path
12. **DESIGN** retargeting campaigns per segment:
    - Creative that acknowledges their behavior ("We noticed you were interested...")
    - Message escalation over time (value → proof → urgency → final reminder)
    - Frequency caps to prevent ad fatigue
    - Creative rotation schedule
13. **SET** retargeting budget allocation:
    - Allocate 15-25% of total ad budget to retargeting
    - Tier spend by audience warmth (cart abandoners get highest spend per person)
    - Set exclusion rules (don't retarget buyers with the same offer)

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Launch timing and intensity should match audience size and warmth. A 50K subscriber list can support a 7-day launch event. A 2K subscriber list needs a longer pre-launch runway and supplemental paid traffic.

Cold traffic funnels require patience—the economics may not work on Day 1. Design for 30-60 day LTV, not immediate ROI. The entry offer funds the funnel; the backend makes the profit.

Retargeting is where most marketers leave money on the table. The 97% who didn't convert on first touch aren't "lost"—they're "not yet ready." Design retargeting as a conversation that continues, not an ad that repeats.
