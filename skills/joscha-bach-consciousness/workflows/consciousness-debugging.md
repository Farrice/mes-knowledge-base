# Consciousness Debugging

> Bach's radical reframe: suffering is not ambient — it's one part of your mind inflicting pain signals on another part. Debug it like software.

## When to Use

- Creative block / procrastination / resistance
- Existential anxiety or "stuckness" with no clear cause
- Internal conflict between competing goals
- Someone (or you) keeps cranking up self-criticism with no resolution
- Coaching context: client is stuck and "just push through it" isn't working

## The Debugging Protocol

### Step 1: Identify the Pain Generator

Bach: "There is some part of you that is generating suffering... one part of the mind that is inflicting pain on another."

**Questions to ask**:
- Where is the pain signal coming from? (Which "part" of you is generating it?)
- What is the pain signal trying to accomplish? (Every pain signal has a goal)
- Is the pain signal directed at you, or at a situation you can't control?

**Common generators**:
| Generator | What It Wants | Typical Signal |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Achievement system | You to produce output | Anxiety, restlessness, guilt |
| Social system | You to maintain reputation | Shame, fear of judgment |
| Safety system | You to avoid risk | Paralysis, procrastination |
| Identity system | You to maintain coherence | Existential dread, impostor syndrome |
| Values system | You to align behavior with beliefs | Moral discomfort, righteous anger |

### Step 2: Map the Gradient

Bach: "Sometimes the problem is that the pain signal would tell you to act, but acting doesn't fix it because it's a bad gradient."

**Diagnose**:
- Is the gradient pointing at a solvable problem? → Follow it
- Is the gradient pointing at an unsolvable problem? → The signal is broken — you need to renegotiate, not comply
- Is the volume increasing without progress? → Bad gradient — the pain generator is cranking up because compliance isn't producing results

### Step 3: Internal Negotiation

Bach: "You normally need to convince your outer system that your performance is actually going to be better if you stop suffering."

This is not positive affirmations. It's internal diplomacy:

**1. Acknowledge the generator**: "I see that [part] wants [outcome]. That's a valid goal."
**2. Diagnose the failure**: "The current approach (pain signals) is not producing [outcome] because [reason]."
**3. Propose alternative**: "If we try [alternative approach], [outcome] is more likely because [mechanism]."
**4. Negotiate terms**: "Can we reduce the pain signal for [time period] while testing the alternative?"

### Step 4: Resolution Check

After implementing the negotiation:

- Has the pain signal reduced? → Resolution working
- Has the pain signal shifted to a new target? → New conflict surfaced — return to Step 1
- Has the pain signal increased? → Negotiation rejected — revise the proposal in Step 3

## The Conflict Topology

Bach describes suffering as often arising from irreconcilable goal systems:

```
Goal A: "I want creative freedom"
Goal B: "I want financial security"
Conflict: These feel mutually exclusive → system generates suffering to force resolution
```

**Resolution strategies**:
1. **Reframe**: Are they actually mutually exclusive? (Often not)
2. **Sequence**: Can you pursue one first, then the other?
3. **Synthesis**: Is there a third option that serves both?
4. **Accept**: If truly irreconcilable, which goal system do you subordinate? (This requires mourning the subordinated goal)

## Output Format

```
PAIN SIGNAL: [Description of what you're experiencing]

GENERATOR: [Which part of the mind is producing this]
GOAL: [What the pain generator wants]
GRADIENT: [Solvable / Unsolvable / Bad gradient]

CONFLICT MAP: [If applicable — which goals are in tension]
NEGOTIATION: [Proposed internal deal]
RESOLUTION: [What changes if negotiation succeeds]
```

## Quality Gate

- Did you identify a specific generator, not just "I feel bad"?
- Did you evaluate the gradient — is the pain pointing somewhere useful?
- Did you propose a negotiation that acknowledges the generator's goals?
- If there's a conflict, did you map both sides honestly?
