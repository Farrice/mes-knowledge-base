# Postmodernist Trap Audit

> Bach's diagnostic: when narrative disconnects from capability, systems die. "The story that we tell ourselves about what the system is doing becomes far less important than how well the system is able to deal with the ground truths."

## When to Use

- Evaluating a competitor, institution, or niche for real vs. claimed capability
- Auditing your own systems for narrative inflation
- Coaching someone who "talks about doing" more than "does"
- Analyzing why an industry leader is vulnerable to disruption
- System quality check on the Antigravity system itself

## The Audit Protocol

### Step 1: Narrative Capture

Document what the system claims:

| Claim | Source | How Prominent? |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| "We do [X]" | [where they say it] | Core/Secondary/Background |
| "We achieve [Y]" | [where they claim it] | Core/Secondary/Background |

Bach's example: "If you look at NASA, it didn't really achieve its mission for a very long time... it became more of an employment program."

### Step 2: Capability Measurement

For each claim, measure actual capability:

| Claim | Evidence of Capability | Last Verified | Confidence |
|-------|----------------------|---------------|------------|
| "We do [X]" | [actual output/evidence] | [when] | High/Medium/Low/None |
| "We achieve [Y]" | [actual output/evidence] | [when] | High/Medium/Low/None |

**The key move**: Don't accept narrative evidence (testimonials about how great they are). Look for ground-truth evidence (actual output, actual results, actual capability demonstrations).

### Step 3: Gap Score

Calculate the Narrative-Capability Delta:

```
For each claim:
  Narrative Intensity (1-10): How loudly/frequently do they claim this?
  Capability Evidence (1-10): How strong is the ground-truth evidence?
  Gap = Narrative Intensity - Capability Evidence
```

| Claim | Narrative (1-10) | Capability (1-10) | Gap |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Claim 1 | N | C | N-C |
| Claim 2 | N | C | N-C |

**Interpretation**:
- **Average Gap < 2**: Healthy system. Narrative tracks capability.
- **Average Gap 2-4**: Early postmodernization. Narrative starting to drift.
- **Average Gap 5-7**: Advanced postmodernization. Narrative has largely replaced capability.
- **Average Gap 8+**: Terminal postmodernization. System is a story about itself. Ripe for disruption.

### Step 4: Forcing Function Design

Bach's lesson from SpaceX vs. NASA: "SpaceX disrupted them precisely because NASA had become a story."

For each high-gap claim, design a ground-truth forcing function:

| Claim | Gap | Forcing Function |
|-------|-----|-----------------|
| "We do [X]" | [score] | [concrete test that would prove or disprove] |

**Forcing function types**:
1. **Public demonstration**: Make the system perform, not describe
2. **Outcome tracking**: Measure results, not intentions
3. **Competitive benchmark**: Compare against someone who actually does it
4. **Constraint test**: Remove resources and see what still works
5. **User/Customer audit**: Ask the actual recipients, not the providers

### Step 5: Disruption Opportunity Map (Optional)

If auditing a competitor or market:

| High-Gap Claim | Disruption Opportunity | What Would It Take? |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| [their claim] | [how to take this from them] | [resources/capability needed] |

## Output Format

```
SYSTEM: [What you're auditing]

NARRATIVE MAP: [Top 3-5 claims]
CAPABILITY MAP: [Evidence for each claim]
GAP SCORES: [Narrative-Capability delta per claim]
OVERALL HEALTH: [Healthy / Early Drift / Advanced / Terminal]

FORCING FUNCTIONS: [Ground-truth tests for each high-gap claim]
DISRUPTION OPPORTUNITIES: [If applicable]

VERDICT: [One-paragraph assessment]
```

## Quality Gate

- Did you use ground-truth evidence, not narrative evidence?
- Did you resist the temptation to accept the system's story about itself?
- Are your forcing functions concrete and testable?
- Could this audit survive if the system's leadership read it? (If it relies on insider access rather than observable reality, it's weak)

## Self-Application

> This workflow can and should be applied to the Antigravity system itself. Periodically audit: are the skills doing what the SKILL.md files claim? Are the workflows producing the outputs they promise? Is the system telling a story about itself that exceeds its actual capability?
