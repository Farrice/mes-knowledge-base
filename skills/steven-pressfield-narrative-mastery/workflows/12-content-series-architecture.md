# Workflow 12 — Content Series Architecture

> **What this is**: Design multi-part content series where each installment is a chapter in a larger narrative, not a standalone asset. YouTube series, podcast arcs, newsletter sequences, course modules — all connected by mystery threads that create cross-installment gravitational pull.

---

## What This Produces

1. **Series Concept Document** — Overarching narrative, curse, genre + spin, and the "unsolvable mystery" that binds all installments
2. **Episode/Installment Map** — Each chapter with its own arc, hook, and contribution to the larger narrative
3. **Mystery Thread Architecture** — What questions open, close, and compound across installments
4. **Retention Engineering** — Specific techniques for pulling audiences from one installment to the next
5. **Series Bible** — Character/theme consistency guide across all chapters

---

## Input Required

- **Series topic or domain** — What the series covers
- **Format** — YouTube, podcast, newsletter, course, or hybrid
- **Installment count** — Target number of episodes/issues/modules
- **Audience context** — Who they are, what they already know, what they resist
- **Strategic goal** — Build authority, generate leads, sell offer, educate, entertain

---

## Load Context

Read [genius.md](../genius.md) before execution. Key patterns for this workflow:
- **GP1 (Three-Act Gravitational Field)** — Applied to both individual installments AND the full series arc
- **GP2 (Inciting Incident as Future-Flash)** — Episode 1 must flash-forward to the series climax
- **GP3 (Start at the End)** — Design the series finale FIRST, then reverse-engineer
- **GP4 (Genre + Spin)** — Establish the series genre early, then twist one element
- **HK1 (The Story Behind the Story)** — The series-level mystery that never fully resolves
- **R4 (Territory vs. Hierarchy)** — Series must feel territory-driven, not algorithm-chasing

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Series Climax Design

Start at the end. Answer:
- What is the EMOTIONAL DESTINATION of the entire series?
- What transformation does the audience undergo across all installments?
- What is the "big reveal" or culminating insight?
- What mystery remains OPEN even after the series concludes?

Design the final installment first. Everything else reverse-engineers from here.

### Phase 2 — The Overarching Curse

Every great series has a central tension that won't resolve easily:
- **What is broken?** — The systemic problem, the misconception, the limit
- **Why can't it be fixed quickly?** — What makes this a multi-chapter problem?
- **Why does the audience carry this curse too?** — Pattern-match to their experience

The curse creates the gravitational pull across all installments. Without it, you have a playlist, not a series.

### Phase 3 — Three-Act Series Architecture

Map the full series to Pressfield's three-act structure:

**Act I (Installments 1–25%)** — Ordinary World & Inciting Incident
- Establish the curse and the stakes
- Future-flash to the climax (let them glimpse where this goes)
- Set the genre conventions
- Open the primary mystery thread

**Act II (Installments 25%–75%)** — The Villain Owns the Middle
- Escalate obstacles and complexity
- Let the "villain" (misconception, industry, status quo) dominate
- Open secondary and tertiary mystery threads
- Include the "All Is Lost" moment at ~70% mark
- Each installment adds a layer; none can be skipped without loss

**Act III (Installments 75%–100%)** — Resolution & New Mystery
- The epiphany reframe
- Transformation crystallized
- Primary mystery thread resolved
- A BIGGER mystery opened (sequel potential or ongoing engagement)

### Phase 4 — Installment-Level Architecture

For EACH installment, produce:

```
EPISODE [#]: [Title]

HOOK — Which hook pattern? (Future-Flash / Mystery Thread / Gossip Receptor / Quiet Solo)
CONTRIBUTION — What does this episode add to the series narrative?
INTERNAL ARC — What micro-transformation happens within this episode?
MYSTERY MANAGEMENT:
  - Threads CARRIED from previous: [list]
  - Thread OPENED in this episode: [new question]
  - Thread RESOLVED in this episode: [answer given]
  - Thread COMPOUNDED: [question deepened]
CLIFFHANGER/BRIDGE — How does this episode pull into the next?
FIVE AIMS CHECK — Drama ✓ | Internal→External ✓ | Meaning ✓ | Universal ✓ | Beautiful ✓
```

### Phase 5 — Mystery Thread Map

Create a visual tracking document:

| Thread | Opened (Ep) | Deepened (Eps) | Resolved (Ep) | Status |
|--------|-------------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| Primary Mystery | 1 | 3, 5, 7 | 10 | Core |
| Secondary | 2 | 4, 8 | 9 | Supporting |
| Tertiary | 4 | 6 | Never | Ongoing |

Rules:
- Never open more than 2 threads in one installment
- Always resolve at least 1 thread before the audience's patience breaks
- The primary thread compounds — each "answer" reveals a deeper question
- At least one thread never resolves (the "unsolvable mystery" — see GP17)

### Phase 6 — Retention Engineering

Apply these cross-installment techniques:

1. **The Episode 1 Future-Flash** — Show the audience the climax in Episode 1. They'll stay to see how you get there.
2. **The Mid-Series "All Is Lost"** — At the midpoint, everything the series has built appears to collapse. Audiences who survive this become committed.
3. **The Recurring Element** — A phrase, image, or concept that appears in every installment, gaining new meaning each time.
4. **The Gossip-Pattern Tease** — End each installment with information the audience CANNOT sit on.
5. **The Identity Investment** — By Episode 3, the audience should identify with the series protagonist (even if that's "the idea"). Leaving the series now means abandoning that identity.

## Output Contract

Deliver:
1. ✅ Series Concept Document (climax, curse, genre + spin, overarching mystery)
2. ✅ Full Episode/Installment Map (every chapter with hook, contribution, internal arc, mystery management)
3. ✅ Mystery Thread Map (visual tracker)
4. ✅ Retention Engineering Annotations (specific techniques per installment transition)
5. ✅ Series Bible (character/theme/voice consistency guide)

---

## Quality Gate

Run against the Five Aims at BOTH levels:
- Does each INDIVIDUAL installment heighten, externalize, mean, universalize, and beautify?
- Does the FULL SERIES arc heighten, externalize, mean, universalize, and beautify?

Additional series-specific checks:
- [ ] Could someone skip installments and still feel satisfied? (If yes → mystery threads are too weak)
- [ ] Does the series feel like a SERIES, not a collection? (Connected arcs, not standalone repeats)
- [ ] Is the final installment earned by everything before it?
- [ ] Does a mystery remain open after the series ends?
