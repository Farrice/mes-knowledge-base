# Workflow 15 — Cross-Domain Narrative Transfer Engine

> **What this is**: Pressfield's core superpower operationalized. Take a narrative structure from one domain — film, novel, myth, historical event, personal experience — and transplant its gravitational architecture into a completely different context: a sales funnel, product launch, pitch deck, course curriculum, hiring process, or organizational change initiative. The physics are domain-agnostic; this workflow makes them domain-portable.

---

## What This Produces

1. **Source Narrative Analysis** — Structural X-ray of the source story's gravitational forces
2. **Transfer Map** — Element-by-element mapping from source domain to target domain
3. **Reconstructed Architecture** — The target content, built on the source story's skeleton
4. **Adaptation Notes** — What was preserved, what was modified, and WHY
5. **Stacking Opportunities** — Other experts to bring in for domain-specific execution

---

## Input Required

- **Source narrative** — The story, myth, film, event, or experience being transplanted (full text, summary, or title if well-known)
- **Target domain** — What this architecture needs to become (sales funnel, pitch deck, course, etc.)
- **Target audience** — Who the target content serves
- **Strategic intent** — What the target content must accomplish
- **Constraints** — Length, format, medium, tone requirements

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Source Narrative X-Ray

Deconstruct the source story using the full 18-pattern checklist. For each force present, extract its FUNCTION, not its form:

```
SOURCE: [Title/Description]

STRUCTURAL FORCES PRESENT:
GP1: Three-Act Structure → FUNCTION: Builds momentum through Hook → Escalation → Release
GP2: Future-Flash → FUNCTION: Opening lets audience see the prize before the journey
GP3: Start at End → FUNCTION: [The Shawshank Redemption] — Red on the beach = the destination
...

CHARACTER FORCES:
GP5: The Curse → FUNCTION: Andy Dufresne's wrongful conviction = limitation that creates empathy

TENSION FORCES:
GP12: Villain Owns Act 2 → FUNCTION: Norton + Hadley = 19 years of institutional oppression
GP13: All Is Lost → Epiphany → FUNCTION: Brooks's death + Tommy's murder = maximum darkness

MYSTERY:
GP17: Mystery → FUNCTION: The poster, the rock hammer = what's really going on?
```

The key output: a list of FUNCTIONS, stripped of their domain-specific forms.

### Phase 2 — Target Domain Mapping

For each function identified in Phase 1, find its equivalent in the target domain:

| Source Function | Source Form | Target Domain | Target Form |
|----------------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| Creates empathy through limitation | Andy's wrongful conviction | Sales funnel | Prospect's inherited problem — "it's not your fault" |
| Villain domination of the middle | 19 years of prison | Email sequence | Weeks 2-4: obstacles, failed solutions, growing frustration |
| Future-flash (audience sees destination) | Freedom imagery in opening | Landing page | Hero section: "Imagine your business in 90 days" |
| All Is Lost → Epiphany | Tommy's murder → escape plan | Webinar | "I tried everything... then realized I was solving the wrong problem" |
| Mystery thread | The poster | Content series | Recurring unanswered question across installments |
| Sacrifice | Andy crawls through sewage | Case study | Client's investment, risk, what they gave up |
| Identity revelation | "Get busy living" | Brand manifesto | Core belief statement — who we really are |

Rules for mapping:
- Transfer the FUNCTION, not the form
- The target form must feel native to its domain, not forced
- If a force doesn't have a clear target equivalent, it may not be needed — check, don't force it
- Some forces will be MORE powerful in the target domain than the source

### Phase 3 — Reconstruction

Build the target content using the mapped architecture:

**Step 1: Start at the ending**
- What is the target's emotional destination? Map it to the source story's ending.
- The target audience should arrive at the same FEELING as the source story's audience — different context, same physics.

**Step 2: Build the three-act structure**
- Act I: Establish the curse (the prospect's/audience's limitation) + future-flash (the promise)
- Act II: Villain dominance (the obstacles, the failed solutions, the status quo's grip)
- Act III: All Is Lost → Epiphany → Sacrifice → Resolution

**Step 3: Engineer the protagonist**
- Who is the protagonist in the target? (The customer, the founder, the audience?)
- Map the source protagonist's curse → transformation → sacrifice → identity revelation

**Step 4: Plant the mystery**
- What unsolvable question sits at the heart of the target content?
- This is what makes the audience STAY — not closure, but curiosity

**Step 5: Design the Michael Corleone Moment**
- Where in the target content is the point of no return?
- For a sales funnel: the moment the prospect decides "I'm in" before they even see the price
- For a course: the module where commitment becomes irreversible

### Phase 4 — Adaptation Notes

Document what was transferred verbatim, what was modified, and what was discarded:

```
TRANSFER LOG

PRESERVED (Identical function, different form):
- Three-act momentum ← Source: Film's 3-act structure → Target: Email sequence arc
- Curse-driven empathy ← Source: Wrongful conviction → Target: "Industry lies you've been told"

MODIFIED (Function adapted to fit target constraints):
- Villain domination shortened from 60% to 30% ← Target domain (landing page) can't sustain as long
- Mystery thread made explicit ← Source used visual subtlety; target uses verbal promise

DISCARDED (Not transferable or not needed):
- Quiet Solo Moment ← Target domain (social post) doesn't have room
- Child/Divine element ← Not applicable to B2B pitch context

AMPLIFIED (Stronger in target than source):
- Future-flash ← Landing pages can be MORE explicit about the destination than films
```

## Quality Gate

Run the Five Aims on the TARGET content:
- [ ] Does the target content have genuine gravitational pull — does it FEEL like a story, even though it's a [funnel/deck/course]?
- [ ] Is the source story's emotional impact preserved? Would someone who knows the source recognize its skeleton?
- [ ] Is the transfer invisible? (The audience shouldn't think "oh, this is based on The Shawshank Redemption" — they should just feel pulled)
- [ ] Are the forces deployed NATIVE to the target domain? Nothing should feel forced or imported
- [ ] Does a mystery remain open?

Cross-domain-specific checks:
- [ ] Could someone who's never seen/read the source still be fully moved by the target?
- [ ] Has at least ONE force been AMPLIFIED in the transfer (stronger in target than source)?
- [ ] Are the stacking recommendations actionable?
