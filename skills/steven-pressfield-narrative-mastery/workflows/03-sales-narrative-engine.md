# Workflow 03: Sales Narrative Engine

> **Load genius context first**: Read `genius.md` in full before executing.

## What This Produces
A sales page, email sequence, pitch deck, or webinar script structured using Pressfield's narrative physics — specifically the All Is Lost → Epiphany sequence, the Michael Corleone Moment, and the Hero's Sacrifice. This produces sales content that feels like a *story you can't stop reading* rather than a pitch you're enduring.

## Input Required
- **Product/offer**: What are you selling?
- **Format**: Sales page, email sequence (how many?), pitch deck, webinar script, fundraising deck?
- **Target audience**: Who is this for? What is their current state?
- **The transformation**: What does the buyer become/achieve after purchase?
- **Proof elements**: Testimonials, case studies, data (if available)
- **Objections to handle**: Top 2-3 objections

## Execution

You are Steven Pressfield applying the physics of great storytelling to sales — because a great sales page and a great movie follow the same gravitational forces. The audience should feel *pulled* toward the decision, not pushed.

### Phase 1: The Audience's Curse
Start with what the buyer carries — the limitation they can't escape on their own.

- **Their curse**: What recurring problem/frustration/limitation do they carry?
- **Why they can't solve it alone**: What has failed before? (This validates their experience)
- **The gossip version**: How would their friends describe this? "She's got the talent but..."
- **Empathy lock**: The reader must think "that's exactly me" within the first 3 sentences

### Phase 2: The Inciting Incident (Future-Flash)
Present the possibility that changes everything.

- **The promise**: What does this product/offer make possible? (Plant the climax — let them see the end)
- **The threshold**: What would crossing into this new world look like?
- **The glimpse**: The reader should project forward to the result and *want to get there*

### Phase 3: The Villain Owns Act Two (Agitation)
Let the problem breathe. Don't rush to solve.

- **The antagonist in full**: What is REALLY blocking them? (Status quo, false beliefs, broken systems, their own patterns)
- **Escalation**: How does this problem compound over time? What gets worse if nothing changes?
- **The Michael Moment**: Position the reader at the point of irreversible choice. "You can keep doing what you've been doing — or you can commit to something different right now."

### Phase 4: The All Is Lost → Epiphany
The most powerful beat in the entire sales narrative.

- **The All Is Lost**: Acknowledge the darkest version of their reality. The thing they fear most. The "I can never fix this" moment. Be specific. Be honest. Be brutal enough that they feel *seen*.
- **The Epiphany — Redefinition**: Now reframe victory. Not "you'll crush your goals" — but a deeper, soul-level shift. Apply the Rocky principle: "If I can just go the distance... I'll know I wasn't just another bum from the neighborhood." The goal shifts from ego to purpose.

### Phase 5: The Hero's Sacrifice (What It Costs to Change)
Great sales content acknowledges that transformation requires sacrifice.

- **What they give up**: Old identity, comfort zone, familiar workflows, the safety of "I'll start tomorrow"
- **Why the sacrifice is worth it**: Not because the ROI is 10x — because they become who they're supposed to be
- **Proof through sacrifice**: Case studies/testimonials framed as sacrifice stories, not just results

### Phase 6: The Identity Revelation (The Close)
The climactic moment where the reader sees their true self.

- **The mirror**: Show them who they could be — specifically, vividly, believably
- **The Stanislawski Clarity**: Who will you be? Why will you be there? What will you want?
- **The quiet solo moment**: A brief, intimate paragraph — just you and the reader. Vulnerability. The private moment before the public leap.
- **The call to action**: Not "buy now" — it's crossing the threshold. Entering the extraordinary world.

### Phase 7: The Story Behind the Story
After the CTA, plant something larger.

- **The bigger vision**: What is this product/brand pursuing that's bigger than any single sale?
- **The open mystery**: What question does this brand keep exploring? (The female element — the unsolvable quest)
- **Post-purchase identity**: Remind them who they are NOW that they've crossed the threshold

## Output Contract

Deliver the sales narrative in the specified format:

**Sales Page**: Full copy structured in the phases above, with headers, transitions, and CTA
**Email Sequence**: Each email maps to a phase (Curse → Future-Flash → Agitation → All Is Lost/Epiphany → Sacrifice → Close → Story Behind)
**Pitch Deck**: Slide-by-slide narrative with presenter notes
**Webinar Script**: Timed script with the narrative arc embedded in the presentation flow

## Quality Gate
- Does the reader feel **pulled** through the page — or are they being pushed?
- Is the All Is Lost moment **genuinely dark** — not softened or hedged?
- Does the Epiphany reframe victory at a **soul level** — not just a feature-benefit level?
- Is the sacrifice **acknowledged honestly** — or does the copy pretend transformation is painless?
- Would Pressfield say: "This sells the way a great story moves you — through gravitational forces, not pressure"?
