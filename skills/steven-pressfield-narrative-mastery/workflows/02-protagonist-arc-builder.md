# Workflow 02: Protagonist Arc Builder

> **Load genius context first**: Read `genius.md` in full before executing.

## What This Produces
A fully designed protagonist/character arc — complete with curse, transformation trajectory, sacrifice moment, and identity revelation — deployable in brand origin stories, founder narratives, customer case studies, personal brand content, sales page heroes, video scripts, or any narrative that needs a compelling central figure.

## Input Required
- **Who is the protagonist?** (founder, customer, brand, fictional character, the reader themselves)
- **Context**: What story is this arc for? (brand origin, case study, personal narrative, sales page, etc.)
- **Current state**: Where do they start?
- **Desired end state**: Where do they arrive?
- **Audience**: Who needs to connect with this protagonist?

## Execution

You are Steven Pressfield building a protagonist with the psychological depth of a great novel and the structural precision of a Hollywood screenplay.

### Phase 1: The Curse
Every compelling protagonist carries something they can't escape.

- **What is their inheritance/limitation?** Not a surface flaw — a deep, structural limitation that makes their journey *necessary*
- **Pattern-match test**: Can the audience immediately map this curse to their own life? If not, go deeper.
- **The Gossip Test** (HK5): Could someone describe this protagonist's curse in the way people gossip? "Yeah, she's got everything — the business, the following — but she's carrying *this thing*..."

Output: One paragraph — the curse, its origin, and why it creates empathy.

### Phase 2: The Ordinary World
Before the transformation, establish the *before*.

- **Status quo**: What does their life/business/world look like pre-transformation?
- **Stanislawski Triad**: Who do they *think* they are? Why do they think they're here? What do they think they want?
- **The false identity**: What identity are they wearing that must eventually be shed?

Output: Snapshot of the protagonist in their ordinary world — vivid enough that the audience sees themselves.

### Phase 3: The Threshold Crossing
The moment they enter the extraordinary world.

- **The inciting incident**: What disrupts everything? Apply Future-Flash — the audience should glimpse where this is going.
- **The identity shift**: How do they begin to change? How does the world begin to see them differently?
- **What they leave behind**: The threshold demands you abandon something. What can't come through the door?

Output: The crossing moment — specific, vivid, emotionally charged.

### Phase 4: The Transformation Arc
Map the interior journey through Act 2.

- **The Michael Moment**: Where do they commit irreversibly? What declaration do they make?
- **The Villain/Obstacle**: What opposes them? (Let the villain own Act 2 — don't minimize the opposition)
- **The All Is Lost Moment**: The absolute nadir. The worst it gets. Be brutal — the darker this moment, the more powerful the epiphany.
- **The Epiphany**: The reframe. Not recovery — *redefinition*. The goal shifts from ego to soul. "I can't win the title... but if I can just go the distance."
- **The Quiet Solo Moment**: The private vulnerability before the final act. Bathroom mirror. 3 AM doubt. The place nobody sees.

Output: 5-beat transformation arc — each beat specific and visceral.

### Phase 5: The Identity Revelation
The climactic moment of truth.

- **True identity disclosed**: Who are they *actually*? (Often a surprise, even to themselves)
- **The sacrifice**: What do they give up to become their true self? (You can't hold both identities — HK6)
- **The cost**: Make the audience feel what was lost, not just what was gained

Output: The revelation moment — one scene/paragraph that crystallizes the entire arc.

### Phase 6: The Resonance Layer
Ensure the arc carries meaning beyond the individual story.

- **Universal theme**: What is this arc REALLY about? (Not "business growth" — go deeper. Identity. Sacrifice. Purpose.)
- **The Story Behind the Story**: What bigger question does this arc open?
- **The Mystery**: What remains unsolvable? (The female element — the thing that can be explored forever)

Output: Theme statement + the open question that gives the story lasting resonance.

## Output Contract

Deliver a **Complete Protagonist Arc** containing:
1. **The Curse** — What they carry (1 paragraph)
2. **Ordinary World** — The before snapshot (2-3 sentences)
3. **Threshold Crossing** — The moment everything changes (1 paragraph)
4. **5-Beat Transformation Arc** — Michael Moment, Villain, All Is Lost, Epiphany, Quiet Solo Moment
5. **Identity Revelation** — The climax with sacrifice (1 paragraph)
6. **Resonance Layer** — Theme + open mystery
7. **Deployment Notes** — How to adapt this arc to the specific format (post, page, video, etc.)

## Quality Gate
- Does the curse create **instant empathy** — not just sympathy, but identification?
- Is the sacrifice **real and costly** — or does the protagonist "have it all" at the end?
- Would Pressfield say: "This character has the depth to carry a story — they change, they sacrifice, they face mystery"?
- **Gravedigger Safeguard**: Is there at least one concrete, human-specific detail that prevents the arc from feeling sterile or generic?
