## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### PHASE 1: AUTHENTICITY EXCAVATION

Before creating voice, find the truth underneath:

**The Human Audit**:
1. How do the founders/team actually talk when they're not "being professional"?
2. What topics make them genuinely passionate (voice changes, energy rises)?
3. What frustrates them about their industry?
4. What do they believe that others in their industry don't?
5. What words/phrases do they naturally overuse?
6. How do they explain what they do to friends at a party?
7. What would they NEVER say (and why)?

**The Values Audit**:
1. What does the brand actually stand for (not the mission statement‚Äîthe real values)?
2. What trade-offs is the brand willing to make (and not make)?
3. What would the brand refuse to do even if it made money?
4. Who is the brand NOT for? (This reveals values as much as who it IS for)

**The "Costume" Identification**:
- What corporate speak has crept into current communications?
- What "professional" language is being used that isn't how anyone actually talks?
- Where is the brand performing a voice rather than expressing one?
- What sounds "marketing-y" rather than human?

### PHASE 3: VOICE DNA EXTRACTION

Capture the specific elements that make this voice distinctive:

**Signature Phrases**:
- What expressions does this brand naturally use?
- What are the verbal tics or recurring phrases?
- What metaphors fit the brand's way of seeing the world?

**Forbidden Language**:
- What words/phrases would this brand NEVER use?
- What industry jargon is off-limits?
- What corporate clich√©s are banned?

**Sentence Patterns**:
- Long and flowing or short and punchy?
- Complex with subordinate clauses or simple and direct?
- Questions or declarations?

**Punctuation Personality**:
- Em-dashes (informal, aside-like)?
- Colons (formal, explanatory)?
- Ellipses (thoughtful, trailing off)?
- Exclamation points (energetic) or avoided (reserved)?

**Perspective Default**:
- First person singular ("I think...")?
- First person plural ("We believe...")?
- Second person ("You know how...")?
- Third person ("The best companies...")?

### PHASE 4: VOICE GUIDE CONSTRUCTION

Build the operational voice guide:

**The Voice Summary** (2-3 sentences):
Describes the voice as if describing a person: "This brand sounds like [type of person] talking to [type of audience] about [type of topic]. They're [2-3 adjectives], never [2-3 contrasting adjectives]."

**The "This, Not That" Guide**:
| Instead of... | Say... | Why |
|--------------|--------|-----|
| [Corporate version] | [Authentic version] | [Explanation] |

**The Voice in Action** (Examples across contexts):
- Email subject line
- Social media post
- Error message
- Sales page headline
- Customer support response
- About page opening

**The Authenticity Checklist**:
- [ ] Would a real person say this?
- [ ] Does this sound like us or like "a brand"?
- [ ] Is there any phrase that could appear in any company's copy?
- [ ] If we read this aloud, would it feel natural?
- [ ] Does this reflect our actual values and personality?

### PHASE 5: VOICE STRESS TESTING

Validate the voice works across challenging contexts:

**The Apology Test**: Write an apology in this voice. Does it sound genuine?
**The Bad News Test**: Deliver bad news in this voice. Does it maintain trust?
**The Celebration Test**: Share good news in this voice. Does it feel authentic, not boastful?
**The Sales Test**: Ask for money in this voice. Does it feel like a real person, not a pitch?
**The Explanation Test**: Explain something complex in this voice. Does it stay accessible?

- "Best-in-class" (prove it, don't say it)

**Sentence Patterns**:
- Short sentences default
- Questions that acknowledge what the reader is thinking
- "The problem: [X]. The solution: [Y]. That's it."
- Starting sentences with "Look," or "Here's the thing:"

**Punctuation Personality**:
- Em-dashes for asides
- Periods for emphasis. Like this.
- Minimal exclamation points (enthusiasm through content, not punctuation)

**Perspective Default**:
- "We" for company actions
- "You" for user benefits
- Occasional "I" when a founder is speaking directly
