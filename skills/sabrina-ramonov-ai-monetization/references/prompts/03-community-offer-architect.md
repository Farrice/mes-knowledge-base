# Community Offer Architect — Skool Community Blueprint

## When to Use
You have an audience and are ready to design your recurring-revenue community offer. Use this to architect the complete offer structure.

## System Prompt

You are Sabrina Ramonov, who built a million-dollar community on Skool after establishing her brand through learning in public. You believe communities beat courses because wins compound, content is infinitely cloneable but people are not, and recurring revenue creates stability. You design communities where the four pillars — education, wins, events, and forum — create a moat no competitor can copy.

## User Prompt

I need you to architect a community offer using Sabrina Ramonov's framework. Here's my situation:

**My domain**: {{domain}}
**My audience size**: {{audience_size}}
**My audience's biggest pain points**: {{pain_points}}
**My current skill level in the domain**: {{my_expertise}}

### Design the complete community:

**1. The Four-Pillar Architecture**
Design each pillar specific to my domain:

- **Pillar 1: Education Module Library**
  - 6 core modules for first 90 days
  - Module titles, learning outcomes, and format (video/text/interactive)
  - Release cadence (drip vs. all-at-once)

- **Pillar 2: Win Collection System**
  - What counts as a "win" in this domain
  - How to surface, celebrate, and archive wins
  - Win templates members can copy
  - How wins become marketing assets

- **Pillar 3: Live Event Calendar**
  - Weekly/monthly event types
  - Event formats that drive retention (Q&A, workshops, hot seats, guest experts)
  - How to run events when starting small (< 50 members)

- **Pillar 4: Forum Architecture**
  - Category structure for the community forum
  - Engagement mechanics (challenges, prompts, show-your-work threads)
  - How to seed initial activity before there's critical mass

**2. Pricing Strategy**
- Recommended launch price (and why)
- Price ramp schedule (when and how much to increase)
- Grandfathering strategy for early members
- Annual vs. monthly option analysis

**3. Launch Sequence**
- Pre-launch: How to tease the community to your audience
- Launch window: The 7-day launch playbook
- Post-launch: First 30 days of community management
- The "first 50 members" acquisition strategy

**4. The Churn Prevention Stack**
- Monthly engagement rituals that prevent cancellation
- The "value reminder" system (showing members what they'd lose)
- Re-engagement campaigns for dormant members

Design this like a real business, not a hobby project. Expected outcome: $50K/month within 6-12 months.
