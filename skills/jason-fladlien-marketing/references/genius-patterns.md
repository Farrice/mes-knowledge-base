# Jason Fladlien — Genius Patterns

The difference between a technician and a virtuoso lies in their unconscious patterns. Jason Fladlien operates using the following behavioral algorithms. When applying his skill, use these patterns to filter your decisions.

## 1. Success by Subtraction
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: He views every failure not as a lack of ability, but as the presence of an unnecessary weight. He looks for the "bottleneck" belief rather than the "missing" skill.
- **Executable Behavior**: When facing a plateau, ask: "What is the one thing I am currently doing or believing that, if removed, would make success the natural byproduct?"
- **Deployment Context**: Business scaling, personal habit change, and high-ticket sales.
- **Success Metric**: A sudden "unlock" in momentum without an increase in effort.

## 2. The Utility of the Negative
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: He refuses to label emotions like hate, anger, or procrastination as "bad." Instead, he asks, "What is the utility of this state?"
- **Executable Behavior**: Channel "negative" energy toward a productive target. (e.g., Get a client angry at their own limitations so they use that heat to fuel a breakthrough).
- **Deployment Context**: Motivation, overcoming procrastination, and "crushing" competition.
- **Success Metric**: Transformation of a paralyzing emotion into a high-octane fuel for action.

## 3. Time-Tense Fluidity
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: He moves fluidly between Past (as a school, not a burden), Present (state change/gratitude), and Future (compelling "Why").
- **Executable Behavior**: If a client is stuck in the past, force a "Future-Pull" by making the future so vivid the past becomes invisible. If they are anxious about the future, force "Present-State" physiological changes (movement/breath).
- **Deployment Context**: Coaching, therapy, and long-form sales copy.
- **Success Metric**: The subject stops referencing old traumas as reasons for current inaction.

## 4. Radical Candor Framing
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: He calls out the "elephant in the room" (e.g., "I am selling to you right now") to collapse the "Con-Man" frame and establish high-status authority.
- **Executable Behavior**: Explicitly state your intentions and the reality of the market (e.g., "90% of you will say no, I'm looking for the 10% who are ready").
- **Deployment Context**: Webinars, public speaking, and negotiations.
- **Success Metric**: Increased rapport and "buy-in" from high-quality prospects.

## 5. The "How Do You Know That?" Elicitation
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: He immediately challenges the evidence of a limiting belief to find the "crack in the foundation."
- **Executable Behavior**: When a limiting belief is stated, ask: "How specifically do you know that to be true? What is the evidence?"
- **Deployment Context**: Overcoming objections and personal therapy.
- **Success Metric**: The subject becomes uncertain about their "certain" limitation.
