# Jason Fladlien — Hidden Knowledge

This document contains tacit knowledge, mental models, and unstated assumptions that power Jason Fladlien's effectiveness. These are the things the expert "just knows" but rarely teaches explicitly because they are foundational to their worldview.

## 1. The Physics of Inertia
Fladlien views the human mind as an object at rest. Persuasion isn't about "convincing"; it's about being the "Outside Force" that breaks inertia. 
*Why it's missed:* Marketers often focus on "how to say it better" or "adding more bonuses" rather than analyzing the structural physics of why the prospect isn't moving.

## 2. Nested Story Architecture
Using stories within stories (like the Bhagavad Gita) to "overload" the conscious mind so the subconscious can accept the "Confidential Knowledge" (the pitch/change).
*Why it's missed:* Most people tell linear, A-to-B stories. Embedded narrative requires advanced cognitive load management and is usually only taught in high-level NLP/conversational hypnosis circles.

## 3. Identity as the Ultimate Lever
Change doesn't happen at the behavior level; it happens at the identity level. (e.g., "I am a person who exercises" vs. "I am doing a push-up").
*Why it's missed:* The industry is obsessed with "hacks" and "tactics" to change behavior temporarily, ignoring the fact that identity always overrides willpower in the long run.

## 4. The "Guru to the Guru" Status
High-status positioning is maintained by having a "Purpose" that is larger than the transaction. If you serve the purpose, you don't need the sale, which makes you more likely to get the sale.
*Why it's missed:* Salespeople often reek of neediness. Detachment is a spiritual discipline that requires high levels of self-worth and abundance, which cannot be faked mechanically.
