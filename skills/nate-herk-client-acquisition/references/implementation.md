# Nate Herk Implementation Pathway

## 1. Offer First (Validation Phase)
Pick a niche and define an outcome-based offer before building the technical system.
- Decide what process you are automating (e.g., lead capture, customer onboarding).
- Create a hyper-specific outcome claim ("I will save you 10 hours a week on X").

## 2. Validate With Volume (Outreach Phase)
Send high-volume outreach (100+/day). Use responses (even rejections) as market signals to refine the offer.
- **Goal**: Reach >5% reply rate. If you hit this, you have a validated offer.
- **Action**: Use simple, value-focused messages ("I'm just getting started..."). Don't get technical.
- **Rule**: Do not build the complex backend automation until you get someone who actually wants it.

## 3. Deliver & Document (Fulfillment Phase)
Build the system, over-deliver, and track before/after KPIs for case studies.
- **Baseline**: "How long does the process take manually right now?"
- **Post-Launch**: Measure the new KPIs. Build a simple dashboard to show the client the exact ROI of your work.
- **The Ask**: Wait 1-2 months. Do a performance review. Ask the Golden Question: "Do you know any other business owners who might need AI automation?"

## 4. Scale with Borrowed Authority (Trojan Horse Phase)
Use the documentation to pitch existing agencies on the Trojan Horse method, accessing their warm client base.
- Identify marketing, accounting, or consulting agencies in your space.
- Pitch: "I'll do free AI diagnostic calls for your clients. I look like your white-label expert. You get 20% of whatever closes."
- Run Discovery Calls using the Constraint Protocol ("What breaks at 300 clients?").
