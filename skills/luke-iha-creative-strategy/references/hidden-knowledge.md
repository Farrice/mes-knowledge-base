## Creative Strategy ≠ Copywriting
Iha draws a hard line. Copywriting is *how* you say it. Creative strategy is *what* to say, *to whom*, and *why*. A creative strategist decides the angle, the audience segment, the emotional lever, the competitive positioning — THEN hands that brief to a copywriter (or AI). This meta-skill commands 2-3x the rates because it sits upstream of all execution. If the strategy is wrong, perfect copy still fails.

## The Review Economy
On platforms like Upwork, reviews aren't feedback — they're compound interest. One 5-star review makes the next gig easier to land. Five reviews unlock algorithmic visibility and the "Rising Talent" badge. Ten reviews with specifics ("he tripled our conversion rate") create an unassailable moat. But one bad review early can destroy momentum.

Iha's solution: over-deliver so aggressively that a bad review is *structurally impossible*. Do $3K work on a $500 gig. The client would feel guilty leaving less than 5 stars.

## Portfolio Bootstrapping
You don't need real clients to have a portfolio. Pick brands you admire. Rebuild their worst ad, landing page, or email sequence. Record a Loom explaining your strategic decisions — not just what you changed, but *why*. This "spec work" demonstrates competence identical to paid work — and often *better*, because you chose the subject and had full creative control.

The portfolio IS the pitch. The Nuclear VSL isn't just a technique — it's the realization that your best work, presented well, does all the selling for you.

## Positioning Is "How Can I Be #1?"
Most freelancers ask "how can I be good?" The right question is "how can I be #1?" And the answer isn't being the BEST — it's being the most SPECIFIC. Create a category so narrow you're the only occupant. [Specific Skill] + [Specific Niche] + [Specific Outcome] = Category of One. "I write VSLs for supplement brands doing $1-5M/yr that need to scale past the $5M ceiling" — no one else can claim that exact positioning.

## Entry Offers Are Trojan Horses
The entry offer isn't where you make money. It's how you get IN THE DOOR. Design entry offers to be: low-risk, fast-delivery (3-7 days), demonstrably excellent, and — most importantly — naturally create the expansion conversation. "Now that we fixed your email sequence, let me show you the 3 funnel breaks that are costing you $50K/month." The expansion offer is where revenue lives. The entry offer's purpose is to make the expansion inevitable.
