# Psychological Trigger Architect

> Deploy Jeremy Miner's Frame/Deframe/Reframe Engine to engineer belief shifts in any context.

## When to Use

- Writing sales copy or landing pages that must shift a belief
- Preparing for a sales call where the prospect holds a limiting belief
- Creating content that challenges a common misconception
- Designing presentations that need to move an audience from A to B
- Crafting email sequences that transform perception

## Phase 1: Frame Identification

Identify the prospect/audience's current belief system (frame).

**Questions to answer:**
1. What does your prospect currently believe about this topic?
2. Where did that belief come from? (childhood, culture, bad experience, competitor messaging)
3. How is that belief serving them? (protection, identity, comfort)
4. What language reveals this frame? (exact phrases they use)

**Output:** A clear statement: "My prospect currently believes [X] because [Y], and this belief protects them from [Z]."

**Example:** "My prospect believes that hiring a ghostwriter means they're being inauthentic, because they were taught that real leaders write their own content. This belief protects them from the vulnerability of someone else representing their voice."

## Phase 2: Deframe Construction

Design the disruption that makes their current belief unstable.

**Deframe Mechanics (choose 1-3):**

| Mechanic | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Consequence Exposure** | Show the cost of keeping the belief | "Every week you spend writing your own posts is a week you're NOT coaching the clients who need you..." |
| **Identity Contradiction** | Reveal the belief conflicts with who they want to be | "The executives I work with who care most about authenticity are the ones who realized..." |
| **Third-Person Story** | Bypass defenses through narrative | "I had a client last year who felt the same way. She spent 6 hours every week writing posts. Her coaching business stayed flat..." |
| **100% Framing** | Force evaluation against perfection | "So you're 100% happy with the engagement your current content is getting?" |
| **Neutral Doubt Seeding** | Subtle uncertainty without direct attack | "Your current content strategy is... fairly decent. What's made you start looking at alternatives?" |
| **Self-Persuasion Question** | Let them articulate the problem | "What's caused you to feel like writing all your own content might not be the best use of your time?" |

**Rules:**
- NEVER directly attack their current belief (triggers fight-or-flight)
- Use questions, not statements
- Use concerned or curious tone, never challenging tone (that comes later)
- The deframe should feel like a DISCOVERY, not a lecture

## Phase 3: Reframe Installation

Install the new belief that makes your solution the natural conclusion.

**Reframe Architecture:**

1. **State the new frame** as if it's obvious, not revolutionary:
   - "The coaches who grow fastest figured out something counterintuitive..."
   
2. **Provide social proof** through indirect attribution:
   - "My most authentic clients are actually the ones who use a ghostwriter..."
   
3. **Lock with identity language:**
   - "You seem like the type of person who focuses on their zone of genius rather than getting stuck in tasks anyone could do..."
   
4. **Binary identity lock:**
   - "Is that strategic thinking something you've always had, or something you developed?"

## Phase 4: Trigger Sequence Assembly

Chain the three phases into a deployable sequence.

**Format for each trigger:**

```
CURRENT FRAME: [what they believe]
↓
DEFRAME: [the disruption — question or story]
 └─ Tone: [curious / confused / concerned]
 └─ Mechanic: [which deframe type]
↓
REFRAME: [the new belief to install]
 └─ Identity Label: [who they become by accepting this]
 └─ Binary Lock: [the either/or that confirms it]
```

**Deliver 3-5 trigger sequences** for the given context, each targeting a different limiting belief.

## Phase 5: Tonal Annotation

Annotate every line with tonal direction:

- 🔵 **Curious** — open, questioning uplift
- 🟡 **Confused** — slightly puzzled, gets them to elaborate
- 🔴 **Challenging** — direct, firm (use only mid-sequence, never at start)
- 🟢 **Concern** — slow, downward, empathetic (the money tone)
- 🟣 **Playful** — light, humorous (nervous system calming)

Add verbal pacing markers: `...` = strategic pause for internalization.

## Output Format

For each trigger sequence, deliver:
1. **Target Belief** — the frame you're shifting
2. **Trigger Sequence** — the full deframe → reframe flow with tonal annotations
3. **Deployment Context** — where/when to use it (opening, mid-call, email, post, etc.)
4. **Self-Persuasion Checkpoint** — the question that makes them articulate YOUR conclusion

## Quality Gate

Before delivering, verify:
- [ ] Does the deframe use questions, not accusations?
- [ ] Does the reframe install an identity, not just an idea?
- [ ] Is the tonal flow correct (never challenging at the start)?
- [ ] Would the prospect feel DISCOVERED something, or feel SOLD?
- [ ] Does every trigger sequence end with a binary lock?
