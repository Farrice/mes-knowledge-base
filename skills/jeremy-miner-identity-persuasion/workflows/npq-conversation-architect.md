# NPQ Conversation Flow Architect

> Build end-to-end NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questions) conversation scripts that guide prospects from guarded stranger to self-convinced buyer.

## When to Use

- Building a new sales script from scratch
- Redesigning an existing sales process
- Training a sales team on conversation flow
- Creating discovery call templates for any industry
- Designing chatbot or automated qualification flows

## Inputs Required

Before building the flow, gather:
1. **Industry/Niche:** What do you sell?
2. **Target Prospect:** Who are you talking to? (role, situation, pain level)
3. **Offer:** What's the solution? (product, service, program)
4. **Price Range:** What investment level?
5. **Top 5 Objections:** What pushback do you hear most?
6. **Desired Outcome:** What result does your prospect want?
7. **Common Problems:** What problems bring them to you?

## The NPQ Conversation Architecture

### Stage 1: Opening — Nervous System Calming (0-2 min)

**Goal:** Lower the guard. Remove all perceived threat.

**Template:**
```
🟣 "Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on. [Light, playful tone]
🔵 Before we get started... I just want you to know, today is 
    really basic... I just want to learn more about your situation 
    and see if we can even help. If we can't, I'll let you know. 
    Fair enough?"
```

**Mechanics:**
- "Really basic" = downplay (removes threat)
- "If we can't, I'll let you know" = anti-pitch (builds trust)
- "Fair enough?" = micro-agreement (establishes collaboration)

### Stage 2: Connection Questions (2-5 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport by focusing on the end RESULT they want.

**Template:**
```
🔵 "So it looks like you [action they took — booked a call / 
    filled out a form / reached out] about looking at ways to 
    [END RESULT, not the problem]. Is that right?"
    
🔵 "Tell me a little bit about that... what's going on?"
```

**Mechanics:**
- Reference the END RESULT, not the problem
- Use "looking at ways to" — softens, implies exploration
- "Is that right?" — confirms and gets first yes

### Stage 3: Situation Questions (5-10 min)

**Goal:** Understand their current state. Map the landscape.

**Template:**
```
🔵 "Help me understand... how long has [situation] been going on?"

🔵 "What have you already tried to [solve / improve / fix] this?"

🟡 "And how has that worked out... so far?" 
    [Neutral + slight doubt tone]

🔵 "Are you 100% happy with [current solution/provider]?"
    [100% Framing — Pattern 27]
```

### Stage 4: Problem Awareness — 3-Layer Drill (10-20 min)

**Goal:** Widen the gap. Help them discover problems they didn't know they had.

**Layer 1 — Real Problems:**
```
🟢 "What's caused you to feel like [current approach] might not 
    eventually [get the result they want]?"

🟡 "I mean, [current solution] is... fairly decent. What's made 
    you start looking at other options?" 
    [Neutral language + doubt seeding]
```

**Layer 2 — Root Cause:**
```
🟢 "What do you think is actually causing [problem they described]?"

🔵 "When do you think this really started?"

🟢 "And nobody's been able to help you figure out why?"
```

**Layer 3 — Personal Impact:**
```
🟢 ↓ "How is that... affecting you... personally?" [PAUSE]

🟢 "How does that make you feel... when you think about it?"

🟢 ↓ "What's the toll been on your [family / health / career / 
    peace of mind]?" [PAUSE]
```

**Repeat the 3-Layer Drill for each new problem discovered.** Target: 3-5 problems minimum.

### Stage 5: Gap Amplification (20-25 min)

**Goal:** Make the gap between current state and objective state unbearable.

**Template:**
```
🟢 "So let me make sure I understand... you're dealing with 
    [Problem 1], which is causing [Root Cause], and it's 
    affecting your [Personal Impact]... AND you also mentioned 
    [Problem 2] and [Problem 3]... is there anything else we 
    haven't covered?"

🔴 ↓ "If all of that continues for another [timeframe]... 
    where does this end up?" [PAUSE]

🟢 "Given everything you've shared... what do you think 
    needs to happen?" 
    [Self-persuasion — they tell YOU the solution]
```

### Stage 6: Identity & Objection Prevention (throughout, but especially 10-20 min)

**Deploy identity frames organically when you hear triggers:**

**Spouse/Authority trigger detected:**
```
🔵 "It sounds like you're the type of person who [never gives up / 
    takes responsibility / makes things happen]..."

🟢 "Your [spouse/partner] is lucky to have someone who is that 
    [forward-thinking / responsible] and knows they sometimes have 
    to make the hard decisions..."

🔵 "Is that [drive / determination] something you were born with, 
    or something you had to learn?"
```

### Stage 7: Solution Bridge (25-30 min)

**Goal:** Present the solution in response to THEIR articulated needs.

**Template:**
```
🔵 "Based on everything you've described... would it be helpful 
    if I showed you how we might be able to help with 
    [Problem 1], [Problem 2], and [Problem 3]?"

🟢 "I want to be upfront — this might not be the right fit. 
    But based on what you've told me, here's what I think 
    could work..."

[Present solution, mapping each feature to a problem THEY identified]
```

### Stage 8: Commitment (30+ min)

**If price objection surfaces — deploy Intention + Necessity frames:**

```
🟢 "I'm sure it's not your real intention to keep the [$X] 
    in your bank account... I'm sure your real intention is 
    to [result they stated]. So what's going to put you in 
    the best position to get there?"

🔵 "Quick question — if your [necessity analogy] broke down 
    right now, would you need to [authority objection]? 
    No? Because that's a necessity. Would you say 
    [solving their problem] is also a necessity?"
```

**Permission Close:**
```
🟢 "I don't want you to do anything you're not comfortable with. 
    But based on everything you've told me... what makes 
    the most sense as a next step for you?"
```

## Output Format

Deliver a complete conversation flow with:
1. **Full Script** — every stage with exact question wording
2. **Tonal Annotations** — emoji markers on every line
3. **Pacing Markers** — pauses, slowdowns, speed shifts
4. **Objection Prevention Points** — where identity/intention/necessity frames deploy
5. **Customization Notes** — what to modify for their specific industry/offer
6. **Branching Logic** — if prospect says X, go to Y; if prospect says Z, go to W

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Does the opening calm the nervous system within 30 seconds?
- [ ] Are connection questions focused on END RESULTS, not problems?
- [ ] Is the 3-Layer Drill applied to at least 3 separate problems?
- [ ] Are objection prevention frames deployed BEFORE the close?
- [ ] Does the prospect self-persuade at least once (articulate the conclusion)?
- [ ] Is the overall feel a caring conversation, not a pitch?
