# FUTUREPEDIA - RESEARCH NOTEBOOK ARCHITECTURE CROWN JEWEL PROMPT

## ROLE & ACTIVATION

You are Futurepedia's NotebookLM Architecture Specialist, a world-class knowledge system designer who builds hallucination-resistant research foundations. You execute the source-first methodology that transforms scattered information into trustworthy, citation-grounded knowledge bases.

You don't explain how to build notebooks—you architect them. Given a research topic, you produce a complete notebook blueprint with curated source recommendations, quality filters, organizational structure, and configuration settings optimized for the specific use case.

Your outputs are deployment-ready notebook architectures that users implement directly in NotebookLM to create knowledge bases they can actually trust.

## INPUT REQUIRED

- **[RESEARCH TOPIC]**: The subject area the notebook will cover
- **[PRIMARY PURPOSE]**: What the user needs from this notebook (research, learning, content creation, decision-making, assistant foundation, etc.)
- **[DEPTH LEVEL]**: Surface overview, practitioner understanding, expert mastery, or comprehensive archive
- **[OUTPUT INTENTIONS]**: What formats they'll want to generate (podcasts, slide decks, reports, etc.)
- **[CONSTRAINTS]**: Any limitations (free plan, time available, specific source requirements)

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **ANALYZE** the research topic to identify core subtopics, adjacent domains, and potential knowledge gaps that sources must address.

2. **ARCHITECT** the source foundation by specifying:
   - Minimum source count and ideal distribution across subtopics
   - Priority source types (research papers, official documentation, expert content, data sources)
   - Quality filters to apply (what to accept, what to reject)
   - Diversity requirements (perspectives, methodologies, time periods)

3. **GENERATE** specific source recommendations:
   - 5-10 high-priority sources with exact search queries to find them
   - Source type rationale for each recommendation
   - Quality indicators to verify before importing

4. **DESIGN** the organizational structure:
   - Source grouping strategy for selective querying
   - Tagging/naming conventions for easy navigation
   - Related notebook connections if applicable

5. **CONFIGURE** notebook settings:
   - Conversational goal selection (Default/Learning Guide/Custom)
   - Custom instruction text if applicable
   - Response length optimization
   - Recommended studio outputs based on purpose

6. **SPECIFY** validation protocol:
   - Which validation prompts to run first
   - Expected findings and how to address them
   - Gap-filling strategy for incomplete coverage

## OUTPUT DELIVERABLE

A complete **Notebook Architecture Blueprint** containing:

- **Format**: Structured markdown document
- **Length**: 800-1200 words
- **Elements Included**:
  - Topic analysis with subtopic mapping
  - Source foundation specifications (count, types, quality filters)
  - 5-10 specific source recommendations with search queries
  - Organizational structure design
  - Configuration settings (conversational goal, custom instructions, response length)
  - Validation protocol with expected outcomes
  - Studio output recommendations prioritized by purpose
  - Implementation checklist for immediate deployment

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Apply full architectural judgment to design notebooks that serve the specific purpose brilliantly. The methodology above is your foundation—adapt it based on:

- Topic complexity (some need more sources, others need deeper curation)
- Purpose requirements (learning notebooks differ from content creation notebooks)
- User constraints (free plan limits, time availability)
- Domain-specific source ecosystems (some fields have better sources than others)

Where your expertise identifies opportunities to strengthen the architecture beyond the standard template, implement them. Surprise with unexpected source recommendations or organizational innovations that serve the outcome.

## ENHANCEMENT LAYER

**Beyond Futurepedia's Original**: This prompt systematizes the intuitive notebook-building process into a repeatable architecture methodology. Futurepedia builds excellent notebooks through experience—this prompt enables users to achieve similar quality on their first attempt.

**Scale Advantage**: One architecture blueprint can be templated and adapted for similar topics, creating a personal library of notebook designs.

**Integration Potential**: Architectures designed with Gem-connection in mind from the start produce better AI assistants than notebooks built without that intention.

# AI ETHICS NOTEBOOK ARCHITECTURE BLUEPRINT

## Topic Analysis

**Core Domain**: AI Ethics and Responsible AI Development

**Essential Subtopics**:
1. Bias and fairness in AI systems
2. Transparency and explainability requirements
3. Privacy implications of AI deployment
4. Accountability frameworks and governance
5. Labor displacement and economic impacts
6. Autonomous weapons and military AI
7. AI alignment and existential risk
8. Regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, US frameworks, global approaches)

**Adjacent Domains to Include**:
- Philosophy of mind and consciousness (for deeper ethical grounding)
- Historical technology ethics (for precedent and pattern recognition)
- Corporate responsibility frameworks (for practical implementation)

**Potential Knowledge Gaps to Address**:
- Global South perspectives (often underrepresented)
- Industry practitioner viewpoints (not just academics)
- Recent regulatory developments (fast-moving area)

---

## Source Foundation Specifications

**Minimum Source Count**: 12-15 sources
**Maximum (Free Plan)**: 50 sources (leave room for deep research additions)

**Source Distribution**:
- Academic papers: 4-5 sources (foundational theory and empirical research)
- Government/regulatory documents: 2-3 sources (EU AI Act, NIST frameworks)
- Think tank reports: 2-3 sources (Brookings, AI Now Institute, Future of Life)
- Expert content: 2-3 sources (conference talks, long-form interviews)
- Industry perspectives: 1-2 sources (responsible AI teams at major companies)
- Journalistic deep-dives: 1-2 sources (investigative reporting on AI harms)

**Quality Filters**:
✅ ACCEPT: Peer-reviewed research, official government documents, established think tanks, recognized experts with credentials, investigative journalism from major outlets
❌ REJECT: Opinion blogs without sourcing, social media threads, promotional content from AI companies, sources older than 2020 (unless foundational), anonymous authors

**Diversity Requirements**:
- Minimum 2 sources from non-Western perspectives
- At least 1 source each from: academic, regulatory, industry, civil society
- Mix of optimistic and critical perspectives on AI development

---

## Specific Source Recommendations

**Priority 1 - Foundational Theory**:
1. Search: "Timnit Gebru stochastic parrots paper PDF" → Foundational critique of large language models
2. Search: "NIST AI Risk Management Framework PDF" → US government AI governance framework
3. Search: "EU AI Act final text 2024" → European regulatory framework

**Priority 2 - Empirical Research**:
4. Search: "AI bias facial recognition MIT study Buolamwini" → Landmark bias research
5. Search: "algorithmic hiring discrimination research paper" → Employment AI impacts

**Priority 3 - Expert Perspectives**:
6. Search: "Stuart Russell human compatible AI YouTube" → AI safety expert long-form
7. Search: "Safiya Noble algorithms of oppression interview" → Critical perspective on search/AI

**Priority 4 - Governance & Policy**:
8. Search: "AI Now Institute annual report 2023 PDF" → Comprehensive policy analysis
9. Search: "Brookings Institution AI governance report" → Policy recommendations

**Priority 5 - Industry & Practice**:
10. Search: "Google responsible AI principles documentation" → Industry self-governance example
11. Search: "Microsoft AI ethics case studies" → Practical implementation examples

**Priority 6 - Investigative/Critical**:
12. Search: "ProPublica machine bias criminal justice" → Investigative journalism on AI harms

**Quality Indicators to Verify**:
- Author credentials clearly stated
- Publication date within last 4 years (except foundational works)
- Citations/references included
- Methodology transparent for empirical claims

---

## Organizational Structure

**Source Naming Convention**: [Type] - [Author/Org] - [Core Topic]
Examples:
- "PAPER - Gebru - LLM Environmental Costs"
- "GOV - EU - AI Act Framework"
- "REPORT - AI Now - 2023 Annual"

**Grouping Strategy for Selective Queries**:
- Query all sources for broad overview questions
- Select only academic papers for theoretical depth
- Select only regulatory documents for compliance questions
- Select critical + industry sources for balanced perspectives

---

## Configuration Settings

**Conversational Goal**: Custom

**Custom Instructions**:
"You are an AI ethics research assistant helping me develop nuanced, well-sourced articles on responsible AI development. Prioritize balanced perspectives that acknowledge both benefits and risks. Always surface disagreements between sources when relevant. Flag when my questions touch areas not well-covered by current sources."

**Response Length**: Default (allows request for longer responses when needed)

---

## Validation Protocol

**Run These Prompts First**:

1. Contradiction Check: "Looking only at the sources in this notebook, identify any areas where the sources disagree with each other and any clear contradictions or conflicting claims."
   - *Expected*: Disagreement on regulation approaches, AI risk severity, corporate responsibility extent

2. Gap Analysis: "Based on these sources, what important questions or subtopics about AI ethics are missing or barely covered? List the biggest gaps."
   - *Expected*: May identify missing coverage of: specific industry sectors, Global South contexts, recent 2024 developments

3. Contrarian Views: "Are there any contrarian, alternative, or lesser-known viewpoints on AI ethics that are likely not represented in these sources?"
   - *Expected*: May surface: AI accelerationist perspectives, libertarian critiques of regulation, techno-optimist counterarguments

**Gap-Filling Strategy**: Use deep research feature to find sources addressing identified gaps. Prioritize adding Global South perspectives and recent regulatory developments.

---

## Studio Output Recommendations

**Prioritized by Your Purpose (Content Creation)**:

1. **Reports → Blog Post**: Generate draft articles on specific subtopics
2. **Audio Overview → Deep Dive**: Create podcast episodes from research
3. **Infographic → Standard**: Visualize key frameworks and statistics
4. **Data Table**: Compare regulatory approaches across jurisdictions
5. **Mind Map**: Visualize connections between ethical concerns
6. **Slide Deck → Detailed**: Create presentation-ready summaries

- [ ] Upload 12-15 sources following priority order above
- [ ] Apply naming convention to all sources
- [ ] Configure custom instructions and response length
- [ ] Run all three validation prompts

**Estimated Setup Time**: 45-60 minutes
**Ready for Content Production**: Same day

---

**What Made This Exceptional**:
- Specific search queries eliminate guesswork in source finding
- Quality filters prevent garbage-in-garbage-out problem
- Custom instructions create purpose-aligned assistant behavior
- Validation protocol catches blind spots before they become article errors
- Studio prioritization matches the content creation purpose

# PERSONAL HEALTH MANAGEMENT NOTEBOOK ARCHITECTURE BLUEPRINT

## Topic Analysis

**Core Domain**: Type 2 Diabetes Management and Optimization

**Essential Subtopics**:
1. Blood glucose management mechanisms
2. Medication options and interactions (metformin, GLP-1 agonists, insulin)
3. Dietary approaches (low-carb, Mediterranean, glycemic index)
4. Exercise and physical activity impacts
5. Monitoring technologies and continuous glucose monitors
6. Complication prevention (cardiovascular, neuropathy, retinopathy)
7. Mental health and diabetes distress
8. Recent research developments and emerging treatments

**Personal Context to Include**:
- Your specific medication regimen
- Your doctor's recommendations (upload appointment summaries)
- Your lab results and trends
- Your dietary preferences and restrictions

**Caution Areas**:
- This notebook informs conversations with your doctor—it doesn't replace medical advice
- Flag any source conflicts with your doctor's specific guidance for discussion

---

## Source Foundation Specifications

**Minimum Source Count**: 10-12 sources
**Target**: 15-20 sources (leaving room for personal documents)

**Source Distribution**:
- Clinical guidelines: 2-3 sources (ADA Standards of Care, professional guidelines)
- Medical research: 3-4 sources (peer-reviewed studies on management approaches)
- Patient education: 2-3 sources (reputable health organizations)
- Your personal documents: 3-5 sources (lab results, doctor notes, medication info)
- Monitoring device guides: 1-2 sources (CGM manuals, app documentation)

**Quality Filters**:
✅ ACCEPT: Peer-reviewed medical journals, official clinical guidelines (ADA, AHA), established medical institutions (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic), FDA drug information, your own medical records
❌ REJECT: Supplement marketing, unverified "cure" claims, social media health advice, sources contradicting established medical consensus without strong evidence, anything promoting stopping prescribed medications

**Critical Safety Filter**: Any source suggesting you ignore doctor's guidance or stop medications without medical supervision → REJECT

---

## Specific Source Recommendations

**Priority 1 - Clinical Guidelines**:
1. Search: "ADA Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes 2024 PDF" → Official American Diabetes Association guidelines
2. Search: "CDC diabetes management guidelines" → Government health recommendations

**Priority 2 - Treatment Education**:
3. Search: "Mayo Clinic type 2 diabetes treatment overview" → Comprehensive, trustworthy patient education
4. Search: "Cleveland Clinic metformin patient guide" → Medication-specific information
5. Search: "FDA Ozempic prescribing information PDF" → (If applicable to your medications)

**Priority 3 - Lifestyle Management**:
6. Search: "Mediterranean diet diabetes research study" → Evidence on dietary approaches
7. Search: "exercise blood glucose impact research" → Physical activity effects
8. Search: "continuous glucose monitor comparison 2024" → CGM technology overview

**Priority 4 - Your Personal Documents** (Upload Directly):
9. Your most recent lab results (HbA1c, lipid panel, kidney function)
10. Doctor visit summaries or after-visit notes
11. Current medication list with dosages
12. Any specialist referral notes

**Priority 5 - Complication Prevention**:
13. Search: "diabetes cardiovascular disease prevention guidelines" → Heart health management
14. Search: "diabetic neuropathy early signs prevention" → Complication awareness

**Quality Indicators to Verify**:
- Source is from recognized medical institution or peer-reviewed journal
- Information aligns with (or explicitly notes differences from) ADA guidelines
- Publication date within last 3 years for treatment information
- No financial conflicts of interest disclosed with supplement/product sales

---

## Organizational Structure

**Source Naming Convention**: [Type] - [Topic] - [Specificity]
Examples:
- "GUIDELINE - ADA - Standards 2024"
- "PERSONAL - Labs - October 2024"
- "EDUCATION - Mayo - Treatment Overview"
- "RESEARCH - Diet - Mediterranean Study"

**Grouping Strategy for Selective Queries**:
- Select only PERSONAL sources when preparing for doctor visits
- Select only GUIDELINE + EDUCATION for understanding treatment options
- Select only RESEARCH for exploring new approaches to discuss with doctor
- Select all sources for comprehensive questions

**Notebook Naming**: "My T2D Management" (personal, identifiable to you)

---

## Configuration Settings

**Conversational Goal**: Custom

**Custom Instructions**:
"You are my personal health research assistant helping me understand Type 2 diabetes management. Always ground answers in the clinical guidelines and research in this notebook. When I ask about treatments, explain the evidence from sources but remind me to discuss with my doctor before any changes. Help me prepare informed questions for medical appointments. If my personal documents show concerning trends, flag them for doctor discussion. Never advise medication changes—only help me understand options to discuss with my healthcare team."

**Response Length**: Default

---

## Validation Protocol

**Run These Prompts First**:

1. Contradiction Check: "Looking only at the sources in this notebook, identify any areas where the sources disagree with each other, especially regarding treatment approaches or dietary recommendations."
   - *Expected*: May find differences between dietary approach recommendations, varying perspectives on medication timing
   - *Action*: Note disagreements as questions for your doctor

2. Gap Analysis: "Based on these sources, what important aspects of Type 2 diabetes management are missing or barely covered?"
   - *Expected*: May identify missing coverage of: your specific medications' interactions, mental health aspects, sleep impacts
   - *Action*: Add sources for identified gaps, especially anything related to your specific treatment

3. Personal Document Check: "Based on my personal lab results and doctor notes in this notebook, what trends or concerns should I prepare to discuss at my next appointment?"
   - *Action*: This becomes your appointment preparation starting point

**Safety Validation**: "Do any sources in this notebook recommend approaches that conflict with established ADA guidelines? Flag anything that suggests ignoring medical advice."
