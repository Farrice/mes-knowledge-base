## CORE METHODOLOGY: The Removal Game Copy Framework

### The Fundamental Shift
**Conventional copy**: Here's why we're good → Here's why you should buy → Call to action
**Removal Game copy**: Here's the doubt you have → Here's why that doubt is resolved → Action feels obvious

### The 4-Layer Removal Architecture

**Layer 1: Doubt Identification**
Before writing a single word, identify the specific doubts blocking action:
- Trust doubts: "Can I believe these claims?"
- Value doubts: "Is this worth the money?"
- Fit doubts: "Will this work for my specific situation?"
- Timing doubts: "Is now the right time?"
- Risk doubts: "What if this doesn't work?"

**Layer 2: Doubt Prioritization**
Rank doubts by blocking power. The doubt that stops the most people is your headline. The second doubt is your lead. Stack removal in order of blocking severity.

**Layer 3: Doubt Dissolution**
Each doubt requires a specific dissolution mechanism:
- Trust doubts → Voice the skepticism first, then prove
- Value doubts → Reframe cost as investment with specific ROI
- Fit doubts → Narrow with exclusion, then include specifically
- Timing doubts → Show cost of delay with precision
- Risk doubts → Name the fear, then reverse it

**Layer 4: Certainty Installation**
After doubt removal, certainty emerges naturally. Don't re-sell—confirm the certainty and direct action.

## DEPLOYMENT PROTOCOL

### STEP 1: Doubt Audit (10 minutes)
List every reason someone might NOT take action:
- What have they tried before that failed?
- What do they believe about solutions like this?
- What are they afraid might happen?
- What would their skeptical friend say?
- What's their excuse for not acting?

### STEP 2: Doubt Hierarchy (5 minutes)
Rank by blocking power:
1. [Primary blocker] → Headline
2. [Secondary blocker] → Lead/Opening
3. [Tertiary blocker] → Body section 1
4. [Supporting doubts] → Body sections 2-3
5. [Final friction] → Close

### STEP 3: Dissolution Strategy (5 minutes per doubt)
For each doubt, determine:
- The exact language the prospect uses to express this doubt
- The dissolution mechanism (evidence, reframe, acknowledgment, proof)
- The certainty state that emerges after dissolution

### STEP 4: Removal Sequence Writing
Write the copy following the dissolution sequence:
- Headline: Name the primary doubt in their language
- Lead: Acknowledge the doubt is valid, begin dissolution
- Body: Complete dissolution, repeat for secondary doubts
- Close: Confirm certainty, direct obvious action

### STEP 5: Addition Audit (Final check)
Review copy and remove:
- Claims without doubt-dissolution purpose
- Benefits that don't address specific barriers
- Persuasion language that adds rather than removes

**Every tool failed because it asked your team to work differently.**

We sell you when the math is undeniable. Not before.

**"We don't have time to switch systems."**

Correct. You don't.

So there's no migration.

Flowstate runs parallel to whatever you're using now. It integrates with your existing tools—Slack, email, whatever chaos you're currently operating in.

It starts by reading, not replacing. It builds its understanding from your reality. Then, gradually, it starts adding value: surfacing stuck projects, flagging deadline risks, routing information to the right people.

Your team doesn't "switch" to Flowstate. They notice one day that they're using it—because it's been useful enough that they kept coming back.

That's adoption without adoption. Migration without migration.

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 2: High-Ticket Email Sequence (Email 1 of 3)

**Context**: A business coach selling a $15,000 6-month program. Audience has seen many coaching offers and is deeply skeptical that "another coaching program" will help. Primary doubt: "Coaching programs all sound the same and I don't know if this will actually work for my specific situation."

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

### DOUBT AUDIT RESULTS

| Doubt | Language | Blocking Power |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| "Coaching programs all sound the same" | "What's different about THIS?" | PRIMARY |
| "Doesn't apply to my situation" | "My business is different" | SECONDARY |
| "$15K is a lot for coaching" | "That's a huge investment" | TERTIARY |
| "I don't have time for another program" | "I'm already overwhelmed" | SUPPORTING |
| "I've tried coaching before" | "How is this different from what didn't work?" | FINAL |

### EMAIL 1: THE DOUBT-VOICING OPENER

---

**SUBJECT LINE**: I know what you're thinking about coaching programs

---

**BODY**:

I'm going to say something that most business coaches won't say:

**You should be skeptical about business coaching.**

If you've been running a business for more than a few years, you've probably:

- Invested in a program that sounded transformative in the sales pitch
- Gotten advice that worked for someone else but didn't fit your reality
- Felt like you were paying for motivation rather than actual strategic help
- Wondered why "successful" coaches seem to make most of their money... selling coaching

I'm not going to tell you I'm different. That's what everyone says.

Instead, I'm going to tell you exactly what I've noticed about why coaching fails—and let you decide if what I do addresses it.

**Why most coaching programs fail (the honest version):**

**Problem 1: Generic frameworks applied to specific situations.**

Most coaches have ONE methodology. They apply it to everyone. If your business fits the methodology, great. If it doesn't, you get advice that sounds right but creates friction when you try to implement it.

The tell: When the advice requires you to fundamentally change how your business operates rather than optimizing how it already works.

**Problem 2: Coaching calls as the core product.**

A 60-minute weekly call is not transformation. It's conversation. Transformation happens between calls—in the implementation. Most programs give you a call and leave you alone to figure out the rest.

The tell: When you leave calls inspired but don't know exactly what to do before the next call.

**Problem 3: Success stories that don't match your stage.**

"I helped a company 10x their revenue!" Great. What stage were they? What were their resources? What was their market? A strategy that works at $5M doesn't work at $500K. A strategy that works in SaaS doesn't work in services.

The tell: When testimonials don't include specific context about where the client started.

**Problem 4: Coaches who haven't run your type of business.**

There's a difference between someone who studies business and someone who has operated one. Theory sounds good until you're making payroll decisions and managing humans who don't behave like case studies.

The tell: When you ask "have you dealt with [specific situation]?" and they answer with frameworks instead of stories.

---

**Here's why I'm sending you this email:**

Not to convince you I don't have these problems. But to tell you that if you evaluate any coaching program (including mine) against these four problems, you'll make a better decision.

Tomorrow, I'll tell you specifically how I approach each of these. You can decide if my answers satisfy your skepticism or not.

For now, I want you to know something:

Your skepticism is data. It means you've seen enough to know what doesn't work. That's actually valuable.

The right coach for you isn't the one who overcomes your skepticism with persuasion. It's the one who addresses it with answers that make sense for your specific situation.

More tomorrow.

P.S. — If you want to jump ahead, reply to this email with a 2-sentence description of your business and your biggest current challenge. I'll tell you honestly whether I think I can help or not—and if I can't, I'll tell you who might be a better fit.
