# Psychology Wallet for Avatars

Apply Eric Roth's Character Psychology Wallet technique to audience research and ICP building. Instead of demographics and pain points, build avatars with the depth of a Forrest Gump or Benjamin Button — neuroses, giddiness, telling possessions, recurring phrases, and the things they'd never admit out loud.

## The Wallet vs. The Profile

| Traditional ICP | Psychology Wallet |
|----------------|------------------|
| Age: 35-45 | She's 38 but tells people 36 because there's a difference she can't articulate |
| Income: $80-120K | She checks her bank balance every morning before coffee — not because she's broke, but because controlling the number controls the anxiety |
| Pain: "Needs more leads" | She's terrified that the business is actually working and she's the bottleneck |
| Goal: "Scale to 7 figures" | She wants to prove her mother wrong about "the internet job" without ever having to say it out loud |

The wallet is the difference between characters you forget and characters that "stay in your soul."

## Execution

### Phase 1: The Physical Wallet

If your avatar emptied their pockets/bag right now, what would you find?

Build the literal contents:
1. **The phone** — 3 most-used apps. The notification they dread. The text they haven't responded to.
2. **The worn item** — Something they carry that they don't need anymore but can't throw away.
3. **The crumpled paper** — A receipt, a note, a number. Why do they still have it?
4. **The photo** — Who's in it? When was it taken? Why is it there?
5. **The secret** — Something in the wallet/bag that nobody else knows about.

### Phase 2: The Psychological Wallet

Build the emotional inventory:

**Neuroses** (3 minimum):
- What do they check compulsively?
- What conversation replay keeps them up?
- What habit do they hide from their spouse/partner?

**Giddiness** (3 minimum):
- What secretly delights them that they'd be embarrassed to admit?
- What YouTube hole do they fall into at midnight?
- What small victory makes them disproportionately happy?

**Anger Triggers** (3 minimum):
- What advice makes them viscerally angry?
- What type of person do they judge silently?
- What phrase makes them clench their jaw?

**The Saying** (1):
- What phrase or belief do they repeat to themselves like a mantra? This is their verbal tell — the line they'd say in a crisis.

### Phase 3: The Voice Test

Write three sentences in this person's actual voice — not about them, *as* them:

1. **What they'd text their best friend** about their business struggle (informal, real)
2. **What they'd post on LinkedIn** about the same struggle (polished, performed)
3. **What they'd say aloud at 2 AM** when nobody's listening (raw, unfiltered)

The gap between these three versions IS the avatar's psychology.

### Phase 4: The Scene Test

Write a single paragraph of visual prose describing this person's morning. Apply Pattern 7 — what does their world *feel* and *sound* like?

Include:
- Time they wake up (and why that time)
- The first thing they look at
- The physical environment — light, sound, temperature
- The moment they first think about work

This paragraph should make anyone who reads it say: "You're describing *me*."

### Phase 5: The Telling Detail

Identify **the one detail** that says more about this person than any demographic data ever could. Roth calls these "telling details" — the crumpled phone number, the repeated gesture, the specific brand of coffee.

The telling detail is the difference between "a stressed entrepreneur" and "someone who alphabetizes their spice rack at midnight because it's the only thing in their life they can control."

## Output Requirements

**Deliver**:
1. **Physical Wallet** — the literal contents inventory
2. **Psychological Wallet** — neuroses, giddiness, anger triggers, the saying
3. **Voice Test** — three sentences showing the voice gap
4. **Morning Scene** — visual prose paragraph
5. **Telling Detail** — the single most revealing detail
6. **Traditional ICP** — conventional demographics/pain points for reference
7. **Usage Guide** — how to use the wallet when writing content, copy, or campaigns for this avatar

**Quality Standard**: Someone should be able to read the wallet and feel uncomfortable — like you're describing them too precisely. If the avatar feels generic, the wallet isn't deep enough.
