# Ghostwriter Elevation Protocol

Apply Eric Roth's writing mastery to ghostwriting — the highest-difficulty application because you must deploy Oscar-level craft *inside someone else's voice*. This protocol ensures ghostwritten content doesn't just sound like the client — it sounds like the **best version** of the client.

## The Ghostwriting Paradox

Ghostwriting is Roth's "Third Rail" in its purest form: the final piece must be something **neither** the client nor the writer would create alone. The client provides the knowledge, the stories, the voice. The writer provides the craft — subtext, sensory prose, thematic structure, memorable lines. The result is a "third" piece that's better than either could produce.

## Execution

### Phase 1: Client Psychology Wallet

Before writing a single word of content, build a Roth-depth wallet for the client's voice:

**Voice DNA**:
1. **Metaphor reach**: What domains do they naturally draw metaphors from? (Sports? Cooking? Construction? Parenting?)
2. **Sentence rhythm**: Short punchy? Long flowing? Mixed? How do they end paragraphs?
3. **Verbal tics**: Phrases they overuse. Words they lean on. Transitions they repeat.
4. **Energy register**: When are they most animated? (Stories? Data? Contrarian takes?)
5. **Discomfort zone**: What topics make them stilted or formal? (These need the most craft intervention.)

**Personality Tells**:
1. **The laugh line**: What kind of humor do they use? Self-deprecating? Dry? Observational? None?
2. **The authority move**: How do they establish credibility? (Stories? Numbers? Credentials? Casual mastery?)
3. **The vulnerability line**: How close to personal truth do they go? (Never? Occasionally? Raw?)

### Phase 2: Erosion-Compatible Writing

Write the first draft using the client's voice DNA, then apply erosion:

**Erosion Pass 1: Shore Up the Voice**
- Read from the top. Where does the voice slip from theirs to yours? Correct it.
- Where does their voice feel flat? Shore it up — use *their* metaphor domains, not yours.
- Where is the energy dropping? These are the spots where their voice needs amplification, not replacement.

**Erosion Pass 2: Inject Visual Prose (Carefully)**
- Add sensory details where the client's content is abstract — but use details from *their* world, not yours.
- A tech founder's visual prose involves monitors, coffee shops, and whiteboards
- A fitness coach's visual prose involves gym floors, mirrors, and early-morning cold
- The prose must feel like their observations, not yours

**Erosion Pass 3: Off-Center Subtext**
- Find the direct statements that feel like lectures and displace them into the client's natural storytelling mode
- If the client tells stories: displace through narrative
- If the client uses data: displace through ironic juxtaposition (the data says X, but what it really means is Y)
- If the client uses humor: displace through comedic framing

### Phase 3: The Elevation Layer

This is where the ghostwriter earns the premium. Add craft the client doesn't know they're capable of:

1. **One Visual Prose Opening** per piece that makes the reader feel physically present
2. **One Memorable Residue Line** per piece that sounds like the client at their most quotable
3. **Theme Compass Alignment** — subtle thematic coherence across a series that the client might not consciously notice but their audience will feel
4. **Off-Center Closings** — endings that resonate rather than summarize

### Phase 4: Third Rail Calibration

Before delivery, run the Third Rail test:
- [ ] Would the client recognize this as their voice? (Must be yes)
- [ ] Would the client have written this without help? (Must be no)
- [ ] Does the piece surprise the client with its quality while still feeling like them? (Must be yes)
- [ ] Can you identify 3 specific craft elevations the client doesn't know are there? (Must be yes)

If all four pass, you've found the Third Rail.

### Phase 5: The Invisible Signature

The best ghostwriting has an "invisible signature" — the client's audience senses something has shifted (the content is *better*), but they can't identify what changed. This comes from:
- Consistent thematic coherence (Theme Compass working silently over multiple pieces)
- Sensory openings that create "home invitation" energy (Pattern 12)
- Memorable lines that get screenshotted and shared
- Natural pacing that creates "I couldn't stop reading" momentum

## Output Requirements

**Deliver**:
1. **Client wallet** — voice DNA + personality tells
2. **First draft** — in client's voice
3. **Erosion log** — what changed in each pass and why
4. **Final piece** — with elevation layers integrated
5. **Invisible signature map** — the craft moves present in the piece that the client's audience won't consciously notice

**Quality Standard**: The client should read the piece and think "Wow, I said that well." Not "Wow, someone else wrote this well." If they detect the ghostwriter, the elevation failed.
