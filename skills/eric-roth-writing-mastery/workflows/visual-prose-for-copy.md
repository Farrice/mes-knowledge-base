# Visual Prose for Copy

Apply Eric Roth's visual prose technique — what he calls writing "what a scene feels like and sounds like" — to sales copy. Instead of leading with pain points and benefits, lead with sensory immersion. Place the reader *inside* an experience before asking them to act.

## Execution

### Phase 1: The Sensory Scene Brief

Before writing copy, build the scene. Answer:

1. **Where is the reader right now?** (Not metaphorically — *physically*. At their desk? In bed at 2 AM? On the train?)
2. **What time of day?** (Time of day = emotional control. 6 AM = ambition. 11 PM = doubt. 3 PM = exhaustion.)
3. **What do they hear?** (The hum of fluorescent lights? A Slack notification? Their kid calling from the other room?)
4. **What do they see?** (The cursor blinking on an empty doc? The dashboard with flat numbers? The inbox at 47 unread?)
5. **What do they physically feel?** (Cold coffee. The chair they've sat in too long. The tightness in their jaw.)

### Phase 2: The 5-Layer Prose Opening

Build the copy's opening paragraph using all five sensory layers:

| Layer | What It Does | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Visual** | What the reader sees | "The cursor blinks on a blank Google Doc, 47th time today" |
| **Auditory** | What they hear | "The Slack notifications have gone quiet — even the team has given up for the night" |
| **Tactile** | What they physically feel | "Your coffee went cold two hours ago, but you keep sipping it anyway" |
| **Temporal** | Time of day, season, passage of time | "It's 11:43 PM on a Tuesday" |
| **Atmospheric** | The emotional weather of the environment | "The office feels smaller at night, like the walls are leaning in" |

**The opening paragraph should place the reader so firmly in a moment that they forget they're reading copy.**

### Phase 3: The Invisible Bridge

After the sensory opening, bridge to the product/offer without breaking the spell.

**Wrong**: "Sound familiar? That's because you need [Product]."
**Right**: Continue the scene. Let the solution enter the narrative the way a new character enters a film — naturally, from within the world.

"And then, somewhere between sip 14 and sip 15, you stopped trying to write the thing from scratch and started rearranging the things that already existed."

The product is implied. The transformation is shown, not told.

### Phase 4: Sensory Contrast Architecture

Structure the full copy as a **before/after** — but both halves are written in visual prose:

1. **The Before Scene** (visual prose): Immerse the reader in the problem world
2. **The Bridge** (narrative transition): A natural turning point within the story
3. **The After Scene** (visual prose): Immerse the reader in the transformation world
4. **The Residue Line**: One memorable truth that crystallizes the change
5. **The Invitation** (not a CTA): A natural next step that feels like continuation, not redirection

### Phase 5: CTA as Home Invitation

The final action should feel like Pattern 12 — the reader has "found a home" and is simply walking through the door.

**Instead of**: "Click here to start your free trial"
**Try**: "The blank page doesn't have to stay blank tonight."

The CTA is embedded in the emotional resolution of the story, not bolted onto the end.

## Output Requirements

**Deliver**:
1. **Sensory scene brief** — the 5-answer character study of the reader's current state
2. **5-layer prose opening** — the immersive first paragraph
3. **Full copy** with sensory contrast architecture
4. **Residue line** identified and highlighted
5. **Standard version** — a conventional copy version for A/B testing comparison

**Quality Standard**: A reader should feel physically present in the opening scene. If they can read the first paragraph without any sensory activation, the visual prose isn't working.

## Copy Type Adaptations

| Type | Modification |
|------|-------------|
| **Landing Page Hero** | 5-layer opening as the hero. Keep to 3-4 sentences. Let visuals do the rest. |
| **Email** | Full sensory opening. 2-3 sentences. The email IS the scene. |
| **VSL Script** | The visual prose becomes the video's opening montage direction. Write what the viewer sees and hears. |
| **Ad Copy** | Compress to one sensory image + residue line. "It's 11 PM. The cursor is still blinking. [Product] was made for this moment." |
| **Sales Page** | Full treatment. Each section opens with a sensory shift (Pattern 8 — Change the Weather). |
