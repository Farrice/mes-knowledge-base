# Social Subtext Engine

Deploy Eric Roth's off-center subtext technique for social media. Posts that work on four layers simultaneously: surface story, emotional subtext, displaced insight, and memorable residue.

1. **Genius Context**: `skills/eric-roth-writing-mastery/genius.md` — cross-domain operating principles
2. **Key Patterns**:

## Execution

### Phase 1: Identify the Payload

Before writing, answer:
1. **What truth do I want the reader to feel?** (Not "understand" — *feel*.)
2. **What's the direct version?** (Write this out — you'll never use it.)
3. **What's the off-center version?** (What story, memory, or observation carries the same truth without stating it?)

### Phase 2: Build the 4 Layers

Every social post operates on four layers:

| Layer | What It Is | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Surface Story** | The literal narrative — what happened | "I watched my daughter build a sandcastle" |
| **Emotional Subtext** | The feeling beneath the story | The fragility of creation, the courage to build knowing destruction is coming |
| **Displaced Insight** | The professional truth carried by the story | Great work is always temporary — build it anyway |
| **Memorable Residue** | The one line they'll screenshot | "She knew the tide was coming. She built it anyway." |

### Phase 3: Apply Visual Prose Opening

Start with one sensory sentence. Not a hook — an **invitation home**.

**Instead of**: "Here's what I learned about resilience"
**Write**: "The wet sand was cold between her fingers at 6 PM, the tide 20 minutes away."

The sensory opening makes readers feel they are *there* before they know what the post is about.

### Phase 4: Craft the Post

Structure:
1. **Visual prose opening** (1-2 sentences) — sensory, specific, lived-in
2. **Surface story unfolds** (3-5 sentences) — let the narrative breathe, don't rush to the point
3. **The turn** (1 sentence) — where the professional truth emerges *through* the story, not after it
4. **Memorable residue line** (1 sentence) — the line they'll remember tomorrow
5. *(Optional)* **Soft bridge** — if needed, a single sentence connecting to your professional context. Never an explicit CTA.

### Phase 5: Subtext Integrity Check

Before publishing, verify:
- [ ] Could a reader get the insight without ever reading an explicit statement of it?
- [ ] Does the surface story work as a standalone narrative?
- [ ] Is there exactly ONE memorable residue line?
- [ ] Does the opening pass the "close your eyes" test — can you see the scene?
- [ ] Would this post feel *wrong* with a hard CTA at the end?

## Output Requirements

**Deliver**:
1. The 4-layer breakdown (planning document)
2. The final post with all layers integrated
3. One alternate surface story (different displacement, same truth)

**Quality Standard**: A reader should feel the truth before they can articulate it. If the insight is obvious before the turn, the subtext isn't deep enough.

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Modification |
|----------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Allow the soft bridge to professional context. Posts can be 150-300 words. |
| **X/Twitter** | Compress to surface story + turn + residue line only. 280 chars max for core. |
| **Newsletter** | Full 4-layer treatment with extended visual prose opening. 500+ words. |
| **Instagram** | Lead with the residue line as the hook. Visual prose in caption body. |
