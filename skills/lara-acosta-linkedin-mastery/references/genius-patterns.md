## 17 Genius Patterns Identified

### Pattern 1: Position Before Content
Never create content until positioning is clear. Most people post into a void because they haven't defined what they want to be known for.

**Executable**: Before any content, complete: "I help [WHO] achieve [WHAT] through [HOW] so they can [OUTCOME]"

### Pattern 2: Dual-Persona Content Design
Every piece of content serves TWO audiences:
- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**: The people who pay you
- **IFP (Ideal Friend Profile)**: The people who SHARE you

Most people only write for buyers. Lara writes for amplifiers too.

**Executable**: Ask "Who will share this AND who will buy from this?"

### Pattern 3: The 4-3-2-1 Content Ratio
Weekly content mix:
- 4 Educational posts (value, how-to, frameworks)
- 3 Story posts (personal, client wins, journey)
- 2 Opinion posts (takes, industry hot takes)
- 1 Promotional post (offer, CTA, direct sell)

**Executable**: Build weekly calendar following exact ratio

### Pattern 4: First-Principle Hook Testing
A hook must work even if the reader has no idea who you are. Most people write hooks that only resonate with existing followers.

**Test**: Remove your name and avatar. Does the hook still stop scrolling?

### Pattern 5: The Authority Trigger Stack
Credibility comes from stacking specific proof elements:
- Specific numbers over vague claims
- Named clients/companies over "clients"
- Timeline specificity over "eventually"
- Process transparency over black-box results

### Pattern 6: Social Proof Archaeology
People don't realize what proof they already have. Dig for:
- Testimonials buried in emails/DMs
- Before/after transformations
- Specific results (even small ones)
- Borrowed authority (who do you know?)

### Pattern 7: Content Pillar Excavation
Three pillars should emerge from expertise intersection:
1. What you DO (core skill)
2. What you BELIEVE (philosophy)
3. What you ENABLE (transformation)

### Pattern 8: The SLAY Framework for Stories
Transform any story into authority-building content:
- **S**tory - The raw narrative
- **L**esson - What it taught you
- **A**pplication - How they can apply it
- **Y**our turn - CTA or question to engage

### Pattern 9: Voice Extraction Protocol
Authentic voice comes from patterns in:
- Signature phrases they use repeatedly
- Sentence length and rhythm
- Emotional intensity moments
- Topics they get passionate about
- What makes them laugh

### Pattern 10: Zero-to-Authority Roadmap
90-day journey from unknown to recognized:
- Days 1-30: Foundation (positioning, pillars, first 30 posts)
- Days 31-60: Momentum (consistency, engagement, first lead magnets)
- Days 61-90: Authority (thought leadership, speaking, press)

### Pattern 11: Lead Magnet Virality
A lead magnet should do both:
1. Capture email (obvious)
2. Go viral as content (overlooked)

Design lead magnets as standalone content pieces, not just gated assets.

### Pattern 12: Format Emulation
Viral formats are patterns. When you see one work:
1. Strip the topic away
2. Identify the structure
3. Apply to your domain
4. Test with your hook

### Pattern 13: Algorithm Optimization Layer
LinkedIn rewards:
- Early engagement (first hour is critical)
- Dwell time (longer posts that hold attention)
- Native content (no external links in post)
- Authentic voice (not obvious AI)
- Conversation starters (questions, hot takes)

### Pattern 14: Ghostwriting Voice Deployment
Writing AS someone requires:
1. Voice profile (extracted patterns)
2. Topic approval (their ideas, your execution)
3. Authenticity check (would they say this?)
4. Iteration loop (feedback, refine, improve)

### Pattern 15: Authority Acceleration Tactics
Fast-track credibility when starting from zero:
- Borrow authority (interview experts)
- Stack micro-proof (small wins, consistently shown)
- Niche down aggressively (own a small pond first)
- Consistency signal (daily presence signals commitment)

### Pattern 16: Client Attraction Content
Content that converts readers to buyers:
- Problem-aware posts (name their pain)
- Solution-aware posts (show your methodology)
- Decision-stage posts (handle objections)
- Trust-building posts (proof, testimonials, behind scenes)

### Pattern 17: The Founder's Content Gap
Founders struggle because they:
- Know too much (curse of knowledge)
- Assume audience context
- Skip the "obvious" (which isn't obvious to readers)
- Fear being "salesy" (so never sell)

First 30 posts should address all four gaps.
