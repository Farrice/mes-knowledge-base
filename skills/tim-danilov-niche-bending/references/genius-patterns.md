## 1. Format-Market Decomposition
**What They Do**: Danilov sees every content piece as two independent layers: **Market** (audience vertical) and **Format** (delivery container). He never thinks in "topics" — only in these two variables.

**Executable Behavior**: When analyzing any content space, always separate WHAT the content is about (market) from HOW it's delivered (format). Markets are fixed. Formats are infinite and transferable across markets.

**Deploy When**: Starting any content strategy work, entering a new market, assessing competition, brainstorming content ideas.

**Success Metric**: You can articulate your content as "[Market] + [Format]" and neither element alone describes what you do.

---

## 2. The Empty Square Method (Grid Mapping)
**What They Do**: Map proven formats against all major markets and look for *absences* — cells where a format has gone viral elsewhere but hasn't been tried in a specific market. Absences aren't failures; they're uncontested territory.

**Executable Behavior**: Create a grid. Rows = markets/verticals. Columns = proven viral formats. Fill in what exists. Every empty cell is a blue ocean opportunity with dual validation: the format works (proven elsewhere) and the audience exists (proven market).

**Deploy When**: Content strategy sessions, product ideation, entering new markets, brainstorming content series, client audits.

**Success Metric**: You identify 3+ empty squares you have the expertise to fill.

---

## 3. Viral Format Transplantation
**What They Do**: Instead of inventing new formats (extremely hard), take formats already proven viral in one market and surgically transplant them into different markets. Virality mechanics transfer because they exploit universal human psychology, not market-specific interest.

**Executable Behavior**: Find a viral video/post in ANY market. Extract the format skeleton (title structure, pacing, narrative arc, visual language, hook mechanics). Rebuild that skeleton with your market's content and native expertise.

**Deploy When**: Creating individual content pieces, designing a content calendar, launching a new content series.

**Success Metric**: The transplanted format outperforms your typical content by 3x+ (as demonstrated in every case study).

---

## 4. Language Adoption (Borrowed Fluency)
**What They Do**: Tazoo doesn't just use gaming *thumbnails* — he speaks in full gaming *language* ("current meta," "patch update," "nerfed," "OP African builds"). The format adoption goes beyond surface packaging into native vocabulary.

**Executable Behavior**: When bending a format, study the vocabulary, metaphors, and cultural references of the source market. Your content must be bilingual — fluent in both your expertise domain AND the borrowed format's native tongue.

**Deploy When**: Writing scripts, headlines, hooks, copy, or any content using a borrowed format.

**Success Metric**: Someone from the source market would recognize the language as native; someone from your market would find it surprisingly fresh.

---

## 5. Audience Bridge Architecture
**What They Do**: Niche bending doesn't change your expertise — it changes the *audience gateway*. Tazoo makes people who'd never watch nature documentaries watch nature documentaries because the gateway is gaming.

**Executable Behavior**: Identify your target audience's *entertainment diet* — what formats they already consume for fun. Use those formats as bridge structures to deliver your expertise to people who wouldn't otherwise encounter it.

**Deploy When**: Your content is high-value but struggles with engagement, retention, or discoverability. You want to expand your audience to adjacent markets.

**Success Metric**: Your content acquires viewers/readers who "don't usually watch/read this kind of thing."

---

## 6. The Expertise Constraint
**What They Do**: Danilov's single non-negotiable: never bend a format you can't fill with genuine expertise. A dentist can rank toothpaste in a tier list; a random creator cannot use finance formats without understanding money. Format is the wrapper; expertise is the substance.

**Executable Behavior**: Before bending any format, ask: "Can I deliver world-class content *inside* this container?" If no → find a different format. If yes → proceed. This prevents niche bending from becoming gimmickry.

**Deploy When**: Every niche bending decision. This is the quality gate.

**Success Metric**: Your audience learns something genuinely valuable, not just entertaining.

---

## 7. Outlier-Driven Format Scouting
**What They Do**: The smartest format intelligence comes from *small channels with outlier videos* — videos getting 3x-100x their channel's normal views. Big channels succeed through distribution; small channels with outlier videos succeed through format innovation. The signal is cleaner.

**Executable Behavior**: Systematically scan channels (especially small ones) for videos dramatically outperforming their channel average. The format of that outlier is what you study and potentially transplant.

**Deploy When**: Weekly or bi-weekly content research sessions. Before starting any new content series.

**Success Metric**: You identify 5+ outlier formats per research session that could be transplanted.

---

## 8. Saturation Arbitrage
**What They Do**: Danilov specifically targets *saturated* markets and makes them feel new through format innovation. Saturation means proven demand. Audience boredom with existing formats means hunger for novelty. Both are prerequisites, not problems.

**Executable Behavior**: Instead of avoiding competitive markets, seek them out. The larger and more saturated the market, the more hungry the audience is for format innovation. Use the grid to find where format innovation hasn't happened yet.

**Deploy When**: Market selection, niche validation, overcoming "my niche is too competitive" paralysis.

**Success Metric**: You enter a "competitive" market and immediately differentiate through format rather than topic.
