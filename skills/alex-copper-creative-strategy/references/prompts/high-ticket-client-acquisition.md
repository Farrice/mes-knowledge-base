## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **Ideal Client Crystallization**: Define the perfect buyer with extreme specificity. Vague targeting = vague results. Know exactly who, what situation, what triggers action.

2. **Watering Hole Identification**: Map exactly where ideal clients spend attention—platforms, communities, events, publications. Presence must be strategic, not scattered.

3. **Attraction Asset Development**: Create specific content/proof assets that demonstrate capability to ideal clients. Not general authority—targeted demonstrations.

4. **Warm Outreach Architecture**: Design outreach sequences that feel like value delivery, not pitching. The goal is conversations, not closes.

5. **Conversation-to-Client Framework**: Structure discovery calls and sales conversations that qualify, build trust, and convert—without being salesy.

6. **Referral Engineering**: Design systems that turn every client and conversation into potential referrals. Premium clients know other premium clients.

7. **Pipeline Management**: Create tracking and follow-up systems that ensure no opportunity falls through cracks.

8. **Inbound Acceleration**: Design authority content specifically to attract inbound inquiries from qualified prospects.

## OUTREACH SYSTEM

### Warm Outreach Protocol

**NOT Cold Pitching. Warm Conversation Starting.**

**Step 1: Identify & Engage (Before Any DM)**
- Find ideal-fit DTC founders on LinkedIn/Twitter
- Engage meaningfully with their content for 1-2 weeks
- Like, comment with genuine insight (not "Great post!")
- Become a familiar name in their notifications

**Step 2: Value-First DM (After Engagement Period)**

Template:
```
Hey [Name],

Been following your journey with [Brand] — [specific observation about their business or recent content].

I've been doing deep analysis on DTC creative strategies and just finished a breakdown of [competitor or similar brand's] ad approach. Thought it might be interesting given what you're building.

Want me to send it over?

Either way, keep crushing it.

Fresh
```

**What This Does:**
- Leads with value, not pitch
- Shows you've done homework
- Offers something useful
- Creates easy "yes" response
- Opens conversation naturally

**Step 3: Value Delivery → Conversation**
- Send the resource when they say yes
- Ask one thoughtful question about their creative approach
- Let conversation develop naturally
- If pain points emerge, offer to look at their account

**Step 4: Diagnostic Offer**
```
Sounds like you're dealing with [specific problem they mentioned].

I've been doing creative strategy audits for DTC brands in your range — I could take a quick look at your ad account if that would be useful.

No pitch, just diagnosis. Usually takes me 30 minutes and I can tell you exactly what's working, what's not, and why.

Interested?
```

**Step 5: Diagnostic Call → Engagement**
- Deliver genuine value on diagnostic call
- Show Fresh's expertise through insight density
- If fit exists, introduce the Intensive
- No pressure—let value speak

### Outreach Volume Targets

**Weekly Activity:**
- 20 new founders identified and added to tracking
- 10 engagement comments on ideal client content
- 5 value-first DMs to warmed prospects
- 2-3 diagnostic calls booked

**Expected Conversion (Conservative):**
- 5 DMs → 2-3 responses → 1 diagnostic call

- **3-4 clients/month target requires ~15 diagnostic calls/month**

## CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

### Discovery/Diagnostic Call Structure (45 minutes)

**Phase 1: Rapport & Context (5 min)**
- Thank them for time
- Confirm what they're hoping to get from the call
- Set expectation: "Let me understand your situation, then I'll share what I see"

**Phase 2: Situation Deep-Dive (15 min)**

Questions to ask:
- "Walk me through your creative process right now"
- "What's your hit rate on new ads? How many winners per batch?"
- "What's happened to your CAC over the last 6 months?"
- "Where do you feel most stuck with creative?"
- "What have you tried to fix this?"

Listen for:
- Specific pain points
- Failed attempts to solve
- Urgency indicators
- Budget signals

**Phase 3: Diagnosis Delivery (15 min)**

Based on what they've shared:
- "Here's what I'm seeing..."
- Identify 2-3 specific issues
- Explain the root cause (not just symptoms)
- Share what good looks like

This is where Fresh's expertise shines—specific, insightful diagnosis that makes them think "he sees things I don't."

**Phase 4: Bridge to Offer (10 min)**

If good fit:
```
"Based on what you've described, here's what I'd recommend:

You need [specific thing 1], [specific thing 2], and [specific thing 3].

I have an intensive specifically designed for this situation. It's a 90-day engagement where I [brief description of deliverables and outcome].

Would it be helpful if I walked you through how it works and we could see if it's a fit?"
```

If they say yes, explain the offer. If they want to think about it, send follow-up.
