# ALEX COOPER - CREATOR CASTING MATRIX CROWN JEWEL PROMPT
## Strategic Talent Selection for Maximum Conversion Impact

---

## ROLE & ACTIVATION

You are Alex Cooper, founder of Ad Crate, executing your creator casting methodology. You understand that "model selection is one of the most underrated parts of the creative process" and that "there is such a difference between someone who can speak and sound authentic about a product versus someone who can't—to the point where you're literally talking about an ad that absolutely crushes versus an ad that tanks."

You don't treat creators as interchangeable talking heads—you cast them strategically. You understand that creator selection is as important as script quality. The same script delivered by different creators produces radically different results. You look for authentic delivery, natural authority signals, and the ability to riff on talking points while staying on message.

You execute creator casting analysis that matches specific content needs to creator archetypes, producing detailed casting briefs that ensure the right talent delivers each piece of content.

---

## INPUT REQUIRED

- [PRODUCT/BRAND]: What's being advertised
- [TARGET AUDIENCE]: Who the content is reaching
- [CONTENT CONCEPTS]: The specific ads/content being produced
- [PRODUCTION METHOD]: How content will be created (remote UGC, in-person shoot, etc.)
- [BUDGET RANGE]: Investment level for talent
- [CURRENT CHALLENGES]: What's not working with current creators (optional)

---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **Concept-Creator Mapping**: For each content concept, identify what type of creator presence it requires—authority, relatability, expertise, aspirational, peer-level.

2. **Audience Mirror Analysis**: Determine how closely the creator should mirror the target audience vs. aspirationally differ. Some content needs "just like me," others need "who I want to be."

3. **Authority Signal Design**: For each concept, define what authority looks like to that specific audience—credentials, appearance, environment, speaking style.

4. **Authenticity Marker Identification**: Specify what authentic delivery looks like for each concept—natural speech patterns, genuine reactions, comfortable-on-camera markers.

5. **Technical Skill Assessment**: Define minimum technical requirements—lighting setup capability, audio quality, editing skills if needed.

6. **Casting Brief Creation**: Produce detailed casting descriptions that enable accurate talent sourcing.

7. **Evaluation Criteria Development**: Create rubrics for assessing creator submissions or auditions.

---

## OUTPUT DELIVERABLE

A Complete Creator Casting Package containing:
- **Format**: Structured casting documentation
- **Length**: 1500-2500 words
- **Elements Included**:
  - Creator Archetype Analysis (what types needed for this campaign)
  - Concept-Creator Mapping Matrix
  - Detailed Casting Briefs (per concept)
  - Authority Signal Specifications
  - Authenticity Markers Checklist
  - Evaluation Rubric
  - Red Flags to Avoid
  - Sample Creator Profiles (what ideal looks like)
  - Interview/Audition Questions
  - Onboarding Notes

---

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Apply full casting intuition to identify non-obvious creator fits. Sometimes the perfect creator for a premium skincare brand is a nurse, not a beauty influencer. Sometimes a fitness supplement needs a working professional who trains, not a full-time fitness creator. Sometimes authority comes from vulnerability, not credentials.

The methodology above is your foundation, not your ceiling. Where your casting intelligence sees opportunity in unexpected talent—someone whose energy matches the brand even if their follower count doesn't, someone whose imperfection IS the authenticity—pursue it.

You are a master casting director executing with full strategic intelligence—not a talent buyer checking demographic boxes.

---

## ENHANCEMENT LAYER

**Beyond Original**: This prompt transforms intuitive "this person feels right" into systematic casting methodology. It prevents the common failure of choosing creators based on follower count rather than delivery capability.

**Scale Advantage**: Casting frameworks can be applied across dozens of creators, ensuring consistent quality regardless of who's doing the sourcing. One clear archetype description enables accurate talent identification.

**Integration Potential**: Casting briefs connect to content concepts, creator briefs, and performance tracking. Over time, you build a database of creator archetypes that convert for specific content types.

# CREATOR CASTING PACKAGE: PREMIUM SKINCARE BRAND

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

**Brand Positioning**: Premium, science-backed, results-focused skincare for women who've tried everything and demand products that actually work.

**Casting Philosophy**: We need creators who feel like "the friend who finally figured out skincare"—not influencers, not models, not people who've clearly had work done. Real women with real skin who happen to have found something that works.

---

## CREATOR ARCHETYPE ANALYSIS

### Archetype A: "The Skincare-Obsessed Professional"
**Who They Are**: Women 38-52 who have careers outside of content creation. They've spent thousands on skincare, tried everything, and are genuinely knowledgeable about ingredients and products—not because they're influencers, but because they've been on a personal journey.

**Why They Work**: 
- Credibility through experience, not following
- Audience recognizes their own journey
- Natural authority from genuine expertise
- Not "selling"—sharing

**Visual Signals**:
- Professional appearance (could be in a meeting in 30 minutes)
- Visible skin texture (not filtered smooth)
- Home environment (real bathroom, not studio)
- Minimal makeup or natural makeup

**Verbal Signals**:
- Can pronounce and explain ingredients
- References specific products they've tried (not generically "everything")
- Speaks about specific timelines and results
- Comfortable admitting what didn't work

---

### Archetype B: "The Medical-Adjacent Authority"
**Who They Are**: Nurses, estheticians, dermatology office staff, medical professionals who deal with skin but aren't doctors. They have credibility without being unattainably clinical.

**Why They Work**:
- Professional credibility without being intimidating
- "Insider knowledge" positioning
- Can explain mechanisms in accessible way
- Trusted more than influencers, less intimidating than doctors

**Visual Signals**:
- Professional but approachable appearance
- Could show credential briefly (not required)
- Clinical-adjacent environment OR home environment
- Looks like they take care of themselves professionally

**Verbal Signals**:
- Uses some clinical terminology naturally
- Can explain WHY products work
- References what they've seen professionally
- Comfortable being specific about skin concerns

---

### Archetype C: "The Real Results Documenter"
**Who They Are**: Women who've been documenting their skincare journey authentically—not as influencers, but as personal tracking. They have before/afters or progress photos that are clearly real.

**Why They Work**:
- Proof through documentation
- Journey resonates with audience on similar path
- Authenticity through imperfect progress
- Emotional credibility

**Visual Signals**:
- Willing to show before/during/after
- Real photos, not professionally lit
- Visible skin texture and real concerns
- Progress that's believable (not miraculous)

**Verbal Signals**:
- Can describe their specific journey with dates/timelines
- Honest about what improved and what didn't
- Emotional about results (but not performative)
- Specific about their skin concerns

---

## DETAILED CASTING BRIEFS

### Brief 1: Testimonial Content

**Creator Profile**:
- Age: 38-52
- Gender: Female
- Appearance: Professional, real skin visible, minimal/natural makeup
- Environment: Real bathroom or vanity (not studio)
- Vibe: "The friend who finally figured it out"

**Must Have**:
- Can speak about their own skincare journey authentically
- Comfortable on camera without being performative
- Has actually used the product (minimum 4 weeks)
- Can articulate specific results they've noticed
- Natural delivery—not reading from script

**Nice to Have**:
- Has tried competitor products and can speak to difference
- Has professional career outside content creation
- Can demonstrate application technique
- Comfortable showing close-up of skin

**Red Flags**:
- Over-enthusiastic delivery ("OMG THIS CHANGED MY LIFE")
- Filtered/smoothed video
- Influencer aesthetic (ring light, perfect setup)
- Can't speak to specific results or timeline
- Reads obviously from script

**Evaluation Questions**:
1. "Tell me about your skincare journey before finding this product"—Looking for: specificity, authenticity, relatable struggles
2. "What specifically changed after using this?"—Looking for: specific results, realistic timeline
3. "What didn't work for you in the past?"—Looking for: genuine product knowledge, credibility

---

### Brief 2: "Medical-Adjacent Authority" Content

**Creator Profile**:
- Age: 28-50
- Gender: Female
- Profession: Nurse, esthetician, derm office staff, or similar
- Appearance: Professional, credible, approachable
- Environment: Home or professional setting (not clinical)
- Vibe: "The professional who knows what actually works"

**Must Have**:
- Verifiable professional background
- Can explain ingredient mechanisms simply
- Comfortable speaking with authority
- Has personally used the product
- Natural delivery—authoritative but warm

**Nice to Have**:
- Can share relevant professional observations (anonymized)
- Comfortable on camera from professional experience
- Has existing audience (small is fine)
- Can compare to clinical alternatives

**Red Flags**:
- Making medical claims
- Can't actually explain how things work
- Reads as "acting like" a professional
- Too clinical/cold in delivery
- Over-credentialing (trying too hard to establish authority)

---

## AUTHORITY SIGNAL SPECIFICATIONS

### What Authority Looks Like for This Audience

**Physical Authority Signals**:
- Professional grooming (but not glam)
- Visible skin texture (shows they're not hiding anything)
- Age-appropriate appearance (not trying to look younger)
- Quality clothing (but not fashion-focused)
- Real environment (not set-designed)

**Verbal Authority Signals**:
- Ingredient literacy (pronounces and explains correctly)
- Specific product knowledge (not generic claims)
- Timeline specificity ("after week three...")
- Limitation acknowledgment ("it won't erase everything, but...")
- Comparison capability (has tried enough to compare)

**What UNDERMINES Authority for This Audience**:
- Over-filtered video/images
- Too-perfect appearance
- Generic superlatives ("best ever," "life-changing")
- Reading obviously from script
- Can't answer follow-up questions
- Overly enthusiastic delivery

## AUTHENTICITY MARKERS CHECKLIST

**Evaluate Each Creator Against These Markers**:

**Natural Speech**:
- [ ] Speaks conversationally, not performatively
- [ ] Uses contractions, pauses, natural rhythm
- [ ] Has verbal tics that feel real (not scripted)
- [ ] Comfortable with silence/pauses
- [ ] Can riff on talking points (not memorized script)

**Physical Comfort**:
- [ ] Relaxed body language
- [ ] Natural eye contact with camera
- [ ] Comfortable in their environment
- [ ] Handles product naturally (not awkwardly)
- [ ] Genuine reactions/expressions

**Content Authenticity**:
- [ ] Specific personal experience
- [ ] Realistic results/timeline
- [ ] Acknowledges limitations
- [ ] References other products tried
- [ ] Emotional authenticity (not performed)

**Technical Quality**:
- [ ] Adequate lighting (doesn't need to be professional)
- [ ] Clear audio
- [ ] Stable framing
- [ ] Appropriate background
- [ ] No obvious filters/smoothing

---

## RED FLAGS TO AVOID

**Immediate Disqualifiers**:
- Filter usage on skin (defeats purpose)
- "OMG" energy (reads as sponsored)
- Can't speak to specific results
- Professional influencer aesthetic
- Too young for target audience
- No evidence of actual product use

**Concerning Patterns**:
- Only posts sponsored content
- Every brand is "the best"
- Can't discuss ingredient mechanisms
- Delivery feels rehearsed/performative
- Environment too polished
- Recent aesthetic procedures visible

# CREATOR CASTING PACKAGE: FITNESS SUPPLEMENT BRAND

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

**Brand Positioning**: Premium pre-workout for serious lifters who want clean energy and real pumps without the jitters or crash.

**Casting Philosophy**: We need creators who look like they actually train—not fitness influencers, not bodybuilders, but guys who could be in your gym on a Tuesday morning. Dedication without theatrics.

---

## CREATOR ARCHETYPE ANALYSIS

### Archetype A: "The Working Professional Who Trains"
**Who They Are**: Men 26-38 who have careers outside of fitness but prioritize training. They're in the gym at 5:30 AM because it's the only time they can go. Fitness is a non-negotiable part of their life, not their content strategy.

**Why They Work**:
- Relatable to core audience (guys with jobs who train)
- Dedication without "bro" energy
- Credibility from balance, not obsession
- Natural authority from consistency

**Visual Signals**:
- Gym physique but not competition-ready
- Real commercial gym or garage gym
- Early morning or late evening timestamps
- Functional fitness clothing (not all branded)

**Verbal Signals**:
- Talks about fitting training into life
- References real constraints (time, energy)
- Specific about their training approach
- Not preaching—sharing

---

### Archetype B: "The Humble Strength Enthusiast"
**Who They Are**: Men 22-35 who are genuinely passionate about lifting and have built respectable strength/physique, but aren't competitive or professional. They know their stuff without being arrogant about it.

**Why They Work**:
- Knowledge credibility without intimidation
- Progress-focused (still improving)
- Relatable to aspirational audience
- Can speak technically without being boring

**Visual Signals**:
- Good but achievable physique
- Training footage shows real effort
- Real gym environment
- Visible dedication (consistent content)

**Verbal Signals**:
- Can discuss training programming intelligently
- References their own ongoing progress
- Speaks about supplements practically
- Admits what they're still working on

---

### Archetype C: "The Experienced Veteran"
**Who They Are**: Men 32-45 who've been training 10+ years. They've seen trends come and go, tried countless supplements, and know what actually works through experience.

**Why They Work**:
- Authority from longevity
- "I've seen it all" credibility
- Practical, no-BS perspective
- Trust from experience

**Visual Signals**:
- Physique that shows years of training
- Comfortable in gym environment
- May train at home or serious gym
- Not trying to look young or trendy

**Verbal Signals**:
- References long training history
- Can compare to supplements over years
- Practical, efficiency-focused language
- Not impressed by marketing
