## DELIVERY STYLE BREAKDOWN

### Speech Patterns

**Pacing**: Top performers speak 10-15% slower than average TikTok content. This creates:
- More processing time for product claims
- Higher perceived authority
- Less "salesy" energy

**Verbal Tics That WORK**:
- "Okay so..." (conversational opener)
- "I need to tell you..." (urgency without aggression)
- "This is actually crazy..." (genuine surprise signal)
- "I've been using this for [specific time]..." (credibility)
- Pauses after claims (lets information land)

**Verbal Tics That FAIL**:
- "You NEED this!" (too aggressive)
- "Link in bio!" (salesy, breaks spell)
- "Best product ever!" (hyperbole triggers skepticism)
- Rushing through benefits (feels scripted)

### Authenticity Signals

**Physical Authenticity**:
- Visible skin texture (not smoothed)
- Hair not perfectly styled
- No visible makeup or "natural makeup" look
- Real nails (not fresh manicure)
- Comfortable clothing (not glam)

**Behavioral Authenticity**:
- Looking at product, not camera, while applying
- Genuine reactions (not performative surprise)
- Admitting when products don't work
- Acknowledging price point concerns
- Referencing past skepticism

### Confidence Indicators

**High-Converting Confidence**:
- Direct eye contact during claims
- No qualifying language ("I think," "maybe")
- Specific details (exact days used, specific results)
- Calm certainty (not excited overselling)
- Willingness to show imperfect results ("It's not magic, but...")

## HOOK PATTERN LIBRARY

### Category: Conversational Openers (Most Effective)
- "Okay so I need to tell you about this"
- "I've been gatekeeping this for too long"

- "My [family member/friend] asked what I'm using"

### Category: Problem Acknowledgment
- "If your skin does [specific thing]..."
- "I've struggled with [problem] for [time]"
- "No one talks about [specific issue]"
- "Every product I tried for [problem] failed until..."

### Category: Authority/Discovery
- "As a [credential/background]..."
- "I spent [time] researching this"
- "The ingredient that actually works for [problem]"
- "[Authority figure] recommended this"

### Category: Pattern Interrupt
- Start mid-application (no verbal hook, just action)
- Reaction to mirror (genuine surprise at results)
- Unboxing but not typical unboxing (already opened, showing use)
- Direct close-up of skin texture (uncomfortable = attention)

**Hook Performance Ranking**:
1. Conversational openers (highest completion rate)
2. Problem acknowledgment (highest conversion)
3. Authority/discovery (good for complex products)
4. Pattern interrupt (highest initial hook, variable completion)

## CONVERSION TECHNIQUE ANALYSIS

### Product Introduction Timing
- Average: 8-12 seconds into video
- Top performers: Often 15-20 seconds (more setup)
- Key insight: Rushing to product DECREASES conversion

### Demonstration Techniques

**High-Converting**:
- Apply product while talking (multitasking = natural)
- Show texture on hand before face (de-risks for viewer)
- Multiple angles of application (thorough)
- Real-time results when possible (immediate proof)
- Return to product after application (reinforcement)

**Low-Converting**:
- Holding product and talking AT camera
- Reading benefits from packaging
- Separate B-roll of product (feels edited, less authentic)
- Over-demonstrating (feels infomercial)

### CTA Approaches

**Soft CTA (Higher Conversion)**:
- "I'll link it" (casual, not demanding)
- "It's in my shop if you want it" (permission-based)
- Yellow basket icon with no verbal mention
- End on results, let product speak

**Hard CTA (Lower Conversion)**:
- "Click the link NOW" (aggressive)
- "You NEED to try this" (pressure)
- Countdown timers (feels scammy in beauty)
- Multiple CTA repetitions (desperate energy)

## UGC PRODUCTION GUIDELINES

### Technical Requirements
- **Camera**: iPhone preferred (authenticity), propped not tripod
- **Lighting**: Natural window light or soft overhead; NO harsh ring light
- **Environment**: Real bathroom, vanity, or bedroom; NOT studio
- **Framing**: Chest-up or face-focused; product secondary
- **Audio**: Natural room sound; no added music during talking

### Talent Direction
- **Before filming**: "Pretend you're telling a friend about this"
- **Delivery**: Conversational, NOT presentational
- **Eye line**: Look at product during application, camera during claims
- **Pace**: Slower than feels natural (will feel right on video)
- **Authenticity**: Show real skin, real texture, no smoothing

### Script Structure
- **0-5s**: Conversational hook (no product mention)
- **5-15s**: Problem acknowledgment or context
- **15-40s**: Product introduction + demonstration (simultaneous)
- **40-55s**: Results or expectations (realistic)
- **55-60s**: Soft CTA or product name only

### Avoid
- Studio lighting setups
- Scripted-sounding delivery
- Holding product to camera
- Aggressive CTAs
- Over-claiming results
- Perfect appearance (too polished = less trust)
