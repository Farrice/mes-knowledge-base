## INPUT REQUIRED

- [BRAND/PRODUCT]: What's being advertised

---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **Performance Pattern Analysis**: Map all metrics against benchmarks. Where did this creative over-index? Under-index? What's the signature pattern?

2. **Funnel Stage Diagnosis**: Analyze where in the funnel performance deviated from normal. Strong hook but weak hold = promising angle, wrong body. Strong completion but weak CTR = targeting issue, not creative issue.

3. **Element Attribution**: Isolate which specific elements likely drove performance. Hook style? Problem framing? Proof structure? Talent energy? Visual approach? Identify 3-5 primary drivers.

4. **Hypothesis Formation**: Develop testable hypotheses about WHY these elements worked. What psychological mechanism? What audience insight? What market condition?

5. **Transferable Learning Extraction**: Convert hypotheses into principles that can be applied to future creative. Not "this hook worked" but "hooks that surface hidden costs outperform product benefit hooks for problem-aware audiences."

6. **Counter-Factual Analysis**: What would have happened with different choices? What variations would test the hypotheses? What would have made this even better?

7. **System Encoding**: Translate learnings into specific updates to creative playbooks, brief templates, and review checklists.

8. **Next-Move Recommendations**: Based on learnings, what should be built next? What should be avoided? What hypotheses need testing?

# PERFORMANCE FORENSICS REPORT: FOUNDER STORY VIDEO

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Key Learnings:**

1. **Problem-First Framing Outperforms Solution-First by 68%**: This founder story spent 47 seconds on problem articulation before mentioning the product. The product demo control led with features. Problem resonance drove massive engagement advantage.

2. **Specific Industry Targeting Unlocks Hidden Demand**: "Creative agencies" specificity in opening hook drove 120% CTR improvement. Generic "teams" messaging in control reached more people but converted fewer.

3. **Founder Credibility Transfers to Product Credibility**: "I ran a 30-person agency for 8 years" established authority that made product claims believable without proof stacking.

4. **Earned Insight > Feature Description**: The line "The problem isn't your process—it's that your process lives in 7 different places" generated 340+ comments specifically referencing this insight. No product feature generated equivalent engagement.

5. **Authentic Frustration > Polished Presentation**: B-roll showing founder's actual messy whiteboard and real Slack screenshots outperformed the control's clean product animations.

## FUNNEL STAGE DIAGNOSIS

### Hook Performance (0-3 seconds)
**Metric**: 52% hook rate (137% of account average)
**Driver**: Industry-specific opening—"If you run a creative agency, you already know the 3pm panic."
**Learning**: Hyper-specific audience callout outperforms broad benefit hooks for B2B.

### Early Body (3-15 seconds)
**Metric**: 44% hold rate (152% of account average)
**Driver**: Problem validation without product mention. Founder describing his own agency chaos.
**Learning**: Delaying product introduction increases hold rate when problem resonance is high.

### Mid-Body (15-45 seconds)
**Metric**: 31% retained at 45s (vs. 18% account average)
**Driver**: "Earned insight" moment at 28 seconds—the "7 different places" line.
**Learning**: A single profound insight can anchor attention through long-form content.

### Completion (full 90 seconds)
**Metric**: 18% completion (164% of account average)
**Driver**: Story arc completion desire + late product reveal creating curiosity.
**Learning**: Narrative structure with delayed payoff works for high-intent B2B audiences.

## TRANSFERABLE LEARNING STATEMENTS

**Encode these into creative playbooks:**

1. **For B2B SaaS**: Lead with industry-specific problem articulation, not product category. "Creative agency project management" outperforms "project management for teams."

2. **For Founder Stories**: Delay product introduction until 40%+ of video duration. Use problem dominance (3:1 or higher problem-to-solution ratio).

3. **For Long-Form Content**: Anchor attention at 25-35% mark with an "earned insight"—a reframe that makes the viewer feel understood at a new level.

4. **For Authority Building**: Specificity of credentials > impressiveness of credentials. "30-person agency for 8 years" beats "successful entrepreneur with extensive experience."

5. **For Engagement Optimization**: If targeting high engagement, prioritize "earned insights" over testimonial proof stacking.
