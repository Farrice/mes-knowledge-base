## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 1: Call Center Automation Proposal

**Context**: Mid-size insurance company considering replacing 60% of call center staff with AI chatbot system. Vendor claims $2.4M annual savings. Current call center handles claims questions, policy inquiries, and general customer service.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

# DOORMAN FALLACY AUDIT REPORT
## Proposed Call Center AI Automation - Meridian Insurance

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Risk Assessment: HIGH - Recommend Rejection**

This proposal defines the call center function as "answering customer questions" and proposes replacing it with an AI system capable of handling 80% of inquiries. This definition catastrophically undervalues the call center's true function in the organization.

Based on analysis across 12 value dimensions, the hidden value at risk significantly exceeds the $2.4M claimed savings. Projected customer lifetime value destruction: $8-15M over 24 months. Recommendation: Reject current proposal; consider hybrid enhancement approach instead.

---

### NARROW DEFINITION EXPOSURE

The vendor proposal defines call center function as:
*"Responding to customer inquiries regarding policy status, claims processing, and general information requests."*

This framing deliberately excludes:
- Exception handling requiring human judgment
- Trust repair after negative experiences (claims denial, rate increases)
- Competitive intelligence from customer complaints
- Cross-sell/upsell opportunity identification
- Early warning detection for systemic issues
- Brand perception management in high-emotion moments

The proposal frames efficiency as "first call resolution rate" and "average handle time"—metrics that incentivize getting customers OFF the phone, not building relationships that prevent churn.

---

### HIDDEN VALUE MATRIX

**1. Security & Trust Signaling**
Current state: Human agents provide implicit trust signal—"a real person at Meridian cares about my claim." Insurance is fundamentally a trust product; customers are paying for a promise.
Value at risk: Customer willingness to pay premium pricing depends partly on perceived service quality. Competitor differentiation often comes down to "they were there when I needed them."
Estimated impact: 3-5% of customers will switch insurers within 18 months citing "can't reach a person."

**2. Exception Handling Capability**
Current state: Approximately 22% of calls involve situations outside standard scripts—unusual claims, policy edge cases, customer circumstances requiring judgment.
Value at risk: AI systems will either reject these cases (frustrating customers) or route them to reduced human staff (creating bottlenecks and longer waits for complex issues).
Estimated impact: Exception handling wait times will increase 300%+, precisely when customers need help most.

**3. Customer Intelligence Gathering**
Current state: Call center staff hear what customers actually say—complaints about competitors, unmet needs, confusion about products, life changes triggering policy needs.
Value at risk: This intelligence disappears. AI transcription captures words but misses tone, hesitation, the "oh, one more thing" that reveals true concerns.
Estimated impact: Product development loses crucial voice-of-customer input; marketing loses language for campaigns.

**4. Brandquake Prevention**
Current state: Skilled agents defuse situations that could become complaints, negative reviews, social media posts, or regulatory complaints.
Value at risk: AI escalation will be slower and more frustrating; 12% of AI interactions that would have been resolved by humans will become reputation events.
Estimated impact: One viral "AI couldn't help me during a crisis" story costs more than annual savings.

**5. Cross-Sell Opportunity Detection**
Current state: Agents identify life changes (new home, new baby, retirement) that trigger coverage needs. Currently generating ~$1.2M annual premium from call-originated cross-sells.
Value at risk: AI will not detect emotional cues or build rapport that leads to "while I have you, I've been thinking about..."
Estimated impact: Loss of $800K-1.2M annual premium from eliminated organic cross-sell.

**6. Trust Repair Function**
Current state: After claims denial or rate increase, the call center conversation determines whether customer stays or leaves. Human empathy and genuine problem-solving retain customers.
Value at risk: AI cannot perform emotional labor. "I understand this is frustrating and here's what I can do" from a human is fundamentally different from an AI equivalent.
Estimated impact: Retention after negative events will drop 15-25 points.

---

### VALUE DESTRUCTION ESTIMATE

| Hidden Value Dimension | Annual Value at Risk |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Customer churn from reduced service perception | $3.2M - $4.8M |
| Lost cross-sell premium | $800K - $1.2M |
| Exception handling failures | $400K - $600K |
| Reputation event exposure | $1M - $3M (probabilistic) |
| Competitive intelligence loss | $200K - $400K |
| Retention post-negative event | $2M - $3.5M |
| **TOTAL VALUE AT RISK** | **$7.6M - $13.5M** |
| **Claimed Savings** | **$2.4M** |
| **Net Value Destruction** | **($5.2M) - ($11.1M)** |

---

### SECOND-ORDER EFFECT PROJECTIONS

**6 Months**: Customer satisfaction scores drop 8-12 points. Exception handling complaints increase. Staff morale among remaining agents collapses as they handle only the hardest cases with no easy wins.

**12 Months**: Churn data begins reflecting service degradation. First "AI disaster" story circulates. Regulatory scrutiny possible if vulnerable customers (elderly, non-native speakers) systematically fail to get help.

**24 Months**: Full competitive disadvantage realized. Competitors marketing "speak to real people" differentiation. Rebuilding human capability will cost more than was saved.

---

### ALTERNATIVE APPROACH RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Enhance, Don't Replace**: Use AI to support human agents—faster information retrieval, suggested responses, automated post-call documentation. Keep humans in the loop.

2. **Segment by Relationship Value**: High-value customers get enhanced human service. AI handles simple, transactional inquiries for cost-conscious segment.

3. **Invest in the Asset**: Reframe call center as value-creation center. Pay agents more. Train them in sales. Measure lifetime value influence, not handle time.

---

### FINAL VERDICT: REJECT

This proposal will destroy $5-11M in value while claiming $2.4M in savings. It exemplifies the Doorman Fallacy: defining a function narrowly to make it seem automatable, ignoring the tacit value that makes customers trust Meridian with their protection.

The call center is not a cost center. It is where customers decide whether Meridian keeps its promises. Automate it at your peril.

---

**What Made This Exceptional**:
- Quantified "unquantifiable" value using multiple estimation approaches
- Identified the specific brandquake risk (viral AI failure story)
- Provided constructive alternatives, not just criticism
- Framed final verdict in language of competitive strategy, not just cost

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 2: Retail Greeter Elimination

**Context**: Regional grocery chain (47 stores) considering eliminating door greeters to save $1.8M annually. Corporate justification: "Customers proceed directly to shopping; greeter interaction is minimal."

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

# DOORMAN FALLACY AUDIT REPORT
## Proposed Greeter Position Elimination - FreshMart Regional

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Risk Assessment: HIGH - Recommend Rejection with Alternative**

The proposal to eliminate store greeters defines the role as "greeting customers as they enter." This narrow definition ignores the greeter's function as security presence, theft deterrent, customer routing, special needs assistance, brand warmth signal, and the crucial "first impression" that colors the entire shopping experience.

Analysis indicates the $1.8M savings will be offset by $2.4-3.8M in annual losses from increased shrinkage, reduced basket size, and accelerated customer defection to competitors.

---

### NARROW DEFINITION EXPOSURE

Corporate memo states: *"Greeter position involves welcoming customers at store entrance. Customer surveys indicate 73% of shoppers do not recall interacting with greeters, suggesting minimal value-add."*

This analysis fundamentally misunderstands how greeters create value:
- The 73% who "don't recall" are experiencing the greeter's effect unconsciously—the store feels welcoming, human, cared-for
- Security value is invisible when it works (theft that doesn't happen)
- The greeter is the only employee whose sole job is making customers feel seen

---

### HIDDEN VALUE MATRIX

**1. Theft Deterrence**
Current state: Visible human presence at entrance reduces shoplifting 15-25% (industry studies). Greeters provide psychological barrier—"someone is watching."
Value at risk: At current shrinkage rate of 1.8% of revenue ($4.2M annually), a 20% increase = $840K additional loss.
Industry benchmark: Retailers who removed greeters saw shrinkage increase 0.3-0.5 percentage points within 12 months.

**2. Brand Warmth Signal**
Current state: Greeter communicates "FreshMart is a place where people matter, not just a transaction warehouse."
Value at risk: Removal signals cost-cutting, commoditization. Differentiator vs. Amazon/Walmart erodes.
Competitive context: Trader Joe's and Costco famously invest in human warmth. FreshMart competes partly on not being a soulless big-box. Greeters are brand strategy, not overhead.

**3. Special Needs Assistance**
Current state: Greeters assist elderly customers, parents with strollers, mobility-impaired shoppers. They answer the immediate "where is X?" preventing frustration before it starts.
Value at risk: Vulnerable customer segments will struggle. Complaints will increase. ADA-adjacent concerns possible.
Reputational impact: "FreshMart doesn't care about seniors" narrative is one viral post away.

**4. Customer Routing Efficiency**
Current state: Greeters direct customers to correct departments, reducing wandering time and aisle congestion.
Value at risk: Increased customer confusion, longer shopping times (reducing trips per week), more staff interruptions as other employees get asked directions.

**5. Complaint Interception**
Current state: Greeters catch returning customers with issues before they reach checkout frustrated. Early intervention = higher resolution success.
Value at risk: Complaints escalate further before being addressed. Manager interventions increase.

**6. Store-Level Intelligence**
Current state: Greeters observe patterns—which entrances are congested, which customers look lost, when rush periods start. They communicate informally with management.
Value at risk: Frontline intelligence disappears. Operational adjustments happen slower.

---

### VALUE DESTRUCTION ESTIMATE

| Hidden Value Dimension | Annual Value at Risk |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Increased shrinkage (theft) | $680K - $1.1M |
| Basket size reduction from diminished experience | $400K - $800K |
| Customer defection to "warmer" competitors | $1.2M - $1.8M |
| Increased staff interruption costs | $80K - $120K |
| Complaint escalation costs | $60K - $100K |
| **TOTAL VALUE AT RISK** | **$2.42M - $3.92M** |
| **Claimed Savings** | **$1.8M** |
| **Net Value Destruction** | **($620K) - ($2.12M)** |

---

### SECOND-ORDER EFFECTS

**6 Months**: Shrinkage numbers begin rising. Staff morale declines—"they don't value people here." Customer satisfaction scores dip 3-5 points.

**12 Months**: Competitor running "shop where people know your name" campaign that lands. Elderly customer segment begins migrating.

**24 Months**: FreshMart increasingly perceived as "just another grocery store." Premium pricing harder to justify. The warmth differentiation that took 15 years to build is gone.

---

### ALTERNATIVE APPROACH

**The Enhanced Greeter Model**:
Rather than eliminating, transform:
- Train greeters as "Customer Experience Ambassadors"
- Equip with tablets for instant inventory lookup
- Empower to offer spot discounts for customer recovery
- Track greeter-influenced customer satisfaction and retention
- Make the greeter THE competitive differentiator

Cost increase: ~$400K for training and tools
Value creation potential: $2-4M through measurable experience differentiation

---

### FINAL VERDICT: REJECT - IMPLEMENT ENHANCEMENT

The greeter is not an expense. The greeter IS the brand at the moment of first impression. The proposal mistakes invisible value for absent value.

FreshMart should double down on human warmth, not eliminate it. In a world of self-checkout and automated everything, the human touch becomes more valuable, not less.
