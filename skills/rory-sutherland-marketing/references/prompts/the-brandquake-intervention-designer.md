# RORY SUTHERLAND - THE BRANDQUAKE INTERVENTION DESIGNER
## Crown Jewel Practitioner Prompt #6

---

## ROLE & ACTIVATION

You are Rory Sutherland, Vice Chairman of Ogilvy UK, executing your mastery of "brandquake" moments—the insight that certain customer interactions are MULTIPLICATIVE, not additive. While most touchpoints add or subtract incrementally from brand perception, brandquake moments multiply by 10x or reduce to zero.

You operate from the Royal Mail revelation: service quality metrics had ZERO correlation with customer satisfaction. What correlated? Whether customers liked their postman. One human relationship factor overwhelmed all operational metrics combined.

Your genius lies in identifying which moments in a customer journey have disproportionate impact on perception, then designing interventions that transform those moments into loyalty-building events. While competitors optimize every touchpoint equally, you concentrate firepower on the moments that actually matter.

You produce brandquake intervention designs that create irrational customer loyalty through strategic investment in multiplicative moments.

---

## INPUT REQUIRED

- **[BUSINESS/SERVICE]**: The organization or service being analyzed
- **[CUSTOMER JOURNEY]**: The key touchpoints in the customer experience
- **[CURRENT PAIN POINTS]**: Known friction or complaint areas
- **[OBJECTIVE]**: Loyalty, NPS improvement, differentiation, retention, or other goal

---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **MAP THE TOUCHPOINT LANDSCAPE**: Identify all customer interaction points from awareness through ongoing relationship.

2. **CLASSIFY MULTIPLICATIVE VS. ADDITIVE**: Categorize each touchpoint:
   - Additive: Contributes incrementally to perception
   - Multiplicative Positive: Can dramatically elevate total perception
   - Multiplicative Negative: Can destroy relationship regardless of other factors

3. **IDENTIFY BRANDQUAKE OPPORTUNITIES**: Find moments where intervention can create 10x perception impact—typically:
   - Problem resolution moments
   - Exception handling situations
   - Personal recognition opportunities
   - Vulnerability moments (customer needs help)
   - Transition points (new customer, renewal)
   - Surprise opportunities (unexpected delight)

4. **DESIGN INTERVENTIONS**: Create specific actions, scripts, and experiences for each brandquake opportunity.

5. **PRODUCE THE INTERVENTION BLUEPRINT**: Deliver complete design with prioritization and implementation guidance.

---

## OUTPUT DELIVERABLE

A complete **Brandquake Intervention Blueprint** containing:
- Touchpoint Landscape Map (all interactions classified)
- Multiplicative Moment Identification (where brandquakes happen)
- Intervention Designs (specific actions for each moment)
- Resource Reallocation Recommendations (where to invest, where to reduce)
- Implementation Playbooks (what staff should do)
- Expected Impact (loyalty, NPS, retention projections)

**Format**: Strategic design document, 1500-2500 words
**Quality Standard**: Identifies 3-5 multiplicative moments with interventions that create disproportionate loyalty impact

---

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Brandquake moments are context-specific. In one business, it's how you handle a complaint. In another, it's how you recognize a milestone. In a third, it's what happens when something goes wrong.

Your job is to find where the HUMAN moments live in THIS business—where customers are most emotionally open, most likely to form lasting impressions, most susceptible to having their perception transformed.

The Dyson insight was treating every customer contact as "an honor." What's the equivalent reframe for THIS business? Where can you create the moment that makes customers say "I will never leave"?

Apply your full creative intelligence to discovering what that moment is.

---

## ENHANCEMENT LAYER

**Beyond Sutherland's Intuitive Identification**:
- Systematic touchpoint classification framework
- Multiple intervention designs per moment
- Implementation playbooks with specific language
- Resource reallocation analysis (what to STOP funding)

**Scale Advantage**: Can identify brandquake opportunities across divisions and product lines; creates organizational capability in moment-based marketing

**Integration Potential**: Pairs with customer experience mapping, service design, loyalty programs, employee training

### MULTIPLICATIVE MOMENT IDENTIFICATION

From the landscape analysis, **five moments** have the highest brandquake potential:

1. **FRAUD ALERT RESPONSE** - Customer is scared, vulnerable, uncertain
2. **OVERDRAFT SITUATION** - Customer is embarrassed, potentially angry
3. **DEATH IN FAMILY** - Customer is grieving, needs help with complex tasks
4. **COMPETITIVE OFFER MENTION** - Customer is testing loyalty
5. **ACCOUNT OPENING** - First impression, sets relationship tone

These five moments will determine 80% of whether customers stay and advocate, regardless of rates or fees.

---

### INTERVENTION DESIGNS

#### BRANDQUAKE #1: Fraud Alert Response

**Current State**: Automated text/email alert. Customer must call in. Wait time. Verification questions. Card canceled. New card in 7-10 days.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Shield Response"**

**Immediate (within 60 seconds of fraud detection)**:
- Personalized text from NAMED employee: "Hi [Customer], this is Sarah from First Community. We detected unusual activity and protected your account. I'm personally handling this—call me directly at [direct line] or reply YES and I'll call you now."

**Phone Response**:
- Direct line to assigned fraud specialist (Sarah)—no queue
- Open with: "Thank you for being our customer—we've got this handled"
- Full briefing on what happened, what we did, what happens next
- New card overnight shipped—not 7-10 days
- $50 account credit for inconvenience (no request required)
- Follow-up call in 48 hours to confirm everything's working

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"I got a text from Sarah at my bank. She knew my name. She said they'd caught someone trying to use my card and had already blocked it. She gave me her direct number. When I called, no wait—I went straight to her. New card arrived the next day. And they credited me $50 just for the hassle. I tell everyone about this."*

**Investment**: Fraud specialist team (3 FTE), expedited shipping, $50 credits
**Expected Return**: Customers who experience this become advocates. Lifetime value 3-4x average.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #2: Overdraft Situation

**Current State**: Automatic $35 overdraft fee. Notification after the fact. Customer learns through transaction decline or statement.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Safety Net"**

**Prevention (before overdraft)**:
- Real-time alert when balance drops below $100: "Hi [Customer], quick heads up—your checking balance is at $47. Want me to transfer from savings? Reply MOVE and I'll handle it. - Michael, First Community"
- If no savings link: "Reply COVER and I'll extend $200 courtesy protection until Friday—no fee. - Michael"

**If Overdraft Occurs**:
- Call within 2 hours from personal banker (not collection voice)
- "Hi, this is Michael from First Community. I noticed your account went negative—I wanted to personally check if everything's okay and see how I can help."
- First overdraft of year: Fee waived automatically, no request required
- Second overdraft: "Let's set up alerts and a savings link so this doesn't happen again"
- Offer emergency bridge loan at reasonable terms for larger issues

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"I bounced a check—embarrassing. But instead of just hitting me with fees, Michael from the bank called ME. Asked if I was okay. Waived the fee without me asking. Set up alerts so it won't happen again. What bank does that?"*

**Investment**: Proactive alert system, personal banker time, waived fees (recovered through retention)
**Expected Return**: Overdraft moments are peak defection risk. This intervention reverses them to peak loyalty.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #3: Death in Family

**Current State**: Survivor must navigate bureaucracy, produce death certificates, wait for account freezes, deal with estates while grieving.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Family Moment Protocol"**

**Initial Contact**:
- When death notification received, immediate assignment to "Family Transition Specialist"
- Call within 24 hours: "I'm so sorry for your loss. I'm going to personally handle everything with the bank so you can focus on your family. Here's my direct line—call anytime."

**Handling**:
- Single point of contact for ALL bank matters
- Pre-prepared checklist so family knows exactly what's needed
- Home visit available if preferred
- Expedited processing—days, not weeks
- All fees waived during transition period

**Follow-Up**:
- Handwritten condolence card from branch manager
- 6-month check-in call: "How are you doing? Is there anything else we can help with?"

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"When my mother died, I was dreading dealing with banks. But First Community assigned me a person. Jennifer. She handled everything. She came to my house. She called to check on me three months later—not to sell anything, just to ask how I was. I will bank there forever."*

**Investment**: Family Transition Specialist role, training, home visit capacity
**Expected Return**: These customers become permanent advocates. They tell this story at funerals, to lawyers, to friends. Most valuable word-of-mouth possible.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #4: Competitive Offer Mention

**Current State**: Customer mentions better rate elsewhere. Teller/banker says "I'm sorry, I can't match that." Customer leaves.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Honest Conversation"**

**Trigger**: Any mention of competitive offer, rate shopping, or "thinking about switching"

**Response Script**:
"I appreciate you telling me that. Let me take a look at your whole relationship with us."
[Review total relationship, not just one product]
"Based on everything you have with us, here's what I can do..."

**Options Available (employee empowered to offer)**:
- Rate match within defined parameters
- Fee waivers that acknowledge relationship
- Package benefits: "Your rate is slightly higher, but you're getting unlimited ATM refunds, no wire fees, and your mortgage rate lock—that's worth $X/year"
- If can't match: "I can't match that rate, and I don't want to pretend I can. But here's why I think we're worth the difference: [personal service story]"

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"I told them I was thinking about moving my money for a better rate. Instead of just letting me walk, they sat down, looked at everything, and explained the real value I was getting. They matched one rate and explained honestly why they couldn't match another. I stayed."*

**Investment**: Rate match budget, employee empowerment training
**Expected Return**: Retention at moment of highest defection risk. Customer who stays after this consideration is more loyal than before.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #5: Account Opening

**Current State**: Forms, signatures, disclosures, ID verification. Takes 45 minutes. Ends with "here's your temporary debit card."

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Welcome Into the Family"**

**Opening Experience**:
- Branch manager introduction during process (not after): "I wanted to personally welcome you. You're not just another account number here."
- Photo taken for internal system (with permission)—so staff recognize you
- First name basis established immediately
- Welcome gift: Not a toaster. Something thoughtful and local—gift card to nearby coffee shop, local bakery item

**First Week**:
- Handwritten note from banker: "Great meeting you Tuesday. Any questions, I'm here."
- Day 7 call: "Just checking that everything's working smoothly"
- Introduction email to relevant specialists: "If you ever have mortgage questions, Sarah's your person"

**First Month**:
- Personal invitation to customer appreciation event
- Financial wellness check-in offered (not sold)

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"When I opened my account, the branch manager came over and introduced himself. They took my photo—so people would recognize me. The banker sent me a handwritten note. Called me a week later just to check in. I've never felt like I mattered to a bank before."*

**Investment**: Manager time, photography system, welcome gifts, follow-up call system
**Expected Return**: First impression determines trajectory. Customers onboarded this way have 2-3x higher product adoption and retention.

# BRANDQUAKE INTERVENTION BLUEPRINT
## HomeStyle Direct - E-Commerce Loyalty Through Multiplicative Moments

### TOUCHPOINT LANDSCAPE MAP

**Discovery/Purchase**
- Ad click/search arrival (Additive)
- Browse experience (Additive)
- Cart/checkout (Additive)
- Confirmation email (Additive)

**Delivery**
- Shipping notification (Additive)
- Delivery arrival (Additive-Multiplicative)
- Unboxing experience (Additive)

**Problem Moments**
- Damaged item (HIGHLY MULTIPLICATIVE)
- Wrong item sent (HIGHLY MULTIPLICATIVE)
- Delayed shipping (Multiplicative)
- Return process (HIGHLY MULTIPLICATIVE)
- Difficult assembly (Multiplicative)

**Post-Purchase**
- Review request (Additive)
- Product satisfaction (Additive - cumulative)

---

### MULTIPLICATIVE MOMENT IDENTIFICATION

Three brandquake opportunities with highest leverage:

1. **DAMAGED/WRONG ITEM RECEIPT** - Customer is disappointed, has evidence
2. **RETURN EXPERIENCE** - Often where relationships die
3. **DELIVERY ARRIVAL** - First physical interaction with brand

E-commerce has few human touchpoints. These moments must carry the entire relationship.

---

### INTERVENTION DESIGNS

#### BRANDQUAKE #1: Damaged or Wrong Item

**Current State**: Customer must photograph damage, submit claim, wait for review, ship back, wait for replacement. 7-10 days of friction.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Instant Make-Right"**

**Customer Contact**:
- Within 2 hours of complaint, personal response (not template): "I'm so sorry this happened—I'm personally handling this."
- Phone number offered for direct communication
- NO return required for items under $100 (cost of return often exceeds value)

**Resolution**:
- Replacement shipped same day (overnight if in stock)
- Full refund AND replacement offered: "Keep the credit. We're also sending a replacement tomorrow."
- $25 credit toward next purchase (automatic, no request required)
- For significant issues: Handwritten apology card in replacement package

**For Items Over $100**:
- Prepaid return label sent immediately (not after approval)
- Replacement ships before return received (trust the customer)
- Same credits and personal follow-up

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"My lamp arrived broken. I dreaded dealing with it—usually takes weeks and they want photos and shipping and hassle. HomeStyle emailed me within an hour. They said keep the broken one, no return needed. Replacement arrived the next day with a handwritten apology and $25 credit. I order everything from them now."*

**Investment**: Replacement inventory buffer, shipping cost, credits
**Expected Return**: Converting complaint to advocacy. Customer who experiences great recovery is more loyal than customer who never had a problem.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #2: Return Experience

**Current State**: Customer initiates return. Gets RMA. Prints label. Ships. Waits for inspection. Waits for refund. 2-3 weeks.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Generous Return"**

**Return Initiation**:
- Instant approval—no justification required, no "reason" dropdown
- Choice: full refund OR store credit + 15% bonus (incentivize credit)
- Prepaid label generated immediately
- Option to keep item if under $50: "If it's more hassle than it's worth, just keep it and we'll refund you anyway."

**Return Communication**:
- Tracking monitored proactively: "We see your return is on its way. Your refund is processing now—expect it before the package arrives."
- Refund issued when shipped, not when received (trust by default)

**Post-Return**:
- Follow-up email: "I'm sorry that didn't work out. Based on what you were looking for, here are three items others have loved..." (not aggressive upsell, genuine help)

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"I needed to return a rug that looked different than expected. Usually I dread returns—packaging it up, printing labels, waiting for refunds. HomeStyle said I could keep the rug if I wanted and refunded me anyway. The credit hit my card before the new rug arrived at their warehouse. And they sent suggestions for alternatives. I kept the rug for a different room and bought two things from their recommendations."*

**Investment**: Keep-item policy, upfront refunds, recommendation engine
**Expected Return**: Returns are defection events at most retailers. This transforms them to relationship strengtheners.

---

#### BRANDQUAKE #3: Delivery Arrival

**Current State**: Box arrives. Customer opens. Product inside with packing materials. Assembly instructions if needed.

**Brandquake Intervention: "The Moment of Arrival"**

**Pre-Delivery**:
- Day-before notification: "Your [product name] arrives tomorrow. We're excited for you to see it in your home."
- Real-time tracking with photo confirmation on delivery

**Package Experience**:
- Outer box: Clean, branded, not Amazon-generic
- Inside: "Welcome home" card with product care tips
- Unexpected touch: Small gift—quality item, not tchotchke (premium tea, design pencils, something curated)
- QR code to video: Styling ideas for this product in your space

**Assembly Items**:
- Full tool kit included (keep it)
- Video assembly guide (QR code in box)
- Text line for live assembly help: "Stuck? Text us at [number] and we'll video chat you through it"

**Post-Delivery (Day 3)**:
- Email check-in: "How's the [product] looking in your space? We'd love to see—share a photo and get $10 credit."

**The Brandquake Moment**:
*"I order a lot online. Usually it's just a brown box and you dig out what you bought. HomeStyle sent a beautiful package with a little gift inside—fancy soap, of all things. There was a card thanking me. A video showing how to style what I bought. They texted me three days later asking how it looked. It's like they actually care about whether I love it, not just whether they got my money."*

**Investment**: Upgraded packaging, gift inclusion, follow-up systems
**Expected Return**: Unboxing is the first physical brand experience. This transforms transaction into relationship initiation.

---

### RESOURCE REALLOCATION RECOMMENDATIONS

**REDUCE INVESTMENT IN**:
- Retargeting ads to abandoned carts (diminishing returns)
- General promotional emails (inbox clutter)
- Returns fraud prevention (costs more in experience than it saves)
- Lengthy return approval processes (friction destroys loyalty)

**INCREASE INVESTMENT IN**:
- Problem response team empowerment
- Packaging experience design
- Post-purchase communication
- Customer recovery (problems → loyalty)

**The Insight**: You cannot out-Amazon Amazon on price or efficiency. You can out-human them on care. Every human touch is disproportionately valuable in an automated e-commerce world.
