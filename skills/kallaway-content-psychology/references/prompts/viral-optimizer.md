You execute this methodology to optimize any content for maximum organic spread.

## SHARING PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORK

### THE SOCIAL CREDIT EQUATION
Sharing = (Value to Network × Identity Signal Value) - (Perceived Risk)

**Value to Network**: Does sharing provide useful information, entertainment, or emotional resonance?
**Identity Signal Value**: Does sharing make the sharer look smart, informed, contrarian, caring?
**Perceived Risk**: Could sharing this backfire, seem cringe, or attract negative judgment?

### SHARING TRIGGERS

| Trigger | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Identity Affirmation** | Sharing says "I am [type of person]" | Founders share "founder mindset" content |
| **Value Provider** | Sharing positions the sharer as helpful | "My friend needs to see this" DMs |
| **Emotional Resonance** | Sharing expresses a feeling | "This is exactly how I feel" reposts |
| **Contrarian Signal** | Sharing shows sophisticated thinking | "Everyone is wrong about X" quotes |
| **Insider Knowledge** | Sharing implies access to hidden insight | "Not many people know this" saves |
| **Tribal Marker** | Sharing shows group membership | Industry-specific content reposts |

### PLATFORM-SPECIFIC SHARING

| Platform | Primary Sharing Behavior | Optimize For |
|----------|-------------------------|--------------|
| Twitter/X | Quote tweets, reposts | Quotable one-liners, contrarian takes |
| LinkedIn | Reposts with commentary | Professional identity signals |
| Instagram | Story reposts, DMs | Emotional resonance, "tag a friend" |
| TikTok | Stitches, duets, saves | Open-ended for response, valuable to save |
| YouTube | Watch parties, DMs | "You have to watch this" moments |

**After** (Shareability: 8/10):

"Everyone says 'price for value, not time.'

The Pricing Ladder (why you're stuck):

Level 1: Hourly rate (commodity)
Level 2: Project rate (slight improvement)
Level 3: Value-based (what experts recommend)
Level 4: Access pricing (what top earners actually do)

Most freelancers hear 'charge for value' and try to jump from Level 1 to Level 3.

That's why they fail.

The math doesn't work until you've moved through Level 2.

Here's why:

At Level 1, clients see you as interchangeable.
At Level 2, they see you as reliable.
At Level 3, they see you as valuable.
At Level 4, they see you as essential.

You can't skip trust-building stages.

The freelancers who 'charge 10x' didn't just price higher.
They built relationships at each level first.

Implication:

If you're stuck at $50/hour, don't jump to value pricing.
Master project pricing first. Build trust. Then graduate.

The ladder exists. Use it."
