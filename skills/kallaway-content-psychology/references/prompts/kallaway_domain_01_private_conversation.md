## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Intelligence Gathering

1. **IDENTIFY CONVERSATION ENVIRONMENTS**: Locate where your audience talks openly:
   - Reddit threads (subreddit-specific language patterns)
   - Amazon reviews (problem articulation in purchase context)
   - Facebook groups (community language and shared frustrations)
   - Twitter/X threads (real-time emotional expression)
   - YouTube comments (response to content in your space)
   - Forum discussions (deep problem exploration)
   - Customer service transcripts (complaints = gold)
   - Sales call recordings (objections = language patterns)

2. **EXTRACT VERBATIM LANGUAGE**: Capture exact phrases, not paraphrases:
   - How they describe the problem (their words, not yours)
   - What they've tried before (and how they talk about failure)
   - What they wish existed (aspiration language)
   - What they're afraid of (fear language)
   - What they're embarrassed about (shame language)
   - What they blame (external attribution patterns)
   - What they secretly believe is the real problem (private diagnosis)

3. **IDENTIFY RECURRING PATTERNS**: Look for language that appears multiple times:
   - Phrases that show up across different sources = universal language
   - Emotional intensity markers (ALL CAPS, exclamation, profanity)
   - Questions they keep asking = open loops in their mind
   - Complaints that repeat = ongoing private frustration

### Phase 2: Language Mapping

4. **CREATE PRIVATE CONVERSATION MAP**:

   ```
   PROBLEM ARTICULATION
   └── How they describe the surface problem: "[exact phrase]"
   └── How they describe the deeper problem: "[exact phrase]"
   └── How they describe the emotion: "[exact phrase]"

   FAILED SOLUTIONS
   └── What they've tried: "[exact phrase]"
   └── Why they think it failed: "[exact phrase]"
   └── How they feel about the failure: "[exact phrase]"

   HIDDEN BELIEFS
   └── What they secretly think is true: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What they're embarrassed to admit: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What they blame: "[exact phrase]"

   ASPIRATIONS
   └── What they wish they could do: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What they imagine success looks like: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What they would feel if solved: "[exact phrase]"

   OBJECTIONS
   └── Why they think they can't: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What they're afraid of: "[exact phrase]"
   └── What's stopped them before: "[exact phrase]"
   ```

5. **RANK BY EMOTIONAL INTENSITY**: Score each phrase:
   - Frequency (how often it appears)
   - Intensity (emotional charge in original context)
   - Specificity (how precisely it captures the feeling)

### Phase 3: Deployment Engineering

6. **CREATE INFILTRATION ASSETS**: Transform language into deployment-ready copy:

   **HOOKS**: Use their exact problem articulation as opening lines
   - "I just want [their aspiration phrase] without [their frustration phrase]"
   - "[Their failed solution] didn't work because [their private belief]"

   **BODY COPY**: Echo their internal dialogue, then redirect
   - "You've probably tried [their tried solutions]. And you probably felt [their failure emotion] when it didn't work."

   **PROOF INTEGRATION**: Address objections using their language
   - "I know you're thinking [their exact objection]. Here's why this is different..."

   **CALLS TO ACTION**: Use their aspiration language
   - "Finally [their wish phrase] without [their fear phrase]"

7. **VALIDATE INFILTRATION**: Test copy against original source material:
   - Would someone in that audience feel "seen"?
   - Does it sound like their friend wrote it?
   - Is the language theirs or yours dressed up?
