## THE SIX PROBLEMS FRAMEWORK

### PROBLEM 1: CHANNEL TYPE PROBLEM
**Symptom**: High views, zero sales. Entertainment-style content.
**Mechanism**: Entertainment channels train audiences to expect entertainment, not transformation. When you try to sell, they bounce.
**Fix**: Pivot to education-focused content or create separate channel for buyer-attraction.

### PROBLEM 2: PRECISION PROBLEM
**Symptom**: Views but wrong audience. Content all over the place.
**Mechanism**: Without laser focus on one buyer profile, you attract fragments of many audiences instead of concentration of one. Pavlovian trust never builds.
**Fix**: Define buyer precisely. Cut topics that attract other profiles, even if they perform well.

### PROBLEM 3: PAIN POINT IDENTIFICATION PROBLEM
**Symptom**: Content seems right but doesn't resonate. Topics too broad or high-level.
**Mechanism**: You're talking about the general AREA of buyer interest, not their specific PAINS. Curiosity attracts, but pain converts.
**Fix**: Map buyer workflow. Identify specific friction points. Create content around pain, not topic.

### PROBLEM 4: SOLUTION NOVELTY PROBLEM
**Symptom**: Content is helpful but forgettable. Generic advice, same as everyone.
**Mechanism**: When your solution matches what they've heard before, no trust gap is created. No trust gap = no trust transfer.
**Fix**: Develop genuinely differentiated solutions. Be first, better, or different. Quantify the gap.

### PROBLEM 5: WALK-AWAY VALUE PROBLEM
**Symptom**: Content is novel but not actionable. All insight, no implementation.
**Mechanism**: If viewers can't apply what you teach without your paid help, you create resentment, not trust. Paradoxically, giving away implementation creates purchase desire.
**Fix**: Every piece must include specific steps they can take immediately. Make free content genuinely useful standalone.

### PROBLEM 6: CONSISTENCY PROBLEM
**Symptom**: Content is good but infrequent. Sporadic posting, no momentum.
**Mechanism**: The 7-11-4 rule requires sustained exposure before trust converts to purchase. Inconsistency resets the clock constantly.
**Fix**: Commit to minimum posting frequency. Create content batching system. Accept that results lag effort by months.

# SIX-PROBLEM DIAGNOSTIC REPORT
## Business Strategy Channel — Course Monetization Failure

### EXECUTIVE DIAGNOSIS

**Primary Problem**: PRECISION PROBLEM (Score: 3/10)
Your content attracts anyone interested in business/productivity—a massive, fragmented audience where your specific buyer (people wanting to START an online business) is a tiny minority.

**Secondary Problem**: PAIN POINT IDENTIFICATION PROBLEM (Score: 4/10)
Even when topics align with entrepreneurship, they're about general interest (Elon's style, cool AI tools) rather than specific pains of your buyer (don't know how to start, overwhelmed by options, fear of failure).

**Root Cause**: You've built a channel for "business curious" people, but you're selling to "business starters." These are different populations with different needs.

### PROBLEM DEEP-DIVES

**PRIMARY: PRECISION PROBLEM**

Your content strategy has a fatal flaw: you're making videos for "people interested in business" when you're selling to "people wanting to start an online business."

Let's look at your sample content:
- "Why Elon Musk's Management Style Actually Works" → Appeals to: entrepreneurs, managers, Elon fans, business curious, tech enthusiasts
- "5 AI Tools That Will 10x Your Productivity" → Appeals to: knowledge workers, AI curious, productivity nerds, students, freelancers
- "The Book That Changed How I Think About Business" → Appeals to: book readers, self-improvement seekers, business curious

**Who's NOT in these audiences?** People actively trying to start an online business right now. They're watching "How to start an online business in 2024" not "What Elon thinks about management."

**The Math**: 5% of your 30K viewers might be considering starting a business. That's 1,500 potential buyers per video. With 3% click rate and 2% conversion = 0.9 sales per video. You're getting <10/month from 8 videos—that tracks.

**The Mechanism**: You've built a general business audience where your specific buyer is a tiny minority. You're selling sunscreen at a ski lodge.

---

**SECONDARY: PAIN POINT IDENTIFICATION**

Even your more relevant content doesn't address specific PAINS of someone starting an online business. It addresses INTERESTS.

Your buyer's actual pains:
- "I don't know what type of online business to start"
- "I'm afraid I'll pick the wrong niche"
- "I don't have time with my job"
- "I've tried before and failed"
- "How do I get my first customer?"

Your content doesn't address ANY of these. You're swimming near the right ocean but not diving into the specific waters where your buyers are drowning.

### ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

**Why Did This Happen?**

1. **Chased Views Over Buyers**: Elon/AI topics get views. You optimized for the wrong metric.
2. **Didn't Define Buyer First**: You started making content, then created a course. Reverse the order.
3. **Confused Interest With Intent**: "Interested in business" ≠ "Actively trying to start a business"
4. **Algorithm Addiction**: Broad topics perform well algorithmically, creating a feedback loop.

### PRESCRIPTION PACKAGE

**PRIORITY 1: Buyer Profile Lock-In**

Stop everything. Define your buyer in one sentence:
"[Who] trying to [accomplish what] struggling with [specific friction]"

Example: "Full-time employees trying to start their first profitable online business struggling with choosing a business model and finding time to build."

Every future content idea must serve THIS buyer.

**PRIORITY 2: Content Pivot (Gradual)**

Don't delete your channel. But shift your content mix:
- Week 1-4: 50% buyer-focused, 50% broad
- Week 5-8: 75% buyer-focused, 25% broad
- Week 9+: 90% buyer-focused, 10% broad (for algorithm diversity)

**PRIORITY 3: Pain Point Content Bank**

Map your buyer's workflow and create 20+ topic ideas around specific pains:
- Choosing a business model
- Finding a niche
- Building while employed
- Getting first customers
- Overcoming fear of failure

**PRIORITY 4: Reframe Existing Expertise**

You CAN still talk about AI tools, books, etc.—but frame them for your buyer:
- "5 AI Tools for Building Your Online Business" (not productivity)
- "The Book That Helped Me Start My Business While Working Full-Time"

---

### 30-DAY RECOVERY PLAN

**Week 1: Foundation**
- Day 1-2: Complete Buyer Profile Engineering exercise
- Day 3-5: Map buyer workflow, identify top 10 pain points
- Day 6-7: Audit last 20 videos—score each for buyer-alignment

**Week 2: Pivot Begins**
- Create 2 videos directly addressing buyer pain points
- Reframe 1 "broad" topic idea into buyer-focused angle
- Update channel description for buyer focus

**Week 3: New System**
- 2 more buyer-focused videos published
- Create lead magnet specifically for buyer journey
- Start email sequence that bridges content to course

**Week 4: Assessment**
- Compare click-through rates on new vs. old content
- Track lead magnet conversions
- Survey new subscribers: "What's your biggest challenge with starting an online business?"
- Plan Month 2 content calendar—100% buyer-focused

---

### SUCCESS METRICS

**Leading Indicators (Weeks 1-4)**:
- Comment quality shifts—more "I'm trying to start..." comments
- Lead magnet conversion rate >5%
- Course page click rate increases from 3% to 5%+

**Lagging Indicators (Months 2-4)**:
- Course sales increase 3-5x
- Views may initially dip, then stabilize
- Email list quality improves
- Customer testimonials reference specific videos

**Warning Sign**:
If no improvement after 60 days of buyer-focused content, issue may be offer-market fit, not content strategy. Diagnose the offer itself.
