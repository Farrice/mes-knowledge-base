## Core Frameworks

### 1. The 6-Level Dopamine Ladder
| Level | Name | Mechanism |
|-------|------|-----------|
| 1 | **Stimulation** | Bottom-up visual processing (color, motion, novelty) |
| 2 | **Captivation** | Curiosity gap creation (open questions) |
| 3 | **Anticipation** | Delayed satisfaction (tension ratcheting) |
| 4 | **Validation** | Reward completion (satisfying payoffs) |
| 5 | **Affection** | Relationship building (personality, warmth) |
| 6 | **Revelation** | Pavlovian conditioning (automatic trust) |

### 2. The 7 Lego Bricks
Content is composed of extractable, remixable elements:
1. **Topic** — What the content is about
2. **Angle** — The specific perspective
3. **Hook** — Attention-capturing opening
4. **Story Structure** — Narrative arc and pacing
5. **Visual Format** — The visual template
6. **Key Visuals** — Memorable visual moments
7. **Audio** — Music, sound, voice

**The 88/12 Rule**: Hold 88% of winning bricks constant, remix 12% with your unique angle.

### 3. The 2x2 Strategy Matrix
| | Low Education Need | High Education Need |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual Trust** | Entertainment Personal Brand | Educational Personal Brand |
| **Brand Trust** | Entertainment Brand | Educational Brand |

### 4. The Pain-Solution-Trust Triangle
Every buyer-attracting content must hit: **Pain** (validate struggle) + **Solution** (non-obvious answers) + **Trust** (credibility)

### 5. The 7-11-4 Rule
Buyers need **7 hours** of exposure, across **11 touchpoints**, in **4 different formats** before purchase.
