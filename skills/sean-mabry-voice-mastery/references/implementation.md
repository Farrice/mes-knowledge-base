# Implementation Guide — Sean Mabry Voice Mastery

## Voice Mastery Progression

### Phase 1 — Immersion (Week 1-2)

1. **Content Saturation**: Consume everything the client has produced — articles, emails, podcast appearances, social posts, sales copy, keynotes.
2. **Podcast Loop Method**: Listen to guest podcast appearances on repeat. Keep the voice "in your ear." This is where you pick up cadence, filler patterns, and natural storytelling rhythm.
3. **Handwriting Calibration**: Handwrite the client's best previous work. Not for copy structure — for physical voice internalization. You'll start predicting the next phrase.
4. **Controversy Line Map (v1)**: Build initial three-zone map from content review:
   - **Flag-plant**: Strong, repeated stances they're known for
   - **Nuanced**: Topics they engage carefully with caveats
   - **No-go**: Topics conspicuously absent from their content

### Phase 2 — Prediction (Week 2-4)

1. **Topic Predictions**: Generate 5-10 predictions weekly: "What would [client] say about [trending topic / new industry development]?"
2. **Prediction Review Calls**: Present predictions in bi-weekly calls. Frame as: "Here's what I think you'd say about X. How close am I?"
3. **Correction Mining**: Accept every correction as gold. Each correction refines the mental model faster than more content consumption.
4. **Hidden Gems Bank**: Build a separate story bank from stories the client mentions casually but doesn't flag as important.

### Phase 3 — Calibration (Month 2-3)

1. **Trust Ladder — Stage 1**: Write → client reviews → someone else publishes.
2. **Edit Density Tracking**: Count edits per piece. Track the downward trend.
3. **The "No Edits" Milestone**: First piece returned with zero edits = turning point.
4. **Trust Ladder — Stage 2**: Write → client reviews → you publish.

### Phase 4 — Mastery (Month 3+)

1. **Speed Benchmarking**: Time yourself writing. Reduce by 10-minute increments (never try to jump from 60 min to 15 min in one leap).
2. **Story Remixing**: Apply known stories to new offers, new contexts, new audiences.
3. **Micro-Voice Details**: Punctuation, sign-off style, emoji patterns, sentence length preferences.
4. **Trust Ladder — Stage 3**: Write → publish without review. Client offers this — you don't ask.

## Memoir Book Architecture

### Step 1 — Strategic Positioning (Authorship Pyramid)

Map the client's industry into the pyramid:

```
         [0.01% — Move the industry]
        /                            \
       [0.1% — Respond to INFLUENCE]
      /                                \
     [Top tier — Network + masterminds]
    /                                    \
   [Mid-tier — Climbing, reading phase]
  /                                        \
 [Base — Dreamers, aspirational, evangelists]
```

- **First book** → Target the base. Memoir showing the hero's journey.
- **Second book** → Tactical. Now you have the base; the top opens doors.
- The base becomes evangelists. The top responds to your influence over the base.

### Step 2 — Memoir vs. Biography Distinction

| | Biography/Autobiography | Memoir |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | Entire life story | Specific topic / period |
| **Objectivity** | Expected to be comprehensive | Subjective, thesis-driven |
| **Purpose** | Historical record | Brand / business strategy |
| **First book?** | Almost never | Yes — focused, controllable |

**Key conversation**: When clients say "I want to tell my whole life story," redirect: "For what you want to accomplish, we need 3-5 chapters of your life that support the goal. That's a memoir, and it's more powerful."

### Step 3 — Book Proposal

Create an outline + light market research blueprint. Essential rules:
- The proposal is a **starting point**, not a contract
- Stay flexible — expect 30% restructuring as real interviews reveal deeper material
- Watch for the "entity channeling" signal — when the outline starts feeling like a conversation with the spirit of the book

### Step 4 — Interview-Driven Drafting

- Conduct structured interviews per book section
- The first interview covers listed topics
- The **second and third interviews** always produce the real material — new ideas, deeper stories
- When interviews reveal better material, restructure the outline immediately

### Step 5 — Hero's Journey Architecture

Use Dan Harmon's Story Circle (simplified Joseph Campbell):

1. **Comfort zone** — A character is in a zone of comfort
2. **Need** — But they want something
3. **Go** — They enter an unfamiliar situation
4. **Search** — Adapt to it
5. **Find** — Get what they wanted
6. **Take** — Pay a heavy price for it
7. **Return** — Go back to their familiar situation
8. **Change** — Having changed

**Critical insight**: The **dilemma** at the midpoint (Step 6 — "I can't but I must") reveals true character. A dilemma is choosing between two things you genuinely value. Whichever you sacrifice reveals who you actually are. This is what makes a book memorable.

## Deep Work / Workhorse Mode-Split

### Day Types

| Day Type | Purpose | Clock Relationship | Goal |
|----------|---------|-------------------|------|
| **Free Day** | Recharge | No clock | Be human. Disconnect fully. |
| **Buffer Day** | Admin + maintenance | Timed (Pomodoro) | Speed. Reduce time per task. |
| **Deep Work Day** | Transformative projects | Clock-forgotten | Immersion. If watching clock, it's a signal. |

### Speed Drilling (Buffer Days)

1. Benchmark current time per task
2. Reduce by 10-minute increments
3. Never try to jump from 60 min to 15 min
4. A few months of incremental drilling achieves mastery speed

### Deep Work Signals

- ✅ You can disappear into the task → Alignment confirmed
- ❌ You're watching the clock → Misalignment signal. Ask: "Am I playing too small? Too big? Wrong project?"
- ✅ Raw word count matches workhorse mode but directed at transformative work
- ❌ Constantly switching between workhorse and deep work → Neither works. Separate them.

### Schedule Template

```
Monday    — Deep Work
Tuesday   — Buffer (meetings, admin, calls)
Wednesday — Deep Work
Thursday  — Buffer (meetings, admin)
Friday    — Deep Work
Saturday  — Free
Sunday    — Free
```

Adjust to your reality, but protect deep work days "like your life depends on it."
