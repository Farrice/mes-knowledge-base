## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Career Path Assessment

**Input Required**: The writer's target medium (ghostwriting, copywriting, content writing, newsletter, brand strategy, channel strategy, or screenwriting) + their current positioning + their existing skills/experience.

**Execute**:

Map the writer against Joanna's 7-career taxonomy:

| Career Path | Revenue Model | Ceiling Breaker | Client Relationship |
|---|---|---|---|
| Channel Strategist | Retainer + performance bonus | Revenue share above milestone | Long-term optimization partner |
| Messaging Strategist | Intensive workshop + retainer | Value of consistent messaging | Strategic advisor |
| Brand Strategist | Retainer + brand system | Irreplaceable voice ownership | Creative partner |
| Copywriter | Project → ongoing → performance | Measurable conversion lift | Revenue architect |
| Content Writer | In-house or retainer | Pipeline value attribution | Invisible sales engineer |
| Ghost Writer | Project + adjacent services | Entire problem ownership | Irreplaceable partner |
| Newsletter Writer | Retainer + list growth + sponsors | Platform mastery bundle | Growth partner |

Identify which path(s) align with the writer's skills, market, and income goals. If multiple paths intersect (they often do), map the overlap.

### Step 2 — Consultant Frame Construction

**Execute**:

Transform the writer's self-concept from "service provider" to "consultant who solves business problems."

1. **Problem Definition**: What specific, measurable business problem does this writer solve? Not "I write blog posts" but "I generate qualified leads through educational content that moves readers from problem-aware to buyer-ready."

2. **Outcome Language**: Translate all service descriptions from effort-based ("12 posts/month") to outcome-based ("content system that generates 30+ inbound leads/month").

3. **Identity Vocabulary**: Build a lexicon of consultant-level language:
   - ❌ "I'm a freelance writer" → ✅ "I consult on [channel] optimization for [industry]"
   - ❌ "My rate is $X/word" → ✅ "I offer a channel audit starting at $X"
   - ❌ "I'll write whatever you need" → ✅ "I specialize in [specific outcome] for [specific client]"

4. **Credibility Architecture**: Map what gives this writer authority — years of experience, specific results, unique methodology, client testimonials, published work. Even without traditional credentials, frame expertise around outcomes delivered.

### Step 3 — Revenue Architecture

**Execute**:

Design the pricing and offer structure using Joanna's principles:

1. **Door Opener**: A tightly scoped project that demonstrates ROI within 2-4 weeks. Priced to be accessible but not cheap. Must naturally reveal 2-3 other problems that need fixing. Must position you as the obvious person to fix them.

2. **Core Retainer**: Monthly ongoing engagement tied to measurable outcomes. Include base fee + clear deliverables + performance metric tracking.

3. **Performance Kicker**: Revenue share or bonus tied to specific milestones. Only kicks in when the client is already winning. Rule: the percentage should feel generous to the client (they keep the lion's share of upside).

4. **Adjacent Services Bundle**: 2-3 services that surround the core offering and make you irreplaceable. These create switching costs — replacing you means replacing an entire system, not just a writer.

### Step 4 — Financial Foundation Mapping

**Execute**:

If the writer has a "dream" career goal (book, screenplay, IP products, personal brand), design the financial foundation strategy:

1. Which of the 7 career paths is the **financial foundation**?
2. What does that foundation fund?
3. What's the timeline to transition from foundation to dream?
4. How does the foundation **feed** the dream? (e.g., ghostwriting builds writing skill, client relationships, and industry knowledge that make the personal brand inevitable)

### Step 5 — Competitive Differentiation

**Execute**:

Apply Joanna's Liquid Death principle: could a competitor copy your service but not your approach?

1. **Methodology Moat**: What unique process, framework, or approach makes your work categorically different? (e.g., "voice capture via diagnostic interview" vs. "write based on a brief")
2. **Positioning Statement**: "I [specific outcome] for [specific audience] using [unique approach] — which means [measurable result]"
3. **Anti-Positioning**: What you are NOT. What you don't do. What clients are wrong for you. Edge sharpens identity.

## Phase 3: Output Contract

**Deliver**: A complete Writer Career Positioning Document containing:

1. **Career Path Selection** — which path(s), with rationale
2. **Consultant Identity Statement** — the one-paragraph positioning
3. **Revenue Architecture** — door opener + retainer + performance kicker + adjacent services, with specific pricing
4. **Financial Foundation Map** — how this career funds the larger vision
5. **Competitive Differentiation** — methodology moat + anti-positioning
6. **90-Day Launch Plan** — first 3 months of execution milestones
