## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Service Core Definition

**Input Required**: What you write. For whom. What business result it produces.

**Execute**:

Define the **atomic unit** of value you deliver. Not "I write LinkedIn posts" — but "I make invisible coaches visible to their ideal clients through voice-matched LinkedIn content, generating inbound leads and shortening sales cycles."

Then answer Joanna's two diagnostic questions:
1. **What measurable business problem do you solve?** (Be specific: "coaches losing clients to less experienced competitors who simply have better LinkedIn presence")
2. **What is the before/after transformation?** (Before: brilliant expertise, zero visibility, losing deals to louder voices. After: consistent LinkedIn presence that sounds like them on their sharpest day, inbound inquiries, shorter sales cycles.)

### Step 2 — Door Opener Design

**Execute**:

Design the first engagement using Joanna's Door Opener principles:

**The 4 Rules of a Great Door Opener**:
1. **Produces a measurable, visible result within 2-4 weeks** — the client must SEE the impact quickly enough to want more
2. **Scoped tightly enough that you can over-deliver** — build in margin to exceed expectations
3. **Naturally reveals 2-3 other problems that need fixing** — the audit shows gaps that need ongoing work
4. **Positions you as the obvious person to fix those problems** — your methodology is already embedded in the diagnostic

**Format**:
```
DOOR OPENER: [Name]
Price: $[X] (pilot rate: $[Y] for first [N] clients — testimonial exchange)
Timeline: [N] days
Includes:
  - [Diagnostic/capture component]
  - [Primary deliverable — enough to demonstrate quality]
  - [Strategic insight — shows you see the bigger picture]
  - [Revision rounds]
  - [Guarantee if applicable]
What it reveals: [The 2-3 problems the client will discover need fixing]
Natural next step: [The retainer tier this leads into]
```

### Step 3 — Retainer Tier Architecture

**Execute**:

Design 2-3 retainer tiers using the Goldilocks architecture (from Joanna's persuasion skill):

**Tier Design Rules**:
- **Tier 1** should feel like the "smart choice" — enough to produce real results, accessible price point
- **Tier 2** should feel like the "obvious upgrade" — more deliverables, deeper engagement, better outcome tracking
- **Tier 3** (if applicable) should feel like "the full partnership" — comprehensive, premium, irreplaceable

**For each tier**:
```
TIER [N]: "[Name]" — $[X]/month
Deliverables:
  - [Quantity + type of writing]
  - [Strategy/planning component]
  - [Review/optimization component]
Client engagement:
  - [Call frequency and duration]
  - [Revision rounds]
  - [Reporting/metrics included]
Commitment: [Month-to-month or minimum term]
Outcome they're buying: [Not the deliverables — the business result]
```

**Performance Kicker Option** (attach to any tier):
```
PERFORMANCE BONUS:
Trigger: When [specific metric] exceeds $[milestone]/month
Structure: [X]% of [metric] above the milestone
Why the client says yes: They only pay when they're already winning
Why you propose it: Removes income ceiling, aligns incentives, makes you an investment not a cost
```

### Step 4 — Adjacent Services Bundle

**Execute**:

Apply Joanna's "Solve the Entire Problem" principle. For each core service, identify the adjacent needs that, if unmet, reduce the impact of your core work:

**Adjacency Mapping**:
```
CORE SERVICE: [What you primarily deliver]

ADJACENT NEEDS:
1. [Related need that amplifies core impact] → Add-on: $[price]
2. [Related need that creates switching costs] → Add-on: $[price]
3. [Related need that extends into new revenue] → Add-on or project: $[price]
4. [Related need that locks in long-term relationship] → Included in Tier 2+

WHY THIS CREATES IRREPLACEABILITY:
[When you solve all of these, replacing you means replacing an entire system, not just a writer]
```

### Step 5 — Revenue Projection & Capacity Planning

**Execute**:

Map the financial architecture:

```
REVENUE MODEL:

Door Openers: [N] per month × $[X] = $[total]
Tier 1 clients: [N] × $[X]/mo = $[total]
Tier 2 clients: [N] × $[X]/mo = $[total]
Add-ons: [estimated monthly] = $[total]
Performance bonuses: [estimated monthly] = $[total]

MONTHLY REVENUE: $[total]
CAPACITY: [max clients at your time constraint]
EFFECTIVE HOURLY RATE: $[rate] (including AI leverage)

TIMELINE:
- Month 1-2: $[target] (door openers + first retainer)
- Month 3-4: $[target] (retainers building)
- Month 5-6: $[target] (full capacity approaching)
- Month 7+: $[target] (steady state + performance bonuses accumulating)
```

## Phase 3: Output Contract

**Deliver**: A complete Writing Service Offer Package containing:

1. **Service Core Definition** — the atomic unit of value, the problem solved, the transformation
2. **Door Opener** — fully specified entry offer with pricing, scope, and guarantee
3. **Retainer Tiers** — 2-3 tiers with pricing, deliverables, and terms
4. **Performance Bonus Structure** — milestone-based kicker design
5. **Adjacent Services Menu** — add-ons with pricing and irreplaceability rationale
6. **Revenue Projection** — monthly targets by quarter with capacity constraints
7. **Proposal Template** — ready-to-customize language for pitching the offer

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Is every tier priced around **outcomes**, not deliverable counts?
- [ ] Does the Door Opener naturally create demand for the retainer?
- [ ] Would the client struggle to replace you because of adjacent services?
- [ ] Is the performance bonus designed so the client WANTS to pay it?
- [ ] Does the revenue projection account for capacity constraints (hours/day)?
