# Workflow 03: Channel Audit & Funnel Diagnostic

> **Produces**: A complete marketing funnel diagnostic report — identifies the specific "clog" in a client's marketing pipeline, quantifies the revenue impact, and delivers fix recommendations tied to writing/content solutions.
> **Use When**: A client has traffic but low conversions, inconsistent messaging across channels, or wants you to prove the value of strategic writing before committing to a retainer.

## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Channel Selection & Scope

**Input Required**: Client's business, their primary marketing channels, available data (traffic numbers, conversion rates, email metrics, social engagement), and their revenue goal.

**Execute**:

Identify the primary channel to audit. Joanna's taxonomy:

| Channel | Key Metrics to Audit | Common Clogs |
|---|---|---|
| **Website/Landing Pages** | Traffic → leads conversion rate, bounce rate, time on page | Weak headlines, feature-heavy copy, no clear CTA, cognitive friction |
| **Email** | Open rate, click-through rate, reply rate, unsubscribe rate | Generic subject lines, no voice, wrong awareness stage, asking too much too soon |
| **LinkedIn** | Impressions → engagement rate, profile views → connection requests → DMs | No hook architecture, generic content, missing keyword CTAs, weak profile |
| **Webinar/VSL** | Registration → attendance, attendance → completion, completion → purchase | Drop-off points reveal specific copy failures — wrong objection handling, pacing issues |
| **Sales Pages** | Traffic → purchase, add-to-cart → checkout completion | Misaligned messaging, missing social proof, friction at decision point |
| **Newsletter** | Growth rate, open consistency, click patterns, sponsor interest | Weak value proposition, inconsistent frequency, no personality, "blogging with email" |

Select 1-2 channels maximum. Depth beats breadth.

### Step 2 — The Plumbing Map

**Execute**:

Map the client's funnel as a flow system. For each stage, document:

```
FUNNEL STAGE: [Stage name]
Current Metric: [What the data shows]
Benchmark: [What "healthy" looks like in this industry]
Gap: [Difference between current and benchmark]
Revenue Impact: $[What this gap costs per month]
```

**Draw the full pipeline**:
```
[Traffic Source] → [Landing/Profile] → [Engagement/Opt-in] → [Nurture] → [Sales Conversation] → [Purchase]
     ↓                    ↓                    ↓                 ↓                ↓                ↓
  [metric]            [metric]             [metric]          [metric]         [metric]          [metric]
```

Identify where the biggest drop-off occurs. This is **the clog**.

### Step 3 — Copy & Messaging Diagnosis

**Execute**:

For the clogged stage, run a diagnostic battery:

**A. Persuasion Level Audit** (from the Persuasion Mastery skill):
- Run the Pronoun Audit — count first-person vs. second-person language
- Score the copy on the 5-level persuasion hierarchy
- Identify the highest persuasion level present and where it breaks

**B. Messaging Consistency Check**:
- Pull messaging samples from 3-5 touchpoints across the funnel
- Compare: Are they saying the same thing? Using the same voice? Addressing the same pain points?
- Identify messaging contradictions, tone shifts, and promise mismatches

**C. Voice of Customer Gap Analysis**:
- Compare the language used in the copy vs. what customers actually say (reviews, testimonials, social comments)
- Identify money words the audience uses that the copy doesn't
- Flag places where the copy uses "professional" language instead of customer language

**D. Toll Booth Detection** (from Persuasion Mastery):
- Identify every point where the reader has to think, be confused, or make an unnecessary decision
- Flag unfamiliar jargon, unexpected tone shifts, cognitive overload, broken expectations
- Count total toll booths — each one leaks conversion

### Step 4 — Revenue Impact Quantification

**Execute**:

Translate the diagnostic findings into dollars:

```
THE CLOG: [Specific stage and issue]

CURRENT STATE:
- [Metric] at this stage: [X]%
- Monthly traffic/volume at this stage: [N]
- Current downstream revenue: $[X]/month

PROJECTED STATE (after fix):
- Target [metric]: [Y]% (based on benchmarks)
- Projected downstream revenue: $[Z]/month

REVENUE IMPACT: $[Z - X]/month = $[annual impact]/year

This means: [Plain-language version — "You're leaving $X on the table every month because your email click-through rate is 1.2% when it should be 3.5%"]
```

### Step 5 — Fix Recommendations

**Execute**:

For each identified clog, provide specific, actionable fix recommendations:

```
FIX #[N]: [Name]
The Problem: [What's broken and why]
The Fix: [Specific writing/messaging intervention]
Expected Impact: [Metric improvement + revenue]
Implementation: [How, timeline, what you'd need from the client]
Priority: [High/Medium/Low — based on revenue impact and ease of fix]
```

Tier the fixes:
1. **Quick Wins** (implement in 1-2 weeks, immediate impact) — these prove your value fast
2. **Core Fixes** (implement in 4-6 weeks, significant impact) — these become the retainer work
3. **Strategic Upgrades** (implement over 2-3 months, transformational impact) — these become the ongoing partnership

### Step 6 — Transition to Retainer

**Execute**:

Design the natural bridge from audit to ongoing engagement:

"Based on this audit, here's what an ongoing optimization engagement looks like..."

Map audit findings → service tier:
- Quick Wins → included in first month of retainer as proof of value
- Core Fixes → the 3-month retainer focus
- Strategic Upgrades → the ongoing partnership evolution
- Performance Bonus → triggered by the metrics identified in the audit

## Phase 3: Output Contract

**Deliver**: A Channel Audit Report containing:

1. **Executive Summary** — the clog, the revenue impact, the fix (one paragraph a CEO can read in 30 seconds)
2. **Funnel Map** — visual pipeline with metrics at each stage
3. **Diagnostic Findings** — persuasion audit, messaging consistency, VOC gap, toll booths
4. **Revenue Impact Quantification** — dollars left on the table
5. **Fix Recommendations** — prioritized by revenue impact, with implementation plans
6. **Retainer Proposal** — natural transition from audit findings to ongoing engagement
