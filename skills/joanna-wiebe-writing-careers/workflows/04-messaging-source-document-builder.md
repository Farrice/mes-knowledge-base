# Workflow 04: Messaging Source Document Builder

> **Produces**: A complete Messaging Source Document — the master reference that tells everyone in an organization exactly what to say, when to say it, how to say it, and why it matters. The foundation on which all content, copy, and communications are built.
> **Use When**: A client has inconsistent messaging across channels, is scaling their team, is launching/repositioning, or wants a strategic messaging foundation before investing in content production.

## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Customer Journey Mapping

**Input Required**: Client's business, their customer segments, their product/service offerings, and access to customer data (reviews, testimonials, sales call recordings, survey responses, support tickets).

**Execute**:

Map the complete customer journey from first awareness to loyal advocate:

```
STAGE 1: UNAWARE
→ They don't know they have a problem
→ Message needed: [What creates problem awareness?]

STAGE 2: PROBLEM AWARE
→ They know the pain but don't know solutions exist
→ Message needed: [What names their pain in their language?]

STAGE 3: SOLUTION AWARE
→ They know solutions exist but don't know YOUR solution
→ Message needed: [What differentiates your approach?]

STAGE 4: PRODUCT AWARE
→ They know your offering but haven't decided
→ Message needed: [What removes friction and objections?]

STAGE 5: MOST AWARE
→ They're almost ready — just need a nudge
→ Message needed: [What triggers action NOW?]

STAGE 6: CUSTOMER
→ They've purchased — now what?
→ Message needed: [What confirms their decision and builds loyalty?]

STAGE 7: ADVOCATE
→ They love you — how do you activate them?
→ Message needed: [What makes them want to tell others?]
```

### Step 2 — Voice of Customer Mining

**Execute**:

Run the VOC-to-Copy Pipeline from Joanna's methodology:

1. **Review Mining**: Collect customer reviews, testimonials, case study quotes. Pull exact language — not paraphrased, verbatim.
2. **Sales Call Analysis**: If recordings are available, listen for:
   - How prospects describe their problem (their words, not your words)
   - What questions they ask (reveals what's unclear in current messaging)
   - What objections they raise (reveals what's missing from current messaging)
   - What makes them say "yes" (reveals what's working)
3. **Social Listening**: Scan comments, DMs, social mentions for recurring language patterns
4. **Survey Data**: If available, pull open-ended responses about why they bought/didn't buy

**Output**: A **Customer Language Bank** organized by:
- Pain points (in their words)
- Desired outcomes (in their words)
- Objections (in their words)
- Decision triggers (what made them act)
- Identity language (how they describe themselves — the money words)

### Step 3 — The Message Matrix

**Execute**:

Build the matrix that maps every message the business needs across the customer journey:

```
| Journey Stage | Primary Message | Supporting Messages | Proof Required | Emotion to Create | Channel(s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | [Problem-agitation message] | [Relatable scenarios] | [Market data / trend] | Curiosity, recognition | Social, ads |
| Problem Aware | [Name their pain precisely] | [Pain consequences] | [Social proof] | Urgency, hope | Blog, social, email |
| Solution Aware | [Your unique approach] | [Differentiation] | [Methodology, results] | Confidence, intrigue | Landing pages, content |
| Product Aware | [Specific offer + benefit] | [Objection handling] | [Case studies, testimonials] | Trust, desire | Sales pages, email |
| Most Aware | [Direct CTA + urgency] | [Risk reversal] | [Guarantee, ROI projection] | Conviction | Email, retargeting |
| Customer | [Decision validation] | [Onboarding, quick wins] | [Early results] | Relief, excitement | Email, in-product |
| Advocate | [Referral activation] | [Community, recognition] | [Peer stories] | Pride, belonging | Email, social, events |
```

For each cell, provide:
- The **exact message** (not a description of a message — the actual words)
- **Why this message** at this stage (the strategic rationale)
- **Example application** across 2-3 channels

### Step 4 — Brand Voice Definition

**Execute**:

Define HOW these messages should be delivered. This is the brand strategy layer:

```
BRAND VOICE ARCHITECTURE:

Personality: [3-5 adjective spectrum — e.g., "Bold but not aggressive, warm but not saccharine"]
Tone Spectrum: [How tone shifts by context — marketing vs. support vs. sales]
Signature Phrases: [Recurring language that builds recognition]
Forbidden Language: [Words, phrases, and tones that are off-limits]

THE LIQUID DEATH TEST:
Could a competitor copy our product/service but not our voice?
→ If yes: [What specifically makes our voice uncopyable?]
→ If no: [What needs to change to make it distinctive?]
```

### Step 5 — Channel-Specific Guidelines

**Execute**:

For each active channel the client uses, translate the messaging framework:

```
CHANNEL: [e.g., LinkedIn]

Primary Journey Stages Served: [Which stages does this channel address?]
Tone Calibration: [How the brand voice adapts to this platform]
Content Types: [What formats work here]
Messaging Do's: [Channel-specific best practices]
Messaging Don'ts: [Channel-specific anti-patterns]
Key Messages for This Channel: [Top 3-5 messages from the matrix, adapted]
Success Metrics: [How to measure if the messaging is working here]
```

### Step 6 — Implementation & Governance

**Execute**:

Design how the organization will actually USE this document:

```
IMPLEMENTATION PLAN:

1. Team Training (Week 1): Walk through the document with all content creators, marketers, and sales team
2. Channel Migration (Weeks 2-4): Update each channel's messaging to align with the source document
3. Quality Review Process: [How new content gets checked against the source document]
4. Update Cadence: [How often the messaging gets refreshed — quarterly recommended]
5. Ownership: [Who "owns" the messaging source document and approves changes]
```

## Phase 3: Output Contract

**Deliver**: A complete Messaging Source Document containing:

1. **Executive Overview** — the messaging problem, the solution, how to use this document
2. **Customer Journey Map** — 7 stages with message requirements
3. **Customer Language Bank** — verbatim VOC data organized by category
4. **Message Matrix** — every message mapped to journey stage, with proof and emotion
5. **Brand Voice Architecture** — personality, tone spectrum, distinctive language
6. **Channel Guidelines** — specific guidance for each active channel
7. **Implementation & Governance Plan** — how to roll out and maintain

---

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Is every message built from **customer language**, not invented by the writer?
- [ ] Does the document cover the **complete journey** from unaware to advocate?
- [ ] Would every content creator in the organization know **exactly what to say** after reading this?
- [ ] Does the brand voice pass the **Liquid Death Test** — distinctive and uncopyable?
- [ ] Is the implementation plan realistic and actionable?
