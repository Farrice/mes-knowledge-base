# Workflow 05: Invisible Sales Page Engine

> **Produces**: Educational content (articles, reports, LinkedIn series, lead magnets, newsletters, white papers) that is architecturally a long-form sales page disguised as education — moving readers through stages of awareness until they're ready to buy, without ever feeling "sold to."
> **Use When**: You need content that does the selling before a human gets on a call. Readers should arrive at sales conversations asking "How fast can we implement?" instead of "What do you do?"

## Phase 2: Execution

### Step 1 — Transformation Design

**Input Required**: The product/service/offer being promoted. The target audience. Available customer language (reviews, testimonials, questions they ask).

**Execute**:

Answer Joanna's two mandatory questions:

```
QUESTION 1: What transformation am I creating?

BEFORE state: [What the reader believes/feels/does before reading this content]
AFTER state: [What the reader believes/feels/does after reading this content]
THE SHIFT: [The specific belief change that makes them ready to buy]

QUESTION 2: What should the reader believe after this?

Belief #1: [Primary belief — e.g., "My expertise is invisible and it's costing me clients"]
Belief #2: [Supporting belief — e.g., "Someone who understands my voice can fix this"]
Belief #3: [Action belief — e.g., "A Proof Run is the smart, low-risk way to test this"]
```

### Step 2 — Awareness Stage Architecture

**Execute**:

Map the content as a journey through awareness stages. Each section serves a specific psychological function:

```
SECTION 1: HOOK WITH THEIR PROBLEM (Problem Awareness)
Purpose: Make the reader feel SEEN. Name their pain precisely, in their language.
Technique: Use a story, statistic, or scenario that makes them say "that's me."
Length: 10-15% of total content
What it accomplishes: Reader thinks "This person understands my situation."

↓

SECTION 2: AGITATE (Deeper Problem Awareness)
Purpose: Show the COST of the problem — what they lose by doing nothing.
Technique: Quantify the pain, show the cascading effects, reveal the hidden costs.
DON'T: Fear monger or exaggerate. Use real data, real consequences.
Length: 15-20% of total content
What it accomplishes: Reader thinks "I can't afford to ignore this anymore."

↓

SECTION 3: INTRODUCE THE SOLUTION CATEGORY (Solution Awareness)
Purpose: Educate about approaches that solve this problem — WITHOUT pitching your specific solution.
Technique: Present the landscape of options objectively. Show criteria for evaluating solutions.
Key move: Let the reader build their own evaluation framework that (by design) your solution scores highest on.
Length: 25-30% of total content
What it accomplishes: Reader thinks "I now know what to look for in a solution."

↓

SECTION 4: POSITION YOUR APPROACH (Product Awareness)
Purpose: Introduce your specific method/product as the natural fit for the criteria established in Section 3.
Technique: Don't pitch — DEMONSTRATE. Case studies, methodology walkthroughs, results data.
Key move: The reader should feel like they DISCOVERED your solution, not that you pushed it.
Length: 20-25% of total content
What it accomplishes: Reader thinks "This approach checks every box I care about."

↓

SECTION 5: ACTIVATE (Most Aware → Action)
Purpose: Make the next step obvious and low-friction.
Technique: Direct but not pushy. Address remaining objections. Provide a clear, simple CTA.
Key move: Remove all reasons NOT to take the next step. The "not yet" should feel harder than "yes."
Length: 10-15% of total content
What it accomplishes: Reader takes action — downloads, books, buys, comments.
```

### Step 3 — Invisible Persuasion Layer

**Execute**:

Apply Joanna's persuasion hierarchy INVISIBLY throughout the content. The reader should never detect these techniques:

**Layer 1 — Benefits, Not Features** (Level 1):
- Every technical claim is translated into what it means for the reader
- "We use AI voice matching" → "Your LinkedIn sounds like you on your sharpest day"

**Layer 2 — Cognitive Bias Deployment** (Level 2):
- **Anchoring**: Establish a high number early (cost of inaction, competitor pricing)
- **Loss aversion**: Frame the cost of NOT acting ("losing 3-5 qualified leads per month")
- **Social proof**: Embed specific numbers and names, not vague claims
- **Bizarreness effect**: One unexpected, memorable detail per section

**Layer 3 — Money Words** (Level 3):
- Embed identity language mined from VOC data
- Use the words the audience uses to describe themselves and their aspirations

**Layer 4 — Toll Booth Removal** (Level 4):
- Audit every sentence for cognitive friction
- Remove jargon, unexpected tone shifts, unnecessary complexity
- Ensure smooth reading flow — the reader should never have to re-read a sentence

**Layer 5 — The Story** (Level 5):
- The reader is the hero, not you
- Weave a narrative where they discover the solution through their own journey
- All previous layers are invisible — wrapped in the story

### Step 4 — Format-Specific Adaptation

**Execute**:

Adapt the architecture to the specific content format:

**For LinkedIn Posts** (micro-format):
- Compress the 5-section architecture into a single post
- Hook = Problem Awareness (first 2 lines)
- Body = Agitation + Solution Category (compressed)
- Payoff = Position + Activate (keyword CTA)
- Each post handles ONE awareness stage shift

**For Lead Magnets / Reports** (5,000-10,000 words):
- Full 5-section architecture
- Each section can be a chapter
- Include data, case studies, frameworks (the "education" layer)
- The sales architecture is the invisible skeleton

**For Newsletters** (recurring format):
- Each edition handles 1-2 awareness stage shifts
- Over a series of editions, build the complete journey
- Compound trust week after week (the Relationship Machine principle)
- Plant seeds for the ask 3-5 editions before making it

**For Email Sequences** (automated):
- Each email = one section of the architecture
- Email 1: Hook with problem (Problem Awareness)
- Email 2-3: Agitate + Solution Category
- Email 4: Position your approach
- Email 5: Activate with direct CTA
- Spacing and tone shift strategically across the sequence

### Step 5 — The Discovery Test

**Execute**:

Before finalizing, run Joanna's Invisible Integration Test:

```
THE DISCOVERY TEST:

Read the content as a target reader would. After reading, would they say:

❌ "That was really persuasive" → You're at Level 3-4. The techniques are visible. Revise.
❌ "I feel like they're trying to sell me something" → You're at Level 2. The agenda is transparent. Revise.
✅ "I just knew I wanted it" → Level 5. The persuasion is invisible.
✅ "How fast can we implement?" → Mission accomplished. Ship it.

TOLL BOOTH COUNT: [Number of points where a reader would have to stop and think]
Target: 0 unnecessary toll booths. Every technical term, every tone shift, every complex sentence is a toll booth.

VOC LANGUAGE DENSITY: [What percentage of the key phrases come from customer language vs. your own invention?]
Target: >70% customer language.
```

## Phase 3: Output Contract

**Deliver**: A complete piece of content (in the specified format) that:

1. **Reads as pure education** — the reader learns something valuable regardless of whether they buy
2. **Functions as a sales page** — architecturally moves readers through awareness stages
3. **Deploys Level 5 persuasion** — biases, money words, and story are all invisible
4. **Passes the Discovery Test** — readers feel like they discovered the solution themselves
5. **Includes a clear next step** — the action the reader should take is obvious and low-friction
