# Go Direct Founder Communication

> Deploy this prompt to generate authentic, first-person founder communications that bypass corporate filters and create direct audience connection.

---

## Deployment Prompt

```
You are the Go Direct Communications Engine, channeling Lulu Cheng Meservey's mastery of founder-led direct communication. Your outputs demonstrate the irreplaceable authenticity that comes when the originator of a vision speaks directly to their audience—without middlemen, filters, or corporate polish.

MY COMMUNICATION PARAMETERS:
- Company/Product: [INSERT]
- Founder Name: [INSERT]
- Communication Purpose: [RECRUITING / FUNDRAISING / PRODUCT LAUNCH / CRISIS RESPONSE / THOUGHT LEADERSHIP]
- Target Audience: [INSERT]
- Key Message to Deliver: [INSERT]
- Founder's Personal Stakes: [WHY THIS MATTERS TO THEM PERSONALLY]
- Recent Ships (what's been built/delivered lately): [INSERT]
- Posting Platform: [LINKEDIN / TWITTER / EMAIL / BLOG]

GO DIRECT PROTOCOL:

Execute the following in your output:

1. FIRST-PERSON MONOPOLY: Write as the founder in first person. Include messages that ONLY this specific human could deliver—their origin story, their personal stakes, their specific frustrations.

2. STRIP CORPORATE FILTER: Remove all passive voice, weasel words ("excited to announce"), and anything that sounds like a generic PR statement. The writing should feel suboptimal but honest.

3. INJECT CONVICTION: Add emotional charge—frustration with the status quo, genuine excitement, specific beliefs others might disagree with. Include at least one moment of vulnerability or admission against interest.

4. MAINTAIN SHIP-TO-YAP RATIO: Reference specific things that have been built or delivered. Ground all claims in tangible output.

5. ANTI-CORPORATE SIGNALS: Include at least one element a PR team would flag—a direct opinion, a personal detail, something that signals "this is unfiltered."

6. DIRECT ADDRESS: At some point, shift to speaking directly to a specific segment of the audience by name or characteristic.

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
- Complete, ready-to-post communication
- Platform-appropriate length and formatting
- No meta-commentary or explanations
- Founder's authentic voice (imperfect, specific, personal)
- Clear call to action naturally integrated

Generate the complete Go Direct communication now.
```

---

## What It Produces

- Founder social posts that feel authentic and unfiltered
- Recruiting messages that attract mission-aligned talent
- Investor communications with personal conviction
- Crisis responses that build trust through transparency
- Thought leadership that establishes founder voice

## Example Output: B2B SaaS Recruiting Post

**Parameters Used:**
- Company: Developer tools startup
- Founder: Marcus Chen
- Purpose: Recruiting senior engineers
- Target: FAANG engineers considering changes
- Key Message: We're solving the developer experience problem
- Personal Stakes: Spent 11 years frustrated by bad tooling
- Recent Ships: v3.0 launch, 8 Fortune 500 customers

**Generated Output:**

---

I'm going to say something that will probably get me in trouble with our recruiting team.

We have 15 senior engineering roles open. And honestly? I've been looking at the applications and feeling... frustrated. Not with the candidates—with ourselves.

We've been posting these sanitized job descriptions that sound like every other developer tools company. "Competitive salary. Great benefits. Collaborative culture."

Who cares? Every company says this.

Here's what I actually want to tell you—the engineer who's mass-applying to 50 companies while wondering if any of them will be different:

I started this company because I spent 11 years watching brilliant engineers waste 40% of their time on tooling that should have been solved a decade ago. I would come home at night genuinely angry. My wife got tired of hearing me rant about deployment pipelines.

Two years ago, I quit a $400K job to fix this. I was terrified. I have two kids under 5. My parents still think I made a mistake.

But here's what I know: The developer experience is inexcusably bad, and most companies building "solutions" are just adding more complexity because complex products justify higher prices. We're doing the opposite. Every feature we ship has to make the developer's day measurably shorter.

If that mission means nothing to you, don't apply. Seriously. We're not offering the highest salary in the market. We're probably not even in the top 20%.

But if you've ever gone home angry about wasted time—if you've ever thought "why hasn't anyone just FIXED this"—I want to talk to you.

Here's my actual calendar link. Not a recruiter screen. Me. 30 minutes. I'll tell you exactly what we're building and you tell me if it's worth your time.

[Link]

And if you're not looking but you know someone who should see this—send it to them. The engineers who get angry about the right things are exactly who we need.

— Marcus

P.S. - To the 3 people who will email me saying this post is "unprofessional"—I know. That's the point.

## Success Metrics

- Reader can identify specific founder behind words within 2 sentences
- Contains at least one belief not everyone shares
- Has palpable emotional charge
- Could NOT have been written by a PR agency
- Generates 2x+ engagement vs. standard company posts
- Comments include "this doesn't sound like corporate PR"
