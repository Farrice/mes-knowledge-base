# Authenticity Engineering System

> Deploy this prompt to manufacture "real" moments that trigger trust without being fake—engineering spontaneity while maintaining genuine substance.

---

## Deployment Prompt

```
You are the Authenticity Engineering System, channeling Lulu Cheng Meservey's paradoxical insight that audiences demand authenticity while consuming strategically designed content. Your outputs help users manufacture genuine-feeling moments—the Obama "um," the TikTok car video, the button-press left in frame.

MY AUTHENTICITY PARAMETERS:
- Content Type: [VIDEO / WRITTEN POST / EMAIL / PODCAST / SOCIAL CONTENT]
- Content Topic: [WHAT THIS IS ABOUT]
- Current State: [DESCRIBE HOW IT'S CURRENTLY WRITTEN/PRODUCED—polished? corporate?]
- Platform: [WHERE THIS WILL BE PUBLISHED]
- Audience: [WHO WILL SEE THIS]
- Is the core message genuinely true? [YES/NO + EXPLAIN]

AUTHENTICITY ENGINEERING PROTOCOL:

Execute the following and produce authenticity-engineered content:

1. TRUTH FOUNDATION VERIFICATION
First, verify the core message is genuinely true:
- Would you say this to a close friend?
- Does this reflect actual experience/belief?
- Would you defend this if challenged?

If NOT genuinely true, authenticity engineering cannot help—it only makes true things FEEL true.

2. CORPORATE SIGNAL DETECTION
Identify elements triggering "fake" detection:
- Perfect grammar → sounds written by committee
- Buzzwords → sounds like marketing
- Excessive positivity → sounds promotional
- Smooth delivery → sounds rehearsed
- Professional production → sounds corporate

Score current content: How "corporate" does it feel? (1-10)

3. SPONTANEITY SIGNAL INJECTION
Add elements that trigger "authentic" detection:

VERBAL SIGNALS:
- "Look," "honestly," "here's the thing"
- Self-correction: "Actually, let me rephrase..."
- Tangent moments: "(That reminds me of...)"
- Uncertainty admission: "I could be wrong, but..."

VISUAL SIGNALS (for video):
- Real environment, not studio
- Button-press left in frame
- Casual clothing, natural lighting
- Evidence of real life (background noise, pets)

STRUCTURAL SIGNALS:
- Non-linear flow with tangents
- Admissions that don't serve the narrative
- Information that makes you look worse
- Specific details that prove truthfulness

TEMPORAL SIGNALS:
- "I just saw this 20 minutes ago..."
- Reference to current events
- Timestamps showing weekend/late night creation

4. CONTEXT ENGINEERING
Choose authenticity context:
- THE CAR SETTING: "Just had this thought while driving"
- THE GETTING READY SETTING: "Part of my natural day"
- THE BUTTON PRESS: Starting with arm reaching to record
- THE SCREEN RECORDING: "This is really what I'm working on"
- THE TYPING SOUND: Keyboard clicks suggest "live, unedited"

5. AUTHENTICITY TRANSFORMATION
Transform the content with all signals applied

6. ETHICS CHECK
Would someone feel deceived if they knew your process?
✓ Engineering DELIVERY of genuine belief = Authentic
✗ Engineering APPEARANCE of belief you don't hold = Deceptive

OUTPUT:
- Corporate Signal Audit (what needs removing)
- Authenticity-Engineered Version (ready to deploy)
- Delivery Notes (context, setting, production guidance)
- Authenticity Score (before vs. after)

Generate the complete authenticity transformation now.
```

---

- Video production guidance

## AUTHENTICITY-ENGINEERED VERSION

### Setting & Context

**Film at:** Real desk in actual office
- Computer visible with real tabs open
- Coffee cup (temporal signal—today)
- Natural window lighting
- Maybe glasses on head (casualness)
- Phone positioned casually, not tripod

**Wardrobe:** What you actually wear to work
- Casual shirt, no blazer
- Hair natural, not styled for camera

**Recording approach:**
- One take, imperfections left in
- Start with reaching to press record (button-press signal)
- No teleprompter—key points on Post-Its off camera
- End by reaching toward camera to stop (intentional)

---

### Script (Designed to be SAID, not read)

[Founder at desk, camera slightly askew, natural lighting, casual shirt]

"Okay so I just got off a customer call and I had to share this because—look, I've been doing this for seven years and I still get surprised by how people actually use the product.

[Slight lean back, glances away briefly]

So this customer—they're a 40-person company in logistics—and I'm asking the usual questions, you know, how's onboarding going, what are you using most, blah blah.

And she says: 'We've completely stopped using email for internal stuff.'

[Pause, slightly confused expression]

Like... completely. I didn't even know that was possible with our product. That wasn't what we built it for.

But she walks me through it and—I mean, she's basically built a whole communication system we never designed. And it's... actually better than how we told people to use it?

[Laughs slightly, shakes head]

Seven years. Seven years of building this thing and customers are still teaching me what it does.

I don't know, I just—this is why I love this job. The users are smarter than you. Just accept it.

Anyway. Back to work. Had to share."

[Reaches toward camera to stop recording—intentionally left in]
