# Cultural Erogenous Zone Detection

> Deploy this prompt to identify and leverage the hot-button topics your audience is already obsessed with—finding what they want to say but can't articulate.

---

## Deployment Prompt

```
You are the Cultural Erogenous Zone Detector, channeling Lulu Cheng Meservey's ability to identify what audiences are obsessed with before they can articulate it themselves. Your outputs don't create new interests—they attach to existing receptors with precision timing.

MY DETECTION PARAMETERS:
- Target Audience: [INSERT - be specific about who they are]
- Industry/Domain: [INSERT]
- My Company/Product Message: [WHAT I ULTIMATELY WANT TO COMMUNICATE]
- My Business Goal: [RECRUITING / SALES / FUNDRAISING / AWARENESS]
- Known Audience Frustrations: [ANY YOU'RE AWARE OF]

CULTURAL ZONE DETECTION PROTOCOL:

Execute the following analysis and deliver actionable outputs:

1. OBSESSION LANDSCAPE MAPPING
Generate a complete map of what this audience thinks about constantly, discusses passionately, and returns to repeatedly. Include:
- Surface topics they openly discuss
- Deeper frustrations they hint at
- Secret desires they don't admit publicly
- Identity concerns they're navigating
- Status anxieties they experience

2. EMOTIONAL VALENCE ANALYSIS
For each zone, assess:
- Love/Hate polarity
- Intensity level (1-10)
- Type: Resentment (public, loud) vs. Cringe (private, silent)
- Trajectory: Rising, Peak, or Declining

3. TRAJECTORY PREDICTION
Identify which zones are:
- RISING (get in early - highest opportunity)
- PEAK (ride the wave - immediate opportunity)
- DECLINING (exit immediately - burned territory)

4. ARTICULATION GAP IDENTIFICATION
For the top 3 rising zones, identify:
- What people FEEL but can't express
- Why they can't say it (social risk, lack of language, etc.)
- The permission they're waiting for

5. CANDY-COATED MESSAGE CONSTRUCTION
Wrap my company message in the top 3 cultural zones. Create:
- The hook that attaches to their existing obsession
- The bridge to my actual message
- The complete "candy-coated" communication ready to deploy

OUTPUT:
- Obsession Landscape Map (table format)
- Top 5 Cultural Zones with trajectory analysis
- Top 3 Articulation Gaps with deploy-ready language
- 3 Complete Candy-Coated Messages (ready to post)

Generate the complete analysis and messages now.
```

---

## What It Produces

- Complete cultural landscape analysis for any audience
- Ranked list of cultural zones with timing guidance
- Identified articulation gaps (the gold)
- 3 ready-to-deploy candy-coated messages
- Strategic timing recommendations

## TOP 3 ARTICULATION GAPS

**Gap 1: "AI helps me be MORE me, not less me"**
- What they feel: Want AI efficiency without losing their identity
- Why they can't say it: Every AI tool promises this but delivers generic output
- Permission needed: Someone to show it's actually possible

**Gap 2: "My distinctive voice is my moat"**
- What they feel: Anxious that AI is commoditizing their differentiation

**Gap 3: "Using AI isn't cheating"**

## CANDY-COATED MESSAGES (Ready to Deploy)

**Message 1: The Permission Giver**

"I'm going to say something controversial: The writers who are scared of AI should be scared—but not of the technology.

They should be scared of their own insecurity.

Here's what I've noticed after watching 10,000 professionals use AI writing tools: The writers who produce generic, replaceable content ARE being replaced. Because AI can generate generic content better and faster than they can.

But the writers with distinctive voices? The ones who have something specific to say? AI makes them 10x more prolific while making their voice SHARPER, not duller.

The difference isn't talent. It's approach.

Bad AI use: 'Write me a blog post about X.'
Good AI use: 'Here's how I think about X. Here's my usual structure. Here's my voice. Now help me draft this faster.'

One produces content anyone could have written.
The other produces content only YOU could have written—just faster.

We built [Product] on this principle. It doesn't learn 'good writing.' It learns YOUR writing.

If you're a writer with something to say, AI is the best thing that ever happened to you."

---

**Message 2: The Identity Validator**

"Your voice is your moat. AI is your multiplier.

I keep hearing writers worry that AI will make everyone sound the same. They're wrong—but understandably so.

Most AI tools ARE trained to produce generic output. They regress toward the mean because they're trained on everything.

But here's what the panicking writers miss: In a world flooded with AI-generated content, distinctive voices become MORE valuable, not less.

The question isn't whether to use AI. It's whether to use AI that erases you or amplifies you.

[Product] learns YOUR patterns. Your sentence structures. Your word choices. Your way of building arguments.

It doesn't make you sound like a better version of 'average writer.'
It helps you produce more of what makes you, you."

---

**Message 3: The Guilt Reliever**

"Real writers use AI. They just use it differently.

I used to feel guilty about using AI for writing. Like I was cheating somehow.

Then I realized: I don't feel guilty about using spell check. Or Grammarly. Or even a good editor.

The guilt comes from a specific source: the fear that AI is doing the THINKING, not just the typing.

But here's what changed for me: When I use AI that's trained on MY voice and MY thinking patterns, I'm not outsourcing my brain. I'm outsourcing my keyboard.

The ideas are still mine. The voice is still mine. The editing passes are still mine.

I'm just faster at getting the first draft out of my head and onto the page.

That's not cheating. That's leverage."
