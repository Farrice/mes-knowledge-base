- 2-3 real examples illustrating the principle
- The mechanism (WHY the consensus is wrong)
- Top 3 objections and responses

5. POSITION STAKING CONTENT
Produce complete contrarian content:
- Manifesto version (full argument, 800+ words)

OUTPUT:

- Evidence architecture

## MANIFESTO: STOP MAXIMIZING REACH

# Most Marketing Advice Is Corporate Malpractice—And I'm Done Being Polite About It

I've spent 12 years in SaaS marketing. I've worked inside big agencies. I've seen the expensive campaigns. And I need to tell you something that will get me uninvited from industry conferences:

**Most marketing advice is lazy garbage that actively harms companies.**

I'm not hedging. I'm not saying "some" or "occasionally." The dominant paradigm in marketing is fundamentally broken, and every day it continues, companies leave money on the table while agencies collect fees for sophisticated-sounding frameworks that don't survive contact with reality.

**The "More Reach" Myth**

You've heard it a thousand times: "Expand your reach. Build your audience. Get in front of more people."

It sounds logical. More people = more potential customers. Simple math.

Except it's wrong.

Here's what the reach-obsessed approach actually produces:
- Rising CAC (up 60% in 5 years)
- Declining engagement (down 73% since 2020)
- Content commodity crisis (everyone publishing, no one reading)
- Channel fatigue (same message, everywhere, ignored everywhere)

**The math doesn't math.**

More reach means more dilution. When you try to speak to everyone, you speak to no one. Your message gets smoothed into something inoffensive enough for mass consumption—and inoffensive content doesn't convert.

**What Actually Works**

The companies making marketing work aren't optimizing for reach. They're optimizing for **resonance**.

Resonance measures how deeply your message lands—not how many people see it.

The difference:
- Reach: 100,000 people see your message, 50 convert
- Resonance: 10,000 people feel your message, 500 convert

Same marketing. 10x results.

I've helped 23 SaaS companies cut their audience targeting by 60-80% while increasing revenue 2-4x. Not because they found tricks. Because they stopped trying to be heard by everyone and started trying to be **indispensable** to someone.

**The Resonance Shift**

Resonance-based marketing looks different:

1. **Narrower targeting, deeper messaging**—10,000 right people > 100,000 wrong people
2. **Conviction-forward content**—Say things some will disagree with (that's how you know you're saying something)
3. **Channel depth over channel breadth**—Own one channel before expanding
4. **Conversion, not impressions**—Measure what matters, not what's easy

**Why I'm Saying This Out Loud**

I could keep this insight for clients. It would be safer.

But I watched a company I cared about blow $2M on a "reach expansion" campaign that produced worse results than their previous narrow strategy. They fired their CMO. They almost ran out of runway.

That experience radicalized me.

**The industry is lying to you—not maliciously, but systematically.** Agencies get paid for impressions. Platforms sell reach. Consultants justify themselves with complexity.

No one gets paid to tell you: Maybe you should do less, better.

So I'm telling you for free: **Stop maximizing reach. Start maximizing resonance.**

The math actually works.

→ "Show them CAC trend lines and LTV instead. Any investor who prefers vanity metrics over unit economics isn't paying attention. Resonance-based marketing produces better numbers—show those."
