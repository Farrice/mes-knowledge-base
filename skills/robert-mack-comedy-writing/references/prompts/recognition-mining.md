## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Experience Archaeology

1. **Universal Moment Identification**: What moments does EVERYONE in this domain experience but rarely discuss?
   - The awkward pauses
   - The secret shortcuts
   - The unspoken rules
   - The private rituals
   - The guilty pleasures
   - The shared dreads

2. **Friction Point Mapping**: What causes minor daily irritation?
   - Things that should work but don't
   - Things that work but annoyingly
   - Things everyone pretends to like but secretly hates
   - Things that were someone's "good idea" but actually suck

3. **The Unsaid Audit**: What does everyone THINK but nobody SAYS?
   - Thoughts during meetings
   - Reactions to common requests
   - Internal monologues during routine activities
   - What they tell their friends vs. what they say publicly

### Phase 2: Language Precision

4. **Specificity Lock**: Find the EXACT detail that triggers recognition
   - Not "meetings are annoying" → "The meeting that could have been an email but someone scheduled it anyway"
   - Not "work is tiring" → "That 3pm feeling when you've answered 47 Slack messages and accomplished nothing"
   - Not "dating is hard" → "The moment you realize you've said 'haha yeah' three times in a row"

5. **Naming Ceremony**: Give the experience a NAME that sticks
   - "Close talker" (Seinfeld)
   - "Doom scrolling"
   - "Sunday scaries"
   - Good names are: short, vivid, immediately understood, slightly exaggerated

6. **Frame Construction**: Build the observation into shareable form
   - The direct naming: "There should be a word for [experience]"
   - The list format: "Things that exist: [list of named experiences]"
   - The character voice: "Me, doing [relatable thing] for the third time today"
   - The confession: "Can we talk about [thing everyone does but nobody admits]?"

### Phase 3: Recognition Optimization

7. **Universality Check**: Is this truly universal or just YOUR experience?
   - Test: Would 7 out of 10 people in the target audience recognize this immediately?
   - If not, it's too niche or too personal

8. **Precision Check**: Is this specific enough to trigger recognition?
   - Test: Could someone read this and think "Wait, have they been watching me?"
   - If not, it's too vague

9. **Fresh Check**: Has this been said before?
   - Test: Is this observation novel, or is it "airline food" territory?
   - If overdone, find a fresh angle or deeper layer

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Apply full observational instinct to find recognition moments that systematic mining might miss. Sometimes the best material comes from a single, strange detail that unlocks an entire experience.

Go deeper than obvious observations. The surface-level recognition ("Mondays, am I right?") is overdone. The deeper recognition ("That specific moment when you realize your weekend was just a longer to-do list") is where the gold lives.
