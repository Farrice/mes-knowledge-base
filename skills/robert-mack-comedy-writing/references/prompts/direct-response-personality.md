## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Halbert Foundation

1. **Big Promise Engineering**: What's the singular, compelling promise?
   - Specific outcome
   - Timeframe
   - Believability threshold

2. **Unique Mechanism Articulation**: Why does THIS solution work when others failed?
   - Name the mechanism
   - Make it proprietary-feeling
   - Create "aha" moment

3. **Proof Architecture**: What evidence supports the promise?
   - Testimonials
   - Data/statistics
   - Demonstrations
   - Credentials

4. **Objection Inventory**: What stops them from buying?
   - Price concerns
   - Skepticism about results
   - Time/effort required
   - Trust issues

5. **Urgency Engineering**: Why buy NOW?
   - Scarcity (real, not fake)
   - Timing relevance
   - Cost of delay

### Phase 2: Mac Personality Integration

6. **Opening Pattern Interrupt**: Break the "sales letter" expectation
   - Start with story, not pitch
   - Lead with unexpected angle
   - Create curiosity before selling

7. **Benefit Entertainment**: Make benefit revelation enjoyable
   - Use analogy and metaphor
   - Deploy recognition humor around the problem
   - Create "that's so true" moments about their pain

8. **Objection Disarming**: Handle concerns through humor
   - Self-deprecating acknowledgment of skepticism
   - Reframe objections as features
   - Use "I know what you're thinking" with comedic payoff

9. **Voice Consistency**: Maintain personality throughout
   - Conversational, not corporate
   - Specific, not generic
   - Human, not salesy

10. **Memorable Moments**: Create lines they'll remember
    - Quotable phrases
    - Vivid analogies
    - Callback opportunities

### Phase 3: Conversion Optimization

11. **Friction Removal**: Make saying "yes" easy
    - Clear CTA
    - Risk reversal
    - Simple next step

12. **Personality-Urgency Balance**: Create urgency without desperation
    - Confident, not pushy
    - Real reasons, not manipulation
    - Humor softens hard closes
