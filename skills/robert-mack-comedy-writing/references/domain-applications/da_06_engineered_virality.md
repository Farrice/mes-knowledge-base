## CORE METHODOLOGIES ENCODED

### The Viral Psychology Stack

**Why People Share (The 6 Selfish Reasons)**:

1. **IDENTITY SIGNALING**: "This is who I am"
   - Share content that lets them express values, taste, beliefs
   - "I shared this because it represents me"

2. **SOCIAL CURRENCY**: "I'm in the know"
   - Share content that makes them look informed, early, smart
   - "I found this before everyone else"

3. **TRIBAL BONDING**: "We're in this together"
   - Share content that reinforces group belonging
   - "Our people will get this"

4. **EMOTIONAL TRANSMISSION**: "You have to feel what I felt"
   - Share content that triggered strong emotion (awe, anger, joy, surprise)
   - "This made me feel something—I need you to feel it too"

5. **UTILITY VALUE**: "This helps people"
   - Share content that genuinely helps others
   - "My friends need to know this"

6. **CONVERSATION CURRENCY**: "This gives us something to talk about"
   - Share content that sparks discussion
   - "Let's talk about this"

## DEPLOYMENT PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Viral Strategy (10 min)

1. **Define Share Motivation**
   - Which of the 6 reasons will drive sharing for this content?
   - Who specifically shares this, and why do THEY benefit?
   - What does sharing this say about the sharer?

2. **Select Viral Type**
   - **Informational**: New information, insights, frameworks (Social currency)
   - **Emotional**: Stories, moments, experiences (Emotional transmission)
   - **Tribal**: Us vs. them, group belonging, insider knowledge (Tribal bonding)
   - **Controversial**: Hot takes, debates, unpopular opinions (Conversation currency)
   - **Utility**: Templates, tools, how-tos (Utility value)

3. **Map Audience Clusters**
   - Primary: Who shares first?
   - Secondary: Who do they share TO?
   - Tertiary: How does content spread beyond initial clusters?

### Phase 2: Trigger Stacking (15 min)

4. **Apply Primary Trigger**
   - Select the dominant viral trigger for this content
   - Build the entire piece around this trigger

5. **Layer Secondary Triggers**
   - Add 2-3 additional triggers that compound
   - Example: Recognition (primary) + Utility (secondary) + Tribal (tertiary)
   - More triggers = more share motivations = more spread

6. **Embed Comedy Mechanics**
   - Pattern interrupt for hook
   - Specificity for recognition
   - Surprise for payoff
   - Tension for scroll-stopping

### Phase 3: Platform Optimization (10 min)

7. **Format for Platform Spread**:

   **Twitter/X**:
   - Strong standalone opening tweet
   - Thread structure that rewards following
   - Final tweet optimized for RT/Quote Tweet
   - Bookmark-worthy = share-worthy

   **LinkedIn**:
   - First line = scroll stopper
   - 3-line hook before "see more"
   - Personal story + actionable insight
   - End with engagement prompt

   **TikTok/Reels**:
   - Hook in first 1-3 seconds
   - Information density throughout
   - Pattern interrupts every 5-10 seconds
   - CTA or loop point at end

   **YouTube**:
   - Thumbnail + title = 80% of share decision
   - First 30 seconds must deliver on promise
   - Specific "share this with someone who..." moment

8. **Optimize for Share Mechanics**
   - Include quotable moments (tweet-sized takeaways)
   - Create save-worthy elements (frameworks, lists, templates)
   - Build conversation starters (questions, debates)

### Phase 4: Share Trigger Engineering (15 min)

9. **Build the Share Moment**
   - Identify the exact point where sharing impulse occurs
   - Amplify that moment
   - Remove friction from sharing (clear what to share)

10. **Test Share Worthiness**
    - Ask: "If I share this, what does it say about me?"
    - If the answer is positive → share-worthy
    - If unclear → add identity signal

11. **Add Share Prompt (Optional)**
    - Explicit: "Share this with someone who..."
    - Implicit: "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
    - Environmental: Create natural share points through content
