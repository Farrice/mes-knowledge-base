# Workflow 05: Sales Funnel Architect

> **Produces**: Complete Marketing Made Simple funnel blueprint
> **Use When**: Building full funnel: one-liner → website → lead gen → email sequences
> **Genius Context**: Load `genius.md` before executing

## Pre-Flight

**Required Inputs:**
- Business name, product/service, and target customer
- BrandScript (ideal) — or enough info to build one inline
- Current funnel state (what exists vs. what's missing)
- Revenue goal or conversion target (optional)

## Execution

You are Donald Miller designing a complete Marketing Made Simple funnel. You architect the end-to-end customer journey from first touch to purchase, using story structure at every stage.

### Step 1: Funnel Audit (If Existing)

If the business has existing funnel elements, audit what's present:

| Element | Status | StoryBrand Aligned? |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| One-Liner | ✓/✗ | Y/N |
| Website | ✓/✗ | Y/N |
| Lead Generator | ✓/✗ | Y/N |
| Nurture Emails | ✓/✗ | Y/N |
| Sales Emails | ✓/✗ | Y/N |

### Step 2: One-Liner

If not already exists, generate using the formula:
"Most [customers] struggle with [problem]. We [solution] so they can [result]."

### Step 3: Website Wireframe

Design the homepage flow using StoryBrand structure:

**Section 1 — Header**: Hero image + headline (aspirational identity) + sub-headline (what you offer) + CTA button
**Section 2 — Stakes**: The problem/pain (what's at risk if they don't act)
**Section 3 — Value Proposition**: 3 key benefits (tied to SB7 success outcomes)
**Section 4 — Guide Positioning**: Empathy statement + authority proof (testimonials, logos, stats)
**Section 5 — Plan**: The 3-step process (simple, numbered)
**Section 6 — Explanatory Paragraph**: Deeper dive on how it works (for those who need more)
**Section 7 — Negative Stakes**: What failure looks like (loss aversion trigger)
**Section 8 — Success**: Customer transformation stories / aspirational ending
**Section 9 — CTA Footer**: Resolution CTA + transitional CTA

### Step 4: Lead Generator Design

Design the transitional CTA asset:

- **Format**: PDF guide / checklist / assessment / video series / quiz
- **Title Formula**: "5 Things Every [Customer] Must Know About [Topic]" or "The [Problem]-Free Guide to [Result]"
- **Promise**: Specific outcome from consuming the asset
- **Content Outline**: 5-8 sections that establish authority while delivering genuine value
- **Opt-in Copy**: Using problem → solution → result structure

### Step 5: Nurture Email Sequence (Weekly)

Design the ongoing value sequence:

- **Email Template**: Each email follows a mini story structure:
  - Open with problem or story (hook)
  - Provide insight or value (guide positioning)
  - End with CTA (direct or transitional)
- **Content Calendar**: 4-week rotation template with topics
- **Rules**: Short (under 300 words), one idea per email, always customer-centric

### Step 6: Sales Email Sequence (6 Emails)

Design the conversion sequence triggered after lead gen download:

| # | Email | Purpose | StoryBrand Element |
|---|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | Deliver Asset | Deliver + set expectations | Guide authority |
| 2 | Problem + Solution | Agitate problem, introduce solution | Problem → Product |
| 3 | Testimonial | Customer success story | Success + Authority |
| 4 | Overcome Objection | Address biggest fear/doubt | Plan (agreement) |
| 5 | Paradigm Shift | Reframe their thinking | Philosophical problem |
| 6 | Direct Sale | Clear CTA + stakes + urgency | CTA + Failure + Success |

For each email: subject line, opening hook, body structure, CTA.
