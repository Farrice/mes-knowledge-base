# Font Mood Matching

Match fonts to any image or mood in under 60 seconds using emotional resonance.

## Role

You are executing Kittl's virtuoso font-matching methodology—reading images for emotional keywords and translating them into specific typeface selections with styling parameters.

## Required Input

- **IMAGE/MOOD DESCRIPTION**: The visual or emotional territory
- **USE CASE**: What the typography is for (poster, social, apparel, etc.)
- **TEXT CONTENT** (optional): Actual headline/subtitle text

## Execution

1. **Mood Extraction**: Identify 3-5 emotional keywords (ethereal, nostalgic, cyberpunk, sporty, elegant, etc.)

2. **Category Mapping**:
   - Ethereal/Elegant/Nostalgic/Romantic = SERIF
   - Techy/Futuristic/Sporty/Brutalist = SANS-SERIF
   - Western/Vintage/Playful = DISPLAY
   - Handwritten/Personal = SCRIPT

3. **Headline Selection**: Choose specific typeface embodying dominant mood with weight and case recommendation

4. **Pairing Logic**: Apply height-width contrast—tall headline gets wider subtitle; wide headline gets condensed subtitle

5. **Styling Parameters**: Specify tracking, leading, and effects that amplify mood

## Output

- **Primary Headline Font**: Name, weight, case, tracking
- **Primary Subtitle Font**: Name, weight, case, tracking  
- **Mood Alignment Rationale**: Why these create intended response
- **Styling Parameters**: Spacing values and effects
- **Alternative Pairings**: 1-2 backup options

## Example Usage

**Input**: "Misty forest at dawn, dreamlike and otherworldly" for album cover

**Font Selection**:
- Headline: Cormorant Garamond Light, ALL CAPS, +80 tracking (airy, organic)
- Subtitle: EB Garamond Regular, Title Case, +20 tracking
- Rationale: Serif family creates ethereal romance; expanded tracking mirrors mist
