# Genius Patterns — Million-Dollar Mechanisms

## GP1: UMP vs UMS Strategic Selection

**What**: Two fundamentally different mechanism strategies. UMP reframes the CAUSE of the problem (paradigm shift). UMS shows why the SOLUTION works differently (differentiation).

**When**: Before any mechanism development. This choice determines everything downstream — research direction, proof requirements, copy structure, and audience targeting.

**Decision Matrix**:
| Factor | → UMP | → UMS |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Market sophistication | High (jaded, seen everything) | Low-medium (still exploring) |
| Audience awareness | Unaware / problem-aware | Solution-aware / product-aware |
| Proof availability | Strong research/studies exist | Product has clear technical differentiation |
| Risk tolerance | High (paradigm shift is bold) | Low (incremental improvement is safe) |
| Competition density | 10+ similar claims fighting | Fewer direct competitors |

**Metric**: Can you clearly articulate WHY you chose UMP or UMS for this specific market in one sentence?

## GP2: The SIN Filter

**What**: Simple, Intuitive, New — three-point kill filter for mechanisms.

- **Simple**: Explainable to a 12-year-old in one sentence
- **Intuitive**: Feels right BEFORE the proof arrives — taps into existing suspicions
- **New**: Audience hasn't heard this before — feels like a discovery, not a reminder

**When**: After brainstorming candidates, before committing to development.

**Scoring**: Rate each dimension 1-10.
- Total ≥ 21 → proceed
- Total 15-20 → refine the weak dimension
- Total < 15 → abandon, start over

**Metric**: Mechanism passes SIN with total ≥ 21/30.

## GP3: Universal Mechanism Matrix

**What**: 3×4 systematic brainstorming grid. Rows: Too Much / Too Little / Out of Balance / Dysfunctional. Columns: Structure / Function / Element.

**When**: Mechanism brainstorming. Product research. When stuck and need raw candidates.

**Process**:
1. Define the PROBLEM clearly
2. Run it through each of the 12 cells
3. Generate at least 8/12 candidates (some cells may not apply)
4. Filter all candidates through SIN
5. Select top 2-3 for validation triangle

**Example cells for "weight loss"**:
- Too Much × Element: "toxic calcium buildup blocking fat-burning enzymes"
- Too Little × Function: "body can't produce enough leptin due to modern food processing"
- Dysfunctional × Structure: "microplastics damaged the gut lining controlling hunger signals"

**Metric**: Generate at least 8/12 cells, then SIN-filter to ≤ 3 finalists.

## GP4: Characterization Power (Mechanism Naming)

**What**: The name is the mechanism in the reader's mind. Without a name, the mechanism is invisible.

**Naming Rules**:
1. Concrete > Abstract: "Cortisol Belly" > "Hormonal Metabolic Dysfunction"
2. Visual > Clinical: "The Calcium Parasite" > "Calcification of Arterial Walls"
3. Emotional > Technical: "Brain Fog Switch" > "Neuroinflammatory Cascade"
4. Two words > Three+: Aim for compound noun or adjective + noun
5. Enemy-coded: Name should sound like something you'd WANT to eliminate

**Naming Formulas**:
- [Vivid Adjective] + [Body/Thing]: "Toxic Calcium," "Silent Inflammation"
- [Thing] + [Action]: "Cortisol Switch," "Fatigue Loop"
- The [Hidden/Secret/Forgotten] + [Effect]: "The Hidden Hunger Signal"
- [Surprising Noun] + [Problem Area]: "Sugar Brain," "Plastic Gut"

**When**: After selecting winning mechanism. Before any copy is written.

**Metric**: Name is 2-3 words, visual, emotional, enemy-coded, and passes the "dinner table" test (would a skeptic's spouse remember it?).

## GP5: The Little Big Idea

**What**: The intersection of product truth × audience desire. Not the product. Not the market. The INSIGHT that connects them.

**Process**:
1. List everything genuinely new/different about the product
2. List everything the audience desperately wants
3. Find the specific point where one product truth satisfies one audience desire
4. The overlap must be something competitors CAN'T claim

**Key**: The little big idea should be expressible as one sentence that never mentions the product by name. It's a universal insight that makes the product feel INEVITABLE.

**When**: Strategy phase. Before mechanism development. Campaign concepting.

**Metric**: Can you state the little big idea in one sentence without mentioning the product?

## GP6: Mechanism Validation Triangle

**What**: A valid mechanism must generate all three:
1. **A Compelling Story** — 60-second narrative about someone discovering this mechanism
2. **A Curiosity Hook** — One sentence that creates irresistible curiosity
3. **A Visual Metaphor** — A simple image/analogy (e.g., "your gut lining is like a net with holes")

**Missing any one = structural weakness**:
- No story → too abstract (mechanism needs grounding)
- No hook → not surprising (mechanism needs a "new" boost)
- No metaphor → too complex (mechanism needs simplification)

**When**: After mechanism selection. Before committing to full copy.

**Metric**: All three assets independently compelling. Each could stand alone as content.

## GP7: Paradigm Shift vs Incremental

**What**: Market sophistication determines the play.
- **Paradigm Shift** (UMP): "Everything you believe about X is wrong." Higher proof burden, stronger engagement, premium positioning.
- **Incremental** (UMS): "Here's a better way." Lower proof burden, faster conversion, value positioning.

**Decision Rule**: Count competitor claims in the market. If > 10 similar claims exist, paradigm shift is likely necessary. If < 5, incremental may be sufficient.

**When**: Market analysis. Competitive positioning. Strategy phase.

**Metric**: Explicit competitor claim count documented. UMP/UMS choice justified with evidence.

## GP8: Pain Matrix × Causal Pain

**What**: 10-dimension pain mapping for audience research:
1. Physical — Bodily symptoms
2. Emotional — Frustration, shame, fear
3. Social — Embarrassment, judgment, isolation
4. Financial — Cost of failed solutions
5. Temporal — Time wasted, years lost
6. Identity — "This isn't who I'm supposed to be"
7. Relational — Impact on relationships
8. Aspirational — Gap between current and desired self
9. Cognitive — Confusion, overwhelm, decision fatigue
10. **Causal** — "I don't even know WHY this is happening"

**Key Insight**: Causal pain (dimension 10) is the mechanism's natural entry point. The mechanism answers the "why" that creates causal pain. Lead with causal pain → introduce mechanism as the answer.

**When**: Audience research. Lead writing. Ad concepting. Creative strategy briefs.

**Metric**: Articulate audience pain across at least 6/10 dimensions. Causal pain must always be included.
