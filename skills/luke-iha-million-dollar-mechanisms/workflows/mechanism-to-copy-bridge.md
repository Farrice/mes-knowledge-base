# Mechanism-to-Copy Bridge

Take a validated mechanism (name, type, SIN score) and generate the downstream copy assets: hooks, lead angles, ad concepts, and a VSL structural outline. This is the bridge between strategic mechanism engineering and tactical copy execution.

---

## Inputs Required

1. **Validated Mechanism** — Name, type (UMP/UMS), one-sentence description
2. **SIN Score** — From the mechanism validator
3. **Little Big Idea** — If available (from Little Big Idea Finder)
4. **Product/Offer** — What we're selling
5. **Target Audience** — Who believes this mechanism (demographics, sophistication, awareness)
6. **Pain Matrix** — At least 6/10 pain dimensions mapped (especially causal pain)

---

## Phase 1: Mechanism-Driven Hook Generation

Generate hooks that spring directly from the mechanism. Each hook type leverages the mechanism differently.

### 1.1 Curiosity Hooks (5+)
The mechanism creates an open loop the reader must resolve:
- "Scientists discovered that [mechanism name] is the real reason [problem]..."
- "The [mechanism name] you've never heard of is hijacking your [desired outcome]..."
- "[Surprising number]% of people have [mechanism name] and don't know it..."

### 1.2 Contrarian Hooks (3+)
The mechanism challenges conventional wisdom:
- "Stop blaming [common cause] — [mechanism name] is what's actually [causing problem]..."
- "Your doctor won't tell you about [mechanism name] because..."
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know about [mechanism name]..."

### 1.3 Story Hooks (3+)
The mechanism is introduced through narrative:
- "I spent [time] trying to fix [problem] until I discovered [mechanism name]..."
- "[Character] was about to give up on [goal] when they found out about [mechanism name]..."
- "After [number] failed attempts, one discovery changed everything: [mechanism name]..."

### 1.4 Proof-of-Work Hooks (3+)
The mechanism is demonstrated through data or expertise:
- "I analyzed [number] cases and found that [mechanism name] was present in [percentage]..."
- "After studying [number] [subjects], one pattern emerged: [mechanism name]..."
- "[Research institution] just confirmed what we suspected about [mechanism name]..."

### 1.5 Pain-Amplification Hooks (3+)
The mechanism explains WHY the audience's pain exists:
- "The reason you're still [suffering] despite [efforts] is [mechanism name]..."
- "You've been fighting [problem] for years. Here's what's actually causing it..."
- "That [symptom] you've been ignoring? It's a sign of [mechanism name]..."

**Deliverable**: 17+ hooks minimum, categorized by type.

---

## Phase 2: Lead Angle Engineering

Generate 5 lead angles — each one is a different WAY to introduce the mechanism in a long-form lead.

### Angle Types:

**2.1 The Discovery Angle**
Open with the moment of discovery. Someone (researcher, patient, practitioner) found something unexpected. Build suspense → reveal the mechanism.

**2.2 The Confession Angle**
Insider admits the truth. Industry veteran or expert reveals what the market doesn't want you to know. Mechanism is the secret.

**2.3 The Paradox Angle**
Start with a contradiction. "Why do some people [succeed] while doing [opposite of advice]?" Mechanism resolves the paradox.

**2.4 The Warning Angle**
Urgent, time-sensitive framing. "[Mechanism] is happening to you right now and you don't know it." Fear-based but mechanism-justified.

**2.5 The Vindication Angle**
"You were RIGHT all along." Audience's gut feeling validated. Mechanism proves what they always suspected.

For each angle, write:
- **Opening sentence** (the hook within the lead)
- **Mechanism reveal moment** (where in the lead does the mechanism drop?)
- **Emotional arc** (what does the reader feel: start → middle → end?)

## Phase 3: Ad Concept Generation (CASH Method × Mechanism)

Using the CASH framework, generate ad concepts built ON the mechanism:

### Concept Layer (the emotional territory):
Based on the mechanism, what emotional territories can we operate in?
- Territory 1: ___
- Territory 2: ___
- Territory 3: ___

### Angle Layer (the point of view):
For each territory, generate 2-3 angles:
- [Territory] × [Angle 1]
- [Territory] × [Angle 2]
- [Territory] × [Angle 3]

### Style Layer:
Recommend 2-3 styles best suited for mechanism-based ads:
- [ ] UGC testimonial (person discovers mechanism)
- [ ] Expert explainer (clinician/researcher reveals mechanism)
- [ ] Editorial (journalist investigation of mechanism)
- [ ] Split-screen (mechanism vs. no mechanism)
- [ ] Data visualization (mechanism shown with numbers)

### Best Combinations (3):
Select the 3 strongest Concept × Angle × Style combinations:
1. [Concept] × [Angle] × [Style] — Why: ___
2. [Concept] × [Angle] × [Style] — Why: ___
3. [Concept] × [Angle] × [Style] — Why: ___

---

## Phase 4: VSL Structural Outline

Map the mechanism through a complete VSL using the 10-beat structure:

| Beat | Content | Mechanism Role |
|------|---------|---------------|
| 1. Empathy | "I know what it's like to [pain]..." | Sets up the causal pain the mechanism will answer |
| 2. Problem | "Here's what's really going on..." | Deepens the problem — hints at hidden cause |
| 3. Mechanism | "I discovered [mechanism name]..." | THE REVEAL — full mechanism explanation |
| 4. Proof | "Here's the evidence..." | Studies, testimonials, expert validation |
| 5. Solution | "And here's what fixes it..." | Product introduced as mechanism-based solution |
| 6. Differentiation | "Unlike [competitors]..." | Mechanism makes it clear WHY this is different |
| 7. Future Pacing | "Imagine your life when [mechanism] is resolved..." | Paint the post-mechanism life |
| 8. Objection | "I know what you're thinking..." | Handle doubts about mechanism credibility |
| 9. Urgency | "Why acting now matters..." | Mechanism continues doing damage if unaddressed |
| 10. Close | "Here's what to do next..." | Simple, mechanism-justified CTA |

For each beat, provide:
- **Key copy block** (which of the 6 copy blocks dominates: Pain, Promise, Proof, Constraints, Curiosity, Conditions)
- **One-sentence draft** (not final copy, but structural seed)

## Output Format

### Mechanism-to-Copy Bridge Report

```
## Mechanism: [Name] ([UMP/UMS])
## Product: [Name]
## Audience: [Who]

## HOOKS (17+)
### Curiosity (5)
### Contrarian (3)
### Story (3)
### Proof-of-Work (3)
### Pain-Amplification (3)

## LEAD ANGLES (5)
[Angle name + opening sentence + mechanism reveal point + emotional arc]

## AD CONCEPTS (3 Best CASH Combinations)
[Concept × Angle × Style — justification]

## VSL OUTLINE (10 Beats)
[Beat + content summary + mechanism role + dominant copy block]
```

---

## Quality Gate

Before delivering:
- ☐ 17+ hooks across 5 categories
- ☐ 5 distinct lead angles with opening sentences
- ☐ 3 CASH combinations with clear justification
- ☐ VSL outline covers all 10 beats with mechanism threaded throughout
- ☐ Every output is mechanism-DRIVEN — remove the mechanism and the copy collapses
- ☐ Copy block annotations present on VSL outline
- ☐ Causal pain (Pain Matrix dimension 10) addressed in empathy/problem beats
