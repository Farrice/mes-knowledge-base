# Little Big Idea Finder

Discover the insight that sits at the intersection of product truth and audience desire. Not the product. Not the market. The IDEA that makes this product feel inevitable for this audience. This is the strategic foundation that mechanisms, hooks, leads, and ads are built on.

---

## Inputs Required

1. **Product/Service** — What is it? What does it do? How does it work?
2. **Product Truths** — Everything genuinely new, different, or unique. Ingredients, process, source, application, technology, origin.
3. **Audience Profile** — Who are they? What do they want? What have they tried?
4. **Audience Desires** — Outcomes, feelings, status, relief, transformation they seek.
5. **Competitive Landscape** — Who else serves this audience? What do they claim?

---

## Phase 1: Product Truth Inventory

List EVERYTHING that is genuinely true and unique about this product. Be brutally honest — features that competitors also have don't count.

### Categories to mine:
- **Ingredient / Component** — What's in it that's different?
- **Process / Method** — How is it made or delivered differently?
- **Source / Origin** — Where does it come from? Who created it?
- **Application / Use** — How is it used differently?
- **Research / Discovery** — What finding supports it?
- **Scale / Speed** — Does it work faster, bigger, smaller?
- **Combination** — Does it combine known things in a new way?

## Phase 2: Audience Desire Inventory

List everything the audience desperately wants — not features, but *states*.

### Categories to mine:
- **Physical Outcomes** — What do they want their body/health/appearance to look like?
- **Emotional States** — What do they want to FEEL?
- **Status / Identity** — Who do they want to BE?
- **Relief / Freedom** — What do they want to STOP doing/feeling?
- **Time / Energy** — What do they want to reclaim?
- **Relationships** — How do they want to show up for others?
- **Competence** — What do they want to be capable of?

## Phase 4: Little Big Idea Extraction

From the strongest intersections, formulate the Little Big Idea:

### The Test:
The Little Big Idea must be expressible as **one sentence that never mentions the product by name**. It's a universal insight that makes the product feel INEVITABLE.

### Formula:
> "The reason [audience problem/desire] has been so hard is [product truth reframed as insight] — and once you understand this, [transformation promise]."

---

## Phase 5: Downstream Applications

Once the Little Big Idea is locked, map how it flows downstream:

### Mechanism Direction
- Does this idea suggest a UMP (problem reframe) or UMS (solution differentiation)?
- What mechanism candidates naturally emerge from this insight?

### Hook Seeds
- Write 3 hooks that spring from the Little Big Idea
- Each hook should make the reader think: "Wait, what?"

### Lead Angle
- How would a VSL lead open if this was the core idea?
- What's the "movie trailer" version?

### Ad Concept
- What's the CASH method Concept if this is the territory?
- What angles naturally emerge?

---

## Quality Gate

Before delivering:
- ☐ Product truth inventory has ≥ 8 entries with honest uniqueness assessment
- ☐ Audience desire inventory focuses on states/feelings, not features
- ☐ Little Big Idea expressed WITHOUT mentioning the product
- ☐ Idea creates a genuine "aha" moment — not a restatement of the value prop
- ☐ Competitors genuinely cannot make this same claim
- ☐ Downstream applications demonstrate the idea's generative power
