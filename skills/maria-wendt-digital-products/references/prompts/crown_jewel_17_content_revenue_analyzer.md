## THE KEY METRICS THAT MATTER

### Metric 1: Revenue Per Content Piece

**Formula**: Total revenue attributed to piece ÷ 1
**Why It Matters**: Direct measure of content profitability
**Target**: Identify your top 10% of revenue-generating content

### Metric 2: Revenue Per View (RPV)

**Formula**: Revenue attributed ÷ Total views
**Why It Matters**: Normalizes for reach; a 10K-view reel making $500 has higher RPV than a 100K-view reel making $300
**Benchmark**: Track your average, optimize to beat it

### Metric 3: Comments-to-Views Ratio

**Formula**: Comments ÷ Views × 100
**Why It Matters**: Comments = automation triggers = sales opportunities
**Target**: 1%+ (10K views should yield 100+ comments)

### Metric 4: Comment-to-Sale Conversion Rate

**Formula**: Sales from keyword ÷ Comments with keyword × 100
**Why It Matters**: Shows how well your automation converts interest to purchase
**Target**: 5-15% (varies by price point)

### Metric 5: Content ROI

**Formula**: (Revenue - Content Cost) ÷ Content Cost × 100
**Why It Matters**: Some content takes longer to create; is it worth it?
**Note**: For most creators, "cost" is time. Value your time.

## ATTRIBUTION ARCHITECTURE

### Level 1: ManyChat Keyword Tracking (You Can Do This Now)

**The Setup**:

1. **One Keyword Per Content Type** (Start Simple):
   - Reels: Comment "SUNDAY"
   - Carousels: Comment "MEALS"
   - Stories: Comment "SYSTEM"

2. **Tag Subscribers by Entry Point**:
   In ManyChat, when someone comments:
   - "SUNDAY" → Tag: "Entry_Reel"
   - "MEALS" → Tag: "Entry_Carousel"
   - "SYSTEM" → Tag: "Entry_Story"

3. **Track Which Tag Converts**:
   Export ManyChat data weekly. Count:
   - How many "Entry_Reel" subscribers purchased
   - How many "Entry_Carousel" subscribers purchased
   - How many "Entry_Story" subscribers purchased

**What This Tells You**:
- Which content TYPE drives the most sales
- Where to focus your creation energy

### Level 2: Post-Specific Tracking (More Granular)

**The Setup**:

1. **Unique Keywords Per Post**:
   - Monday Reel: "SUNDAY1"
   - Tuesday Reel: "SUNDAY2"
   - Wednesday Carousel: "MEALS1"
   - etc.

2. **Tag Each Uniquely in ManyChat**:
   Create a tag for each keyword that includes the date or post identifier.

3. **Build the Tracking Sheet**:

| Date | Post Type | Keyword | Views | Comments | DMs Sent | Sales | Revenue | RPV |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|----------|----------|-------|---------|-----|
| 1/15 | Reel | SUNDAY1 | 12K | 145 | 145 | 8 | $216 | $0.018 |
| 1/16 | Reel | SUNDAY2 | 8K | 210 | 210 | 12 | $324 | $0.041 |
| 1/17 | Carousel | MEALS1 | 4K | 52 | 52 | 3 | $81 | $0.020 |

**What This Tells You**:
- Reel SUNDAY2 has 2.3x higher RPV than SUNDAY1
- The hook/content of SUNDAY2 converts better
- Create more content like SUNDAY2

### Level 3: UTM Parameter Tracking (More Accurate)

**The Setup**:

1. **Create Unique Links for Each Post**:
   - Instead of: yoursite.com/sunday-system
   - Use: yoursite.com/sunday-system?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=jan15

2. **Track in Google Analytics or Checkout Platform**:
   Most platforms (SamCart, Kajabi) can report sales by UTM parameter.

3. **Build This Into Your Automation**:
   ManyChat can send different links based on keywords, each with unique UTM.

**What This Tells You**:
- Exact revenue per post (not just per content type)
- Which specific hooks/angles convert

**WINNERS** (High Views + High Revenue):
- Your best content

- Algorithm didn't favor it, but buyers LOVED it

- Repost after 60 days

**FOOL'S GOLD** (High Views + Low Revenue):
- Vanity content—looks good, doesn't convert
- Figure out WHY people aren't buying
- Is CTA weak? Wrong audience attracted?
- Fix or stop creating this type

**LOSERS** (Low Views + Low Revenue):
- Not working on any level
- Stop creating this type of content
- Don't feel bad—data is freeing you

## OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOL

### Weekly Review Process (30 minutes)

**Step 1: Export Data (5 min)**
- Export ManyChat conversation data
- Pull Instagram Insights for the week
- Update tracking spreadsheet

**Step 2: Identify Winners (10 min)**
- Sort by Revenue Per Post (highest to lowest)
- Identify top 3 performers
- Note what they have in common (hook type, topic, format)

**Step 3: Identify Losers (5 min)**
- Find bottom 3 performers
- Note what they have in common
- Schedule these patterns for elimination

**Step 4: Plan Next Week (10 min)**
- Create 2-3 variations of winner hooks
- Kill 1-2 loser patterns
- Document hypotheses to test

### Monthly Review Process (1 hour)

**Step 1: Calculate Monthly Metrics**
- Total revenue from content
- Average RPV for the month
- Best and worst performing posts

**Step 2: Update Content Strategy**
- What content types to do MORE
- What content types to STOP
- What experiments to run

**Step 3: Compare to Previous Month**
- Is RPV improving?
- Is comment rate improving?
- Is overall revenue from content growing?

## DECISION FRAMEWORKS

### When to Create More of Something

**Rule**: If RPV is 2x or higher than your average, clone it aggressively.

**Example**:
- Your average RPV: $0.02
- A reel's RPV: $0.05 (2.5x average)
- **Action**: Study this reel. What's the hook? Format? Topic? Create 5 variations.

### When to Kill Something

**Rule**: If RPV is 50% or less of your average for 3+ posts of that type, stop creating it.

**Example**:
- Your average RPV: $0.02
- "Day in my life" reels: $0.005 average (25% of baseline)
- **Action**: Stop creating "day in my life" content. It doesn't convert.

### When to Test Something New

**Rule**: Allocate 20% of content to experiments. Track them separately.

**Example**:
- 7 posts/week
- 5-6 posts: Proven winners (variations of what works)
- 1-2 posts: Experiments (new hooks, formats, angles)
- Evaluate experiments after 4 weeks
