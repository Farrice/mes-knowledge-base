## THE 5 FLOP REASONS FRAMEWORK

### Flop Reason #1: TOO GENERIC

**The Problem**: The product doesn't solve a hyper-specific problem. It's broad, vague, or tries to help "everyone."

**Diagnostic Questions**:
- Can you describe the target buyer in one specific sentence?
- Does the product title contain specific numbers or outcomes?
- Would two different people imagine the same thing when they hear the product name?

**Pass Criteria**: 
- Target buyer is specific (not "entrepreneurs" but "freelance designers charging under $5K/project")
- Product promise is quantified ("$15K raise" not "better salary")
- Differentiation is clear (not "meal prep guide" but "30 dinners in 2 hours")

**Fail Signals**:
- Product could apply to almost anyone
- Promise is vague ("improve your life," "grow your business")
- No specific numbers or timeframes in positioning

---

### Flop Reason #2: VALUE NOT ARTICULATED

**The Problem**: The product might be great, but the creator isn't explaining WHY it's worth the money. The ROI isn't clear. The transformation isn't vivid.

**Diagnostic Questions**:
- Can a cold visitor understand what they get in under 5 seconds?
- Is the ROI stated explicitly (money saved AND/OR money earned)?
- Are benefits described, not just features?

**Pass Criteria**:
- Checkout page shows clear "what you get" section with benefit-first descriptions
- ROI is calculated and displayed ("$47 for $15K potential = 319x return")
- Transformation is vivid (before state ‚Üí after state clearly shown)

**Fail Signals**:
- Checkout page lists features without explaining why they matter
- No ROI calculation or value comparison
- Visitor would need to think hard about why this is worth the price

---

### Flop Reason #3: PRICE TOO HIGH

**The Problem**: The price creates too much friction for the value delivered or the trust level of the audience.

**Diagnostic Questions**:
- Is this priced for impulse purchase (under $50) or considered purchase (over $100)?
- Does the audience trust level match the price point?
- Have you tested lower price points?

**Pass Criteria**:
- Price matches audience temperature (cold traffic = lower price)
- $27-47 range for most Instagram-driven low-ticket products
- Higher prices supported by strong social proof and established trust

**Fail Signals**:
- First-time visitors being asked for $200+ with no prior relationship
- Price point above $50 with weak or no social proof
- Conversion rate significantly below 1%

**Important Note**: Maria says if something doesn't sell at $27, price is NOT the problem‚Äîit's one of the other four reasons. $27 is the "price eliminator" test.

---

### Flop Reason #4: NOT A REAL PROBLEM

**The Problem**: The product solves something that sounds interesting but isn't something people are actively trying to fix. It's a "nice to have," not a "need to solve."

**Diagnostic Questions**:
- Are people actively searching for solutions to this problem?
- Would someone pay to solve this problem, or just think "that's cool"?
- Does this pass the Amazon/YouTube validation test?

**Pass Criteria**:
- 5+ books on Amazon on this topic OR 500+ total 5-star reviews
- 3+ YouTube channels with 100K+ subscribers covering this
- People ask questions about this problem in comments/forums

**Fail Signals**:
- No books or very few reviews on the topic
- Crickets on YouTube for this subject
- Product solves a "problem" the creator invented, not one the market has

---

### Flop Reason #5: MARKETING SUCKS (Product Is Fine)

**The Problem**: The product is good. The offer is solid. The creator just doesn't know how to get it in front of people or convert attention into sales.

**Diagnostic Questions**:
- Is there consistent content being published?
- Is there a working automation (comment ‚Üí DM ‚Üí checkout)?
- Is social proof present and compelling?
- Is the checkout page optimized for conversion?

**Pass Criteria**:
- Posting daily content with clear CTAs
- ManyChat automation working and tested
- Social proof visible immediately on checkout page
- Checkout page loads fast, reads easy, has clear CTA

**Fail Signals**:
- Sporadic posting (not daily)
- No automation or broken automation
- Checkout page is text-heavy, slow, or confusing
- No social proof or weak social proof
