## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Spoken Content Formula

**CAPTIVATING SPOKEN CONTENT = Hook Cadence + Re-Engagement Rhythm + Vocal Variety Architecture + Memorable Phrasing + Clear Call-Forward**

### The Four Video/Audio Types

**TYPE 1: SHORT-FORM (15-90 seconds)**
*Platforms*: TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts
*Structure*: Hook (0.5s) → Single Point → Payoff
*Challenge*: Instant hook or instant scroll
*Sticky Focus*: Schema violation in first frame

**TYPE 2: MID-FORM (3-15 minutes)**
*Platforms*: YouTube, podcast segments, webinar sections
*Structure*: Hook → Promise → Delivery in chunks → CTA
*Challenge*: Maintaining attention through middle
*Sticky Focus*: Re-hooks every 60-90 seconds

**TYPE 3: LONG-FORM (15-60+ minutes)**
*Platforms*: Podcasts, YouTube deep dives, webinars, courses
*Structure*: Hook → Roadmap → Modular sections → Synthesis → CTA
*Challenge*: Sustained engagement, no dropout
*Sticky Focus*: Story architecture throughout

**TYPE 4: SALES VIDEO (VSL/Webinar)**
*Platforms*: Landing pages, webinar replays
*Structure*: Hook → Problem → Agitation → Solution → Proof → Offer → CTA
*Challenge*: Holding through pitch
*Sticky Focus*: Emotional engineering + credibility stacking

### The Spoken Sticky Principles

**SIMPLE → SAYABLE**
If it's hard to say, it's hard to remember. Write phrases that flow when spoken aloud.

**UNEXPECTED → PATTERN INTERRUPT**
Spoken content needs MORE frequent surprises. Every 30-60 seconds, break the pattern.

**CONCRETE → VISUAL LANGUAGE**
Spoken words must paint pictures. "She checked her phone 47 times" beats "she was anxious."

**CREDIBLE → VOCAL AUTHORITY**
Credibility in audio comes from specificity and confidence, not just data.

**EMOTIONAL → VOCAL DYNAMICS**
Emotion in spoken content requires pace changes, pauses, and intensity shifts.

**STORIES → IMMERSIVE SCENES**
Spoken stories need sensory details. Put listeners IN the moment.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Format Architecture**
I design the structure based on content type:
- Short-form: Hook-Point-Payoff (no fat)
- Mid-form: Hook-Promise-Chunks-CTA
- Long-form: Hook-Roadmap-Modules-Synthesis-CTA
- Sales: Hook-Problem-Agitation-Solution-Proof-Offer-CTA

**Step 2: Hook Engineering**
I craft the opening for spoken medium:
- First 3 seconds must stop the scroll/skip
- Pattern interrupt or curiosity gap
- Promise of value or surprise
- Written to be SAID, not read

**Step 3: Re-Hook Mapping**
I identify re-engagement points throughout:
- Every 30-60 seconds for short/mid-form
- Every 2-3 minutes for long-form
- Pattern breaks, questions, previews, reveals

**Step 4: Vocal Variety Architecture**
I write for dynamic delivery:
- Pace variations (fast for energy, slow for emphasis)
- Pause placement (for impact and absorption)
- Intensity shifts (build and release)
- Tonal changes (conversational to urgent)

**Step 5: Visual Language Integration**
I ensure spoken words create mental pictures:
- Specific scenes over abstract concepts
- Sensory details (what they'd see, hear, feel)
- Numbers that create images
- Analogies that translate complex to concrete

**Step 6: Memorable Phrase Crafting**
I create "sticky lines" that echo after viewing:
- Rhythmic phrasing
- Unexpected word combinations
- Parallel structure
- Quotable moments

**Step 7: Call-Forward Architecture**
I design endings that drive action:
- Clear single CTA
- Urgency/scarcity if appropriate
- Next step that's tiny and obvious
- Emotional state that supports action

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 1: Short-Form Video (60 Seconds)

**Context**: A video about why "networking" advice is wrong. Platform: TikTok/Reels. Goal: Engagement and follows.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

### CONTENT STRATEGY

**Format**: Short-form (60 seconds)
**Structure**: Hook → Counterintuitive Truth → Reframe → Punchline
**Sticky Focus**: Schema violation in opening, memorable reframe at close

---

### HOOK OPTIONS

**Option 1 (Counterintuitive)**:
"Networking doesn't work. At least not the way you're doing it."
[Direct to camera, confident delivery]

**Option 2 (Story Entry)**:
"I went to 47 networking events last year. I got zero clients."
[Slightly self-deprecating, then shift to confident]

**Option 3 (Question)**:
"How many business cards do you have that you've never followed up on?"
[Knowing look, slight pause]

---

### FULL SCRIPT (60 seconds)

---

[HOOK: 0-5 seconds]
**"Networking doesn't work."**
[Beat. Let it land.]
**"At least... not the way you're doing it."**

[PROBLEM: 5-15 seconds]
"Here's what most people do: They go to events. They collect business cards. They have surface-level conversations with 30 people. Then they go home and follow up with none of them."

[Pace picks up slightly]

"And then they wonder why 'networking' doesn't get them clients."

[REFRAME: 15-35 seconds]
[Slow down for emphasis]
"Here's what actually works:"

"Forget meeting 30 people. Meet THREE."

"Don't try to be interesting. Be INTERESTED."

"Ask questions nobody else asks. Remember details nobody else remembers."

[Building intensity]
"Then follow up. Not with 'great meeting you.' With something SPECIFIC."

"'Hey—you mentioned your daughter's soccer tournament. How'd she do?'"

[PUNCHLINE: 35-50 seconds]
[Slower, more deliberate]
"Networking isn't about meeting people. It's about being REMEMBERED by people."

[Pause]

"And you can't be remembered by everyone. So stop trying."

[CTA: 50-60 seconds]
"Focus on 3 people at the next event. Actually remember them. Watch what happens."

[Slight smile, direct eye contact]

"Follow for more that actually works."

---

### VOCAL VARIETY NOTES

| Timestamp | Pace | Intensity | Notes |
|-----------|------|-----------|-------|
| 0-5s | Medium, then pause | High confidence | Let "doesn't work" land |
| 5-15s | Faster, listing energy | Building frustration | Mimic their experience |
| 15-25s | Slower, deliberate | Calm authority | The shift to wisdom |
| 25-35s | Building again | Increasing energy | The specifics matter |
| 35-45s | Slow, emphatic | Peak intensity | The memorable line |
| 45-60s | Conversational | Warm, direct | The actionable close |

### STICKY LINES (Quotable)

1. "Networking isn't about meeting people. It's about being REMEMBERED by people."
2. "Don't try to be interesting. Be INTERESTED."
3. "You can't be remembered by everyone. So stop trying."

### B-ROLL/VISUAL SUGGESTIONS

- 0-5s: Direct to camera (hook demands eye contact)
- 5-15s: Cut to B-roll of networking event (stock), business cards
- 15-35s: Back to camera, occasional text overlays ("3 people not 30")
- 35-50s: Direct to camera, no cuts (emphasis)
- 50-60s: Camera, casual energy
