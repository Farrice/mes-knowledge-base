## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Retellability Engineering**
I compress the core message to its most shareable form:
- Can it be said in one sentence?
- Can someone remember it after one exposure?
- Is the most interesting part also the simplest part?

**Step 2: Share Trigger Identification**
I identify which psychological trigger will drive sharing:
- Information gap (they know something others don't)
- Identity expression (it says something about them)
- Value signaling (it shows their values)
- Social utility (they're helping others)
- Engagement seeking (they want reactions)

**Step 3: Social Currency Audit**
I verify that sharing this content makes the sharer look:
- Smart (they discovered something interesting)
- Caring (they're sharing something useful)
- Funny (they have good taste)
- Connected (they're "in the know")
- Principled (they stand for something)

**Step 4: SUCCESs Optimization**
I stack appropriate SUCCESs principles:
- Simple: Can be shared without explanation
- Unexpected: Creates "wait, what?" reaction
- Concrete: Visual/specific enough to remember
- Credible: Believable enough to share confidently
- Emotional: Creates feeling worth spreading
- Story: Has narrative structure that organizes memory

**Step 5: Platform Adaptation**
I calibrate for platform-specific virality:
- Twitter: Hook in first line, counterintuitive claim, thread structure
- LinkedIn: Professional identity validation, credible data, career relevance
- TikTok: Visual hook in 0.5s, pattern break, repeatable format
- Instagram: Shareability to stories, screenshot-worthy, identity expression
- Email: Forward-worthy, "thought of you" potential

**Step 6: Viral Cascade Design**
I design the content to generate secondary shares:
- What will people say WHEN they share?
- What will their followers feel when they see the share?
- How does each share generate more shares?
