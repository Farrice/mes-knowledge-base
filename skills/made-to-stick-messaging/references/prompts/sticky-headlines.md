# Sticky Headlines

Create attention-commanding headlines and hooks using SUCCESs principles.

## Role

You are engineering headlines that violate schemas, open knowledge gaps, and compel clicks without clickbait.

## Required Input

- **CONTENT/OFFER**: What the headline leads to
- **AUDIENCE**: Who you're attracting
- **PLATFORM**: Where this appears (email, social, ad, etc.)

## Execution

1. **Schema Violation Headlines**: Break expectations
   - "The [category] advice that's actually wrong"
   - "Why [common belief] doesn't work anymore"

2. **Knowledge Gap Headlines**: Create irresistible curiosity
   - "The [hidden factor] that determines [outcome]"
   - "What [successful group] knows about [topic]"

3. **Concrete Headlines**: Use specific details
   - Numbers, names, time frames
   - "How I went from $0 to $10K in 47 days"

4. **Identity Headlines**: Speak to who they are
   - "For people who [identity trait]"
   - "This is for the [type] who..."

5. **Story Headlines**: Promise narrative payoff
   - "She was about to quit. Then this happened."
   - "The day everything changed for [protagonist]"

## Output

- **20 Headline Variations**: Across all 5 types
- **Top 3 Picks**: Best headlines with rationale
- **A/B Testing Pairs**: Headlines to test against each other
- **Platform Adaptations**: Adjusted for different channels

## Example Usage

**Input**: Course about email marketing

**Schema Violation**: "Your email open rate doesn't matter. Here's what does."

**Knowledge Gap**: "The 3am trick that doubled my subscriber engagement"

**Concrete**: "How I turned a 847-person list into $47,000 in 11 days"

**Identity**: "For marketers who are tired of 'best practices' that don't work"

**Story**: "I was about to shut down my list. Then I tried one thing."
