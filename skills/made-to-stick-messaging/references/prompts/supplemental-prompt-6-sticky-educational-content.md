## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Sticky Education Formula

**TRANSFORMATIONAL LEARNING = Progressive Revelation + Emotional Investment + Concrete Application + Spaced Retrieval + Identity Integration**

### The Five Laws of Sticky Education

**LAW 1: EXPERIENCE BEFORE EXPLANATION**
Don't explain the concept, then give an example. Give the example, let them wrestle with it, then explain. The brain learns through pattern recognition, not information transfer.

**LAW 2: ONE THING AT A TIME**
Cognitive overload is the enemy. Each lesson should have ONE core concept. Not three. Not five. One. Mastered before the next.

**LAW 3: STRUGGLE IS THE SIGNAL**
If learners aren't struggling slightly, they're not learning—they're just agreeing. Design productive struggle into every module.

**LAW 4: APPLICATION WITHIN 24 HOURS**
Knowledge not applied within 24 hours decays rapidly. Every lesson must end with an immediate application exercise.

**LAW 5: IDENTITY OVER INFORMATION**
"I learned how to do X" is weak. "I am someone who does X" is strong. Education should shift identity, not just add information.

### The Module Architecture

**HOOK (5% of module)**
- Curiosity-creating question or scenario
- Why this matters to THEM specifically
- What they'll be able to DO after

**EXPERIENCE (20% of module)**
- Case study, example, or scenario they engage with
- Challenge or problem to solve
- Struggle that creates readiness for explanation

**EXPLANATION (30% of module)**
- The concept/framework/method
- Tied directly to the experience they just had
- Concrete language, minimal abstraction

**APPLICATION (35% of module)**
- Immediate exercise using the concept
- Real context from their life/business
- Produces an artifact they can use

**INTEGRATION (10% of module)**
- Connection to previous learnings
- Preview of what's next
- Identity reinforcement ("You're now someone who...")

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 1: Online Course Module

**Context**: Module 3 of a course teaching coaches how to price their services. Topic: "Value-Based Pricing." Audience: Coaches currently charging hourly rates.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

**Before This Module**:
- Thinks pricing is about their time and costs
- Feels guilty charging "too much"
- Uses hourly rates as default
- Competes on price with other coaches

**After This Module**:
- Understands pricing is about client outcomes
- Feels confident charging based on value delivered

- Competes on outcomes, not hours

---

### MODULE ARCHITECTURE

**Module 3: Value-Based Pricing**
*Duration: 45 minutes*
*Core Concept: Price based on client outcomes, not your time*

---

**SECTION 1: HOOK (3 minutes)**

[VIDEO SCRIPT]

"Let me show you something that's going to feel uncomfortable.

Here are two coaches. Both help executives with their leadership.

Coach A charges $200/hour. She works 100 hours with a client over a year. She makes $20,000.

Coach B charges $25,000 for a 6-month engagement. She works 40 hours with her client over 6 months. She makes $25,000.

Same result for the client. Same quality coaching. But Coach B makes 25% more while working 60% less.

What's the difference?

Coach A is selling her time. Coach B is selling an outcome.

By the end of this module, you're going to understand exactly how Coach B does it—and you're going to calculate a value-based price for YOUR coaching.

But first, I need you to do something uncomfortable..."

---

**SECTION 2: EXPERIENCE (10 minutes)**

**Exercise: The Client Outcome Calculation**

"Before I explain the framework, I want you to try something.

Think about your most successful client. The one who got amazing results.

Write down answers to these questions:
1. What was their situation BEFORE working with you?
2. What was their situation AFTER?
3. What specific outcomes did they achieve? (Be concrete: promotion, revenue increase, relationship improvement, etc.)
4. If you had to put a dollar value on that change in their life, what would it be?

Don't overthink it. Just estimate.

Got a number? Good.

Now here's the uncomfortable question:

How much did you charge them?

If you're like most coaches, there's a huge gap between the value they received and what you charged.

Let's talk about why that gap exists—and how to close it."

---

**SECTION 3: EXPLANATION (15 minutes)**

**The Value-Based Pricing Framework**

"Here's what's really happening when you charge hourly:

You're saying: 'My time is worth $X.'

The client hears: 'I'm buying hours of coaching.'

And then something weird happens: The faster you help them, the LESS you get paid.

Think about that. If you're so good that you can transform someone's life in 10 hours instead of 20, you make HALF as much.

Your excellence is penalized.

Value-based pricing flips this entirely.

Instead of selling your time, you're selling an outcome. And you're pricing based on what that outcome is WORTH—not how long it takes you to deliver it.

Here's the framework:

**STEP 1: Identify the Specific Outcome**
Not 'better leadership'—that's vague.
'Promoted to VP within 18 months' or 'Team engagement scores up 25%.'
The outcome needs to be specific and measurable.

**STEP 2: Calculate the Value of That Outcome**
What is that outcome worth to the client?
- Financial value (raise, revenue, savings)
- Career value (promotion, opportunity)
- Personal value (time, stress, relationships)

Be conservative. But be honest.

**STEP 3: Price at 10-20% of Value**
If the outcome is worth $100,000 to them (let's say: a promotion from Director to VP with a $40K raise, plus bonus potential, plus career acceleration)...

Then a price of $10,000-20,000 is a no-brainer for them.

They're paying $15K to get $100K+ in return.

That's not expensive. That's intelligent.

Let me show you what this looks like in practice..."

**[CASE STUDY]**

"Jennifer was a career coach charging $150/hour. She typically worked with clients for 10-15 sessions. That's $1,500-2,250 per client.

Her clients? They were getting promoted. Getting raises. Changing careers.

The OUTCOMES were worth $50,000-100,000 to her clients over time.

She was charging 2% of the value she delivered.

Here's what she changed:

Instead of offering '10 sessions of career coaching,' she started offering:

'Executive Career Acceleration: Land your next-level role within 6 months—or we keep working until you do.'

She priced it at $8,500.

What happened?

Her close rate actually WENT UP. Because now clients weren't buying 'hours'—they were buying a guaranteed outcome.

She went from $150/hour to $8,500/outcome.

Working less. Earning more. Attracting better clients who were serious about results.

That's the power of value-based pricing."

---

**SECTION 4: APPLICATION (15 minutes)**

**Exercise: Your Value-Based Price**

"Now it's your turn. We're going to calculate a value-based price for your coaching.

**Worksheet: Value-Based Pricing Calculator**

Part 1: Define Your Outcome
- What specific, measurable outcome do you help clients achieve?
- Write it as a concrete statement: 'My clients achieve [specific outcome] within [timeframe].'

Part 2: Calculate Client Value
- What is the financial value of this outcome? (income increase, revenue, savings)
- What is the career value? (promotion, opportunities, network)
- What is the personal value? (time saved, stress reduced, relationships improved)
- Total estimated value to client: $_______

Part 3: Set Your Value-Based Price
- Conservative price (10% of value): $_______
- Confident price (15% of value): $_______
- Premium price (20% of value): $_______

Part 4: Create Your Outcome Statement
- Complete this sentence: 'I help [specific client type] achieve [specific outcome] within [timeframe], guaranteed.'

Part 5: Gut Check
- Does this price feel uncomfortable? (Good—growth is uncomfortable)
- Would your best past client have paid this for the outcome they received? (Be honest)
- What would need to be true for you to feel confident charging this?

Spend the next 10 minutes completing this worksheet with YOUR coaching business.

This isn't theoretical. By the end of this exercise, you'll have your actual new price."

---

**SECTION 5: INTEGRATION (2 minutes)**

"Let's bring it together.

In Module 1, you learned that your expertise has real value—you're not just 'helping people,' you're creating measurable transformations.

In Module 2, you identified your specific zone of genius—the outcomes you're uniquely positioned to deliver.

Now, in Module 3, you've calculated what that outcome is WORTH and set a price that reflects it.

You're not someone who charges for their time anymore.

You're someone who prices based on the value you create.

That's a fundamental identity shift. And it changes everything about how you sell, how you serve, and how you feel about your work.

In Module 4, we're going to tackle the scariest part: How to actually TELL clients your new value-based price without flinching.

But first—go finish that worksheet. Calculate your new price. Feel the discomfort.

That discomfort is the feeling of growth.

See you in Module 4."

### FACILITATION GUIDE

**Module Objectives**:
- Learners understand value-based pricing conceptually
- Learners can articulate the outcome they deliver
- Learners calculate a specific value-based price for their coaching
- Learners experience productive discomfort with higher pricing

**Key Facilitation Moments**:
- After the hook: Give them time to really answer the questions
- During case study: Pause to let the numbers sink in
- During application: This is where transformation happens—don't rush

**Common Stuck Points**:
- "I can't put a dollar value on personal outcomes"
  → Guide them to indirect financial impacts (what does less stress enable?)
- "My clients would never pay that"
  → Ask: "Would your best client have paid it for the outcome they got?"
- "I feel greedy charging more"
  → Reframe: "You're not charging for your time—you're pricing the outcome fairly"
