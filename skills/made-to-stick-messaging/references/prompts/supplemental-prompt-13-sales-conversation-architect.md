## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Sales Conversation Formula

**CONVERTING CONVERSATION = Genuine Curiosity + Strategic Questions + Belief-Shifting Insights + Natural Close**

### The Five Sales Conversation Failures

**FAILURE 1: THE INTERROGATION**
Rapid-fire questions that feel like an assessment, not a conversation
*Fix*: Follow questions with insights and stories, not more questions

**FAILURE 2: THE FEATURE DUMP**
Listing everything you offer without connecting to their situation
*Fix*: Only discuss features that solve problems they've explicitly shared

**FAILURE 3: THE PREMATURE PITCH**
Starting to sell before you've created a belief shift
*Fix*: Earn the right to propose by first changing how they see the problem

**FAILURE 4: THE GENERIC DIAGNOSIS**
Same analysis for every prospect regardless of situation
*Fix*: Genuine customization based on what you've learned

**FAILURE 5: THE WEAK CLOSE**
Ending with "let me know if you have questions" or vague next steps
*Fix*: Clear recommendation with specific next action

### The Sticky Sales Conversation Architecture

**PHASE 1: CONNECTION (10% of call)**
- Establish rapport authentically
- Set expectations for the conversation
- Create safety for honesty

**PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (30% of call)**
- Understand their current situation
- Uncover the real problem beneath the stated problem
- Map their goals and constraints

**PHASE 3: INSIGHT DELIVERY (25% of call)**
- Share perspective they haven't considered
- Use stories and examples to illustrate
- Create the "aha" moment that shifts belief

**PHASE 4: SOLUTION CONNECTION (20% of call)**
- Connect your offer to their specific situation
- Only address what's relevant to them
- Prove you can deliver with concrete evidence

**PHASE 5: COMMITMENT (15% of call)**
- Clear recommendation
- Address hesitation directly
- Specific next step with timeline

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Buyer Psychology Mapping**
I identify what the buyer typically believes before the call:
- About their problem
- About potential solutions
- About you/your type of service
- About themselves and their situation

**Step 2: Belief Shift Design**
I determine what beliefs must change for them to buy:
- What misunderstanding needs correcting?
- What hidden aspect of their problem needs revealing?
- What possibility needs to become visible?

**Step 3: Question Sequencing**
I design questions that lead to discovery:
- Opening questions that create safety
- Deepening questions that reveal true situation
- Clarifying questions that show you're listening
- Insight-setup questions that prepare for the shift

**Step 4: Insight Preparation**
I develop sticky insights for common situations:
- Schema violations that challenge their assumptions
- Stories that illustrate key points
- Data/proof that creates credibility
- Frameworks that organize their thinking

**Step 5: Transition Architecture**
I design natural transitions between phases:
- Discovery to insight
- Insight to solution
- Solution to commitment

**Step 6: Objection Preparation**
I prepare sticky responses to common objections:
- Reframes that change perspective
- Stories that address concerns
- Questions that reveal the real issue

**Step 7: Close Design**
I create closing approaches that feel natural:
- Clear recommendation language
- Hesitation-addressing questions
- Next step specificity

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 1: Consulting/Coaching Sales Conversation

**Context**: A business coach selling a $5,000 three-month coaching package. Target: Business owners at $200K-500K revenue feeling stuck. Call length: 45 minutes.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

### BUYER BELIEF MAP

**What They Believe BEFORE the Call:**

| Topic | Current Belief |
|-------|---------------|
| Their problem | "I need more clients/revenue/time" |
| Why they're stuck | "I'm not doing enough marketing/sales/the right tactics" |
| What they need | "Better strategies, a system, accountability" |
| About coaching | "It might help, but it's expensive and I'm not sure it's for me" |
| About themselves | "I'm capable but missing something" |

**What They Need to Believe AFTER the Call:**

| Topic | New Belief |
|-------|-----------|
| Their problem | "The real issue is [specific insight], not what I thought" |
| Why they're stuck | "I've been solving the wrong problem" |
| What they need | "I need someone to help me see what I can't see" |
| About coaching | "This specific coach understands my situation" |
| About themselves | "I'm closer to a breakthrough than I realized" |

---

### CONVERSATION FLOW

**PHASE 1: CONNECTION (5 minutes)**

*Goal*: Create safety, establish you're different from typical sales calls

**Opening:**
"Thanks for taking the time. Before we dive in, I want to be clear about what this call is and isn't. This isn't a pitch. I genuinely want to understand what's going on in your business and see if I can be helpful—whether that's working together or pointing you in another direction. Sound okay?"

**Rapport Questions:**
- "Tell me a bit about your business—what do you do and who do you serve?"
- "What's going well right now?" (Start positive, creates safety)

---

**PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (15 minutes)**

*Goal*: Understand the real situation beneath the stated problem

**Opening the Discovery:**
"So what prompted you to book this call? What's going on that made you think coaching might help?"

[Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take notes.]

**Deepening Questions:**
- "When you say [their word], what does that look like day-to-day?"
- "How long has this been the situation?"
- "What have you tried so far to address this?"
- "What happened with those attempts?"

**Getting to the Real Problem:**
- "If you solved [stated problem], what would that actually give you?"
- "What would be different in your life if this wasn't an issue?"
- "What's this costing you? Not just money—energy, time, relationships?"

**Clarifying:**
- "Let me make sure I understand..." [reflect back what you heard]
- "Is that accurate, or am I missing something?"

---

**PHASE 3: INSIGHT DELIVERY (10 minutes)**

*Goal*: Help them see their situation differently

**Transition:**
"Can I share an observation? Based on what you've told me, I think I see what might be happening here."

**The Insight:**
"You mentioned you've tried [their attempts]—more marketing, new offers, working harder. And nothing's really moved the needle.

Here's what I'm noticing: You're trying to solve a growth problem with tactics. But from what you've described, you don't have a tactics problem. You have a [positioning/pricing/focus/confidence] problem.

Let me explain what I mean..."

**Supporting Story:**
"This reminds me of a client I worked with, [Name]. She came to me saying she needed more leads. We spent our first session together not talking about marketing at all—we talked about who she was actually for and what she was actually selling.

Within three weeks, she raised her prices 40% and started turning away clients who weren't a fit. Her revenue went up even though her client count went down.

The problem was never leads. The problem was that she was attracting the wrong people at the wrong price because she hadn't done the foundational work.

Based on what you've shared, I wonder if something similar might be happening for you."

**Schema Violation:**
"Most people at your stage think they need MORE—more leads, more tactics, more hustle. But the successful business owners I work with? They usually need LESS—less distraction, less serving the wrong clients, less doing things that don't actually move the needle.

What would happen if instead of adding more, you subtracted the things that aren't working?"

**Check-In:**
"Does that resonate at all? Or am I off base?"

---

**PHASE 4: SOLUTION CONNECTION (10 minutes)**

*Goal*: Connect your offer to their specific situation

**Transition:**
"So let me tell you how I might be able to help, and you can tell me if it sounds relevant to what you're dealing with."

**Customized Offer:**
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I think you need:

First, you need clarity on [their specific issue]. Not generic business advice—specific guidance on your situation.

Second, you need someone who can see what you can't see. When you're inside your business, it's almost impossible to spot the patterns that are holding you back. That's not a weakness—it's just how brains work.

Third, you need accountability to actually implement, not just plan.

That's what I do in my coaching. We meet every two weeks for three months. The first session is diagnostic—we map out exactly what's happening and why. Then we work through it systematically.

By the end of three months, you'll have [specific outcome relevant to their stated goals]."

**Proof:**
"I've done this with 40+ business owners at your stage. The pattern I see: within the first month, they have a breakthrough on something they've been stuck on for months or years. By month three, they've usually made back the investment in new revenue or saved time.

[Specific example relevant to their situation]"

---

**PHASE 5: COMMITMENT (5 minutes)**

*Goal*: Clear recommendation and specific next step

**The Ask:**
"Based on our conversation, I think I could help you. Here's what I'd recommend:

Let's work together for three months. We start with a deep-dive session where I learn everything about your business and you get a clear action plan. Then we meet every two weeks to implement and adjust.

The investment is $5,000 for the full three months. You can pay upfront or in three monthly payments.

What questions do you have?"

**If They're Hesitant:**
"I'm hearing some hesitation. Can you tell me what's on your mind?"

[Let them share. Address the real concern.]

**If It's About Money:**
"I get it—$5,000 is real money. Let me ask you this: What's it costing you to stay stuck? Not just in revenue, but in time, stress, opportunity cost?

If we work together and you get unstuck, what's that worth over the next year? Over the next five years?

I'm not trying to pressure you. I just want to make sure you're comparing the investment to the right thing."

**If It's About Timing:**
"When would be the right time? Here's what I've noticed: the right time usually isn't when everything calms down. The right time is when the pain of staying stuck outweighs the discomfort of changing.

Is that now, or is this something for later?"

**If They're Ready:**
"Great. Here's what happens next: I'll send you a welcome email with a scheduling link for our first session, plus a questionnaire I'd like you to fill out before we meet. That way we can hit the ground running.

Any questions before we wrap up?"

**Discovery Questions:**
| Phase | Question | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What prompted you to book this call?" | Understand their trigger |

**Insight-Setup Questions:**
| Question | Sets Up |
|----------|---------|

### OBJECTION RESPONSES

**"I need to think about it"**

"That makes sense. Can I ask—what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, whether this is the right approach, or something else?"

[Then address the specific concern]

**"It's expensive"**

"I hear you. Let me make sure we're comparing apples to apples. You mentioned [their cost of staying stuck—revenue, time, stress]. Over the next year, what does that add up to?

If we solve this in three months, and you get [specific outcome], what's that worth?

I'm not trying to hard-sell you. I just want to make sure you're seeing the full picture."

**"I've tried coaching before and it didn't work"**

"Tell me about that. What happened?"

[Listen. Then differentiate.]

"It sounds like [diagnosis of why it failed]. Here's how what I do is different: [specific differentiation]. Does that address the concern?"

**"I don't have time right now"**

"That's exactly why you need this. You don't have a time problem—you have a priority and systems problem. If you had clarity on what actually matters and what you could stop doing, you'd have plenty of time.

But I want to be honest: this does require investment. It's two hours every two weeks, plus implementation time. If that's genuinely impossible right now, it's not the right time.

Is it genuinely impossible, or is it more that you're not sure it's worth it?"
