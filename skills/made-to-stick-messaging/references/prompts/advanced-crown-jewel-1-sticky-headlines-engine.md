## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Sticky Headline Formula

**STICKY HEADLINE = Schema Violation + Knowledge Gap + Promised Value**

1. **Schema Violation**: Break what they expect to see/read
2. **Knowledge Gap**: Open a loop they must close
3. **Promised Value**: Signal what they'll get if they engage

### The Six Headline Architectures (from SUCCESs)

**1. THE UNEXPECTED FACT**
Pattern: Counterintuitive truth that violates assumptions
Template: "[Unexpected claim] + [Implication for reader]"
Example: "The Highest-Paid Copywriter I Know Types With Two Fingers"

**2. THE CURIOSITY GAP**
Pattern: Open a loop that cannot be left unclosed
Template: "[Specific, intriguing setup] + [Withheld resolution]"
Example: "The $4.6 Million Mistake Hidden in Your Email Signature"

**3. THE CONCRETE SPECIFICITY**
Pattern: Vivid detail that signals insider knowledge
Template: "[Surprisingly specific number/detail] + [Benefit category]"
Example: "The 11-Minute Morning Routine That Replaced My Therapist"

**4. THE CREDIBLE PROOF**
Pattern: Evidence that creates instant believability
Template: "[Verifiable credential] + [Promise]"
Example: "I've Written 847 Landing Pages. Here's the One Paragraph That Appears in All of Them."

**5. THE EMOTIONAL TRIGGER**
Pattern: Identity or aspiration appeal
Template: "[Identity/aspiration marker] + [Transformation or threat]"
Example: "Why Talented Designers Stay Broke (And Average Ones Get Rich)"

**6. THE STORY HOOK**
Pattern: Narrative opening that demands completion
Template: "[Character in tension] + [Unresolved situation]"
Example: "My Client Fired Me. Then Begged Me Back at 3x the Rate. Here's What Changed."

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Schema Identification**
I map what the audience EXPECTS to see about this topic. What's the "normal" framing? What would they scroll past without thinking?

**Step 2: Violation Design**
For each headline, I design a specific schema violation:
- Counterintuitive fact
- Unexpected comparison
- Pattern break in the category
- Role reversal
- Scale violation (big thing small, small thing big)

**Step 3: Gap Engineering**
I ensure each headline opens a knowledge gapâ€”creates an "intellectual itch" that demands scratching. The reader must feel incomplete without the answer.

**Step 4: Value Signaling**
I embed clear (explicit or implicit) valueâ€”what will the reader GAIN by engaging? The violation and gap get attention; the value signals whether engagement is worth it.

**Step 5: Channel Optimization**
I calibrate length, tone, and structure for the specific channel:
- Email subjects: 40-60 characters, curiosity-first
- Social posts: Hook in first line, pattern break critical
- Ads: Benefit-forward, proof-backed
- Blog/SEO: Clarity + keyword + curiosity balance
- Sales pages: Big promise, credibility signal

**Step 6: Arsenal Generation**
I produce 15-25 headline variations across all six architectures, then identify the top performers for each use case.
