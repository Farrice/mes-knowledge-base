## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Sales Narrative Formula

**CONVERTING NARRATIVE = Empathy → Problem Escalation → Belief Shift → Solution Bridge → Transformation Vision → Risk Elimination → Action Trigger**

### The Seven-Part Sales Narrative Architecture

**PART 1: EMPATHY ENTRY (30 seconds)**
*"I see you. I understand your world."*
- Enter the prospect's private conversation
- Demonstrate understanding of their specific situation
- Use their language, not yours
- Create "this is for me" recognition

**PART 2: PROBLEM ESCALATION (60 seconds)**
*"Let me show you how bad this really is."*
- Surface pain → Functional pain → Emotional pain → Identity pain
- Make the problem feel urgent and costly
- Reveal hidden consequences they haven't considered
- Create discomfort with status quo

**PART 3: BELIEF SHIFT (60 seconds)**
*"What you think is true... isn't."*
- Schema violation: Break their current belief about the solution
- Explain WHY their previous attempts failed
- Introduce a new framework for understanding the problem
- Create the "aha" moment

**PART 4: SOLUTION BRIDGE (90 seconds)**
*"Here's what actually works, and why."*
- Introduce your solution as the logical response to the belief shift
- Explain mechanism (why it works, not just what it does)
- Stack credibility: authority + antiauthority + vivid details + testable credentials
- Make the solution feel inevitable given the new belief

**PART 5: TRANSFORMATION VISION (60 seconds)**
*"Here's your life after."*
- Paint specific, concrete picture of life post-purchase
- Use Individual Zoom—specific moments, not vague outcomes
- Connect to identity (who they become, not just what they get)
- Make the transformation feel real and achievable

**PART 6: RISK ELIMINATION (30 seconds)**
*"You can't lose."*
- Anticipate and address objections
- Reverse the risk (guarantee, trial, proof)
- Make inaction feel riskier than action
- Remove all friction to "yes"

**PART 7: ACTION TRIGGER (15 seconds)**
*"Here's what to do right now."*
- Single, clear call to action
- Create urgency (why now?)
- Make the next step tiny and obvious
- Reinforce what they're getting
