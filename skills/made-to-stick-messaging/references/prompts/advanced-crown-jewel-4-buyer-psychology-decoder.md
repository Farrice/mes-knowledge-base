## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Buyer Psychology Formula

**DECODED BUYER = Schema Map + Pain Hierarchy + Decision Triggers + Language Extraction**

1. **Schema Map**: What do they currently believe about this problem/solution?
2. **Pain Hierarchy**: Which pains are surface vs. deep vs. identity-level?
3. **Decision Triggers**: What makes them act NOW vs. later vs. never?
4. **Language Extraction**: What words do THEY use (not industry jargon)?

### The Four Layers of Buyer Understanding

**LAYER 1: SURFACE PAIN (What They Say)**
- The problem they'll admit to publicly
- The symptom they're trying to fix
- The language they use in search bars
- Example: "I need help with time management"

**LAYER 2: FUNCTIONAL PAIN (What They Actually Experience)**
- The day-to-day frustration
- The concrete situation causing problems
- What they'd describe to a trusted friend
- Example: "I'm working until midnight and still behind"

**LAYER 3: EMOTIONAL PAIN (What They Feel)**
- The fear, anxiety, frustration, shame underneath
- What they wouldn't admit publicly
- The feeling that keeps them up at night
- Example: "I'm terrified I'm going to burn out and lose everything"

**LAYER 4: IDENTITY PAIN (Who They're Afraid of Becoming)**
- The version of themselves they're fighting against
- The failure narrative they're trying to avoid
- The identity threat that creates urgency
- Example: "I don't want to be the parent who missed their kid's childhood for work"

### The Schema Violation Opportunity Map

For each buyer belief, identify:
- **Held Belief**: What they currently think is true
- **Actual Truth**: What's actually true (counterintuitive)
- **Violation Power**: How surprising is the gap? (1-10)
- **Message Opportunity**: How to use this gap for attention

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Individual Construction**
I create 2-3 specific individuals representing the target audience—not personas with demographics, but PEOPLE with names, situations, internal monologues, and specific problems. I make the abstract concrete.

**Step 2: Schema Mapping**
For each individual, I map their current beliefs about:
- The problem they have
- The cause of the problem
- The available solutions
- Why they haven't solved it yet
- What "success" looks like

**Step 3: Pain Hierarchy Excavation**
I dig through all four layers:
- Surface: What they'd tell a stranger
- Functional: What they'd tell a friend
- Emotional: What they'd tell a therapist
- Identity: What they'd barely admit to themselves

**Step 4: Decision Trigger Identification**
I identify what moves them from "interested" to "action":
- Urgency triggers (why now?)
- Safety triggers (why this is low risk?)
- Social triggers (who else has done this?)
- Identity triggers (what kind of person does this?)

**Step 5: Language Extraction**
I document the EXACT words and phrases they use—not industry terms, not marketing language, but their actual vocabulary when describing their problem and desires.

**Step 6: Messaging Intelligence Synthesis**
I translate all findings into actionable messaging recommendations:
- Headlines that violate their schemas
- Copy that speaks to Layer 3-4 pain
- Proof that addresses their specific objections
- CTAs that trigger decision

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 1: B2B SaaS (HR Software for Startups)

**Context**: An HR software company targeting startup founders (10-50 employees) who are doing HR tasks themselves. The software automates onboarding, payroll, and compliance. Competitors: Gusto, Rippling, Deel.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

### INDIVIDUAL PROFILES

**INDIVIDUAL 1: Marcus, 34 - Founder/CEO, Series A Startup**

Marcus raised $4M eight months ago. He has 27 employees. He still does HR because hiring an HR person feels like "overhead" when they need to ship product.

**His week**: Monday he spent 2 hours setting up a new hire's laptop, email, and Slack access. Wednesday he realized the new hire's offer letter had the wrong equity amount. Thursday he was on a call with their lawyer about a former employee claiming unpaid overtime. Friday he missed his daughter's soccer game because he was doing payroll.

**Internal monologue**: "I didn't raise $4 million to become an HR administrator. But I can't justify the cost of a real HR person yet. I just need to get through this phase. Maybe I can hire a part-time person... but then I have to manage them too. I'm so tired of this."

**What he searches**: "startup HR checklist," "how to do payroll California startup," "do I need an HR person"

---

**INDIVIDUAL 2: Sarah, 29 - COO, 40-Person Tech Company**

Sarah is the "figure it out" person. HR landed on her desk because nobody else would do it. She's not bad at it, but it's not what she wants to be doing. She's already using three different tools that don't talk to each other.

**Her week**: She spent Tuesday manually updating the org chart because they promoted two people. She spent Wednesday chasing down signatures on compliance documents. Thursday she discovered someone's health insurance was never set up—three months after they started.

**Internal monologue**: "I have actual strategic work to do. Why am I spending 10 hours a week on administrative HR tasks? I keep putting together business cases for HR software but it always falls off the priority list. Leadership doesn't see the problem because they're not the ones fixing it."

**What she searches**: "best HR software for startups under 50 employees," "HR software comparison 2024," "Gusto vs Rippling"

---

### SCHEMA MAP

**Current Beliefs About the Problem**:
| Belief | Accuracy | Opportunity |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| "HR is overhead, not strategic" | Partially true | Reframe: Bad HR is overhead; good HR is leverage |
| "We're too small to need real HR software" | False | They're exactly the size where manual HR breaks |
| "I can handle it until we're bigger" | Dangerous | The mistakes made now create liabilities later |
| "HR software is expensive" | Outdated | Modern tools are cheaper than their time |
| "Setting up new software takes forever" | Sometimes true | Our setup is 15 minutes (testable credential) |

**Current Beliefs About Available Solutions**:
| Belief | Accuracy | Opportunity |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| "Gusto is the default choice" | Perception true | "Default" ≠ best; create contrast |
| "All HR tools are basically the same" | False | Feature parity exists; experience differs |
| "Switching would be painful" | Fear-based | Show migration stories that contradict this |
| "I need to evaluate everything before deciding" | Stalling tactic | Create urgency through cost-of-delay |

---

### PAIN HIERARCHY

**LAYER 1 - SURFACE PAIN (What They Say)**:
- "We need to streamline our HR processes"
- "I'm looking for an HR solution for our growing team"
- "We want to automate onboarding"

**LAYER 2 - FUNCTIONAL PAIN (What They Experience)**:
- "I spent 4 hours this week on new hire paperwork"
- "Someone's paycheck was wrong again"
- "I forgot to file the compliance thing and now we might get fined"
- "I have no idea if we're doing any of this right legally"

**LAYER 3 - EMOTIONAL PAIN (What They Feel)**:
- "I'm terrified we're going to get sued over something I don't know about"
- "I feel like a fraud pretending I know how to do HR"
- "I'm resentful that this keeps falling on me"
- "I'm embarrassed that a company our size still does this manually"

**LAYER 4 - IDENTITY PAIN (Who They're Afraid of Becoming)**:
- "The founder who failed because of an HR lawsuit, not a product problem"
- "The COO who's stuck in administrative work while others do strategic work"
- "The leader who burned out on stuff that shouldn't have been their job"
- "The company that lost great people because onboarding was chaotic"

---

### SCHEMA VIOLATION OPPORTUNITIES

| Held Belief | Actual Truth | Violation Power | Message Opportunity |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| "HR mistakes are embarrassing but not serious" | One misclassified employee can cost $50K+ in penalties | 9/10 | Fear-based urgency without manipulation |
| "I'll deal with HR when we're bigger" | HR problems compound—fixing at 50 employees costs 3x vs. fixing at 25 | 8/10 | "The longer you wait, the more expensive it gets" |
| "HR software setup takes weeks" | Full setup in 15 minutes (our actual time) | 7/10 | Testable credential that breaks objection |
| "Doing it myself saves money" | Founder time on HR = $500/hr+ opportunity cost | 8/10 | "You can't afford to do this yourself" |

---

### DECISION TRIGGER ANALYSIS

**What Makes Them Act NOW**:
- A compliance scare (letter from state, audit threat)
- A painful mistake (wrong paycheck, benefits error)
- An embarrassing moment (candidate had bad onboarding experience)
- A visible comparison (saw how competitor does it)
- A time crisis (finally realized how many hours they're losing)

**What Makes Them Act LATER (Stall)**:
- "We're about to hire someone who can handle this"
- "Once we close this round, we'll have budget"
- "We're in the middle of too many changes right now"

**How to Collapse "Later" Into "Now"**:
- Cost calculator showing hours lost per month (escalating)
- Compliance risk assessment showing exposure (fear-based urgency)
- 15-minute setup offer (remove friction objection)

---

### LANGUAGE BANK

**Their Words (Use These)**:
- "Streamline," "automate," "one place"
- "Takes forever," "manual," "spreadsheet"
- "Compliance," "legal," "get in trouble"
- "Growing team," "scaling," "hiring fast"
- "My time," "too much," "shouldn't be doing this"

**Industry Words (Avoid)**:
- "Human capital management"
- "Workforce solutions"
- "Employee lifecycle"
- "Talent optimization"
- "People operations platform"

---

### OBJECTION MAP

| Objection | Real Concern Underneath | How to Address |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| "We're happy with Gusto" | Switching is scary | Migration stories; "most of our customers switched from Gusto" |
| "Seems expensive" | Uncertain about ROI | Time-cost calculator; "your time costs $X/hr" |
| "We're too busy to set up something new" | Fear of implementation pain | "15-minute setup" as testable credential |
| "I need to loop in my co-founder" | Not ready to decide | Give them ammunition: ROI calculator, comparison doc |
| "We'll look at this next quarter" | Not urgent enough | Compliance risk calculator; "every month costs X" |

---

### MESSAGING RECOMMENDATIONS

**Headline Direction**:
- Lead with time recapture: "Stop Being Your Startup's HR Department"
- Fear-based variant: "The $50K HR Mistake 73% of Startups Make"
- Proof-based variant: "847 Startups Switched from Doing HR Themselves Last Month"

**Copy Direction**:
- Speak to Layer 3-4 pain: "You didn't raise money to become an HR administrator"
- Address hidden fear: "One misclassified contractor can cost you $50,000. Here's how to know if you're at risk."
- Identity appeal: "Founders who get this right hire faster and keep people longer"

**CTA Direction**:
- Remove friction: "Set up in 15 minutes—no credit card, no sales call"
- Create urgency: "See your compliance risk score before it's too late"

### "PRIVATE CONVERSATION" SCRIPT

*The internal monologue you're entering:*

"God, I have so much to do today and I'm still dealing with this HR stuff. I can't believe I spent two hours yesterday setting up one new hire. This is ridiculous—I have a real job. I should just hire someone to do this, but that's another person to manage, another salary, and honestly I don't even know what 'good HR' looks like at our stage.

I keep hearing about these tools—Gusto, Rippling, whatever—but they all look the same and I don't have time to evaluate them. Plus, setting up something new sounds like another project I don't have bandwidth for.

But something has to change. Last week I found out we'd been doing the 1099 thing wrong for six months. I have no idea what that's going to cost us. And our last two hires both had a bad first week because I was too busy to set up their accounts properly. One of them almost quit.

I just want this to not be my problem anymore. I want to click a button and have it handled. Is that too much to ask?"

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 2: D2C (Premium Sleep Supplement)

**Context**: A supplement company selling a premium magnesium sleep formula ($45/bottle) targeting professionals aged 30-50 who struggle with sleep but are skeptical of sleep supplements. They've tried melatonin and it didn't work or gave them grogginess.

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

### INDIVIDUAL PROFILES

**INDIVIDUAL 1: Jennifer, 42 - VP of Marketing, Mother of Two**

Jennifer wakes up at 5am for work, is "on" until 9pm when the kids are in bed, then can't fall asleep until midnight. She lies awake thinking about tomorrow's presentation, her son's school situation, whether she's being a good enough mother.

**Her night**: She watches TV to "wind down" (makes it worse). She takes melatonin (feels groggy, doesn't help her stay asleep). She tried CBD (did nothing). She drinks chamomile tea (placebo at best). She scrolls her phone until she can't keep her eyes open (knows this is bad).

**Internal monologue**: "I know I need to sleep better—everyone tells me. I've tried everything that doesn't require a prescription. Nothing works, or it works for a week and then stops. I'm so tired of being tired. But I'm also tired of being sold 'miracle sleep solutions' that don't do anything. The last supplement I bought cost $50 and sits unused in my cabinet."

**What she searches**: "why can't I stay asleep," "melatonin doesn't work for me," "natural sleep remedies that actually work," "sleep supplements without melatonin"

---

**INDIVIDUAL 2: David, 38 - Entrepreneur, Self-Described "Optimizer"**

David tracks everything—sleep score, HRV, steps, macros. He knows his sleep sucks. He's experimented with sleep hygiene, blue light blockers, temperature control. His Oura ring tells him his deep sleep is 45 minutes when it should be 90+.

**His night**: He takes his stack (ashwagandha, L-theanine, sometimes melatonin). His routine is dialed. He still wakes up at 3am and lies there for an hour. His mind won't stop problem-solving. He's considering a sleep study or prescription sleep aids but doesn't want to go that route yet.

**Internal monologue**: "I've optimized everything else but I can't crack sleep. I've read the studies—I know magnesium has evidence behind it. But I've tried magnesium before and didn't notice anything. Maybe it was the wrong form? I'm skeptical of supplements generally—most of them are underdosed garbage with exaggerated claims. But I also know that being at 70% cognitive capacity because of sleep deprivation is costing me more than any supplement would."

**What he searches**: "magnesium glycinate vs citrate for sleep," "how much magnesium for sleep," "best form of magnesium for deep sleep," "magnesium absorption rates"

---

### SCHEMA MAP

**Current Beliefs About the Problem**:
| Belief | Accuracy | Opportunity |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| "I've tried everything" | Probably false—haven't tried right things | "You haven't tried the right form of magnesium" |
| "Supplements don't really work" | Depends entirely on the supplement | Acknowledge skepticism, provide evidence |
| "I just have to accept bad sleep" | False and dangerous | Show what's possible |
| "My brain won't shut off—that's the problem" | Partially true | Magnesium affects nervous system regulation |

**Current Beliefs About Sleep Supplements**:
| Belief | Accuracy | Opportunity |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| "Melatonin is the only option" | False | Explain why melatonin fails for many people |
| "Natural supplements are too weak to work" | Depends on dose and form | Highlight clinical doses and bioavailability |
| "I'll feel groggy in the morning" | True for melatonin, not magnesium | Differentiate mechanism |
| "It's all marketing hype" | Often true | Lean into transparency and evidence |

---

### PAIN HIERARCHY

**LAYER 1 - SURFACE PAIN (What They Say)**:
- "I'm looking for a natural sleep aid"
- "I need something to help me sleep"
- "Melatonin isn't working for me"

**LAYER 2 - FUNCTIONAL PAIN (What They Experience)**:
- "I fall asleep but wake up at 3am and can't get back to sleep"
- "I spend an hour in bed with my mind racing"
- "I never feel rested no matter how much I sleep"
- "I need coffee to function and then can't sleep because of coffee"

**LAYER 3 - EMOTIONAL PAIN (What They Feel)**:
- "I'm scared my health is deteriorating because of sleep deprivation"
- "I feel like a failure because I can't do something as basic as sleep"
- "I'm jealous of people who just fall asleep easily"
- "I'm frustrated that I've spent hundreds of dollars on things that don't work"

**LAYER 4 - IDENTITY PAIN (Who They're Afraid of Becoming)**:
- "The person who's always tired, always foggy, always behind"
- "The parent who's too exhausted to be present with their kids"
- "The professional who makes a big mistake because of sleep deprivation"
- "The health-conscious person who can't figure out basic health"

---

### SCHEMA VIOLATION OPPORTUNITIES

| Held Belief | Actual Truth | Violation Power | Message Opportunity |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| "Magnesium is magnesium" | Absorption varies from 4% to 80% depending on form | 9/10 | Most people take the wrong kind |
| "Melatonin is how you fix sleep" | Melatonin is for jet lag; doesn't address why you can't sleep | 8/10 | "Melatonin treats a symptom, not the cause" |
| "Sleep supplements make you groggy" | Magnesium has no hangover effect | 7/10 | "Wake up clear, not foggy" |
| "I've tried magnesium; it didn't work" | Probably took magnesium oxide (4% absorption) | 9/10 | "You didn't try magnesium—you tried chalk" |
| "50% of people are deficient" | Actually 50%+ ARE deficient and don't know | 6/10 | "Your sleep problem might be a deficiency" |
