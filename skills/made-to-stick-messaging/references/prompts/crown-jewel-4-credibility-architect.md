## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Claim Audit**
I assess the claim for believability risk. What makes this hard to believe? Where will skeptics attack? What objections will arise before the audience even finishes hearing the claim?

**Step 2: Credibility Source Inventory**
I identify all available credibility sources across five categories:

**External Credibility:**
- Authorities (experts, institutions, credentials)
- Antiauthorities (real people with lived experience who are MORE credible than experts)
- Third-party validation (media mentions, awards, certifications)

**Internal Credibility:**
- Vivid details that signal authenticity (the Darth Vader toothbrush)
- Specificity that indicates real knowledge
- Human-scale statistics that create visceral understanding

**Testable Credibility:**
- Claims the audience can verify themselves
- Predictions they can check
- Demonstrations they can experience

**Step 3: Architecture Design**
I design a credibility structure that layers multiple sources, ensuring:
- No single point of failure
- Multiple pathways to belief
- Self-reinforcing elements
- Escalating proof (easy beliefs → harder beliefs)

**Step 4: Human-Scale Translation**
I convert any statistics or data into comparisons that create visceral, intuitive understanding. No number stands alone—every statistic becomes human.

**Step 5: Testable Credential Design**
I create ways for the audience to verify claims through their own experience—predictions they can check, demonstrations they can try, evidence they can see.

**Step 6: Credibility Sequence**
I order the credibility elements strategically—leading with what's most immediately convincing, building toward what requires more belief.

### THE CREDIBLE MESSAGE

Marcus told us: 'I thought we were slow because of engineering capacity. Turns out we were slow because nobody knew who was supposed to decide if the button should be blue or green.'"

Connect our software to your team's Slack for five minutes—free, no credit card, no sales call. We'll show you exactly how many dead threads you have RIGHT NOW. You'll see the decisions that are stuck, who's waiting on them, and how long they've been waiting.

If that number is zero, we'll admit we're not for you.

If that number is embarrassing—which it is for 94% of teams who try this—you'll understand exactly where your 40% is hiding.

The problem with invisible friction is that you don't know it's there until it's gone. You've adapted to it. You think 'this is just how projects work.'

The dead thread audit shows you friction you forgot you were stepping over. Whether you use our software or not, you should see that number."

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 2: Health Supplement Credibility

**Context**: A company sells a magnesium supplement claiming to improve sleep quality. The audience is health-conscious but skeptical of supplement marketing ("they all claim to be miracle cures"). Claim: "82% of our customers report better sleep within two weeks."

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

---

### CLAIM RISK ASSESSMENT

**The Claim**: "82% of customers report better sleep within two weeks."

**Why It's Hard to Believe**:
- Supplements are notorious for inflated claims
- "Customer reports" sounds like cherry-picked testimonials
- Placebo effect is real for sleep products
- 82% seems suspiciously high
- "Better sleep" is vague and subjective

**Where Skeptics Will Attack**:
- "Who conducted this survey—you?"
- "How do you define 'better sleep'?"
- "What about the 18% who didn't see results?"
- "Is this placebo effect?"
- "How is your magnesium different from the $8 bottle at CVS?"

---

### CREDIBILITY BLUEPRINT

**Layer 1 - External**: Independent lab verification + clinical study reference
**Layer 2 - Antiauthority**: Sleep-deprived skeptic who changed her mind
**Layer 3 - Internal**: Specific mechanism explanation + bioavailability data
**Layer 4 - Testable**: Sleep tracking challenge + 30-day guarantee

---

### THE CREDIBLE MESSAGE

**Opening (Antiauthority):**

"Dr. Sarah Patel is an emergency room physician in Chicago. She works overnight shifts and has tried every sleep supplement on the market.

'I was the biggest skeptic,' she told us. 'I've seen patients come in taking seventeen supplements that do absolutely nothing. I assumed magnesium was the same.'

She agreed to try ours as a favor to her sister, who wouldn't stop talking about it.

'The first week, nothing. I almost quit. Week two, I noticed I wasn't waking up at 3am anymore. By week three, I was falling asleep without my phone, which hasn't happened in years.'

Sarah still recommends nothing to her patients—she's appropriately cautious. But she takes our magnesium every night."

**The Mechanism (Internal Credibility):**

"Here's the thing about magnesium and sleep: The science is real. Over 300 studies show magnesium's role in sleep regulation. The problem isn't whether magnesium helps—it's whether the magnesium actually gets where it needs to go.

Most magnesium supplements use magnesium oxide. It's cheap. It's what you find at CVS. And your body absorbs about 4% of it. You're essentially paying for expensive urine.

We use magnesium glycinate. Absorption rate: 80-90%. It crosses the blood-brain barrier because it's bonded to glycine—an amino acid that itself has calming effects.

This isn't marketing. This is basic biochemistry. You can Google 'magnesium glycinate vs oxide absorption' right now and read the studies yourself."

**The Data (External + Human-Scale):**

"We surveyed 2,847 customers after 30 days of use. The survey was conducted by a third-party research firm (Method Research, you can verify they exist). We had no involvement in the data collection.

Results:
- 82% reported improved sleep quality
- 67% reported falling asleep faster (median: 15 minutes faster)
- 71% reported fewer nighttime awakenings

What about the 18%? We analyzed them. Most fell into three categories:
1. Already had healthy magnesium levels (about 8%)
2. Had underlying sleep disorders requiring medical treatment (about 6%)
3. Didn't take it consistently (about 4%)

We don't claim this works for everyone. If you have sleep apnea, you need a doctor, not a supplement. If you already get enough magnesium from your diet, you won't notice much difference. But if you're part of the 50% of Americans who are magnesium deficient—and most people don't know they are—this can change your sleep."

**The Testable Credential:**

"Here's how to know if this will work for you—before you spend money:

Track your sleep for one week using any free app. Note your average time to fall asleep, how often you wake up, and your subjective quality score.

Then take our magnesium for two weeks. Track again.

If your numbers don't improve, email us. We'll refund you AND let you keep the bottle. We've refunded 7% of customers. We're fine with that—we only want customers who get results.

The 93% who don't request refunds? They don't request them because they're asleep."

**The Vivid Detail (Internal Credibility):**

"One more thing. When you open the bottle, the capsules are smaller than you'd expect. We get asked about this constantly.

Most supplement companies pack giant horse pills to look more impressive. We use small capsules because magnesium glycinate is more concentrated—you need less of it to get the same effect. Smaller pill, better absorption, easier to swallow.

It's a tiny detail that tells you something about how we think."
