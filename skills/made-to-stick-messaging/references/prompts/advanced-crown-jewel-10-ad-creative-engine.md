## CORE METHODOLOGY

### The Ad Creative Formula

**CONVERTING AD = Scroll-Stop Hook + Pattern Break + Single Message + Clear CTA**

### The Three-Second Test

Every ad must pass: In the first 3 seconds (or first visual scan), does this:
1. **Stop the scroll?** (Pattern break achieved)
2. **Create curiosity?** (Gap opened)
3. **Signal relevance?** (Right audience recognizes themselves)

If no â†’ Ad fails before the message is even delivered.

### The Five Ad Architectures

**ARCHITECTURE 1: THE COUNTERINTUITIVE CLAIM**
*Pattern*: Lead with unexpected truth that violates assumptions
*Example*: "The highest-paid designers I know have the worst portfolios"
*Best for*: Awareness, authority building, warm audiences

**ARCHITECTURE 2: THE SPECIFIC PROOF**
*Pattern*: Lead with concrete result that creates credibility
*Example*: "847 subscribers in 90 days. Here's the exact system."
*Best for*: Conversion, retargeting, proof-hungry audiences

**ARCHITECTURE 3: THE ENEMY CALL-OUT**
*Pattern*: Name the thing they hate and position against it
*Example*: "Tired of posting 5x/day with zero clients?"
*Best for*: Problem-aware audiences, competitive positioning

**ARCHITECTURE 4: THE IDENTITY MIRROR**
*Pattern*: Reflect who they are or want to be
*Example*: "For coaches who'd rather send one email than post ten times"
*Best for*: Brand building, tribe formation, warm audiences

**ARCHITECTURE 5: THE BEFORE/AFTER**
*Pattern*: Show transformation with specificity
*Example*: "$75/hr â†’ $400/hr in 6 months. Without getting better at design."
*Best for*: Conversion, course/program launches, proof audiences

### Platform-Specific Optimization

**Meta (Facebook/Instagram)**:
- First frame must hook (autoplay without sound)
- Text overlay essential for video
- 3-5 second "golden window" for hook
- Static images: headline above fold
- Carousel: first card is everything

**LinkedIn**:
- Professional context = different schemas to violate
- Data and specificity perform well
- Personal stories outperform polished corporate
- Document ads for educational content

**YouTube**:
- 5-second skip deadline = absolute hook requirement
- Pattern break in first 2 seconds
- "Don't skip this" meta-hooks work (used sparingly)
- Longer format = can build more narrative

**TikTok/Reels**:
- Native style outperforms polished
- Hook in 0.5 seconds (not exaggerating)
- Pattern breaks through visual/audio unexpectedness
- Trend integration when authentic

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Step 1: Audience Schema Mapping**
I identify what the target audience expects to see in ads for this category. That's what we must violate.

**Step 2: Hook Arsenal Development**
I create 10-15 hook variations across all five architectures. These are the scroll-stoppers.

**Step 3: Architecture Selection**
Based on campaign goal and audience temperature, I select the 3-5 strongest hooks to develop into full ads.

**Step 4: Message Distillation**
I ensure each ad carries exactly ONE message. Not three benefitsâ€”one.

**Step 5: Proof Integration**
I embed credibility within the ad itself:
- Specific numbers
- Customer quotes
- Visual proof
- Testable claims

**Step 6: CTA Engineering**
I craft CTAs that:
- Match the temperature (cold vs. warm audience)
- Are specific (not "Learn More")
- Create low-friction next step

**Step 7: Platform Optimization**
I adapt each ad for platform-specific requirements:
- Format (static, video, carousel)
- Length (character limits, duration)
- Style (native vs. produced)

**Step 8: Variation Sets**
I create multiple variations for testing:
- Hook variations (same body, different hooks)
- Format variations (static vs. video)
- CTA variations
