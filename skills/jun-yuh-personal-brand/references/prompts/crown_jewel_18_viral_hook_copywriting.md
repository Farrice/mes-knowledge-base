## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Avatar-Hook Alignment
1. Identify avatar's core pain points (what makes them stop scrolling)
2. Map their private internal dialogue (words in their head)
3. Determine their sophistication level (do they need education or validation?)
4. Identify their "scroll state" for this medium (email = inbox fatigue, social = entertainment mode, search = solution seeking)

### Phase 2: Hook Architecture Selection
Apply Jun's 8 hook types, adapted for copy:

**Type 1: CURIOSITY GAP**
- Creates knowledge asymmetry—they need what you know
- Formula: [Unexpected outcome] + [They don't know why/how]
- Best for: Educational content, product launches, lead magnets

**Type 2: PATTERN INTERRUPT**
- Violates expectation, creates cognitive dissonance
- Formula: [Contradict common belief] + [Promise alternative]
- Best for: Contrarian positioning, crowded markets, sophistication audiences

**Type 3: IDENTITY FILTER**
- Calls out specific people—if it's you, you MUST engage
- Formula: [Specific identifier] + [Specific problem/desire]
- Best for: Targeted ads, niche offers, premium positioning

**Type 4: SPECIFIC NUMBERS**
- Concrete specificity signals research and credibility
- Formula: [Precise number] + [Outcome/problem/timeline]
- Best for: Case studies, results-driven offers, B2B

**Type 5: CONTROVERSY/PROVOCATION**
- Triggers emotional response that demands engagement
- Formula: [Challenging statement] + [Implicit promise to explain]
- Best for: Thought leadership, differentiation, engagement-focused

**Type 6: STORY OPENER**
- Begins mid-narrative, creates "what happened?" pull
- Formula: [Moment of tension/change] + [Implied story continuation]
- Best for: Relationship-based marketing, personal brands, email

**Type 7: PAIN AMPLIFICATION**
- Names their exact suffering with uncomfortable precision
- Formula: [Specific pain] + [Validation or solution signal]
- Best for: Problem-aware audiences, direct response

**Type 8: DESIRE PROJECTION**
- Paints the outcome they want vividly
- Formula: [Specific desired state] + [Implied path]
- Best for: Aspiration marketing, high-ticket, transformation offers

### Phase 3: Medium Optimization
Calibrate for platform:
- **Email subjects**: 6-10 words, mobile-first, preview text consideration
- **Headlines**: Front-load key words, scannable, SEO-aware if applicable
- **Ad copy**: First line = hook, pattern interrupt works best
- **Video hooks**: Speakable in under 3 seconds, curiosity-forward
- **Social posts**: First line before "see more" cutoff is everything
- **Sales pages**: Headline-subheadline combination, specificity wins

### Phase 4: Hook Production
Generate 3-5 hooks per type (24-40 total), rated by:
- Stopping power (1-10)
- Relevance to avatar (1-10)
- Medium fit (1-10)

## VIRAL HOOK ARSENAL: Agency Project Management

### CURIOSITY GAP HOOKS (Stop power: 9/10)

**Hook 1**: "There's a reason your team misses deadlines even when everyone's 'working hard.' It has nothing to do with their effort."
- **Why it works**: Creates mystery about the REAL cause—agency owners blame team, this suggests system failure
- **Best for**: Cold audiences who think they understand their problem
- **A/B variant**: "Your team isn't the problem. Your tools are. Here's proof."

**Hook 2**: "We analyzed 200 agency workflows. 94% had the same invisible bottleneck—and it wasn't what they expected."
- **Why it works**: Research-backed + mystery + "not what you expect" creates open loop
- **Best for**: Data-driven decision makers, skeptical audiences

**Hook 3**: "The difference between agencies that deliver on time and those that don't isn't talent or process. It's this."
- **Why it works**: Eliminates obvious answers, promises novel insight
- **Best for**: Agencies who've tried "better processes" and still struggle

---

### PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOKS (Stop power: 8/10)

**Hook 4**: "Stop making your team use Slack for project updates. Here's why it's destroying your delivery rate."
- **Why it works**: Attacks a tool they're definitely using, creates "wait, what?" response
- **Best for**: Slack-using agencies, contrarian positioning

**Hook 5**: "Project management software isn't your answer. (But what you replace it with might be.)"
- **Why it works**: Category disruption—attacks the solution category while positioning within it
- **Best for**: Audiences fatigued by PM tool promises

**Hook 6**: "Your agency doesn't need another tool. It needs one less."
- **Why it works**: Inversion of typical "add this tool" messaging
- **Best for**: Tool-fatigued operations managers

### HIGH-PERFORMER SUBJECT LINE FORMULAS FOR FUTURE EMAILS

**Formula 1: Question + Pain**
- "Why is it so hard to [thing they struggle with]?"
- Example: "Why is it so hard to leave?"

**Formula 2: Specific Number + Outcome**
- "[Number] [people/days/dollars] [outcome]"
- Example: "$8,400/month working 20 hours"

**Formula 3: Confession + Hook**
- "I almost didn't [action]"
- Example: "I almost didn't share this"

**Formula 4: Contrarian Statement**
- "Stop [common advice]"
- Example: "Stop saving for retirement"

**Formula 5: Identity + Question**
- "[Identity], can I ask you something?"
- Example: "Corporate escapees: can I ask you something?"
