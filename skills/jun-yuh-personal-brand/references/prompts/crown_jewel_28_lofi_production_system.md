## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: The Lo-Fi Hierarchy of Needs

**Variables ranked by ACTUAL impact on performance**:

| Rank | Variable | Impact | Minimum Viable Standard |
|------|----------|--------|------------------------|
| 1 | **Hook/Concept** | 40-50% | Must be strong‚Äîno equipment fixes bad ideas |
| 2 | **Audio Clarity** | 15-20% | Must be audible (any mic > phone mic at distance) |
| 3 | **Lighting (face visible)** | 10-15% | Natural light or one lamp = sufficient |
| 4 | **Framing/Composition** | 5-10% | Face in frame, not cut off = good enough |
| 5 | **Background** | 3-5% | Not distracting = fine |

| 8 | **Camera Movement** | 1% | Static phone = fine |
| 9 | **Transitions** | <1% | Cut = sufficient |
| 10 | **Special Effects** | <1% | Almost never needed |

**The Math**: If your concept is 8/10 and production is 4/10, total = strong content.
If your concept is 4/10 and production is 10/10, total = weak content.

### Phase 2: Minimum Viable Setup

**Tier 1: Zero Budget (Phone Only)**
- Natural light from window (sit facing it)
- Phone propped against books/water bottle
- Built-in mic (speak within 2 feet of phone)
- Any non-distracting background
- **This is enough to build 100K+ followers**

**Tier 2: Basic Upgrade ($50-150)**
- Clip-on lavalier mic ($15-30)
- Phone tripod with adjustable height ($20-30)
- Ring light OR desk lamp ($15-40)
- **This is enough to build 1M+ followers**

**Tier 3: The Jun Setup (~$200-400)**
- iPhone (any model from last 5 years)
- Basic wireless mic
- Simple lighting
- Editing on phone (CapCut)
- **This is enough to build multi-millions**

### Phase 3: Lo-Fi Production Protocols

**Protocol 1: "Good Enough" Checklist**
Before filming, verify:
- [ ] Face is lit (can see eyes clearly)
- [ ] Audio is clear (do 5-second test)
- [ ] Frame includes your face/gestures
- [ ] Background isn't distracting
- [ ] That's it. Film.

**Protocol 2: Speed Over Polish**
- First take that meets checklist = use it
- Stumbles and "um"s = leave them (authenticity)
- Jump cuts = fix anything unfixable
- Done > Perfect

**Protocol 3: The 10-Minute Rule**
If production setup takes >10 minutes, you're overcomplicating.
- Setup: 2-3 minutes
- Filming: 5-10 minutes (multiple takes if needed)
- Basic edit: 10-15 minutes
- **Total time: Under 30 minutes per piece**

### Phase 4: What to STOP Worrying About

**Things that DON'T matter for performance**:
- 4K vs 1080p (no one notices)
- Smooth camera movements (static is fine)
- Fancy transitions (cuts work)
- Color grading (auto is fine)
- Professional backgrounds (clean is enough)
- Expensive microphones (basic clarity is enough)
- Ring lights (window light is often better)
- Teleprompters (natural is more authentic)

**Things that DO matter**:
- Can they hear you clearly?
- Can they see your face/expressions?
- Is the concept/hook strong?
- Is the pacing appropriate?

### FILMING PROTOCOL

**Pre-Film (2 minutes)**:
1. Quick background check (anything distracting?)
2. Light check (can you see your eyes clearly?)
3. Audio test (5-second recording, play back)
4. Frame check (head + shoulders visible)
5. Start filming

**During Filming**:
- Look at camera lens, not screen
- Energy 20% higher than feels natural
- If you stumble badly, pause and restart that section
- If you stumble slightly, keep going (authentic)
- First take that's "good enough" = done

**Time Budget**:
| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| Setup | 2-3 min |
| Review script/points | 2 min |
| Filming (with retakes) | 5-10 min |
| **Total Filming** | **10-15 min** |
