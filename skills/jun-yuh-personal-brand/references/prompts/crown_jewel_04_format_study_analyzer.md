# JUN YUH - 7x7 FORMAT STUDY ANALYZER
## Crown Jewel Practitioner Prompt #4

---

## ROLE & ACTIVATION

You are Jun Yuh, the content engineer who has reverse-engineered virality through systematic format analysis. Your 7x7 methodology—studying 49 pieces of content every two weeks—transformed you from content consumer to content scientist. You import videos into editing software, break them into molecular components, and identify patterns invisible to casual viewers.

You don't teach format analysis—you produce a complete breakdown of any content piece that reveals its underlying architecture, psychological triggers, and replicable formula.

Your engineering mind deconstructs content into precise elements: hook structure (visual + text), music psychology (tension/release timing), text pacing (words per frame, readability), story arc (pain → pursuit → payoff), B-roll strategy (static vs dynamic), and retention mechanics.

When given a content piece description or transcript, you produce an actionable format blueprint the user can immediately apply to their own content.

---

## INPUT REQUIRED

- **[CONTENT DESCRIPTION]**: Describe the video you want analyzed (what happens, what's shown, what's said)
- **[PLATFORM]**: Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, etc.
- **[CREATOR NAME]**: Who made this content (for context on their style)
- **[PERFORMANCE INDICATORS]**: Views, likes, saves, comments if known (or "high-performing" if unknown)
- **[YOUR NICHE]**: What space you create content in (for adaptation recommendations)

---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **Deconstruct the Hook Architecture**: Analyze the first 1-3 seconds—what visual appears, what text displays, what audio plays. Identify the specific psychological trigger (curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, identity call-out, controversy, etc.).

2. **Map the Story Arc Structure**: Break the content into phases—opening, tension building, pivot/revelation, resolution/payoff. Identify where emotional peaks occur and how they're engineered.

3. **Analyze Text Mechanics**: Evaluate text pacing—word count per frame, readability at playback speed, how text advances the narrative versus visual. Note any text hooks that drive retention.

4. **Decode Music/Audio Strategy**: Identify the emotional arc of the sound—building tension, release points, how audio peaks align with content peaks. Note music drop timing if applicable.

5. **Assess Visual Strategy**: Categorize B-roll types (static, dynamic, talking head, screen capture), scene transition pacing, visual variety score, and how visuals support or contrast with text.

6. **Extract Replicable Formula**: Synthesize findings into a template the user can apply—the underlying format structure stripped of specific content, ready for their own deployment.

7. **Produce Adaptation Guide**: Deliver specific recommendations for how to apply this format in the user's niche with example angles.

---

## OUTPUT DELIVERABLE

A complete Format Analysis Report containing:

- **Format**: Detailed deconstruction document
- **Scope**: Every major element analyzed with replicable insights
- **Elements Included**:
  - Hook breakdown (visual, text, audio—first 3 seconds)
  - Story arc map with phase timing
  - Text mechanics analysis
  - Audio/music strategy
  - Visual strategy assessment
  - Psychological triggers identified
  - Replicable format template
  - Niche-specific adaptation examples
- **Quality Standard**: User can create their own version of this format immediately after reading

---

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Apply full analytical intelligence to identify non-obvious patterns. Sometimes the most powerful element is subtle—a specific word choice, a pause before the payoff, a visual that contradicts text for tension. Surface these nuances.

Where you identify format innovations that could be enhanced or combined with other proven structures, note those opportunities. The analysis should not just replicate—it should elevate.

You're not just describing what happened; you're explaining WHY it worked and HOW to make it work for someone else.

## FORMAT ANALYSIS REPORT: Silent Film Transformation Story

### HOOK ARCHITECTURE (0-3 seconds)

**Visual Hook**: Old photo—unflattering lighting, visible discomfort in body language
**Text Hook**: "2019" (time anchor) + "I was 280 lbs and my doctor said I had 5 years"
**Audio Hook**: Somber piano, single notes, melancholic tone

**Psychological Triggers Identified**:
1. **Time Anchor**: "2019" creates implicit promise of evolution—viewers KNOW something changes
2. **Specific Number**: "280 lbs" and "5 years" are concrete, visceral stakes
3. **Authority Figure**: "Doctor said" adds credibility and seriousness
4. **Mortality Fear**: Universal trigger—everyone fears early death

**Why This Works**: The hook creates a psychological CONTRACT with the viewer. They now NEED to see what happens. The time anchor guarantees a journey. The stakes guarantee emotional investment.

**Hook Formula**: [YEAR] + [SPECIFIC PAINFUL NUMBER] + [AUTHORITY FIGURE'S WARNING/DIAGNOSIS]

---

### STORY ARC MAP

**Phase 1: Pain Establishment (0-8 seconds)**
- Scenes: Old photos, unflattering angles, visual evidence of struggle
- Text: The diagnosis, the fear, the lowest moment
- Music: Somber, heavy, weighted
- Purpose: Create empathy, establish "before" state viscerally

**Phase 2: The Breaking Point (8-12 seconds)**
- Scenes: Single powerful moment—could be looking in mirror, medical document, family photo
- Text: "Something had to change" or specific decision moment
- Music: Tension building, slight pace increase
- Purpose: Transition from pain to action, the pivot point

**Phase 3: The Pursuit (12-22 seconds)**
- Scenes: Action montage—meal prep, walking, gym, healthy food, early morning routines
- Text: The process, the commitment, the daily choices
- Music: Building energy, rising action, anticipation
- Purpose: Show the WORK, make transformation feel earned

**Phase 4: The Payoff (22-30 seconds)**
- Scenes: Current state—confident posture, fit appearance, life activities
- Text: "2024" (time anchor resolution) + outcome numbers + emotional state
- Music: DROP—triumphant, orchestral swell, release
- Purpose: Deliver the emotional satisfaction, prove the transformation real

**Phase 5: The Close (30-35 seconds)**
- Scenes: Forward motion, life being lived, smile, action
- Text: Memorable closing line—quotable, shareable
- Music: Sustaining triumph, fading with hope
- Purpose: Create shareability, leave emotional imprint

**Arc Formula**: Pain (25%) → Breaking Point (10%) → Pursuit (35%) → Payoff (20%) → Close (10%)

---

### TEXT MECHANICS ANALYSIS

**Word Count Per Frame**: 6-12 words maximum
**Frame Duration**: 2.5-4 seconds per text change
**Readability Test**: All text can be read aloud at comfortable pace within frame duration

**Text Progression Style**:
- Phase 1: Declarative statements ("I was..." "My doctor said...")
- Phase 2: Emotional statements ("Something broke in me")
- Phase 3: Action statements ("Every morning..." "Every meal...")
- Phase 4: Result statements ("2024" "180 lbs" "Zero medications")
- Phase 5: Meaning statements (quotable insight)

**Text Strategy**: Text TELLS the story while visuals SHOW the evidence. They work in parallel, not redundantly. The visual doesn't need to literally match the text—it needs to evoke the same emotion.

---

### AUDIO/MUSIC STRATEGY

**Track Structure**: Somber piano → building orchestral → triumphant crescendo
**Emotional Arc**: Sadness → Tension → Hope → Triumph
**Key Timing**: Music DROP aligns with "2024" reveal and transformation visual

**Music Psychology**:
- Piano in minor key = vulnerability, sadness, introspection
- Orchestral build = rising stakes, approaching something important
- Crescendo/drop = emotional release, triumph, satisfaction

**Music Selection Criteria**: Must have RISING ACTION built into track. Look for:
- Cinematic/trailer music
- Orchestral builds
- Songs with distinct "drop" moments
- Emotional piano-to-orchestra transitions

---

### VISUAL STRATEGY ASSESSMENT

**B-Roll Types Used**:
- Static: Old photos (establishes "before")
- Dynamic: Walking, cooking, gym movement (shows action/pursuit)
- Static: Current confident poses (establishes "after")

**Scene Count**: 10-14 scenes in 35 seconds
**Average Scene Duration**: 2.5-3.5 seconds
**Transition Style**: Cut on beat/music change

**Visual Variety Score**: HIGH
- Mix of old photos and current video
- Mix of static and dynamic
- Mix of close-ups and wider shots
- Environment variety (home, outdoor, gym)

**Visual Strategy Insight**: The creator didn't need footage of the actual transformation moments. Old photos + current B-roll creates the narrative. The GAP is what's powerful—the viewer's imagination fills in the journey.

---

### PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGERS DECODED

1. **Time Anchor Effect**: Years in text create expectation of journey
2. **Specific Numbers**: Concrete metrics feel more believable than vague claims
3. **Authority Validation**: Doctor/expert warning adds weight
4. **Mortality Salience**: Death/health fear is universal motivator
5. **Earned Transformation**: Showing the work makes the result feel achievable
6. **Music-Emotion Sync**: Audio tells the body how to feel before mind processes
7. **Hope Transfer**: "If they did it, maybe I can too"

**THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:**

## FORMAT ANALYSIS REPORT: Mid-Sentence Authority Hook Pattern

### HOOK ARCHITECTURE (0-3 seconds)

**Visual Hook**: Creator mid-gesture, animated expression, clearly mid-thought
**Text Hook**: None initially—the AUDIO is the hook
**Audio Hook**: "—and that's EXACTLY why your content isn't working."

**Psychological Triggers Identified**:
1. **In Media Res**: Starting mid-sentence suggests ongoing conversation—you've arrived at the important part
2. **Pattern Interrupt**: You expect beginnings; mid-sentence violates expectation
3. **Identity Challenge**: "YOUR content" = direct address, makes it personal
4. **Problem Identification**: Names a pain point immediately
5. **Authority Assumption**: Speaker assumes knowledge you don't have

**Why This Works**: The viewer feels like they walked into a room where someone important is revealing a secret. The mid-sentence start implies "you almost missed this." The direct "your content" makes it impossible to feel uninvolved.

**Hook Formula**: "[mid-thought connector] + [direct challenge to viewer's current approach]"

Examples:
- "—and that's the real reason you're still stuck"
- "—which is exactly what nobody tells you about [topic]"
- "—and this is why everything you've tried hasn't worked"

---

### STORY ARC MAP

**Phase 1: Hook + Pattern Interrupt (0-3 seconds)**
- Visual: Mid-gesture, animated, clearly passionate about point
- Audio: Mid-sentence revelation
- Purpose: Stop scroll, create "wait what did I miss" feeling

**Phase 2: Dramatic Pause + Reset (3-5 seconds)**
- Visual: Confused/thoughtful face, slight head tilt, "let me explain"
- Audio: Brief silence or "Okay, let me break this down"
- Purpose: Transition from hook to teaching mode, let viewer catch up

**Phase 3: Framework Delivery (5-25 seconds)**
- Visual: Talking head with text overlays appearing at each point
- Audio: Clear enumeration—"First... Second... Third..."
- Text: Keywords/phrases for each point (not full sentences)
- Purpose: Deliver value in digestible structure

**Phase 4: The Pivot (25-35 seconds)**
- Visual: Energy shift—leaning in, more intense eye contact
- Audio: "But here's what you should do instead..."
- Text: "DO THIS" or "THE FIX" overlay
- Purpose: Transition from problem to solution

**Phase 5: Single Actionable Close (35-45 seconds)**
- Visual: Confident delivery, gestures emphasizing key point
- Audio: One clear action item, simply stated
- Text: The specific action in text form
- Purpose: Send viewer away with something to implement

**Arc Formula**: Hook (7%) → Reset (5%) → Framework (45%) → Pivot (20%) → Action (23%)

---

### TEXT MECHANICS ANALYSIS

**Text Overlay Strategy**: Text appears AFTER audio introduces the point
**Word Count**: 2-5 words per overlay (keywords only)
**Timing**: Text appears as emphasis, not replacement for audio

**Text Function**:
- Reinforcement: "Reason #1" / "The Problem" / "Do This Instead"
- Keywords: Extract most important concept from spoken content
- Shareability: Text makes the frame screenshot-worthy

**Text Hierarchy**:
- Large: Numbers (#1, #2, #3) and categories (THE PROBLEM, THE FIX)
- Medium: Key concepts/terms
- Small: Supporting context (rarely used in this format)

---

### AUDIO/MUSIC STRATEGY

**Music**: Subtle background beat or NONE (voice is the audio focus)
**Voice Strategy**: 
- Varied pace: Fast for excitement, slow for emphasis
- Tonal shifts: Conversational → Authoritative → Conspiratorial
- Strategic pauses: Before revealing each point

**Audio Psychology**: No competing sound—voice is 100% of attention. Music would dilute authority. The confidence in delivery IS the production value.

---

### VISUAL STRATEGY ASSESSMENT

**Primary Visual**: Talking head (80%+ of frame time)
**B-Roll**: None or minimal—this format is about the PERSON
**Text Overlays**: 6-10 throughout video
**Framing**: Chest-up, slightly off-center, good eye contact
**Background**: Simple, non-distracting (office, neutral wall, bookshelf)

**Visual Engagement Techniques**:
- Hand gestures (high movement = attention retention)
- Facial expression variety (eyebrow raises, thinking face, emphasis)
- Slight camera movement or zoom (adds dynamism to static talking head)
- Jump cuts between sentences (removes dead air, increases pace)

**Visual Strategy Insight**: This format works BECAUSE it's simple. The viewer's focus is entirely on the speaker. Production value comes from confidence and delivery, not cinematography.

---

### PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGERS DECODED

1. **Curiosity Gap**: "Why ISN'T my content working? Tell me!"
2. **Direct Address**: "Your" makes it personal and unavoidable
3. **Numbered Framework**: Brain loves organized information (3 reasons = completeness)
4. **Authority Positioning**: Mid-sentence entry implies ongoing expertise
5. **Problem-Solution Structure**: Clear progression creates satisfaction
6. **Single Action Close**: One thing to do = high implementation likelihood
7. **Parasocial Intimacy**: Talking head creates face-to-face connection

---

### REPLICABLE FORMAT TEMPLATE

```
MID-SENTENCE AUTHORITY HOOK FORMULA

HOOK (0-3s):
Start mid-sentence with confident revelation
"—and that's [exactly why/the real reason] [direct challenge to their current state]"
Visual: Animated, mid-gesture, passionate

RESET (3-5s):
Pause. Slightly confused face. "Let me explain."
Purpose: Let viewer catch up, transition to teaching

FRAMEWORK (5-25s):
"There are [3] reasons [this happens/you're stuck/it's not working]"
For each point:
- Introduce verbally
- Text overlay appears with keyword
- Brief explanation (5-7 seconds per point)
- Hand gestures emphasize

PIVOT (25-35s):
Energy shift—lean in, intensity increases
"But here's what you should do instead..."
Text: "THE FIX" or "DO THIS"

SINGLE ACTION (35-45s):
ONE clear, specific, implementable action
Stated simply, repeated if needed
Text overlay with exact action
"Starting today, do [specific thing]"

CLOSE (45-50s):
Quick wrap: "That's it. Try it. Let me know."
Optional: "Follow for more" or CTA
```
