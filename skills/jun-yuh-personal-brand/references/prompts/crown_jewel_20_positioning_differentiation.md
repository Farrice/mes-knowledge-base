## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Creative Vision Positioning Audit

**The Message Discovery**
- What is the ONE belief that drives everything you do?
- What would you argue for even if you couldn't profit from it?
- What do you wish everyone in your market understood?

**The Pillar Architecture**
- What are the 3-5 distinct areas of value you provide?
- How do these pillars express your core message?
- Which pillar is your "gateway" (first engagement point)?

**The Avatar Precision**
- WHO specifically do you serve best?
- What psychographic markers define them (beyond demographics)?
- What language do they use to describe their situation?

**The Uniqueness Excavation**
- What's your origin story? How does it connect to what you do?
- What skills/experiences do you have that others don't?
- What contrarian belief do you hold about your industry?

### Phase 2: Competitive Landscape Analysis

**Positioning Map Creation**:
- Plot competitors on key dimensions
- Identify white space (unoccupied positions)
- Determine defensible territory

**Differentiation Opportunity Types**:
1. **WHO**: Serve a specific audience others ignore
2. **WHAT**: Offer something others don't
3. **HOW**: Deliver through a unique method/process
4. **WHY**: Lead with a belief/mission others don't share
5. **WHEN**: Serve at a specific moment/stage others miss

### Phase 3: Position Statement Architecture

**The Positioning Formula**:
```
For [SPECIFIC AVATAR] who [SPECIFIC SITUATION/PAIN],
[YOUR BRAND] is the [CATEGORY] that [KEY DIFFERENTIATION]
because [PROOF/REASON TO BELIEVE].
Unlike [ALTERNATIVES], we [UNIQUE VALUE].
```

**The "Only" Test**: Can you complete this sentence truthfully?
"We are the ONLY [category] that [specific differentiation] for [specific audience]."

If yes = strong positioning. If no = keep refining.

### Phase 4: Positioning Deployment Architecture

**Internal Clarity**:
- Positioning statement (internal document)
- Decision filter (what to say yes/no to)
- Language guide (how to describe yourself)

**External Expression**:
- Tagline/headline
- About page positioning
- Social bio architecture
- Sales conversation framing
- Content pillar alignment
