## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Emotional Arc Architecture
Map Jun's Silent Film structure to sales page sections:

**ACT 1: PAIN ESTABLISHMENT (20% of page)**
- Silent Film: Opening scenes of struggle, specific pain visualization
- Sales Page: Problem articulation so precise they think "this is EXACTLY me"
- Goal: Reader feels SEEN and VALIDATED in their struggle

**ACT 2: BREAKING POINT (10% of page)**
- Silent Film: The moment of decision, the realization, the pivot
- Sales Page: The "something has to change" moment—either your story or theirs
- Goal: Create urgency, establish that status quo is unacceptable

**ACT 3: PURSUIT/SOLUTION (30% of page)**
- Silent Film: The journey, the work, the process montage
- Sales Page: Introduction of the methodology, the "how," the system
- Goal: Show the path clearly—make transformation feel achievable

**ACT 4: PAYOFF/PROOF (25% of page)**
- Silent Film: The transformation revealed, the "after" state
- Sales Page: Results, testimonials, case studies, your own results
- Goal: Prove the transformation is REAL and ACHIEVABLE for them

**ACT 5: CLOSE/INVITATION (15% of page)**
- Silent Film: The final frame, the lasting impression
- Sales Page: The offer, the CTA, the invitation to join
- Goal: Make buying the obvious, natural, earned next step

### Phase 2: Music Drop Translation
Jun's "music drop" = the emotional release moment where tension becomes triumph.

**In sales pages, the "drop" occurs when**:
- Objections transform into benefits
- Pain transforms into possibility
- Doubt transforms into confidence
- "I can't" transforms into "I can"

**Engineering the drop**:
- Build tension through problem/pain
- Create doubt about current path
- Introduce solution as the release
- Let testimonials be the "triumphant music"
