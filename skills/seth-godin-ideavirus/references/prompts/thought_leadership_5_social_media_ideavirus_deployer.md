## PLATFORM PHYSICS BREAKDOWN

### LinkedIn
**Spread Mechanics:**
- Algorithm favors dwell time and engagement
- Early comments heavily influence reach
- Saves and shares signal high value
- Posts with images slightly outperform text-only
- Carousels get exceptional engagement
- "Engagement pods" exist but quality > quantity

**Native Formats:**
- Text post (750-1,300 characters optimal)
- Carousel (document upload, 8-12 slides)
- Short-form video (under 90 seconds)
- Newsletter/Article (for deeper dives)

**Spread Triggers:**
- Tagging people who might engage
- Asking questions that invite responses
- Creating saveable/screenshotable insights
- Controversy that invites professional debate
- Vulnerable personal stories with business lessons

**What Dies Here:**
- Pure self-promotion
- Generic advice without specificity
- Content that sounds like marketing
- Political content (except occasionally)

### Twitter/X
**Spread Mechanics:**
- Quote tweets > retweets for algorithm
- Early engagement in first 30 min critical
- Threads get extended visibility
- Controversy spreads faster than value
- Replies and engagement extend life

**Native Formats:**
- Single tweet (under 280, punchy)
- Thread (5-15 tweets, narrative or list)
- Quote tweet with take
- Poll (engagement bait)

**Spread Triggers:**
- Hot takes that demand response
- Specific numbers and data
- Lists and rankings
- Asking for RTs explicitly
- "This." or similar signal boosting
- @mentioning people who might engage

**What Dies Here:**
- Long paragraphs (break them up)
- Links in the main tweet (algorithm buries)
- Threads longer than 15 tweets
- Content without opinion/angle

### Instagram
**Spread Mechanics:**
- Saves are the most important signal
- Carousel gets more time on screen
- Reels reach non-followers more than posts
- Stories reach followers more than non
- Algorithm favors accounts people interact with

**Native Formats:**
- Single image + caption
- Carousel (up to 10 slides)
- Reels (7-60 seconds optimal)
- Stories (quick, ephemeral)

**Spread Triggers:**
- "Save this for later" calls to action
- DM to a friend prompts
- Shareable quotes/insights
- Before/after transformations
- "Share to your story" hooks

**What Dies Here:**
- Text-heavy content without visual appeal
- Links (not clickable except in bio)
- Content that doesn't reward scrolling through

### TikTok
**Spread Mechanics:**
- Algorithm tests content with small audience first
- Watch time % is primary signal
- Looping/rewatching signals value
- Comments heavily influence reach
- Duets and stitches extend life

**Native Formats:**
- Talking head (7-60 seconds)
- Green screen (react to images/text)
- Trending sounds + original take
- Stitch/Duet of viral content

**Spread Triggers:**
- "Stitch this with your take"
- Asking questions in comments
- Controversial opinions that demand response
- "Part 1" implying series
- Cliffhangers that require rewatch

**What Dies Here:**
- Over-produced, polished content
- Content that feels like an ad
- Long windups (first 1 second is everything)
- Content without personality/energy

### YouTube (Short-form & Long-form)
**Spread Mechanics:**
- Click-through rate + watch time = algorithm love
- First 30 seconds determine everything
- Suggested videos drive more views than search
- Thumbnails are half the battle
- Shorts compete separately from long-form

**Native Formats:**
- Shorts (under 60 seconds, vertical)
- Long-form (8-15 min optimal for business)
- Community posts (for engagement)

**Spread Triggers:**
- Thumbnails with faces showing emotion
- Titles with curiosity gaps
- "Subscribe" prompts that give reason
- End screens linking to more content
- Pattern interrupts every 30-60 seconds
