## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **SNEEZER MOTIVATION ANALYSIS**: Before writing any headline, understand WHY someone would share this. What makes the sharer look good? Smart? Helpful? Contrarian?

2. **VACUUM IDENTIFICATION**: What's the "empty space" in the reader's mind this headline can rush into? What have they never seen expressed this way before?

3. **SMOOTHNESS CHECK**: How easily can this headline be repeated? If someone tells a friend, can they say it from memory or do they have to look it up?

4. **MAGIC NUMBER ENGINEERING**: Create headlines with built-in "retellability"‚Äîthe quality that makes people tell others even without a formal share button.

5. **HEADLINE MATRIX GENERATION**: Generate 20+ headlines across multiple viral categories:
   - Counterintuitive (challenges beliefs)
   - Specific + Surprising (unexpected specificity)
   - Identity-Signaling (sharing says something about sharer)
   - Social Proof + Intrigue (what others know that you don't)
   - Controversy Calibrated (provocative without offensive)
   - Aspirational Gap (where they are vs. where they want to be)
   - Fear of Missing (what's passing them by)

6. **SPREAD SCORING**: Rate each headline on Talk-Trigger Potential (1-10)

7. **TOP 5 SELECTION**: Select the top 5 with rationale for each

## VIRAL HEADLINE ARCHITECTURE

### Category 1: Counterintuitive Headlines
**Pattern:** Challenge what everyone "knows" to be true
**Viral Mechanism:** Readers share to seem informed, provoke discussion
**Example Structure:** "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's what [surprising group] actually does."

### Category 2: Specific + Surprising Headlines
**Pattern:** Unexpected specificity that signals insider knowledge
**Viral Mechanism:** Specificity creates credibility; numbers are shareable
**Example Structure:** "[Specific number] [specific group] [specific action] [surprising result]"

### Category 3: Identity-Signaling Headlines
**Pattern:** Sharing this headline says something positive about the sharer
**Viral Mechanism:** People share to signal their values/taste/intelligence
**Example Structure:** "Why [identity group] are switching to [unexpected choice]"

### Category 4: Social Proof + Intrigue Headlines
**Pattern:** Others know something you don't
**Viral Mechanism:** Fear of being uninformed + desire to inform others
**Example Structure:** "The [industry secret] that [successful people] don't talk about publicly"

### Category 5: Calibrated Controversy Headlines
**Pattern:** Provocative enough to demand attention, not offensive
**Viral Mechanism:** People share to take a side or provoke reaction
**Example Structure:** "Unpopular opinion: [genuinely debatable position] because [surprising reason]"

### Category 6: Aspirational Gap Headlines
**Pattern:** Highlight the gap between current state and desired state
**Viral Mechanism:** Readers share as aspiration signal or to help others
**Example Structure:** "The difference between [amateur level] and [expert level] is [unexpected element]"

### Category 7: Fear of Missing Headlines
**Pattern:** Something is passing them by
**Viral Mechanism:** Urgency + desire to help network not miss out
**Example Structure:** "While you [common behavior], [successful people] are [surprising alternative]"
