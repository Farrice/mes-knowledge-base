## THE MAGIC NUMBER FRAMEWORK

### The Core Equation
**Viral Coefficient (K) = R × C**

Where:
- **R (Reach)** = Average number of people each person exposes to the content
- **C (Conversion)** = Percentage of exposed people who take the desired action

**If K > 1.0:** Exponential growth (viral)
**If K = 1.0:** Stable (neither growing nor shrinking)
**If K < 1.0:** Decay (needs continuous input to maintain)

### Extended Variables

**V (Velocity):** How quickly each viral cycle completes
- Fast velocity = rapid compounding
- Slow velocity = delayed growth
- Measured in: time between seeing and sharing

**D (Decay):** How much message fidelity degrades per cycle
- High fidelity = message arrives intact
- Low fidelity = message distorts, loses impact
- Measured in: conversion rate degradation per cycle

### The Complete Formula
**Effective K = (R × C) - D, over time V**

A content piece that reaches 10 people per sharer (R=10) with 20% conversion (C=0.2) has K = 2.0—strong viral potential.

But if each cycle takes 2 weeks (V=slow) and message degrades 30% per cycle (D=0.3), effective spread is much weaker than raw K suggests.

# VIRAL VIABILITY ANALYSIS: Marketing Insider Newsletter

## CURRENT MAGIC NUMBER CALCULATION

- Current subscribers: 5,000

- Estimated subscribers from referrals: ~20/week
- Forward rate (from email data): 2.1% of subscribers forward each issue

**Diagnosis:** Current viral coefficient is 0.345—well below 1.0 threshold. For every 100 subscribers, you're generating 34.5 new subscribers through viral means. This decays without continuous external promotion.

### What Current K Means
- Viral contribution: ~20 subscribers/week from 5,000 base

## VARIABLE BREAKDOWN

### R (Reach): 2.3 people per forwarder
**Current State:**
- 2.1% of subscribers forward each issue (105 forwarders)
- Each forwarder reaches 2.3 people on average
- Total exposed per week: ~240 people

**Limiters:**
- Forward rate is low (2.1% vs. industry excellent of 5%+)
- Reach per forward is low (email forwards reach small groups)
- No sharing mechanisms beyond email forward

**Potential:** If forward rate doubled AND added social sharing, R could reach 5.0+

### C (Conversion): 15% conversion from exposed to subscriber
**Current State:**
- 15% of people who see a forwarded issue subscribe
- This is actually quite good for B2B newsletter

**Limiters:**
- Forwarded emails don't have optimized subscribe prompts
- No "this was sent to you by [name]" personalization
- Landing page for forwarded readers is generic

**Potential:** With optimized forward experience, C could reach 25%+

### V (Velocity): ~7 days per cycle
**Current State:**
- Weekly newsletter = one sharing opportunity per week
- Most forwards happen within 48 hours of send
- Full viral cycle takes ~7 days

**Limiters:**
- Weekly frequency limits sharing opportunities
- No mid-week content to prompt additional sharing

**Potential:** Faster velocity difficult without publication frequency change

### D (Decay): ~20% per cycle
**Current State:**
- Message fidelity is good (email forwards arrive intact)
- Brand/source attribution maintained
- However: forwarded emails lose "fresh" feeling

**Limiters:**
- Forwarded content is the same content subscribers got (not special)
- No "share this specific insight" mechanisms

**Potential:** Lower decay through specific "share this" callouts

## BOTTLENECK DIAGNOSIS

**Primary Constraint: R (Reach)**

The forward rate (2.1%) is the biggest limiter. Only 105 of 5,000 subscribers share each week. Even with perfect conversion, this reach limits total viral potential.

**Secondary Constraint: C (Conversion)**

15% conversion is decent, but the forwarded experience isn't optimized. With targeted improvements, this could increase significantly.

**Mathematical Reality:**
- To reach K = 1.0, you need R × C = 1.0
- Current: 2.3 × 0.15 = 0.345
- Need: Either R = 6.7 at current C, OR C = 43% at current R
- Most realistic: R = 4.0 AND C = 0.25 gives K = 1.0

**R-1: Add Social Share Prompts (Impact: +50% R)**

- Estimated impact: R increases from 2.3 to 3.5

**R-2: Referral Program (Impact: +100% R)**

- Make each issue shareable via unique link (not just email forward)

- Estimated impact: R increases from 2.3 to 3.0

### Conversion (C) Interventions

**C-1: Optimized Forward Experience (Impact: +40% C)**
- Create specific landing page for forwarded/shared readers

- Highlight "why subscribe" with social proof
- Estimated impact: C increases from 15% to 21%

**C-2: "Best of" Entry Point (Impact: +30% C)**

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT 2: Product Launch Campaign Analysis

**Context:** A SaaS company is launching a new feature and wants to understand if their launch campaign can go viral. They're planning email announcement, social posts, and influencer outreach. Current campaign metrics from past launches available.

---

### THE ACTUAL DELIVERABLE:

# VIRAL VIABILITY ANALYSIS: Feature Launch Campaign

## CURRENT MAGIC NUMBER CALCULATION

### Historical Data (Previous Launch)
- Email to 20,000 users
- Email open rate: 45%
- Click rate on "share this" in email: 3%
- Social shares from clicked users: avg 1.8 shares each
- Conversion from social posts: 2% click-to-signup
- Influencer posts: 5 influencers, avg 15,000 followers, 0.5% click-to-signup

### Previous Launch Viral Coefficient

**Direct Email Chain:**
- 20,000 emails → 9,000 opens → 270 sharers → 486 social shares → 9.7 signups from viral
- K = 9.7 / 270 = 0.036 (extremely low)

**Influencer Chain:**
- 75,000 total reach → 375 signups
- But these signups don't generate additional shares
- K (influencer) = essentially 0 (one-time, not viral)

**Combined K = ~0.05**

**Diagnosis:** Previous launch was NOT viral. It was a one-time broadcast that generated signups but no compound spread. The "viral" contribution was negligible.

## BOTTLENECK DIAGNOSIS

**Primary Constraint: C (Conversion from Social)**

2% conversion from social shares is very low. People are sharing but their networks aren't acting.

**Secondary Constraint: R (Reach via Shares)**

Only 3% of openers shared. Most customers didn't spread the word.

**Structural Problem:**

The campaign is designed for ANNOUNCEMENT, not SPREAD. There's no reason for recipients to share beyond "new feature exists." No story, no urgency, no social reward.

## INTERVENTION MENU FOR NEW LAUNCH

### Shareability Redesign

**S-1: Shareable Launch Hook**
- Instead of "We launched X," create "Why we built X (controversial take)"
- Gives sharers something interesting to share, not just news
- Expected impact: Share rate 3% → 8%

**S-2: Social Proof Launch**
- "1,000 people signed up in 24 hours" creates urgency and validation
- Public counter creates race dynamic
- Expected impact: Conversion 2% → 5%

**S-3: Early Access Social Unlock**
- "Share to unlock early access" mechanic
- Direct incentive for sharing
- Expected impact: Share rate 3% → 15%

### Conversion Redesign

**C-1: Landing Page for Social Traffic**
- Current: social clicks go to generic product page
- Redesigned: "Your friend [name] thought you'd want to see this"
- Expected impact: Conversion 2% → 8%

**C-2: Social Proof on Landing**
- Show real-time signup counter
- Show testimonials from early users
- Expected impact: Conversion 2% → 6%

### Network Effects

**N-1: Team Signup Mechanic**
- "Invite your team to sign up together"
- Built-in reason to share with colleagues
- Expected impact: R × 3 (team size multiplier)

**N-2: Referral Rewards**
- "First 100 referrers get [exclusive access]"
- Creates competition to share
- Expected impact: R × 2

## SCENARIO PROJECTIONS (NEW LAUNCH)

### Scenario A: Standard Launch (No Changes)
**K = 0.05**
Launch reaches only those directly touched. Viral contribution negligible.

### Scenario B: Shareability Focus (S-1 + S-3)
**New Variables:** Share rate 12%, Conversion 2%
**K = (12% of 9,000 openers × 1.8 shares × 2% conversion) / sharers**
**K = (1,080 × 1.8 × 0.02) / 1,080 = 0.036 × 1.8 = 0.065**
Still low—conversion is the bottleneck.

### Scenario C: Conversion Focus (C-1 + C-2)
**New Variables:** Share rate 3%, Conversion 8%
**K = (270 sharers × 1.8 × 8% conversion) / 270 = 0.144**
Better but still below 1.0.

### Scenario D: Combined Optimization (S-3 + C-1 + N-1)
**New Variables:** Share rate 15%, Conversion 8%, Team multiplier 3x
**K = (1,350 sharers × 5.4 avg reach × 8% conversion) / 1,350**
**K = (1,350 × 5.4 × 0.08) / 1,350 = 0.432**

Significantly better but still below 1.0. Viral growth not achieved, but viral contribution is now meaningful (40%+ of growth from viral vs. 5%).

### Scenario E: Maximum Viral Design
**Add:** Time-limited exclusivity, public leaderboard, community event
**K estimate: 0.8-1.2**

Possible to achieve viral threshold with complete campaign redesign around spread mechanics.

## REALISTIC RECOMMENDATION

**Viral Launch Likely NOT Achievable** with incremental optimization. The base math doesn't support it—email lists don't typically generate K > 1.0 launches.

**Alternative Strategy:** Optimize for AMPLIFIED LAUNCH instead of VIRAL LAUNCH

**Amplified Launch Goal:**
- Maximize Day 1-3 signups through broadcast
- Generate meaningful viral tail (K = 0.4-0.6) that extends reach
- Viral contribution adds 40-60% to broadcast reach

**This is more realistic and still valuable.** Not everything needs to be viral—some campaigns are broadcasts with viral amplification.
