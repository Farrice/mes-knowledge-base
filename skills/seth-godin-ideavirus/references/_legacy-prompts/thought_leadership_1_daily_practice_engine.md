## THE GODIN DAILY PRACTICE PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Ship Every Day
Not "write every day" but SHIP every day. Publication is the commitment. The world seeing your work is what counts.

### Principle 2: Generous, Not Perfect
Each post is a gift, not a monument. Give value freely. Don't wait until it's perfect—perfect never ships.

### Principle 3: The Streak is Sacred
Once you start, the streak becomes the most valuable asset. Protecting the streak creates the practice.

### Principle 4: Small is Fine
A profound insight in 100 words beats a mediocre essay in 1,000. Value per word, not words per post.

### Principle 5: Ideas Breed Ideas
The more you write, the more ideas you have. Writer's block is a symptom of not writing enough, not writing too much.

### Principle 6: Show Your Work
The practice itself is content. Sharing the journey of becoming a thought leader IS thought leadership.

### Idea Capture System

**Triggers:**

- Reading/podcasts → "What do I disagree with? What do I want to add?"

**Expansion Technique:**
For every idea, ask: "What's the opposite? What's the extreme version? What's the personal story behind this? What's the framework?"

One seed idea → 4 potential posts

### Inventory Management
**Goal:** Always have 30+ ideas in queue
**Weekly Harvest:** Add 10+ new ideas
**Emergency Reserve:** 5 "evergreen" posts pre-written for crisis days

## DAILY RITUAL BLUEPRINT

### The 45-Minute Morning Practice

**5:45am - Wake + Coffee**
No phone. No email. Just presence.

**6:00am - Idea Selection (5 min)**
- Open idea inventory
- Choose what's alive today (what are you feeling?)
- Or: Random selection if nothing sparks

**6:05am - Fast Draft (20 min)**
- Timer on
- Write without editing
- Get the idea OUT, not perfect
- Target: 200-400 words

**6:25am - Light Edit (10 min)**
- One read-through
- Fix obvious errors
- Strengthen opening line
- Ensure it delivers value

**6:35am - Publish (5 min)**
- Add to platform
- No second-guessing
- Ship it

**6:40am - Capture Tomorrow's Seeds (5 min)**
- What did today's writing spark?
- Add 2-3 new ideas to inventory
- Note any frameworks trying to emerge

**6:45am - Done**
Non-negotiable end time. Practice complete.

### The Weekend Rhythm
**Saturday:** No publishing obligation (but can if inspired)
**Sunday Evening (30 min):** 
- Review week's posts
- Harvest ideas that emerged
- Pre-select Monday's topic
- Replenish inventory to 30+

## MINIMUM VIABLE POST DEFINITION

### What Counts as a Post
- **Minimum:** 100 words that deliver ONE insight
- **Typical:** 200-400 words
- **Maximum:** 800 words (rare)

### The Test
Before publishing, ask: "If someone read ONLY this post, would they get value?"
If yes → Ship it
If no → Focus. Remove the setup. Lead with the insight.

### Streak-Saving Posts
For days when you're depleted, these count:
- A meaningful question (50 words)
- A single powerful insight (100 words)
- A personal reflection (150 words)
- A curated idea with your take (100 words)

The goal is the STREAK. Protect it at all costs.

## WRITING TEMPLATES

### Template 1: The Contrarian Insight (150-200 words)
**Opening:** State the conventional wisdom
**Twist:** Reveal why it's wrong
**Evidence:** One example or observation
**Reframe:** The better way to think about it
**Invitation:** Question for the reader

### Template 2: The Pattern Recognition (200-300 words)
**Opening:** "I've noticed something across [X] clients..."
**Pattern:** Describe what you see
**Why It Happens:** Brief explanation
**What To Do:** Practical implication
**Invitation:** "Have you seen this too?"

### Template 3: The Story (300-400 words)
**Hook:** The outcome or tension
**Setup:** Brief context
**Journey:** What happened
**Insight:** What it taught you/them
**Application:** Why this matters to reader

### Template 4: The Question (100-150 words)
**Context:** Brief setup
**The Question:** Clear and provocative
**Why It Matters:** Stakes
**Space:** Leave room for reader to sit with it

### Template 5: The Tool (250-350 words)
**Problem:** What this solves
**Tool:** What it is (name it!)
**How To Use:** 3-5 steps
**Example:** Brief application
**Invitation:** Try it and tell me what happens

## ACCOUNTABILITY ARCHITECTURE

### Internal Systems
- **Streak Counter:** Visual tracker somewhere you see daily
- **Morning Trigger:** Coffee = writing (habitual link)
- **Non-Negotiable Boundary:** Nothing else happens before the post ships

### External Systems
- **Public Commitment:** Announce daily practice to audience
- **Accountability Partner:** One person who checks in weekly
- **Community:** Share in a group of daily creators

### Recovery Protocol
If you miss a day:
1. Don't shame yourself (it damages the practice)
2. Ship something TODAY, no matter how small
3. Don't try to "make up" missed days
4. New streak begins now
5. Adjust system if needed (too ambitious?)

### Celebrating Milestones
- Day 7: First week
- Day 30: One month
- Day 100: Triple digits
- Day 365: One year
- Day 1,000: Legendary

Each milestone → reflect publicly on what you've learned

## 90-DAY LAUNCH PLAN

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-14)
**Goal:** Establish the habit, not the audience
**Focus:** Ship every day, no matter what
**Quality Bar:** Low (just ship)
**Promotion:** None (practice first)

**Daily Checklist:**
☐ Write
☐ Ship
☐ Capture 2 new ideas

### Phase 2: Refinement (Days 15-30)
**Goal:** Find your rhythm and voice
**Focus:** Identify what types of posts feel natural
**Quality Bar:** Medium (value-focused)
**Promotion:** Share to existing network

**Weekly Review:**
- Which posts resonated?
- What feels sustainable?
- Where's friction?

### Phase 3: Expansion (Days 31-60)
**Goal:** Build audience and authority
**Focus:** Consistent quality, strategic topics
**Quality Bar:** High (your standard)
**Promotion:** Active distribution

**New Addition:**
- Repurpose best posts for social platforms
- Engage with comments/responses
- Connect with similar voices

### Phase 4: Compound (Days 61-90)
**Goal:** See compound effects emerge
**Focus:** Reference previous posts, build frameworks
**Quality Bar:** Maintaining excellence
**Promotion:** Audience becoming promoters

**Signs of Success:**
- Ideas coming faster than you can write
- Strangers referencing your work
- Opportunities arriving unsolicited
- Voice feeling distinctly YOURS
