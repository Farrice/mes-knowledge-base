## SUPPORTING FRAMEWORKS

### Framework 1: The Sustainable Growth Model

**Concept:** A four-pillar framework for growing a business without sacrificing what matters.

**The Four Pillars:**
1. **Profit First** - Profitability before revenue growth
2. **Energy Management** - Work that energizes, clients who don't drain
3. **Time Boundaries** - Protected time for life outside business
4. **Enough Calibration** - Regular check-ins on whether you've hit "enough"

**Application:** Used as an assessment and planning tool for any growth decision.

### Framework 2: The Client Quality Hierarchy

**Concept:** A ranking system that categorizes clients by their total impact—not just revenue.

**The Hierarchy (A to D):**
- **A Clients:** High profit, easy to work with, generate referrals, energizing
- **B Clients:** Profitable, generally pleasant, occasional referrals
- **C Clients:** Break-even or low profit, neutral energy
- **D Clients:** Low profit or costly, draining, no referrals

**The Rule:** "Grow A's, maintain B's, upgrade or release C's, eliminate D's."

**Application:** Quarterly client audit using this framework.

### Framework 3: The Subtraction Strategy

**Concept:** The counterintuitive approach of growing through removal—subtracting clients, offers, and complexity to make room for better.

**The Core Insight:** Most business owners think addition (more clients, more offers, more marketing). The Subtraction Strategy focuses on subtraction (fewer but better clients, simpler offers, focused marketing).

**The Principle:** "Before you can have better, you need room for better."

**Application:** "The Subtraction Audit"—what could you remove to make room for better?

### Framework 4: The Lifestyle-First Business Model

**Concept:** Designing business structure around desired lifestyle, then working backward to the required business model—rather than building business first and squeezing in life.

**The Process:**
1. Define the life you want (hours, location, flexibility, income)
2. Calculate what business model supports that life
3. Build toward that model, saying no to opportunities that don't fit

**The Inversion:** Most people ask "How should I grow my business?" This framework asks "What business serves my life?"

## TACTICAL TOOLS

### Tool 1: The Enough Number Calculator

**Purpose:** A simple calculation to determine your personal "enough"—the income that allows you to stop chasing and start maintaining.

**The Formula:**
```
Enough Number = (Essential Expenses + Desired Lifestyle Costs + Security Buffer) × Safety Multiplier

Where:
- Essential Expenses = Non-negotiable monthly costs
- Desired Lifestyle Costs = The life you actually want
- Security Buffer = 3-6 months of expenses
- Safety Multiplier = 1.2-1.5 (for taxes, surprises)
```

**The Insight:** Most people have never calculated their "enough." When they do, it's often lower than they expected—which means freedom is closer than they thought.

### Tool 2: The Energy Audit

**Purpose:** A quick assessment to evaluate which clients/projects give energy vs. drain it.

**The Assessment:**
For each client, rate 1-10:
1. Excitement when you see their name in inbox
2. Energy level AFTER working with them
3. Quality of work you produce for them
4. Likelihood you'd take them on again knowing what you know

**Score Interpretation:**
- 32-40: A Client (protect and replicate)
- 24-31: B Client (maintain)
- 16-23: C Client (upgrade or release)
- Below 16: D Client (release)

### Tool 3: The Subtraction Audit

**Purpose:** A quarterly exercise to identify what to remove to make room for better.

**The Questions:**
1. Which clients would you not take on again?
2. Which offers create the most complexity for the least profit?
3. Which marketing channels produce low-quality leads?
4. Which commitments drain time without return?
5. What would you STOP doing if you had permission?

**The Action:** Choose one thing from each category to eliminate or phase out.
