# Screenplay Architect

Build a complete screenplay structure using Eric Roth's methodology — theme-first, beginning-end locked, one-word middle, visual prose scene descriptions.

## Pre-Flight

Read these files before executing:
1. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/genius.md`
2. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/genius-patterns.md` (Patterns 1-3, 7, 12, 14)

## Workflow

### Step 1: Theme Discovery (Pattern 2 — Theme-First Compass)

Before any character or plot:

> **What is this movie literally about?** Not the plot — the *theme.*
> Articulate it in one sentence under 12 words.
> Examples from Roth:
> - *Forrest Gump*: "An innocent man witnesses the loss of American innocence"
> - *The Insider*: "The cost of telling the truth in a system that punishes it"
> - *Munich*: "Vengeance corrodes the avenger more than the enemy"
> - *Benjamin Button*: "Time is the one thing no love can defeat"

**Deliverable**: One-sentence theme statement that will serve as your compass for every scene.

---

### Step 2: Beginning-End Lock (Pattern 3 — Beginning-End Architecture)

Write your **first scene** and **last scene** before touching anything else.

For the **opening** (Pattern 12 — The Home Invitation):
- Make audiences feel they've "found a home" worth living in for 2 hours
- Establish sensory immersion — weather, light, ambient sound, time of day
- Introduce the emotional register without explaining it
- Ask: "Would someone want to keep reading past page 2?"

For the **ending**:
- Ask: "What emotional truth does this character arrive at?"
- The ending must feel like the *inevitable destination* of the opening
- Plant a seed in the opening that blooms in the ending

**Deliverable**: Opening scene (2-3 pages of visual prose) + Closing scene (1-2 pages).

---

### Step 3: One-Word Forward Outline (Pattern 14 — One-Word Outlining)

Outline the middle with single words or short phrases. No detailed scene descriptions. Know the next 5-8 beats maximum.

Format:
```
ACT ONE (Setup):
- [opening scene — already written]
- meeting
- first glimpse
- the proposition
- refusal
- catalyst

ACT TWO (Confrontation):
- threshold
- new world
- first test
- the ally
- midpoint reversal
- darkest hour
- decision

ACT THREE (Resolution):
- preparation
- final confrontation
- aftermath
- [closing scene — already written]
```

**Deliverable**: One-word outline spanning three acts (15-25 beats).

---

### Step 4: Character Wallet Construction (Pattern 4)

For every major character, build a psychology wallet:

| Wallet Item | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Name & Age** | |
| **What they love** (2-3 things) | |
| **What they fear** (1-2 things) | |
| **Neuroses** | Specific behavioral quirks that reveal psychology |
| **Anger triggers** | What makes them disproportionately angry |
| **Giddiness** | What makes them childishly happy |
| **Speech pattern** | Verbal tics, vocabulary level, rhythm |
| **Wallet contents** | Literal: what's in their pocket/purse/phone? |
| **Backstory seed** | One formative event that shaped who they are |
| **Theme connection** | How do they embody or challenge the theme? |

**Deliverable**: Character wallets for 3-5 major characters.

---

### Step 5: Scene-Level Architecture

Expand each one-word beat into a scene description using Visual Prose Cinematography (Pattern 7):

For each scene, write:
1. **Time of day** (chosen for emotional register — see HK-8)
2. **Weather/atmosphere** (sensory immersion)
3. **What happens** (action, not dialogue)
4. **Subtext** (what's really being communicated beneath the surface)
5. **Theme application** (how this scene relates to the compass)

**Deliverable**: 15-25 scene descriptions, each 3-5 sentences of visual prose.

---

### Step 6: Theme Pressure Test

Review every scene against the theme compass:
- Does this scene apply to the theme? Even obliquely?
- If not — can it be reworked to connect, or should it be cut?
- Are there three consecutive scenes with the same emotional register? Insert contrast.

**Deliverable**: Revised scene outline with theme annotations.

---

## Output Format

```
TITLE: [Working Title]
THEME: [One sentence]
GENRE: [Primary / Secondary]
TONE: [2-3 adjectives]

OPENING SCENE:
[Full visual prose — 2-3 pages]

CLOSING SCENE:
[Full visual prose — 1-2 pages]

CHARACTER WALLETS:
[Major characters with full wallet entries]

SCENE OUTLINE:
[15-25 scenes with visual prose descriptions, time of day, and theme annotations]
```

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Theme stated in under 12 words
- [ ] Opening creates a "home" — someone wants to live in this world
- [ ] Ending feels like the inevitable destination of the opening
- [ ] Every scene connects to the theme (even obliquely)
- [ ] Each major character has a distinct voice and full wallet
- [ ] Visual prose present in scene descriptions (not just slug lines)
- [ ] Middle was *discovered*, not over-planned
