# Villain Psychology Builder

Build antagonists who are "intelligently evil" — not mustache-twirling cartoon villains, but psychologically complete human beings whose motivations make uncomfortable sense. Roth's villains are often the most fascinating characters in his stories because they're right about something.

## Pre-Flight

Read these files before executing:
1. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/genius.md`
2. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/genius-patterns.md` (Patterns 4, 6)

## Input Required

- **Story context** — genre, theme, world
- **Protagonist** — who the villain opposes
- **Type of antagonism** — systemic, personal, ideological, physical

---

## Workflow

### Step 1: The Sympathetic Foundation

Every Roth villain starts with something true:

> **What is the villain RIGHT about?**
>
> Roth's villains:
> - *The Insider*: The tobacco executives are right that the system protects corporate speech
> - *Munich*: The targets are right that their cause has legitimacy
> - *Killers of the Flower Moon*: Hale genuinely believes in his benevolence

The villain must have at least one legitimate grievance, correct observation, or defensible position. Without this, they're a cardboard obstacle.

---

### Step 2: The Full Villain Wallet

Build their psychology wallet (same rigor as heroes):

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **What they believe** | Their ideology or worldview, articulated sincerely |
| **What they want** | The specific outcome they're pursuing |
| **What justified their first moral compromise** | The origin of their villainy |
| **What they love** | 2-3 things they genuinely care about |
| **Their blind spot** | What they can't see about themselves |
| **Their version of the story** | How they'd describe events from their POV |
| **Their voice** | Educated or crude? Calm or volatile? Direct or manipulative? |
| **Physical presence** | How they occupy space — dominating, slithering, unnervingly still? |

---

### Step 3: The Mirror Test

A great villain is a **dark mirror** of the protagonist:

| Protagonist | Villain | Shared Trait | Different Choice |
|-------------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| [name] | [name] | Both value [X] | Protag chooses [Y], villain chooses [Z] |

The villain should be *one decision away* from being the hero. They share an origin point but diverged. This creates the unsettling question: "Could I have become this person?"

---

### Step 4: Intelligence Calibration

A villain who is dumber than the protagonist creates no tension. Calibrate:

- **Strategic intelligence**: Can they anticipate the protagonist's moves?
- **Emotional intelligence**: Can they read and manipulate people?
- **Self-awareness**: Do they know they're doing something wrong, or are they genuinely deluded?
- **Adaptability**: When their plan fails, how quickly do they adjust?

Mark each as Low / Medium / High. At least two should be High.

---

### Step 5: The Humanity Moment

Give the villain at least one scene where they're undeniably human:

- A moment of genuine tenderness (Hale with his family in *Killers*)
- A flash of self-doubt (a moment alone where the mask drops)
- An interaction with someone they have no reason to manipulate

This moment must NOT redeem them. It complicates them. The audience should feel uncomfortable feeling empathy.

---

## Output Format

```
VILLAIN: [Name]
TYPE: [Systemic / Personal / Ideological / Physical]

WHAT THEY'RE RIGHT ABOUT:
[The legitimate truth at the core of their worldview]

FULL WALLET:
[Complete psychology wallet]

MIRROR TEST:
[Protagonist-villain comparison — shared trait and divergent choice]

INTELLIGENCE CALIBRATION:
- Strategic: [L/M/H]
- Emotional: [L/M/H]
- Self-awareness: [L/M/H]
- Adaptability: [L/M/H]

HUMANITY MOMENT:
[One scene where they're undeniably human — described in visual prose]

VOICE SAMPLE:
[A short passage of the villain speaking — distinct from all other characters]
```

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Villain is right about at least one thing
- [ ] Full psychology wallet completed (not just motivation)
- [ ] Mirror relationship to protagonist established
- [ ] At least two intelligence dimensions rated High
- [ ] One humanity moment that complicates without redeeming
- [ ] Voice is distinct from protagonist and supporting cast
