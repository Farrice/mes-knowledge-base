# Adaptation Transmuter

Transform source material into a screenplay using Eric Roth's adaptation philosophy: the source gives you a foundation to build on, but 60-70% of what ends up on screen is original creation. Bad books make good movies. The profound idea is the seed; everything else grows from scratch.

## Pre-Flight

Read these files before executing:
1. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/genius.md`
2. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/hidden-knowledge.md` (HK-5)

## Input Required

- **Source material** — novel, short story, article, true story, or concept
- **What attracted you to it** — the core idea that sparked interest
- **Target format** — feature film, limited series, pilot

---

## Workflow

### Step 1: Source Excavation

Read the source completely. Then answer:

1. **The Profound Idea**: What is the single most powerful concept here? (For Button, it was "aging backwards." Everything else was disposable.)
2. **The Emotional Core**: What feeling does this material provoke at its best?
3. **The Cinematic Potential**: What could this look like on screen that it can't do on the page?
4. **The Excess**: What needs to be cut because it's literary, not cinematic?

**Roth's rule**: "I used to underline so dramatically… and then also I was able to use it more as something to look back at."

---

### Step 2: Freedom Assessment

Rate the source on a scale:

| Source Quality | Adaptation Freedom | Strategy |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Bad source, brilliant concept** | Maximum freedom — invent everything | *Benjamin Button* approach: keep the idea, build from scratch |
| **Good source, cinematic structure** | Moderate freedom — reshape and deepen | *The Insider* approach: compress, combine characters, intensify |
| **Great source, complex narrative** | Selective freedom — honor the spine, reinvent details | *Killers of the Flower Moon* approach: find the angle in, restructure POV |
| **True story** | High freedom with factual anchors | *Munich* approach: accurate events, invented inner lives |

---

### Step 3: Character Transplant

For each key character from the source:
- **Keep, cut, or combine?** (Roth routinely combines 3 source characters into 1)
- **What's their cinematic motivation?** (may differ from the book's)
- **Build their wallet** — use Character Psychology Engine for each major transplanted character
- **Find the fanciful element** — what makes them more than real?

---

### Step 4: Structure Reimagining

The source's structure is almost never the film's structure. Answer:
- What's the **opening image** that invites audiences home?
- What's the **closing image** that completes the emotional arc?
- Should this be **linear, non-linear, or time-spanning**?
- What can be told **visually** that the source told verbally?

Build a one-word outline (Pattern 14) that may have zero resemblance to the source's chapter structure.

---

### Step 5: Theme Extraction

The source's theme and the film's theme may differ. Ask:
- What theme did the source author intend?
- What theme does the material *actually* embody when you strip away the author's intentions?
- What theme resonates with a contemporary audience?

Lock the film's theme. It may be adjacent to the source's — or perpendicular.

---

### Step 6: Originality Integration

For each major sequence:
| Source Element | Keep/Adapt/Invent | Reasoning |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------|
| [scene/element] | | |

Target: at least 60% of the screenplay is original invention. The source is "something to look back at," not something to transcribe.

## Output Format

```
SOURCE: [Title and type]
PROFOUND IDEA: [One sentence — the seed worth keeping]
FREEDOM LEVEL: [Bad source / Good source / Great source / True story]

THEME:
- Source theme: [original author's intent]
- Film theme: [your adaptation's compass]

CHARACTER TRANSPLANTS:
[For each major character: keep/cut/combine + wallet summary]

STRUCTURAL REIMAGINING:
- Opening image: [description]
- Closing image: [description]
- Structure type: [linear / non-linear / time-spanning]
- One-word outline: [15-25 beats]

ORIGINALITY MAP:
[Table showing source vs. invented elements — targeting 60%+ original]
```

## Quality Gate

- [ ] The profound idea is preserved
- [ ] At least 60% of the screenplay is original creation
- [ ] Theme has been independently established (not just inherited from source)
- [ ] Characters have been transplanted with new psychology wallets, not copied
- [ ] Structure serves the film, not the book
