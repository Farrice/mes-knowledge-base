# Time Architecture Engine

Design stories that use time not as a container but as a *character* — unstoppable, crushing, beautiful. Span decades. Age backwards. Compress a life into two hours. Make the audience feel what time does to love, memory, and identity.

## Pre-Flight

Read these files before executing:
1. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/genius.md`
2. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/genius-patterns.md` (Pattern 10)
3. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/hidden-knowledge.md` (HK-8)

## Input Required

- **Story** — plot summary, characters, theme
- **Time span** — how many years/decades does the story cover?
- **Why time matters** — what does time do to these characters that a single-day story couldn't?

---

## Workflow

### Step 1: Temporal Structure Selection

Choose your relationship with time:

| Structure | How It Works | Roth Example | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Epic Span** | Linear across decades — aging, change, historical sweep | *Forrest Gump* (30 years) | Lives defined by era changes |
| **Reverse Chronology** | Time moves backwards — consequences before causes | *Benjamin Button* | Exploring fate, inevitability, irony |
| **Fixed Point** | Same physical space, time flows through it | *Here* (one room across generations) | Place-based stories, family sagas |
| **Compression** | An entire life compressed into tight runtime | *A Star Is Born* (career arc in 2 hours) | Rise-and-fall narratives |
| **Parallel Timelines** | Two time periods illuminating each other | *The Insider* (past exposure, present trial) | Mystery, revelation, justice themes |
| **Memory Architecture** | Present-day frame with non-linear memories | *Munich* (mission interspersed with aftermath) | Trauma, guilt, reckoning stories |

---

### Step 2: Temporal Markers

Design the visual and sensory markers that tell the audience where/when they are:

| Marker Type | Purpose | Examples |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **Physical aging** | Time on the body | Gray hair, slower movement, changing posture |
| **Technology shifts** | Time in the world | Rotary phone → iPhone, horse → car |
| **Fashion/decor** | Time in the details | Bell-bottoms → skinny jeans, wallpaper patterns |
| **Music** | Time in the ear | Each decade has its soundtrack |
| **Language shifts** | Time in speech | Slang, formality levels, vocabulary |
| **Environmental change** | Time in space | Trees growing, buildings appearing, neighborhoods transforming |

Choose 3-4 primary markers and apply consistently.

---

### Step 3: The Time-Theme Connection

How does time interact with your theme?

| Theme | Time's Role | Dramatic Tension |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Love | Time as enemy — eroding, aging, separating | Can love survive time's assault? |
| Justice | Time as revealer — truths surface, consequences arrive | How long before the truth catches up? |
| Identity | Time as sculptor — shaping who we become | Are we the same person across decades? |
| Memory | Time as editor — selecting, distorting, preserving | What do we choose to remember? |
| Mortality | Time as countdown — finite, precious, unstoppable | "You can't stop it, so you have to appreciate it" |

---

### Step 4: Transition Architecture

Design how you move between time periods:

**Methods:**
- **The Match Cut** — visual rhyme between eras (child's hand → elderly hand)
- **The Object Bridge** — same object in different times (a watch, a photograph, a house)
- **The Sound Bridge** — audio from one era bleeding into another
- **The Weather Shift** — same location, different season = different era
- **The Direct Address** — narrator moves us through time ("That was the year everything changed")

Each transition should feel inevitable, not arbitrary.

---

### Step 5: Emotional Pacing Across Time

Map the emotional register across your time span:

```
DECADE 1: [Emotional quality — hope, innocence, energy]
DECADE 2: [Emotional quality — ambition, struggle, first love]
DECADE 3: [Emotional quality — loss, disillusion, compromise]
DECADE 4: [Emotional quality — wisdom, acceptance, peace]
```

**Key rule**: The emotional arc of time should mirror but not duplicate the plot arc. Time has its own emotional trajectory — from possibility to gravity.

```
TEMPORAL STRUCTURE: [Selected type]

TEMPORAL MARKERS:
[3-4 primary markers with specific details per era]

TRANSITION ARCHITECTURE:
[How you move between time periods — methods and specific instances]

EMOTIONAL MAP:
[Emotional register per era/decade]

SCENE BREAKDOWN BY ERA:
[Which scenes take place when, with visual prose descriptions of each era's atmosphere]
```

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Time serves the theme (not just the plot)
- [ ] Temporal markers are consistent and specific
- [ ] Transitions feel inevitable, not arbitrary
- [ ] Emotional arc across time mirrors but doesn't duplicate plot arc
- [ ] Audience can always orient themselves in time
- [ ] The passage of time creates emotional impact beyond what a single-day story could
