# Collaboration Marriage Protocol

Navigate creative partnerships using Roth's "marriage" framework — a writer-director relationship is not a hierarchy; it's a negotiation between two expert perspectives. The goal is always the "third rail" — a solution neither of you would reach alone.

## Pre-Flight

Read these files before executing:
1. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/genius.md`
2. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/genius-patterns.md` (Pattern 11)
3. `skills/eric-roth-screenwriting-mastery/references/hidden-knowledge.md` (HK-7)

## Input Required

- **Your creative position** — what you believe the scene/project should be
- **Your collaborator's position** — what they want (or what you anticipate they want)
- **The creative question** — the specific point of disagreement or uncertainty

---

## Workflow

### Step 1: Position Mapping

Articulate both positions with genuine respect — not strawmanning the other side:

| Dimension | Your Position | Their Position |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **What they want** | | |
| **Why they're right** | | |
| **What they might be missing** | | |
| **What they bring that you don't** | | |

**Roth's principle**: "I always try to find the answer is somewhere in between, in some other version, in some other way of doing it."

---

### Step 2: The Third Rail Search

Neither position is the answer. The question is: **What would you BOTH be proud of?**

Generate 3 "third rail" options:

1. **Synthesis** — combines elements from both positions into something new
2. **Elevation** — takes the shared concern behind both positions and solves it differently
3. **Surprise** — a solution neither party would have considered that makes both say "oh, that's better"

---

### Step 3: The Director's Final Authority (Humility Check)

Roth's hard truth: "Eventually it's the director who's going to make a decision."

Before presenting options, rate your conviction:
- **Hill to die on** ← this scene/choice fundamentally changes the story's meaning → Push back firmly but once
- **Strong preference** ← you believe your version is better but the alternative isn't harmful → Present both, advocate, accept
- **Mild preference** ← either way works → Defer gracefully

Only fight for hills. Everything else is collaboration.

---

### Step 4: The Yoda Room Method (HK-7)

If the disagreement is complex, convene a wider perspective:
- Identify 2-3 trusted creative voices who could offer insight
- Present the question neutrally — not as "I think X, they think Y"
- Ask: "What would you do?"
- The universal consensus across masters: **authenticity above all else**

---

### Step 5: Resolution Documentation

Record the outcome for future reference:

```
CREATIVE QUESTION: [The specific point]
MY POSITION: [What I wanted]
THEIR POSITION: [What they wanted]
THIRD RAIL CHOSEN: [The solution found]
WHY IT'S BETTER: [What this achieves that neither original could]
CONVICTION LEVEL: [Hill / Strong / Mild]
LESSON LEARNED: [For future collaborations]
```

---

## Application Beyond Film

This framework applies to ANY creative collaboration:
- Writer and editor
- Designer and client
- Content creator and brand
- Musician and producer
- Co-founders disagreeing on product direction

The principle is universal: the marriage model beats the hierarchy model because it produces solutions neither party would reach alone.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Both positions articulated with genuine respect
- [ ] At least 3 third-rail options generated
- [ ] Conviction level honestly rated
- [ ] If Hill-level conviction: pushback was firm, specific, and done once
- [ ] Final decision documented with lessons learned
