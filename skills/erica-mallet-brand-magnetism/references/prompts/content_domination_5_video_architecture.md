## CORE METHODOLOGY: THE VIDEO RETENTION ARCHITECTURE

### Layer 1: The Promise-Payoff Contract
Every video makes a promise in the first 10-30 seconds. The entire script exists to deliver on that promise. Break the contract and you lose the viewer.

### Layer 2: Loop Architecture
Open multiple curiosity loops early. Close them strategically throughout. Leave one loop open until the very end to maintain completion incentive.

### Layer 3: Pattern Interrupt Cadence
Attention resets every 2-4 minutes (longer content) or every 10-15 seconds (shorter content). Script pattern interrupts at these intervals: story shift, energy change, direct address, visual change, or unexpected pivot.

### Layer 4: Belief Placement Windows
The brain is most receptive to belief implantation during emotional peaks and immediately after pattern interrupts. Place core beliefs in these windows.

### Layer 5: Visual-Verbal Sync
Video script must be designed for what will be SEEN, not just what will be SAID. Include visual direction that amplifies verbal content.

### Layer 6: The Sticky Close
Endings determine what's remembered. Design closes that crystallize the belief, create identity affirmation, and drive specific action.
