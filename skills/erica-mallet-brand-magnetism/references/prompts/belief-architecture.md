# Belief Architecture

Build complete brand identity system with through-line, themes, beliefs, and enemy.

## Role

You are executing Erica Mallet's belief-based brand architectureâ€”the complete identity system that transforms random content into coherent worldview.

## Required Input

- **BACKGROUND**: Skills, experiences, interests, career history
- **CURRENT CONTENT**: What you're creating now (if any)
- **AUDIENCE**: Who you want to reach
- **FRUSTRATIONS**: What you're tired of seeing in your space

## Execution

1. **Through-Line Discovery**: Find the thread connecting ALL your experiences/skills

2. **Theme Extraction**: Identify 3 broad themes you could discuss for 5+ years

3. **Belief Matrix**: Under each theme, articulate 3 specific beliefs (= 9 beliefs total)

4. **Enemy Definition**: Name the force/system/approach you stand against

5. **Hero Definition**: Who is your audience becoming with your help?

6. **Tone Triad**: Choose 3 words that describe your voice

7. **Positioning Statement**: Synthesize into "I help [audience] [outcome] through [unique mechanism]"

## Output

- **Through-Line**: The connecting thread
- **3 Themes**: Broad areas of focus
- **9 Beliefs**: 3 per theme, specific and staked
- **Enemy Profile**: What you oppose and why
- **Hero Definition**: Audience transformation
- **Tone Words**: 3 voice descriptors
- **Positioning Statement**: Complete summary

## Example Usage

**Input**: Career-transition coach, former corporate hiring manager, frustrated by generic "passion" advice

**Through-Line**: Strategic courage (calculated risk-taking in career moves)

**Themes**: 
1. Resume Strategy
2. Interview Psychology  
3. Negotiation Power

**Enemy**: Hustle culture's "follow your passion" advice that ignores practical realities

**Positioning**: I help corporate professionals find fulfilling roles without financial risk through strategic courage methodology
