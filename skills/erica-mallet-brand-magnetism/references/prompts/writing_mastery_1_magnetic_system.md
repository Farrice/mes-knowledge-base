## CORE METHODOLOGY: THE MAGNETIC WRITING ARCHITECTURE

### The Three Laws of Magnetic Writing

**LAW 1: TRUTH OVER INFORMATION**
Information is commodity. Truth is rare. Magnetic writing doesn't share facts—it reveals truths that readers recognize but have never heard articulated. The reader's response should be "YES, that's exactly it" not "Huh, I didn't know that."

**LAW 2: FEELING OVER UNDERSTANDING**
Understanding is cognitive. Feeling is visceral. Magnetic writing doesn't explain things so readers understand—it creates experiences so readers FEEL. The goal isn't comprehension—it's resonance.

**LAW 3: SPECIFICITY OVER UNIVERSALITY**
The more specific you are, the more universal you become. Generic writing connects with no one. Writing about the exact moment at 3am when you can't sleep and you're replaying the conversation you wish you'd handled differently—THAT connects with everyone who has ever been human.

### Layer 1: The Magnetic Opening Architecture

**THE FIRST SENTENCE COMMANDMENTS**

Your first sentence must do ONE of these:

| Type | What It Does | Example Pattern |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **THE PATTERN INTERRUPT** | Violates expectation immediately | "Everything you know about X is wrong." |
| **THE RECOGNITION STRIKE** | Describes their private experience | "You know that moment when..." |
| **THE CURIOSITY GAP** | Opens a loop that demands closure | "There's something nobody tells you about X." |
| **THE PROVOCATION** | Challenges a sacred cow | "X isn't what you think it is." |
| **THE SCENE DROP** | Places them in a vivid moment | "It's 11pm. You're staring at [specific thing]." |
| **THE CONFESSION** | Vulnerability that creates trust | "I used to believe [common belief]. I was wrong." |

**THE OPENING PARAGRAPH FORMULA**

```
SENTENCE 1: Stop them (any method above)
SENTENCE 2-3: Deepen the hook (add specificity or stakes)
SENTENCE 4: The turn (signal that insight/transformation is coming)
```

### Layer 2: The Triple-Layer Content Architecture

Every piece of magnetic writing operates on three levels:

**LEVEL 1: SURFACE (Entertainment)**
What makes it enjoyable to read.
- Rhythm and pacing
- Vivid language and specificity
- Story and scenario
- Voice and personality
- Surprise and delight

**LEVEL 2: MIDDLE (Insight)**
What makes it valuable to consume.
- Reframe or paradigm shift
- Practical application
- New understanding
- Actionable takeaway
- Framework or model

**LEVEL 3: DEEP (Meaning)**
What makes it matter.
- Universal truth
- Emotional resonance
- Identity connection
- Worldview implication
- Life significance

**THE INTEGRATION PRINCIPLE:**
Magnetic writing weaves all three levels together—never just one. Entertainment without insight is empty. Insight without entertainment is ignored. Both without meaning is forgettable.

---

### Layer 3: The Rhythm and Pacing System

**SENTENCE VARIATION PATTERNS**

Magnetic writing has RHYTHM. The reader should feel the writing, not just read it.

**Pattern 1: The Build**
```
Short.
A bit longer now.
Building toward something important that takes more space to develop.
And then: short again.
```

**Pattern 2: The Staccato**
```
This is important.
Read it again.
Slower.
This matters.
```

**Pattern 3: The Flow**
```
Sometimes you need a longer sentence that carries the reader through 
an idea, building momentum like a wave gathering force before it 
crashes on the shore of meaning.

Then stop.
```

**Pattern 4: The Question Turn**
```
Statement of fact or observation.
But here's the thing.
What if that's not actually true?
What if the opposite is?
```

**THE RHYTHM RULE:**
If all your sentences are the same length, your writing is dead. Vary between 3-word punches and 25-word flows. The variation creates music.

### Layer 4: The Emotional Architecture

**EMOTIONAL BEATS IN WRITING**

Every piece should move through emotional states:

**BEAT 1: ATTENTION (Curiosity/Surprise)**
The opening captures attention through gap or disruption.

**BEAT 2: RECOGNITION (Being Seen)**
Early in the piece, the reader should feel "this is about me."

**BEAT 3: TENSION (Stakes)**
What's at risk? Why does this matter? What happens if they don't get this?

**BEAT 4: INSIGHT (The Turn)**
The paradigm shift, the reframe, the new way of seeing.

**BEAT 5: RELEASE (Relief/Hope)**
The tension resolves into possibility, permission, or path forward.

**BEAT 6: LANDING (Meaning/Belonging)**
The final beat connects to something larger—identity, truth, tribe.

**THE ARC PRINCIPLE:**
Not every beat needs equal weight. But skipping beats creates incomplete experiences. A piece with insight but no recognition feels preachy. A piece with recognition but no insight feels empty.

---

### Layer 5: The Truth-Finding Protocol

**HOW TO FIND TRUTHS WORTH WRITING**

Magnetic writing requires truths that resonate. Here's how to find them:

**METHOD 1: The Private Experience Mine**
What do people experience but never discuss? What do they feel but never admit? What do they think but never say? THAT'S where truth lives.

Questions to ask:
- What are people embarrassed to admit about [topic]?
- What do people secretly feel but publicly deny?
- What's the experience of [thing] that nobody talks about?

**METHOD 2: The Conventional Wisdom Inversion**
What does everyone believe that's actually wrong? What advice is given that doesn't work? What "obvious truth" is actually false?

Questions to ask:
- What's the standard advice about [topic] that I think is backwards?
- What does everyone assume that doesn't match reality?
- What worked for me that contradicts conventional wisdom?

**METHOD 3: The Pattern Recognition Reveal**
What pattern have you noticed that explains seemingly unrelated things? What connects dots that others haven't connected?

Questions to ask:
- What do [seemingly different situations] have in common?
- What underlying dynamic explains [surface behaviors]?
- What's the real reason [thing happens] that nobody sees?

**METHOD 4: The Permission Mining**
What do people need permission to do/feel/believe that no one is giving them? What burden are they carrying that they don't need to?

Questions to ask:
- What are people waiting for permission to do?
- What guilt or shame are they carrying unnecessarily?
- What would relieve them to hear?
