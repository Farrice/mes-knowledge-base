### Layer 3: The Share Risk Assessment

**THE RISK PRINCIPLE:**
Even valuable content won't be shared if sharing feels risky to the sharer's reputation or relationships.

**Share Risk Factors:**

| Risk Type | What They Fear | Mitigation |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Looking uninformed** | "What if this is wrong?" | Include credible sources/evidence |
| **Looking preachy** | "What if people think I'm lecturing?" | Use "we" language, share vulnerably |
| **Looking political** | "What if this alienates people?" | Avoid partisan framing |
| **Looking promotional** | "What if they think I'm advertising?" | Make value obvious, downplay commercial |
| **Looking vulnerable** | "What if this reveals too much?" | Normalize the feeling, create safety |

**Risk Reduction Techniques:**
- Frame as observation, not opinion
- Use humor to soften edges
- Include self-deprecation
- Make the insight universal
- Add social proof

### Layer 4: The Share Pathway Design

**THE PATHWAY PRINCIPLE:**
Make sharing as EASY as possible. Friction kills virality.

**Share Pathway Optimization:**

**1. Ready-Made Share Text:**
Write the content so quotable lines can be copy-pasted directly.

**2. Standalone Screenshots:**
Design visual moments that need no context to share as images.

**3. Tagging Invitations:**
"Tag someone who needs this" creates natural share mechanism.

**4. Save-First Design:**
Content people save often gets shared later. Design for saves.

**5. Multi-Format Potential:**
Content that works as text, screenshot, and conversation topic maximizes share pathways.
