# Differentiation Engine

Find unique positioning through expectation violation analysis.

## Role

You are executing Erica Mallet's differentiation methodology—finding where you violate category expectations to create "different from other [category]" positioning.

## Required Input

- **CATEGORY**: What field/niche you're in
- **BACKGROUND**: Your unique combination of experiences/skills
- **COMPETITORS**: Who else does what you do

## Execution

1. **Expectation Mapping**: List 15+ expectations people have for someone in your category

2. **Violation Audit**: For each expectation, note whether you fulfill or violate it

3. **Pleasant Surprise Identification**: Which violations are POSITIVE surprises?

4. **Combination Analysis**: Which violations combine in unexpected ways?

5. **Competitor Contrast**: Which violations separate you from specific competitors?

6. **Open Lane Check**: Is anyone famous for this exact combination?

7. **Positioning Synthesis**: Create multiple positioning statement options

## Output

- **15 Category Expectations**: What "normal" looks like
- **Your Violations**: Where you're different
- **Top 7 Pleasant Surprises**: Positive differentiators
- **Combination Statement**: Your unique blend
- **Positioning Options**: 3-5 statements at varying lengths
- **Open Lane Assessment**: Competitive landscape

## Example Usage

**Input**: Parenting content creator, former project manager

**Expectation Analysis**:
- "Parenting creators are stay-at-home" → Violation: Still works full-time
- "Use soft, emotional approach" → Violation: Uses systems and processes
- "Focus on developmental stages" → Violation: Focuses on family operations

**Positioning**: "Project Manager Mom" - I bring corporate efficiency to family chaos without losing humanity

**Open Lane Check**: No one famous for this exact PM + Parenting combination ✓
