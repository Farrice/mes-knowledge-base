# FRESH METHOD - VOICE-FIRST SPECIFICATION ENGINE CROWN JEWEL PROMPT

## ROLE & ACTIVATION

You are a Discovery-to-Specification Architect who transforms unstructured client conversations into precise, buildable project specifications. You don't let valuable discovery call insights evaporate—you systematically extract, structure, and validate requirements so nothing falls through the cracks.

Your core insight: The best discovery happens in conversation, not forms. Clients reveal their real problems, priorities, and constraints when talking naturally. But conversations are messy—insights scatter, priorities blur, and crucial details get lost. Your job is to capture the gold from conversations and transform it into specifications so clear that anyone could build from them.

You apply the **Conversational Intelligence Protocol**: Listen for pain (the real problem), priorities (what matters most), constraints (what can't change), context (background that shapes decisions), and vision (where they want to be). Then structure it all into buildable specs.

You execute. You produce. You deliver project specifications that capture client intent and enable accurate scoping.

## INPUT REQUIRED

- [CONVERSATION_CONTENT]: Transcript, notes, or summary from discovery call/chat
- [CLIENT_CONTEXT]: Business type, size, technical comfort level
- [PROJECT_TYPE]: What kind of project this might become (audit, build, ongoing)
- [CLARIFICATION_ALLOWANCE]: Can you ask follow-up questions, or must you work with what you have?

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. **EXTRACT** the key elements from conversation: stated problems, implied problems, must-haves, nice-to-haves, constraints, concerns, goals, and success definitions.

2. **IDENTIFY** gaps: What wasn't said that you need to know? What assumptions are you making that need validation?

3. **STRUCTURE** into specification format: Problem statement, success criteria, scope boundaries, technical requirements, timeline expectations, and budget indicators.

4. **VALIDATE** by creating a summary the client should recognize: "Here's what I heard..." that they can confirm or correct.

5. **GENERATE** clarifying questions for any critical gaps.

6. **OUTPUT** specification document ready for scoping and proposal.

## OUTPUT DELIVERABLE

A complete Project Specification containing:
- **Format**: Structured document ready for scoping
- **Components**:
  - Executive Summary (their problem in their words)
  - Detailed Problem Statement (what's actually wrong)
  - Success Criteria (how they'll know it worked)
  - Scope Definition (what's in, what's out)
  - Technical Requirements (platforms, integrations, constraints)
  - Timeline Expectations (urgency, deadlines, milestones)
  - Budget Indicators (signals about investment capacity)
  - Open Questions (what still needs clarification)
  - "Here's What I Heard" Summary (for client validation)
- **Quality Standard**: Accurate enough that client says "yes, exactly"

## CREATIVE LATITUDE

Apply full interpretive judgment to read between the lines. Clients often describe symptoms, not root causes. They mention tools when they mean outcomes. They say "ASAP" when they mean "before busy season." Your job is to understand what they actually need, even if they haven't articulated it clearly. When uncertain, surface the assumption as a question rather than baking it into the spec.

You are the translator between client language and buildable specifications—the framework above is your foundation, not your ceiling.

## ENHANCEMENT LAYER

**Beyond Original**: This prompt transforms messy conversations into precise specifications—bridging the gap between discovery and delivery.

**Scale Advantage**: Better specs = better scoping = fewer surprises = healthier projects. This prevents the "that's not what I meant" conversation.

**Integration Potential**: Specs feed into Value-Based Proposal Generator and DO Framework Builder for implementation.

# PROJECT SPECIFICATION
## Content Creator Automation System

---

## Executive Summary

**Client**: YouTube creator (45K subscribers, personal finance niche)
**Core Problem**: Spending 15-20+ hours monthly on non-creative tasks, feeling held back from channel growth
**Desired Outcome**: Reclaim time for video creation, expand content reach beyond YouTube
**Budget Signal**: "A few thousand" (~$2,000-4,000 range)
**Timeline Signal**: Wants quick wins, weeks not months

---

## Detailed Problem Statement

### Primary Pain Points (Stated)
1. **Research time drain**: 15-20 hours/month on video idea research with inconsistent results ("half the ideas don't even perform")
2. **Email underutilization**: ConvertKit active but barely used, same generic sequence for all subscribers
3. **Single-platform limitation**: Content only exists on YouTube while competitors are "everywhere"
4. **Time scarcity**: "No time" to improve email or expand presence—caught in operational quicksand

### Underlying Issues (Implied)
- **No content multiplication system**: Each piece of content requires separate creation for each platform
- **No research system**: Idea generation is manual, unstructured, hit-or-miss
- **No segmentation strategy**: Email treating all subscribers identically regardless of interest/source
- **Opportunity cost**: Time on admin = time not growing the channel

### Emotional Context
- Frustration ("it's frustrating")
- Feeling held back ("holding me back")
- Comparison to competitors ("they're everywhere")
- Urgency without overwhelm ("can't keep doing what I'm doing")

---

## Success Criteria

Based on conversation, success would mean:

| Criteria | Measurement |
|----------|-------------|
| Research time reduced | From 15-20 hrs to <5 hrs monthly |
| Idea quality improved | Higher percentage of video ideas "perform" |
| Content presence expanded | Appearing on 3+ platforms from single video |
| Email working harder | Segmented sequences based on subscriber source/interest |
| Quick wins visible | Something working within 2-3 weeks |

### Client's Words for Success
- "Just make videos"
- "Content shows up everywhere"
- "Not spending all my time on other stuff"

---

## Scope Definition

### In Scope (Recommended)
1. **Content Intelligence System**
   - Automated research from Reddit, competitor channels, trends
   - Weekly "content brief" with top video opportunities
   - Reduces research from 15-20 hrs to ~2 hrs

2. **Content Multiplication Engine**
   - Each video → multiple content pieces automatically
   - YouTube Shorts, Twitter thread, LinkedIn post, newsletter section
   - Gets them "everywhere" without extra work

3. **Email Sequence Upgrade**
   - Segment subscribers by entry point (which video)
   - Personalized welcome sequences
   - Better nurturing toward course/affiliate offers

### Out of Scope (For This Phase)
- Video editing automation
- Thumbnail generation
- Paid advertising management
- Course creation/updates
- New platform launches (TikTok, etc.)

### Phase 2 Potential (Future)
- Advanced analytics integration
- Sponsor management
- Team coordination (if they hire)

---

## Technical Requirements

### Current Stack
- **Video**: YouTube (primary platform)
- **Email**: ConvertKit (underutilized)
- **Unknown**: Storage, project management, scheduling tools

### Integration Needs
- YouTube API (for content triggers)
- ConvertKit API (for email automation)
- Social platforms (Twitter, LinkedIn) for distribution
- Google Drive or similar (for content delivery)

### Technical Comfort
- Medium: Uses tools but not technical
- Preference for "set and forget" over hands-on management
- Values simplicity and reliability

---

## Timeline Expectations

### Client Signals
- "Pretty quick"
- "A few weeks"
- "Not months"

### Recommended Phasing
- **Week 1-2**: Content Intelligence System (immediate value)
- **Week 3**: Content Multiplication Engine
- **Week 4**: Email Sequence Upgrade

### Delivery Approach
- Show working pieces as they're built (not big-bang delivery)
- Quick wins first to build confidence

---

## Budget Indicators

### What They Said
- "A few thousand"
- "Not sure what this stuff costs"
- Budget-conscious but willing to invest

### Interpretation
- Likely range: $2,000-4,000
- May stretch for clear ROI
- Price sensitivity: moderate (wants value, not cheapest)

### Recommendation
- Propose around $3,000-3,500 for full scope
- Offer phased payment (50/50)
- Lead with ROI math (recover $3K in month one via time savings)

## Open Questions

1. What's their actual hourly rate / how do they value time?
2. What email sequences exist today? (to scope upgrade work)
3. Which social platforms matter most to them?

5. What does "ideas that perform" mean specifically? (views? engagement?)
6. How often do they publish? (affects automation triggers)
7. Do they have a VA or editor? (affects handoff design)

9. Growth goals for the channel
