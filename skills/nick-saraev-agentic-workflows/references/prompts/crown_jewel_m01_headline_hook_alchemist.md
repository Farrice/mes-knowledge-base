## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: PSYCHOLOGY MAPPING
Identify the dominant emotional drivers for this audience and topic:
- **Fear**: What are they afraid of happening or missing?
- **Desire**: What do they desperately want?
- **Curiosity**: What pattern interrupt would stop them?
- **Identity**: How do they see themselves? How do they want to be seen?
- **Frustration**: What have they tried that hasn't worked?

### Phase 2: FORMULA APPLICATION
Generate headlines using these proven frameworks:

**The Nicolas Cole Credibility + Curiosity Matrix:**
- [Credibility Marker] + [Curiosity Gap] + [Implied Promise]
- "A Harvard neuroscientist explains why most productivity advice is backwards"

**The Bond Halbert Specificity Amplifier:**
- Replace every vague word with a specific one
- "Make more money" â†’ "Add $2,847 to your monthly income"
- "Lose weight fast" â†’ "Drop 11 pounds in 14 days without giving up carbs"

**The Pattern Interrupt Formula:**
- [Conventional Wisdom] + "is wrong. Here's why:"
- Contradict what everyone believes â†’ create irresistible curiosity gap

**The "They Ask, You Answer" Framework:**
- Start with the exact question they're typing into Google
- "How do I [their exact search]? I tested 7 methods. Here's what worked."

**The Status Play:**
- Appeal to affiliation (belonging) or dominion (achievement)
- "What elite performers do differently in the first hour of their day"

### Phase 3: STRESS TESTING
Score each headline against these criteria (1-10):
- **Specificity**: Does it include numbers, timeframes, or concrete details?
- **Credibility**: Does it include proof or authority markers?
- **Curiosity**: Does it open a loop that MUST be closed?
- **Relevance**: Does it speak to their exact situation?
- **Emotion**: Does it trigger feeling, not just thinking?

### Phase 4: VARIANT GENERATION
Create multiple angles for the same content:
- Fear angle
- Desire angle
- Curiosity angle
- Contrarian angle
- Social proof angle
