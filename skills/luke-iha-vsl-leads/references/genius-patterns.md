## 1. The 7 Micro Lead Types
**What They Do**: Deploy 7 proven opening architectures (50-200 words each), each triggering curiosity through a different psychological mechanism. The micro lead sits ON TOP of the lead body as the initial hook. Independently testable — the fastest path to validating whether an offer's angle resonates.
**Executable Behavior**: For any offer, write ONE micro lead of each type. Rate each for curiosity (1-10) and relevance (1-10). Select top 3 for testing. Test as standalone social posts, email openers, or ad hooks BEFORE committing to full lead development.
**Types**: Contrarian Claim | Insider Confession | Paradoxical Question | Look Closer | Surprise Connection | Invisible Difference | Quiz Opening
**Deploy When**: Starting any VSL, sales page, advertorial, or long-form email. Quick-testing offer angles.
**Success Metric**: At least 5/7 types generate a viable opening. Top 3 scored 14+/20 (curiosity + relevance).

## 2. The Movie Trailer Principle
**What They Do**: Frame the entire lead as a movie trailer — its job is to create desire to WATCH, not desire to BUY. The lead creates questions; the body answers them. The moment you resolve curiosity in the lead, you kill the body's conversion power. Every element exists to stack open loops.
**Executable Behavior**: After writing a lead, audit every paragraph: does it create a NEW question or resolve an existing one? Resolved questions in the lead = leaked conversion power. The lead should end with the reader having 3-5 unresolved curiosity loops that can ONLY be resolved by continuing.
**Deploy When**: Lead quality review. Diagnosing low watch-time or read-through rates. Teaching lead-writing principles.
**Success Metric**: Lead ends with 3+ unresolved curiosity loops. Zero mechanism reveals in the lead.

## 3. Lead Body Architecture
**What They Do**: Structure the lead body in 4 distinct phases: Hook (micro lead), Build Belief (story + problem + failed solutions + mechanism tease + credibility), Handle Objections (skepticism + proof + contrarian), Close (future pacing + fascinations + constraints + bridge to body). Each phase uses specific copy blocks at specific velocities.
**Executable Behavior**: Write each phase as a distinct section. Use the 15-element checklist to ensure completeness. Audit copy block velocity within each phase — Build Belief should cycle Pain→Curiosity→Promise at moderate velocity. Handle Objections should cycle Proof→Conditions→Constraints at measured velocity.
**Deploy When**: Writing any lead longer than 500 words. Structuring VSL scripts. Planning advertorials.
**Success Metric**: Each phase is independently compelling. Phase transitions feel seamless. No phase is skippable.

## 4. The 15-Element Lead Checklist
**What They Do**: Provide a comprehensive element inventory for lead completeness. Not all 15 are required for every lead, but the best leads hit 10+. Elements: Problem, Promise, Solution tease, Story, Contrarian nature, Unique mechanism, Nickname, Skepticism acknowledgment, Credibility, Testimonials, Constraints, Qualifiers, Fascinations, Future pacing, Failed solutions.
**Executable Behavior**: After completing a lead draft, score it against all 15 elements. Each element present = 1 point. Below 8 = significant gaps that need addressing. Between 8-10 = solid lead. Above 10 = strong lead. Focus additions on the highest-impact missing elements.
**Deploy When**: Lead quality auditing. Post-draft gap analysis. Competitive lead comparison.
**Success Metric**: Score 10+/15 on lead checklist. Missing elements are intentional omissions with stated rationale.

## 5. Fascination Bullet Engineering
**What They Do**: Create curiosity-dense bullets that each open an independent loop. The "you'll also discover..." engine. Each fascination is a PROMISE of knowledge the reader cannot verify without buying. Specificity, numbers, and impossible-sounding claims drive fascination strength.
**Executable Behavior**: Take the product's feature/benefit list. For each feature, write 3 fascination bullets using these formulas: "The [specific] reason why [common thing] is actually [surprising result]" | "How to [outcome] in [short time] — without [sacrifice]" | "The [number]-second trick that [dramatic result]." Filter for: each bullet must be independently curiosity-triggering and incomplete (resolution requires the product).
**Deploy When**: VSL lead close sections. Sales page bullet lists. Email teasers. Social media content hooks.
**Success Metric**: Each fascination is independently shareable. Reader can't verify the promise without the product.

## 6. AI-Assisted Lead Architecture
**What They Do**: Use AI for STRUCTURAL completeness (hitting all 15 elements, generating all 7 micro lead types, producing fascination variations) while reserving HUMAN judgment for voice, emotional specificity, and strategic selection. AI generates the scaffold; human adds the soul.
**Executable Behavior**: Research → AI drafts 7 micro leads → human selects/refines → AI expands lead body from 15-element brief → human injects voice and story → AI generates 30+ fascinations → human culls to strongest → human does final voice pass over complete lead.
**Deploy When**: Lead writing sessions (every time). AI speeds up the structural phase by 5-10x.
**Success Metric**: Lead passes human quality gate. AI involvement invisible in final output. Structure is complete AND voice is authentic.

## 7. Reverse Outline Engineering
**What They Do**: Deconstruct winning leads into their architectural skeleton — which elements appear, in what order, with what copy blocks at what velocity. Then rebuild the architecture with new product content. Learn the ARCHITECTURE, not the words.
**Executable Behavior**: Take a winning lead. Step 1: Tag every sentence with its copy block. Step 2: Identify the 15-element checklist items present. Step 3: Map the micro lead type. Step 4: Chart the velocity. Step 5: Note phase transitions. Step 6: Rebuild the skeleton with your product's content.
**Deploy When**: Learning from competitors. Swipe file analysis. Client onboarding (analyze their best leads). Skill development.
**Success Metric**: Can articulate WHY each element is placed where it is. Rebuilt lead uses the architecture but sounds original.
