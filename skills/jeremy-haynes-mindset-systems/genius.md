## Genius Patterns

10 mastery patterns decoded from Jeremy Haynes' mindset engineering methodology. These are the practitioner-derived systems that took him from $550K/year to multi-millions, running 12-15 rev-share deals at $100K+/month each.

## Pattern 1: Identity Versioning Protocol

**The Pattern:** Identity is not a fixed thing you discover — it's a document you iterate. Jeremy writes explicit lists of traits and characteristics for his next identity version, posts them visibly, and references them during live situations (sales calls, decisions, conversations). He's on version 47.0.

**Why It's Genius:** Most "identity work" is vague journaling. Jeremy made it operational — a spec sheet you glance at mid-call to course-correct behavior in real time. Each version may change only ONE trait. The granularity makes it actionable rather than aspirational.

**When To Deploy:** Any time a specific trait or characteristic is blocking growth. Not for general "I want to be better" — for "this specific behavior pattern is the ceiling."

**Key Mechanic:** *"Every trait and characteristic of you is fully fluid and dynamic. 100% of what you consider you can change."* The shift is a decision, not a process. Light switch, not dimmer.

## Pattern 2: Dynamic Reasons Engine

**The Pattern:** Motivational drivers expire. The reasons that got you to $1M won't get you to $10M. Old reasons must be consciously identified and swapped for new ones — which may be selfish, philanthropic, or irrational. What matters is emotional stir, not moral purity.

**Why It's Genius:** Solves the "arrived and stuck" syndrome that hits entrepreneurs at every plateau. Most advice says "find your why" once. Jeremy says your why has a shelf life and must be refreshed like an expired subscription.

**When To Deploy:** Revenue plateau, loss of drive, "golden handcuffs" feeling, post-achievement flatness.

**Key Mechanic:** After giving a reasons talk at his mastermind, the number of clients hitting $1M/month nearly doubled (from ~5 to 9 in 3 months). The framework demonstrably accelerates revenue growth.

## Pattern 3: Pain Leverage Inversion

**The Pattern:** Human beings are neurologically wired to avoid pain (Daniel Goleman's blind spot research). Instead of fighting this wiring, weaponize it. Deliberately amplify the pain of your current circumstances until escape velocity is achieved. You can also diminish pain of desired actions using NLP techniques (Richard Bandler — shrink the memory, make it black-and-white, turn down the volume).

**Why It's Genius:** Works WITH neurology instead of against it. Most motivation advice says "overcome fear" — Jeremy says "make your current situation more terrifying than the risk."

**When To Deploy:** Stuck in comfort, unable to make a scary decision, need escape velocity from a limiting situation.

**Key Mechanic:** *"You can justify anything. Look up 'is the sun good, is the sun bad' — you'll have information equal to both. In all decisions we have the ability to choose which direction to use it."*

## Pattern 4: Asymmetric Risk Assessment

**The Pattern:** Risk is rarely 50/50. Most entrepreneurial decisions have significantly more upside than downside (asymmetric upside). The fire/water analogy: if you're on fire and there's a pool that might be water or gas — you're jumping. Because staying on fire is certain death.

**Why It's Genius:** Replaces emotional risk-aversion with structural analysis. Three categories: (1) High asymmetric upside — jump. (2) Unknown unknowns — trust intuition/faith. (3) Asymmetric downside — avoid unless forced.

**When To Deploy:** Any decision paralysis. Especially when "it feels risky" but you haven't actually quantified the risk.

**Key Mechanic:** When you can't risk capital, risk time and relationships instead. "Time and effort based risks have longer cycles but still have financial upside." The podcast-hosting example: Grant was already taking the risk (equipment, flights, studio rental) but not capturing the financial upside (affiliate deals).

## Pattern 5: Revenue Math Decision Framework

**The Pattern:** When facing chaos or a scaling wall, stop everything and do the math. Write it on a wall. What does the goal actually require? Jeremy's pivotal moment: $1M/month ÷ 10 deals = $100K/deal. This simple math invalidated his entire operating model and led to firing 23 of 27 staff.

**Why It's Genius:** The math is always available but rarely done. Jeremy could have done this calculation before scaling to 3 offices and 27 staff — he calls himself "idiotic" for not doing it sooner. The math provides "clear evidence" that cuts through emotional attachment to bad strategies.

**When To Deploy:** Scaling chaos, over-hiring, service proliferation, any time complexity is growing but revenue isn't proportional.

**Key Mechanic:** Start with the goal, work backward to deal structure, then ruthlessly eliminate everything that doesn't serve that structure. The rev-share model ($10K base + 10% net) emerged directly from the math — not from industry benchmarking or mentorship.

**Why It's Genius:** Goes beyond "curate your feed" to include environmental propaganda (moved homes over a sewage smell at $300K cost), kinesthetic input, and deliberate anti-propaganda (following people he demonizes as negative reference points).

## Pattern 7: Responsibility-Expansion Correlation

**The Pattern:** As wealth increases, responsibility increases. Most people's broken mental model: "more responsibility → must risk less." Correct model: "more responsibility → must expand more aggressively to carry the weight higher."

**Why It's Genius:** Identifies and names the exact mental model error that traps family entrepreneurs. Uses the anchor/hot-air-balloon visualization to make the correction intuitive and visceral.

**When To Deploy:** When someone says "I can't take risks because I have a family/employees/obligations." This is the direct counter-framework.

**Key Mechanic:** The broken cycle (get richer → more responsibility → contract → pressure → forced action → eventually get richer) has a massive inefficiency gap that can be eliminated with one mental model shift. Correct cycle: get richer → more responsibility → continue expansion-oriented action → get richer again.

## Pattern 8: Momentum Snowball Effect

**The Pattern:** Building momentum is exponentially harder at the start than at any point after. The snowman metaphor: packing the first ball is brutal. After that, everything compounds. Most people look at untouched snow and decide to drink hot chocolate inside.

**Why It's Genius:** Names the specific trap (comfort alternatives) and the specific mechanism (validation cycles that build psyche). "If you do that for even 13 months, you come out the other side a monster."

**When To Deploy:** In the slog of starting. Before the first major win. When comfort alternatives are most tempting.

**Key Mechanic:** "Excessive accomplishment in short time frames is really just a byproduct of the momentum they've created." The compound effect is real — but only accessible to those who endured the initial packing of the snowball.

## Pattern 9: Scarcity Domination Protocol

**The Pattern:** When scarcity feelings arise — even tiny ones — immediately spend to obliterate them. Don't budget your way out of scarcity mindset. Crush it with action that proves the limitation is false.

**Why It's Genius:** Inverts conventional financial wisdom. Instead of saving to feel safe, spending to prove safety. The cable-snap gym story is the physical metaphor: rage against the limitation until the equipment breaks. "Mission accomplished. The inner voice will shut up now."

**When To Deploy:** Any time a purchase or investment creates anxiety. Any time you're hesitating based on "can I afford this?" rather than "is this strategically sound?"

**Key Mechanic:** *"Spending the money unlocks a heightened level of creativity and a new way to make a fuckload more money that you previously would not have committed to."* The spend itself creates the pressure → creativity → revenue cycle.

## Pattern 10: Validation Cycle Stacking

**The Pattern:** Confidence and capacity aren't built through affirmations — they're built through completed loops: attempt → succeed → internalize proof → attempt bigger. Stack enough loops and you become "a monster."

**Why It's Genius:** Makes confidence mechanical rather than emotional. It's not about belief — it's about evidence accumulation. Each cycle literally expands what you view as possible because you have proof.

**When To Deploy:** Before any major leap. Review stacked validations to establish pattern of capability. Also: design small validation cycles into any new venture to build momentum before the big bet.

**Key Mechanic:** Jeremy's proof chain: sales trainer skills validated repeatedly → quit to start agency ($550K yr1) → scaled to $2M yr2 → did the math → contracted → rev-share model with Dan Luck → doubled Dan's revenue in 2 weeks → validation → scaled Dan to $2M/month in 8 months → model proven → accumulated more deals.
