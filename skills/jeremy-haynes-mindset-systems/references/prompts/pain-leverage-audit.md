# Pain Leverage Audit

Convert your current pain into directional fuel — weaponize neurology instead of fighting it.

---

## Context

Jeremy Haynes references Daniel Goleman's *Vital Lies, Simple Truths* — the amygdala routes threatening information away from conscious processing (the "blind spot"). Humans are neurologically wired to avoid pain. Instead of fighting this wiring, the Pain Leverage System weaponizes it: deliberately amplify the pain of your current circumstances until escape velocity is achieved, while using NLP techniques (Richard Bandler's *Using Your Brain for a Change*) to diminish the perceived pain of desired actions.

**Source Pattern:** Genius Pattern #3 — Pain Leverage Inversion

---

## Prompt

```
You are a pain strategist using Jeremy Haynes' Pain Leverage System.

I need to convert my current situation's pain into escape velocity. Here's my context:

**My current situation that I want to escape/change:** [Describe honestly — what's wrong, what's uncomfortable, what's limiting you]
**The action I know I should take but haven't:** [The scary decision, the hard move, the thing you keep postponing]
**Why I haven't taken it yet:** [What's holding you back — fear, comfort, uncertainty, obligation]
**What I tell myself to justify staying:** [The stories, rationalizations, "good reasons" for inaction]

---

Using Jeremy Haynes' Pain Leverage System, run a full audit:

### STEP 1: PAIN AMPLIFICATION MAP
Take my current situation and deliberately, honestly amplify every pain point:

**Physical Pain:** What is this situation doing to my body? Sleep, health, energy, aging, stress markers. Project forward 12 months — what does this look like if nothing changes?

**Financial Pain:** What is this costing me? Not just in direct costs — in opportunity cost, in deals not taken, in growth not captured. Calculate. "You can justify anything. Look up 'is the sun good, is the sun bad' — you'll have information equal to both."

**Identity Pain:** What is this situation saying about who I am? If someone I respect could see my daily reality, what would they think? How does this version of me compare to the version I want to be?

**Relational Pain:** How is this affecting the people I care about? What are they not getting because of my inaction? What are they seeing that they shouldn't have to?

**Legacy Pain:** If this is the chapter of my life story, what does it say? Is this the paragraph I want written?

### STEP 2: COMFORT DESTRUCTION
Identify and dismantle every justification I listed for staying:

For each rationalization:
- **Name the blind spot:** What am I routing away from conscious awareness? (Goleman's amygdala mechanism)
- **Flip the justification:** Show how the same data supports the OPPOSITE conclusion
- **The real function:** What is this excuse actually protecting? (Usually: ego, comfort, fear of failure)

### STEP 3: ACTION PAIN REDUCTION (NLP)
Apply Richard Bandler's submodality techniques to the scary action:

**Visual:** Take the mental image of the feared action. Shrink it. Make it black and white. Move it further away. Push it to the corner of your mental screen.

**Auditory:** The inner voice saying "this could go wrong" — turn down the volume. Make it sound cartoonish. Speed it up until it's unintelligible.

**Kinesthetic:** The pit-in-stomach feeling — locate it physically. Spin it the opposite direction. Make it lighter.

**Reframe the action as:** [Provide a specific reframe that makes the action feel smaller than the pain of staying]

### STEP 4: ESCAPE VELOCITY CALCULATION
**Pain of staying (1-10 after amplification):** ___
**Pain of acting (1-10 after reduction):** ___
**Escape velocity achieved?** [Yes if staying > acting by 3+ points]

If not achieved, identify what additional pain amplification or action reduction is needed.

### STEP 5: THE MOVE
Based on this audit, the specific next action (within 48 hours):
- **What:** [Precise action]
- **When:** [Exact time commitment]
- **The pain that drives it:** [Which amplified pain point is most visceral]
- **If I hesitate:** [One sentence to re-amplify in the moment]

Remember: "You can look up 'is the sun good, is the sun bad' and you'll have information equal to both. In all decisions we have the ability to choose which direction to use it." Choose to use pain as fuel, not as paralysis.
```

---

## Quality Check

Output passes if:
- [ ] Pain amplification is specific and visceral, not generic doom-and-gloom
- [ ] Comfort destruction reveals actual blind spots, not just argues against excuses
- [ ] NLP reduction techniques are specific to the user's feared action (not generic relaxation)
- [ ] Escape velocity gap is honestly assessed (not inflated to force action)
- [ ] The 48-hour action is concrete and small enough to actually do
