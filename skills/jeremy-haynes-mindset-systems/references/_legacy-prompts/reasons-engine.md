# Reasons Engine

Discover, rank, and replace your motivational drivers — because reasons have a shelf life.

---

## Context

Jeremy Haynes discovered (via Jim Rohn's 1981 Anaheim talk) that motivation requires REASONS — and those reasons expire. The reasons that fuel you to $500K become dead weight at $2M. After delivering a reasons talk at his mastermind, the number of clients hitting $1M/month nearly doubled. This framework is the single most deployable insight for breaking revenue plateaus.

Three types of reasons work: selfish, philanthropic, and irrational. The only test is emotional stir — not moral purity.

**Source Pattern:** Genius Pattern #2 — Dynamic Reasons Engine

---

## Prompt

```
You are a motivational diagnostician using Jeremy Haynes' Dynamic Reasons Engine.

I need to audit my current reasons and find replacements. Here's my context:

**Current revenue/income level:** [Where you are financially]
**Revenue/income target:** [Where you want to be]
**How long I've been at this level:** [Time at plateau]
**What used to drive me that doesn't anymore:** [Old reasons that feel flat now]
**What I think should motivate me but doesn't:** [Reasons that "should" work but don't stir anything]

---

Using Jeremy Haynes' Reasons Engine, run a full diagnostic:

### STEP 1: REASON AUTOPSY
Analyze my expired reasons. For each one:
- **Why it worked then:** What emotional gap did it fill?
- **Why it's dead now:** What changed? (Usually: the gap closed. You achieved what you wanted.)
- **The trap:** How is this dead reason creating a ceiling? (e.g., "Family security was your reason. Family is secure. Now you have no reason to push harder — so you don't.")

### STEP 2: REASON DISCOVERY
Generate 10 potential new reasons across three categories:

**Selfish Reasons (3-4):**
Unapologetically personal. Material desires, ego goals, status markers, experiences. Jeremy's framework: "Selfish reasons are valid. What matters is emotional stir."

**Philanthropic Reasons (3-4):**
Impact-driven. But NOT generic "help people." Specific: who, what, measurable outcome. Reference Edward's discovery: "What if you 2-3x'd every employee's pay? What if YOU are the reason they have healthcare, better schools, quality of life?"

**Irrational/Emotional Reasons (2-3):**
These don't need to make logical sense. Hatred, spite, fear, proving someone wrong, honoring someone. Reference the Homeless Shelter Builder: pure hatred of homeless people → channeled into building better facilities → 3.5x'd revenue. The emotion was ugly. The outcome was philanthropic. The revenue followed.

### STEP 3: EMOTIONAL STIR TEST
For each of the 10 reasons, rate on a 1-10 scale:
- **Gut punch factor:** Does reading this make you FEEL something right now?
- **Durability:** Will this reason survive achieving the next revenue milestone, or will it expire too?
- **Action clarity:** Does this reason point to a specific action, or is it just a feeling?

Rank all 10, identify the top 3 with highest combined scores.

### STEP 4: REASON INSTALLATION
For the top 3 reasons:
1. **The Anchor Object** — A physical object, image, or reminder placed in your workspace that ties to this reason
2. **The Morning Trigger** — How this reason gets activated within the first 30 minutes of your day
3. **The Decision Filter** — When facing a hard choice, how does this reason inform the answer? ("If my reason is X, then the obvious choice is Y")
4. **The Expiration Check** — What signal will tell you this reason has expired and needs replacement? (So you catch it proactively instead of plateauing again)

### STEP 5: REASON REFRESH SCHEDULE
Based on your target revenue and typical reason shelf life:
- **Checkpoint 1:** [Revenue milestone] — Re-run this audit
- **Checkpoint 2:** [Revenue milestone] — Re-run this audit
- **Warning signs:** [Behavioral signals that reasons are expiring: loss of urgency, comfort creep, "going through the motions"]

Remember: "The reasons that got you there become invalid reasons. They just do. There's fulfillment and there's purpose and meaning." Old reasons die of success. New reasons must be found — or you stall.
```

---

## Quality Check

Output passes if:
- [ ] Expired reasons are diagnosed with specific gap-closure logic (not just "you got bored")
- [ ] New reasons include at least one that feels uncomfortable or ugly (selfish/irrational) — sanitized reasons don't stir
- [ ] Emotional stir test produces genuine differentiation (not all 7-8s)
- [ ] Installation steps are physical and daily, not abstract
- [ ] Expiration signals are behavioral, not calendar-based
