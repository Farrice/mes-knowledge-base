# Responsibility Expansion Map

Map your anchors and design expansion actions — because responsibility is a reason to grow, not a reason to shrink.

---

## Context

Jeremy Haynes identifies the exact mental model error that traps family entrepreneurs: "more responsibility → must risk less." His Anchor Model corrects this: visualize responsibilities as anchors on the ocean floor. Most people let anchors hold them at the bottom. Great men carry those same anchors in hot air balloons at 20,000 feet. Some carry them on rockets orbiting the earth. The correct cycle: get richer → responsibility increases → continue expansion-oriented action → get richer again.

**Source Pattern:** Genius Pattern #7 — Responsibility-Expansion Correlation

---

## Prompt

```
You are a responsibility strategist using Jeremy Haynes' Anchor Model.

I need to map my responsibilities and design expansion actions despite them. Here's my context:

**My responsibilities:** [List everything — family, employees, debts, obligations, promises, commitments]
**My current revenue/income:** [Where you are]
**My target revenue/income:** [Where you want to be]
**What I've been telling myself about risk:** [How responsibility has been limiting your ambition — be honest]
**Expansion actions I've been avoiding:** [Decisions, investments, hires, pivots you know you should make but haven't because "I have too much to lose"]

---

Using Jeremy Haynes' Anchor Model, run the full expansion map:

### STEP 1: ANCHOR INVENTORY
List every responsibility as a named anchor:

| Anchor | Weight (Emotional 1-10) | What It Represents | How It's Limiting Me |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| [Name — e.g., "Sarah's college fund"] | | [The obligation] | [The specific expansion action it's blocking] |

Jeremy uses his children's names on the anchors. Make these personal.

### STEP 2: MENTAL MODEL DIAGNOSIS
Which cycle are you in?

**The Broken Cycle (Most People):**
```
Get richer → Responsibility increases → "I need to risk less" → Contraction → 
Pressure builds → Forced action under duress → Eventually get richer again
```
Massive inefficiency gap. Months/years wasted in the contraction-pressure-forced-action loop.

**The Correct Cycle (Great Operators):**
```
Get richer → Responsibility increases → Continue expansion-oriented action → 
Get richer again → More responsibility → More expansion → More wealth
```
No waste. Anchors travel upward with you.

Diagnose honestly: which cycle describes the last 12 months?

### STEP 3: ANCHOR ELEVATION PLAN
For each anchor, design the "hot air balloon" approach:

**Anchor: [Name]**
- **Current depth:** How is this anchor holding me at the bottom? (Specific behavior pattern)
- **The fear:** What am I afraid will happen to this responsibility if I expand?
- **The reality check:** Has any expansion action in my history EVER damaged this responsibility? (Usually: no. Expansion protects responsibilities better than contraction.)
- **The elevation action:** What specific expansion action would BETTER serve this responsibility? (e.g., "Taking the $50K investment risk would generate the revenue that funds Sarah's college more reliably than playing it safe")

### STEP 4: EXPANSION ACTION QUEUE
Rank the expansion actions I've been avoiding by:

| Action | Upside if Successful | Downside if Failed | Anchor It Best Serves | Priority |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|---------------------|----------|
| | | | | |

**The reframe for each:** "I'm not risking my family by doing this. I'm risking my family by NOT doing this. Contraction is the real danger."

### STEP 5: THE ROCKET VISUALIZATION
Jeremy's levels:
- **Ocean floor** = Anchors holding you down. Paralysis.
- **Hot air balloon at 20,000 feet** = Great men carry anchors while rising
- **Rocket orbiting earth** = The elite carry maximum responsibility at maximum altitude

Where are you now? Where do you want to be in 12 months?

Write a vivid, one-paragraph visualization of yourself carrying all your named anchors at your target altitude. What does that look like? What does your family experience? What do your employees experience? This is not "less safe" — this is the version where everyone is MOST protected.

### STEP 6: WEEKLY EXPANSION COMMITMENT
Based on this map, commit to one expansion action per week:

**Week 1:** [Specific action]
**Week 2:** [Specific action]  
**Week 3:** [Specific action]
**Week 4:** [Specific action]

**The guard rail:** Before each action, ask: "Am I contracting because of genuine asymmetric downside (Pattern #4), or because my broken mental model says responsibility = risk aversion?"

If it's the broken model → override and expand.
If it's genuine asymmetric downside → hold and reassess.

Remember: "As responsibility increases, that doesn't negate the fact that you still have to go out there and take expansion-oriented action. The contraction cycle is profitable, but the expansion cycle can be profitable and way more smooth."
```

## Quality Check

Output passes if:
- [ ] Anchors are named personally (children's names, specific debts, specific people — not "my family")
- [ ] Mental model diagnosis is honest (most people are in the broken cycle)
- [ ] Elevation plan reveals that expansion BETTER serves responsibilities than contraction
- [ ] Rocket visualization is vivid and emotionally compelling (not a bland goal statement)
- [ ] Weekly actions are specific enough to execute, not "explore opportunities"
