# Example: SLAY Post Generator

**Context**: 
- Core Lesson: How to retain clients in an agency.
- Story: I used to lose clients because I only communicated when I needed money.
- Audience: Agency Founders

**THE DELIVERABLE:**

My agency almost collapsed in 2022.

I was bleeding $10k a month.

I thought I had a fulfillment problem.
I thought my team was underperforming.
I actually just had a communication problem.

I only spoke to my clients when an invoice was due.

That single realization changed my entire retention strategy.
Here is exactly how I fixed it in 30 days:

1. The Friday Loom: Every Friday at 3PM, I send a 2-minute video update. No text. Just my face and their metrics.
2. The 90-Day Roadmap: Clients never guess what happens next. They have a shared Notion board with a 90-day timeline.
3. The "No Ask" Check-in: I text them every other Tuesday. Just to say "Hope the launch is going well." No asks.

Retention isn't about delivering perfect work.
It's about making them feel safe while you work.

The hardest part of agency life isn't getting clients.
It's keeping them when things get quiet.

What is your #1 strategy for client retention?
