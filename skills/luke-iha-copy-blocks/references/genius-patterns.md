## 1. Copy Block Velocity
**What They Do**: Cycle through the 6 blocks (Pain, Promise, Proof, Constraints, Curiosity, Conditions) at variable speeds. The SPEED of cycling IS the engagement mechanism. High-performing copy interleaves blocks rapidly with fresh angles on each reappearance.
**Executable Behavior**: Map any piece of copy sentence by sentence — tag each sentence with its block type. Count consecutive same-block sentences. If any block dominates for more than 3 consecutive sentences, the velocity has dropped. Rewrite by inserting a different block.
**Deploy When**: Auditing any existing copy. Writing new long-form copy. Diagnosing "it feels flat" feedback.
**Success Metric**: No single block dominates more than 3 consecutive sentences. Opening 100 words hit 3+ different blocks.

## 2. The CASH Multiplication Engine
**What They Do**: Generate infinite ad variations through systematic multiplication: Concept × Angle × Style × Hook. Each dimension multiplies (not adds) the total output, turning one product into 100+ unique ads.
**Executable Behavior**: For any product: (1) Brainstorm 3-5 Concepts (emotional territories, NOT product descriptions). (2) For each Concept, generate 3-5 Angles. (3) Define 3-4 Styles (UGC, editorial, talking head, meme). (4) For each C×A×S combination, write 2-3 Hooks. Output: structured matrix with all combinations.
**Deploy When**: Monthly creative refreshes. Client asks "we need more ads." New product launch. Testing new markets. Any time you feel creatively stuck.
**Success Metric**: Minimum 50 unique hook+angle combinations per product per session.

## 3. Solve vs Show Hook Architecture
**What They Do**: Deploy two fundamentally different hook psychologies. Solve hooks promise to resolve a problem (reader must acknowledge the problem). Show hooks demonstrate a result (reader witnesses evidence without needing to self-diagnose).
**Executable Behavior**: For every campaign, generate hooks in BOTH architectures. Solve: "Here's why [problem] (and the fix)." Show: "I [achieved result] using [unexpected method]." Test both. Show hooks typically win for unaware audiences. Solve hooks win for problem-aware audiences.
**Deploy When**: Every hook-writing session. Awareness-level targeting. A/B test architecture selection.
**Success Metric**: Both architectures tested. Show hooks indexed for top-of-funnel. Solve hooks indexed for mid-funnel.

## 4. Proof-of-Work Hooks
**What They Do**: Create hooks that DEMONSTRATE expertise through their very structure. The hook itself IS proof the person knows what they're talking about. Data-driven hooks, live analysis hooks, prediction hooks, demonstration hooks.
**Executable Behavior**: Instead of "I'm an expert, listen to me" → write hooks that PROVE expertise: "I analyzed 10,000 ads and found one pattern that predicted 90% of winners." The hook contains verifiable or demonstrable claims that signal deep knowledge.
**Deploy When**: Competitive niches where credibility is the conversion bottleneck. B2B. High-ticket offers. Expert positioning.
**Success Metric**: Hook contains at least one specific, verifiable or demonstrable claim.

## 5. Three-Factor Ideation
**What They Do**: Never rely on a single ideation source. Three parallel engines feed creativity: (1) CASH Method for structural completeness, (2) Organic swiping for pre-validated patterns, (3) Raw brainstorming for originality.
**Executable Behavior**: Every ideation session uses all three: Start with CASH matrix for structural variations. Then swipe 10+ proven hooks from organic viral content and adapt. Then brainstorm 10+ wild ideas with no constraints. Minimum 30% of ideas from each source.
**Deploy When**: Every ideation session. Before any major creative sprint.
**Success Metric**: Output traceable to all three sources. No single source exceeds 50%.

## 6. The Concept Layer
**What They Do**: Begin every campaign by defining the EMOTIONAL TERRITORY before any creative work. The Concept is the paradigm the ad operates within — expressible in one sentence without mentioning the product. Most copywriters skip straight to angles and lose the strategic foundation.
**Executable Behavior**: Before writing: articulate the Concept in one sentence. Validate: does it describe an emotional territory, NOT a product feature? "The skincare industry is lying to you" = concept. "Our serum has niacinamide" = product feature. All angles, styles, and hooks must serve the concept.
**Deploy When**: Campaign strategy. Before any CASH Method execution. Quarterly creative direction reviews.
**Success Metric**: Concept expressible in one sentence without product mention. All downstream creative traceable to concept.

## 7. Ad Body Architecture
**What They Do**: Move beyond the simplistic Hook → Body → CTA structure into a psychologically sequenced ad body: Hook → Payoff (deliver on hook's promise) → Mechanism reveal → Proof stack → Reason-to-buy sequence → Constraint/urgency → CTA.
**Executable Behavior**: After hook, immediately deliver the PAYOFF (what the hook promised). Then reveal the mechanism (WHY it works). Then prove it (evidence). Then differentiate (why THIS solution). Then create urgency. Then CTA with clear single action.
**Deploy When**: Writing any ad longer than a hook. VSL scripts. Landing page body copy. Email sequences.
**Success Metric**: Each section serves a distinct psychological function. No section could be removed without weakening the sequence.
