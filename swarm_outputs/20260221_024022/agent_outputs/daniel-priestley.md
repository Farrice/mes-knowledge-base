**SUMMARY**
The current profile suffers from "Service Provider Syndrome"—it focuses on the *process* of doing work rather than the *tension* between the Manual Martyr's current grind and their desired sovereignty. To become a Key Person of Influence (KPI), the profile must shift from a "help-wanted" tone to an "oversubscribed authority" tone that prioritizes cognitive white space as the ultimate premium product.

**KEY FINDINGS**
*   **Lack of Supply/Demand Tension:** The current copy doesn't signal that your time is a scarce resource; it reads like you are available for hire by anyone with a budget, which devalues the "Bespoke" promise.
*   **Solution-First Bias:** You are speaking to the 4% of the market looking for a "system designer" rather than the 65% of the market who are currently "drinking from a firehose" and don't yet know a system is the answer.
*   **The "Manual Martyr" Gap:** The copy acknowledges the pain but doesn't fully weaponize the "fear of irrelevance." In the age of AI, a Manual Expert isn't just tired; they are becoming obsolete. That tension is your greatest sales tool.

**RECOMMENDATIONS**

**1. The "Unbothered Authority" Headline**
*   *Current Style:* "I help experts automate their business."
*   *Priestley Rewrite:* **Restoring Sovereignty to the Manual Expert. I trade your ‘Manual Martyrdom’ for Cognitive White Space. | Architect of Bespoke, Human-Centric Systems. | Currently operating via waitlist for Q3.**
    *   *Why:* This uses "Problem-Aware" language immediately and signals scarcity ("waitlist") to engineer demand.

**2. The "About Section" (The KPI Narrative)**
Structure this using the **Problem-Aware Positioning** framework:
*   **The Hook (The 65%):** "Most experts are drowning in the very expertise that was supposed to set them free. You’re drinking from a firehose of grinding admin, playing the 'Manual Martyr' while your best work sits on the back burner."
*   **The Tension:** "In a world moving at AI-speed, manual labor is a slow march toward irrelevance. If you’re still the bottleneck in your own brilliance, you don't have a business; you have a high-stress job with no boss."
*   **The Shift (The Mountain):** "I specialize in engineering the 'Bespoke Pivot.' We move you from the grind to Sovereignty—creating the cognitive white space required for high-level human-centric work."
*   **The Authority Call to Action:** "I only work with 3 experts at a time to ensure the systems remain bespoke. If you’re ready to stop the grind and reclaim your status, let’s see if there’s a fit for my next opening."

**3. The "Services" (Product Ecosystem)**
Don't list tasks; list **Capacity Milestones**:
*   **The Diagnostic (The Assessment):** "The Martyrdom Audit – A deep dive into your manual bottlenecks to find where your sovereignty is leaking."
*   **The Core Offer (The Product):** "The Sovereignty System – A bespoke, end-to-end architecture that automates the admin and protects your white space."
*   **The High-End (The Partnership):** "Fractional Capacity Officer – Ongoing strategic oversight for experts who refuse to ever go back to the firehose."

**CONFIDENCE**: High

**DISSENT**
Traditional LinkedIn "experts" will tell you to be "accessible" and "helpful." I disagree. For a high-ticket, bespoke service, accessibility is the enemy of perceived value. If you look too available, the "Manual Expert" will subconsciously assume you aren't successful enough to be busy. You must maintain the "With or Without You" energy—the idea that your systems work, and while you’d love to help them, your capacity is the ultimate constraint. If you don't actually have a waitlist yet, create a "Discovery Queue" to maintain the tension.
