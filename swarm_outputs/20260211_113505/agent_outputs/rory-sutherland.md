**SUMMARY**
From a behavioral standpoint, we are shifting the brand from "Maintenance" (low perceived value) to "Alchemy" (high perceived value). By reframing **Resurrection** as **Dormant Power Reactivation**, we move away from the "Repair Shop" fallacy and toward a "Prestige Restoration" model that appeals to the executive ego’s desire for latent potential rather than its fear of failure.

**KEY FINDINGS**
- **The Prestige Reframe:** "The Rebuild" sounds like a depreciating asset (a car or a house); "The Resurrection" sounds like a mythic return to power. In marketing psychology, the latter allows for a significantly higher price premium because it suggests the restoration of something irreplaceable.
- **Signal Strength:** High-earning executives ($250K+) are allergic to "crisis" language because it signals weakness to their peers. "Dormant Power" signals a temporary strategic lull, making the coaching an "optimization upgrade" rather than a "bailout."
- **Psychological Sunk Cost:** These men have already "built the empire." By using "Resurrection," we are validating their past success while suggesting that their current physical state is merely a "system hibernation" that requires a specific, high-level key to unlock.
