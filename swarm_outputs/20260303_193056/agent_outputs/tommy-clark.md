**SUMMARY**
The rise of AI agent frameworks, like Claude's skills and plugins, is commoditizing generic "how-to" content and traditional knowledge work, demanding a radical shift towards founder-led, experiential narratives. B2B founders must decouple their unique tacit knowledge from AI-generated outputs, leveraging "How I" stories and surgical audience design to build high-intent pipelines in a saturated digital landscape.

**KEY FINDINGS**
- **Tacit Knowledge Commoditization & Narrative Premium**: AI agent frameworks are rapidly automating and standardizing explicit "how-to" information, making generic advice worthless. The new premium is on a founder's unique, lived "How I" experiences, including failures, specific processes, and contrarian insights that no AI can replicate.
- **Stealth Hook Imperative**: As AI-generated content saturates feeds, the "ad filter" for traditional hooks intensifies. Only hyper-specific, casual, and often negativity-biased "Stealth Hooks" will bypass this filter, capturing attention by sounding like a direct message from a trusted peer, not a broadcast.
- **Audience Engineering over Vanity Metrics**: The ease of AI-generated content will dilute algorithmic reach for broad content. Building a high-intent pipeline now demands a manual, sniper-like "Audience Design" approach (e.g., the 20/5 protocol) to directly engage ICPs and leverage "Hub accounts," making follower count an irrelevant vanity metric.

**RECOMMENDATIONS**
- **Implement a "How I" Narrative Content Engine**: For every piece of advice, pivot from "How to do X" to "Here's the messy reality of how *I* did X, including the $50k mistake and the exact system *I* use now." Focus on specific "gravedigger details" (e.g., "$1.2M in ARR" instead of "millions") that prove lived experience.
- **Master Stealth Hook Crafting**: Prioritize hooks that use negativity bias ("Stop doing X," "The biggest lie in Y"), specific data points, and casual, "Slackified" language to instantly differentiate founder content from AI-generated noise. Avoid formal, generic opening statements at all costs.
- **Adopt the 20/5 Audience Design Protocol**: Dedicate daily time to manually connect with 20 Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) individuals and strategically comment on 5 high-visibility "Hub accounts" (e.g., industry analysts, major influencers, competitors' top performers). This engineers a high-intent algorithm feed and builds direct relationships.
- **Leverage AI for Extraction, Not Creation**: Utilize AI agents within a "Content Interview System" to transcribe and initially structure raw founder brain dumps. However, the critical human role remains identifying the contrarian insights, injecting the unique "How I" narrative, and refining the "graalth" details that AI cannot originate.

**CONFIDENCE**: High

**DISSENT**:
While AI agents can significantly boost efficiency for content *extraction* and *initial drafting*, there's a significant risk of founders over-relying on them for *creation*. This could lead to a subtle dilution of their unique voice and the accidental generation of "fake" "How I" narratives, which sophisticated B2B buyers will quickly detect. The temptation to automate the very *authenticity* that AI makes valuable is a critical pitfall to avoid.
