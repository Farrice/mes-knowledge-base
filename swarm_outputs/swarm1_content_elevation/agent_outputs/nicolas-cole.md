**SUMMARY**
To resonate with a $300k+ executive, we must strip away the "coaching" fluff and replace it with "diagnostic" precision. By applying atomic sentence craft—terminal word power, economic compression, and 1/3/1 rhythm engineering—we transform the content from a motivational plea into a biological imperative.

**KEY FINDINGS**
*   **The "Terminal Word" Gap:** Most executive coaching content ends sentences on weak words (e.g., "...to help you feel better"). High-status writing ends on "power words" that linger (e.g., "...to stop the atrophy").
*   **Adverbial Dilution:** Words like "really," "truly," and "deeply" signal insecurity. High-ticket authority is established through "The Eraser"—cutting every word that doesn't advance the diagnostic.
*   **Rhythmic Friction:** Blocky paragraphs are ignored by busy COOs. We must use "Visual Pacing" (1/3/1 structure) to pull the reader down the page before their "busy-ness" reflex kicks in.

**RECOMMENDATIONS**
*   **Apply "The 1/3/1 Rhythm":** Start with one punchy hook. Follow with three sentences of diagnostic context. End with one clear takeaway or CTA. This mimics the brevity of an executive briefing.
*   **Vocabulary Calibration:** Use a 4th-grade base for readability, but inject one "Elevated Diagnostic" word per post (e.g., *homeostasis, autonomic, cortisol, entropy*). This signals expertise without being academic.
*   **Terminal Word Optimization:** Every sentence must be audited for its final word. If the most impactful word is in the middle, rewrite it.
