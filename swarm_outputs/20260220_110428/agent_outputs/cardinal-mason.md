**SUMMARY**
I’ve architected three high-conversion hooks for an AI consulting offer by applying the **Rule of One** and **Specificity Over Claims**. We’re moving away from "AI strategy" (which sounds like a homework assignment) and moving toward "Profit Systems" (which sounds like a bank deposit).

**KEY FINDINGS**
*   **The "AI Hype" Fatigue:** Business owners are tired of hearing what AI *could* do; they are starving for someone to tell them exactly what it *will* do for their specific P&L this month.
*   **The Specificity Gap:** Generic claims like "improve efficiency" are noise. Hooks that mention specific dollar amounts, hour counts, or "invisible" overhead are the only ones that cut through the LinkedIn/Email static.
*   **The "Human Taste" Requirement:** The biggest objection to AI consulting is the fear of "robotic" output. The hook must imply that the human strategy (the 20%) is what makes the AI (the 80%) actually work.

**RECOMMENDATIONS**

**Hook 1: The "Bleeding Neck" Specificity Hook**
*"Stop paying a $6,000/month retainer for 'human' data entry that a $20 API can execute in 4.2 seconds with 0% error rate. We don't just 'consult' on AI—we install the specific workflows that reclaim 15+ hours of your leadership team's week."*
*   **Why it works:** It uses Principle 3 (Specificity). It pits a high cost ($6k) against a low cost ($20) to create immediate logical buy-in.

**Hook 2: The "Competitor Gap" Agitation Hook**
*"You aren't losing to Artificial Intelligence. You’re losing to the leanest competitor in your niche who just automated 80% of their lead gen while you’re still debating which ChatGPT prompt to use. The 'AI Gap' is widening—here is the 3-step diagnostic to close it before Q3."*
*   **Why it works:** It uses Principle 4 (Agitate Before Solving). It targets the fear of being out-paced, which is a stronger motivator for B2B than the promise of "innovation."

**Hook 3: The "Invisible Employee" System Hook**
*"The 'Invisible Employee' Framework: How to build a custom AI Operating System that handles your fulfillment, reporting, and first-pass creative while you sleep. No new hires. No 'hallucinations.' Just a system that works 24/7 for the price of a Starbucks latte."*
*   **Why it works:** It uses Principle 2 (The Rule of One). It frames the offer as a singular, proprietary "System" rather than a vague consulting service.

**CONFIDENCE**: High

**DISSENT**
Most AI consultants focus on "Prompt Engineering." This is a mistake. Prompts are a commodity. **I disagree with selling "AI Training."** Business owners don't want to learn how to prompt; they want the *result* of the prompt without having to touch the keyboard. Your copy should sell the **Integration and Outcome**, not the **Instruction**. If you sell "teaching," you’re selling work. If you sell "systems," you’re selling freedom. Focus on the latter.
