## DISSENT

The primary contrarian view (Research Finding 2) correctly identifies the "authenticity paradox" and the "pricing problem" as critical failure modes. My recommendations explicitly address this by:
1.  **Reframing the service**: Moving from "ghostwriting" to "voice articulation" and "authority amplification" directly confronts the authenticity issue by emphasizing the *preservation* and *enhancement* of the coach's unique identity and voice, rather than outsourcing it.
2.  **Leveraging core differentiator**: The operator's 18 years of psychology and coaching experience, combined with AI, is positioned as the *solution* to the authenticity paradox, not just a feature. This allows for "psychology-tuned content" and "undetectable authenticity."
3.  **Strategic pricing and launch**: The pilot phase with a lower price point and explicit "Founding Partner" status mitigates the "zero-proof" liability and allows for the development of conversion-focused case studies. The free "Executive Voice & Impact Audit" is designed to generate immediate "How did they know?" moments, bypassing the need for extensive proof initially and directly addressing the unspoken fear of inauthenticity.
4.  **Managing expectations**: While not explicitly detailed above, the service agreement must clearly outline engagement requirements from the coach (as suggested by the contrarian view) and set realistic timelines for ROI (focusing on discovery calls, not just vanity metrics).

The risk remains that coaches fundamentally don't believe LinkedIn drives client acquisition. However, by positioning as an "Identity Articulator" who provides the *feeling* of effortless eminence and tangible client acquisition, rather than just "content," this service aims to overcome that skepticism by speaking to deeper psychological and identity-based drivers.
