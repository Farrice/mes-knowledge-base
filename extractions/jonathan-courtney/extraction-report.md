## Genius Patterns

### 1. The Builder Trap Diagnosis
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Before discussing any AI tool, Courtney first reframes the CEO's job description. He notices that most founders self-identify as "builders" when their primary job is promotion. He diagnoses the identity misalignment before offering solutions.
- **Executable Behavior**: When evaluating any business, first ask: "Who is doing the promoting?" If the answer is "nobody directly," the business has a structural deficiency that no amount of product improvement will fix.
- **Deployment Context**: Consulting engagements, business audits, when someone asks "why isn't my product getting traction?"
- **Success Metric**: The CEO can articulate their promotion activities as clearly as their building activities.

### 2. The Promoter Blueprint Loop
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Courtney operates from a closed-loop marketing system: Traffic → Holding Pattern → Selling Event → (conversion or loop back). He doesn't teach one-off tactics — everything maps to this loop. Every activity he performs, he can point to which phase it serves.
- **Executable Behavior**: Map every marketing activity to one of three buckets: (1) Traffic generation (organic/paid), (2) Holding pattern nurture (newsletter, podcast, social), (3) Selling events (webinars, email campaigns, retargeting, direct outreach). If an activity doesn't map, it's procrastination.
- **Deployment Context**: Any business building a marketing system, planning campaigns, or diagnosing why revenue isn't flowing despite audience engagement.
- **Success Metric**: You can predict revenue from traffic inputs — e.g., "5,000 new newsletter subs → 200 webinar attendees → 12 sales."

### 3. The Proof-by-Celebrity Legitimacy Stack
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Courtney validates his "CEO = Promoter" thesis not with abstract logic but by demonstrating that Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Peter Levels, and Jason Fried all spend 50%+ of their time promoting. He turns their implicit behavior into explicit evidence that promotion is the CEO's core job, not a side activity.
- **Executable Behavior**: When making any counterintuitive business argument, stack 3-5 examples of undeniably successful people who already do the thing you're advocating. Let the audience connect the dots rather than lecturing.
- **Deployment Context**: Sales pitches, webinars, content marketing, persuasion situations where the audience has a pre-existing belief you need to dislodge.
- **Success Metric**: The audience stops defending the status quo and starts asking "how?"

### 4. The Claude-to-Claude Code Handoff
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Courtney uses standard Claude Projects for research, ideation, and copywriting (creative phase), then explicitly transitions to Claude Code for building (execution phase). The handoff mechanism is key: he has Claude generate a CLAUDE.md context file and an HTML draft, downloads both, and opens them in Claude Code. This preserves context across tools without manual re-briefing.
- **Executable Behavior**: (1) Start in Claude Projects with instructions + reference files. (2) Do ideation, research, copywriting. (3) When ready to build, ask Claude: "Create a CLAUDE.md file with full project context and export the current HTML." (4) Download both, open in Claude Code project folder. (5) Build from there.
- **Deployment Context**: Any project that starts conceptual and becomes technical — campaign pages, lead magnets, one-off marketing sites, event pages.
- **Success Metric**: Context loss between tools is near zero; Claude Code picks up exactly where Claude Projects left off.

### 5. The Voice-First Brain Dump Protocol
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Courtney records 15-minute verbal brain dumps on his phone, feeds the audio directly into Claude, and has it produce ADHD-friendly scannable documents. This bypasses the written-to-written bottleneck that slows most people down. His prompts are also voice-dictated via WhisperFlow.
- **Executable Behavior**: (1) Record a voice memo of everything in your head about the project. (2) Upload to Claude. (3) Have it produce scannable, non-linear reference docs you can glance at in real-time. (4) Use voice-to-prompt tools for all subsequent interactions.
- **Deployment Context**: Podcast prep, webinar planning, any creative ideation session, ADHD-friendly workflows.
- **Success Metric**: Total prep time under 1 hour for content that would previously take a full day.

### 6. The Self-Teaching Context Loop
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: At the end of every Claude chat session, Courtney asks Claude to produce updated instructions that he pastes back into the project's system instructions. The AI teaches itself what it learned, creating a compounding knowledge base that gets sharper with every session.
- **Executable Behavior**: End every meaningful chat with: "Give me a pasteable set of instructions for this project that captures everything we've learned so far, ready to be added to your system instructions."
- **Deployment Context**: Any long-running Claude Project where context accumulates over multiple sessions.
- **Success Metric**: Session 10 of a project is meaningfully smarter than session 1 without manual briefing.

### 7. The Abundance Mindset Over Efficiency
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Rather than using AI to reduce team size or cut costs, Courtney uses AI to multiply output. Instead of "do 1 campaign/month cheaper," it's "do 5 campaigns/month with the same team." He explicitly warns against the efficiency trap.
- **Executable Behavior**: When evaluating any AI-enabled process change, ask: "Am I using this to *do less* or to *do more*?" The correct answer is always *more*. Keep your team and triple their throughput.
- **Deployment Context**: Team planning, AI tool evaluation, business scaling decisions.
- **Success Metric**: Output volume (campaigns, content pieces, experiments) increases 3-5x while team size stays constant.

## Hidden Knowledge

### 1. The Ask-User-Question Interrogation Method
**Tacit Insight**: Courtney's most powerful webinar prep technique isn't prompting — it's being grilled. He trains a Claude Project on his ideal webinar structure, then uses "ask user question" mode in Claude Code to have the AI interrogate him for 30 minutes. The AI's questions force him to articulate what he hasn't thought through yet, and the resulting content is dramatically sharper than anything he could have prompted for directly.

**Why Others Miss It**: Most people use AI to get *answers*. Courtney uses it to provoke *better questions from himself*. The value isn't in Claude's output — it's in what Courtney produces under interrogation.

### 2. The 3-Day-to-Half-Day Compression Is the Real ROI
**Tacit Insight**: Courtney's key metric isn't cost savings — it's time compression. A lead magnet campaign that took his team 2-3 days now takes half a day. This specific ratio (4-6x compression) is the actual ROI number for AI-assisted marketing, not the "100x" hyperbole common on social media.

**Why Others Miss It**: The AI discourse focuses on "replacing" work. Courtney's actual experience shows AI doesn't replace — it compresses. And 4-6x compression on the *right* activities (selling events, campaigns) is worth more than 100x compression on the wrong ones (admin optimization).

### 3. The Off-The-Shelf Trap Warning
**Tacit Insight**: He shares Laura's story: she spent 3 days building a custom proposal builder, then asked Claude if an off-the-shelf solution existed. It did. AI tools can tempt you into building custom solutions for problems already solved. The meta-skill is knowing when to build vs. when to buy.

**Why Others Miss It**: The vibe coding community celebrates building everything from scratch. But a CEO's time is allocated to promotion, not engineering. Every hour spent building something that already exists is an hour *not* spent promoting.

### 4. The 17,766-Line Swipe File Strategy
**Tacit Insight**: Courtney has compiled a 17,766-line document of other people's marketing emails, had Claude extract patterns and learnings, and feeds those into his marketing project's context. This isn't casual research — it's a systematically curated competitive intelligence corpus that makes his AI outputs grounded in battle-tested copy rather than generic marketing.

**Why Others Miss It**: People set up Claude with their own content. Courtney sets up Claude with *the best content from his entire industry*. The training data isn't his voice — it's the market's proven language.

### 5. The "I Don't Prepare" Preparation Method
**Tacit Insight**: Courtney calls himself an "ADHD entrepreneur who doesn't prepare" — but what he actually does is replace linear preparation with ambient AI-assisted preparation. Voice dumps, Claude chats, visual blueprints — he's not winging it. He's preparing in a non-traditional format that suits his cognitive style. This is a hidden UX insight: AI tools enable preparation styles that don't look like preparation.

**Why Others Miss It**: They interpret "I don't prepare" literally instead of recognizing that AI enables entirely new preparation modalities. The prep happens, it just looks different.

## Methodology

### The Promoter Blueprint — 3-Stage Revenue Engine

**Stage 1: Traffic Generation**
- Organic: Podcasts, networking, social media posts, free content
- Paid: Meta ads, TikTok, YouTube ads
- Key principle: Push traffic into *your* world, not directly to a purchase

**Stage 2: Holding Pattern (Audience Warming)**
- Email newsletter (primary)
- Podcast, YouTube, social feeds (secondary)
- Purpose: Build trust and familiarity through consistent value
- This is where daily/weekly content lives — personal stories, insights, engagement
- NOT where you sell

**Stage 3: Selling Events (Revenue Activation)**
- Webinars / live demos / workshops
- Email campaigns (3-4 email sequences)
- Retargeting campaigns (upload email lists → Meta lookalike audiences)
- Direct outreach (manual review of new signups for enterprise leads)
- Non-converters return to Holding Pattern for next cycle

### Revenue Math
Traffic volume → Holding pattern size → Selling event attendance → Conversion rate
- 5,000 new newsletter subs → 200 webinar attendees → 12 purchases
- This ratio is predictable once the engine is running
