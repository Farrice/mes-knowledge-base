## Genius Patterns

### 2. The Sheep Cycle
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Diagnoses the copy-dependency trap that most creators are stuck in without realizing it
- **Executable Behavior**: Map the cycle: Copy → OK performance → Copy again → Try original → Low views → Panic → Copy more → Stuck. Recognize that copied content attracts trend-followers who don't stick. The cycle self-reinforces because each copy success trains away from originality.
- **Deployment Context**: Creator coaching, content audit, self-diagnosis, strategy pivots
- **Success Metric**: Creator can produce original content that outperforms their copied content within 30 days

### 3. Principle vs. Surface Extraction
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Converts specific content observations into niche-agnostic transferable principles
- **Executable Behavior**: After any content breakdown, write one sentence that describes WHY it worked without referencing the specific video, topic, or creator. "He used a time constraint to create urgency and aspirational visuals that made the viewer feel left behind" — NOT "he made a video about morning routines with a countdown format."
- **Deployment Context**: After every content analysis, before any content creation session
- **Success Metric**: The principle sentence could apply to 10+ completely different niches and content formats

### 4. The Purple Cow Test
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Uses Seth Godin's Purple Cow as the binary litmus: copying = choosing to be a brown cow
- **Executable Behavior**: Before publishing any content, ask: "If I covered the account name, could someone tell this is mine?" If no → rework. If the content could belong to any of 10 competitors → it's a brown cow.
- **Deployment Context**: Content review gate, publishing decision, brand differentiation audit
- **Success Metric**: Content is immediately attributable to you without the profile picture

## Methodology

### The Detail Stack Method (5 Steps)

**Step 1 — Stop and Notice**
Train yourself to notice *the moment you stop scrolling*. Ask: "Why did I stop? Was it the hook, the visual, the color, the face, something about the text?" Build the muscle of NOTICING rather than passively consuming.

**Step 2 — Break It Down (4-Layer Deconstruction)**
Pull apart every element across four layers:
- **Topic**: What is it actually about?
- **Packaging**: Thumbnail, title, cover, first frame
- **Content Style**: Visuals, editing, pacing, delivery, audio
- **Emotional Triggers**: What is it making you feel and WHY?

**Step 3 — Go Deeper Than Anyone Else**
This is where most people stop. Go past the surface breakdown into invisible decisions:
- Background choice → credibility signaling
- Font and text placement → eye guidance
- Pacing of cuts → retention engineering
- Wardrobe → trust architecture
- Audio choices → pattern interrupt design
- Set dressing → status signaling
- Ask: "Why did they choose that angle? What rule did they break? What problem were they solving? What did they refuse to do?"

**Step 4 — Extract the Principle**
Write one sentence describing what worked — WITHOUT referencing the specific video, topic, or creator. This is the transferable principle. Test: can this sentence apply to 10+ completely different niches?

**Step 5 — Create From the Principle**
Apply the extracted principle to YOUR niche, YOUR voice, YOUR audience. The principle gives you the structure. Your creativity gives you the execution. If you're making a video about email subject lines, and the principle is "map boring onto high-interest," you'd explain subject lines as dating app opening messages — same psychology, completely different energy.
