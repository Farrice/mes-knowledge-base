## Core Frameworks Identified

### 1. Identity Versioning Protocol (Jeremy 2.0 → 47.0)

Jeremy treats identity as a deliberately iterable system. Starting from when he quit his sales trainer job, he created "Jeremy 2.0" — a large sticky-note sheet with a stick figure surrounded by desired traits and characteristics. He would reference this during sales calls to consciously act through new traits.

**Key mechanics:**
- Each "version" doesn't redefine everything — it may change only one trait
- Current iteration (47.0) added God as a core driver
- Written as explicit trait/characteristic lists posted visibly
- Acts as a "light switch" — identity shifts are instantaneous decisions, not gradual processes
- Foundation principle: "Every trait and characteristic of you is fully fluid and dynamic. 100% of what you consider you can change."

**Quote:** *"I'm on Jeremy 47.0 right now. That's how many times I've intentionally done this exercise."*

### 2. Dynamic Reasons Engine

Derived from Jim Rohn's 1981 Anaheim presentation where Rohn introduces the concept of "needing reasons." Jeremy expanded this into a full system: motivational drivers are dynamic — they expire, shift, and must be actively swapped out.

**Key mechanics:**
- Reasons that got you to your current level become invalid at that level
- Selfish reasons and philanthropic reasons are both valid — what matters is emotional stir
- At higher income levels ($2M+/month), life becomes so peaceful that old emotional drivers disappear
- You must actively find replacement drivers
- After giving a reasons talk at his mastermind, Jeremy saw nearly double the number of clients hitting $1M/month milestones (from ~5 to 9)

**Three case studies from transcript:**
1. **The Homeless Shelter Builder** — Fueled by hatred of homeless people; channeled it into building better facilities and lobbying politicians. 3.5x'd revenue from ~$2M to ~$7M/month. Pure hatred → philanthropic outcome.
2. **Stephen ($20M/year)** — Lost all drive after achieving family goals. Unlocked growth by reconnecting with selfish motives he'd suppressed for a decade.
3. **Edward ($20M/year)** — Couldn't find personal reasons. Instead 2-3x'd staff pay. Discovered "selfish leadership" — being the reason employees have healthcare, schools, quality of life. Revenue followed.

### 3. Pain Leverage System

Core belief: human beings are neurologically wired to avoid pain (references Daniel Goleman's *Vital Lies, Simple Truths* — the blind spot metaphor). This avoidance can be strategically weaponized.

**Mechanics:**
- Pain in current circumstances can be deliberately amplified to create escape velocity
- "Justify it as bad and painful because the brain wants to move away from pain"
- Strategic relocation (Ohio → Colorado → Miami) was unconscious pain leverage before he understood the mechanism
- Richard Bandler's *Using Your Brain for a Change* — you can manipulate memory intensity (volume, color, size) to control emotional response
- Both directions work: amplify pain of status quo OR diminish pain of desired action

**Quote:** *"You can justify anything. You can look up 'is the sun good, is the sun bad' and you'll have information equal to both."*

### 4. Asymmetric Risk Assessment

Not all risk is symmetrical. Jeremy uses a fire/water analogy: "If you're on fire and there's a body of what might be water or might be gas in front of you — are you jumping in?"

**Framework:**
- **High asymmetric upside** — Most entrepreneurial decisions have far more upside than downside. Jump.
- **Unknown unknowns** — Some decisions have genuinely unknowable risk. Requires intuition + faith.
- **Asymmetric downside** — Rare situations where downside exceeds upside. Avoid unless forced.
- Most people never even think in risk-assessment terms — they react emotionally
- "Time and effort based risks" vs "money-based risks" — when you can't risk capital, risk time and relationships
- Train tracks/police story: even when asymmetric risk goes wrong, the consequences are manageable

### 5. Revenue Math Decision Framework

The pivotal decision in Jeremy's career: writing the math on a glass wall to determine what it would take to hit $1M/month.

**The calculation:**
- Goal: $1M/month
- Desired number of deals: 10 (audacious constraint)
- Required per deal: $100K/month
- Mechanism: $10K base fee + 10% net profit share
- Net profit share only on actions trackable back to Megalodon
- Month-to-month contracts (maximum trust signal)
- Client must net $1M for Jeremy to earn $100K

**What the math revealed:** The current chaos model (27 staff, 3 offices, dozens of services) could never scale to $1M/month. Led to firing 23 staff, refunding $109K in deals, closing all offices, and contracting to 4 people.

**Quote:** *"The best decision I ever made was doing the math."*

### 6. Worldview Propaganda Protocol

Jeremy treats all sensory input as "propaganda" that shapes worldview, which in turn shapes all decisions.

**System:**
- **Content consumption is curated warfare** — follow people who validate your desired worldview, demonize content that threatens it
- **Physical environment matters** — sewage smell in half his home → spent $300K to move rather than tolerate degraded input
- **Comparison architecture** — follow $200M-$400M yacht owners to make $5M yacht feel "almost free." Follow Asian ultra-wealthy to make 3 Birkin bags feel insignificant.
- **Active rejection** — will tell people mid-sentence to stop talking if content threatens worldview
- **Subconscious processing** — cites hypnotist Dr. George Pratt: "Subconscious fires 20-40 million neurons/second vs conscious brain at 20-40 thousand/second"
- Includes deliberate "anti-propaganda" — following people he considers limiting (the Camry/double-wide-trailer guy) as negative reference points

### 7. Responsibility ≠ Risk Aversion (Anchor Model)

Responsibility increases as wealth increases, but most people use the wrong mental model: "more responsibility → less risk allowed."

**The correct model (Anchor/Hot Air Balloon):**
- Visualize responsibilities as anchors on the ocean floor (kids' names, wife, debt, mortgage, business costs)
- Most people let anchors hold them at the bottom
- Great men carry those same anchors in hot air balloons at 20,000 feet
- Some carry them on rockets orbiting the earth
- The cycle: Get richer → Responsibility increases → Continue expansion-oriented action → Get richer again
- The broken cycle: Get richer → Responsibility increases → "I need to risk less" → Contraction → Pressure → Forced action → Eventually get richer (massive inefficiency)

**Quote:** *"As responsibility increases, that doesn't negate the fact that you still have to go out there and take expansion-oriented action."*

### 8. Momentum Snowball Effect

Uses snowman-building metaphor: the hardest part is packing and rolling the first ball. Everything after that first ball is easier.

**Applied mechanics:**
- Most people never start rolling (hot chocolate by fireplace = comfort traps)
- Corporate trap, college trap, comfort trap — all prevent initial momentum
- Once momentum compounds, "excessive accomplishment in short time frames" is just a byproduct
- Validation cycles: doing hard things → succeeding → psyche-building → capacity for harder things
- "If you do that for even 13 months, you come out the other side a fucking monster"

### 9. Scarcity Domination

When Jeremy feels even the smallest hint of scarcity, he deliberately spends to obliterate it.

**Mechanics:**
- 3 Birkin bags in 6 weeks ($25K + $30K + $24K) — not because he wanted them, but because the scarcity feeling was unacceptable
- "If you're telling me a piece of leather is bigger than you — no way"
- Spending unlocks heightened creativity and new revenue pathways
- "Hire the employee that costs more than what you think you can afford. They always make more money when they're true A-players."
- The cable snap gym story: physically dominating the "inner voice" that says you can't

### 10. Validation Cycle Stacking

Systematic: take action → succeed → stack that proof → use it to expand capacity for larger actions.

**Examples from transcript:**
- Sales trainer job: skills validated multiple times before going independent
- Dan Luck deal: consultancy → doubled revenue in 2 weeks → rev-share → $2M/month in 8 months
- "How many validation cycles... how many loops you successfully go through that gets you to the other side? You come out a monster."
- Each cycle builds psyche/identity for the next level
