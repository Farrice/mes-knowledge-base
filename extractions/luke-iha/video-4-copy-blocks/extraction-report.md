## 1. Core Frameworks Extracted

### 1.1 The 6 Copy Blocks

Every piece of persuasive copy is composed of exactly 6 atomic building blocks, cycled at variable speeds:

| Block | Function | Example Triggers |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| **Pain** | Surface the problem the reader feels | "You're spending $50K/mo on ads that don't convert" |
| **Promise** | What life looks like after the solution | "Imagine cutting your CPA in half within 30 days" |
| **Proof** | Evidence the promise is real | Testimonials, data, case studies, credentials |
| **Constraints** | Scarcity, urgency, qualification | "Only for DTC brands doing $1M+/yr" |
| **Curiosity** | Open loops that demand resolution | "There's a hidden reason your ads fail — and it's not your targeting" |
| **Conditions** | Qualifying the reader in/out | "If you're still running broad targeting, this isn't for you" |

**Key Insight — Copy Velocity**: The SPEED at which you cycle through blocks determines engagement. High-performing copy doesn't just use all 6 blocks — it interleaves them rapidly, introducing new angles on each block rather than exhausting one before moving to the next.

### 1.2 The CASH Method (Ad Ideation System)

Systematic framework for generating infinite ad variations from a single product:

**C — Concept**: The emotional territory or big idea. NOT the product. The paradigm, worldview, or category the ad operates within. Example: "The skincare industry is lying to you" (concept) vs "Our moisturizer has hyaluronic acid" (product feature).

**A — Angle**: The specific perspective within the concept. One concept generates 5-20 angles. Example angles within "industry is lying":
- The ingredient they hide
- What dermatologists actually use
- The $3 solution they don't want you to know

**S — Style**: The creative execution format. Same concept + angle can be delivered via:
- UGC testimonial
- Editorial/listicle
- Talking head expert
- Text-on-screen
- Demonstration/unboxing
- Meme/humor format

**H — Hook**: The first 1-3 seconds (video) or first line (text). The specific opening that stops the scroll. Each combination of C×A×S yields multiple hooks.

**Multiplication Effect**: 3 concepts × 5 angles × 4 styles × 3 hooks = **180 unique ad variations** from one product.

### 1.3 Ad Structure (Beyond Hook → Body → CTA)

Luke's actual ad architecture:

```
HOOK (stop the scroll — 1-3 seconds)
  └─ Multiple hook variants tested independently
BODY
  ├─ Payoff (deliver on the hook's implicit promise)
  ├─ Mechanism reveal (WHY it works — the unique mechanism)
  ├─ Proof stack (evidence the mechanism actually works)
  ├─ Reason-to-buy sequence (what makes THIS solution unique)
  └─ Constraint/urgency (why act now)
CTA (clear, single action)
```

### 1.4 Hook Psychology: Solve vs Show

Two fundamental hook architectures:

**Solve Hooks**: Promise to resolve a problem.
- "Here's why your ads aren't converting (and the fix)"
- "The #1 mistake killing your ROAS"
- Pattern: Problem identification → implied solution

**Show Hooks**: Demonstrate a result without promising to teach.
- "I turned $500 into $50K using this one ad"
- "Watch me rewrite this failing ad in 60 seconds"
- Pattern: Result demonstration → curiosity about method

**Key Insight**: Show hooks outperform solve hooks for unaware audiences because they don't require the viewer to believe they have a problem.

### 1.5 Three-Factor Ideation System

Three parallel ideation engines that feed the CASH method:

1. **CASH Systematic**: Run the C×A×S×H matrix mechanically
2. **Organic Swiping**: Steal proven hooks from organic viral content (YouTube titles, tweet threads, Reddit posts, TikTok formats). Pre-validated curiosity signals
3. **Raw Brainstorming**: Unconstrained idea generation. "What would make ME stop scrolling?"

**Process**: Start with CASH for structural completeness → supplement with swiping for proven patterns → fill gaps with brainstorming for originality.

### 1.6 Positioning Types for Hooks

Hooks can be categorized by their positioning strategy:

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Contrarian** | Contradicts common belief | "Everything you know about protein timing is wrong" |
| **Authority** | Leverages credibility | "After writing $100M in ads, here's what I know" |
| **Demonstration** | Shows the result | "Watch this $0 ad outperform a $50K campaign" |
| **Confession** | Insider reveals truth | "I worked at [Company] and here's what we hid" |
| **Data-Driven** | Uses surprising statistics | "97% of Facebook ads fail because of this one metric" |
| **Story** | Opens with narrative | "Last Tuesday my client called me panicking..." |

## 2. Genius Patterns

### GP1: Copy Block Velocity
**What**: The speed of block cycling IS the engagement mechanism. The best copy doesn't use blocks sequentially — it weaves them. Pain → Curiosity → Promise → Pain → Proof → Curiosity → Constraint.
**When**: Any persuasive writing. Audit existing copy for velocity dips (places where one block dominates for too long).
**Metric**: No single block should dominate more than 3 consecutive sentences.

### GP2: The CASH Multiplication
**What**: Systematic ideation that guarantees you never run out of ad ideas. Each dimension multiplies, not adds.
**When**: Client asks for "more ads." Monthly creative refreshes. Testing new markets.
**Metric**: Minimum 50 unique hook+angle combinations per product.

### GP3: Solve vs Show Architecture
**What**: Two fundamentally different hook psychologies. Solve = reader must acknowledge a problem. Show = reader witnesses a result.
**When**: Awareness mapping. Unaware → show hooks. Problem-aware → solve hooks.
**Metric**: Test both for every campaign. Show typically wins at top of funnel.

### GP4: Proof-of-Work Hooks
**What**: Hooks that DEMONSTRATE expertise through their structure. Data analysis hooks, live rewrites, prediction hooks. The hook itself IS the proof.
**When**: Competitive niches where credibility is the bottleneck.
**Metric**: Hook contains at least one verifiable or demonstrable claim.

### GP5: Three-Factor Ideation
**What**: Never rely on one ideation source. CASH for structure. Swiping for validation. Brainstorming for originality.
**When**: Every ideation session. Default to this trifecta.
**Metric**: Minimum 30% of ideas from each source.

### GP6: Copy Block Interleaving
**What**: High-performing copy introduces NEW ANGLES on recurring blocks rather than new information. The 3rd pain reference uses a different metaphor than the 1st.
**When**: Long-form copy (VSLs, sales pages, emails sequences).
**Metric**: Each block reappearance uses a fresh angle.

### GP7: The Concept Layer
**What**: Most copywriters skip the concept and start at the angle. The concept is the EMOTIONAL TERRITORY — the paradigm the ad operates within. Getting the concept right makes every angle downstream more powerful.
**When**: Campaign strategy. Before writing any creative.
**Metric**: Concept should be expressible in one sentence without mentioning the product.

## 3. Hidden Knowledge

### HK1: Hooks Are 80% of Performance
Most copywriters spend 80% of their time on body copy. But the hook determines whether anyone reads the body. The optimal time allocation is inverted: 80% on hooks, 20% on body.

### HK2: Style Changes Rhythm, Not Content
In the CASH method, "Style" (UGC vs editorial vs talking head) doesn't change WHAT you say — it changes the RHYTHM of how copy blocks are deployed. UGC has rapid, informal block cycling. Editorial has measured, evidence-heavy cycling.

### HK3: Concept ≠ Product
The most common CASH failure is treating the Concept as the product description. The concept is the EMOTIONAL TERRITORY the ad operates in. "Your skin is aging because of inflammation" is a concept. "Our serum contains niacinamide" is a product feature. The concept frames the entire conversation.

### HK4: Copy Blocks Aren't Sequential
Beginners use blocks in order: Pain → Promise → Proof → CTA. Experts interleave them: Pain → Curiosity → Promise → Pain (new angle) → Proof → Curiosity → Constraint → Promise (new angle) → Proof → CTA. The variable speed creates engagement.
