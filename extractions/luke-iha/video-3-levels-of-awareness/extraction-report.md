## Genius Patterns

### 1. Curiosity × Relevance Architecture
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Every hook is evaluated on two axes simultaneously — how much *curiosity* it creates and how much *personal relevance* it carries. Too much curiosity without relevance = clickbait that doesn't convert. Too much relevance without curiosity = boring ad that doesn't get clicked.
- **Executable Behavior**: Score every hook on a 1-10 scale for both curiosity and relevance. Only deploy hooks scoring 7+ on BOTH axes. If curiosity is high but relevance is low, add a personal stakes element. If relevance is high but curiosity is low, add a contradiction or paradox.
- **Deployment Context**: Ad hooks, email subject lines, YouTube thumbnails, social media openers.
- **Success Metric**: CTR above 2% (baseline) with conversion rate above 1% (not just clicks, but qualified clicks).

### 2. Worldview Porn
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Instead of presenting new information, they articulate beliefs the audience *already holds* but hasn't seen expressed. This creates an instant "god, YES" identity resonance that bypasses all skepticism.
- **Executable Behavior**: Research your audience's tribal beliefs — what do they already believe but rarely see validated? Frame these beliefs as declarations. "Everyone knows that [tribal belief] but nobody's willing to say it." The audience feels *seen*, not sold to.
- **Deployment Context**: Top-of-funnel ads for unaware audiences, content hooks, brand manifestos, community building.
- **Success Metric**: High share rates and comment engagement ("I've been saying this for years!").

### 3. The Open Loop Engine
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Never resolve curiosity until after the mechanism reveal. Each section of the ad opens a new question while partially answering the previous one. The reader is always one step behind, chasing resolution.
- **Executable Behavior**: After the hook, open a loop ("And the reason this works is something most people have never heard of..."). Deliver the payoff of the HOOK, but open a NEW loop about the mechanism. Deliver the mechanism, but open a loop about the solution. The product is the resolution of ALL open loops.
- **Deployment Context**: Long-form ads, VSLs, webinar scripts, email sequences.
- **Success Metric**: Read-through rate above 60% for long-form ads (vs. typical 20-30%).

### 4. Organic Hook Theft
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: They don't invent hooks from scratch. They systematically steal hooks that have already been validated by organic platforms — YouTube titles with millions of views, viral tweets, Reddit posts with thousands of upvotes. If it went viral organically, the curiosity mechanism is proven.
- **Executable Behavior**: Build a hook bank by: (1) Searching YouTube for "[your topic] + surprising/secret/hidden" and noting titles with 500K+ views. (2) Scanning Reddit's top posts in relevant subreddits. (3) Collecting viral tweet threads. (4) Adapt the hook structure to your product. Keep the curiosity engine, swap the subject.
- **Deployment Context**: Ad creation, content ideation, email subject lines.
- **Success Metric**: Hook bank of 50+ validated hooks per niche. 3x faster ad creation.

### 5. The Paradox Hook
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Structure hooks as contradictions to common beliefs. "The thing everyone does to [desired outcome] is actually the reason they never achieve it." This creates cognitive dissonance — the reader MUST click to resolve the contradiction.
- **Executable Behavior**: Identify the most common advice in your market. Invert it. "Why [popular approach] is secretly killing your [desired result]." Support with a mechanism that explains why the paradox is true.
- **Deployment Context**: Unaware and problem-aware audience ads. Contrarian content. Category creation.
- **Success Metric**: CTR 2-4x higher than direct benefit hooks.

### 6. The Conspiracy Frame
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Position the reader as an insider discovering hidden truth. "They don't want you to know this." Creates an enemy (the establishment, big pharma, traditional experts), a secret (the hidden mechanism), and a hero (the reader, now armed with truth).
- **Executable Behavior**: Identify your market's "establishment." Frame their conventional wisdom as either ignorant or deliberately misleading. Present your mechanism as the suppressed alternative. Tone: rebellious insider, not tinfoil hat.
- **Deployment Context**: Health, finance, education, self-improvement — any market with an "old guard."
- **Success Metric**: High emotional engagement. Strong "us vs. them" community formation.

### 7. The Lost Wisdom Hook
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Reference ancient, forgotten, or obscure techniques that outperform modern approaches. "A 2,000-year-old technique that [modern experts] forgot." Taps both nostalgia and anti-establishment sentiment.
- **Executable Behavior**: Research historical practices in your market. Find one with genuine efficacy that's been abandoned. Frame it as rediscovered wisdom. Works especially well when combined with modern scientific validation ("Ancient monks knew this — and now Stanford has confirmed it").
- **Deployment Context**: Health, productivity, relationships, spirituality — markets with historical depth.
- **Success Metric**: Curiosity + authority in one hook. Higher trust than pure novelty hooks.

### 8. Vectors of Winning Angles
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Generate unlimited hook variations by running any topic through three systematic axes: **Time** (past patterns, present actions, future predictions), **Behavior** (what they do vs. don't do), and **Object** (unexpected tools, ingredients, or mechanisms). Each axis × each variation = dozens of unique angles.
- **Executable Behavior**: For any product, fill this matrix:
  - **Time**: "In 2024, [surprising trend]..." / "10 years ago, nobody knew..." / "By 2030, everyone will..."
  - **Behavior**: "People who [unexpected behavior] get [result]..." / "Stop [common action] if you want [outcome]..."
  - **Object**: "This [unexpected object] is the secret to [outcome]..." / "Why [common tool] is worse than [obscure alternative]..."
  Generate 3-5 hooks per axis. Select the best 5 for testing.
- **Deployment Context**: Ad creative generation, content ideation sprints, hook bank building.
- **Success Metric**: 20+ unique hooks generated per session. No more staring at blank pages.

## Hidden Knowledge

- **Unaware Ads = 10x Value**: Most advertisers compete for problem-aware and solution-aware audiences. These audiences already know they have a problem and are comparing solutions — making CPMs expensive and competition fierce. Unaware audiences are untouched gold. They cost less to reach (no competitive bidding), they're more grateful when educated (loyalty), and they have no competing solution to compare against (exclusive positioning). The skill to write unaware ads is the moat.

- **The Ad Structure Isn't Linear**: Amateur copywriters go Hook → Problem → Solution → Product. Iha's structure is: Hook → **Payoff** → Mechanism → Solution → Product → Reasons to Buy. The payoff comes BEFORE the mechanism reveal. You validate the hook's promise with a satisfying "aha" before explaining how it works. This keeps the reader engaged through the mechanism section (which is inherently less exciting).

- **Transformation > Information**: The unaware ad's job isn't to inform. It's to *transform the reader's worldview*. By the time they see the product, their beliefs have already shifted to make the product feel inevitable. The ad does the intellectual and emotional work. The product page just confirms the decision already made.

- **Hook Banks Are Gold Mines**: Systematically collecting organic viral hooks creates a competitive moat over time. While competitors stare at blank pages, you're selecting from validated curiosity mechanisms. The hook bank compounds — every entry becomes a template for future ads. This is an unfair advantage that grows with time.

## Methodology: Unaware Ad Architecture

### The 6-Part Structure
1. **The Hook** — Curiosity × Relevance. Use paradox, conspiracy, lost wisdom, or any of the 10 types. Score 7+ on both axes.
2. **The Payoff** — Deliver on the hook's promise. Give the reader the "aha" before asking them to buy anything. This builds trust and keeps them reading.
3. **The Mechanism** — Explain *why* the payoff works using a mechanism they haven't heard before. This is where Jargon Flurries and Explanatory Reasoning deploy.
4. **The Solution** — Transition from mechanism to "and there's a way to get this working for you." The solution is the bridge between understanding and action.
5. **The Product** — Now introduce the product as the vehicle for the solution. Not before. The product arrives after the worldview shift is complete.
6. **Reasons to Buy** — Stack proof, social validation, guarantees, urgency. This is where the proof-mechanisms skill plugs in.

### Hook Generation: The 10 Archetypes
1. Paradox — "X everyone does is actually causing Y"
2. Conspiracy — "They don't want you to know about X"
3. Lost Wisdom — "Ancient technique that does X better than any modern method"
4. Counter-Intuitive — "Why doing less X actually gives you more Y"
5. Prediction — "By 2030, X will replace Y entirely"
6. Transformation Story — "How a [unlikely person] achieved [unlikely result]"
7. Hidden Connection — "The link between X and Y that nobody talks about"
8. Status Quo Attack — "Why everything you've been told about X is wrong"
9. Specificity Hook — "The 3.7-second technique that [precise result]"
10. Identity Hook — "Why [identity group] secretly struggle with X"
