## Genius Patterns

### Pattern 1: The 2007 Pattern Recognition Filter
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Every new trend, Ethan maps it to a historical analogue. AI content spam → 2007 shopping comparison scraping. AEO "newness" → TikTok search → Instagram search → Facebook search. This isn't nostalgia — it's a predictive model.
- **Executable Behavior**: Before reacting to any new channel/trend, ask: "What did this look like in 2007/2012/2018?" The historical pattern predicts the outcome with high accuracy.
- **Deployment Context**: When evaluating any new marketing channel, platform shift, or "SEO is dead" narrative.
- **Success Metric**: You avoid the hype cycle entirely and invest only in what the historical pattern says will persist.

### Pattern 2: The Citation Frequency Principle (Head vs. Tail Divergence)
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan recognizes that AEO fundamentally changed the relationship between ranking and winning. In Google, position #1 wins. In LLMs, *frequency of citation* across the summary wins — the answer mentioned most often across sources becomes the top recommendation.
- **Executable Behavior**: Stop optimizing for single page rank. Instead, map every source that could cite your brand for a target question and systematically get mentioned across as many of those sources as possible.
- **Deployment Context**: Any head query where the LLM summarizes multiple citations (e.g., "What's the best X for Y?").
- **Success Metric**: Share of voice (% of time you appear in LLM responses) increases across multiple surfaces.

### Pattern 3: The Growing Pie Thesis
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Instantly reframes "Google is dying" as "The pie is getting bigger." Each new platform (TikTok, LLMs, Instagram) adds ON TOP of Google, never replaces it. Google's slice stays the same; total search volume increases.
- **Executable Behavior**: Never abandon a working channel because a new one appears. Stack the new channel on top. Budget allocation = maintain existing + add incremental for emerging.
- **Deployment Context**: Strategic planning when new channels emerge, budget allocation decisions.
- **Success Metric**: Multi-channel presence grows without cannibalizing existing traffic sources.

### Pattern 4: The Anti-Spam Immunity System
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan has a deeply embedded "will this get nuked?" detector. His 2007 experience of watching entire categories of spam sites get wiped out by algorithm updates created permanent antibodies against short-term manipulation.
- **Executable Behavior**: For every growth tactic, ask: "If someone at [platform] saw what I'm doing, would they applaud or would they build an algorithm to stop me?" If the latter, it will work temporarily and then destroy you.
- **Deployment Context**: Any optimization tactic, especially Reddit, YouTube, or citation seeding.
- **Success Metric**: Tactics that survive algorithm updates for 2+ years.

### Pattern 5: The Longtail Resurrection
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Recognizes that the longtail died in traditional SEO (Google favored broad, authoritative pages) but has been *resurrected* in LLMs because people ask follow-up questions naturally. 25 words per query vs. 6 in Google. The tail is massive again.
- **Executable Behavior**: Mine every obscure, hyper-specific question from sales calls, customer support, and community forums. Many of these questions have ZERO existing answers — meaning you can be the only citation.
- **Deployment Context**: Early-stage companies and niche products especially. Any B2B product with complex use cases.
- **Success Metric**: Capturing longtail queries where you're the sole citation.

### Pattern 6: The 5% Landing Page Rule
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan carries the data that 1 in 20 landing pages drives roughly 85% of all traffic. This fundamentally changes the investment model — instead of cheap content at scale, invest heavily in the few pages that will actually win.
- **Executable Behavior**: Instead of 20 mediocre pages, identify the 1 page that matches a high-value topic cluster and invest 20x the typical budget in making it definitively the best resource that exists.
- **Deployment Context**: Content strategy prioritization, budget allocation, SEO/AEO planning.
- **Success Metric**: Per-page ROI increases dramatically as wasted content is eliminated.

### Pattern 7: The Reddit Authenticity Protocol
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan immediately dismisses the obvious growth-hacker approach to Reddit (fake accounts, automated spam) and recognizes that Reddit's community immune system is stronger than any automation. The winning strategy is embarrassingly simple: be a real person, say who you are and where you work, and give useful information.
- **Executable Behavior**: Find citation-relevant Reddit threads. Create an account under your real name. State your name and company. Give a genuinely useful answer. Even 5 high-quality comments can drive significant AEO impact.
- **Deployment Context**: Off-site citation optimization for any product that shows up in LLM RAG results.
- **Success Metric**: Comments survive community moderation AND appear in LLM citations.

### Pattern 8: The Experimental Design Imperative
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan applies academic research rigor to growth experiments. He uses control groups (100 questions untouched) vs. test groups (100 questions with intervention), tracks over time, and demands reproducibility before calling something a best practice.
- **Executable Behavior**: Never A/B test without a control group. Never trust a result you've only seen once. Reproduce experiments multiple times. Treat published "best practices" as unvalidated hypotheses until you've personally reproduced them.
- **Deployment Context**: Any AEO or SEO optimization experiment.
- **Success Metric**: Only tactics validated through controlled, reproducible experiments make it into your playbook.

### Pattern 9: The Surface Divergence Awareness
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan knows that ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude all use similar foundational algorithms (LLM + RAG) but produce *very different citation results*. ChatGPT citations overlap only 35% with Google results; Perplexity overlaps 70%. Different surfaces = different optimization needs.
- **Executable Behavior**: Track share of voice across multiple LLM surfaces, not just one. Recognize that optimizing for ChatGPT doesn't automatically win you Perplexity or Gemini.
- **Deployment Context**: Multi-surface AEO strategy, answer tracking setup.
- **Success Metric**: Consistent share of voice growth across 3+ LLM surfaces simultaneously.

### Pattern 10: The Information Gain Heuristic
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan identifies "information gain" as the fundamental quality signal — did you say something nobody else said? Combined with "typicality" detection — if your content is too similar to what already exists, it gets flagged as derivative.
- **Executable Behavior**: For every piece of content, ask: "What does this say that no existing result says?" If the answer is nothing, the content is wasted. Add original research, proprietary data, or unique experience.
- **Deployment Context**: Content creation for any channel (SEO, AEO, newsletters, podcasts).
- **Success Metric**: Content consistently offers novel insights not found in competing resources.

### Pattern 11: The Help Center as AEO Weapon
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan recognizes that help centers — traditionally ignored by SEO teams — are actually ideal AEO assets because LLM users ask detailed product questions ("Does X integrate with Y?") that map perfectly to help center content. Most companies have zero coverage of the tail in their help centers.
- **Executable Behavior**: (1) Move help center to subdirectory, not subdomain. (2) Cross-link help center pages to each other. (3) Mine sales calls and support tickets for tail questions that have no existing help center articles. (4) Open to community Q&A to let users fill the tail naturally.
- **Deployment Context**: Any B2B SaaS or product company with an existing help center.
- **Success Metric**: Help center pages appear as citations in LLM responses to product-specific queries.

### Pattern 12: The Hidden Attribution Problem
- **What He Does Unconsciously**: Ethan knows that LLM traffic is massively underreported because users don't click citations — they copy the brand name, open a new tab, type it into Google or directly navigate to the site. This shows up as "branded Google search" or "direct traffic," completely masking the LLM's role.
- **Executable Behavior**: Don't rely solely on referral tracking to measure AEO impact. Add post-conversion surveys ("How did you hear about us?") and cross-reference branded search volume increases with AEO experiment timelines.
- **Deployment Context**: AEO ROI measurement, attribution modeling, growth reporting.
- **Success Metric**: True AEO impact is measured (not just last-touch referral clicks).

## Hidden Knowledge

### 1. The Convergence Inevitability
LLMs are adding search features (maps, shopping carousels, clickable cards). Google is adding LLM features (AI Overviews, AI Mode). They are converging on a single experience. The endpoint is one interface that searches AND summarizes AND takes action. This means AEO and SEO aren't parallel channels — they're converging into one thing. Optimize for both or you're optimizing for neither.

### 2. The Misinformation Ratio
~90%+ of published AEO "best practices" are unvalidated. They spread through the same mechanism they warn against: someone states something, it gets repeated, and nobody does analysis. Ethan treats ALL external advice as unvalidated hypotheses. This is the most important meta-skill — skepticism toward published best practices, verified only through your own controlled experiments.

### 3. The 6x Conversion Signal
WebFlow saw 6x higher conversion rates from LLM traffic vs. Google search traffic. This is because LLM users have been through an extended conversation building intent — they've narrowed, refined, and committed before clicking. This means LLM-sourced leads are disproportionately valuable, making AEO ROI dramatically higher per-lead than SEO.

### 4. The Tool Commoditization Trap
The AEO tool market is reproducing the keyword tracking market's worst dynamics: $50,000 for what is essentially a commodity task (answer tracking = keyword tracking with different inputs). Ethan's advice: pick the cheapest tool that does the job. There is no "premium" version of tracking whether you're mentioned or not.

### 5. The January 2025 Inflection Point
LLM referral traffic inflected dramatically in January 2025 — driven by both increased adoption AND increased clickability (maps, shopping carousels, clickable cards within answers). This is the earliest external data point validating that AEO is now a real traffic channel, not just an intellectual curiosity.

### 6. The Domain Authority Bypass
In traditional SEO, startups can't compete because they lack domain authority — a moat that takes years to build. In AEO, this moat doesn't exist. A brand-new company can be mentioned on Reddit, have a YouTube video made about them, and be cited by a blog tomorrow — and show up in LLM answers immediately. AEO is the first search channel where early-stage companies can compete from day one.

### 7. The Derivative Collapse Model
If AI-generated content worked at scale, it would create an infinite loop of derivatives: AI writes content → content enters RAG corpus → AI summarizes its own derivatives → wisdom of the crowd collapses to a single opinion. The result: "the best ice cream flavor is vanilla" with no other options. This is why purely AI-generated content doesn't and can't work — it destroys the diversity that makes the system valuable. The counter: AI-*assisted* content with human information gain is the only sustainable path.

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Question Research
1. Extract your paid search terms and competitor paid search terms
2. Transform keywords into natural-language questions (use ChatGPT to convert)
3. Mine sales call transcripts, customer support tickets, and Reddit threads for tail questions
4. Identify questions that have NEVER been answered before (sole-citation opportunities)

### Phase 2: Answer Tracking
1. Select an answer tracking tool (cheapest that does the job)
2. Track share of voice: % of time mentioned × average position × across multiple LLM surfaces
3. Account for variance: same question → different answers each run → track distribution
4. Account for question variance: different wordings of the same intent → track all variations

### Phase 3: On-Site Optimization
1. Create landing pages matching the page types that appear in citations (listicles, category pages, articles, tool pages)
2. Answer ALL follow-up questions on each page (not just the head question)
3. Maximize information gain: original research, proprietary data, expert perspective
4. Optimize help center: move to subdirectory, cross-link, fill the tail

### Phase 4: Off-Site Citation Optimization
| Citation Source | Strategy | Difficulty | Control |
|----------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Paid affiliates (Forbes, DotDash) | Pay for mentions | Low | High |
| YouTube/Vimeo | Create videos for non-glamorous high-LTV keywords | Low | High |
| Reddit | Real account, real name, real company, real answer | Medium | Medium |
| Quora | Similar to Reddit but less community policing | Low | Medium |
| Third-party blogs | Guest posts, PR, partnerships | Medium | Medium |
| UGC platforms | Organic mentions driven by product quality | High | Low |

### Phase 5: Experimental Validation
1. Take 200 target questions → 100 control, 100 test
2. Track baseline for 2+ weeks
3. Intervene on test group (one tactic at a time)
4. Compare test vs. control
5. Reproduce experiment at least once before adopting as standard practice
6. Discard anything that doesn't reproduce

### Phase 6: Measurement
1. Answer tracking (share of voice across surfaces)
2. Post-conversion surveys ("How did you hear about us?")
3. Branded search volume correlation with experiment timelines
4. Direct traffic anomaly detection (hidden LLM attribution)
