## THE 7 COPYWRITING PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: The More Human, The Better
Write like you talk. Use filler words. Sound like a real person texting a friend, not a robot or someone trying to sound fancy. If it sounds like it was written, rewrite it until it sounds like it was said.

**Violation Signals**: Formal language, complex sentences, no contractions, sounds "written"

### Principle 2: Be Clear, Not Clever
Say exactly what you mean. No abstract phrases, no trying to sound cute, no "turn your ideas into income with the power of tomorrow" nonsense. If the reader has to think about what you mean, you've failed.

**Violation Signals**: Vague promises, abstract language, metaphors without clarity, trying to sound like Apple

### Principle 3: Benefits Over Features
People don't buy products, they buy what products do for them. Don't talk about the pulleys and levers - talk about the abs. Focus on transformation, not mechanism.

**Violation Signals**: Feature lists without "so that...", talking about what it IS vs. what it DOES for them

### Principle 4: Details Equal Trust
Specific beats vague every time. "Laya started from zero, transitioned from sales, now makes $12K/month working 5 hours a week" beats "lots of students make money." Names, numbers, timelines, specifics.

**Violation Signals**: Vague claims, "many people," "great results," no specific examples or numbers

### Principle 5: Speak Your Buyer's Language
Different audiences have different fears, desires, and vocabulary. A 35-year-old mom buying weight loss supplements has different psychology than a 50-year-old dad. Use their words, their concerns, their worldview.

**Violation Signals**: Generic language that could apply to anyone, not speaking to specific fears/desires, wrong tone for audience

### Principle 6: Lead with Emotion, Close with Logic
Hook their heart first - that's what stops the scroll and gets attention. Then close with logic - that's what justifies the purchase. Feeling first, thinking second.

**Violation Signals**: Opening with facts/logic, no emotional hook, closing without logical justification

### Principle 7: Nobody Cares About You - Make It About the Buyer
Stop talking about what you do. Talk about what they get. "We send emails" = boring. "You collect revenue from your dormant email list" = interesting. The customer is the hero, not you.

**Violation Signals**: "We/I/Our" heavy, talking about the business vs. the customer, features without customer framing
