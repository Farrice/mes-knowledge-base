## Genius Patterns

### 1. The 4-3-2-1 Content System
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: Eliminate decision fatigue by defining a rigid weekly posting structure that balances brand-building with lead generation.
- **Executable Behavior**: Write 4 posts/week. Split them into 1 Education, 1 Storytelling, 1 "Edu-Telling" (both combined), and 1 Education (again). Target 3 pillars: Total Addressable Market (TAM), Growth/Startup, Sales. Speak to 2 audiences: ICPs and IFPs. Offer 1 Lead Magnet.
- **Deployment Context**: When a founder is struggling with consistency or "doesn't know what to write about."
- **Success Metric**: A full week of content drafted in one sitting without hesitation about topic selection.

### 2. The Unselfish "Financial Fast Principle"
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: They realize that nobody actually cares about their personal story unless it translates to a financial or time-saving payoff for the reader.
- **Executable Behavior**: In the first two lines of *any* story post, immediately hint at how this will make the user money, save time, or improve their life. Make the reader the "main character" of your personal story.
- **Deployment Context**: When drafting a storytelling post about a personal achievement (e.g., "I quit my 9-to-5").
- **Success Metric**: Hook retention increases because the reader sees immediate personal value.

### 3. "Edu-Telling" Technical Translation
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: They prevent technical content from becoming dry by wrapping "how-to" tutorials in personal authority narratives.
- **Executable Behavior**: Open with a personal credibility marker ("I started coding at 15 and built two 6-figure SaaS products"), then immediately transition into the educational payload ("Here is how to vibe code your own SaaS in a weekend").
- **Deployment Context**: When an expert wants to share "nerdy" details but needs broad appeal.
- **Success Metric**: High engagement from both beginners (IFP) and potential buyers (ICP).

### 4. Format Reverse-Engineering
- **What They Do Unconsciously**: They look at top creators outside their niche and steal the *structure* of their posts, not the content.
- **Executable Behavior**: Find a viral post from an unrelated industry. Strip away the niche-specific words, keep the formatting (bullet counts, line breaks, hook structure), and inject your own industry's context.
- **Deployment Context**: When needing a proven format for a complex or dry topic.
- **Success Metric**: A post that looks visually identical to a viral hit, but with entirely original technical content.

## Hidden Knowledge

- **The IFP (Ideal Follower Persona) Engine**: Most marketers only target buyers (ICP). Lara targets the 18-20 year olds who can't buy, but will engage/share heavily, thereby algorithmically pushing the content to the ICP. 
- **The "High-Status Photo" Anchor**: Text-only posts speed up scrolling. Adding a graphic or candid photo of the founder "looking the part" (e.g., looking like a tech nerd at a laptop) takes over the feed visually and buys the writer an extra 3 seconds of attention to land their text hook.
- **Passive Sub-Communication**: Rather than saying "I am an authority," you show it passively through photos (e.g., wearing a suit, working in a certain environment) or by referencing past achievements casually before teaching.

## Methodology

1. **Authority Audit**: Establish what the founder wants to be known for (e.g., "The Tech Guy"). Identify High-Value Skills and Monetizable Expertise.
2. **Pillar Construction**: Define the TAM topics (AI, Productivity, Remote Work, LinkedIn), Growth topics (building a SaaS, quitting 9-to-5), and Sales topics.
3. **The 4-3-2-1 Matrix Construction**: Map the 4 post types (Edu, Story, Edu-Tell, Edu) against the 3 pillars, 2 personas, and 1 lead magnet.
4. **AI Output Acceleration**: Use the established matrix to prompt an LLM for Ideas/Hooks first, validate them against viral formats, and then generate the drafts.
5. **Visual Anchoring**: Pair the generated draft with a context-enhancing authentic photo or graphic.
